## Frameworks (how he thinks)

- **The best technologies are invisible.** "You shouldn't even notice they work" — good design disappears into use (5G Smart Data Mode, ProMotion). A core design value. [2020-10-25] ([[../wiki/sources/2020-10-25-yt-X1b3C2081-Q]])
- **Vertical integration beats raw spec sheets.** When one company controls hardware + software, it outperforms higher-spec rivals; don't review off the spec sheet. [2013-10-12] ([[../wiki/sources/2013-10-12-yt-uBEaG6n0XDs]])
- **Margins + perceived value.** Price is manufacturing cost plus a gap that funds R&D, packaging, and marketing; paying for brand/image is a legitimate choice, "not necessarily a bad thing." Decide whether you're buying performance or the name. [2014-08-31] ([[../wiki/sources/2014-08-31-yt-ZsxQxS0AdBY]])
- **Grade on the right curve (price-to-value).** Never judge a budget device on a flagship curve — "B+ camera, A+ for the price." Value is a first-class review axis. [2018-05-25] ([[../wiki/sources/2018-05-25-yt-0PrUr3bQdwM]]) · [2019-09-30] ([[../wiki/sources/2019-09-30-yt-OoY7zp8GkLI]])
- **"Bust" = expectations minus reality.** Disappointment is the delta between what a product could have been and what shipped, not raw badness. [2019-12-24] ([[../wiki/sources/2019-12-24-yt-4OnwUz-I2GI]])
- **"Why now?"** When a company finally ships an overdue feature, ask what it waited to get right instead of dunking on the lateness. [2020-06-23] ([[../wiki/sources/2020-06-23-yt-ZLyDvABxGF0]])
- **"The tech works, the ecosystem isn't ready."** The bottleneck on bleeding-edge tech is usually the surrounding display/content/ecosystem, not raw compute (8K gaming, Dolby Vision HDR, 8K OLED). Don't buy *for* the buzzword; treat it as a future bonus. [2020-09-23] ([[../wiki/sources/2020-09-23-yt-kFz9afj8lu0]]) · [2020-10-25] ([[../wiki/sources/2020-10-25-yt-X1b3C2081-Q]])
- **Scratch-vs-shatter is an inverse tradeoff.** Softer glass shatters less but scratches more, and vice versa — a repeatable durability lens. [2020-10-25] ([[../wiki/sources/2020-10-25-yt-X1b3C2081-Q]])
- **Correct exposure beats over-brightness.** In blind camera testing, a correctly-exposed photo beats an over-bright/over-saturated one; brightness only helps a bad camera avoid last place. [2022-12-23] ([[../wiki/sources/2022-12-23-yt-MYrfLmm_cT4]])
- **Frame rate is king, but resolution buys immersion.** His stated position on the refresh-vs-resolution trade-off. [2020-09-23] ([[../wiki/sources/2020-09-23-yt-kFz9afj8lu0]])

Defers durability to JerryRigEverything's Zack and benchmarks to Linus Tech Tips / Hardware Canucks — "I'm just going to live my life."
