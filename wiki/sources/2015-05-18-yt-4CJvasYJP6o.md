# YouTube Processing: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-05-18)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"** breakdown of how YouTube compresses and delivers video — a **creator/production +
consumer-education** landmark. He explains that you never watch the original uploaded file: processing
transcodes each upload into multiple resolutions (240p→4K) and **adaptive-bitrate streaming** swaps you
between them by connection strength, and even the 4K version is "compressed to about a tenth of the
original file size." Includes his own upload workflow ("uploading" → "processing" tweets) and a biography
note (when he started, "every MKBHD video was 720p HD").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-05-18] **You don't watch the original file.** 300 hours are uploaded per minute; a video like his
  can be a couple of GB. Playing that back raw "would look like [buffering] unless you have Google Fiber,"
  and on a phone would "overheat or destroy your data cap." So YouTube **processes** every upload into many
  resolutions/codecs.
- [2015-05-18] **Adaptive-bitrate streaming.** Each video gets 240p/360p/480p/720p/1080p/1440p/2160p
  versions; YouTube "constantly determines every few seconds which processed version to show you based on
  your connection strength" — to avoid ever showing the buffering bar. Even the **full 4K on YouTube is
  ~1/10 the original file size.**
- [2015-05-18] **His upload workflow (creator note).** "Every time I upload I tweet 'uploading' [file →
  servers], then 'processing.'" A window into his publish process.
- [2015-05-18] **History + biography.** Until March 2008 the max was 240p, then an HQ 360p button; "when I
  first started uploading, the highest quality was 720p — so every MKBHD video was 720p HD back in the day."
- [2015-05-18] **A personal theory (creator-business).** "Adaptive-bitrate streaming is why YouTubers can't
  see analytics on who's watching in what resolution / who watches in 4K" — you might see who *clicks* 4K,
  but not who stays there.
