# Quick Note on Speedfan (@mkbhd, 2009-01-30)

## Summary
A short "note" from age-15 Marques warning that the fan-control freeware **SpeedFan** "does not work
on most laptops." Demoing it on his **HP Pavilion dv7t** (a review he says is coming soon), he shows
that on a laptop SpeedFan only reads temperatures and cores — no fan-speed controls, options, or
editing — "really really just lifeless on a laptop," "completely empty," versus the full-featured
experience you see in other YouTube videos on a desktop. Because software cooling doesn't work well
for him, he says he ordered a physical **laptop cooler** (a Cooler Master-type pad, garbled in the
captions) from **Newegg** for ~$60–70 for his 17-inch laptop, and judges a hardware cooler "better
than software to cool your computer." He notes he'd need a camera to do a proper review of the cooler.

## Key claims
- [2009-01-30] **SpeedFan does not work well on most laptops** — on his HP Pavilion dv7t (and a
  friend's laptop) it only recognizes temperatures and cores, with no fan controls, options, or
  editing; "it's completely empty because it's running on a laptop."
- [2009-01-30] On a **desktop**, SpeedFan works fine (full fan info, temperatures, editing), as seen
  in other YouTube videos — the limitation is laptop-specific.
- [2009-01-30] He also mentions **RivaTuner** (run it, "it's okay") in the same context.
- [2009-01-30] Recommendation: for a laptop, a **physical laptop cooler** beats software — he ordered
  one from **Newegg** for ~**$60–70** for his 17-inch laptop; "it's better, I'd say, than software to
  cool your computer."
- [2009-01-30] Forward references: a review of the **HP Pavilion dv7t** laptop is coming "soon," and
  he'd need to "get a camera" to review the cooler.
