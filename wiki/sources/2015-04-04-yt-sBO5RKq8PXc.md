# Oxygen OS on the OnePlus One! (@mkbhd, 2015-04-04)

## Summary
A look at **Oxygen OS 1.0**, OnePlus's own ROM built for the OnePlus One after the Cyanogen split — which
Marques again **flashed himself**. His verdict: a **very simple, very clean, near-stock** ROM (Android
5.0.2) whose main new feature is customizable quick-settings; "a good place to start — stable — and a good
base to build on." A dated node in the OnePlus/Cyanogen saga (the phone he's "done the most individual
videos on"), reinforcing his control-over-software habit. Low net-new yield.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-04-04] **"Probably the phone I've done the most individual videos on."** Owners were waiting on two
  things after the Cyanogen split: CM12s and OnePlus's own Oxygen OS. He **flashed the 750 MB Oxygen OS zip
  himself** (it's not an OTA) — his control-over-software habit again.
- [2015-04-04] **Near-stock, one headline feature.** "How close it feels and looks to stock Android"
  (Nexus-like), built on Android 5.0.2 (not the latest). The standout: **rearrangeable quick-settings tiles**
  (toggle what shows up). Carries CM11s options (on-/off-screen button choice, screen-off gestures — which
  "activated in my pocket," so he turned them off) and uses the plain Google Camera.
- [2015-04-04] **Verdict: a stable base, not a destination yet.** "A very simple, clean, near-stock ROM —
  it's not the only one (Paranoid Android, Bliss Pop, CyanogenMod)… but they needed something stable out
  before CM12. A good place to start and build on with future updates."
