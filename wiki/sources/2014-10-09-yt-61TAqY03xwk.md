# Apple iPhone 6 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-09)

## Summary
The full iPhone 6 review — a clean anchor for several core doctrines. It restates the **no-cases → dbrand
skin** habit and the **anti-thinness thesis** (small battery + protruding camera + slippery), and it's a
textbook **don't-review-off-the-spec-sheet / vertical-integration** case: a 1.4 GHz dual-core / 1 GB
iPhone "performs like any other phone with a 2.5 GHz chip and 3 GB — thank Apple's vertical integration
and the A8 chip." The camera earns strong praise (fast, great image processing, a middle ground between
HTC's UltraPixels and Samsung's megapixel-cramming). Verdict: "a safe step forward, not a mandatory
upgrade" — and he notes his personal iPhone pick is the **6 Plus**, not the 6.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-09] **No cases → a skin, restated.** The rounded all-metal body is seamless but slippery ("my
  confidence holding it is dropping"); "you guys know how I feel about cases, so a high-quality skin will
  suit me just fine" — a dbrand skin for grip.
- [2014-10-09] **Vertical integration / don't-review-off-the-spec-sheet (canonical).** "If you look at
  the numbers you'll see a 1.4 GHz dual-core and 1 GB of RAM, but the thing performs like any other phone
  with a 2.5 GHz chip and 3 GB — you can thank Apple's vertical integration and the awesome A8 chip." Raw
  specs mislead; integration is what delivers.
- [2014-10-09] **The bigger screen's benefit "stops" at size** — everything's bigger, but iOS 8 adds
  almost nothing to *use* the extra pixels (just Reachability). "I wish they did a bit more with all those
  extra pixels" (more multitasking, bigger touch targets).
- [2014-10-09] **Camera praise — image processing is the differentiator.** "One of the best and one of
  the fastest" — fast UI/capture/AF, a fast wide aperture, and a bias toward faster shutter (like the
  OnePlus One) trusting **top-notch image processing** to clean noise. Framed as the **middle ground
  between HTC's UltraPixels and Samsung's megapixel-cramming** — "maybe this is what we were asking HTC to
  do all along." Only downside: 8 MP means **no 4K video** ("4K is 8.3 MP") until the next one.
- [2014-10-09] **Anti-thinness, restated.** The 1810 mAh battery is small (10–15% better than the 5S;
  great standby, but heavy days need a charger). "I just wish the phone was a little thicker so they could
  fit a 2000–2100 mAh battery… and get rid of the camera protrusion."
- [2014-10-09] **iOS 8 caveats + app-scaling lag** — the 8.0.1 update "bricked the phone" (quickly
  fixed), and unusually, major devs lagged in updating apps for the bigger display ("big and awkward and
  fuzzy").
- [2014-10-09] **Verdict + disclosed baseline.** "As a heavy Google-service user, iOS and iPhones aren't
  my first choice, obviously" — but among iPhones his pick is the **6 Plus, not the 6**. The 6 is "a safe
  step forward… a definite upgrade from the last iPhone but not a mandatory upgrade."
