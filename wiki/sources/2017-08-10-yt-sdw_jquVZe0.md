# Snakes in the Studio? Ask MKBHD V19! (@mkbhd, 2017-08-10)

## Summary
A monthly Ask-MKBHD Q&A — a biography goldmine. Reveals a **turned-down TV-tech-correspondent offer** (the
creator-independence value), the behind-the-scenes of the **Project Loveday win** (4-person crew; Elon's
first-ever tweet at him), the merch philosophy, why products leak, and his editor "Chatter."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-10] **BIOGRAPHY — turned down a TV tech-correspondent job (creator independence).** "In college I
  was invited to a media company's HQ — they needed a young, knowledgeable TV tech correspondent, and I fit
  the role. I was 100% not interested. The whole scripted, live, story-after-story thing isn't what I'm into
  — I'm on YouTube for a reason: this format suits how I want to talk about things. And the who's-paying-you-
  vs.-what-you-can-talk-about thing is weird too. I really like being independent and using this platform to
  talk about whatever I want." A cornerstone creator-independence statement.
- [2017-08-10] **BIOGRAPHY — the Project Loveday win + the first-ever Elon tweet.** "That 90-second Tesla
  contest entry won — it got shown on stage, all the employees watched it live (I was there, saw their
  reaction). It took a **4-person team** and 2 days of shooting (why the weather/bag colors change between
  scenes) — a car video is physically impossible to make with one person, unlike a phone review. And fun
  fact: this is the **first time Elon Musk ever tweeted at me** — he had to go out of his way to find my
  handle." Production-scaling + a milestone.
- [2017-08-10] **Merch philosophy.** "The custom MKBHD circuit-design shirt is on sale (my third Teespring
  shirt). The goal: something you can wear anywhere where people don't ask 'what is that?' — a cool design
  that just happens to incorporate the logo." A creator-business value (subtle, wearable branding).
- [2017-08-10] **Teach-the-mechanism: why products leak.** "It's 2017, everything leaks. The supply chains
  (Samsung, Apple) are huge — thousands of people across design → manufacture → approval; if one leaks, it's
  hard to track, punish, or stop. People pay Foxconn workers a year's wage to show the iPhone's front panel —
  they get fired, so what, they have a year's wage now." A follow-the-incentives explainer.
- [2017-08-10] **On an Apple car / self-driving.** "I've photographed Apple 'Maps' cars in NY/NJ — way too
  many sensors to just be Maps; looks like a self-driving project. But **Apple doesn't have to make a car to
  make a self-driving-car project** — half of what Tesla does is software (Tesla is half a car company, half
  a software company), and Apple is a software company." A sharp industry read.
- [2017-08-10] **Personal/studio details.** As a fellow Model S owner, the thing he most wants: **Spotify in
  the Tesla music player** ("long time coming, still isn't there"). The Studio Tours series is ~3–4×/year
  (big productions). The **ball pythons** in videos belong to the studio's neighbors. Confirms his editor
  **"Chatter" now edits basically everything.** Pixel 2 "probably won't have a headphone jack."
