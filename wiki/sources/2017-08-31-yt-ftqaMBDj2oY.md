# The Essential Phone Review: Save Your Money! (@mkbhd, 2017-08-31)

## Summary
A negative-verdict review of the Essential Phone (Andy Rubin's new company) — "Save Your Money." A clean
example of the **great-hardware-undone-by-one-fatal-flaw** structure: he genuinely admires the titanium/
ceramic industrial design and clean stock software, but the **camera and camera app are so bad they sink the
$700 phone.**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-31] **The hardware is genuinely impressive (rare for a new company).** "All-black titanium and
  ceramic, no protrusions, **no logos or branding at all** — just an essential rectangle. Titanium sides
  (harder than aluminum, better against drops) and a polished-ceramic back (harder than glass, less
  scratch-prone but more brittle). I like the industrial design — simple, not flashy, but signature." Credits
  what a first-time OEM usually gets wrong. (A fingerprint magnet → "a rare instance where a dbrand skin makes
  it look *better*.")
- [2017-08-31] **The thin-bezel cutout works — "it disappears a couple hours in."** "The 5.7" display with a
  cutout for the front camera and tiny bezels — I thought I wouldn't like it or it'd be distracting, but it
  disappears a couple hours into using it; the phone looks awesome. When not using the full display, it blacks
  out that bar to match the bottom." An impressions-vs-use reversal on the notch.
- [2017-08-31] **THE FATAL FLAW: the camera + camera app.** "This is the weakest part, straight up — if you
  want to tell someone why not to buy this phone, link them to this part. Dual 13 MP color + monochrome that
  most people will never notice; the **camera app is the only thing I've seen lag hard** on this phone
  (lags to shoot, to toggle HDR, to switch modes), and the photos are mediocre at best — soft, low dynamic
  range, noisy in anything but perfect light, often wrong exposure; HDR barely helps." The single dealbreaker.
- [2017-08-31] **The pure-stock software is a real strength.** "Where it lives up to the name: basically
  stock Android 7.1.1, zero bloatware, the same experience as a Pixel — clean. Already two updates since I got
  it." His stock-Android-restraint value, credited.
- [2017-08-31] **The verdict + the "essential" joke.** "The lacking camera + no waterproofing + no headphone
  jack ('the easiest joke: they didn't include a jack, which is essential to most people') make this a
  save-your-money. Software *could* improve the camera over time, but I've never seen one go from not-good to
  really-good on updates alone — so I feel pretty safe saying skip it." A clear negative with the honest
  "software rarely fully fixes a bad camera" caveat.
