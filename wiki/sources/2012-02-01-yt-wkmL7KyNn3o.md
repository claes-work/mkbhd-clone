# Cyborg RAT 7 Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-02-01)

## Summary
A review of the Cyborg R.A.T. 7 gaming mouse — the peripheral he says he'll use full-time
with the Mac Mini build — structured as a tour of its adjustability: sliding palm rest,
five removable weights, swappable pinky rest, tiltable thumb rest, and nine buttons. Delivered
in his heaviest comedic register of the batch. Published as a **stopgap while waiting on the
SSD** for the Mac Mini series, which he says openly.

## Key claims
- [2012-02-01] **Scheduling transparency**: "this is coming to you before part two of the world's
  fastest Mac Mini series, because I am waiting on the SSD to ship." He explains the publishing
  order rather than letting the series silently stall.
- [2012-02-01] **Superlative issued as a challenge**: "this is hands down the most customizable
  mouse I have ever seen. If you can show me one more adjustable than the RAT 7, well, please show
  me. But until then, I give this a title — this gets the crown of the most customizable
  peripheral." He invites falsification of his own superlative.
- [2012-02-01] **Own-gear disclosure**: it's the mouse "that I'm using and that I will be using
  full-time with the Mac Mini" — reviewed as a daily driver, consistent with the lived-with-it
  standard.
- [2012-02-01] Adjustability documented step by step: sliding palm rest for "massive hands or tiny
  paws"; five internal weights, removable individually via a rear screw; a hex tool in the base
  for swapping or removing the pinky rest (two or three are supplied) and tilting the thumb rest
  ~15° either way.
- [2012-02-01] Button tour: a left-side "sniper" button that drops DPI on hold "for those headshot
  or **video editing** or anything else precise" — the editing use-case named alongside the gaming
  one; forward/back buttons; a side-scroll wheel; a three-profile switcher (red/blue/pink,
  software-configurable); a DPI rocker with indicator lights; plus the standard clicks and wheel —
  nine total.
- [2012-02-01] **Recommendation with a real exclusion**: "this is a top-notch peripheral, I highly
  recommend it — **unless you're a lefty**." The one genuine limitation is stated as the
  qualifier on the recommendation.
