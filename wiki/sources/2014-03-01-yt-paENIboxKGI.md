# The State of 4K: Early 2014! (@mkbhd, 2014-03-01)

## Summary
A comprehensive survey of the 4K ecosystem — his **4K-adoption thesis fully developed**.
The core argument: **the whole pipeline is hard** (shoot → process → output → display →
playback), so most "4K TVs" have never displayed 4K anything. Introduces the **"retina-
display effect"** (once you see 4K, everything else looks fuzzy) and frames the state of 4K
in one word: **momentum**. (The video was itself uploaded in 4K.)

## Key claims
- [2014-03-01] **The whole-pipeline-is-hard thesis**: "why isn't everything already 4K? It's
  difficult — to shoot, process, output, display, and play back. The cameras that shoot 1080p fine
  don't have the sensors/processing for 4K [the GoPro Hero 3+ shoots 4K but only at 15 fps; the Note
  3 only at a low bit rate]. High-quality, high-frame-rate, high-bit-rate 4K needs a very expensive,
  powerful camera." The content-scarcity thesis, itemized across the whole chain.
- [2014-03-01] **"A 4K TV that has never displayed 4K anything ever"**: "this is a real 4K panel,
  fully able to display 4K — but it never has, because what's hooked up [PS4, Xbox One, Apple TV, a
  Chromecast] all output only 1080p. So it's still acting exactly like a 1080p TV — really no reason
  to go buy a 4K TV." The ecosystem-isn't-ready thesis, concretely.
- [2014-03-01] **The "retina-display effect"**: "most people are fine with 1080p — but once you see
  4K played back natively without any of the usual problems, it's amazing, and then you can't really
  go back. I call it the retina-display effect — once you see such high resolution, everything else
  starts to look fuzzy." His high-resolution-is-a-one-way-door principle.
- [2014-03-01] **The high-DPI-scaling problem (his Mac Pro + 4K monitor)**: "on the ASUS 4K monitor
  hooked to my Mac Pro via Thunderbolt, the desktop looks super tiny — macOS has very limited high-
  DPI scaling. I hope when Apple ships their own 4K display, they update macOS with more high-DPI
  options." A lived production-setup datum + honest ecosystem gap.
- [2014-03-01] **"State of 4K in one word: momentum"**: "a while ago that word was *potential*; now
  it has a lot of momentum. This is the year I think we'll see the biggest shift from 1080p-as-
  standard to 4K-as-standard — a lot more 4K TVs in stores, a lot more 4K talk in ads." His
  adoption-curve read (updating his 2013 4K-Explained prediction).
- [2014-03-01] **Uploaded in 4K — an encoding milestone**: "below this video you'll see the 2160p
  option for the 3840×2160 version." He publishes the video itself in 4K (a production/encoding
  milestone).
