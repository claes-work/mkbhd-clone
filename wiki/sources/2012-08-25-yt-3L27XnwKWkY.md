# Top 3 Gadgets of Summer 2012! (@mkbhd, 2012-08-25)

## Summary
A seasonal top-three, made as a video response to another creator (Mr TBOX 123): #3 the Retina
MacBook Pro, #2 the Galaxy S3, #1 the Nexus 7. Mostly a recap of positions already stated, but it
consolidates three durable threads in one place — the pixel/display obsession tied to the channel
name, updates-as-a-value-axis, and the form-factor-creates-a-market thesis — and adds a couple of
dated personal markers (back to school, new apartment, custom black S3 back).

## Key claims
- [2012-08-25] **#3 Retina MacBook Pro — the pixel obsession, name-linked again**: "I'm MKBHD, so if you
  didn't already know the HD stands for high definition, which basically means I like a lot of pixels."
  He calls the 15" Retina "the most impressive technologically… that came out of Apple's entire event."
  Notes he **doesn't own one** ("I have a desktop and a tablet and a phone, so it doesn't really fit in")
  and states the roadmap wish: retina moving "phones → tablets → laptops → desktops → some crazy 8K TVs
  later."
- [2012-08-25] **#2 Galaxy S3 — chosen over the One X for the multi-carrier accessory story**: "this
  takes the cake over the HTC One X for me because of the simultaneous multiple-carrier launch — an
  identical-on-the-outside version for Verizon, AT&T, Sprint and T-Mobile all at the same time, which
  means an accessory works no matter your carrier." He's running it as his daily driver on **CM10 nightly
  (Android 4.1)** with a **custom black back**.
- [2012-08-25] **#1 Nexus 7 — updates as the deciding value axis**: it beats the newer Transformer
  Infinity "because it is a Nexus device — this will get updates straight from Google as soon as they're
  available… when the next version of Android comes out, I can guarantee the Nexus 7 will get that update
  immediately. The support is beyond awesome." Update longevity weighed as the top buying criterion, and
  the form-factor-creates-a-market thesis restated ("it's a new form factor, it's a new product market
  because it's the first 7-inch true Android tablet").
- [2012-08-25] **A quality-control caveat, honestly flagged**: the Nexus 7 "had some display issues, so
  you might get a faulty one — maybe a one in 10,000 chance — but if you do, just return it and get a new
  one." Names a defect risk and the remedy rather than omitting it.
- [2012-08-25] **Explicit about what he left off and why**: "some things I left out like the Nintendo 3DS
  XL, or even some software like iOS 6 and Android 4.1 — they can't all be on the list." No silent
  omission.
- [2012-08-25] **Dated personal markers**: "it's the end of the summer, which means if you're a student
  like me, back-to-school time… this is the last video you'll see in this sort of place — the very next
  video will be in my new apartment." Corroborates the school/apartment timeline from
  [[2012-08-10-yt-_hhEz4Uo79k]].
- [2012-08-25] **Format note**: made as a **video response** to another creator's list — a dated instance
  of the response-video convention and peer engagement.
