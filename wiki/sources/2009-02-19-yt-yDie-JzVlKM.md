# HD Laptop Buying Guide (@mkbhd, 2009-02-19)

## Summary
★ **L3-candidate.** A substantial origin-era **buying guide** (Marques, age 15) that is
an early ancestor of the MKBHD review format: a component-by-component walkthrough of
what to weigh when buying a laptop, structured as processor → RAM → hard drive → graphics
→ screen → battery, using his own **HP Pavilion DV7 (17-inch)** as the reference machine.
It shows him already reasoning in **trade-offs** (size vs. speed, DDR2 vs. DDR3, 32-bit
vs. 64-bit, integrated vs. discrete graphics) and repeatedly promising deeper single-topic
follow-up videos — the systematic, explainer-first instinct that later defines his channel.
His own rig: Intel **Core 2 Duo @ 2.83 GHz**, **8 GB DDR3** (self-installed, no factory
8 GB option at purchase), **250 GB 7200 RPM** drive plus a 500 GB Western Digital external,
**512 MB Nvidia GeForce 9700M** (stated "I'll have to double check"), **1680×1050** 17" screen.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-19] Frames laptop choice as six weighted components: **processor, RAM, hard
  drive, graphics, screen, battery** — an early structured-review scaffold.
- [2009-02-19] Recommends **Intel Core 2 Duo** (vs. AMD Turion 64) and says Core 2 Duo
  lasts longer on battery than an AMD Turion.
- [2009-02-19] RAM: **at least 2 GB for Windows Vista** even in basic mode; prefer **DDR3
  over DDR2** if affordable. Notes **Windows Aero uses ~200 MB of RAM** (caption says "2
  gigs" for savings — likely garble; the Aero figure he states is ~200 MB).
- [2009-02-19] 32-bit Vista only recognized ~**3.2 GB** of his 4 GB; upgrading to 8 GB
  required **64-bit** Windows Vista.
- [2009-02-19] Hard drive = **size vs. speed** trade-off; with external storage he chose
  **speed** — a **250 GB 7200 RPM** over 5400/4200 RPM, calling the performance increase
  "worth it."
- [2009-02-19] Integrated Intel graphics are "going to be terrible"; gamers/desktop-
  replacements want a **512 MB discrete** card (his: Nvidia GeForce 9700M).
- [2009-02-19] Recommends **customizing/configuring** a laptop and shopping deals at
  online retailers (Newegg is garbled as "newag.com"/"scystar.com").

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "You're shopping for a laptop. What are you doing? What do you want? What do you want in your laptop?"

> "I didn't need size, so I just went with speed ... trust me, the performance increase was worth it."

> "The built-in integrated graphics by Intel or whatever ... they're going to be terrible. I'll tell you that off the bat."
