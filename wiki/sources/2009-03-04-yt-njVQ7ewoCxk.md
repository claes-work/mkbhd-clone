# 2009 Mac Desktop Refresh: Mac Mini (@mkbhd, 2009-03-04)

## Summary
Video **one of a planned playlist** covering Apple's early-2009 desktop refresh (announced
2009-03-03). Marques (age 15) walks the apple.com Mac-mini page, reading off the marketing
("faster and greener, still mini") and translating it: the new **Nvidia GeForce 9400M**
graphics (Apple's claimed up-to-5× / "seven times" better than the previous baseline),
up to **4 GB RAM**, five USB ports, FireWire 800, Mini DisplayPort, gigabit Ethernet.
He prices the line — **$600 baseline (2.0 GHz Core 2 Duo, 120 GB, 1 GB RAM)** and an
**$800 model (320 GB, 2 GB RAM)** with configurable upgrades — and positions the Mac mini
as a **budget/baseline desktop for word processing, not video editing**. An early example
of the spec-walkthrough-plus-buying-verdict format that later matures into his review style.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-04] New Mac mini uses the **Nvidia GeForce 9400M**; Apple claims up to ~5×
  (chart shows "seven times") the graphics of the previous baseline mini.
- [2009-03-04] Pricing: **$600** baseline (2.0 GHz, 120 GB, 1 GB RAM) and **$800** (320 GB,
  2 GB RAM); customizable to 2.26 GHz (+$150) and 4 GB RAM.
- [2009-03-04] Verdict: the baseline mini is fine for **basic OS X / word-processing use,
  not video editing** — an early "who is this for" buying framing.
- [2009-03-04] Announces a **multi-video series** on the whole refresh (Mac mini, iMac,
  Mac Pro) — an early structured content plan.
