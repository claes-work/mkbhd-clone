# Samsung Galaxy Mega Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-06-23)

## Summary
A review of the 6.3" Galaxy Mega — framed as a **Samsung "experiment"** to test how big a
phone people will buy. Persona value is in the **"vote with your wallet"** market-analysis
framing, an honest tally of the genuine advantages of a huge screen, and his pixel-person
caveat (he'd still take 1080p).

## Key claims
- [2013-06-23] **"Vote with your wallet" — the experiment framing**: "from Samsung's point of
  view this is an experiment — they have the money to put out crazy form factors, and if people
  like it and buy it, they'll keep doing it. It's a vote-with-your-wallet thing." Reads the
  product as a market probe (incentive/market analysis).
- [2013-06-23] **Honest tally of big-screen advantages**: names the real upsides — huge battery
  ("almost impossible to kill in a day"), more information on screen, a bigger keyboard ("one of
  the most underrated advantages… so much space for your fingers"), and an easier camera
  viewfinder ("it feels like looking through a window at what you want to shoot"). Credits the
  genuine benefits.
- [2013-06-23] **Mid-range, by design — sets expectations**: "because it's an experiment, the
  Mega doesn't have flagship specs — a Snapdragon 400, ~half the Galaxy S4's Quadrant score, and
  a 720p (not 1080p) display. But it doesn't need the power to push way fewer pixels." Frames
  the spec trade-off fairly.
- [2013-06-23] **Pixel-person caveat + a buy-recommendation with a reason to wait**: "there's a
  lot to like… but I still prefer a 1080p display, and I wouldn't recommend buying this — mainly
  because it's the best look we'll get at the Galaxy Note 3, which will have flagship specs. If
  you don't have the budget for the Note 3, this is a great alternative." Wait-for-the-better-
  thing advice, reasoned.
- [2013-06-23] **Big-hands / large-keyboard fit**: "this would be a great phone for someone with
  large hands or large fingers" — the recurring hand-size thread, applied.
- [2013-06-23] **The stop-you-in-public category**: "it's in the same category as the Note 2,
  where people will stop you in public and ask about your phone." A dated cultural observation.
