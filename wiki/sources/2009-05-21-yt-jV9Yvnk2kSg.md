# Unboxed: Fire-i Digital Camera by Unibrain (@mkbhd, 2009-05-21)

## Summary
An unboxing/first-impressions video of the Unibrain **Fire-i** FireWire machine-vision
camera (with driver disc, FireWire cable and mini tripod). Its durable value is not the
product but the **production experiment stated on camera**: he has moved the camera to a
new "down the line" angle for smaller boxes and is testing whether his new microphone's
audio syncs against the camera footage — and he asks the audience to tell him whether the
new setup works. ★ L3-candidate for the `production-filmmaking` origin thread: the first
explicit, dated instance of him iterating his unboxing camera framing in public.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-21] **New camera framing, announced as an experiment**: "I'm trying a new little
  style format here — I moved the camera a little bit for a smaller box"; the goal is "a
  Down the Line perspective, not quite like right over the product," so viewers "get a
  pretty good idea of what I see when I'm unboxing."
- [2009-05-21] **Audience is asked to grade the setup**: "just tell me if you guys like the
  new setup" — the request loop applied to his own production choices, not just topics.
- [2009-05-21] **Dual-source audio sync is still unsolved**: "I'm trying to sync this audio
  with my new microphone so we'll see whether or not it actually syncs well enough," and
  mid-video he apologizes — "sorry about the mics." Honest on-camera acknowledgement of a
  technical failure rather than a re-shoot.
- [2009-05-21] **Packaging/shipping commentary as a fixed segment**: FedEx Ground, ~2-day
  from California, heavily taped and bubble-wrapped; he infers the packing density from the
  product's value ("it's a pretty expensive product and they don't want this to get
  broken"). The struggle to cut the tape is left in the video.
- [2009-05-21] **What's in the box**: the Fire-i camera itself, a FireWire cable, the
  driver/software disc, and a mini tripod that clips into grooves on the camera back.
- [2009-05-21] **Product read**: "a lot smaller than I expected it to be"; FireWire port
  plus a second port for **daisy-chaining multiple cameras** for a multi-camera setup;
  drivers enable webcam use on **Windows and Linux**; VGA recording.
- [2009-05-21] **Selling point he identifies for the audience**: it's FireWire, so it
  "doesn't take another USB port" — a practical framing aimed at people with crowded USB.
- [2009-05-21] **Deferred-review workflow restated, with a possible split**: full specs and
  a software walkthrough come "in a review video... one to two weeks," possibly "more than
  one review video," and he floats uploading **test footage** so viewers can judge quality
  themselves.
