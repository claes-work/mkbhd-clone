# Google Nexus 5X Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-11-06)

## Summary
A "short and sweet" review of the Nexus 5X — "the same software as the 6P, different hardware." A
successor to "the beloved Nexus 5," LG-built, keeping its **"Stormtrooper panda" look** (a callback to his
own aesthetic tic). He likes the flat-sided, soft-touch, lightweight, grippable design (some say it "feels
cheap" without metal — "I agree a bit"), notes the **LCD-not-AMOLED** display (less saturated) and a
smaller battery (average life), and praises **Doze** as Marshmallow's best feature.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-11-06] **Same software as the 6P.** "In name and in person, the Nexus 5X is a successor to the
  beloved Nexus 5 (LG again), keeping a lot of the **Stormtrooper panda** looks and bringing them into 2015
  specs. This is what a lot of people were looking for in a new Nexus, and I like it a lot."
- [2015-11-06] **Grippable, lightweight — but "feels cheap" to some.** Flat sides, soft-touch finish, easy
  to grip (the rear fingerprint is easier to reach than on the bigger 6P). "Some have said it feels cheap
  without any metal, and I do tend to agree a bit — but I've had no problems with the build; it's put
  together very well." A slightly bigger camera hump makes it rock when table-typing.
- [2015-11-06] **LCD-not-AMOLED display.** A 5" 1080p (423 ppi, "still plenty sharp"), but an **LCD** panel
  now — "colors are less saturated and pop less; most people won't notice until side-by-side with a much
  better screen." The one visible LCD tell: ambient display lights the whole panel at night even where it
  should be black.
- [2015-11-06] **Average battery + Doze love.** A 2700 mAh cell — "decent for what's inside," gets him
  through a day "but I pretty much never made it past a full day." **Marshmallow's best feature is Doze** —
  "I've noticed this with pretty much every Marshmallow phone I've tested" (standby battery).
