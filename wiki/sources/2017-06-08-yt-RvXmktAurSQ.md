# iMac Pro: Is It A Trap? (@mkbhd, 2017-06-08)

## Summary
A deep-dive on why the just-announced iMac Pro might be "a bit of a trap" — a clean buying-logic essay. The
core: it's a stunning, non-upgradeable workstation shipping *six months* before Apple's promised modular Mac
Pro, aimed at people who (by their own past choice) will probably want the modular Mac Pro instead.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-06-08] **The trap: buy a non-upgradeable beast right before the modular Mac Pro.** "The iMac Pro is a
  monster — up to 18-core Xeons, Vega GPUs, 128 GB ECC RAM, 4 TB SSD at ~3 GB/s, 10 GbE, a new thermal
  architecture (sounds a lot like the trash-can Mac Pro). But it isn't out till December, and you **can't
  upgrade the RAM, CPU, drives, or GPU** — and the new **modular Mac Pro is right around the corner** next
  year, way more upgradeable/expandable. So by the time you get an iMac Pro, its replacement is nearly here."
  The timing-and-upgradeability case.
- [2017-06-08] **The who-actually-buys-this logic.** "Apple's clearly said most Mac-desktop buyers buy iMacs
  — makes sense (the Mac Mini is weak, the Mac Pro is niche). So Apple built a more powerful desktop for the
  people pushing iMacs to their limits. But those aren't usually the actual *professionals* — the people who
  chose the Mac Pro over the iMac chose it for a reason, and those same people will most likely choose the
  new modular Mac Pro over the iMac Pro again." A sharp segment-the-buyer read.
- [2017-06-08] **The regular 2017 iMac is already more than most need.** "This highest-end regular 2017 iMac
  is already more power than most enthusiasts or casual users ever really need; the iMac Pro leapfrogs all of
  it — even the current trash-can Mac Pro." Grades against actual need, not spec envy.
- [2017-06-08] **The honest self-aware pull (bias flagged).** "You like the iMac Pro, don't you Marques?" —
  he admits the temptation openly, consistent with the WWDC recap's "maybe I'm not learning my lesson from
  the trash-can Mac Pro, but I really want one." Separates the object of desire from the smart purchase.
- [2017-06-08] **Praises the pro I/O and the space-gray touch.** "It keeps the SD card slot (great), 4× USB-C
  Thunderbolt 3, headphone jack, 10 GbE — a robust suite for a pro; plus exclusive space-gray accessories."
  Fair about what's genuinely appealing.
