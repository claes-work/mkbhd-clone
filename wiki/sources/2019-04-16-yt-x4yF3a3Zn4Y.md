# Samsung Galaxy Fold Unboxing: Magnets! (@mkbhd, 2019-04-16)

## Summary
A short retail-unboxing / second-impressions video for the **Samsung Galaxy Fold**, the first
foldable phone going on sale to the masses. Marques frames it as a **luxury device** — priced at
**$1,980**, far above any other high-end Samsung flagship — noting that while most people won't buy
it, some will, and this is what those buyers get in the box. He walks the "box within a box"
presentation (Samsung "trying really hard to make you feel like your money was well spent"): a
two-piece Kevlar case and SIM tool, a pair of **Galaxy Buds** (the ~$139 AirPods-equivalent wireless
earbuds, a notable inclusion given the Fold has no headphone jack), a fast charger, USB-C-to-USB-A
dongle and cable, and extra ear tips. The phone itself ships **folded flat** in a thin white box. He
notes the setup is identical to the Galaxy S10 and that he prefers the larger inner screen for typing.
The signature bit is a demo of how strong the Fold's magnets are, using "magnet paper" as his "new
favorite scientific observation tool" and showing the field pick up washers, nails, keychains, a small
wrench, an Apple Pencil, and a large Allen key. He signs off noting this is distracting him from the
Huawei P30 Pro he's reviewing, and teases **Retro Tech episode one on the original Game Boy**. This
unboxing predates (by two days) his "Broken Galaxy Folds: Explained!" video about the review-unit
screen failures.

## Key claims
- [2019-04-16] The Galaxy Fold is the **first foldable phone really going on sale to the masses**,
  priced at **$1,980** — far above any other high-end Samsung flagship — so it's "clearly positioned
  as a luxury device." Most people won't buy it, but some will.
- [2019-04-16] In-box contents: a two-piece **Kevlar case** + SIM ejector tool; a pair of **Galaxy
  Buds** (~$139 wireless earbuds, the AirPods equivalent in the Samsung/Android world) — "a pretty
  big inclusion"; a fast charger; a USB-C-to-full-size-USB dongle and a USB-C-to-USB-A cable; extra
  rubber tips for the Buds.
- [2019-04-16] Because the Fold **has no headphone jack**, bundling the Galaxy Buds is "one step
  closer to committing to wireless" — there are no other dongles, just wireless headphones.
- [2019-04-16] The phone **ships folded flat** in a thin box; he's unsure whether it's to avoid a
  factory crease or just for drama — "or both or neither, I don't know."
- [2019-04-16] Setup is "pretty much identical to the Galaxy S10 or any other Samsung phone" (Google
  + Samsung account sign-in); he prefers doing it on the **bigger inner screen** because the front
  keyboard is small. Default is a black butterfly "smart stock" wallpaper.
- [2019-04-16] Any Galaxy Fold case must be foldable or two-piece; the included **two-piece Kevlar**
  design (flexible, no moving parts, optional adhesive) is "the smart way to do it." It installs and
  removes fine and, like any case, adds protection while getting "a little uglier."
- [2019-04-16] Magnet demo: there's a magnet on each side of the Fold about two-thirds of the way
  down, strong enough to have a field outside the phone that can pick up washers, nails, whole bags
  of nails, keychains, a small wrench, an Apple Pencil (metal inside for iPad magnets), and a large
  Allen key — "this magnet is for real."
- [2019-04-16] Personal workflow note: the Fold is "really distracting me from the P30 Pro" (Huawei
  P30 Pro) he's been using, with that review "coming up as well."
- [2019-04-16] Teases upcoming content: **Retro Tech episode one on the original Game Boy** — "it's a
  good time to be a video nerd either way."
