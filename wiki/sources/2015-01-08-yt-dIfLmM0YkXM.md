# Quantum Dot TVs: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-01-08)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"** demystifier of quantum-dot TVs (Samsung's "SUHD") from CES 2015. The consumer-education
verdict: it's **just improved LED/LCD backlighting** — real and legit (brighter, more efficient, wider
gamut), but "not something super insane like the name implies," and **still no OLED**, which he calls "the
future of television." A clean instance of demystifying a marketing buzz-term (noting the tech has existed
since the 1990s and is only "now getting the fancy branding").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-01-08] **What it actually is:** improved backlighting. Normal LED/LCD shines white light that
  must be filtered to RGB (losing light/vividness); quantum-dot backlighting shines purer R/G/B without as
  much filtering → **brighter and more efficient, wider color gamut**. "Technically the claims are
  correct."
- [2015-01-08] **But you may not see the difference** without a side-by-side vs a normal LCD, and it's
  **still no OLED.** "You can definitely tell when something is an OLED display" — self-lit pixels, no
  backlight, inky blacks, "beautiful, and it's the future of television."
- [2015-01-08] **Demystify the branding.** "The idea has been around since the 1990s… quantum-dot
  backlit displays have been on the market for two years — they're just now getting the fancy branding
  and SUHD sticker from Samsung. Here at CES 2015 is where it gets branded, a sexy buzz term alongside 4K,
  curved, 3D, flexible." A dated example of naming marketing-driven category hype.
- [2015-01-08] **Prediction:** quantum-dot displays will reach phones eventually (more efficient → better
  battery; brighter → better outdoor visibility, "where we use our phones anyway") once it shrinks enough.
