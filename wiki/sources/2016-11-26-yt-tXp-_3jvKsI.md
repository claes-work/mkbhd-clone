# 2016 MKBHD Studio Tour in 360°! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-26)

## Summary
A 360° tour of the now-furnished 2016 studio — a major production/biography node showing exactly how he
works. The gear chest, the "dream desk," the "Mega Desk" production/editing bay, the acoustic treatment,
the entertainment/VR-testing lounge, and a poker table he keeps as **a reminder of companies that take
design risks.** A window into his craft and taste circa late 2016.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-26] **Why a studio, restated.** "The idea of a studio that's not just my bedroom: it's a lot
  bigger, more space to set things up, and it lets me host and bring things in — that makes the videos more
  interesting." (Extends the studio-era arc from [[2016-04-08-yt-K_34vZ9haCk]].)
- [2016-11-26] **The gear chest = organization as production value.** An IKEA-built cabinet holding all his
  camera gear ("I know exactly what's in every drawer — sound here, batteries there") plus the review
  devices, "to keep things off surfaces so it looks pretty for video all the time." Reveals his
  organization-serves-the-shot ethic — and a candid "what do you do with devices after reviewing them? I
  don't know either."
- [2016-11-26] **The poker table = a philosophy prop.** "It's a reminder for me of the companies taking
  risks and doing things differently — it's rare to see a company take a real gamble and go all in on
  something crazy, like these bezel-less phones; they look crazy but I love it, I love the risk they're
  taking." Physically embodies the pro-bold-design value ([[2016-10-26-yt-m7plA1ALkQw]]).
- [2016-11-26] **The production bay ("Mega Desk") — the craft core.** A custom oversized NextDesk Air Pro
  (sit-stand, standing/sitting presets) with dual ASUS 4K displays, a 12 TB RAID for finished files, ColorWare
  black keyboard, Herman Miller Embody chair, ColorWare Sennheiser HD800s + Yamaha HS8 monitors, and the 2013
  trash-can Mac Pro: "this is where the production of most of the videos you watch is happening. I'm doing
  sound editing here just as much as video editing." Notes he uses the **Kessler Pocket Jib Pro for the
  overhead unboxing shots**. Foam everywhere — "a lot of foam, it helps."
- [2016-11-26] **This 360° video is shot on a 16-GoPro Google Jump rig.** "16 GoPros on a platter, all
  recording a stereoscopic 3D image, with a Zoom spatial mic — that's what you look like when I'm talking to
  you." His format-experimentation instinct (VR/360 in 2016).
- [2016-11-26] **The 1M-subscriber plaque + gratitude.** Shows the YouTube 1M-subscriber plaque: "super
  important to me — I just wanted to say thanks in person." A creator-business/biography artifact. (Notes a
  4K OLED TV in the lounge for streaming/VR testing — "almost never any actual TV signal," the cord-cutter.)
