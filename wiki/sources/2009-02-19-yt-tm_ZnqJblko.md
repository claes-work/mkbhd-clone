# HD Battery Saver #5 — Hibernate (@mkbhd, 2009-02-19)

## Summary
Episode **5** of Marques's origin-era **Battery Saver** tutorial series (age 15). He
shows how to hibernate a Windows Vista laptop — the quick way via Start → shutdown arrow
→ **Hibernate**, and how to remap the physical power button's behavior via **Control
Panel → Hardware and Sound → Power Options → change what the power buttons do** (with
separate settings for on-battery vs. plugged-in). He frames hibernate as superior to
sleep for battery: it fully powers down, stops the hard drive spinning, and restores the
session, "saving a lot more battery life than just ... putting into sleep." Another entry
in his systematic, series-based approach to a single practical topic (battery life) this
early.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-19] **Hibernate** saves more battery than sleep because it completely shuts
  down and stops the hard drive, then restores the session on resume.
- [2009-02-19] Vista quick path: Start → shutdown arrow → **Hibernate**; to change what
  the physical power button does, use **Power Options → "change what the power buttons
  do,"** with distinct on-battery and plugged-in settings.
- [2009-02-19] Part of a numbered **Battery Saver series** (this is #5) — evidence of a
  deliberate multi-part content structure in the origin period.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
