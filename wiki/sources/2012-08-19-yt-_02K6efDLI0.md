# Blackberries: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-08-19)

## Summary
A short, **data-driven market analysis** of BlackBerry's decline: he starts from a 1% US usage-share
figure and works through the arithmetic — 1% of ~115 million US smartphone owners ≈ 1.15 million
people — then decomposes *why* a million people still use them (mostly enterprise-issued). Comedic in
tone, but a clean early example of him reasoning about a company's position from numbers and market
structure rather than vibes.

## Key claims
- [2012-08-19] **Starts from data and does the arithmetic on camera**: a poll shows BlackBerry at ~1% US
  smartphone web-usage share; with ~300 million Americans and ~115 million smartphone owners in 2012,
  "1% of these 115 million… means there are 1.15 million people using BlackBerries today in the US.
  That's a lot of people."
- [2012-08-19] **Quantifies the decline**: "as recently as October 2011, more than 5% of people using
  smartphones were BlackBerry — so that's more than a 75% drop-off in just less than a year."
- [2012-08-19] **Explains the residual via market structure — the analytical move**: "you have to consider
  that a lot of the people using BlackBerries got them because of **enterprise** — that's a huge market
  BlackBerry and RIM used to hold. Most people getting jobs that give out phones… get a BlackBerry for
  free to use for business." He estimates ~75% are enterprise-issued, leaving ~250,000 personal-choice
  or locked-in users. Structural decomposition of a market rather than a headline number.
- [2012-08-19] **Isolates the genuinely puzzling segment**: "if you're one of the maybe 100 or 150,000
  people that chooses to use a BlackBerry over anything else for the price, that's confusing to me,"
  noting the BlackBerry Bold 9930 "costs the same as the Samsung Galaxy Nexus" on Verizon. A
  price-to-value point behind the confusion.
- [2012-08-19] **Comedic RIM jab, but not cruel**: a "graphic that summarizes the state of Research In
  Motion" (played for laughs), then "I kid, I kid, RIM — you're doing all right, okay? You got a million
  people, that's solid." Ribs the company while acknowledging a real user base.
- [2012-08-19] **A physical-keyboard-and-pocketability bit**: he shows a BlackBerry ("very pocketable…
  the little keyboard, you can hear the clicks, they're very tactile") — an early, dated appreciation of
  tactile physical keys that resonates with the persona's later "physical controls matter" value.
- [2012-08-19] **Return-from-break marker**: "I'm back to doing regular videos, in case you haven't
  noticed — I am back from my **one-week break**" — the ultimate-frisbee Nationals trip from
  [[2012-08-10-yt-_hhEz4Uo79k]].
