# Android 5.0 Lollipop Feature Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-28)

## Summary
A feature rundown of near-final **Android 5.0 Lollipop** on his Nexus 5. The through-line is that
**Material Design's animations are a usability feature, not decoration** — "everything you touch brings
something onto the screen or moves it off, so you know exactly where things are coming from and where
they're going… I would actually consider that a feature." Walks the new notifications/quick-settings,
priority/do-not-disturb modes, guest profiles, Smart Lock, battery saver, and screen pinning. Mostly
informational; useful as a dated statement of animation-as-usability and a couple of personal habits.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-28] **Material Design = animations = usability.** "You can sum it up best in one word:
  animations… it's legitimately helping create an intuitive UI." Still gripes that "the back button in
  Android is still just as inconsistent as ever."
- [2014-10-28] **Impressed by the frame rate on old hardware** — running smoothly on a one-year-old Nexus
  5, "which is beginning to look like a steal at under 400 bucks" if the Nexus 6 is too big.
- [2014-10-28] **Personal habits surfaced:** he leaves **priority mode** on when he sleeps "so the only
  thing that can wake me up is my alarm"; uses **Smart Lock** with his Moto 360 constantly.
- [2014-10-28] **Favorite new features:** screen pinning (hand someone your phone locked to one app —
  with a kid-and-a-game joke), guest profiles, a battery-saver mode (bright-orange bar so you can't
  forget), and small delights (the clock app's time-of-day background, the slow fade-to-grayscale on
  screen-off).
- [2014-10-28] **The one reservation:** the lighter, bright-white aesthetic "is something we need to get
  used to" versus the all-dark Holo of Android 4.0.
