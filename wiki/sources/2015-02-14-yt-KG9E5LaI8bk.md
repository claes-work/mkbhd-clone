# Dell Venue 8 7000 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-02-14)

## Summary
A review of Dell's first premium Android tablet — the world's thinnest (6 mm) — that lets Marques restate
two positions at once: **anti-thinness** ("not exactly sure being so thin is a good thing… I wouldn't mind
it thicker to house extra battery") and **champion-the-risk-taker** ("it's a risk… I'm glad Dell took the
risk, and taking risks is good"). The 8.4" OLED display is best-in-class; the bezelless "Aquos-Crystal-
grown-into-a-tablet" design creates one-sided-speaker/grip ergonomics he's honest about — and he's
source-critical enough to flag a shipping-damage incident. Verdict: not his first-choice midsize tablet.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-02-14] **Anti-thinness, tablet edition.** "It's the world's thinnest tablet, 6 mm — although I'm
  not exactly sure that's a good thing; being thicker wouldn't make it harder to hold, and it's actually
  kind of hard to pick up off a flat table. I wouldn't mind it thicker to house some extra battery."
- [2015-02-14] **Bezelless design = phone-first ergonomics.** Reminds him of the Sharp Aquos Crystal
  "blown up" — three thin bezels + a chin housing a front speaker/camera. Great in portrait/one-hand, but
  awkward in landscape (one-sided speaker, no palm rejection). Well-built metal chassis, clicky buttons;
  the glass back is a fingerprint magnet.
- [2015-02-14] **Best-in-class OLED display.** 8.4" 2560×1600 OLED — "seriously one of the best-looking
  tablet displays I've ever seen," great for media/gaming. But he sees **dropped frames** in everyday use
  pushing all those pixels (on KitKat; hoping Lollipop helps). Near-stock Android with minor Intel/Dell
  additions he "used once or twice to demo, then never again."
- [2015-02-14] **Intel RealSense triple-camera** (depth capture, like the M8 Duo) — "I didn't really use
  it… I don't use my tablet to take pictures for exotic reasons." (Know-what-you-actually-use honesty.)
- [2015-02-14] **Source-critical about his unit.** His first review unit arrived with a bumped Best Buy
  box that cracked the tablet's corner (it sits too close to the packaging edge) — "this isn't going to
  happen to everyone, but I have to mention it."
- [2015-02-14] **Champion-the-risk-taker + honest verdict.** "I'm glad Dell took the risk, and taking
  risks is good" — but "I don't think I'd recommend it as my first choice; the Nexus 9, iPad mini Retina,
  and NVIDIA Shield are all better buys for the size/price. I'd probably recommend it more if it were $100
  less." He gives it the edge on **display** among midsize tablets.
