# Virtual Reality: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-03-28)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"** primer on VR (with a hands-on of the HTC Vive prototype at MWC 2015). Two durable
ideas: the **content-ecosystem decides adoption** — "VR content will take off when there's a lot of VR
stuff to look at, like 3D TV would have only made it if people everywhere made 3D videos… that didn't
happen, and there's a risk VR might not either" — but VR has **something 3D TV didn't**: a genuine "wow,
this is impressive" first-demo reaction. Also a clean **VR-vs-AR distinction** (HoloLens/Glass = AR).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-03-28] **What VR is:** replace your reality — "just like headphones take over your sense of sound,
  this headset totally takes over your vision." Origin: Air Force flight sim (practice without crashing
  real planes).
- [2015-03-28] **Two types.** Type 1 = phone-as-screen/engine (Google Cardboard, Gear VR — your phone's
  gyro/accelerometer drives it); Type 2 = self-contained (Oculus Rift, HTC Vive — built-in screen +
  external tracking sensors). Type 2 tracks your head in 3D space (peek around a corner) but needs room to
  set up **and cables to the computer** — "you're not exactly untethered when really using it."
- [2015-03-28] **Making a good VR headset is hard** — you have to balance ppi (magnified right against
  your eye), field of view, and pixels-per-degree.
- [2015-03-28] **VR ≠ AR (the clarification).** VR *replaces* your reality (fully immersed); AR
  *augments* it (overlays on the real world). "Microsoft HoloLens is actually augmented reality, not VR,"
  as is Google Glass.
- [2015-03-28] **The immersion factor is what makes VR special** — "you're essentially blindfolded and
  put into an entire new world with whatever the artist makes."
- [2015-03-28] **Content-decides-adoption (the durable thesis).** "VR content will take off when there's
  a lot of VR stuff to look at — like 3D TV would only have made it if people everywhere made 3D videos.
  That didn't happen, and there's a risk VR might not either. But there's something special here 3D TV
  didn't have: the first time someone sees a really good VR demo, 'wow, this is impressive.'" Notes the
  category went "from almost nothing to five or six major players so fast" (Rift, Gear VR, Cardboard,
  Vive, Project Morpheus).
