# Top 5 Ubuntu Phone Features: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-01-12)

## Summary
An "Explained!"-format primer on the just-unveiled **Ubuntu Phone** OS (seen at CES
2013), covering what it is, how it might ship, and his top-5 features. Persona value is
in a clear early statement of what excites him about mobile: **convergence** — the
"Android in your pocket, full desktop Ubuntu when you dock it" idea — plus his taste for
fast, controllable settings, and a dated prediction. Otherwise a dated feature tour of
an OS that never shipped at scale.

## Key claims
- [2013-01-12] **Convergence is the feature he most wants — "option number two"**: of
  the three shipping models he lays out, "something I'd like to see is higher-end phones
  with quad-core processors and extra RAM… Android in your pocket, but then when you hook
  them up to a dock and a display you'll be able to run a full version of desktop Ubuntu
  Linux… an Android phone in your pocket, but also a desktop PC when you hook it up." The
  **one-device-that-becomes-your-desktop** idea, named as his favorite — a durable
  fascination datum (recurs across his career as the phone-as-computer thread).
- [2013-01-12] **Quick Settings toggles as his #1 feature — a controllability value**:
  "the settings toggles in Ubuntu Phone give you really fast but also quick and intuitive
  access… right above Android I would put Ubuntu Phone's Quick Settings toggles." He ranks
  OSes partly on how fast and granular their settings control is — consistent with his
  control-over-software instinct.
- [2013-01-12] **Even-handed on a thing he's excited about**: repeatedly flags the
  rough edges — "the hands-on demos haven't been super smooth, we've seen a lot of frame
  stuttering… this is very Alpha, way pre-release," and the multitasking "is slightly
  incomplete… no real way to close open things." Enthusiasm plus honest caveats.
- [2013-01-12] **The learning-curve / no-home-button observation**: "there's no central
  home location or home button… it's going to have a little bit of a learning curve." A
  UX note on the all-gesture interface (later mainstreamed by iPhone X-era gestures — a
  dated-but-prescient read).
- [2013-01-12] **A dated, self-labeled prediction**: "an unofficial prediction — I kind
  of have a feeling we're going to be seeing this on tablets soon this year, just because
  of how thumb-friendly the OS is." He flags it as unofficial (his habit of marking a
  guess as a guess). Ubuntu tablet convergence did surface but never went mainstream.
- [2013-01-12] **Discloses his own OS breadth**: "I'm not one to really play around with
  a ton of different operating systems — I've stuck to maybe three total on my phones:
  Windows Phone, Android, and iOS… but I think I would give this a try." A dated
  self-characterization.
- [2013-01-12] **Request-driven**: "a lot of people have been asking me to do a video on
  Ubuntu Phone." The audience-programmed content pattern again.
