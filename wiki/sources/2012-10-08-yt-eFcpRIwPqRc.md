# Top 3 YouTube App Missing Features! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-08)

## Summary
A critique of the YouTube mobile app — missing annotations, a crippled comment system, and diluted
search/sort — argued from his position as a heavy YouTube creator and user. It closes on a principle
that reaches well beyond the app: **something can look beautiful and feel smooth and still not be
good if it lacks functionality.** A clean, dated statement of his function-over-form / feature-parity
value.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-08] **The closing principle — function over polish**: "some things can be really beautiful,
  really good-looking or really smooth and can seem to just work — but if they're lacking functionality
  then you have to wonder if it's really all that great. And that applies to more than just the YouTube
  mobile app." A generalized value stated plainly.
- [2012-10-08] **Feature parity with the desktop as the quality bar** — the recurring standard: he
  explicitly contrasts YouTube's app with Google+'s ("it's so good because almost all the features
  available on the desktop are available on the app — YouTube not so much"). The same criterion he
  praised in [[2012-09-29-yt-C-b9hmerTac]], now used to criticize.
- [2012-10-08] **The three gaps, from a creator's-eye view**: (1) **annotations** — no mobile support,
  which breaks end-screens, subscribe links, cause links, and on-screen mistake corrections ("a lot of
  times annotations are really important to the context of a video"); (2) **the comment system** — no
  inbox/notifications, no reply button, no top-rated view, no rating ("the whole comment system is very
  very limited"); (3) **the little things** — uneditable playlists, un-searchable subscriptions/uploads,
  no browse-by-most-viewed, a "diluted" search filter, and a broken back-button flow.
- [2012-10-08] **Reasons from scale**: "more than 200 million people use YouTube on mobile devices every
  single day" — sizing the stakes of the missing features before making the case.
- [2012-10-08] **Self-positioned as a power user AND creator**: "I upload videos here all the time, I
  leave thumbs up all the time, I interact in the comments all the time" — the critique comes from
  someone who lives in the product, which he states as his standing.
- [2012-10-08] **Wants the comments improved most**: "I really want to see the comment section beefed up
  and really awesome in the new YouTube app" — a dated statement of valuing the discussion layer.
