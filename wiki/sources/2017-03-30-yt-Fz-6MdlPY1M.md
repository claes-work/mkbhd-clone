# Samsung Galaxy S8 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2017-03-30)

## Summary
First impressions of the Galaxy S8 / S8+ — the phone that delivers the bezel-less "dream" to the mainstream
("this finally feels like a 2017-or-even-2020 futuristic design… without sacrificing any functionality").
Clone-relevant beats: the bezel-less dream realized without tradeoffs, Samsung finally going to software
buttons, the awkward fingerprint-reader-by-the-camera, and a skeptical first take on the redundant **Bixby**.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-03-30] **The bezel-less dream, realized without sacrifice.** "The Infinity Display — I'm calling it
  the almost-bezel-less look — fits a 5.8"/6.2" screen into the footprint of a much smaller phone. This
  finally feels like the seamless, futuristic design we dream of, but without sacrificing functionality: it
  still has USB-C, a headphone jack, ip68, wireless charging, and microSD." The 8/10 concept from his Future-
  Features video, shipped. The S7 Edge's sharp edges are now "much softer, more seamless."
- [2017-03-30] **Samsung finally goes to software buttons — with caveats.** "Finally Samsung switches from
  hardware buttons to on-screen ones — I love them. But Samsung's software buttons are ugly (maybe just me),
  and out of order (back should be on the left) — luckily you can reorder them." His standing Samsung-nav
  gripe, now in software.
- [2017-03-30] **The fingerprint-reader-by-the-camera is a misstep.** "The reader moves to the back, way up
  by the camera. I don't mind back readers (Pixel XL), but on these tall phones even my big hands have to
  stretch past the camera to reach it — many people will need two hands; in Samsung's own commercial the guy
  uses two hands." An ergonomics critique from real use.
- [2017-03-30] **Bixby: "SNL rebooted" vibes — redundant with Google Assistant.** "The biggest new software
  feature is Bixby, Samsung's assistant — with its own dedicated button. But this is still Android 7.0, so
  it *also* has Google Assistant on a long-press: two things doing the same thing next to each other. Weird.
  They couldn't even demo the image/context-recognition features that ship with it." Early skepticism (fully
  argued in [[2017-04-05-yt-xfG15i_uQvc]]).
- [2017-03-30] **"Samsung picked their battles."** "They clearly went all-out where they wanted — the design
  and display, and it shows — but the areas where they're *not* pushing are also obvious: the camera (same
  as the S7) and the battery (3,000 mAh on a 5.8" display, not sure it holds up)." A balanced read of where a
  maker invested vs. played safe.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "This finally feels like a 2017 or even 2020 futuristic smartphone design — in the seamless, beautiful way
> we dream of — but without sacrificing really any functionality or features."
> "It's still Android 7.0, which means it also has Google Assistant… I got this SNL-rebooted type of vibe
> with those two things doing the same thing existing next to each other."
> "It looks like Samsung really picked their battles with this one."
