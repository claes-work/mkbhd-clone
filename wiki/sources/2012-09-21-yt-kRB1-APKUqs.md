# The Setup Tour 2012 (@mkbhd, 2012-09-21)

## Summary
The full setup-tour video — a community project compiling dozens of viewer-submitted setups, capped by
**a complete, narrated tour of his own 2012 apartment workstation**. This is **the most detailed
documentation of his workspace and gear in the entire origin corpus**: the self-built desk, the
Mac Pro he moved up to, the Dell 3007WFP, the audio chain, and his daily-driver phone — plus several
dated biography markers (new apartment, still on a school ultimate-frisbee team, "I tend to be a neat
person").

## Key claims (his own setup)
- [2012-09-21] **The desktop — a 2012 6-core Mac Pro, part-DIY**: "I've moved past the Mac Mini. I'm
  aware there's a new Mac Pro coming out soon, but this will hold me over — this is the 2012 3.33 GHz
  6-core Mac Pro. The only things I bought from Apple were the Mac itself and the upgraded 5870 graphics
  card; the 512 GB SSD and 16 GB of RAM I bought third-party." The Mac Mini build ([[2012-03-02-yt-G_H6mRHS9pc]])
  is now superseded, and he still buys only what he must from Apple and self-upgrades the rest.
- [2012-09-21] **The monitor — Dell UltraSharp 3007WFP, 2560×1600**: "easily my favorite monitor I've
  ever used, I love the 1600 vertical pixels," running a single-monitor setup with a Logitech C910 webcam
  and openly weighing a second identical 30".
- [2012-09-21] **The self-built desk**: "this is the desk, it's an IKEA Alex, and I did build this
  myself — had to drill the holes for cable management below the desk." Neatness by design.
- [2012-09-21] **Daily-driver phone confirmed**: the phone on his desk is "the Samsung Galaxy S3 running
  CyanogenMod 10… getting a full day of battery," consistent with [[2012-08-30-yt-IT73da-C4sw]].
- [2012-09-21] **The audio chain, itemized**: iKey Audio M-505V2 studio monitors ("amazing sounding, I'm
  almost hesitant to try anything else because I'm afraid I won't like the way they sound compared to
  these") on Editor's Keys foam isolation pads, angled toward his ears ("it's really important to have
  them facing you"); the Blue Spark mic he's "been using for more than the past year" for videos and
  Google+ hangouts, on an LED lamp he counts among his favorite pieces of furniture.
- [2012-09-21] **Note-taking laptop**: the Asus Zenbook Prime UX32VD — "this is my favorite… the only
  thing I've taken notes on so far, it's just that nice," carried to class. Plus a "fatty" school-provided
  laptop he doesn't like.
- [2012-09-21] **Router chosen on merit**: the Asus RT-N66U ("Dark Knight") — "I got it because it was
  rated basically the best router out there"; ISP is ~60 down / 20 up.
- [2012-09-21] **Tablets**: the Transformer Pad Infinity as his main media-consumption/reading tablet, and
  the Nexus 7 ("Breakfast of Champions") kept "just kind of for having a Nexus" and stock Android, used
  less often. Peripherals: Apple keyboard + trackpad ("only for Mission Control… three spaces open"),
  Cyborg R.A.T. 9 wireless mouse (candidly, "one day on battery — this is not a joke and not a defect").

## Key biography / persona markers
- [2012-09-21] **Ultimate frisbee corroborated in his own space**: on the far-left of the desk is "the
  disc — this is the design for the school that I play for right now, that I'm on their ultimate frisbee
  team." A second on-camera confirmation after the [[2012-08-10-yt-_hhEz4Uo79k]] reveal.
- [2012-09-21] **"I tend to be a neat person"** — stated as the reason the setup looks tidy; the
  minimal/neat taste documented since 2011, applied to his real workspace.
- [2012-09-21] **New apartment confirmed**: the router was chosen "when I was looking up a router to get
  for the apartment"; the move ([[2012-08-10-yt-_hhEz4Uo79k]]) is now his filming location.
- [2012-09-21] **Setup changes constantly** — a stated working habit: "my setup rarely stays the same,"
  and he'll "probably be doing this sort of project every year."
