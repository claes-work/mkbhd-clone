# The Problem with Phone Cases! (Why I Don't Use Cases) (@mkbhd, 2014-06-02)

## Summary
A **landmark "why I don't use cases" doctrine video** — one of the earliest full statements of a
position he becomes known for. The argument is built on his **specs-converge → design-is-how-you-pick**
thesis: because every top-of-lineup phone now performs great, you choose by software features and by
**design / feel in the hand**, so wrapping that design in a bulky case defeats the reason you bought
it. His answer is **skins, not cases** (dbrand, DecalGirl, SlickWraps) — they preserve thinness and
feel, protect against scratches, and peel off clean for resale. Cross-links directly to the MKBHD ×
dbrand partnership.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-02] **Specs matter less and less; design matters more and more.** "Basically every phone at
  the top of every manufacturer's lineup performs great, so the way to pick which phone you buy is
  number one the software features and number two the design and the hardware and the feel in the hand."
- [2014-06-02] **A case defeats the point of caring about design.** If you cover a carefully-designed
  phone "with a piece of rubber or plastic," you throw away the very thing you chose it for.
- [2014-06-02] **His solution is a skin.** A carbon-fiber-textured skin on his HTC One M8 solves the
  drop-risk of its smooth rounded metal (adds grip) **without** changing the shape or thinness — "the
  only thing that's really changed is the way it feels in the hand." He also runs a DecalGirl decal on
  the 13" Retina MacBook Pro (scratch protection in the bag; peels off to like-new metal for resale).
- [2014-06-02] **Explicit shout-out to the skin makers** — "I'm going to encourage the skin makers out
  there, the **dbrands**, the DecalGirls, the SlickWraps, all those guys, to keep making more awesome
  skins." (Ties to the dbrand partnership established the prior batch.)
- [2014-06-02] **If he genuinely needs protection:** "I'll put it back in my pocket, or get an Otterbox
  Defender" — i.e., real protection is a deliberate, occasional choice, not a default.
