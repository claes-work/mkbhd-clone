# iPhone 6s #Chipgate: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-10-15)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"** on #Chipgate — and, like BendGate and the sapphire video, a **teach-the-mechanism +
refuse-the-hysteria** piece. The iPhone 6s's A9 chip is built by **two manufacturers (TSMC and Samsung —
"one of Apple's biggest enemies")** at slightly different sizes, and a Geekbench battery test showed the
TSMC chip lasting 20–30% longer. His verdict: **the Geekbench test is a worst-case scenario (100% CPU max
load until dead) that doesn't translate to real use** — real-world tests show ~2–3% at worst, "you
probably won't notice." Ends with a jab at his own pet peeve (the Magic Mouse's charging port).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-10-15] **Two chipmakers, by necessity.** Apple designs the A9 and contracts it out; this year
  **both TSMC and Samsung** build it (likely a volume issue — "neither could crank out 13 million alone").
  The TSMC chip is slightly bigger (104.5 mm², 16 nm) vs Samsung's (96 mm², 14 nm); you can only tell which
  you have after buying (via the Lirum app). "On an SoC, think of chip size like sensor size on a camera —
  bigger usually enables better performance and efficiency."
- [2015-10-15] **The benchmark is worst-case and doesn't translate (anti-benchmark).** "The Geekbench test
  is essentially the worst-case scenario for a CPU — maxing it out at 100% until the battery's dead. Real-
  world behavior constantly cycles between performance levels… at full max load the bigger TSMC chip is an
  advantage, but at every other level the difference is almost nothing." Cites Austin (1% diff over an
  hour-long video), John/TLD (5% over a 30-min timelapse), and Ars Technica (minimal across the board).
- [2015-10-15] **Not really a scandal — and Apple does this all the time.** "Apple's used multiple
  manufacturers for RAM and flash before; the unusual part is the chips being different sizes. Apple's
  answer, loosely paraphrased: 'close enough.'" He had the Samsung chip in his review unit "and was happy
  with the battery — it wasn't a big deal to me."
- [2015-10-15] **The Magic Mouse joke.** "If we need another gate — which, do we really? — it should be
  about something actually terrible, like the way this mouse charges" (the Magic Mouse's bottom charging
  port — his standing pet peeve).
