# Top 3 Battery Myths! (@mkbhd, 2011-12-28)

## Summary
A research-driven explainer debunking three widespread lithium-ion battery beliefs, made
for people who got "battery-powered gifts this holiday season." He states the work behind
it up front — "I was actually doing some research on lithium-ion batteries… to sum up my
hours of research" — then dispatches the myths one at a time with mechanism, not assertion.
Notable for a moment of plain intellectual honesty (he admits he himself believed one of
these until recently) and for a production note: **the entire video was filmed on the Galaxy
Nexus**, two weeks after buying it.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-28] **Myth 1 — initial "priming" charge is unnecessary**: lithium-ion batteries
  "don't require any sort of initial charge, or priming is what they call it. So if your new
  iPod comes with 40% battery out the box, you can just start using it at 40%."
- [2011-12-28] **Myth 2 — overcharging by leaving it plugged in is not a thing**, with the
  mechanism explained: built-in circuits cut power at full charge; the charge curve rises fast
  to ~80% then fills the last 20% gradually, and "if you look at it really really closely it
  actually bounces off 100 a couple times — 100 to like 97 to 100 to 97. That's the battery
  rejecting the charge." Only overheating is a genuine risk, "and that pretty much never
  happens unless you're using it in a really hot environment."
- [2011-12-28] **Myth 3 — calibration is a software issue, not a battery one**: full
  discharge-to-zero cycles don't help the cell; "it's the phone software itself that sometimes
  has trouble reading the battery percentage left, so you may want to do this every year or
  so." Lithium-ion cells "have the same performance level from just about any percentage
  discharge."
- [2011-12-28] **Why the myths exist — the historical explanation**: "old battery technology
  was nickel based. These old nickel batteries needed to be calibrated and discharged from 100
  to get their peak performance." He doesn't just correct the belief, he accounts for where it
  came from.
- [2011-12-28] **Intellectual honesty, stated plainly**: on the overcharging myth — "this is
  one of the most common myths, and I only recently figured out that this is actually also
  completely false." He includes himself among the people who were wrong.
- [2011-12-28] **Production milestone**: "this entire video was filmed on a Samsung Galaxy
  Nexus" — a dated instance of shooting a full upload on a phone, two weeks after buying it
  and days after criticizing its stills sensor ([[2011-12-18-yt-jm3wDComxLQ]]).
