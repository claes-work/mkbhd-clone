# Google Home Review: Assistant in a Box! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-29)

## Summary
Review of the Google Home smart speaker ("Google's version of the Amazon Echo/Alexa, with Google smarts").
The clone-relevant beats: his **"I like when technology blends in and doesn't have to stand out to do its
job"** design value, his identification of the **one-account limitation** as the key thing holding smart
speakers back, and a measured "mild convenience" verdict from real ownership (he has two).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-29] **"I like when technology blends in and doesn't have to stand out."** "The hardware is simple-
  looking — an air-freshener-looking plastic computer. It can fit into most homes and not stand out, which is
  a good trait. I kind of like it when technology blends in and doesn't have to stand out to do its job." A
  durable industrial-design value (unobtrusive tech).
- [2016-11-29] **The one-account limitation = the real ceiling.** "The main thing holding it back: it's a
  one-person device, still like your phone but not like your home. It'd be great if it replied differently
  to different people — I ask how my calendar looks, my roommate asks in his voice and gets his — but the
  voice recognition isn't that good yet. For now it's one account, one person." Names the precise gap that,
  once solved, unlocks the category. (An accurate forward read — multi-user voice-match arrives later.)
- [2016-11-29] **Speaker quality "as good as it has to be."** "Better than your phone's speaker — obviously
  not replacing a home stereo, but that's about as good as it really has to be." Right-sizing expectations.
- [2016-11-29] **The honest "do you need one?" verdict.** "Does anyone really need one? Probably not — it's a
  really nice gift, usually discounted this time of year. It's a mild convenience, but once you get used to
  it, it's kind of nice to have. I actually have two — one upstairs, one downstairs — and I've gotten used to
  just asking how's the traffic to work or what my calendar looks like." Ownership-grounded, unhyped.
- [2016-11-29] **Extends the ambient-computing thread.** Frames Home as part of "Google's big AI push" (the
  Assistant in the Pixel, better Google Now cards). Continues his "Google Home = the future" read from I/O
  ([[2016-05-19-yt-mGojonPKBkY]]), now tempered by hands-on limits.
