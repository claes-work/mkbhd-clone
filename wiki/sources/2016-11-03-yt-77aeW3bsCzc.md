# Jaybird X3 Review: Best Bluetooth Earbuds 2016! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-03)

## Summary
A quick review of the Jaybird X3 — an incremental update to the X2 — that he ends up **preferring over the
pricier Freedom** he'd reviewed earlier. Clone-relevant threads: the matte-black preference, his
self-identification as an athlete, the app-tuned-firmware feature, and a recurring pro-consumer note about
old models dropping in price.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-03] **Incremental is fine — headphones aren't smartphones.** "The X3s are definitely an
  incremental improvement to the X2s, which makes sense — it's not like smartphones where there are new
  features to add every year." Applies his anti-annual-churn view to the category.
- [2016-11-03] **"I'll take matte black wherever I can get it."** On the soft-touch finish replacing the
  glossy original: "again, I'll take matte black wherever I can get it." The consistent matte-black-over-
  glossy hardware preference stated outright.
- [2016-11-03] **Self-ID as an athlete.** "As an athlete I'll take any enhancements to the build I can
  get — they're fully sweat/water resistant, take them out on the sand, to the track, wherever." A
  personal-identity tell (recurs across his fitness/ultimate-frisbee notes).
- [2016-11-03] **App-tuned firmware EQ again.** The MySound app writes a sound signature to the earbud
  firmware "to follow it around device to device" — same feature he praised on the Freedom
  ([[2016-05-30-yt-GiNSOLpC1-Y]]); "one of the best flat sound signatures of any wireless pair."
  Honest-about-his-limits aside: "these can't be audiophile-grade comments — I'm not an audiophile,
  I'm streaming Spotify over Bluetooth."
- [2016-11-03] **The price is the biggest upside + the old-model-drops-in-price note.** "The X3s launch
  at $129.99 — an improvement over headphones that launched at $180. And one of my favorite things when
  new headphones launch is the old ones drop in price — you can get the X2s for ~$80 now." Pro-consumer
  value framing. Notably: "I don't recommend the Freedoms as much as these — I prefer these."

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "It's not like smartphones where there's new features to add every year."
> "I'll take matte black wherever I can get it."
> "One of my favorite things about when they launch new headphones is the old ones go down in price."
