# Bose QC35: Best Noise Cancelling Headphones? (@mkbhd, 2016-06-30)

## Summary
Review of the Bose QuietComfort 35 — the wireless successor to the QC25, which had been "my go-to for
train rides and flights for the past couple years." Frames premium noise-cancellers on four axes (build,
comfort, sound, noise cancellation) and concludes they'll replace his QC25s. Clone-relevant: a personal-
daily-driver data point, the red-and-black "colorware" identity again, and his consistent honesty that
Bose sound is "good but nothing spectacular."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-06-30] **The four-axis rubric for premium NC headphones.** "In a pair of really premium noise-
  cancelling headphones you look for build quality, comfort, sound quality, and how good the noise
  cancellation actually is." A reusable evaluation frame.
- [2016-06-30] **Personal daily driver + the red-and-black identity.** "These have been my go-to for
  train rides and flights for the past couple years… they're red and black, so they've got some colorware
  going on." Reinforces his personal red/black hardware aesthetic (biography/appearance node).
- [2016-06-30] **Comfort is the standout; sound is honestly "nothing spectacular."** "Comfort is a huge
  plus — light, flexible, super-soft foam and leather." But: "sound quality is pretty much exactly the
  same as the QC25s — pretty good but nothing spectacular, won't blow your mind; on par with other
  $150–$300 headphones. You aren't buying these to monitor for music production." Refuses to oversell the
  audio of a famous brand.
- [2016-06-30] **Battery-life "don't-think-about-it" heuristic.** ~20-hour active battery: "if you can
  listen to a pair for the entire week and charge once a week with heavy listening, you pretty much don't
  have to think about it — that's a strong point." His recurring "good battery = one you forget about"
  framing.
- [2016-06-30] **Noise cancellation = Bose's core competency.** "Bose does this pretty much better than
  anyone else — it's what they're best at… that's why you buy noise-cancelling headphones in the first
  place." Tactile blind-press buttons on the ear cup are called out as important.
- [2016-06-30] **The buyer's breakdown.** "If you specifically want *wireless* noise-cancelling, pay the
  extra $50 for the 35s; if you don't care about losing the wire, the $300 QC25s are functionally the
  same. Either way, if you're shopping premium ~$300 NC headphones, these should be on your short list."
