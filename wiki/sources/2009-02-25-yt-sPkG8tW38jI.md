# HD Tutorial: Safari 4.0 (@mkbhd, 2009-02-25)

## Summary
An install-and-first-look tutorial (Marques, age 15) for the **Safari 4.0 beta** on Windows.
He upgrades from Safari 3.2.2, walking the Apple.com download, the installer (which preserves
favorites, autofill, and preferences), and swapping the icon into his RocketDock. He then
tours the new features and repeatedly frames Safari 4 as **strikingly similar to Google
Chrome** — tabs on top, a "top sites" visited-page wall, and a "+" new-tab button — plus the
new **Cover Flow** bookmark browsing. Early example of his instinct to review new software by
comparing it to a reference competitor (here, Chrome).

## Key claims
- [2009-02-25] Safari 4 beta looks and behaves "a lot more strikingly like Google Chrome" —
  tabs on top, top-sites wall, and a "+" new-tab button.
- [2009-02-25] The installer removes the old Safari but **keeps favorites, preferences, and
  autofill**; he confirms autofill/login persisted post-upgrade.
- [2009-02-25] New in Safari 4: **Cover Flow** browsing of bookmarks/history and a bug-report
  button. Prior version was Safari 3.2.2; download ~25-31 MB.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "it's going to look a lot more strikingly like Google Chrome in that the tabs are on the top here"

> "this is a lot reminiscent of Google Chrome. First of all, the fact that you can add a new tab just by hitting that plus button"
