# Nexus 4 and Nexus 10 First Look! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-29)

## Summary
A rundown of Google's newly-official Nexus lineup — Nexus 4, refreshed Nexus 7, and Nexus 10 — made
from spec sheets because **Google's event was cancelled by Hurricane Sandy**. Two durable items: he
announces he's **leaving Verizon** (over the Note 2 handling and the Nexus 4's lack of a Verizon LTE
version), and he raves about the Nexus 10's display and the Nexus 4's aggressively low unlocked price
using explicit competitor comparisons.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-29] **Leaving Verizon — stated intent, dated**: "there won't be a Verizon version this time on
  LTE, but that's okay because **I'm leaving Verizon anyway after all this crap with the Galaxy Note 2**."
  A dated carrier-frustration decision. (Note: he was still on Verizon in mid-Nov per
  [[2012-11-13-yt-RQy0NeVnxLo]], so record as *stated intent*, not completed at this date.)
- [2012-10-29] **The Nexus 4 price framed by competitor anchors — value analysis**: "$300 for the
  unlocked version, which is an insanely competitive price… to give perspective, the Galaxy S3 started
  about $650 unlocked and the iPhone 5 unlocked is about $800. So 300 bucks unlocked, good stuff Google."
  The loss-leader/aggressive-Nexus-pricing story ([[2012-07-31-yt-YddnWW_JYiY]]) continuing.
- [2012-10-29] **The Nexus 10 display, over-the-top praise tied to pixel obsession**: 2560×1600, "the
  pixel density is over 300 — higher than the new iPad 3 and iPad 4, higher than any other tablet or any
  MacBook Pro Retina or any screen you use with two hands. Take my money, take all of it." Display-primacy
  + "I love pixels" at full volume; the Nexus 10 is his favorite of the three.
- [2012-10-29] **Nexus 4 read as a Galaxy Nexus successor**: clean no-branding front, curved look,
  Snapdragon S4 Pro, sealed 2100 mAh battery, 8 MP camera, 9.1 mm, wireless charging ("inspired by the
  Palm charging dock from a while back"), NFC — "kind of like a Galaxy Nexus 2." Confirms his
  Nexus-4-from-Optimus-G / clean-design reads.
- [2012-10-29] **Nexus 7 refresh noted**: 8 GB dropped; 16 GB now $199, new 32 GB $250, and a 32 GB
  HSPA+ 3G at $300 — the value framing continued.
- [2012-10-29] **Sandy context**: "obviously there was no event today because of the hurricane right
  outside" — the cancelled Google event, tying to [[2012-11-08-yt-Tqm8Izfn_oU]].
- [2012-10-29] **A channel milestone, loosely stated**: "thanks for sticking around for 500 videos on the
  MKBHD Channel" — a ~500-video milestone marker (approximate; see note).
