# Fastest Mac Mini in the World! [Part 4] FINALE! (@mkbhd, 2012-03-02)

## Summary
The finale of the four-part build: final specs, the desk setup around it, **crowdsourced benchmarks**
(he asked Twitter which tools to use), a full **cost breakdown**, and the conclusion — "this is the
new MKBHD desktop." The most consequential content is personal rather than technical: he states plainly
that he has **never been a full-time macOS person** and is now trying it as his daily driver while
waiting for a Mac Pro refresh, closing with reassurance to his PC audience. The clearest single
document of his platform transition.

## Key claims
- [2012-03-02] **The macOS transition, stated outright**: "I've never really been a full-time Mac OS X
  person, but I'm giving OS X a shot right now to see if I can get used to it as my daily driver, as I
  wait for Apple to refresh the Mac Pros hopefully as soon as possible with Ivy Bridge… it won't be so
  hard to get used to. Right now it's going pretty well." Then, to his existing audience: **"don't
  freak out PC people — Windows isn't going anywhere."**
- [2012-03-02] **Final build**: 2011 Mac Mini, 2.7 GHz Core i7, user-installed **16 GB DDR3** and a
  **240 GB OCZ Vertex 3 Max IOPS SSD**, OS X 10.7 Lion, AMD Radeon HD 6630M — "the same mobile
  motherboard found in the high-end MacBook Pros," which he sums up as "basically a glorified MacBook
  Pro."
- [2012-03-02] **Full cost breakdown, itemized**: Mac Mini $999 + SSD $480 + 16 GB RAM (Other World
  Computing) $250 + "surgery supplies" $30 = **$1,759** — framed against "barely more than the slowest
  27-inch iMac."
- [2012-03-02] **Benchmarks crowdsourced from the audience**: "I polled on Twitter asking you guys to
  reply @MKBHD what I should use to test this little machine." NovaBench: **834 points** against
  "the fastest stock Mac from Apple will average roughly 383," landing it just below a 2008 Mac Pro and
  a 2011 iMac. Cinebench: weaker on the CPU render test, strong on OpenGL.
- [2012-03-02] **Reports the weak result and answers it with lived evidence**: "it didn't perform great
  on the CPU test — but of course I am editing this video on the Mac Mini, so if you see this video on
  YouTube then I got through it." The benchmark is not hidden; it's contextualized by the actual task.
- [2012-03-02] **The strongest recommendation in the series is the SSD, not the machine**: "the real
  showstopper here, the real difference maker in the performance is the OCZ Vertex 3 SSD… **I cannot
  recommend switching to an SSD enough** if you're still on a traditional hard drive… it makes the
  biggest difference in terms of the user experience." Measured write ~250 MB/s, reads much faster.
- [2012-03-02] **Setup documented**: Apple wireless keyboard and trackpad, the Cyborg R.A.T. 7 mouse
  ([[2012-02-01-yt-wkmL7KyNn3o]]), a FiiO E9 amplifier, and a single Dell UltraSharp 24" — with
  instructions for driving a second and third display, though "we like simplicity so let's do some
  serious work with this one display."
- [2012-03-02] **First macOS opinions, dated**: Mission Control is "my favorite feature of OS X Lion so
  far… as a person who's used to multiple monitors it's a really great feature"; "definitely recommend
  picking up a trackpad… the Magic Mouse, they suck"; "I think Launchpad is just silly."
- [2012-03-02] Series close: annotations point back to the first three episodes; continuation of
  "more things like it" gated on the thumbs-up.
