# Canon 24mm f/1.4 L Review (@mkbhd, 2011-10-21)

## Summary
A review of the Canon EF 24mm f/1.4L II — a $1,600 lens he did not buy. The video opens
by resolving that immediately: the lens came **on loan from BorrowLenses.com**, a rental
service he explains by analogy ("just like a library book"), with the cost of a week's
rental stated as under $100. The review is framed throughout around a specific reader:
**a YouTuber deciding whether to rent it**, not a photographer deciding whether to own it —
and the verdict splits accordingly, praising the glass while calling the purchase price
unjustified.

## Key claims
- [2011-10-21] **Provenance disclosed in the first fifteen seconds**: "this lens retails
  for $1,600. I didn't pay $1,600 for this lens — and it's not for any other illegal
  reasons. I actually got this lens courtesy of BorrowLenses.com." Continues the
  self-purchased / seeded / **rented** distinction running through the 2011 reviews.
- [2011-10-21] **Sub-scores**: image quality **9/10** ("only because it does get a little
  bit soft at wide angles and on a full-frame body this will be a tiny bit distorted"),
  build quality **10/10**, **overall 9.5/10**.
- [2011-10-21] **Price verdict separated from quality verdict**: "the price you pay, if you
  pay full price, isn't really worth it — but borrowing this lens, you're definitely going
  to be impressed." A 9.5/10 product he explicitly does not tell you to buy; renting is
  the recommendation.
- [2011-10-21] **Use-case reasoning for a creator**: two stated reasons it suits YouTube
  work — (1) wide angle means "I can sit here in my room and almost be able to touch the
  camera" and still frame the shot, good for indoor/small spaces; (2) f/1.4 lets in enough
  light to "film like I am right now with no studio lights" while keeping shallow depth of
  field.
- [2011-10-21] **Dated gear facts**: he is filming with the **Canon T2i**; the 24L II
  outweighs the T2i body itself and "dwarfs" the 18–55mm plastic kit lens; on the T2i's
  **crop sensor** he reports effectively no distortion, while flagging that a full-frame
  body would show some.
- [2011-10-21] **Stated aesthetic preference**: "I absolutely love shooting at f/1.4" — tied
  back to his earlier Sigma 30mm f/1.4 review as the same reason. A dated, explicit
  statement of the shallow-depth-of-field look as a personal signature.
- [2011-10-21] Technical observations: L-series lenses ship with a hood; ultrasonic
  autofocus is "silent and tack sharp at just about any exposure"; the manual focus ring is
  praised specifically for video pulls "when doing sort of cinematic panning shots."
