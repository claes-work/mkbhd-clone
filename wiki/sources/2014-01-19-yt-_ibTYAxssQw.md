# Samsung Galaxy Note 3: Revisited! (@mkbhd, 2014-01-19)

## Summary
A four-months-later revisit of the Note 3. Reinforcing, but a strong instance of his
**control-over-software** value (he tries to turn it into a "Google edition" without
rooting), his **anti-TouchWiz** stance, and a couple of durable personal habits: **no cases
or screen protectors** on any of his phones, and a clean restatement of the **TouchWiz-slows-
both-operation-and-updates** fragmentation mechanism.

## Key claims
- [2014-01-19] **Make-it-stock without rooting — control-over-software**: "since getting it I've
  done everything short of rooting to make it a Google-edition Note 3 — grabbed the KitKat Google
  Experience Launcher, the AOSP Google-themed apps and keyboard, and mirrored it to my Nexus 5 as
  much as possible… but no matter how hard you try, you can't shake TouchWiz." His control-over-
  software instinct, applied.
- [2014-01-19] **Anti-TouchWiz, stated plainly**: "at this point I don't really like TouchWiz that
  much — I much prefer stock Android from something like the Nexus 5." His stock preference, hardened.
- [2014-01-19] **No cases / no screen protectors — a personal habit**: "I take really good care of
  all my electronics, and I don't use any cases or screen protectors on any of my phones, which
  some people think is crazy — but the Note 3 has been perfectly fine." A dated personal-habit datum.
- [2014-01-19] **TouchWiz slows both operation AND updates — the fragmentation mechanism**: "it's
  such a heavy skin the phone needs all 3 GB of RAM, and it impacts update speed — that's why
  Motorola phones like the Moto X/G get super-fast KitKat updates [they run near-stock], and that
  will never happen with the Note 3 because of TouchWiz." His update-fragmentation thread, applied.
- [2014-01-19] **The two-of-a-lot-of-apps workaround**: "not everything has a perfect stock
  equivalent — the Play Store stock camera apps can't record 4K or do Photo Sphere like the
  TouchWiz camera, so I keep two of a lot of apps, one AOSP and one TouchWiz." His pragmatic
  control-over-software compromise.
- [2014-01-19] **Stylus-skepticism + wants a CyanogenMod build**: "I'm not a person who uses the
  stylus that much, so I'm really into this phone still because when it gets a stable CyanogenMod
  AOSP build, it'll get a lot more use from me." Control-over-software as the unlock.
- [2014-01-19] **The Note line is no longer an experiment**: "the Galaxy Note line started as an
  experiment, and this third version sold way more than 10 million — so they're serious about it
  now, it's no longer an experiment." His market-read update (cf. the Galaxy Mega "vote with your
  wallet").
