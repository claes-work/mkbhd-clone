# What I REALLY Think of the iPhone! (@mkbhd, 2021-03-05)

## Summary
A rare no-time-limit "everything" video on the iPhone 12 Pro after five months of use — and, more importantly for the clone, an explicit, extended articulation of **how Marques thinks about reviewing itself**. The opening act is a first-person manifesto on the review as a craft: Apple's messaging machine, the embargo, and the core thesis that "every single one of these reviews is fundamentally a balancing act" — a review is "the art of compressing and distilling as much useful information as possible into one piece," constrained by limited time, YouTube retention metrics, and production load ("being a video reviewer on YouTube today is like nine different jobs in one"). He explains why his own reviews landed in the 15–20 minute range and frames this video as the exception where nothing hits the cutting-room floor. The body is a deep, opinionated long-term assessment of the iPhone 12 Pro across design, screen, cameras, MagSafe, battery, software/ecosystem, 5G, and a meta-riff on the annual hunt for an iPhone "-gate." Solo throughout. Closes by soliciting whether the audience wants more long-form like this, and (self-aware) that the shirt changes because a long video takes more than one day to shoot.

## Key claims
- [2021-03-05] Review philosophy: a review is a balancing act / an art of compression — "there is no possible way to meaningfully test every single new thing," so reviewers cut different things and make different pieces; his own reviews grew to ~15–20 min "to give you as much information as possible in as short a time as possible." YouTube "audience metrics and retention" are an explicit input. (Marques) ★
- [2021-03-05] "Being a video reviewer on YouTube today is like nine different jobs in one" (testing, analysis, writing, shooting, editing, color, music, creative choices). (Marques) ★
- [2021-03-05] Design: he loves the flat iPhone 12 industrial design (echoing iPhone 4/5); ideal iPhone 12 = the Pro's matte back + the regular 12's matte camera bump and aluminum rails. The glossy stainless rails are constant fingerprint magnets; the glossy raised camera rings collect dust (cosmetic, not performance). (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] Haptics are the underrated best part — the large linear Taptic Engine gives the best haptics of any phone; Apple is so confident in haptics they replaced physical buttons (e.g. the MacBook trackpad) with glass + a taptic engine. (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] Screen: incredible on paper (flat 6.1", 460ppi OLED, 1200 nits, Dolby Vision, world-class color) but "how much do people really care?" His iPhone-10R lesson: a low-res 720p LCD on a $749 phone drew outrage yet "it turned out it was fine ... apple knew that people didn't really care." Two live gripes: the notch is still "the biggest notch in any new phone" (he "hate[s] it every time" but got used to it) and it's still 60Hz — "the most expensive new phone that doesn't have a high refresh rate display." The 12 Pro is "the smoothest 60hz phone" due to the 120Hz touch sample rate. (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] On 120Hz: "once you go 120 hertz it is really hard to go back to 60 — except for the iPhone." iPad Pro has had 120Hz since 2017; the cited reason the iPhone lacks it is OLED supply/quality-control at iPhone scale. (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] Ceramic Shield: not magic — "glass is glass"; still scratches at Mohs level 6 (grooves at 7) and still shatters. Any real-world drop improvement is likely from the flat stainless rails taking impact, not the coating. (Marques; nods to Zack/JerryRigEverything's "glass is glass")
- [2021-03-05] Cameras — the core framework: the iPhone wins on **point-and-shoot** by pushing furthest into Steve Jobs's 2007 "ease of use vs. smart" corner. "Where the iPhone separates itself is delivering consistent great results with minimal effort." But there's no true Pro mode in the stock app, so for manual control it can get beaten; in a hypothetical all-manual blind test "you might be surprised which one comes out on top." (Marques) ★
- [2021-03-05] Video is where the iPhone is the best overall system — not just the footage but **app support**: many camera apps historically screenshotted the Android viewfinder instead of the camera feed, so the iPhone's development advantage ("one version of the app for many millions of people") is "the main reason why I carry an iPhone." (Marques) ★
- [2021-03-05] Dolby Vision HDR "looks super good when it works" but the pipeline breaks on export (HLG compatibility) — SDR tone-mapped versions "don't look great"; he's "still waiting for the best most reliable HDR pipeline." (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] MagSafe: "hardly any more safe than a normal wire" (bump it and the phone comes with it); the charging puck is still the best MagSafe accessory five months on (the Belkin car mount slowly sinks/falls); capped at 15W (12W on the mini) — "Apple has never led the industry in charging speed ... never led ... in battery size," which is better for long-term battery health. His 12 Pro still reads 100% battery health at 5 months; 6–7 hrs screen-on-time typical. (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] Software: iOS's value is consistency ("older iPhones will have literally the exact same software experience"). iOS 14 widgets are "better and worse at the same time" — more polished but fewer and less customizable than Android's decade-old widgets (a Twitter poll found ~75% of users use home-screen widgets). Persistent gripe: too many settings buried in the Settings app; the power button triggering Siri. Walled garden = "making it harder to leave ... and that's on purpose." (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] 5G "definitely didn't suddenly get real" from one phone — it's an infrastructure buildout; real-world 5G "not actually that much dramatically better than 4G," with mmWave the rare fast exception that dies behind any obstacle. Apple minimized 5G's battery hit via smart-data switching. (Marques)
- [2021-03-05] The "-gate" riff: a true gate is something big enough to force an Apple response. This year's candidate = **no charger in the box** — he sees through the environmental framing (the included USB-C-to-Lightning cable means most iPhone owners must still buy a brick), and warns of the ripple effect on the whole industry's fast-charging innovation. Xiaomi Mi 11 and Galaxy S21 followed Apple in dropping the in-box charger. (Marques) ★
- [2021-03-05] Meta-thesis on Apple's influence: "there are companies that make entire phones based on rumors of what apple might do in a future iphone just so they can beat apple to the punch." (Marques) ★
