# LG Ultrafine 5K Display Unboxing + Setup! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-23)

## Summary
Unboxing/setup of the LG Ultrafine 5K (the Apple-Store display Apple co-developed after exiting the display
business). Framed as "part one" of an experiment: **replacing his 2013 trash-can Mac Pro with a maxed 15"
MacBook Pro + two 5K displays** — a significant production-workflow inflection, with a pointed read that
"the Mac Pro is dead."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-23] **"Apple is sadly pretty much not in the display business anymore."** "As much as we loved
  the Cinema Display and wanted a standalone 5K Apple display for Mac, it's probably never going to happen —
  the upside is Apple worked with LG to make this one as good as possible for the MacBook Pro." A wistful
  industry read on Apple abandoning first-party displays.
- [2016-11-23] **The editing-rig experiment: laptop replaces the Mac Pro.** "I'm dabbling with replacing my
  trash-can Mac Pro with one maxed 15" MacBook Pro and two of these 5K displays. This is exactly what Apple
  touted at the MacBook Pro event, which makes me feel like the Mac Pro is dead — so as a Final Cut editor I
  kind of have to get on board with this laptop setup." A production-workflow pivot he narrates in real time.
- [2016-11-23] **The video-editor's use case for 5K.** "The main advantage is a native 4K editing window
  plus extra pixels around it to actually use Final Cut Pro." Concrete pro-editor justification for the
  resolution.
- [2016-11-23] **"Editing with a laptop is crazy… but at least it's possible."** "It doesn't make a ton of
  sense to make a laptop your full-time machine, but it's kind of nice — I can pack it up and go. I'm going
  to need a lot of adapters." The dongle reality applied to his own workflow; he'll edit *this* video on it
  as a test ("if you never see this video, it didn't work or I gave up").
- [2016-11-23] **Notes the one-cable charging + port-expansion.** The single Thunderbolt cable does video +
  charging; the display's back adds three more Thunderbolt ports ("seven total instead of four"). Values the
  reduced-cable-clutter (his cable-management thread).
