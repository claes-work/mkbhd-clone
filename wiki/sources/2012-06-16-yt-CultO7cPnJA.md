# Top 5 Tech Myths! (@mkbhd, 2012-06-16)

## Summary
A myth-busting listicle debunking five widespread tech beliefs. Its value to the clone is that
**myth #1 is the optics-not-megapixels belief restated as a general principle** — the position the
wiki already treats as canonical, here articulated cleanly and early — and that the video is
threaded with his habit of correcting a misconception by explaining the mechanism, including
correcting *himself* on the last one.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-16] **Myth 1 — "more megapixels the better," debunked with the optics principle**: "a good
  camera is made by overall good optics — a good lens, fast lens, good glass, an overall wide
  aperture… and the sensor that's behind them all." A restatement of "it's not the megapixels, it's
  the optics" ([[2011-12-18-yt-jm3wDComxLQ]]), now generalized. He also folds in the pixel-count
  correction from [[2012-02-26-yt-pzGsUp3yM8w]]: an 8 MP image has twice the *total* pixels of a 4 MP
  image, not twice the width and height — "you don't have to worry about having a drastically larger
  image when going from 18 to 22 megapixels."
- [2012-06-16] **Myth 2 — "[tweak] will drastically speed up your computer"**: "there is no magical
  thing you can do to drastically speed up your computer or phone or tablet." Android task-killers are
  the worked example: "a bit of a placebo effect, because Android itself is actually very good at
  managing memory." The real answer is **maintenance** — "actually knowing what you're installing and
  how it affects what you're doing."
- [2012-06-16] **Myth 3 — "Macs can't get viruses"**: "yes, Macs can get viruses." He grants the
  kernel of truth and explains it by incentives: more Windows malware exists "from the perspective of a
  malicious hacker — who are you going to write it for? Windows, because Windows is on 92% of the active
  computers out there." The incentive-structure lens applied to malware economics.
- [2012-06-16] **Myth 4 — "unplugging a USB drive without ejecting will destroy it"**: the real risk is
  pulling it *mid-transfer* ("you pretty much just lost that picture"); idle, "you can just unplug it,
  it's very safe actually" — and you can disable the warning. Told with a story about a teacher panicking
  to safely-eject after a finished PowerPoint.
- [2012-06-16] **Myth 5 — "Internet Explorer sucks," and he corrects himself**: "I've been a bit of a
  culprit for this one. I do know that Internet Explorer is a much better browser than it used to be…
  while it isn't quite as good as Chrome or Firefox or Opera, that doesn't mean it sucks." Public
  self-correction of a bias he admits holding — then a joke: "looks like we have a new myth… 'Beats by
  Dre suck.' That's not a myth."
- [2012-06-16] **Dated output note**: "I do post videos a lot more often now that it is summertime" —
  confirming the college-term cadence effect (heavier uploads over the summer break).
