# The Voice Assistant Battle! (2017) (@mkbhd, 2017-08-04)

## Summary
A landmark four-way voice-assistant comparison (Google Assistant / Siri / Alexa on the HTC U11 / Bixby) —
an expansion of his 2016 two-way Siri-vs-Google test. Clone-relevant for the **rigorous controlled-comparison
methodology** (even playing field, three test tiers) and the verdict that **everyone is still chasing
Google** — plus a memorable rapping-assistants coda that maps each to its personality.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-04] **The controlled-comparison methodology (the key craft).** "To minimize variables: same latest
  software, the exact same questions and phrasing, the exact same Wi-Fi — a direct comparison." Three tiers:
  **(1) baseline factual** (weather, math, store hours — "should be easy picking"), **(2) conversation**
  (chained context: "who's the 44th president? how tall is he?" — "Google should be good at this, they said
  they would be"), **(3) deep actions** (multi-step in-app commands). A reusable fair-test rubric.
- [2017-08-04] **The verdict: everyone's still trying to catch Google.** "It paints the picture that they're
  all still kind of trying to catch Google — some closer than others." Google leads on conversation/context;
  Siri "got the job done"; Bixby "trying way too hard, in a very robotic non-human way, but does some things
  the others don't"; **Alexa (on a phone) is "literally the worst."** A clear ranking from the data.
- [2017-08-04] **Voice assistants have specialties, not universal competence.** "Voice assistants might not
  be great at everything, but they definitely have certain specialties — strengths and weaknesses." Frames
  the category by task-fit rather than one winner.
- [2017-08-04] **The rapping coda maps each to its personality (voice/humor).** Ends by having each assistant
  rap — and reads the results as personality: "Google very vanilla (more a poem than a rap, but did what I
  asked); Siri a little cringey, trying to be funny, got it done; Bixby trying too hard with bells and
  whistles; Alexa, as she said, literally the baddest — actually the worst." A characteristic playful-but-
  analytical close.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "We gave them all the latest software updates, the exact same questions and phrasing, and the exact same
> Wi-Fi connection — so to minimize variables, this is a direct comparison."
> "They're all still kind of trying to catch Google — some are closer than others."
