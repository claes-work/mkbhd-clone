# Samsung Galaxy Alpha Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-02)

## Summary
A short review of the Samsung Galaxy Alpha, whose verdict stays "**meh**" — his honest first reaction,
kept even after using it. The Alpha is a "**half-premium**" phone: a genuinely nice chamfered metal band
(iPhone-5S-like) wrapped around the same old plasticky Samsung back and a merely-okay 720p display, so it
"seems confused" about whether it wants to be fully premium. A dated instance of naming a device's core
identity problem, and of praise/criticism both following the hardware.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-02] **His reaction was and stays "meh."** When the ad first ran he even commented "meh" — "it's
  so similar to a lot of other Samsung phones," a mini-Note-4 / improved-S5. Coming to the US next to the
  S5 and Note 4 forced a closer look.
- [2014-10-02] **The metal band is the whole point, and it works.** A chamfered metal frame all around
  (like the iPhone 5S) "makes a really big difference in the way it feels" — "one of my favorite phones
  just to hold."
- [2014-10-02] **But it's only half-premium.** Flip it over and "suddenly it's the same old Samsung" —
  plasticky removable soft-touch back. "It kind of seems confused: does it really want to go all the way
  to a premium feel, or only part of the way?" Same story with the specs: an octa-core Korean model with
  LTE, but a downgraded **12 MP camera** (older sensor) and a **720p OLED** that "breaks down up close."
- [2014-10-02] **Grade-on-what-it-is:** not a full flagship (small 1860 mAh battery, Moto-X-like life),
  but smooth (high-end chip pushing only 720p), a nice 4.7" size with thin bezels. "It'll compete with
  top-of-the-line phones… almost a bridge between the high-end super-premium stuff and last year's Samsung
  feel."
- [2014-10-02] **The tell:** "this phone gets me more excited about the Galaxy Note 4 than it does about
  itself" — the Alpha's metal is a preview of the (better) Note 4.
