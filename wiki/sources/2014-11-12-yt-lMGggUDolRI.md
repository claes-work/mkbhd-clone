# The Dream Smartphone! (2014) (@mkbhd, 2014-11-12)

## Summary
The annual **"Dream Smartphone"** — a Frankenstein phone assembled from the best parts of 2014, which
doubles as the single clearest statement of **what Marques actually values in a phone**. The picks map
directly onto his standing doctrines: front-facing speakers, **anti-thinness / a big battery**, a great
display without a huge body ("**screen size is not phone size**"), **pure stock Android**, **image
processing over raw sharpening**, and a **no-gimmicks-but-if-it's-there-it-must-work** ethic. Produced in
collaboration with The Verge's video team (their animations). A high-value persona/beliefs source.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-11-12] **Body:** the HTC One M8 (with its **BoomSound front-facing speakers** — "nothing beats
  BoomSound right now"), but squared off like the Note 4 to fix the M8's slipperiness, with a Nokia Lumia
  **soft-touch finish** ("easier to hold and built like a tank").
- [2014-11-12] **Display: the LG G3's 5.5" QHD, with thinner bezels.** "5.5" might seem big for some, but
  **screen size is not phone size**" — tight bezels + soft-touch make it feel great in the hand.
- [2014-11-12] **Anti-thinness / battery (his recurring stand).** Praises Motorola's battery approach
  (the Droid Turbo's 3900 mAh in a not-big phone): "phones have been getting so much thinner lately at the
  expense of battery size, and I'm not a big fan of that trend." Wants **≥3500 mAh** (≈6 hr screen-on).
- [2014-11-12] **Camera: image-processing over sharpening.** He'd take the iPhone 6 Plus camera for its
  quality + OIS, but it's 8 MP (no 4K), so he picks the **Note 4's 16 MP OIS sensor (shoots 4K)** — "but
  I'll still take the **image processing from the iPhone 6** just to take the edge off Samsung's
  super-sharpened, super-saturated look." (Any good Sony sensor would do.)
- [2014-11-12] **OS: pure stock Android 5.0 Lollipop**, "basically stock… they kept that pure smooth
  performance and feel."
- [2014-11-12] **The no-gimmicks ethic (canonical).** "I am not a huge bells-and-whistles type of person.
  I don't need any swiveling camera or heart-rate monitor or ring flash. **But if it's in the phone, it
  better work — otherwise it's just taking up space.**" 128 GB storage, no expansion needed. "A phone I'd
  be comfortable using daily for the next two years."
