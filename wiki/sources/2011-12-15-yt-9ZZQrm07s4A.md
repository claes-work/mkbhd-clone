# Verizon Galaxy Nexus Unboxing & First Boot! (@mkbhd, 2011-12-15)

## Summary
Launch-day unboxing of the phone he'd spent months waiting for — **bought himself, in
person, after standing in a line**. Box contents, first boot, boot animation, and the
initial setup pulling in his Google account live on camera. Short and openly excited
("I walked home with this phone with the biggest grin on my face"), and notable for a
recurring bit of the era plus several small dated personal facts.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-15] **Self-purchased at retail, launch day**: he walked home with it, "there was a
  line for this phone," and he was "tweeting furiously" from the OG Droid while waiting.
  Consistent with the day-one purchase intent stated six weeks earlier
  ([[2011-10-30-yt-VuIPe93ZmiM]]) — not a review unit.
- [2011-12-15] **Upgrade context stated**: coming from the 3.7" original Motorola Droid to a
  4.65" 720p display — "this is a huge upgrade for me."
- [2011-12-15] **Personal device-care choices, dated**: he applies screen protectors but says
  "I probably won't be using a case at all with this," delivered with the recurring line
  "I like my phone like I like my woman" (also used two days later in the display video).
- [2011-12-15] **Headphone habit (self-reported)**: "I usually don't use headphones with my
  phone, but when I do, I'm listening to music and I'm using the M50s" — the same
  Audio-Technica pair he reviewed in September and gave away in November.
- [2011-12-15] **Sync habit stated in passing**: "our USB cable for connecting to our computer
  and charging — probably only for charging, because I never connect phones to the computer."
- [2011-12-15] Specs recited at first boot: 1.2 GHz dual-core, same GPU as the Nexus S, 1 GB
  RAM, Android **4.0.1**, 4.65" 720p display, Verizon LTE; the device arrives pre-activated
  and immediately restores apps and email from his Google account.
- [2011-12-15] **Format honesty**: "I know unboxings aren't the most exciting thing in the
  world, but I will be getting a lot of hands-on time with this phone" — he undersells the
  format and redirects to the coverage to come, then asks the audience what to cover.
- [2011-12-15] Finals-week marker: on the manual, "some literature to read, as if I don't
  already — finals."
