# Adding 140 TERABYTES to the Studio with Linus! (@mkbhd, 2017-06-13)

## Summary
The full story of how Marques manages footage — from deleting-everything to now archiving all his raw 8K —
culminating in a 140 TB NAS (a Storinator) delivered and installed with Linus Sebastian (Linus Tech Tips).
A rich production/biography node about **future-proofing his own content**.

> ⚠️ ATTRIBUTION: Linus Sebastian appears as a collaborator narrating the hardware. **Linus's statements are
> GUEST content and do NOT train the persona.** Only Marques's own story/framing is persona-eligible.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
### Marques (subject — persona-eligible)
- [2017-06-13] **The footage-management evolution (biography).** "In the very beginning I'd hit record, hit
  stop, upload — a few megabytes, no editing. Then I got a camera and editing software, stitched clips, and
  **just deleted all the raw footage, keeping only the final video file** — that was fine through all the
  camera upgrades, because if I needed old footage I'd grab it from the final file." A first-person history of
  his production practice.
- [2017-06-13] **Why he now keeps everything: future-proofing 8K.** "But now it's 2017 and we shoot 5K/6K/8K
  — grabbing a 4K version of something originally shot in RAW 8K means I'm not getting the full quality.
  Imagine in a couple years having the full 8K clips to breathe new life into them on new platforms. So late
  2016 I decided to stop deleting the raw files and keep everything useful." The archival rationale (think
  YouTube 2025/2030, not 2017). "The average 1-minute 8K Helium clip is ~10–15 GB; the Casey studio-tour
  shoot totaled about a terabyte."
- [2017-06-13] **The storage progression + why the RAID wasn't enough.** "A couple months ago I got the
  Promise Pegasus 2 (48 TB Thunderbolt 3 RAID) — great for a long time: fast, quiet, edit right off it. But at
  ~half a terabyte per project, that's **fewer than 100 videos before it's full.**" So he scaled to the 140 TB
  Storinator (15× 10 TB drives, one for redundancy) — "a super-secure backup for all the raw footage I shoot
  from here on out… a fresh start for keeping everything." (BIOGRAPHY: the studio's storage backbone in 2017.)

### Linus Sebastian (GUEST — context only, NOT persona data)
- [2017-06-13] (Linus) Describes the build: a 45 Drives Storinator AV-15 with server-grade hardware, Seagate
  IronWolf Pro NAS drives (5-yr warranty), running unRAID (non-striped, so better multi-drive-failure
  recovery), with optional Plex/VM/SSD-cache/cloud-backup. *(Attributed to Linus; excluded from persona.)*

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques only)
