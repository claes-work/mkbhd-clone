# USB-C ALL the Things! (@mkbhd, 2016-12-31)

## Summary
A year-end accessory guide arguing that **USB-C is the inevitable universal standard** — the fullest
statement of his "one cable, one charger" future thesis and a confident "**this is here to stay, unlike 3D
TVs or modular**" call. Also a notable production/biography aside: he's just started **archiving all his raw
footage** for the first time.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-12-31] **"USB-C all the things" — the one-cable future.** "USB is *universal* serial bus — keyword
  universal — but our devices are too small and powerful to keep using this huge legacy port. We're closing
  in on a day where you go on vacation and bring one cable and one charger for your laptop, tablet, phone,
  everything. We're not quite there yet." His clearest articulation of the convergence thesis.
- [2016-12-31] **"USB-C is here to stay" — a dated conviction.** "This is not a fading trend — unlike 3D TVs
  which rose and fell, or the modular thing sparking in and out, USB-C is gaining momentum and is going to be
  everywhere to stay. You're going to want to be on board." Distinguishes a durable standard from a fad, with
  named counterexamples.
- [2016-12-31] **BIOGRAPHY/PRODUCTION — he's now archiving raw footage.** "As of three videos ago, I'm now
  keeping all my raw video — fun fact, for years I was NOT doing that; I just kept the final file and threw
  away all the raw footage because 8K raw is a lot. Now I'm going to keep it all" (on a 48 TB Promise Pegasus
  RAID). A concrete production-practice change + a candid admission about his past workflow.
- [2016-12-31] **The video-editor's USB-C picks (production).** Highlights USB-C mobile SSDs for on-the-go
  big-file editing (the Samsung T3 after the Glyph "had issues for multiple people including me"), the
  Griffin USB-C MagSafe replacement (mourning MagSafe again), Anker battery banks, and port expanders/hubs.
  Practical, production-driven recommendations.
- [2016-12-31] **The teaching-challenge framing (voice).** "My challenge for this video is to talk about USB
  and keep it somewhat interesting and get a point across." A self-aware nod to making dry topics engaging —
  characteristic of his explainer craft.
