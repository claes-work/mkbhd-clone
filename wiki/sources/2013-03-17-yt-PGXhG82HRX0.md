# Samsung Galaxy S4 Features: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-17)

## Summary
A ★★ **business-strategy / incentive** analysis dressed as a features explainer. His
central read: **Samsung is no longer selling hardware — it's selling software features**,
and the whole (famously corny) Radio City presentation was engineered for average
consumers, not tech reviewers. One of his clearest early statements of the
tech-press-vs-mainstream-buyer gap and of reading a launch by the company's incentives.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-17] **The core thesis — value-via-software, not hardware**: "the fact that it's
  high-end hardware isn't really a big deal to Samsung, because they're not selling hardware
  anymore… Samsung is now adding value to their devices via software, not hardware. No one
  is buying the S3 for its 8-megapixel camera — they're buying it for Samsung features."
  Reads the strategy behind the product, not just the spec sheet.
- [2013-03-17] **Evidence from the presentation's structure**: "they never even said it ran
  Android during the presentation — they saved the full specs for a final slide after the
  credits… they demoed the features for hours rather than going over the specs, because
  that is what they're selling." Infers the strategy from what the company chose to
  emphasize (incentive-reading method).
- [2013-03-17] **The tech-press-vs-average-consumer gap, named**: "we tech people were
  rolling our eyes when Samsung spent half an hour showing how to swipe tab-to-tab without
  touching the screen — but that wasn't for us, the couple hundred thousand tech people
  watching. That was for the average people who've never seen that and think 'can the iPhone
  do that? No — so I want this one.'" His recurring insistence on whose decision a product
  is actually for.
- [2013-03-17] **Why it matters to enthusiasts now too**: "it's becoming more important to
  us — if I root this and put CyanogenMod on it, am I going to lose that S Pen or Air View
  feature? So the S features are getting to us too." Notes the software-lock-in cuts against
  his own stock-Android instinct.
- [2013-03-17] **A Kindle-Fire-style fork speculation, reasoned**: "could Samsung move off
  Android? I don't think so anytime soon, but it could go the way of the Kindle Fire —
  built on Android technically, but you'd never know unless they told you, because they skin
  it so heavily." A dated, mechanism-grounded industry prediction.
- [2013-03-17] **Because it's software, the S3/Note 2 will get it too**: "pretty much all
  these things will also be available in an update of the Galaxy S3 and Note 2." Traces the
  consequence of the software-first strategy (updates-as-value, again).
- [2013-03-17] **Student marker**: "I was actually out of town for spring break during the
  Galaxy S4 announcement, but I watched the whole thing." Dated college-life datum.
