## Summary
The panel reacts to Snap's newly announced consumer AR Spectacles ($2,195–$2,200) and,
later, to XREAL's Android XR glasses. Snap's glasses are standalone (no puck, no wire),
run two Snapdragon chips, ~4-hour battery, 130–136g, with a 51° field of view, LED
recording bar, 16M colors and clear-to-tinted electrochromic glass. The hosts credit the
engineering feat of cramming Vision-Pro-class compute into 130g glasses, but hammer two
problems: they look far too thick/heavy (you can't see the wearer's eyes through the
waveguides), and Snap's positioning is wrong — pitching a dev-kit-class device as a
consumer "wearable computer" with no real daily-use apps beyond Snapchat AR. The better
framing, they argue, is a Vision-Pro-style developer platform, not an everyday product.
They contrast it with XREAL's lighter (~90g) tethered Android XR glasses, whose real
utility is a private big screen for planes/desks (soft-locking a video in the corner of
your vision).
