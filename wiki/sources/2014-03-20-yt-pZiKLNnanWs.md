# Android Wear: State of Wearable Tech! (@mkbhd, 2014-03-20)

## Summary
Analysis of Google's just-announced Android Wear (a wearable-specific Android). Persona
value: the **Moto 360 round-watch enthusiasm** ("instabuy territory, honestly a work of
art"), his **display-primacy concern applied to watches** (LCD vs the more natural, always-
on e-ink), excitement for the **circular display**, and a continued **Apple-Watch
prediction**. Notable as an early appearance of his signature "dope."

## Key claims
- [2014-03-20] **The Moto 360 — round-watch love**: "the Moto 360 is instabuy territory for me
  right now — a beautiful, honestly a work of art to do what it does, making all the right design
  decisions to look like a regular watch. The Pebble is a rectangle; no matter what watch face you
  use, you're not fooling anyone into thinking it's a traditional watch, and that matters to a lot
  of people." His industrial-design-first read on wearables.
- [2014-03-20] **Display-primacy applied to watches — LCD vs e-ink**: "my one concern is the LCD
  displays — there's a reason many smartwatches went e-ink: it's more natural, like looking at
  paper, with much longer battery life and constant-on. You just look and the time is there; you
  don't have to shake it or do a gesture. If the Moto 360 can nail outdoor visibility, battery
  life, and always-on convenience, you can have all my money." His display + naturalness lens.
- [2014-03-20] **Excited about the circular display — a genuine new-form-factor beat**: "can we
  talk about the fact that this watch is a circle, not a square? Literally every other smartwatch
  has been a square — that's why we thought flexible displays were the answer. I'm excited to see
  what developers do with a circular display and that odd resolution. This is the first circular
  display I'm actually excited to be excited about." Genuine curiosity about a new shape.
- [2014-03-20] **The open-platform advantage**: "Android Wear is still the open-source OS, so it'll
  likely talk to any Android phone [Nexus, Moto X, whatever], unlike modded-Android watches that
  only work with a few devices — maybe even iOS or Windows Phone in the future." His platform-reach
  reasoning.
- [2014-03-20] **Continued Apple-Watch prediction**: "now we know what the future of smartwatches
  looks like, and it's looking pretty dope, not gonna lie. Apple, I hope you took notes — I'm
  keeping an eye on Apple." His Apple-Watch-is-coming call, restated (cf. the Top-5-Wearable video).
- [2014-03-20] **Rate-of-change excitement**: "if we can go from [the Galaxy Gear] to [the Moto
  360] in three months, imagine where we'll be in three or four more." His fast-maturation optimism.
