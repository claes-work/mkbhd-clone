# Google Nexus 4 Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-27)

## Summary
The full Nexus 4 review — the LG-built successor to the Galaxy Nexus he'd predicted. Verdict: "one of
the best value smartphones out there right now, and certainly the best Nexus to date." Notable for a
clean **anti-benchmark demonstration** (Quadrant scores "unusually low," real-world "noticeably snappier
than any Android phone I've ever used"), a **buyer's-guide close** (three reasons to buy), and honest
verdicts on the camera (better than expected) and battery (just average).

## Key claims
- [2012-11-27] **Verdict — best value, best Nexus to date**: "one of the best value smartphones out there
  right now, and it's certainly the best Nexus to date. It competes with the highest end on the market even
  at its lower price point." A dated, superlative-but-scoped verdict.
- [2012-11-27] **Anti-benchmark demonstrated on camera** — the standout: "I got 5300 or so on Quadrant,
  while the Optimus G with the same processor and RAM broke 7,000 — so optimization is an ongoing process.
  But **real-world performance is still noticeably snappier than any other Android phone I've ever
  used**." He sees a low benchmark and dismisses it in favor of lived speed, exactly per his stated
  position ([[2012-10-12-yt-VdsMwGWvhO4]]).
- [2012-11-27] **The buyer's-guide close** — three reasons to buy: "if you want stock Android and the
  fastest updates straight from Google; if you want a very inexpensive high-bang-for-buck unlocked phone;
  or if you won't settle for anything less than the Snapdragon S4 Pro." Partition-the-buyer framing again.
- [2012-11-27] **Honest on the weak spots**: "I wouldn't buy this phone specifically for the camera or the
  battery life, but they're certainly acceptable." Battery is "just average, nothing spectacular — gets you
  through the day"; the speaker is "loud but super tiny, you can accidentally cover the whole thing."
- [2012-11-27] **Camera surprised him upward**: "the new 8 MP camera is quite good — it actually surprised
  me. About on par with the Galaxy S3 and Note 2, especially with HDR mode on… can look like it came out of
  something other than a cell phone camera." A disclosed expectation-beaten.
- [2012-11-27] **Design read as a hybrid**: "an almost perfect hybrid falling right between the LG Optimus
  G and the Galaxy Nexus" — the Optimus G's square glass-both-sides body, the Galaxy Nexus's clean
  no-branding Nexus front, plus a unique glittery under-glass back. Clean/no-branding aesthetic praised.
- [2012-11-27] **The no-LTE caveat stated plainly, with the honesty about the "hack"**: "you won't be able
  to get this on Verizon… it still has the LTE chip but no amplifier and barely supports one band — not
  exactly true LTE support natively," a bonus only for some Canadian carriers / possibly T-Mobile. No
  overclaiming.
- [2012-11-27] **A candid durability warning**: "don't drop this phone — Gorilla Glass 2 on the back and
  everything, but if it hits the right angle it will crack anyway. Phones aren't meant to be dropped."
