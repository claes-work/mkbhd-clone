# Audio Technica ATH-M50 Review (@mkbhd, 2011-09-12)

## Summary
An HD review of the Audio-Technica ATH-M50 headphones, opened with Marques's own
superlative framing — "my self-proclaimed best headphones for under 200 bucks period."
Walks the build (mostly metal, gold-plated jack, coiled 1.2 m cable), adjustability and
comfort, then the sound (flat, accurate frequency response), and closes by awarding the
**third-ever MKBHD 10/10**. Notable on the business side: he states he **bought the pair
himself for $160** (not a seeded unit) and would happily have paid $250, and he gates a
giveaway on the video reaching 800 likes — asking the eventual winner to make their own
video review in return.

## Key claims
- [2011-09-12] The ATH-M50 earns **the third 10/10 product rating ever given on the MKBHD
  channel** — a dated data point on how rare the top score was at this stage (the prior
  two: iKey-Audio M808 V2 studio monitor, Asus UL30A-X5).
- [2011-09-12] **Self-purchased review unit**: "as you can see by the link in the
  description I only paid 160," against a stated willingness to pay $250 — an early,
  explicit separation of bought-it-myself units from PR-seeded ones.
- [2011-09-12] Review criteria applied, in order: build quality → adjustability/fit →
  comfort → portability (fold-up + pouch) → sound. Sound is framed as the thing a
  headphone review cannot omit ("what's a headphone review without word on how they
  sound — well, that'd be pretty stupid").
- [2011-09-12] Sound verdict is described in the flat/accurate frame rather than a
  bass-forward one: "the frequency response is flat and amazingly accurate," lows deep
  but "not overpowering at all," mids balanced, highs "extremely nice."
- [2011-09-12] Value is stated as budget-relative, not absolute: "if you have a $200
  budget this is the pair of headphones you need to buy."
- [2011-09-12] **Likes-gated giveaway** (800 likes → a pair given away, details on
  Twitter), with the added ask that the winner post their own video review — an early
  audience-participation loop.
