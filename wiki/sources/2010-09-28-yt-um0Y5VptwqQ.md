# Blackberry Playbook Tablet - First Look (@mkbhd, 2010-09-28)

## Summary
Commentary on RIM's own BlackBerry PlayBook advertisement (no hands-on access),
walking through the specs shown (1GHz dual-core, 7" screen, rear 1080p/3MP camera +
front-facing HD camera), RIM's dedicated tablet OS and developer platform, and the
ad's business/multitasking framing, with comparative references to the iPad and
Galaxy Tab throughout.

## Key claims
- [2010-09-28] **Discloses upfront that the source is an advertisement, not hands-on
  time**: "today in fact we're looking at the PlayBook's ad put out by BlackBerry not
  too long ago... the fact that it is an advertisement may put some of you guys off,
  but it is very very telling" — an explicit, early instance of transparently sourcing
  a "first look" from manufacturer marketing rather than presenting it as a hands-on
  review, paralleling the same disclosure pattern in the same-week Twitter-parody
  video.
- [2010-09-28] **Comparative framing against the two dominant tablets of the era**:
  repeatedly benchmarks PlayBook's shown UI/animation quality and screen
  resolution/content (Flash support) against "something like the iPad or the smaller
  Galaxy Tab," and notes phone-pairing parity with "already going on with iOS devices
  and with Android."
- [2010-09-28] **Names RIM's platform strategy as a notable step**: highlights that
  RIM built its "own operating system dedicated to this tablet," opening the platform
  to third-party developers — treated as a meaningful strategic move, not just a spec
  bullet ("good call, RIM").
- [2010-09-28] **Withholds a verdict pending pricing**: closes without a score,
  explicitly deferring judgment — "it'll be nice to see how they price this and what
  it'll really bring to the market in the future."
