# HTC One M9 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-03-03)

## Summary
Hands-on with the HTC One M9 — "the equivalent of the HTC One M8s": an **incremental improvement, in the
best possible way**, since the M8 was already one of 2014's best. Same metal design (buttons moved to the
right, brushed finish, two-tone), still **BoomSound** (now with Dolby) and **microSD** (which Samsung's
S6 dropped), still a great 1080p display. The one big change — a **20 MP no-OIS camera** — is the thing
he'll pixel-peep in the full review. No-cases/skin instinct on the slippery metal.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-03-03] **Incremental, in the best way.** "Most of the outside hardware stayed the same while the
  specs got bumped — it's the equivalent of the HTC One M8s. An incremental improvement, but in the best
  possible way, because the M8 was already one of the best phones of last year." (His iterative-refinement
  view.)
- [2015-03-03] **What stayed (and why that's good):** all-metal, **BoomSound** front-facing speakers
  ("one year later still some of the loudest, crispest, clearest," now with Dolby), **microSD expandable
  storage** ("noticeably missing from Samsung's new flagship"), and a great **1080p** display (441 ppi) —
  "I don't mind [not QHD]; I suspect we'll see QHD in the next one." The bottom-bezel HTC logo houses the
  display drivers ("they have to go somewhere," given how much space the speakers take).
- [2015-03-03] **What changed:** buttons moved to the right (easier reach), a brushed two-tone look, and
  the **20 MP camera (no OIS, 4K video)** — "the number one thing I'll be testing in the full review." He
  won't judge it from a bad-lit tethered press room: "I really want to go out and test this camera as
  deeply as I can, bring images back to the computer, **pixel peep**."
- [2015-03-03] **No-cases instinct:** expects it to be as slippery as the M8 — "I think I'm going to end
  up putting a skin on it," but "I'll at least give it a shot with no skin or case for my full review."
