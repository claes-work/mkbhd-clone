# Internet Explorer 9 Beta (@mkbhd, 2010-09-24)

## Summary
A direct comparative follow-up to [[wiki/sources/2010-09-21-yt-X-X-IZsnbEE|the Google
Chrome 7 video three days earlier]]: reviews IE9 beta's UI overhaul ("interface lift"),
then re-runs the same two benchmarks (fish tank hardware-acceleration test, Acid3) on
IE9 to compare directly against the Chrome 7 numbers just reported. Closes with a
qualified positive verdict that stops short of switching browsers.

## Key claims
- [2010-09-24] **Runs the identical benchmark pair from the Chrome video as an
  explicit same-week comparison**: "that fish tank test, if you remember I did that
  in the Chrome 7 video... this is going to perform a lot better than Chrome 7, I
  never thought I'd say that" — IE9 hits ~55fps at 20 fish and 30fps+ at 100 fish
  with hardware acceleration, beating the Chrome 7 numbers cited three days earlier. ★
- [2010-09-24] **Acid3 result contradicts a viewer claim from the Chrome video's
  comments**: IE9 scores "an awkward 70... 95," and he notes "I do recall people
  saying in the Chrome video that they got 95 out of 100" (vs. his own reported
  Chrome-7 100/100) — flagged here as a discrepancy between his own on-camera result
  and viewer-reported results, not resolved on camera.
- [2010-09-24] **States a self-surprised but still qualified verdict**: "they've done
  a much better job than I had anticipated, but it's still not going to be bringing me
  off my trusty Google Chrome" — praises IE9's speed and UI improvements while
  explicitly maintaining brand loyalty to Chrome, reinforcing the Google-affinity
  statement from the prior video.
- [2010-09-24] **Names the browser's opening speed as "definitely going to force me
  to drop something like Firefox"** (not IE9 itself, but Firefox as the casualty) —
  a three-way comparative browser-loyalty statement in one video (IE9 > Firefox on
  speed; Chrome remains his daily driver over both).
