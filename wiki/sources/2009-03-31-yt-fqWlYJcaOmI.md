# Protect Your PC - Conficker Worm! (@mkbhd, 2009-03-31)

## Summary
A news-reaction / PC-security explainer (Marques, age 15, recorded March 31, 2009) about the
**Conficker worm**, which rumor held would "activate" on **April 1st (April Fool's Day)**. He
explains the worm spreads via **USB/flash drives and email attachments**, lies **dormant**
until a trigger date, and (on PCs only) can **disable firewalls and block Windows updates/
patches**. Mitigations he recommends: **disable AutoPlay/AutoRun for USB devices and CDs**
(via Vista's Start menu → type "autoplay"), **install the latest Microsoft patches** (link in
description), and **update antivirus + use caution with downloads**. He cites Microsoft's
figure of **20+ million infected PCs** and its **$250,000 bounty** for information on the
worm's author, and repeatedly (self-deprecatingly) fumbles the phrase "in the description."
Ends unsure whether the April 1st threat is real or "an April Fool's hoax."

## Key claims
- [2009-03-31] The **Conficker worm** spreads by **USB and email**, stays **dormant** until a
  trigger date, and on PCs can **disable firewalls and Windows updates**; rumored to activate
  **April 1, 2009**.
- [2009-03-31] Recommended defenses: **disable AutoPlay/AutoRun**, **install Microsoft's
  latest patches**, **update antivirus**, and **be cautious with downloads/clicks**.
- [2009-03-31] Cites **Microsoft: 20M+ PCs infected** and a **$250,000 bounty** for
  information leading to the worm's author.
- [2009-03-31] Early example of MKBHD doing **tech-news reaction/explainer** content (not just
  tutorials), hedging on hype ("maybe it isn't an April Fool[s]").

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "this virus is different because it's laying dormant inside your computer ... until a certain date when it decides to be activated — and yes, only PCs"
