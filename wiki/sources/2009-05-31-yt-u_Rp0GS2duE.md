# Tech Channel of the Week #4: TechTechMan (@mkbhd, 2009-05-31)

## Summary
The fourth instalment of the weekly channel-shoutout series, featuring **TechTechMan**. Its
value to the clone is not the featured channel but the two dated career markers Marques
drops in passing: he states that he has **"recently achieved" 1,000 subscribers** — the goal
he had publicly set two and a half weeks earlier at ~900 subs
([[2009-05-14-yt-5PbShASzrbs]]) — and confirms his **1,000-subscriber giveaway** is "coming
up soon." He also studies TechTechMan's channel out loud as a **growth case study**: partner
status below 1,000 subscribers, giveaways as a subscriber engine, and content mix as the
reason for growth. This is the origin-era MKBHD watching the YouTube business model as
closely as he watches gadgets. ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-31] **Milestone — 1,000 subscribers reached**: "my next giveaway which will be
  coming up soon in honor of my 1,000 subscribers which I recently achieved." Dates the
  1,000-subscriber mark to on/just before 2009-05-31 (self-reported).
- [2009-05-31] **The giveaway is still pending**, announced but not yet run — consistent with
  the "coming very soon" of [[2009-05-27-yt-b7Nc78QqDs0]].
- [2009-05-31] **Partner status is not a subscriber threshold**: TechTechMan "became a
  YouTube partner back when he had actually less than a thousand subscribers... because of
  the quality of his videos" — Marques's own dated understanding that **quality, not scale,
  unlocked partnership** in 2009.
- [2009-05-31] **Growth attributed to content quality**: "his channel's been growing ever
  since and because of the quality of his videos."
- [2009-05-31] **Giveaways read as a growth mechanism**: a $15 iTunes gift-card giveaway for
  hitting 2,000 subs "has gotten him a lot more subscribers" — Marques states the causal
  read plainly, days before running his own.
- [2009-05-31] **Content-mix analysis**: he catalogues the channel as "a mix of hardware,
  software, a giveaway, etc.", noting most videos are application reviews.
- [2009-05-31] **Owning hardware unlocks formats**: "now that he has an iPod touch, he can do
  application reviews, maybe a case review" — the origin-era insight that the device you own
  determines the videos you can make (Marques was himself a non-iPhone owner at this point).
- [2009-05-31] **Reciprocity in the partner ecosystem**: partners can list recommended
  channels, "and I happen to be one of them. Yay me."
- [2009-05-31] **Real-time subscriber watching**: "he just reached 4,000 subscribers a couple
  of minutes ago" — the counter is checked live.
- [2009-05-31] **Series mechanics**: channel link in the description, plus a quick read of the
  channel's visual identity ("pretty simple channel, got the blue and yellow going on") —
  channel *design* is part of how he evaluates a channel.
