# Smartphone Awards 2022! (@mkbhd, 2022-12-27)

## Summary
The annual **Smartphone Awards** — "one of my favorite videos… a bit of a tradition
around here" — in which Marques hands out (physical) trophies across the phones of 2022.
Winners: **Best Big Phone** → Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra (a "return of the Note," so
complete it's "kind of boring… which is actually a good thing"); **Best Small Phone** →
ASUS Zenfone 9 ("the best small phone I've ever seen"); **Best Camera System** → iPhone
14 Pro (its **fourth straight year** — "the longest defending champion of a single
category ever" — decided mostly on video); **Best Battery** → ASUS ROG Phone 6 (6,000 mAh,
65 W); **Design** → Nothing Phone (1) ("the Nothing Phone won — get it?"); **Value / bang
for the buck** → Pixel 7 ($599); **Bust of the Year** → OnePlus 10T; and dual **MVP** →
Pixel 7 (also **Most Improved**) with the Zenfone 9 as an extremely close second. He
also debuts the year's **blind smartphone camera test** website — where the public voted
the Pixel 6A the overall winner — while separating "photo-vote winner" from his own
"best camera system" judgment. Solo video — all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2022-12-27] Framing/philosophy: the awards revisit every phone of the calendar year to
  "recognize and reward some of the best and some of the worst"; categories have evolved
  over time (e.g. the design award drifted from "best build quality" toward "who can step
  outside the box"), and "one thing is for sure is they're my awards."
- [2022-12-27] **Best Big Phone → Galaxy S22 Ultra**: does it all (6.8" display, S Pen +
  5,000 mAh, dual telephotos) to the point of being "kind of boring, which I think is
  actually a good thing… you don't want anything to be too weird about it" at that price —
  "a return of the Note." Runner-up ASUS ROG Phone 6; honorable mention Xiaomi Mix Fold 2.
- [2022-12-27] **Best Small Phone → ASUS Zenfone 9** ("the best small phone I've ever
  seen"): 5.9" flagship, and he was "genuinely surprised" by its battery life — rare for
  him. Recurring refrain: "if this phone this small can have a headphone jack, then what's
  everybody else's excuse?"
- [2022-12-27] The year's **blind smartphone camera test** ran as a public voting website
  across 16 phones in three categories; the overall public winner was the **Pixel 6A**
  (runner-up Pixel 7) — but he explicitly does not equate the photo-vote win with "best
  camera system."
- [2022-12-27] **Best Camera System → iPhone 14 Pro**, its **fourth year in a row** ("the
  longest defending champion of a single category ever"), decided on video: "in the video
  department it's a whole different dimension and it's not really even close" (ProRes,
  color consistency, sharpness, audio) from running the Auto Focus channel on it; he'd
  give the photos "an A-minus to a B-plus." Repeats that offloading files "still takes a
  long time… and I hope they fix that."
- [2022-12-27] **Best Battery → ASUS ROG Phone 6** (6,000 mAh, 65 W, 0→100% under an hour,
  deep battery-management features), knocked only for no wireless charging. Runner-up
  iPhone 14 Plus (big 60 Hz display + massive battery); honorable mention Zenfone 9.
- [2022-12-27] **Design → Nothing Phone (1)** — "the Nothing Phone won, get it?" — for the
  transparent look and Glyph interface that "skirts the line right between gimmick and
  feature," rewarded because "you don't see a lot of phones trying new stuff." Honorable
  mention Xiaomi Mix Fold 2 for folding "to about the same thickness as a normal phone,"
  which he calls a folding-phone breakthrough moment.
- [2022-12-27] **Value → Pixel 7** ($599): "one of the best $599 phones I have ever seen,"
  full Google flagship experience (Tensor, same main sensor as the Pro) so good it makes
  the 7 Pro "feel like not that good of a deal." Runner-up Zenfone 9 ($699, "the best $699
  phone I've ever seen"); honorable mention Realme 10 Pro+ (~$350, second in votes-per-
  dollar in the blind test).
- [2022-12-27] **Bust of the Year → OnePlus 10T**, for a big gap between expectations and
  result and an eroded identity — "you can't even describe a OnePlus phone without feeling
  really generic."
- [2022-12-27] **MVP** = his favorites list (best overall phone + experience + industry
  impact). Two MVPs, top being the **Pixel 7** (also **Most Improved** — "Google really
  found their groove," recognizable from across the street), with the **Zenfone 9** an
  extremely close second and the S22 Ultra an honorable mention. Closing bit: companies
  can DM him on Twitter and he'll literally ship the trophy.
