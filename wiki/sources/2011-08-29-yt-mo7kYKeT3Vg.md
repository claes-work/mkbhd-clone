# Top Reasons the Galaxy Nexus WINS (@mkbhd, 2011-08-29)

## Summary
A pre-release "top reasons" listicle about the then-unreleased Nexus Prime
(later Samsung Galaxy Nexus), built strictly from rumors, with an explicit
upfront caveat that the phone "doesn't really exist yet." Reasons given:
expected Android 4.0 (Ice Cream Sandwich) and the Nexus line's history of
first-to-update/most developer-friendly status; a rumored Super AMOLED Plus
display at 1280x720 on a larger (~4.5–4.65") panel; strong expected battery
life from OLED tech plus a large rumored battery; a rumored dual-core
1.2–1.5GHz processor; and rumored 4G LTE, hoped for on Verizon rather than
Sprint. Closes with an explicit, pointed request to Verizon not to repeat
the Galaxy S2 carrier mishandling.

## Key claims
- [2011-08-29] Explicit rumor-vs-fact caveat stated on camera before the
  list starts — an early, dated instance of flagging speculative coverage
  as speculative rather than presenting rumors as confirmed fact.
- [2011-08-29] States a standing positive prior toward the Nexus line
  specifically because of update priority and developer/application
  support — a recurring "why I like Nexus phones" rationale, independent of
  any single device's specs.
- [2011-08-29] Names a carrier grievance directly: criticizes how Verizon
  handled the Galaxy S2 launch and explicitly asks Verizon "please do not
  screw this up" with the rumored Nexus Prime — an early instance of
  carrier-specific criticism as distinct from device criticism.
