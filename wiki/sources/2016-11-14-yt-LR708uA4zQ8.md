# Google Pixel Review: Best Android Smartphone! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-14)

## Summary
The full Pixel review, structured around a central thesis: **"a phone you have to actually use to
understand how good it is."** He runs it through the **five pillars**, crowns the camera **his new favorite
in any smartphone** (thanks to HDR+ image processing, not the sensor), and — despite calling the design
"boring" repeatedly — lands it as **his favorite smartphone experience right now.**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-14] **THE THESIS: a phone you have to use to understand.** "The Pixel is uninspiring — the
  design's not exciting, not turning heads — but use it full-time for a couple weeks and it's a completely
  different thing." Substance-over-first-impressions, his core reviewer stance.
- [2016-11-14] **Applies the five pillars explicitly.** Build (metal, solid, but not water resistant =
  "okay"), performance ("yes, no question — the smoothest Android phone you can get, out of the box and
  way later"), battery ("one of the best in any smartphone — 5+ hrs screen-on with my high-intensity use,
  ending the day at 25%+"), display ("pretty solid AMOLED, could be brighter, not best-in-class"), camera
  ("special"). ([[2016-08-17-yt-ptni6tRrU54]] is the framework's canonical source.)
- [2016-11-14] **"My new favorite camera in any smartphone" — and processing, not the sensor, is why.**
  "The number one reason the Pixel takes such good photos is the image processing — HDR+ is really
  important; leave Auto HDR+ on all the time and you get much better photos with every shot; the Snapdragon
  821 makes it instant." Skeptical-but-vindicated on the DxOMark claim ("does that rating even matter
  anymore? …bottom line, yeah, this is a really good camera"). Cites a **blind test on Twitter** (Pixel vs.
  iPhone 7 Plus) where people couldn't tell — an early instance of his blind-test instinct.
- [2016-11-14] **The "wow out loud" shot (a camera-judgment tell).** Describes a shot from a moving car
  window (both cars at 50 mph) that's "tack sharp, action frozen, the dynamic range with the blue sky —
  holy crap that is good… I think we got a winner." How he actually judges a camera: does it make him say
  wow.
- [2016-11-14] **Source-critical on the "software fixes lens flare" claim.** "Google claims the lens flare
  can be fixed with a software update — I don't know how much, because lens flare is caused by hardware and
  physical lens elements. But I don't really care, I love this camera enough." Won't repeat a marketing
  claim he doubts, even for a product he loves.
- [2016-11-14] **The verdict — favorite experience, but priced for enthusiasts.** "The only thing the
  Pixel isn't great at is the industrial design — not water resistant, no expandable storage, bad speakers,
  and it's just so boring. But all that melts away when you actually use it… the Pixel is right now my
  favorite smartphone experience, tops in my book." Caveat: "next to a Galaxy S7 or Note 7 with all the
  bells and whistles, a normal person will skip it — but for an enthusiast, hands down I recommend it."
- [2016-11-14] **"Made by Google, built by HTC" — source-critical.** "It's made by Google, which I respect,
  but you should know HTC built it — some say it's a body HTC was already working on. HTC pioneered
  BoomSound, and here's the Pixel with one of the worst speakers of the year — funny." The stabilization
  "uses gyroscope data for aggressive EIS — sometimes looks tripod-smooth, sometimes jittery."

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The Google Pixel is right now my favorite smartphone experience, tops in my book. There, I said it."
