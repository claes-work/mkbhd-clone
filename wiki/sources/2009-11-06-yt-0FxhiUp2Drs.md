# gScreen Spacebook: Dualscreen Laptop! (@mkbhd, 2009-11-06)

## Summary
A **press-material-only preview** (not hands-on) of the gScreen Spacebook, a first-of-its-kind
dual-15.4"-screen laptop from a small Alaska-based company. He reads through the
company's publicly posted specs (CPU, GPU, HDD, battery) and gives his own reactions/hopes for
the concept, while explicitly stating he does not have a review unit and would love to get
one.

## Key claims
- [2009-11-06] **No review unit — commentary from public specs only.** Explicitly frames
  this as an overview built from the company's website/Facebook page, not a hands-on: "it
  would be really awesome if I could get a review unit from G screen."
- [2009-11-06] **Aspirational/novelty framing.** Reacts to the concept itself (two screens
  in one laptop, "the cool factor") rather than judging real-world execution — an early
  instance of covering a product for its novelty/hype value alone, which is the same
  editorial lane his later "Dope Tech" franchise (est. Dec 2019) occupies.
- [2009-11-06] Specs relayed: 15.4"/16"/17" configurations, Core 2 Duo 2.53–2.8GHz,
  Nvidia GeForce 9800GT (0.5GB), 250GB/500GB SATA HDD, 802.11n Wi-Fi, HDMI out, targeted for
  Q4 2009 release.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "It would be really awesome if I could get a review unit from G screen — that would be absolutely awesome, and I would definitely do a number of videos about the hardware, the software, and the integration with the dual monitors."

> "I really would love to be able to do something like that — have an HDMI monitor connected to my dual-screen laptop, that would just be an awesome-looking setup."
