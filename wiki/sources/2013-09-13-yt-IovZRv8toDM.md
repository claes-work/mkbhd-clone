# Phonebloks: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-09-13)

## Summary
An explainer on the viral Phonebloks modular-smartphone concept — and a clean **incentive/
economics** analysis of *why* it's near-impossible to build, even as he loves the idea. He
explains the anti-waste motive, the supply-chain economics that make integrated phones
cheap, and the missing manufacturer incentive. A precursor to his later Project Ara
coverage.

## Key claims
- [2013-09-13] **The concept + its anti-waste motive**: "people usually replace a phone for a
  single reason — it's slow, the camera, a broken screen — and throw out the whole thing. Phone-
  bloks is a modular phone: a base all the parts plug into, like Legos, so you swap one part
  instead of the whole phone." States the idea sympathetically.
- [2013-09-13] **Why it's near-impossible — the incentive gap**: "there's no incentive right now
  for these companies to buy in — they're all invested in making their own products, because
  that's where the margins are." Incentive-first reasoning on why manufacturers won't cooperate.
- [2013-09-13] **Why it's near-impossible — the supply-chain economics**: "smartphone makers
  crank out millions of identical phones with parts crammed and woven together to stay thin and
  cheap; the more they make, the lower the cost. Building individual modular parts without
  weaving them is going to be a very big and very expensive phone — a Phonebloks S4 would cost
  about twice as much and be much larger." Teaches the manufacturing economics.
- [2013-09-13] **Loves the idea despite the logistics**: "the logistics are damn near impossible
  right now, but that's not the point — this is a campaign to spread the *idea* of a modular
  phone. I love the idea and I hope it does really well." Separates the aspiration from the
  feasibility (his even-handed enthusiasm).
- [2013-09-13] **His own prior modular concept**: "I'll throw in a much older video I did with my
  own modular-smartphone idea — the ideas here are strikingly similar." Notes he'd had the same
  dream (a taste/idea datum).
- [2013-09-13] **Reads it as half concept, half social-media campaign**: "half tech concept, half
  social-media campaign — Thunderclap, 300,000+ signed up to all tweet at once." His media-savvy
  read of how the idea spread.
