# Top 10 Google I/O 2013 Announcements! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-17)

## Summary
A condensed recap of Google I/O 2013's top 10 announcements (Google Play Music All Access,
Hangouts, Google Now improvements, the new Google Maps, the Nexus-experience GS4, etc.).
Mostly event coverage — low persona yield — but it carries a couple of recurring notes:
his **"Google doesn't make its services international"** critique and his standard
**"not everything is fully baked yet"** caveat.

## Key claims
- [2013-05-17] **The international-availability critique, restated**: on Google Play Music All
  Access, "it might still be US-only, which is unfortunate because Google has a habit of not
  making their services internationally available." A recurring, even-handed criticism of a
  favored company.
- [2013-05-17] **"Not everything is fully baked yet" — his standard caveat**: "Google Hangouts
  could really use SMS support and a couple of other things could use some fine-tuning." His
  habitual reserve-judgment-on-unfinished-things stance.
- [2013-05-17] **Values endless-discovery in a music service**: on Play Music All Access, "you
  can create a radio station off a song or playlist and it just creates an endless playlist…
  endless music discovery, that's what it should be all about — Spotify is on notice." His
  music-service priorities (consistent with the heavy-listener disclosure).
- [2013-05-17] **Defends Google+ as connective tissue**: "a lot of people say no one uses
  Google+, and that's fine if you think that, but whether you like it or not it's becoming a
  huge part of tying all of Google's services together." His recurring (date-scoped) Google+
  advocacy.
- [2013-05-17] **Personal note — a trip**: "I've been using the [Play Music] app the past
  couple of days and I'm going on a trip very soon, so I'm making playlists for the airplane."
  A small dated biography aside (consistent with heavy-listener + travel).
- [2013-05-17] **Singles out an underrated winner**: "the new Google Maps was one of the most
  underrated and awesome things at Google I/O." His attention to under-appreciated announcements.
