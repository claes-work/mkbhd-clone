# Google Nexus 9 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-12-01)

## Summary
The full Nexus 9 review — "HTC's safest bet ever," and a clean **value-mismatch** verdict. The build
disappointed because HTC (of the M8's premium metal + BoomSound) shipped "essentially a Nexus 7 with
metal sides," yet Google prices it at $400 — "you've got to pick somewhere between really well-priced and
really premium, and right now it's not striking that balance." Recommendation narrows to **buy it only
for the stock-Android software experience**; awful buttons (quietly revised to a "Nexus 9 2.0"), a
light-bleeding panel, and the NVIDIA Shield cannibalizing it at $300 all count against the hardware.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-12-01] **Build-expectation letdown.** "When we heard it was made by HTC we had high expectations
  for build quality and beastly speakers [like the M8]… but it's built exactly the same as the Nexus 7,
  just with metal sides." The **buttons are terrible** ("if you close your eyes you wouldn't know when you
  turned it on") — HTC silently revised to a clickier "Nexus 9 2.0" for later orders, "but that's not
  solving my problem."
- [2014-12-01] **Display good with one flaw** — sharp 4:3 2048×1536, saturated, good angles, but
  **backlight light-bleed** at the edges "you only find on really low-quality panels," visible in the dark.
- [2014-12-01] **Front-facing speakers again valued** — "not even that great… but simply placing them on
  the front makes them so much better than the iPad's or Nexus 7's side/rear-facing" ones.
- [2014-12-01] **Tablet-is-a-blown-up-phone, restated** — Android 5.0 is "the phone experience blown up…
  I'd still like to see it take advantage of the larger display in a few more places." Smooth on the K1 +
  2 GB, with occasional app-launch hangs when RAM fills.
- [2014-12-01] **The value-mismatch verdict.** "$400 is hard to recommend — it essentially feels like a
  slightly larger Nexus 7 [which was known for being cheap]. This is a better tablet at $300." The NVIDIA
  Shield ($300, similar specs, nice build) is "cannibalizing" it. **Buy the Nexus 9 for the software
  experience (immediate Lollipop) — not the hardware.** (Even the iPad mini Retina "far outclasses" the
  plastic tablets on build.)
