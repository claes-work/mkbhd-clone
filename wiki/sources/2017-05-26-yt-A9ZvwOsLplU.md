# Dear Twitter! (@mkbhd, 2017-05-26)

## Summary
A "Dear Twitter" wishlist (the format after Dear Apple/Samsung) — four asks that reveal his product values
around platforms: **a chronological timeline**, **stop hobbling third-party apps**, **bring back @replies**,
and **let us edit tweets**. Rich voice (the comedic edit-tweets riff) and platform-design commentary; also a
concrete personal-use disclosure (he uses a third-party client to keep tweets in order).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-05-26] **"Many of us would like a chronological timeline."** "Twitter is the most time-sensitive
  social media — I use a third-party app just to guarantee I read tweets in order as they happen; there's
  nothing like a 1-day-old post right above a 45-second-old one. At least make the 'show this less often'
  button actually show it less often." His pro-chronological, anti-algorithmic-feed stance.
- [2017-05-26] **"Stop hobbling third-party apps."** "Twitter has a **token limit** — a ceiling on how many
  users a third-party app can have; hit it and the app is dead to new users. Several apps I've used died this
  way (Falcon, plus Android/Mac/iOS apps) — all it takes is getting popular. Either stop being developer- and
  user-hostile, or make your first-party apps way better (I haven't seen a good Twitter-for-Mac update in
  months) — or both." A pointed platform-incentives critique (developer-hostility).
- [2017-05-26] **"Let us edit tweets" — the comedic riff + a concrete proposal.** "It's 2017 — we have a
  machine digging a tunnel under LA for a hyperloop, we found the God particle, but we still can't edit
  tweets." Airs the case *against* (edit-bait sponsored abuse) then proposes a scoped fix: **a 60-second edit
  window, an edit indicator, and public edit history** — "solves pretty much all the downsides; the half-life
  of a tweet is really short, so most activity happens in the first minutes — you notice a typo 45 seconds
  and 3,000 retweets later; that little edit button just for that would be so nice." A characteristic
  argue-both-sides-then-propose-a-scoped-solution move.
- [2017-05-26] **"Bring back @replies the way they were 3–4 months ago."** "It was just better that way." A
  concrete UX-regression complaint.
- [2017-05-26] **The platform-respect framing.** "Twitter is so important — like YouTube, it gives millions
  of people a voice, it's a cultural phenomenon — but you're not perfect." Frames the critique as coming from
  a heavy, invested user (the user-first credibility frame).
