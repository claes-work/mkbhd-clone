# Top Galaxy Nexus Accessories (@mkbhd, 2012-01-14)

## Summary
An accessories round-up for the Galaxy Nexus — SGP and Incipio cases, wet- and dry-install
screen protectors — with a **guest segment handed to another creator**: Lamar of Wilson Tech 1
reviews a 6000 mAh battery pack. Two things make it worth a page: it's a rare **mixed-attribution**
video in this era, and Marques states a **change in his own position** on phone cases between
the unboxing four weeks earlier and this video.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-14] **Position change, stated on camera**: he restates that he "typically carries his
  phone naked" (as in [[2011-12-15-yt-9ZZQrm07s4A]]) but closes with "for the longest time I did,
  but I'm starting to really like the cases I've been using." A small, dated, explicit reversal
  of a personal preference — the kind of thing that must not be flattened into a single timeless
  claim.
- [2012-01-14] **Guest segment delegated in full**: the battery-pack review is handed to Lamar of
  **Wilson Tech 1**, who addresses "MKBHD audience" directly. All statements in that segment are
  **his, not Marques's** — see attribution note below.
- [2012-01-14] Case assessments (Marques): the SGP Ultra Capsule is flexible, trivially on-and-off,
  and cheap enough to keep spares in the car; the Incipio Double Cover layers a soft shell under a
  hard shell for "a whole second layer of protection," at extra cost and bulk. Neither covers the
  front.
- [2012-01-14] **Screen-protector craft, self-reported**: "I'm proud of my near perfect installs of
  screen protectors" — wet-install (spray-on, "the Flex") versus dry-install ("the ultra
  oleophobic," with an oil-resistant coating that makes the surface slicker than bare glass).
- [2012-01-14] Content sourced from comments: the battery segment exists because "a couple of you
  guys in the comments were telling me that your Galaxy Nexus has less than desirable battery
  life" — request-driven, as usual.

## Guest-segment claims — NOT Marques (Lamar / Wilson Tech 1)
`attribution: guest` — the following are the guest's assessments and must never flow into
`persona/`:
- The SGP Cool F6Q is 6000 mAh against the Galaxy Nexus's 1850 mAh, so roughly three full
  recharges; a switch selects Android or iPhone output; it failed to charge an iPad.
- Verdict: at **$124** it's "definitely a niche product for a niche audience" — justified only for
  travel, hiking, or "people who are at CES this week" where power is unavailable, and not for the
  everyday user.
