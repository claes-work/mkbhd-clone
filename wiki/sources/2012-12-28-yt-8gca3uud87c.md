# Samsung Galaxy Camera Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-12-28)

## Summary
The full review of the Android-powered Samsung Galaxy Camera, and a clean example of a **mixed verdict
where the core function is the weak point**: it's the most connected camera on the market with a
genuinely good manual-control UI, but the actual image quality — the whole point of a camera — falls
short. Verdict: Samsung "swung and missed"; wait for the sequel. A good template for how he resolves a
product that's great at everything except its primary job.

## Key claims
- [2012-12-28] **The core-function-fails framing — the review's spine**: "this is supposed to be a camera
  first, but ironically that's where most of its shortcomings are… for the price, this f/2.8–5.9 lens and
  a slightly small 16 MP sensor and the image and video quality it produces doesn't really match up. That's
  where Samsung kind of **swung and missed** with this product." A device excellent at its Android/
  connectivity side but weak at its actual purpose.
- [2012-12-28] **What it does uniquely well — connectivity + control**: "the most connected camera out
  there right now" (SIM + Wi-Fi + 4G, full Play Store, real-time sharing to Instagram/Dropbox/Gmail/Google+),
  running Android 4.1 with Project Butter on a 4.8" Super AMOLED touchscreen; a strong **manual-control app**
  (aperture/shutter/ISO priority with **real-time preview** behind the sliders). He credits the software
  generously.
- [2012-12-28] **Real-use testing, including a family tester (context)**: "I gave it to my not-so-text-savvy
  sister to take pictures during the recent holiday" (full-auto, indoors) — a real-world test with a
  non-expert user. **His sister is context, not the subject.** Result: ISO "only acceptable up to ~1600";
  no auto-flash, so "shutter speed went down to an eighth/quarter of a second → motion blur indoors."
- [2012-12-28] **A phone kept up with it — the damning comparison**: "the Galaxy Note 2 went shot for shot
  for shot with this camera until we broke out that 21× optical zoom." His own phone matching a dedicated
  camera is the evidence for the weak-image-quality verdict (and continues the camera-replaces-point-and-
  shoot thesis).
- [2012-12-28] **The zoom is the real differentiator**: "the 21× optical zoom is nice — tremendously
  useful, especially outdoors," where phones fall back on digital zoom.
- [2012-12-28] **Names a specific speed annoyance honestly**: switching from photo-taking to photo-viewing
  "took an extra second… I'm sure this can be fixed with a software update." Flags the flaw and its likely
  fix.
- [2012-12-28] **Verdict + a recommendation to wait**: "I have faith in Samsung to get it right on the next
  release. I am absolutely looking forward to the Samsung Galaxy Camera 2, and **that's the one I would
  recommend waiting for.**" A dated instance of telling the audience to skip a first-gen and wait.
