# Google Edition Galaxy S4: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-16)

## Summary
Analysis of the surprise "Nexus experience" (stock-Android) Galaxy S4 announced at Google
I/O 2013. Persona value is in his **incentive-based reasoning** — he'd dismissed the rumor
precisely because he saw "no incentive" for either company — and a structured **two-paths**
read of what it means for the Nexus line. Restates his stock-Android / updates-straight-
from-Google preference.

## Key claims
- [2013-05-16] **Incentive-first reasoning — why he dismissed the rumor**: "I just dismissed
  it… there is no incentive for Samsung to do that, no incentive for Google to do that, why
  would that ever happen? Never going to happen." Reasons from company incentives (and is
  candid that the outcome surprised him). His standing incentive-analysis lens.
- [2013-05-16] **The value proposition — updates straight from Google**: "with stock Android
  you get ~10 GB back [vs TouchWiz eating storage], and updates are managed straight from
  Google — you don't wait for Samsung or the carrier to add their software before pushing. As
  soon as there's a new Android version, you get it. That's the Nexus experience." The
  updates-as-value + stock-Android benefit, concretely.
- [2013-05-16] **A structured two-paths analysis**: lays out the two futures for the Nexus
  line — (1) "giving up on Nexus" (all flagships just get stock Android) which he judges
  **unlikely** because Google uses Nexus as a direction-setting Halo device (on-screen buttons,
  no SD card — the GS4 has neither), vs (2) continuing Nexus proper *plus* adding "Nexus
  experience" phones, which he finds more likely. Reasons from Google's revealed product
  direction.
- [2013-05-16] **Honest about the product's awkward positioning**: "not as solid as the HTC
  One, not as feature-packed as a regular S4, not as cheap as the Nexus 4… it's in its own
  category, competing with nothing. I don't know if anyone's going to buy this — there's not
  much incentive to buy a $650 unlocked phone." A candid who-is-this-for verdict.
- [2013-05-16] **Credits the rumor source — props habit**: "props to Russell Holly" (who
  published the confirmed rumor). His credit-your-sources ethic again.
