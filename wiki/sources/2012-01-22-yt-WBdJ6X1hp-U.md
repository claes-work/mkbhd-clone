# Asus Transformer Prime Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-22)

## Summary
The full review promised in his 01-06 first impressions, sixteen days later — the deferral
honoured, which is itself the point. Build quality, the 600-nit display, the Tegra 3's
fifth companion core, Ice Cream Sandwich, browser performance, gaming, and the two issues
users had been reporting (Wi-Fi strength and GPS lock). Verdict: "the best tablet money can
buy." Notable for addressing **known complaints he did not personally experience**, and for a
joke that shows unusual awareness of how praise reads.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-22] **Superlative verdict**: "overall really, this is the best tablet money can buy" —
  a 32 GB Wi-Fi unit at ~$500, available in grey or gold. Also "probably the best build quality
  of any tablet on the market to date" and "hands down the best Android gaming tablet, I
  promise."
- [2012-01-22] **Display argued with a number and a comparison**: 10.1" 1280×800 Super LCD at
  roughly **600 nits**, "like most computer monitors only go up to 300 or 350 nits — so you can
  read this thing in sunlight unlike some other tablets."
- [2012-01-22] **Tegra 3's fifth core explained mechanically**: a 500 MHz companion core handles
  background work and home-screen swiping, offloading the 1.3 GHz quad core, "and this actually
  saves battery life a lot… because it offsets all the stuff that would normally be on the quad
  core chip for the more demanding stuff."
- [2012-01-22] **Reported issues addressed even though he didn't hit them** — the notable move:
  the GPS problem "was fixed with a recent firmware update," while the Wi-Fi weakness "while
  it's never cut out on me, has always been a tad little bit weaker because of the metal body."
  He reports the community's complaints, gives the mechanism, and separates them from his own
  experience.
- [2012-01-22] **Actionable tip embedded in the review**: enable *force GPU rendering* in
  developer settings to get "liquid smooth performance with the apps that aren't ICS enabled
  yet," applicable to ICS phones too.
- [2012-01-22] **Browser performance reported as genuinely mixed**: the SoC scales down for
  light pages, so heavy sites can feel sluggish until the quad core engages; his workaround is
  keeping multiple tabs open. A hedged verdict rather than a clean win.
- [2012-01-22] **Praise for ICS taken to a self-aware extreme**: "it's like if Google hired me to
  find something wrong with Ice Cream Sandwich, I'd be fired."
- [2012-01-22] **Sponsorship-perception awareness, joked about openly**: after praising Nvidia's
  gaming performance — "it sounds like they paid me to say that. I mean, yeah, they probably
  should have actually." He names how the enthusiasm might read, and denies it by joking.
- [2012-01-22] **Criticism delivered through comedy**: on the speaker placement — "it's like
  someone at Asus thought, hey dude, what's the most inconvenient place we can possibly put a
  speaker? Right where they hold the tablet, right where they put their hand? Okay, that's
  exactly where we'll put it" — followed immediately by the mitigating fact that it sounds good
  and is loud.
- [2012-01-22] **Missing-comparison honesty**: "this is one of the times I wish I still had my
  Xoom, so I could compare and show you guys just how much slower it was" — he names the
  comparison he *can't* make.
