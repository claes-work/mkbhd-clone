# Google Fiber: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-08-07)

## Summary
An "Explained!" entry on the just-announced Google Fiber — what the three plans are, what gigabit
internet actually means, and where you can get it (only Kansas City at launch). Mostly enthusiastic
explainer, notable for a careful **gigabit-vs-gigabyte unit correction** made tangible with download
examples, and for reasoning about which plan is the real sweet spot rather than just listing them.

## Key claims
- [2012-08-07] **The three plans, laid out**: (1) $300 install → free 5/1 Mbps internet "forever"; (2)
  no install fee, $70/month for **gigabit** (1000 Mbps up and down) plus a Fiber router; (3) plan 2 plus
  Google TV with a Nexus 7 as the remote.
- [2012-08-07] **A reasoned pick, not just a list**: "I think plan two is the sweet spot right now. I
  don't think plan three is going to be the most popular option because there's not a lot of TV channels
  [ESPN, HBO missing] — but plan two, a thousand megabits down and especially a thousand megabits up,
  that's awesome." He evaluates and recommends rather than neutrally describing.
- [2012-08-07] **The gigabit-vs-gigabyte correction, made concrete** — a teaching move: "if anyone's
  unfamiliar with gigabits per second, that is not the same as gigabytes per second. One gigabit per
  second is about 125 megabytes per second… that's one gigabyte every eight seconds." Then tangible
  examples: "25 huge songs per second… download enough movies to fill a 64-gig iPad in nine minutes…
  a YouTuber could upload a one-gigabyte video in eight seconds." Bits-vs-bytes literacy taught with
  scale.
- [2012-08-07] **A creator's-eye benefit called out**: "if you're a YouTuber you could upload your
  one-gigabyte video in eight seconds" — framing the spec through his own upload workflow.
- [2012-08-07] **Availability reality check**: "it's only available right now in one city in one country
  in the world — Kansas City" (on the MO/KS border), with a sign-up link to register interest elsewhere.
- [2012-08-07] **Roots for it to spread**: "I'm hoping this is really successful in Kansas City so it's
  able to expand all over the country and internationally" — the same want-good-tech-to-win posture as
  the OLED-TV rant, applied to ISPs (a market he clearly wants disrupted).
- [2012-08-07] **Doesn't own/can't have it** — honest positioning: "why aren't you already involved in
  this? … well, it's only available in Kansas City." He reviews the concept, not personal experience,
  and says so.
