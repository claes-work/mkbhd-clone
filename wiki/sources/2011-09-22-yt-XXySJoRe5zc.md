# Battery Technology Rant (@mkbhd, 2011-09-22)

## Summary
A "mini rant" arguing that **battery technology is the one component failing to keep pace**
with the rest of the smartphone. The argument is built as a two-device spec comparison,
not an assertion: the original Motorola Droid (late 2009) versus the just-announced
Samsung Galaxy Note (2011). Every other spec has moved dramatically — clock speed, cores,
RAM, pixel count, thinness — and battery life has not moved at all. Opens by directly
addressing the viewer ("take your phone out… how much battery life do you have left,
really, be honest") and closes on a demand that is explicitly qualitative, not
quantitative: **better, not bigger**.

## Key claims
- [2011-09-22] The comparison, stated on camera: Motorola Droid — 800 MHz single-core,
  <0.5 GB RAM, 3.7" 854×480 LCD, half an inch thick, ~a day of battery. Galaxy Note —
  1.5 GHz dual-core, 1 GB RAM, 5.3" 1280×800 OLED, 9.6 mm, ~a day of battery. Deltas he
  computes aloud: ~180% faster clock, 2× cores, >2× RAM, ~250% the pixel count, at ~70% of
  the thickness.
- [2011-09-22] **Core position**: bigger batteries are not progress. The Note's 2,500 mAh
  cell is offset by more power-hungry radios, a faster processor, more graphics load and a
  larger screen — "the battery needs to be better, not bigger. Better."
- [2011-09-22] He explicitly credits the Droid's significance from personal experience —
  "it was a groundbreaking and revolutionary device, I would know" — consistent with it
  being his own daily driver at this date (see [[2011-09-27-yt-8W3bJGflOic]]).
- [2011-09-22] **Dated biography marker (self-reported)**: "I'm not an engineer *yet*" —
  places him mid-engineering-track as a student in September 2011, and frames the wish as
  a limit on his own ability to solve it rather than a demand of others.
- [2011-09-22] Format/CTA detail: the "mini rant" is named as a recurring video type, and
  the call to action is a click-to-tweet share link — an early instance of designing the
  share mechanism into an opinion video.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "I want you to take your phone out your pocket or off your desk or whatever and tell me:
> how much battery life do you have left? Really — be honest."

> "You can't deny the facts. Battery technology just isn't keeping up at all."

> "The Droid's battery life: about a day. The Note's battery life: about a day."

> "I wish — and I only wish, because I'm not an engineer yet — but I sincerely wish that we
> could come up with some amazing new groundbreaking battery technology the way we are in
> other sorts of components in phones."

> "The point is: the battery needs to be better, not bigger. Better."
