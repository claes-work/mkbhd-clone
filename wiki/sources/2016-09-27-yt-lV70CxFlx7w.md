# Samsung Galaxy Note 7 Review! (@mkbhd, 2016-09-27)

## Summary
The full Note 7 review, delivered mid-recall — "the design refresh, some camera improvements, a bunch of
new software features, and **explosions**." He praises it as arguably the best phone Samsung makes (best
display + best camera) while addressing "the elephant in the room" head-on: the battery recall. This is
the **first-recall** stance ("get the green-battery replacement and you're set") — which his own follow-up
video overturns days later, a resolved contradiction (see Notes).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-09-27] **Addresses "the elephant in the room" directly.** "No matter how good a phone is, if a
  hundred of them explode within a month, that's pretty bad — the main issue was battery overheating while
  charging. Samsung offered a voluntary recall." His honesty-first handling of the defining story.
- [2016-09-27] **The (soon-overturned) green-battery verdict.** "Assuming we're talking about the new
  Note 7s with the green battery indicator, this is the best phone Samsung makes right now, alongside the
  S7 Edge. Mine never exploded, but I returned it — if you have an old one you should too, get a new one."
  ⚠️ This "the replacements are safe" position is reversed in [[2016-10-12-yt-b-Bux7YrzxY]].
- [2016-09-27] **"The best display in a smartphone," stated flatly.** "This is still what Samsung does
  best in the business — other phones have great displays, but none match this in vividness, brightness,
  sharpness and color. Hands-down the best display in a smartphone." A durable, oft-repeated verdict.
- [2016-09-27] **"The best camera in any smartphone" (a camera that encourages you to shoot).** Same 12
  MP f/1.7 OIS as the S7: "when it came out I'd say absolutely the best camera in any smartphone; the
  iPhone 7 gets close. It's one of those cameras that encourages me to take a lot of photos — fast to open,
  fast to shoot, so it feels easy to take lots of great photos." The confidence/ease criterion again.
- [2016-09-27] **The Note = the power-user's phone.** "The Note has always been popular with power users
  like me — not just big, but packed to the gills with the best features Samsung offers (S Pen, multi-
  window). Samsung's done a good job keeping the features without the software feeling overwhelming —
  they're waiting around the corner, not screaming in your face." Self-identification as a power user.
- [2016-09-27] **Battery is the weak point (the Note lost its identity).** "It has a *smaller* battery
  than the S7 Edge despite being bigger, because of the stylus slot — battery life is really just about
  average. The Note used to be THE phone everyone took to CES and on road trips because of a massive
  removable battery; both of those are no longer the case." A pointed note on what the line gave up.
- [2016-09-27] **Fast charging is underrated + waterproofing respect.** "The best fast-charging phones
  are life-savers to me — I think it's underrated." Praises the ip67 waterproofing done without ugly flaps
  ("people don't appreciate how hard it is to water-seal from the inside without affecting the outside"),
  with the usual speaker caveat.
- [2016-09-27] **The all-black preference restated.** "The all-black is definitely my favorite look — I'm
  a fan of the all-black look on pretty much any smartphone." Iris scanner = "seems straight out of the
  future… learned my eyes over time," but "I still prefer a fingerprint reader."
