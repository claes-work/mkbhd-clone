# The Bezel-less Smartphone: Xiaomi Mi Mix! (@mkbhd, 2016-10-26)

## Summary
An enthusiastic hands-on with the Xiaomi Mi Mix — "the new world's most interesting phone in my opinion…
my god, take a look at that future." The clone-relevant value is a full-throated statement of a core
value: **loving companies that take crazy design gambles**, as a direct rebuttal to "smartphones are
boring."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-10-26] **"I love seeing companies go all in on crazy unique gambles like this."** "People keep
  saying smartphones are boring — well sure, we've technically seen this before (the Sharp Aquos Crystal),
  but this takes it to the next level, and I don't care. It looks like the future." His pro-risk,
  anti-"boring" design value, stated plainly. (Recurs against the iPhone-clone default.)
- [2016-10-26] **Why the big swing matters — an unknown company doing the opposite of safe.** "The easy
  route for a company you've never heard of is to build something close to what's already familiar —
  that's why you see so many iPhone clones. But this crazy gamble of a phone, actually meant to be sold?
  I love it. If you said smartphone design is getting boring, this one's for you." Celebrates courage over
  imitation.
- [2016-10-26] **Reads the bezel-less engineering (teach-the-mechanism).** 91.3% screen-to-body; to hit
  it, "the proximity sensor is replaced by an ultrasound sensor, and the earpiece is a piezoelectric
  speaker that vibrates the metal frame to make sound." Explains the trade-offs enabling the design.
- [2016-10-26] **Honest cons: durability + slipperiness.** "Polished ceramic body — about the slipperiest
  phone of all time; drop it without a case and it doesn't stand a chance, and there's only the one case
  it ships with, no skins." Balances the enthusiasm with the real risks.
- [2016-10-26] **"I'm glad it exists."** "It's not even available in the US, but I hope it does well
  enough in China that they bring it over. I'm more than happy to share this kind of stuff with the
  world." Frames championing bold concepts as part of his job.
