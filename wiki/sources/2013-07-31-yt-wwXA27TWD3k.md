# Top 5 Android 4.3 Features! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-31)

## Summary
A rundown of Android 4.3's (mostly under-the-hood) improvements, shown on the 2013 Nexus 7.
Persona value is in his **teach-the-underlying-tech** register (explaining TRIM, OpenGL
3.0, Bluetooth LE) and his defense of **incremental "under-the-hood" updates** as
cumulatively important — a callback to the rolling-release thesis.

## Key claims
- [2013-07-31] **Defends incremental updates — the framing**: "after my 'Google is taking back
  Android' video, people think 4.3 must be a super-minor update, mostly under-the-hood — but an
  improvement is still an improvement… under-the-hood improvements and minor OS updates actually
  contribute a lot to how much Android has grown; without them we'd still be back in the days of
  Gingerbread or Froyo." Values the accumulation of small gains (cf. the rolling-release piece
  [[2013-06-07-yt-H9HH0vqb4q8]]).
- [2013-07-31] **Teach-the-tech — TRIM explained**: "#1 is TRIM — it keeps onboard storage clean.
  Every install creates a data point; the OS builds an index to track them, and without TRIM
  there's no way to clean up the index when you delete something, so it gets garbagey. 4.3 lets
  the OS erase the garbage, leaving the device feeling newer and faster — grab the update on an
  old Nexus 7 and it's like getting a new tablet." His explain-the-mechanism register.
- [2013-07-31] **Corrects a common misnomer**: "'Bluetooth Low Energy' is a bit of a misnomer —
  it doesn't refer to the energy consumed, but to the type of devices you connect to [wearables,
  fitness bands, Google Glass]." His precision habit.
- [2013-07-31] **Notes what he personally won't use, honestly**: on restricted profiles, "I
  probably won't use this much because I'm the only one who uses my tablet, but if you share it,
  it's very useful." Separates his use from the general recommendation.
