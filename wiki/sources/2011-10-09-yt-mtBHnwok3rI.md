# What if Phones Were Like Desktop Computers? (@mkbhd, 2011-10-09)

## Summary
A short thought-experiment video, explicitly cross-posted from a Google+ post he'd written
weeks earlier: **what if you could spec a smartphone the way you spec a desktop PC** —
walk into a store, pick a chassis, pick components, pick the OS, and assemble your own.
He works the idea through concrete 2011 examples (an iPhone 4 with the antenna moved; a
Galaxy S2 with a Retina display dropped in), then answers his own question with his
personal dream build and turns it back on the audience. Notable as an early instance of a
recurring MKBHD move: pose a hypothetical the industry isn't offering, then reason about
why people would or wouldn't want it.

## Key claims
- [2011-10-09] The core hypothetical: phones built like PCs — "you start from the chassis,
  you pick the body, you pick all the internal components and then you pick Windows and
  then you put it all together and it's your dream computer. If you could do this with a
  smartphone…" — i.e. a modular/bespoke phone years before the Project Ara era.
- [2011-10-09] **His stated dream build (dated personal preference)**: the **Nexus One's
  exterior**, the **Galaxy S2's screen**, **stock Android — explicitly not TouchWiz**, a
  1.5 GHz processor instead of 1.2 GHz, and 1 GB of RAM: "I just load up on all the specs
  and go for the max and pay whatever I have to pay for that."
- [2011-10-09] **Dated stock-Android preference stated as a personal default** — the
  skin-vs-stock position that recurs throughout his Android coverage, here in its
  simplest form.
- [2011-10-09] He explicitly bounds the idea's appeal rather than universalizing it: "I
  know not everyone would want this," and maps the split onto the PC analogy —
  iPhone buyers are like all-in-one buyers who take what's on the shelf, and that's
  treated as a legitimate preference, not a failing.
- [2011-10-09] **Cross-platform workflow, dated**: the idea originated as a Google+ post
  and was then adapted for YouTube — evidence of Google+ functioning as his
  idea-scratchpad in 2011, with the video as the second surface.
- [2011-10-09] Ends on an open audience prompt (what would you spec?), continuing the
  request/vote-driven engagement pattern of the era.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "You know what I like? I like phones. And you know what else I like? I like computers.
> I'm sure we probably share some of that in common."

> "What if you could just walk into a store — like you could walk into a computer store —
> and just start picking out components and put them all together and put Android on it?
> That would be one of the coolest things ever."

> "I would have the Nexus One's outside with the Galaxy S2 screen, and it would be running
> stock Android — not the TouchWiz interface. And I'd also have the 1.5 GHz processor in
> there instead of the 1.2."
