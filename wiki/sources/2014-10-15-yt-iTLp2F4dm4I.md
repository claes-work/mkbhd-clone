# Nexus 6 & Nexus 9 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-15)

## Summary
A round-up of the officially-announced Nexus 6, Nexus 9, and **Android 5.0 Lollipop**. Marques is most
enthusiastic about the **Nexus 9** ("the most impressive Android tablet I've ever seen" — HTC-built, metal,
BoomSound front-facing speakers), curious-but-wary about the giant **Nexus 6** (a "Moto X blown up"), and
carries two recurring concerns forward: **stock-Android/Motorola camera image processing**, and his
preference for **front-facing speakers**. Also a nice practical framing: the 6" Nexus 6 is the same
vertical height as the 5.5" iPhone 6 Plus.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-15] **Android 5.0 Lollipop is final** — rolling to Nexus/GPe devices (his Nexus 5 + GPe M8):
  full Material Design, a battery-saver mode (+~90 min), multiple-account/guest profiles on phones, and
  Smart Lock (trusted Bluetooth devices skip the password).
- [2014-10-15] **Nexus 9 = the standout.** HTC-built, 8.9" 2048×1536, dual-core 64-bit Tegra K1, metal
  sides, **BoomSound front-facing speakers** ("thank goodness HTC kept that"), a first-party keyboard case.
  "The most impressive Android tablet I've ever seen." He's fine with it replacing the Nexus 10 because it
  keeps (even improves) the front-facing speakers he values. From $399.
- [2014-10-15] **Nexus 6 = a Moto X blown up.** 6.0" QHD AMOLED, Snapdragon 805, 3 GB, Adreno 420 — "top
  of the line," thick (10–12 mm), which he frames as a *virtue*: room for a **3200 mAh battery and a 13 MP
  OIS camera**. Practical size note: at 159 mm tall it's the **same height as the 5.5" iPhone 6 Plus**.
- [2014-10-15] **Recurring camera concern.** Because it's a Motorola-optics camera and "cameras/image
  processing have never been a high point on Nexuses / Android," he's cautious — "but because it's stock
  Android it'll likely be a little different; we'll see." (Ties to the Nexus wishlist's software-not-
  hardware camera critique.)
