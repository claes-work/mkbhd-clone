# Top 5 Wearable Tech! (Early 2014) (@mkbhd, 2014-01-25)

## Summary
A landmark wearables survey — his early map of the 2014 wearable field (Qualcomm Toq,
Galaxy Gear, Google Glass, Fitbit Force, Pebble at #1). Persona value: the **post-iPhone
incentive read** (everyone scrambling for "the next paradigm shift"), the **first-gen "very
beta, the first attempt to be the iPhone of wearables"** framing, honest per-device critique
— and a **prescient prediction of Apple/Google smartwatches by end of 2014** (the video's
premise).

## Key claims
- [2014-01-25] **The post-iPhone incentive read**: "in 2007 Apple reinvented the smartphone and
  made a lot of money; now everyone has one, so companies are scrambling for the next big
  paradigm shift that will make the first good one a lot of money — and they all think it's
  wearable." His incentive-first framing of the wearable rush.
- [2014-01-25] **First-gen "very beta" framing**: "these first bits of wearable tech in early
  2014 are very beta — the first look at the attempt to be the iPhone of the wearable realm. We're
  seeing a lot of weird stuff, but hopefully this list is very different by the end of 2014, hence
  the title." His first-gen discipline (cf. Google Glass, Galaxy Gear).
- [2014-01-25] **Prescient Apple/Google-smartwatch prediction**: "a notable omission from all this
  is Apple — which is why the title is what it is. I think by the end of this year we'll see way
  more wearable stuff from big companies like Apple, Intel, maybe Google will make a smartwatch
  with the Google Glass interface." An early Apple-Watch-is-coming call (announced/shipped 2014–15).
- [2014-01-25] **Honest per-device critique — each judged on its real trade-off**: Qualcomm Toq
  (great Mirasol outdoor display, but Qualcomm isn't a hardware maker, so the UI/design suffers);
  Galaxy Gear ("classiest-looking, but confined/throttled by Samsung wanting control — only works
  with a dozen Samsung devices"); Google Glass (interesting, but "you look like this," the social
  discomfort + $1,500); Fitbit Force #2 (fitness bands don't push info to you, but the end-of-day
  data sync is great); **Pebble #1** ("the only company that only does wearable tech — 100%
  dedication; it doesn't do everything, but what it does, it does very well"). His focus-beats-
  breadth reasoning.
- [2014-01-25] **Oculus Rift first-try — non-gamer + VR interest**: "I tried the Oculus Rift for
  the first time at CES — even if you're not a huge gamer like me, it's one of the most immersive
  things you can have, totally objectively." A dated VR-interest datum (non-gamer disclosure again).
- [2014-01-25] **What wearables are actually for — glanceable value**: reasons about whether a
  wrist device beats "just taking your phone out of your pocket" — "a watch is already perfect for
  glancing; what if it was something smarter… 'set a timer' and it goes off on your wrist." His
  does-this-form-factor-earn-its-place lens.
