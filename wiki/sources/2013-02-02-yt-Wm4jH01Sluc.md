# Hackintosh Pro Project! [Part 5: The Finale!] (@mkbhd, 2013-02-02)

## Summary
The finale of the Hackintosh Pro series — benchmarks, the Mac-Pro-vs-hack-Pro verdict,
and the single most **complete on-camera tour of his early-2013 desk/workstation** to
date. High persona value: it locks down the **definitive 2013 editing rig and full
desk/audio/display setup**, states his **price-per-spec + upgradeability + I/O** case for
building over buying, and adds several concrete gear and workflow data (three-space
Premiere/After Effects mission-control workflow, dual-display arrangement, audio chain).
The **★★ landmark** of the workstation thread.

## Key claims
- [2013-02-02] **The definitive 2013 workstation, on camera** — core: Intel i7-3930K
  6-core/12-thread **overclocked to 4.35 GHz** (benchmarked at 4.2), ASUS X79 Sabertooth,
  Gigabyte GTX 670 WindForce OC 2 GB, Corsair H80i (Noctua NF-P12 fan swap), **3× OCZ
  Vertex 4 128 GB SSDs** — two in a **RAID 0 striped array for macOS**, one dedicated to
  **Windows 8** (dual-boot), 32 GB RAM, Fractal Define R4. Benchmarks: Geekbench 2 ~15.8k
  @4.2 (low-17k @4.35), Novabench >1,600, ~700 MB/s RAID reads.
- [2013-02-02] **The full desk setup, itemized (dated)** — **displays**: Dell UltraSharp
  3007WFP (30", 2560×1600) + a **20" portrait display (1600×1200)** matched by height so
  windows don't resize when moved; **audio**: FiiO E10 amp/DAC → iK Audio studio monitors
  + Audio-Technica ATH-M50 headphones; **input**: Apple wired keyboard ("so I can throw it
  into my BIOS to overclock"), Bluetooth Magic Trackpad ("I would never use a Magic Mouse,
  just no"), Cyborg R.A.T. 9 mouse. Pins his early-2013 workstation definitively.
- [2013-02-02] **His mission-control editing workflow — a concrete production habit**:
  "three spaces — desktop one for normal stuff, desktop two for Premiere, desktop three
  for After Effects — I swipe between these… download from the internet, swipe up into
  Premiere, then into After Effects, so the whole project cycle works." How he physically
  organizes an edit (dated).
- [2013-02-02] **The build-vs-buy case, fully argued** — four advantages over a Mac Pro:
  **(1) upgradeability** ("you can change your power supply, RAM, CPU, motherboard —
  whatever you put in that hack Pro you can switch out"), **(2) I/O** ("way more USB,
  ethernet, FireWire… the Mac Pro doesn't even have Thunderbolt yet"), **(3) graphics
  freedom** ("the Mac Pro has outdated graphics — the 5870 is more than a year old… you
  get to pick exactly what card you want"), and **(4) price-per-spec** ("the equivalent
  Mac Pro is more than $1,000 more… you get way more for your money — that is the number
  one reason people build a Hackintosh"). His value/upgradeability doctrine at full length.
- [2013-02-02] **Even-handed — concedes the Mac Pro's strength**: "processing power —
  you could say that's a strength of the Mac Pro… you can throw dual Xeons in and it'll
  crush benchmarks, 18,000–19,000 on Geekbench without breaking a sweat." Names where the
  thing he chose against wins (his standing even-handedness).
- [2013-02-02] **Legally honest about the gray area**: "is it legal to make this run OS
  X? Well, technically no — Apple does not want you to do this at all. I'm not responsible
  for anything you may do." Straight about the EULA violation rather than glossing it.
- [2013-02-02] **"Legos for adults" — the satisfaction of building**: "the satisfaction
  of building it yourself is kind of like Legos for adults, as Dave Dugdale has said…
  picking all your own parts and getting it together is really fun." A dated statement of
  why he enjoys the craft (credits Dave Dugdale for the phrase).
- [2013-02-02] **Silence-for-recording restated**: "if you're video editing and audio
  recording at the same time, you don't want the fans kicking in… so we went with the
  insulated R4 case and a Noctua fan." Production-driven quietness again.
