# HD Tutorial: Youtube Tags (@mkbhd, 2009-09-12)

## Summary
A **YouTube-platform mechanics tutorial** covering special description tags that alter
video playback: `yt:crop=16:9`, `yt:stretch=16:9`, `yt:stretch=4:3`, and — the one he
frames as most important — `yt:quality=high` (equivalent to appending `&fmt=18` to the
URL, forces high-quality streaming by default). Explicitly puts the last one to an
audience vote: since he produces everything in HD but many viewers' connections can't
handle it, he asks whether he should tag all future videos to auto-stream in HQ by
default, and applies the tag to this video as a live test case.

## Key claims
- [2009-09-12] **Full tag inventory demoed**: `yt:crop=16:9` (crops to widescreen,
  loses top/bottom), `yt:stretch=16:9` (distorts 4:3 to fill widescreen — flags his own
  past mistake using this on "MKBHD Update 2.3," where it stretched his square
  watermark into a rectangle), `yt:stretch=4:3` (fixes wrong-aspect-ratio 720×480
  content), and `yt:quality=high`.
- [2009-09-12] **Production-standard decision point, put to a vote**: states he
  produces "all of my videos, all of my tutorials and hardware unboxings and reviews in
  high definition," but acknowledges many viewers' connections can't handle it, and
  explicitly asks the audience whether he should force HQ-by-default across all future
  videos — a dated instance of a production/distribution tradeoff decision being
  crowdsourced rather than made unilaterally.
- [2009-09-12] **Self-correction disclosed on camera**: names his own past tagging
  mistake (`yt:stretch` misapplied to Update 2.3, distorting his watermark) as a
  cautionary example inside the tutorial — continuing the pattern of admitting and
  correcting his own past production errors in public.
