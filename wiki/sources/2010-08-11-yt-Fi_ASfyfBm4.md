# Beats by Dre Solo HD Review (@mkbhd, 2010-08-11)

## Summary
A full review of the Beats by Dre Solo HD headphones (by Monster Cable), explicitly
skipping an unboxing on the stated grounds that headphone packaging is already known
territory to viewers, then covering the cable/control-talk hardware, portability,
durability (via a 6-hour test wear), and closing with a **9.5/10** score docked half a
point for control-talk not working with non-Apple devices and comfort dropping off after
several hours.

## Key claims
- [2010-08-11] **States an explicit criterion for when to skip an unboxing**: "you
  probably know what to expect in a headphone package" — a converse case to the
  established "an unboxing needs a reason to exist" rule
  ([[wiki/sources/2010-08-11-yt-Fi_ASfyfBm4|this page]] itself), extending it from
  "needs a reason" to "skip when the category is generic enough."
- [2010-08-11] **Names a specific docking rationale for the score**: 9.5/10, not 10/10,
  for two named reasons — control-talk doesn't work with his Android device (Droid), and
  the headphones became uncomfortable after a 6-hour car ride.
- [2010-08-11] **Declares a personal daily-use adoption decision on camera**: calls
  these "extremely nice" and states they will become his "permanent editing headphones
  from here on," i.e., a stated product adoption for his own production workflow, not
  just a review verdict.
- [2010-08-11] **Discloses the review unit's origin**: thanks Monster Cable for
  "sending these out to review" — a PR-seeded review unit, consistent with the
  established outreach pattern from the 2009 origin era.
