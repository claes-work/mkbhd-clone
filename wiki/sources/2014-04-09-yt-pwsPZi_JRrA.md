# HTC One M8 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-04-09)

## Summary
The full HTC One M8 review — "one of the best phones you can buy right now." Reinforcing,
but a clean statement of **design-as-a-reason-to-buy** (when flagship specs are all similar,
how a phone feels in the hand becomes the differentiator), plus his honest UltraPixel/duo-
camera assessment and a confident no-reservations recommend.

## Key claims
- [2014-04-09] **Design-as-a-reason-to-buy — the thesis**: "this is one of the best phones you can
  buy — go in a store, try it out, you're probably going to like holding it, and that's a good
  reason to buy it by itself, because the specs are so similar to other flagships." When specs
  converge, the hand-feel/design is the deciding factor (a distinct, dated value framing).
- [2014-04-09] **BoomSound + build quality restated**: the all-metal rounded unibody and front-
  facing stereo speakers remain the identity features (cf. the impressions + the original One).
- [2014-04-09] **Honest UltraPixel/duo-camera verdict**: "even if you aren't a huge fan of the
  camera, it takes good-enough shots that this is probably one of the best phones you can buy." The
  duo-camera refocus is a gimmick that doesn't fully work; he weights it honestly against the whole
  package.
- [2014-04-09] **Credits the auto-brightness — a details note**: "I'm happy with the auto-
  brightness — outside it turns all the way up, inside down to a comfortable level. I usually don't
  even have to talk about that." His attention to under-discussed details.
- [2014-04-09] **No-reservations recommend, scoped**: "I've enjoyed the hell out of using it… I
  don't have any reservations about this phone. It's one of the best phones you can buy right now."
  A confident verdict.
- [2014-04-09] **Flags the Google Play edition as a separate review**: "I have a lot to say about
  the Google Play edition of this phone — that's probably going to be an entire separate review
  video." His stock-Android interest (the GPe gets its own coverage).
