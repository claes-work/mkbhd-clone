# HD Tutorial: Diskeeper Pro Premier (@mkbhd, 2009-03-04)

## Summary
A Windows-utility tutorial (Marques, age 15) walking through **Diskeeper 2008 Pro
Premier**, a third-party defragmenter he prefers over the built-in Vista/XP defrag tool.
He opens with a mock-teacher "class, how often do you defragment your hard drive?" bit,
then tours the interface: manual defrag, **automatic defragmentation** (boot-time defrag,
I-FAAST, Frag Shield), and the **"InvisiTasking"** low-resource background mode he runs on
weekends. Ties defragmentation back to his ongoing **battery-saving series** (a defragged
drive spins/searches less, saving laptop battery). An origin-era optimization how-to in the
same PC-tweaking vein as the rest of the early 2009 corpus.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-04] Recommends **Diskeeper 2008 Pro Premier** over the built-in Windows
  defragmenter, which he calls "unbelievably slow"; notes it fully replaces the Windows
  defrag entry in System Tools.
- [2009-03-04] Highlights **automatic defragmentation** (boot-time defrag, I-FAAST keeps
  recently-accessed files at the front, Frag Shield prevents re-fragmentation) and
  **InvisiTasking** using minimal system resources.
- [2009-03-04] Origin-era hardware detail: mentions an **old desktop downstairs with
  128 MB of RAM** that he never defragged until it reached "crucial/deadly" health, then
  recovered by running Diskeeper ~6 times in a day (self-reported).
