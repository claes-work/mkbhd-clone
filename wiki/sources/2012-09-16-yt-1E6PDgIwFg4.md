# The Super-Retina Display Smartphone! (@mkbhd, 2012-09-16)

## Summary
A spotlight on **Oppo** — "a phone manufacturer based in China… doing amazing things right now that
almost no one is talking about" — and its just-announced Oppo Find 5, the **world's first 1080p
smartphone**. Notable on two fronts: it's his pixel/display obsession applied to the highest-density
phone yet ("the first one that I've actually thought I really want to get"), and it's an early,
dated instance of him **championing an under-the-radar innovator** — the same Oppo that, more than a
decade later, the wiki documents as the company that finally broke iPhone camera dominance.

## Key claims
- [2012-09-16] **Oppo introduced as an overlooked innovator** — the framing: "they are doing amazing
  things right now that almost no one is talking about." He first credits the Oppo Finder as "actually
  the thinnest smartphone in the world" (6.65 mm, vs Droid Razr 7.1, iPhone 5 ~7.6) and "the world's
  thinnest quad-core smartphone" — establishing Oppo as "not a one-hit wonder."
- [2012-09-16] **The Oppo Find 5 = world's first 1080p phone**, argued with density math: a 5" 441 PPI
  display vs the iPhone 5's 4" 326 PPI — "35% higher pixel density… 2.85 times the number of pixels on
  the iPhone 5 screen." The pixel-density framework from [[2012-02-26-yt-pzGsUp3yM8w]] applied to make
  the leap concrete.
- [2012-09-16] **The first phone he says he genuinely wants**: "this 1080p smartphone is the first one
  that I've actually thought that I actually really want to get." A dated, personal want.
- [2012-09-16] **Rooting for the innovator to be noticed — the values note**: "I have high hopes for
  phones like this and for companies like this to be noticed when they're really innovating, when some
  other companies aren't doing exactly the same thing… I really hope this project gets some attention,
  seriously, worldwide." Wanting a small, genuinely-innovating company to get recognition — the
  root-for-good-tech posture applied to an underdog OEM.
- [2012-09-16] **Handles the "you can't tell the difference" objection**: "a lot of people will say your
  eyes won't be able to tell the difference between a 1080p smartphone and one slightly less — but you'll
  definitely be able to tell which one is the better-looking phone when you put them side by side." (A
  softer line than his own pentile-doesn't-matter and viewing-distance arguments — worth noting the
  tension.)
- [2012-09-16] **Realistic about availability**: "based in China, so obviously I'm not expecting it to be
  immediately readily available worldwide or in the US… no date or pricing yet." Excited but grounded.
- [2012-09-16] Specs recited: Android 4.1, quad-core Snapdragon S4, 2 GB RAM, 2500 mAh, expandable
  storage, 12 MP camera.
