# RED Hydrogen One Smartphone: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2017-07-10)

## Summary
An "Explained" decode of the out-of-nowhere RED Hydrogen One — a $1,200 "holographic" Android phone from
**RED, the company whose cameras he shoots on**. Uniquely personal: "this is my two worlds colliding… if
there's any target demographic, it's me — so I bought both." Balances genuine excitement with skepticism
about the vague "holographic" marketing.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-07-03] **"My two worlds colliding" — and he bought both.** "RED — yes, the RED whose cameras I shoot
  these videos on — is stepping into smartphones, where there are massive players (Samsung, Apple) and supply
  chains. If there's any target demographic for a RED phone, it's probably me. So yeah — I bought both (the
  $1,200 aluminum and the $1,600 titanium)." A rare owns-the-bias-and-buys-in beat, tied to his RED loyalty.
- [2017-07-10] **Skeptical of the "holographic" jargon.** "There's only one dark render and a PDF of jargon
  to decode — 'the world's first holographic media machine.' I got the visual of an advanced glasses-free 3D
  experience; **not sure they're using 'holographic' correctly.** Remember the Amazon Fire Phone (2014)? Kind
  of along those lines, but four years later and four years better." Source-critical about the marketing while
  reserving judgment.
- [2017-07-10] **Reads the phone from the render (teach-the-mechanism).** "USB-C, a headphone jack (nice), a
  heat-sink-looking back (this'll be thick), grippy sides, a microSD door, and **pins on the back like the
  Moto Z** — which alludes to modularity. RED's cameras are already super-modular via pins; the Hydrogen plans
  to be its own expanding modular system." Connects it to RED's (expensive, proprietary) modular ecosystem.
- [2017-07-10] **The RED-controls-the-accessory-market caveat.** "RED's modular camera system is awesome —
  build the camera up exactly as much as you need — but it's expensive, because RED makes all the accessories
  and controls the price (who else makes RED accessories?)." A follow-the-incentives note on proprietary
  ecosystems, applied to a brand he loves.
- [2017-07-10] **Cautious optimism on the audio + display promises.** Multi-dimensional audio (likely front-
  facing, BoomSound-style — "hoping it lives up to that"); a "5.7" professional hydrogen holographic display"
  = "the biggest question mark of them all." Reserves the verdict for a real hands-on.
