# What's on my Tech: 2014! (Back to School) (@mkbhd, 2014-08-24)

## Summary
A **"what's on my tech"** back-to-school walkthrough of his daily phone, tablet, and computer setups — a
useful **biography / production / creator-workflow** datum. Confirms his late-summer-2014 daily driver
(the OnePlus One, still on CyanogenMod), his tablet (iPad mini Retina), and his edit rig (the 2013 Mac
Pro + two 4K displays, editing in **Adobe Premiere Pro "95% of the time"**). Also a dated student anchor
("back to school for me… I'm about to be really freaking busy").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-08-24] **Daily driver: the OnePlus One** (Sandstone Black, 64 GB) — "I'm still supporting
  CyanogenMod as a company and I still love what they've done," despite the recent OnePlus-company
  controversy. Keeps a deliberately simple home screen (two screens, one widget, near-stock via the
  Google Now launcher).
- [2014-08-24] **Phone apps that recur in his workflow:** Google Tasks (class to-do list), Google
  Calendar (Today widget), Spotify + Google Play Music, Package Buddy, ACU Weather (minute-by-minute rain),
  Audible (textbooks), Feedly (RSS), Uber, the YouTube Studio app. Notes he **won't post to Instagram
  from Android** ("if I'm going to post them, I'm not going to use an Android device, unfortunately").
- [2014-08-24] **Waiting on the Moto 360** — "Android Wear, which I'm going to be hopefully pairing with
  a Moto 360 any day now."
- [2014-08-24] **Tablet: 128 GB Wi-Fi iPad mini Retina** (still on iOS 7) — mostly for media consumption
  (Feedly, Pocket, YouTube), VSCO for photo editing, the Philips Hue app for his studio lights. "I don't
  use Chrome as much because Chrome just isn't that good yet on the iPad."
- [2014-08-24] **Computer: the 2013 Mac Pro + two 4K displays** (from the Setup Tour), plus the 13"
  Retina MacBook Pro, both on OS X Mavericks (planning to upgrade to Yosemite). Dock/workflow: Chrome over
  Safari, **Adobe Premiere Pro CC 2014 (95% of editing)**, After Effects "when I'm feeling fancier," Red
  Giant plugins, Photoshop/Lightroom, Audacity, an 8 TB RAID array for storage, iStat Menus.
