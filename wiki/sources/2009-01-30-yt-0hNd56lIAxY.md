# Tutorial: Camstudio HQ (@mkbhd, 2009-01-30)

## Summary
An origin-era how-to from age-15 Marques on **recording higher-quality screen videos in CamStudio**,
the free screen-recording software (from camstudio.org) he used to make his early tech tutorials. He
walks the settings: install the **DivX codec** (from divx.com — install "about 100 megs" but you only
need the ~7 MB codec), then in CamStudio's video options select the **DivX 6.8.7 codec** (which "uses
both your CPUs"), set quality to **100%**, auto-adjust to **30 frames per second**, choose the **720
HD profile** at **3,000 kbps**, and set a fixed capture region (his screen is only 640×360, so he
records at 640×360). He recommends enabling **pan** so the capture follows the mouse if you're
recording a region smaller than your full screen. It's the practical companion to his "Camstudio
Clarity" clarification and one of the earliest documented records of Marques's own video-production
workflow.

## Key claims
- [2009-01-30] **CamStudio** is free screen-recording freeware (from **camstudio.org**) — "no need to
  torrent" — and is how he records his tutorials.
- [2009-01-30] For HQ output, install the **DivX codec** from **divx.com** (the installer bundles
  ~100 MB of software but you only need the ~7 MB codec), then pick the **DivX 6.8.7 codec** in
  CamStudio's video options — it "uses both your CPUs."
- [2009-01-30] Recommended capture settings: **100% quality**, auto-adjust to **30 fps**, the **720
  HD profile**, and a bitrate of **3,000 kbps** ("all the way to 3,000 kilobits per second").
- [2009-01-30] Use a **fixed region** to set capture resolution; his own screen resolution is
  **640×360**, so he records at 640×360 (he flashes a list of selectable resolutions to pause on).
- [2009-01-30] If capturing a region smaller than the full screen, **enable "pan"** so the recording
  follows the mouse around the screen — "simple, but it'll give you HD quality video."
- [2009-01-30] Caveat: audio "sometimes gets a little bit off, but that's okay 'cause... it's YouTube."
