# Self Driving Mercedes: Behind the Wheel! (@mkbhd, 2017-01-05)

## Summary
At CES 2017, Mercedes invited him to try their Drive Pilot self-driving system (in a modified E-Class) —
"Mercedes saw what I was doing with all the car videos with Tesla." A hands-on autonomy comparison rooted
in his Tesla ownership, and a source-critical demonstration that the tech "is clearly still evolving —
level two, not level five."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-01-05] **The industry read: everyone's doing autonomy, not just Tesla.** "A lot of people forget
  that other manufacturers are working on self-driving in their own way." Positions Tesla as the famous one
  but not the only one — his even-handed, whole-field lens.
- [2017-01-05] **The key difference: Mercedes adds map data (HERE Maps) to sensors+cameras.** "Tesla uses
  just the sensors and cameras; Mercedes adds map data to augment them" — which enables **map-based
  autonomous turns** (put the blinker on, the car slows, turns, and accelerates back to the limit). A
  concrete teach-the-mechanism comparison.
- [2017-01-05] **The hands-on-wheel difference.** "Tesla is more lax — it reminds you every ~30 seconds;
  Mercedes wants your hands ready to take over, confirming every ~10 seconds via touch pads on the wheel."
  Notes the design trade-off in how each keeps the driver engaged.
- [2017-01-05] **Source-critical: the map dependency fails in the demo.** "GPS isn't perfect — maps aren't
  perfect (we've all seen Apple Maps). I put the blinker on for a turn, but that road wasn't on the map, so
  it just kept going and I had to take control." Shows the limitation honestly rather than only the polished
  case. "It's the little things — clearly still evolving; level two, not level five."
- [2017-01-05] **The real question: trust.** "Will people trust it? Tech enthusiasts like me who trust
  computers are totally fine with it — but would you?" Frames autonomy adoption as a trust/psychology
  problem, not just a tech one.
