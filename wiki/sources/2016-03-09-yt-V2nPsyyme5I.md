# Samsung Galaxy S7 Edge: Biggest Flaws! (@mkbhd, 2016-03-09)

## Summary
The "biggest flaws" companion piece to the S7 Edge review — a format where he nitpicks an
"otherwise amazing phone" precisely *because* it's so good ("a highlight of how good these
phones are getting; the tiny flaws stand out so much because of how good this is"). Cites and
links a Droid Life post as the jumping-off point. The flaws split hardware (fingerprints,
slipperiness, keyboard letters running to the curved edge) vs. software (Samsung's backwards
nav-bar order, the disordered app drawer, TouchWiz redundant-app bloat).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-03-09] **"The best phone out right now" framing.** "Pretty much everyone is positive
  about it; it's an improvement in almost every way over last year's already pretty great S6 —
  bigger battery, better camera, expandable storage, waterproof." The flaws critique is
  affectionate, not a buy/don't-buy verdict.
- [2016-03-09] **The slipperiest/fingerprintiest phone ever.** "Definitely in a tie with some of
  Samsung's other phones for the smudges, fingerprintiest, most reflective, slipperiest phone
  ever" — he'll put a **skin** on it before he drops it. (Recurring anti-glass-slipperiness +
  skins-over-cases thread.)
- [2016-03-09] **Curved-edge keyboard flaw.** On the Edge the display is so wide that keyboard
  letters run all the way to the very edge, so it "still rocks a bit" if you type with the phone
  flat on a table — "super nitpicking."
- [2016-03-09] **Samsung's backwards nav bar (a repeat pet peeve).** "I thought Android had
  established this a while now — multitasking on the right, back on the left, home in the middle…
  What is this Samsung, this is backwards. **Back is always on the left. Why do you keep doing
  this.**" A durable Samsung-specific gripe.
- [2016-03-09] **The disordered app drawer.** Out of the box apps/folders are in no order; new
  installs go to the end of the last page even after you alphabetize — "why Samsung."
- [2016-03-09] **TouchWiz bloat / redundant apps.** "When you have two email apps and two photos
  apps and several music apps and a bunch of redundant competing Samsung apps out of the box —
  plus even more redundant Verizon apps on the Verizon version — it's part annoying, part
  confusing." Ties it to a worry about Android killing the app drawer. (Sets up the HTC 10's
  de-duped-with-Google software as the contrast three days later — [[2016-04-12-yt-5IJWckL5L84]].)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The not-so-amazing things about this otherwise amazing phone are its biggest flaws."
> "Back is always on the left. Why do you keep doing this, Samsung?"
> "It kind of looks like a list of reasons not to buy this phone — but it's really not. It's just
> a highlight of how good these phones are getting."
