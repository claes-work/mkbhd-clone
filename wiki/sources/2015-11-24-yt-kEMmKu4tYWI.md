# Sony Xperia Z5 Premium: A 4K Smartphone! (@mkbhd, 2015-11-24)

## Summary
A look at the Sony Xperia Z5 Premium — the **world's first phone with a 4K display** (5.5", 806 ppi). It
renders everything at 1080p (4:1) for battery/GPU, snapping to full 4K only in high-res content (gallery,
4K video), where "it just really pops." But he applies his **retina-effect / diminishing-returns** lens to
answer the golden question: **can you actually tell 4K from a high-end QHD phone at arm's length? "It's
pretty much impossible to see the difference between these two at an arm's length."** A dated instance of
his display-resolution-vs-viewing-distance skepticism taken to the extreme.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-11-24] **World's first 4K phone display.** A 5.5" 3840×2160 IPS panel — "806 ppi, that's a lot of
  pixels." Otherwise very Sony: a 23 MP camera, Snapdragon 810, IP68, a clean flat metal body (with an
  "unbelievably awkward" volume button placement).
- [2015-11-24] **It renders at 1080p most of the time.** Screenshots/most apps report 1080p — "this saves
  battery and GPU strain." Only high-res content triggers full 4K; you can "actually see it snap into 4K
  mode after a few seconds, and it unlocks a bunch of detail and textures — it really pops."
- [2015-11-24] **The diminishing-returns verdict (retina-effect).** The golden question — can you tell the
  Z5 Premium's 4K from the Nexus 6P's QHD AMOLED at arm's length? His answer: **"it's pretty much impossible
  to see the difference between these two at an arm's length, especially through YouTube."** (His pixel-
  density-vs-viewing-distance skepticism applied to 4K on a phone — a gimmick unless you're pixel-peeping.)
