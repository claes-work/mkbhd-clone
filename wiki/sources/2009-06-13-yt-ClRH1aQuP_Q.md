# 17" Laptop GelaSkin Installation and Review (@mkbhd, 2009-06-13)

## Summary
The review half of the GelaSkins two-step (unboxing: [[2009-05-04-yt-6l3RGnwYAiU]],
~5.5 weeks earlier), and the corpus' clearest early example of **format ambiguity handled
by refusing to resolve it**: "this could more or less be considered a tutorial or a
review, however you want to consider it." He installs the skin **live and unedited**,
explicitly forfeiting the ability to retake — "it's a live application, so I can't really
mess up and go back and redo it" — then fails to line it up on camera, cuts away, and
returns to report honestly that it took "two or three tries later" off-camera. The verdict
is a **numeric 8/10** with stated reasons, continuing the scored-review pattern of the
Alto Edge mic (9/10) and iFrogz D33 (8.5/10). ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-13] **Format deliberately left open**: "this could more or less be considered a
  tutorial or a review, however you want to consider it" — and he signs off the same way
  ("thanks for watching this tutorial or review video, whatever you want to call it").
- [2009-06-13] **Live, unretakeable demo as a deliberate constraint**: "it's a live
  unboxing or a live application, so I can't really mess up and go back and redo it."
- [2009-06-13] **His own failure left in**: "well, I wasn't lined up, so it's not lined
  up" — he peels it back on camera, retries, then admits after the cutaway: "not too long
  after I ended the recording, like two or three tries later, I got it on perfectly
  lined." He also notes he lacked the squeegee that would have made it easy ("I don't
  actually have a squeegee and there wasn't a squeegee that came with").
- [2009-06-13] **Verdict: 8/10**, with reasons — "it's just well designed, it's not poorly
  made or anything, it sticks to the laptop very well and it won't be coming off anytime
  soon, it looks nice."
- [2009-06-13] **Criticism kept in the score**: the skin "fills up most of the laptop —
  mine is a little bit thicker, I guess you could say — but this back part here isn't
  completely covered." A partial-fit flaw, stated plainly, alongside the positive verdict.
- [2009-06-13] **Practical, lived-with framing**: "it's a nice texture actually — if
  you're carrying a laptop, it'll be easy to slide into a laptop bag still."
- [2009-06-13] **Procedure taught, not just shown**: clean the surface with the microfiber
  ("no oils or dust or dirt"), peel from the corner, line up, wipe across to push out air
  bubbles from the middle out; and the recovery note that "you can always re-attempt it
  before it's all the way on."
- [2009-06-13] **Difficulty scaled to the object**: "this is probably a little bit easier
  with a smaller something like an iPod or an iPhone."
- [2009-06-13] **Video-response chaining, again**: "I'll post this as a video response to
  the unboxing video so you can see exactly what comes in the package" — the same
  catalog-linking device used in [[2009-05-27-yt-b7Nc78QqDs0]].
- [2009-06-13] **Camera-framing self-awareness**: "sorry if I'm in the camera a little bit
  guys, I know my arm is going to be in the way because the camera is right there" — the
  fixed single-camera limitation named on air (cf. the "down the line" experiment in
  [[2009-05-21-yt-jV9Yvnk2kSg]]).
- [2009-06-13] **Customization pitched as the product's point**: "you can also pick your
  own skin and maybe use your own design and experiment with the colors."
