# HTC 10 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2016-04-12)

## Summary
First impressions of the HTC 10 — framed as HTC's would-be comeback flagship ("we'll figure out if
this really is the phone to save HTC"). The standout for the clone is the software praise: HTC worked
*with* Google to strip out duplicate apps, the direct antidote to the TouchWiz-bloat complaint he'd
made three days earlier on the S7. Also: HTC = the metal-lovers' brand (vs. Samsung's glass), the
"BoomSound solves a problem no one had" line, and USB-C.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-04-12] **De-duped apps = the software highlight (Bravo HTC).** "They worked with Google to
  remove any duplicate app they don't need that Google already makes — the default calculator is Google
  Calculator, the default calendar is Google Calendar, the default gallery is Google Photos. I've
  talked about this on previous phones, Samsung in particular… I like this step, Bravo HTC." The
  explicit fix to the S7's bloat gripe ([[2016-03-09-yt-V2nPsyyme5I]]).
- [2016-04-12] **Metal vs. glass brand identity.** "Full unibody metal jacket top to bottom… you only
  hear about premium feel when there's glass or metal involved — Samsung likes glass, HTC for the metal
  lovers." A durable materials-taxonomy framing. Praises button placement (ridged power button below
  volume) and "a chamfer like no other."
- [2016-04-12] **"BoomSound" = a solution to a problem no one had.** The 10 drops the big stereo
  front-facing speakers for a woofer-on-bottom + tweeter-up-top split HTC calls BoomSound — "an
  interesting solution to a problem I'm not really sure anyone actually had, but so far it sounds
  decent." Gentle skepticism of marketing-driven engineering.
- [2016-04-12] **Camera = cautious optimism.** 12 MP, f/1.8, OIS, large 1.55-micron pixels
  ("UltraPixel 2"), laser autofocus — "on paper pretty promising… HTC has clearly been struggling with
  this for a bit; I have high hopes but need to test it." Reserving the verdict for the full review.
- [2016-04-12] **Flagship spec + nice software extras.** Snapdragon 820, 4GB RAM, 3,000 mAh,
  microSD, **USB-C**, 24-bit DAC with personal audio profiles, and **native AirPlay support out of the
  box — "definitely a first on an Android phone."** Cleaner, more material-design HTC skin; "why not go
  full Nexus? They still offer their own stuff too."

## Notable verbatim quotes
