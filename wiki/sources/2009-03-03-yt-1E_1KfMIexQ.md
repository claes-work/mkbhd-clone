# HD Tutorial: Tilt In Camtasia Studio 6 (@mkbhd, 2009-03-03)

## Summary
A short request-driven editing tutorial (Marques, age 15) showing how he produces the
**"3D" zoom-and-tilt effect** viewers kept asking about in his videos. He opens Camtasia
Studio 6, imports footage saved with his own **HD preset** (referencing an earlier
tutorial), then uses the **Zoom-n-Pan** tool's **Advanced** panel to add an **XY-axis
tilt** keyframe so the frame leans to draw attention to a UI element (e.g. the Start-menu
search box), and pans back out to finish the edit. Notable as the **earliest documented
instance of the zoom/tilt push-in move** that becomes a hallmark of MKBHD's later
production polish — here explained as a manual Camtasia keyframe trick.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-03] The "3D tilt/zoom" look in his videos is done with Camtasia Studio 6's
  **Zoom-n-Pan → Advanced → tilt on the XY axis**, applied as keyframes to focus viewer
  attention on part of the screen, then reversed to zoom back out.
- [2009-03-03] He edits from his own reusable **HD preset** (covered in a prior tutorial),
  showing an early habit of standardizing a repeatable production pipeline.
- [2009-03-03] Frames the video as a **viewer-requested** how-to ("a lot of people have
  been asking me how I zoom... and tilt"), reinforcing the request-driven origin format.
