# HTC One Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-03)

## Summary
The full review of the HTC One — his #1 1080p phone — and a strong recommend: "I'd have no
hesitation recommending this phone to literally anyone." Praises the all-metal build and,
above all, the **BoomSound front-facing stereo speakers + display = best media-consumption
experience** he'd had. Honest about the UltraPixel camera's limits and Sense 5's quirks.
Carries a notable personal datum: he **no longer has a single daily driver** (uses different
phones for different things).

## Key claims
- [2013-05-03] **Reads it as a "must-win" phone — incentive framing**: "the One is HTC's main
  flagship, seems like their last hope, a must-win phone for them, competing directly against
  the Galaxy S4… HTC took some serious risks you don't usually see." Frames the product by the
  company's stakes.
- [2013-05-03] **BoomSound = best media experience in his pocket**: "these front-facing STEREO
  speakers are amazing — it's a wonder nobody's thought to do this in a smartphone before and
  executed it this well… combined with probably the best smartphone display I've ever used,
  it's the best media-consumption experience I've had in my pocket, and a huge reason to buy
  this phone by itself." A specific, enthusiastic verdict on an under-served feature.
- [2013-05-03] **UltraPixel camera — honest about the trade-off**: "decent in regular light,
  outstanding in low light [next to the Lumia 920]… but because it's only 4 megapixels, there's
  not as much detail when zooming, and HTC adds software sharpening that gives a weird look when
  something's supposed to be blurry." Applies his optics-vs-megapixels analysis honestly (cf.
  the UltraPixel explainer [[2013-03-29-yt-ul3lgaU_lbY]]).
- [2013-05-03] **"I don't really have a daily driver anymore" — a transition datum**: "a lot of
  people ask what's your next daily driver — honestly I don't really have one, I use different
  phones for different things." Marks the shift from a single personal phone to reviewing-many
  (a career/usage-pattern change).
- [2013-05-03] **Speed as his favorite trait — "quick" over "fast"**: "quick is the better word
  — really quick and responsive, very snappy… whatever it does, whether it's weird or not, it
  does it fast, and I love that. That's probably my favorite thing about the HTC One." Values
  responsiveness highly.
- [2013-05-03] **Best smartphone keyboard he'd used, and big-fingers datum**: "probably the best
  keyboard on any smartphone I've ever used… big enough for my pretty big fingers." Corroborates
  the big-hands/height thread.
- [2013-05-03] **Honest about Sense 5 quirks, even in a phone he loves**: flags BlinkFeed (can't
  remove), the merged dock/app-drawer, and the buried wallpaper-change flow as "weird quirks HTC
  could fix." Even-handed criticism inside a strong recommend.
- [2013-05-03] **The confident close**: "I would have no hesitation recommending this phone to
  literally anyone — that's how good the HTC One is… a great choice for the next 2–3 years." A
  rare unqualified recommendation.
