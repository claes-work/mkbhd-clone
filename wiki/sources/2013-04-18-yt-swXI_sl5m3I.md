# Top 5 1080p Smartphones! (@mkbhd, 2013-04-18)

## Summary
A ranking of the best 1080p phones (Xperia Z #5, HTC Butterfly/Droid DNA #4, Oppo Find 5
#3, Galaxy S4 #2, HTC One #1). Mostly consolidates verdicts from his individual reviews,
but carries three durable persona notes: the **channel-name/pixel-person identity**
restated, an **open-platform-drives-innovation** observation, and an honest **need-vs-want**
framing of high-res displays.

## Key claims
- [2013-04-18] **Pixel-person identity, restated as the frame**: "HD stands for high
  definition, which is short for lots of pixels — the more pixels the better." Opens the video
  on his display-primacy identity (cf. the MKBHD-name origin).
- [2013-04-18] **Open-platform-drives-innovation**: "every single phone on this list is an
  Android phone — no iPhones, no Windows phones, no BlackBerries, because there are no 1080p
  versions of those. That's just the nature of pushing the pace when you have manufacturers
  developing on an open platform." A dated industry observation about how open ecosystems
  accelerate hardware races.
- [2013-04-18] **Honest need-vs-want**: "do we really need 1080p phones, such high pixel
  density? No — you don't need it, but it's damn good-looking, and it looks better than a 720p
  phone, that's for sure." Separates need from desirability candidly.
- [2013-04-18] **A falsifiable prediction**: "I think we'll probably see a 1080p iPhone
  somewhere in the next 2 years." (The iPhone 6 Plus, 1080p, shipped ~17 months later.) Logs a
  later-correct call.
- [2013-04-18] **Consolidated verdicts (reinforcing)**: Xperia Z = design/speed but mixed-bag
  display; HTC Butterfly = first US 1080p+LTE but weak battery; Oppo Find 5 = "a dream on the
  spec sheet," steel unibody, software still catching up (Project Firefly); Galaxy S4 = powerful
  but AMOLED dimness + plastic build; HTC One #1 = best all-around hardware+software, highest
  PPI (4.7" 1080p). Each links to his full review.
