# Top 5 Android 4.1 Jellybean Features! (@mkbhd, 2012-06-28)

## Summary
A hands-on walkthrough of Android 4.1 Jelly Bean, installed early on his rooted Galaxy Nexus, counting
down five features: Project Butter smoothness, the redesigned notification panel, home-screen widget
reflow, a batch of small polish improvements, and Google Now / Voice Search. Two durable notes: he
flags that **the camera can't capture the thing being reviewed** (smoothness), and he lands a
**value argument** — a $350 Jelly Bean Galaxy Nexus could be the smarter buy than a new Galaxy S III.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-28] **The camera-can't-show-it disclosure** — a recurring honesty note when the subject is
  motion: "the camera doesn't even really do it justice here… this is like using an entirely different
  device. This is as responsive as the quad-core Galaxy S3." He tells the viewer the medium can't convey
  what he's assessing, so they should weight his description accordingly. (Same instinct as the
  display-clarity caveats in his phone reviews.)
- [2012-06-28] **Project Butter as the headline** — and a fix for a stated prior complaint: the
  smoothness "is something a lot of people had complaints about earlier," now resolved via triple
  buffering and VSync at high frame rates. On a dual-core Galaxy Nexus, "this is as responsive as the
  quad-core Galaxy S3" — a software update closing a hardware gap.
- [2012-06-28] **The value argument, explicit**: "it's pretty much made this a really enjoyable option
  and definitely a very affordable option. If you're looking to get this over like a Galaxy S3, I could
  see someone buying this over the Galaxy S3." Jelly Bean makes the older, cheaper Nexus a live
  alternative to the new flagship — the same value framing he formalizes in
  [[2012-07-13-yt-nzikcaYKOkY]].
- [2012-06-28] **Polish as a first-class axis**: "a lot of people were complaining that Ice Cream
  Sandwich wasn't actually that polished. I think it's the most polished version yet — but Jelly Bean is
  definitely on a whole new level of polish." Treats fit-and-finish as a reviewable quality, not a
  garnish.
- [2012-06-28] **Google Now demoed with real, personal queries** — including navigation to "Galloping
  Hills Golf Course," another dated golf marker ([[2012-01-08-yt-QCRQ7teMxsI]], [[2012-03-31-yt-cp___W5vDAs]]).
- [2012-06-28] **Honest about the conversational gap**: Google Voice Search "is not quite as
  conversational as something like S Voice or Siri… if you ask it something that's more of a sentence
  than an actual search query, it'll probably deliver search results" — names the limitation while
  praising the speed.
- [2012-06-28] **Source-and-web-results transparency praised again**: every card "also gives your source
  and an image… if you're ever uncomfortable with the source, you can always check out the web results
  below it." Same criterion as [[2012-07-01-yt-kw-RzN4xYyE]].
- [2012-06-28] **Cadence marker**: "subscribe if you want to stay tuned for more videos like this as I
  produce some all the time now" — the summer-2012 high-output period.
