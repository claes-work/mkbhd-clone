# MKBHD Edition Macbook Pro! (Colorware Custom) (@mkbhd, 2014-07-04)

## Summary
Marques shows off a **Colorware custom-painted MacBook Pro** he designed — "a legit MKBHD edition
MacBook." The palette is the clearest hardware expression yet of his personal brand colors: **matte
("tuxedo") black with a cherry-red hinge accent**, plus a machine-cut, **backlit custom MKBHD logo**
replacing the Apple logo. "You guys already know I like red and black." It's a strong **appearance /
brand-identity** datum — and it's characteristically source-critical about its own product, flagging
paint durability as the open question and explaining why he chose understated over flashy.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-07-04] **The problem it solves:** Apple ships everything in silver ("every Apple laptop… keyboard
  … trackpad … magic mouse is silver"). Colorware genuinely **paints** devices (not a peel-off skin) via
  a design studio "kind of like Moto Maker" for laptops, tablets, phones, headphones, mice, keyboards.
- [2014-07-04] **His build:** everything **matte black** with a **cherry-red hinge accent** — "I think
  that's a sick accent color; you guys already know I like red and black." Ports/connectors (MagSafe
  interior, headphone jack, SD reader) left unpainted so they still work.
- [2014-07-04] **The MKBHD logo replacement** (a Colorware option usually for companies): they machine-
  cut a custom logo into the metal where Apple's logo sits and wire it to **backlight/glow** on power —
  "a legit MKBHD edition MacBook after the surgery."
- [2014-07-04] **Deliberately understated, and honest about why.** He chose "classy, understated, more
  tuxedo-black" over a "Ferrari red bright painted laptop" precisely because the paint is **permanent**:
  a bright-red laptop is cool for a week, then you have to take it to class every day. A taste choice
  reasoned from lived practicality.
- [2014-07-04] **Source-critical about the product he's showing:** the open question is **durability** —
  "if you scratch a silver Apple laptop there's more silver underneath, no big deal; but if you scratch
  this black painted laptop, how many layers of black are there before you get to silver?" Won't call it
  proven until it's lived with.
- [2014-07-04] **Origin aside:** he first found Colorware hunting for a **black Apple keyboard**, which
  then kept showing up in his video backgrounds (and the Setup Tour Project), prompting viewer questions —
  so he reached out and did the MacBook project.
