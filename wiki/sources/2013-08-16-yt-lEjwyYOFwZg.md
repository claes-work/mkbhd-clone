# Top 5 Best Headphones Under $200! (@mkbhd, 2013-08-16)

## Summary
A back-to-school headphone countdown that doubles as a **personal-gear inventory**: the
**ATH-M50 at #1** (his flat/accurate default, red-and-black version) and the **Jaybird
BlueBuds X at #2** (his daily wireless, the "Stormtrooper" black-and-white pair). Reinforces
the diminishing-returns philosophy, the red-and-black bias, and the standing Beats jab.

## Key claims
- [2013-08-16] **#1 ATH-M50 — his default + the diminishing-returns philosophy**: "my go-to for
  sound quality, period. I have a budget and could spend $400–500, but there's a point of
  diminishing return — I don't even use an amp, just plug and go. Trustworthy flat, accurate
  sound for monitoring my camera/mic audio." His anti-overspend stance and the red-and-black
  version disclosed.
- [2013-08-16] **#2 Jaybird BlueBuds X — his daily wireless, the "Stormtrooper" pair**: "I can't
  get enough of these… I ended up going with the black-and-white, sort of a **Stormtrooper**
  look. I don't leave the house without them — walking to the train, to class, back." Corroborates
  the Five-Facts "Jaybird daily" biography datum and the "Stormtrooper" aesthetic word.
- [2013-08-16] **The Beats jab, restated**: closes "and of course Beats by Dre also makes some —
  no, no, they don't. Sorry, they don't make any good headphones under $200 either." His standing
  Beats skepticism, as a punchline.
- [2013-08-16] **Recommend-what-you'd-buy — the rest of the list**: Beyerdynamic Custom One Pro
  (#5, the tunable bass port), Grado SR80i (#4, "the perfect introductory open-back… like sitting
  next to the artist"), V-Moda Crossfade LP (#3). Honorable mentions Klipsch S4, Sony XB, Denon.
  Recommends from his own use.
- [2013-08-16] **Audio-humility restated**: teaches open-back vs closed-back plainly without
  claiming audiophile authority ("I've been getting more into the audio game"). His know-your-
  lane register.
