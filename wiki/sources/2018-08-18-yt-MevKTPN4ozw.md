# Talking Tech with Elon Musk! (@mkbhd, 2018-08-18)

## Summary
A Marques Brownlee × Elon Musk sit-down interview filmed at the Tesla factory (Fremont), paired with a separate factory-tour video promised at the time. Marques frames and drives the conversation as the interviewer; Elon Musk is the GUEST and answers questions about Tesla products, business strategy, manufacturing, and the future of EVs. - [2018-08-18] (Marques) Frames Musk as commonly seen as "the boss / the face of Tesla / the decision maker," then asks how he actually spends his time there.
- [2018-08-18] (Marques) Notes his crew's Tesla ties for context: he owns a P100D ("undefeated in stoplight races"); audio (Rich) and camera (Brandon) crew are waiting on Model 3 pre-orders — Marques ordered blue, rear-wheel-drive.
- [2018-08-18] (Marques) References an earnings-call detail: one of the top-five most frequent trade-ins for the Model 3 is a Prius, and uses it to ask whether a cheaper Tesla is possible.
- [2018-08-18] (Marques) States he made a day-one Roadster deposit because he was that excited, and made a video about the Roadster reveal event (which he couldn't attend).
- [2018-08-18] (Marques) Recalls the original Tesla "master plan" (low-volume high-price Roadster → Model S → higher-volume lower-price Model 3) and asks what comes after part one.
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT, not persona data) Claims ~89% of his time is spent in the factory / design / engineering / production meetings, not on Twitter.
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Tesla spends no money on advertising or endorsements; growth is word-of-mouth from a product people love; Musk says he pays full retail for his own cars.
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) A ~$25,000 Tesla is achievable "maybe in three years" with hard work, via both design/technology improvements and scale (economies of scale).
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Tesla has more product ideas than resources; plans to run two products at once (previously only one at a time) — Model Y (compact SUV, price comparable to Model 3), plus the Semi, pickup truck, and next-gen Roadster.
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Track Mode will expose an "expert user mode" with adjustable traction control, battery temperature, and other settings, with risk warnings (analogized to overclocking a graphics card).
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Roadster claims: 0–60 in 1.9s, ~200 kWh battery, 600+ mile range; described as roughly two Model S packs' worth of cells, expecting ~10–20% cell improvement by production.
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Typical car side mirrors reduce highway range by ~5% because they act like air brakes; regulations require manufacturers to fit mirrors but (he believes) US owners may modify their own cars.
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Factory automation varies by process (some parts 80–90% automated, others 10–20%); over-automating general assembly was a "big mistake" because humans excel at adaptation and finicky tasks (e.g., connecting a dangling hose).
- [2018-08-18] (Elon Musk — CONTEXT) Calls the Model X "an exercise in hubris" that got carried away with art/technology and distracted from the mission, likely delaying the Model 3.
