# iOS vs Android: A Google User's POV! (@mkbhd, 2012-03-31)

## Summary
A side-by-side tour of his **own** iPad 3 and Transformer Prime home screens, framed as a genuine
open question he hands to the audience: he owns both, can't decide which to keep alongside his
Galaxy Nexus, and asks viewers to argue him into one. The interesting content isn't the verdict
(there isn't one) but what the tour exposes: **how much friction a heavy Google-services user hits
on iOS**, and how candidly he reports being a beginner on a platform he's reviewing.

## Key claims
- [2012-03-31] **The premise is an unresolved personal decision, not a verdict**: "there's been a new
  iPad and a Transformer Prime in this house for the past week and a half or so and I still haven't been
  able to decide which one makes a better pair with my Samsung Galaxy Nexus. So this video is your
  opportunity to convince me one way or the other."
- [2012-03-31] **Self-reported beginner status on iOS, stated repeatedly**: "I'm a bit of a noob with
  iOS"; "my iPad is pretty much noob status right now — if you guys have any suggestions for
  applications that'll spice it up, they are definitely appreciated." He reviews from a stated position
  of inexperience rather than performing fluency.
- [2012-03-31] **The ecosystem-friction observation** — the substantive finding: as "a really really
  heavy Google apps user" he assembles third-party substitutes on iOS (Reeder for Google Reader, the
  Google Search app as a shell for Photos/Calendar/services, the web for Google+ because "there's no
  real good Google+ app for the iPad") — and notes he can't remove Apple's own apps: "all the Apple
  stuff is in this folder here and I wish I could get it off the home screen somehow… but that's the way
  iOS works, I can't get rid of them."
- [2012-03-31] **Credits the rival platform's execution where it's good**: the Google Search app on iOS
  "was recently updated for the retina display… I really do like this, updated for the retina display,
  brand new and free," and he uses it heavily "just because it is that good."
- [2012-03-31] **Unpopular opinion, offered with a flinch**: on the app his audience told him to install
  — "Flipboard, because you guys all told me to get Flipboard, so I've got Flipboard. **It's not that
  great. Don't hurt me for saying that.**"
- [2012-03-31] **The Android side is customized, the iOS side isn't** — and he says why: the Transformer
  Prime home screen is "looking very simple, but that's because I've made it the way I've wanted to make
  it," with rotation/brightness toggles, a calendar-plus-clock widget, a weather widget, and Google
  services across the docks.
- [2012-03-31] **Self-aware about his own bias, by counting it**: "I've said the word Google about 75
  times in this video, if you guys haven't already understood."
- [2012-03-31] Dated personal detail: excited for **the Masters** golf tournament ("a golf tournament
  coming up that I'm really excited for"), corroborating the golf interest dated in
  [[2012-01-08-yt-QCRQ7teMxsI]].
