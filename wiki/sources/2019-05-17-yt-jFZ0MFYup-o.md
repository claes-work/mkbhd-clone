# Dope Tech: The Probe Lens! (@mkbhd, 2019-05-17)

## Summary
Marques dedicates a returning episode of Dope Tech to the Venus Optics Laowa 24mm f/14 macro probe lens, an unusually specialized piece of video gear he had been using for a couple of weeks. He explains what drew him to it — clips floating around the internet, a well-done viral video shooting the lens through various subjects, and most recently Phil Esposito of The Verge using it for the intro shot of the Galaxy Fold review — and then breaks down why the lens is so strange: a nearly foot-and-a-half-long, few-centimeters-wide barrel that covers full frame, is fully manual with de-clicked aperture and focus rings, has 27 optical elements, focuses down to inches from the front of the barrel, keeps a wide 24mm field of view unusual for a macro, ranges from f/14 to f/40, has a waterproof front element for submerged shots, and has a built-in ring of LEDs powered over a micro-USB port. He details the tradeoffs (the narrow aperture demands enormous amounts of light, which is why phone-screen shots must be done in the dark) and then walks through a series of creative demo shots — flying through a rack of phones on a slider, M&Ms spilling over the lens in slow motion, moving through fake plants, a low keyboard/zipper perspective, and a handheld marker-writing shot — concluding that while there is almost no practical reason to own the lens, the rare times you invent a good use for it make it "so worth it."

## Key claims
- [2019-05-17] The lens is the Venus Optics Laowa 24mm macro probe lens, a full-frame, fully manual lens with an aperture range of f/14 to f/40.
- [2019-05-17] It is fully manual, meaning the aperture ring and focus ring are both de-clicked and hand-operated instead of electronic.
- [2019-05-17] The lens has seven aperture blades and 27 optical elements in the whole tube.
- [2019-05-17] As a macro lens its minimum focus distance is about one and a half feet measured from the sensor/flange, which puts the subject just inches from the end of the barrel.
- [2019-05-17] Most macro lenses are around 100mm focal length, which isolates the subject with shallow depth of field; the 24mm keeps a wide-angle field of view with more in focus while still getting super close.
- [2019-05-17] The narrow f/14 maximum aperture is both a blessing and a curse: it prevents almost nothing being in focus, but it means the lens needs a great deal of light.
- [2019-05-17] The front end of the lens is waterproof, allowing it to probe small spaces or be submerged for underwater shots.
- [2019-05-17] The lens has built-in LEDs in a ring at the front, powered via a micro-USB port on the barrel and an included micro-USB cable plugged into a power brick.
- [2019-05-17] Phone screens are not very bright and get dimmer when lights are shined at them, which is why The Verge's Galaxy Fold shot (and Marques's OnePlus 7 Pro shot) had to be filmed in a dark setting to see the display.
- [2019-05-17] The lens was already responsible for a shot in the prior OnePlus 7 Pro review video.
- [2019-05-17] Final shots require post work — coloring, editing to brighten the phone, denoising, and potentially sound design — before they look right.
- [2019-05-17] The RED camera handles 120fps at 4K, which made the slow-motion M&Ms shot feasible.
- [2019-05-17] A GoPro would not fit through the opening (a rack of phones) that the probe lens fits through, so several of these shots are impossible with other lenses.
- [2019-05-17] In the "viral video," the part showing something detaching from the lens is faked/edited, but the actual shots it captured are real and well done.
- [2019-05-17] The lens has almost no reason to own it and you have to invent ways to use it, but the rare successful uses make it worth it — which is what makes it Dope Tech.
