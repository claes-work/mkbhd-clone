# Moto X Features: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-08-02)

## Summary
A ★★ **incentive/marketing-analysis** landmark, distilled into one crisp framework:
**"geeks value specs-versus-price; consumers value features-versus-price."** He uses it to
explain why the $200-on-contract Moto X ships mid-range specs and leans on software
features (touchless control, Active Display, Moto Maker) — it isn't built for tech
reviewers, it's built for the mass market. One of his clearest statements of who a product
is actually for.

## Key claims
- [2013-08-02] **The framework — the video's one-frame summary**: "geeks value specs versus
  price; consumers value features versus price. When you market a phone toward geeks you focus
  on specs; when you market toward the average person you focus on features." His crispest
  statement of the tech-press-vs-mainstream divide (cf. the Galaxy S4 features video).
- [2013-08-02] **Applies the value-via-software thesis to Motorola**: "everyone who complains
  about the Moto X's specs is a geek — but Motorola isn't selling to us geeks. Like Samsung
  with the S4, they're adding value via software instead of hardware, because they want to
  sell as many of these as possible to the average person." Reads the product by its target
  buyer and the maker's incentive.
- [2013-08-02] **Features framed by the retail moment**: repeatedly imagines the store — "the
  sales rep asks 'does the Galaxy S4 do that? No. The HTC One? No. The iPhone? No.' Yeah, I'll
  get this one." Explains why touchless control / shake-to-open-camera / Active Display are
  chosen: they demo well to a non-geek.
- [2013-08-02] **Moto Maker + the customization/"make it your own" value**: "no other phone
  lets you customize the back/front/trim color, engraving, and boot animation… there's a
  certain satisfaction people get out of designing their own things — Moto Maker really nails
  it, very similar to Nike iD." His make-it-your-own value, applied — with a sharp critique of
  the **AT&T exclusivity** ("the dumbest thing you could have possibly done").
- [2013-08-02] **Industry-access datum**: "that secret Google/Motorola event a couple weeks ago
  no one was supposed to talk about — yes, I was there, and Guy Kawasaki explained why he feels
  this is akin to what Apple did with the first colored iMacs." Early insider access.
- [2013-08-02] **Even-handed on the "gimmicks"**: notes a Google Play edition will strip "a lot
  of the Motorola features that us geeks would consider gimmicks" — separating his own geek lens
  from the mass-market value honestly.
