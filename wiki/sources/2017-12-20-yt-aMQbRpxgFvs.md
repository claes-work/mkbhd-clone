# Smartphone Awards 2017! (@mkbhd, 2017-12-20)

## Summary
Marques's annual year-end flagship: he lines up every phone released in 2017 on one desk and hands out an award in each category — a winner, sometimes a runner-up, and honorable mentions. The categories this year are Best Big Phone, Best Compact Phone, Best Camera, Best Budget Phone, Best Battery Life, Design Award, Bust of the Year, Comeback Player of the Year, and the finale MVP (best phone of the year). The through-line is that 2017 was a genuinely strong year across every price tier and use-case — "you'd be hard-pressed going wrong with something on this table." Recurring standouts are the Samsung Galaxy Note 8 (the most complete phone, taking both Best Big Phone and MVP), the Google Pixel 2/2 XL (best camera, plus MVP runner-up), and two newcomers — the Razer Phone and Essential Phone — that punch above their debut status. The iPhone X threads through as a design/video/color contender and the phone that "shifted what it means to be an iPhone."

## Key claims
- [2017-12-20] Best Big Phone winner: Samsung Galaxy Note 8 — it "does it all," with the best display on any phone, a big battery, optically-stabilized dual cameras, the stylus, and the big-screen software features; it remains the slight step above the Galaxy S8 in Samsung's lineup.
- [2017-12-20] Best Big Phone runner-up: Razer Phone — a "shameless" use of being big, keeping huge bezels to fit best-in-class front speakers and a 4000mAh battery; fewer big-screen software features (no split-screen fun, no stylus) but a great use of hardware space.
- [2017-12-20] Best Compact Phone winner: iPhone 8 — a legit flagship with nearly every iPhone 8 Plus feature except dual cameras, in the smallest, lightest package; overshadowed by the iPhone X and 8 Plus but "complete."
- [2017-12-20] Best Compact Phone runner-up: Pixel 2 — same camera as the Pixel 2 XL (answering the 8's missing dual-camera), front-facing speakers, great display; honorable mention to the Galaxy S8 as "by far the best designed compact phone" with the Infinity Display.
- [2017-12-20] Best Camera winner: Google Pixel 2 (and 2 XL) — "ridiculous" photos with detail, dynamic range, sharpness, fast focus, stabilization and low-light; incredible single-camera portrait mode; so good it "spoiled" him against other phones' cameras.
- [2017-12-20] Best Camera runner-up: iPhone X — better video (underrated 240fps 1080p slow-mo, dual OIS) and the "best colors"; he'd combine the Pixel 2's processing/dynamic range/portrait/sharpness with the iPhone's colors if he could.
- [2017-12-20] Best Budget Phone winner: OnePlus 5T — flagship build, thin bezels, dual cameras, 8GB RAM, headphone jack, at $499; "serious bang for the buck." Runner-up moto g5 plus (best under ~$300, closer to $200); honorable mention Huawei Honor 7X.
- [2017-12-20] His recurring line, updated: good cheap phones — "cheap phones are getting really good" even as $1000 flagships persist.
- [2017-12-20] Best Battery Life winner: Razer Phone (its second award) — the 4000mAh battery, built around gaming, lasts "almost forever." Honorable mentions: Pixel 2 XL and the iPhone 8 Plus ("still the king of standby time").
- [2017-12-20] Design Award winner: Essential Phone — best combo of design and build (ceramic back, titanium sides, the notch he warmed to); polarizing and initially not recommendable at launch price, but worth it after price drops. Runners-up: iPhone X (killing the home button) and Galaxy S8/S8 Plus (clean glass sandwich).
- [2017-12-20] Bust of the Year: HTC U Ultra — "worst phone of the year," not a good use of space; he keeps it brief.
- [2017-12-20] Comeback Player / Most Improved: HTC — the U11 turned out great (color-changing back, better camera, squeeze/Edge Sense that later appeared in the Pixel 2), and the later U11+ (never came to the US) was even more complete.
- [2017-12-20] MVP / Best Phone of the Year: Samsung Galaxy Note 8 — "the most complete smartphone that came out this year"; waterproof, headphone jack, stylus, huge battery, best screen, dual OIS cameras, expandable storage; only real nitpick is the fingerprint-reader placement; he doesn't have a problem with the ~$1000 price given the completeness.
- [2017-12-20] MVP first runner-up: Pixel 2 XL — best camera, best software and fastest updates (already on Android 8.1), correct fingerprint placement; only the display held it back, yet it was his longest-running daily driver of the year.
- [2017-12-20] MVP honorable mention: iPhone X — for "shifting what it means to be an iPhone" (modern design, thin bezels, no home button) and paving the way for future modern iPhones, though it also paved the way for $1000 phones.
