# iPhone X Impressions & Hands On! (@mkbhd, 2017-09-13)

## Summary
A landmark hands-on with the iPhone X ("iPhone TEN — don't you dare call it iPhone X") from the Steve Jobs
Theater unveiling. The most significant iPhone redesign in a decade: **the notch, Face ID replacing Touch ID,
and gesture navigation** (no home button). Also his sharp "iPhone 8 = iPhone 7s, let's not get it twisted."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-09-13] **"iPhone 8 is definitely more iPhone 7s — let's not get it twisted."** "iPhone 8/8 Plus are a
  spec bump to the 7/7 Plus — nearly identical design, but a glass back for Qi wireless charging, True Tone,
  the A11 Bionic, and a copper-ish 'rose gold.' An upgrade, but this is iPhone 7s." Calls the naming honestly.
- [2017-09-13] **How Apple handled the notch (his main pre-question, answered).** "My main question with the
  dummies was how Apple would handle the notch with rounded corners — now we have the answer: **time on the
  left, signal/battery on the right, and they flip black/white to contrast the background** so you always see
  them; most apps fill the whole display around the notch for an immersive look (like the Essential's, but a
  wider notch); some content shows a rectangular aspect ratio. The goal is clearly immersion." A careful,
  reads-the-design analysis. (And, as always: "it looks better in real life than the renders.")
- [2017-09-13] **Gesture navigation replaces the home button.** "No home button — going home is a **gesture**:
  swipe up from the bottom (the bar = the home swipe); swipe halfway for multitasking; three different
  swipe-downs (middle = notifications, right corner = control center, home-screen middle = search). A lot to
  get used to, but I'm willing to give it a shot — that's what you get when you remove all the buttons."
  Reserves judgment on a big interaction change.
- [2017-09-13] **Face ID replaces Touch ID — "a lot of big claims to test."** "The advanced front sensors have
  to be good because Apple replaced Touch ID with Face ID — a million-to-one chance someone else gets in. I
  didn't get to test it much, but I definitely will — Apple made a big deal of it, and it's *replacing* a
  core part of iOS." Flags it as the make-or-break to verify in the full review.
- [2017-09-13] **Dual OIS cameras + portrait lighting (triggered by the Note 8) + the front-camera story.**
  "Both 12 MP lenses now OIS (better telephoto/low-light/video). New Portrait Lighting modes (natural →
  studio → contour → stage), adjustable after the shot, and — key — **they work on the front camera too**;
  the front-camera improvements alone are enough reason for some people to get this phone." Notes **Animoji**
  ("animated emojis that move as you move — really good at making people make weird faces"). $1,000 in
  November. Concern: "no ProMotion (not really a concern), and the OLED brightness was in question — my Note 8
  got brighter, for what it's worth."
