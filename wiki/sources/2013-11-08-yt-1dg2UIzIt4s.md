# Google Nexus 5 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-11-08)

## Summary
The full review of the Nexus 5 — one of the year's most anticipated phones, and his own
(he bought the white one, "The Stormtrooper phone"). Persona value: a clean **value-forces-
tradeoffs** analysis (cramming $700 specs into a $350 phone means shortcuts — the speaker
and camera are the shortcuts), the **"league of its own" at its price** verdict, and his
stock-Android + updates-fast preference on KitKat.

## Key claims
- [2013-11-08] **His own phone — "The Stormtrooper phone"**: "I chose the 32-gig white — I'm
  calling it The Stormtrooper phone, others call it the panda phone. Pretty classy, clean, neat
  design." The recurring Stormtrooper aesthetic word, applied to his own device.
- [2013-11-08] **Value forces tradeoffs — the core analysis**: "when you cram the same specs as a
  high-end $700 phone into a $350 phone, you inevitably make shortcuts — the speaker and the
  camera are those shortcuts. It shares almost all the LG G2's internals but a cheaper camera
  sensor; that was a conscious decision to hit $350." Reasons about where the money went.
- [2013-11-08] **"In a league of its own" at its price**: "the Nexus 5 without considering the
  price is great; with the price it's in a league of its own — there really isn't any other $350
  phone with a Snapdragon 800 and the latest Android. It inevitably gets compared with $700
  phones rather than $350 ones." His value-as-a-first-class-axis verdict.
- [2013-11-08] **Camera is fixable via software — the constructive note**: "this is a software
  problem — I'd encourage Google to push the stock camera app to the Play Store like Motorola
  did, and make it better. Cameras have always been the weakest point of Nexus devices." Ties the
  flaw to the rolling-release fix he'd advocated.
- [2013-11-08] **Stock-Android / updates-fast preference on KitKat**: "the main focus of a Nexus
  is the software — this is the most Google phone ever made… KitKat is clean, minimal,
  understated, lets the apps take center stage, and it's the best version of Android. It'll
  perform well for years thanks to TRIM, and you'll get updates fast." His stock + updates
  preference.
- [2013-11-08] **Honest about the flaws — speaker, battery, camera**: "the speaker is very, very
  bad… the battery life was surprisingly inconsistent — I couldn't make it through a day. I'd
  rather LG had made it slightly thicker and more curved with a bigger battery." Names the real
  downsides even in his own phone.
- [2013-11-08] **The scoped recommendation**: "if you want no-compromise specs, the Nexus 5 is
  flat-out not for you — get an HTC One, Note 3, or Lumia 1020. But if you want the most pure
  Google experience and to be first for updates, this is the phone — buy it. If you're
  considering it, you're probably in that group." His match-it-to-your-values close.
