# AirPods Max Review: Luxury Listening! (@mkbhd, 2020-12-16)

## Summary
The full review of Apple's $550 AirPods Max, built around the framework Marques used to "figure them out": there are two kinds of high-end headphones — **reference/studio-grade production** headphones (lightweight, flat sound signature, often need a preamp, geared for *creating*) and **luxury listening** headphones (nice-sounding, wireless, ANC, geared for *consuming*). AirPods Max land "squarely" in luxury listening (he's glad they weren't called "AirPods Studio"), and they "check a set of boxes that no other set of headphones I've ever tested can claim." He praises the all-metal build (differentiating vs all-plastic rivals) while explaining the tradeoff — metal is heavy, which is why everyone else uses plastic — illustrated with his "electric car cornering" analogy for the weight. He contrasts with the all-metal 450g Beats Pro as a cautionary tale of doing metal badly. Sound is "really good" (40mm drivers, low distortion), ANC matches the Sony XM4 as "kings of noise cancellation," and transparency mode is "the best I've ever heard." He *hates* the Smart Case with unusual intensity (a full "I hate…" litany). Verdict: three giant quirks — the stupid case, the heavy metal build, and the price — but if you accept those and you're in Apple's ecosystem, "these are pretty dope headphones."

## Key claims
- [2020-12-16] Framework: high-end headphones split into (a) reference/studio-grade production headphones — lightweight, flat sound, often need a preamp, geared for production; and (b) luxury listening — great-sounding consumer headphones with ANC/wireless, geared for consumption. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] AirPods Max land "squarely" in luxury listening — he's glad they didn't call them "AirPods Studio"; they "check a set of boxes that no other set of headphones I've ever tested can claim." (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Build: aluminum ear cups (cold to the touch outdoors) + stainless-steel headband; tight tolerances, satin finish (he'd prefer matte black — "Apple, why'd you send me the green one"), smooth high-resistance telescoping band, high-quality Digital Crown and button. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Why almost all other headphones are plastic: comfort over long wear is primarily driven by weight, and metal is heavy — so rivals chose plastic. AirPods Max mitigate with a thin flexible mesh top band, but there's no getting around that they're heavy (~380g vs ~250g). (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Weight analogy: "it's kind of like driving an electric car — if you keep it in a straight line you can forget that it's heavy, but as soon as you hit a corner you remember it's a heavy car." Sitting still he forgets the weight; nodding/standing/looking up reminds him. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Cautionary counter-example: the all-metal ~450g Beats Pro ($400) is "about the least comfortable pair of headphones I've ever worn" (on-ear, pinches) — so AirPods Max "could have been a lot worse." (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] No Apple logo anywhere on the headphones; no sweat/water resistance (bad for gym/athletes/rain). Over-ear (preferred over on-ear); mesh + memory-foam cups stay comfortable 2–3 hours before his ears get hot — better than leather. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Magnetic, easily-replaceable ear cups are a rare consumer feature and a genuinely good idea; Apple's $69 replacements are "definitely overpriced ... no way they're worth that much," but the system deserves credit. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Lack of touch controls is the right call; the oversized Apple Watch Digital Crown (rotate = volume, press = media, reversible in iOS) works great with satisfying clicks. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Sound: he prefaces "I'm not an audiophile," but the 40mm drivers sound "excellent," super clear at all volumes with ultra-low distortion; he maxes at ~75–85%. Great with high-quality Spotify streaming (punchy bass, occasional deep sub-bass, great instrument separation, non-fatiguing). Wireless/Bluetooth streaming inherently caps quality; people wanting hi-bitrate local files + a preamp are a different, higher-commitment audience these aren't for. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] ANC: nine mics do "a fantastic job" with white noise and sound surprisingly natural (no pressure feeling); cancelled a vacuum ~3ft away — he puts them "matching the Sony XM4s as kings of noise cancellation." (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Transparency mode is "the best I've ever heard" — not just what's said but *where* sounds come from (directionality); "these are the king of that." No latency/range issues (walks around corners and up/down stairs). Mic quality for calls is "fine." (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] The Smart Case: an unusually intense "I hate…" litany — the look, the pointless bottom slots, the misaligned Lightning notch, hard to insert/remove quickly, weak lip magnet, doesn't protect the delicate mesh band, "looks like a mini purse," and the fact that it's required to trigger low-power mode (no off button; outside the case they drain ~2 hours before auto low-power). (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Battery is fine — 16–20 hours even with ANC, plus 5-minute charge for ~1.5 hours. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Smart features: on-head detection auto play/pause, Siri, instant multi-device Apple pairing, and spatial audio (7.1 / Dolby Atmos head-tracking, same as AirPods Pro, iPhone/iPad only) that's "so great" for movies. (Marques)
- [2020-12-16] Verdict: they deliver a great *listening* experience via sound + ANC + spatial audio + controls, hampered by "top three giant quirks" — the stupid case, the heavy metal build, and the price. If you accept those and you're in Apple's ecosystem, "these are pretty dope headphones. Sounds kind of like a lot of other Apple products, right?" (Marques)
