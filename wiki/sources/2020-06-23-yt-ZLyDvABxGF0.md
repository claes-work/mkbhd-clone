# iOS 14 Hands-On: Everything New! (@mkbhd, 2020-06-23)

## Summary
First-look hands-on with the iOS 14 developer beta the day after its WWDC 2020 reveal, after Marques had run it on his iPhone for about a day. He organizes the update into buckets: the new Home Screen (App Library "app drawer," Home Screen widgets, Smart Stacks, hideable pages), a "compact UI" theme (calls, FaceTime, system-wide picture-in-picture, and a smaller Siri no longer take over the whole screen), "straight from Android" borrows (emoji search in the keyboard, a Translate app, changeable default email/browser apps), App Clips, and a set of "genuinely new stuff Google should copy" details — Back Tap accessibility shortcuts, Sound Recognition, camera/mic privacy indicators, a night-mode gyroscope guide, and sign-language prominence in group FaceTime. His framing throughout is the meta-question "why now?" for features (like widgets) Apple could have shipped years earlier: his answer is that Apple waited to add a few of its own touches on top. Verdict: not a drastic UI overhaul — "very clearly still an iPhone" — but a lot of good detail for the nerds; half of users will never touch most of it. iOS 14 ships to every iPhone since the 6s in the fall.

## Key claims
- [2020-06-23] iOS 14 was revealed at WWDC (the day before this video); Marques had the early developer beta on his iPhone for ~1 day. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] On the "Apple copied Android" memes: Marques made the jokes too, but reframes it as "why now?" — his read is Apple wanted to get a couple of extra things right and layer them on top; it's not necessarily better than the Android version, but Apple does some of it its own way. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] The App Library is a new space to the right of all home screens — an auto-organized, alphabetical, searchable list of every app; effectively iOS's first real app drawer, "it took a decade but it's finally here." (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Home Screen widgets are here but still restricted "in a very iPhone way": they can't go in the middle of a screen, must sit on the left or right, and can't stand alone on the right with nothing to their left. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] All launch widgets are first-party Apple apps, but third-party widgets are expected to follow; Marques is excited for a Tesla widget (unlock trunk / open charge port) and a Google Calendar widget in place of Apple's. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Smart Stack is a carousel widget of common widgets you scroll manually or let auto-rotate on predictions; you can edit stacks to remove useless widgets, and create your own by dragging one same-size widget onto another. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Because there's now an app drawer, you can hide whole home-screen pages — you can build a setup with no app pages at all, just one page of widgets. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] "Compact UI" theme: incoming phone/FaceTime calls now show as a drop-down banner instead of a full-screen takeover; you can dismiss, accept, or reject inline. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] FaceTime now continues in a movable picture-in-picture when you go home, without pausing your video; picture-in-picture is broadly supported system-wide (Apple TV, Safari video), and you can swipe the PiP window off-screen to keep just the audio. He calls it "more like true multitasking." (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Siri is no longer a full-screen takeover — just a small animation at the bottom — but Marques flags it as imperfect: you still can't interact with (e.g. scroll) content underneath the Siri answer without exiting, so it doesn't fully feel like multitasking. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] "Straight from Android" additions: emoji search in the stock keyboard, a stock Translate app (~a dozen languages, expected to grow, good for two-person conversations), and — "finally" — changeable default email and browser apps (he'll set Chrome as default browser and something other than Apple Mail). (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] App Clips let you temporarily use a slice of an app's functionality without installing the whole app. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Back Tap (Settings ▸ Accessibility ▸ Touch ▸ Back Tap) maps a double- or triple-tap on the back of the phone to a shortcut; Marques mapped double-tap to Siri and triple-tap to a screenshot, with a slight delay. In an editor's-cut update he notes you can point the double-tap at a Siri Shortcut and thus at almost anything — he set his to open Google Assistant "which is pretty hilarious." (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Sound Recognition notifies you when the mics detect an important sound (doorbell, fire alarm, running faucet) — built for the hard-of-hearing but broadly useful. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Privacy indicators: a green dot whenever any app uses the camera, an orange dot whenever the mic is accessed. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Other details: a night-mode gyroscope guide to hold the phone steady for handheld shots; group FaceTime auto-promotes anyone the AI detects using sign language to the most prominent tile; more Memoji options including masks. (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] iOS 14 supports every iPhone since the iPhone 6s and ships in the fall (likely ~September). (Marques)
- [2020-06-23] Overall verdict: not a drastic UI overhaul — "very clearly still an iPhone" — half of users will never use most of these features, but there's a lot of good stuff baked in for the nerds. (Marques)
