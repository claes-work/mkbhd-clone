# Nexus 10 Unboxing & First Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-16)

## Summary
An unboxing and first impressions of the Samsung/Google Nexus 10 — a standard unboxing that confirms
he's **back to filming in his studio after the Sandy outage**. Low persona yield, but it documents a
few dated details: his privacy discipline on camera, the front-facing speakers he immediately flags,
and the request-driven review pipeline (asking viewers what to cover in the full review).

## Key claims
- [2012-11-16] **Standout hardware notes on first handling**: **front-facing speakers** on the sides
  ("probably going to be really nice"), a surprisingly "tacky" (grippy) textured back you can hold
  one-handed, and the 2560×1600 display — "Android 4.2 looking good on this display, I'm immediately
  impressed."
- [2012-11-16] **Privacy discipline on camera, restated**: during setup he narrates "it's going to ask me
  for my Google account information, which I also won't be showing you, but I'll show you that screen" —
  and skips the account/password entry. The deliberate on-camera privacy protection seen since the 2011
  Facebook and phone tours.
- [2012-11-16] **Request-driven review pipeline, stated as method**: "the point of me asking you this
  right now is I want to see what you guys want to see in the full review — battery life, web browsing,
  gaming — because **this is how I get all the information to provide reviews for you guys.**" A clean,
  dated statement that his review coverage is audience-directed.
- [2012-11-16] **Backup-first instinct**: on the restore prompt, "I don't want to restore anything right
  now, but I definitely want to keep this tablet backed up to my Google account" — consistent with his
  cloud-first workflow ([[2012-04-25-yt-0FbjsNsfEmE]]).
- [2012-11-16] Notes an off-center front-facing camera ("a little bit off to the right… but no big deal")
  and the fast first boot; Pogo pins for charging.
- [2012-11-16] Implicitly confirms the **studio is back online** post-Sandy — this is a full studio-shot
  unboxing, not a vlog ([[2012-11-08-yt-Tqm8Izfn_oU]] was filmed in the dark).
