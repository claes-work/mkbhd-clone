# Beats Audio: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-23)

## Summary
A ★★ **consumer-education / anti-marketing-manipulation** landmark: he demystifies "Beats
Audio" (the HTC/HP software feature, distinct from Beats headphones) as **just an
equalizer** with two profiles, and exposes the **Best Buy sales trick** that uses it to
sell you. Ends on a durable principle — "everyone's got an EQ, figure out yours" — that
captures his teach-people-so-they-can't-be-fooled ethic. One of the clearest statements of
his standing Beats skepticism.

## Key claims
- [2013-05-23] **Beats Audio is fundamentally an EQ — the debunk**: "besides the marketing
  BS… it's all about the Beats Audio profile, which is basically an equalizer. Devices with
  Beats Audio have two profiles — Beats-on exaggerates the bass and highs and ignores the
  mids; Beats-off actually *under*-exaggerates the bass and highs. So flicking it on creates a
  big difference, and that's what tricks people into believing Beats devices are significantly
  better." Teaches the mechanism from first principles (what an EQ is, 20 Hz–20 kHz).
- [2013-05-23] **The Best Buy sales trick, walked through**: "imagine two laptops side by
  side — one silver without Beats, one black with Beats and a $500 premium. The salesperson
  plays a track with Beats *off* (sounds dull), then turns it *on* (you're blown away). You
  never even touched the silver one — you're already convinced it sounds bad. That's how they
  get you." Exposes a manipulative retail tactic to protect the viewer.
- [2013-05-23] **"Everyone's got an EQ, figure out yours" — the principle**: "there are a lot
  of equalizers between the studio and your ears — your headphones have their own EQ; very few
  pairs are perfectly flat. Beats headphones have an uneven frequency response, heavy on lows
  and highs… don't get tricked into buying something just because it has Beats Audio.
  Everyone's got an EQ, figure out yours." A general, empowering consumer principle.
- [2013-05-23] **Even-handed — credits the legit parts**: "the re-engineered headphone jack,
  a dedicated amp for higher-powered headphones, better stereo separation — those are actually
  decent things to have." Names what's genuinely good before debunking the rest.
- [2013-05-23] **Data-grounded**: references the frequency-response chart of Beats headphones
  (lows/highs emphasized, mids not) and his own test (ATH-M50s with Beats-off on the HTC One
  "sounded pretty good"). Backs the claim with measurement + a personal A/B.
- [2013-05-23] **The mission, stated**: "the overall message is: don't get tricked into buying
  something just because it has Beats Audio — have a listen to other stuff first and decide if
  it makes enough of a difference for you." Consumer-empowerment framing.
