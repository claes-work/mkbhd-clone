# Tesla CyberTruck Impressions & First Drive! (@mkbhd, 2019-11-22)

## Summary
Marques vlogs from Tesla's pickup-truck unveiling event in California, framing himself as a curious non-truck-person there to see one of the few 2019 tech products that didn't leak beforehand. He calls it the most "showy" Tesla event in a while — props, lasers, an excited crowd — and recounts his own shock when the Cybertruck rolled out, comparing its angular, wedge-shaped body to a "low poly count render" or a fan-made video-game mock-up. He walks through the on-stage demos with a source-critical eye: the stainless-steel exoskeleton survived a sledgehammer strike (impressive, but he suspects a single-piece metal body is a nightmare to work with), while the "armor glass" demo backfired live — a metal ball dropped from height onto a loose sheet held, but the same ball tossed at the actual prototype cracked the window twice, leading him to speculate either the prototype lacked the tough glass or Tesla has work to do. He relays the specs as the most impressive part: from $40,000 for a single-motor rear-wheel-drive version up to a tri-motor all-wheel-drive doing 0–60 in 2.9 seconds with 500-plus miles of range and 14,000 lb towing (roughly double an F-150, though the caption garbles the model year). As "first on line" for a test ride, he describes a bare, prototype-feeling interior with a yoke steering wheel like the 2020 Roadster, a Model 3-style touchscreen with new Cybertruck graphics, a camera-fed rear-view display, a six-seat layout with fold-down middle seats, and a strange marble-countertop-like blank surface up front. He notes an acceleration test in what was said to be a dual-motor variant, hitting roughly 60. His verdict lands on the design being one of the most polarizing he's ever seen — badass and Mars-rover-like to him, but not something he'd drive — and he flags a long list of open unknowns (release date, wipers, mirrors, bed material, the companion ATV) while acknowledging the hype-driven unveiling clearly worked.
