# New Mac Pro Unboxing & Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-01-14)

## Summary
The unboxing of the 2013 "trash can" Mac Pro — his new workstation, and the closing of his
own Mac-Pro arc (waited, built the Hackintosh, now the real thing). Persona value is the
config-choice reasoning (an 8-core, "the best choice"), the precise **upgradeable-but-not-
expandable distinction** (his key concept), and genuine design appreciation despite the
trash-can jokes.

## Key claims
- [2014-01-14] **Closes his own Mac-Pro arc**: "it's been so long since Apple updated their
  highest-end desktop that I made videos about it, got tired of waiting, and settled for building
  my own Hackintosh. But now that it's finally happened… I kind of have to try it out." Ties the
  purchase to his documented history (Dear Apple, the Hackintosh series, the 2013 Mac Pro
  explainer).
- [2014-01-14] **His config, chosen deliberately**: "I waited for my custom order — this is the
  8-core Xeon E5 at 3 GHz, which I believe is probably the best choice, fully loaded with dual
  D700 graphics, 1 TB flash, 64 GB RAM." A deliberate, reasoned spec (detailed in the performance
  video).
- [2014-01-14] **The upgradeable-vs-expandable distinction — his key concept**: "it's very
  upgradeable but not very expandable — there's a huge difference between those two words. This is
  the only Mac that lets you upgrade the CPU, plus the RAM, graphics, and storage, but you can't
  add anything internally — expansion is all external." His precise vocabulary on the
  upgradeability doctrine.
- [2014-01-14] **Design appreciation despite the joke**: "before you make a trash-can joke — yeah,
  it resembles a wastepaper basket (I found one on Amazon that looks exactly like it)… but no
  matter what you think of it as a machine, you've got to admit that is a hell of a good-looking
  machine. I'm a fan of technology." Credits the object's beauty even while acknowledging the meme.
- [2014-01-14] **Teases the full-review talking points**: "the things I want to talk about in the
  full review: it's incredibly small and compact, thermally basically a miracle, and the
  upgradeable-not-expandable thing." Previews his analysis.
- [2014-01-14] **Request-driven**: "so many people on Twitter asked me to do something with the
  Mac Pro when I get it — this is for you guys." The audience-programmed pattern.
