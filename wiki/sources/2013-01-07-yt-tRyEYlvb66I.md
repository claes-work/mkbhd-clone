# NVIDIA Project SHIELD at CES 2013! (@mkbhd, 2013-01-07)

## Summary
Day-one coverage from **CES 2013 in Las Vegas — his first CES attendance in the corpus** (he'd
explicitly *skipped* CES 2012 and relied on other creators). A recap of NVIDIA's keynote: cloud
gaming (GeForce Experience, GRID), the Tegra 4, and the "one more thing" — Project SHIELD, a
Tegra-4 handheld console. Notable as a biography milestone and for his read of the keynote's
Apple-style stagecraft.

## Key claims
- [2013-01-07] **First CES attendance — the biography anchor**: "I'm in Las Vegas for CES 2013 and today
  was day one, and I was at NVIDIA's press conference." A dated milestone — a year earlier he told
  viewers "if you're going to CES, put up videos because I want to see it" ([[2012-01-04-yt-NspuoxVOUD4]]);
  now he's on the floor himself.
- [2013-01-07] **Reads the keynote's structure as Apple-style stagecraft**: "NVIDIA definitely pulled a
  classic Apple keynote today — they started slow with basic announcements, revisited old tech, then
  introduced the product we were all waiting for, and wrapped it up with a 'one more thing' we weren't
  expecting at all." He analyzes the *presentation craft*, not just the products.
- [2013-01-07] **Tegra 4 — the headline chip**: quad-core with a fifth companion core (like Tegra 3),
  72 graphics cores, 4G support, "self-proclaimed the fastest mobile processor out there." Highlights a
  camera trick — **single-burst HDR** ("takes the bright and dull images and combines them
  instantaneously… lets you use HDR for any shot"), which he flags as the genuinely new capability.
- [2013-01-07] **Cloud gaming explained**: GeForce Experience auto-tunes settings to your machine; GRID
  renders graphics on NVIDIA servers so "you can have amazing quality… without an expensive gaming
  machine." He translates the concept for the viewer.
- [2013-01-07] **Project SHIELD — the "one more thing"**: a Tegra-4 handheld, "an enhanced Xbox 360
  controller with a 5-inch 720p multi-touch display," HDMI out (demoed to a 4K display), full-size USB,
  microSD, and access to GRID or your own GTX card. "Pretty much destroys the Wii U from what we saw."
- [2013-01-07] **Price left as an open question, invites the audience**: "the only thing they didn't
  mention is the price… my guess is around $400, but feel free to leave your estimates in the comments" —
  the audience-participation pattern.
- [2013-01-07] **Picks the standout**: NVIDIA's keynote was "the most impressive part of CES day one."
