# Tesla Model S Problems: My Experience! (@mkbhd, 2016-12-19)

## Summary
A candid honesty video about the recurring **power-steering failures** on his own P100D ("Apollo") — a
striking example of his balanced, own-evidence-first fairness (he loves the car AND names its serious
problems). Rich biography detail (pro ultimate frisbee, mileage), a durable framing of **Tesla's young-
company QC problem**, and an open question to the audience about whether a great-but-unreliable dream car is
worth it.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-12-19] **Names Tesla's structural QC problem fairly.** "One of the biggest complaints among owners is
  maintenance — Tesla has noted quality-control issues because they're such a young company; they don't have
  the manufacturing prowess of the super-old car companies. Tolerances get tighter with time, and they just
  don't have that time yet." A structural, non-hysterical explanation (rattles, misalignment, loose cables).
- [2016-12-19] **The serious problem: recurring power-steering failure.** Details it happening mid-left-turn
  across traffic — "the power steering essentially stops working, the wheel is straight, and I have to really
  pull it to complete the turn." First at <200 miles (a 12-day steering-rack replacement), then again at
  5,800 miles including a near-miss with a car beside him. Currently towed to service, awaiting a fix.
- [2016-12-19] **The "beta testing this car" tension — presented even-handedly.** "Different people react
  differently: some say 'they're taking care of you, they escalated it, you have a loaner, you're fine';
  others say 'it sounds like you're beta-testing this car — that's a lot of problems this early on a high-end
  car, you should return it.'" He airs both sides without hiding his own stake. Tesla escalated it (an
  engineer flying from California to New Jersey).
- [2016-12-19] **The genuine open question (his verdict is honest, not settled).** "If a car is the best on
  the road *between* issues, but has big issues every couple months — is it really worth it? I'm giving them
  another chance; if it comes back, that's strike three and we do something about it." Refuses a tidy answer;
  asks the audience.
- [2016-12-19] **BIOGRAPHY — pro ultimate frisbee + heavy mileage.** "I put ~5,800 miles on it in ~3 months —
  more than most people — mostly because I love it, and I play ultimate frisbee for the pro team based out of
  Philadelphia now, a long drive a couple times a week." Confirms he plays pro-level ultimate for a
  Philadelphia team (Dec 2016); also a Thanksgiving-in-Virginia road trip. Notes Apollo was "one of the first
  P100Ds off the assembly line" (~Sept 2016), which he ties to the early-production defects.

## Notable verbatim quotes
