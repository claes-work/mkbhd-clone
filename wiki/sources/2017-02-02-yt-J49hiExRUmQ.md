# Top 5 Future Smartphone Features! (@mkbhd, 2017-02-02)

## Summary
A "concept renders vs. reality" explainer rating five future features by feasibility — a canonical
teach-the-mechanism episode that separates the achievable from the sci-fi. Also restates his **portless-
wireless-iPhone thesis** and his personal design taste (side bezels can go, keep the top/bottom for
front-facing speakers).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-02-02] **The framing: concept renders aren't the next phone.** "In August/September it'll be peak
  iPhone-rumor-hype season and these ridiculous concept renders get shared the most — but most people know
  that's not actually the next iPhone. We are taking steps toward that reality." Media-literacy about hype.
- [2017-02-02] **#1 Bezel-less display — 8/10 feasibility.** "This is the dream — but wait, where do your
  fingers go, the webcam, the speakers?" Grounds it in real products (S7 Edge, the Xiaomi Mi Mix's piezo
  earpiece and relocated webcam). His highest-rated concept, tied to what already ships.
- [2017-02-02] **#2 Wireless charging (long-range) — 7/10; and the portless-iPhone thesis.** "Today's pad
  charging is almost *less* convenient than wired. The dream is walk-into-a-room charging (lasers/ultrasound/
  RF), but that's a long way off and I don't think we'll safely get all the way there." Ties it to Apple:
  "one reason Apple killed the jack is marching toward a **completely portless, completely wireless
  iPhone** — to remove the last (charging) port, you need wireless charging; whichever standard Apple picks
  will have a big impact."
- [2017-02-02] **#4 Flexible phones — 4/10 (teach-the-mechanism).** "Every flexible-display demo is really
  just a flexible-*display* demo — you'd need every internal component (processor, RAM, battery) to flex too,
  and they don't; and folding needs one surface to compress and the other to stretch, which makes a very
  malleable, non-durable material. I'm not even sure it's what we really want." Debunks the hype with physics.
- [2017-02-02] **#5 Graphene battery — 2/10 (teach-the-mechanism).** "Lithium keeps improving incrementally,
  but our phones still last one day if we're lucky. Graphene — a one-atom-thick carbon sheet, harder than
  diamond, more conductive than copper — could give charge-in-seconds / lasts-weeks. But the manufacturing
  isn't there: the best process makes an index-card-sized sheet for ~$1,000." Explains why the breakthrough
  isn't close. (#3 voice control rated highly — "I actually think it's pretty good already.")
- [2017-02-02] **Personal design taste.** "I'm all on board with super-thin *side* bezels, but I'll keep the
  top and bottom — I like front-facing speakers. Give me those melting-over-glass sides." His stated
  preference within the bezel-less trend.

## Notable verbatim quotes
