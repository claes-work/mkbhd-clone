# Chromebook Pixel 2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-04-24)

## Summary
A review of the Chromebook Pixel 2 that's a clean restatement of the **Halo-product doctrine**: "the target
demographic — people expected to buy it as a percentage of the population — is almost nothing, but that's
okay." He wasn't going to review it, but "I'm guilty of falling in love with an incredible piece of hardware
even if it has pretty narrow functionality." Gorgeous display, great keyboard/build, **two USB-C ports**
(his USB-C thesis in the wild), and a knock-for-battery trick — a machine that "would make an awesome Windows
or Mac laptop before you find out it runs Chrome OS."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-04-24] **The Halo-product frame.** "The target demographic… is almost nothing, but that's okay" —
  judge it by what it is, not its unit sales. He fell for the hardware "even if it does have pretty narrow
  functionality" (Chrome OS).
- [2015-04-24] **Great hardware.** All-metal, perfectly weighted (open the lid one-handed), a genuinely nice
  keyboard ("a pleasure to type on for long periods"), an OS-X-like gesture trackpad, and an "incredible"
  bright/contrasty 2560×1700 touchscreen with extra vertical pixels for web browsing — "kinetic scrolling
  felt iPad-like." A dope LED light strip + a **knock-three-times-for-battery** trick (clever, since the
  USB-C charger has no indicator light).
- [2015-04-24] **USB-C, done right — two ports.** It swapped its charger for **two USB-C ports (one on each
  side)** + a full-size SD reader + two full-size USB + headphone jack. "You can plug into either side" —
  the convenience he praised on the Chromebook Pixel in the USB-C explainer.
- [2015-04-24] **The weak points:** a "garbage 720p webcam" (at least not 480p) and speakers muffled under
  the keyboard. Otherwise excellent performance and 10+ hr battery. The Chrome Web Store "doesn't do much,
  but what it does, it does very well."
