# MKBHD YouTube Tips | #2 (@mkbhd, 2011-01-03)

## Summary
Episode 2 of the "YouTube Tips" series, on channel graphics: logo, channel
banner/design, video banner, animated intro, and watermark. States the
value of a consistent color scheme and confirms his own is red, black, and
white. Walks through tooling: a template-based intro built from another
YouTuber's tutorial, Photoshop for the channel banner/background, and a
free Photoshop-alternative image editor (name dropped from the auto-caption
— almost certainly **GIMP**, per the explicit "gnu image manipulation
program" naming in the same-week
[[2011-01-19-yt-k9tP0OB7MEA|mobile-banner tips video]]) for the video
banner, watermark, and other still graphics.

## Key claims
- [2011-01-03] **States the red/black/white color-scheme choice explicitly**:
  "all of my graphics are in some sort of form of red, black, and white ...
  those three colors together are pretty important to a consistent color
  scheme" and calls them "my favorite colors" — a dated, first-person
  confirmation of the brand palette that later matures into the crimson
  studio aesthetic.
- [2011-01-03] Frames consistent branding as a starting-out priority: "when
  you're just starting out, or when you're actually building your brand, it
  can be a really good idea to establish a really solid color scheme that
  people can remember you by."
- [2011-01-03] Splits his graphics tooling by task: Photoshop for the
  channel banner/background; the free GIMP-like editor (name caption-
  garbled, see Notes) for the video banner, watermark, and other still
  graphics — an early instance of matching tool to task rather than one
  all-purpose app.
- [2011-01-03] Self-reports the animated intro as template-derived: "my
  intro was created using a template and using a tutorial from another
  YouTube user" and took "a couple days to actually put together" for a
  5-second result.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)

> and the three colors combined happen to be my favorite colors."
