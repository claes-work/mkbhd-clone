# HTC U11: The Squeeze Phone?! (@mkbhd, 2017-07-01)

## Summary
Review of the HTC U11 — "already comeback player of the year for HTC," a redemption after the U Ultra's
"poor use of space." The clone-relevant core is the **gimmick-vs-useful test applied to Edge Sense** (the
squeeze feature): it's genuinely configurable but too-sensitive/too-stiff at the extremes, so its value
hinges on calibration and how you hold the phone.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-07-01] **"Comeback player of the year" — the redemption from the U Ultra.** "Remember how poor a use
  of space and money the U Ultra was less than 6 months ago? At a similar price, the U11 cleaned up real
  nice — they've already wiped the floor with their previous phone; pretty much no reason to buy the U Ultra
  anymore." A pointed callback to his U-Ultra "poor use of space" rant ([[2017-03-17-yt-Nq2Kum-eG8g]]).
- [2017-07-01] **Edge Sense (squeeze) = gimmick or legit? — the calibration verdict.** "The headlining
  feature: squeeze the phone (pressure sensors in the lower half) to launch any app/action. Is it a gimmick or
  legit? It depends on how your hands wrap around it and how natural squeezing feels mid-use. Set the pressure
  too low and it triggers accidentally when you pick it up off a table or pull it from your pocket; too high
  and you need a two-handed vice grip. You have to calibrate it right — I found level 4–5 best." The gimmick-
  vs-useful test in action (a feature that only earns its keep if tuned).
- [2017-07-01] **"Specs aren't everything… but here they are."** "Specs don't tell the whole story, but here
  they do a pretty good job." Snapdragon 835, 4 GB, 3,000 mAh, Quad HD LCD, microSD, on the latest Android
  7.1.1 — "one of the smoothest, most responsive Android phones out there (short, simple animations — that's
  what you want), though I'm less confident that full-time smoothness lasts as long as stock Android." A
  balanced specs-plus-longevity-caveat read.
- [2017-07-01] **The camera is "killing it."** "12 MP f/1.7 with OIS — OIS really matters and helps a lot in
  low light; same Snapdragon 835 as the OnePlus 5 doing instant auto-HDR. It's been taking killer shots."
  Credits a genuine camera strength (a rarity he flags for HTC).
- [2017-07-01] **Battery punches above its number.** "A 3,000 mAh cell we're seeing outperform phones with
  bigger cells and last all day, thanks to optimization — impressive given the display." The optimization-
  beats-raw-capacity read. Notes the LCD "can struggle outdoors." Still no headphone jack.
