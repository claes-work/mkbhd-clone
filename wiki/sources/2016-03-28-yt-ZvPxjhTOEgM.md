# Samsung Galaxy S7 Edge Review! (@mkbhd, 2016-03-28)

## Summary
By-popular-demand "which one should you get" video framed as the differences between the S7 Edge and
the regular S7. He praises Samsung for **simplifying the lineup** (four S6 variants → just two S7s),
walks the real differences (size, marginal dimensions, bigger battery, edge-panel software), and lands
firmly: he **prefers the Edge** and would "almost never recommend the regular S7 over it." The edge
software gets a pointed critique — great potential capped by **no third-party app support**.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-03-28] **Praises the simplified lineup.** "Samsung simplified their lineup a lot from last
  year — S6, S6 Active, S6 Edge, S6 Edge+ — this year we just get two: S7 and S7 Edge, that's it."
  (Approval of product-line discipline.)
- [2016-03-28] **The pixel-density number doesn't matter.** Both are Quad HD Super AMOLED; the bigger
  Edge has a slightly lower ppi "but you won't notice — they're both ridiculous." His recurring
  "diminishing returns past a threshold" stance on spec numbers.
- [2016-03-28] **Edge fixes the S6 Edge's ergonomics.** The old S6 Edge "felt kind of like a blade in
  the hand"; new back/side-rail shaping makes the S7 Edge "not as hard to pick up or literally a pain
  to hold, even when the edge is so thin — while still being easily one of the prettiest phones out
  there, hands down."
- [2016-03-28] **Bigger battery = the real reason he likes the extra size.** "~10–15% longer battery
  life in the Edge thanks to the bigger battery — that right there is enough of a reason for me to
  really like having the extra space inside." The "fill the body with battery" value again.
- [2016-03-28] **Edge-panel software: promise capped by no third-party support.** Nostalgic callback —
  "years ago I used to do exactly this on my Galaxy S3 with an app called **SwipePad**." Task Edge
  "could be my favorite Android skin feature ever, but it only supports 9 first-party apps — I'd love
  to add third-party actions (a new tweet, open the Instagram camera) but I can't, and I don't think
  they plan on adding it." Great-idea-held-back-by-execution critique.
- [2016-03-28] **Verdict — "I definitely prefer the Edge."** Two reasons: "I have bigger hands, I
  usually use bigger phones," and "I'm a bit more of a power user and the larger battery is a big
  help." Would "almost never recommend the regular S7 over the Edge unless you really don't like the
  edge features or have particularly small hands." (Self-characterization: big-phone power user.)
