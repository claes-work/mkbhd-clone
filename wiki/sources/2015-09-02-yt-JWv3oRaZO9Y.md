# Moto 360 2nd Gen Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-09-02)

## Summary
Hands-on with the 2nd-gen Moto 360 — "they took the old one, made improvements based on our feedback, and
made it a lot better," landing just as **Android Wear became iPhone-compatible**. Three sizes now, deep
**MotoMaker customization** (case/bezel/band independently), and quick-swap bands. He defends the
"flat-tire" bottom bar again ("after using the original for so long it's really not much of an issue —
it's either that or bigger bezels"). Opens with a signature self-aware joke about press-event lighting.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-09-02] **The self-aware opener (voice/humor).** "Every press event I go to has terrible lighting,
  so to keep it consistent this whole video will have terrible lighting."
- [2015-09-02] **Android Wear + iPhone.** The v2 lands "just in time for Android Wear to become officially
  compatible with the iPhone."
- [2015-09-02] **Three versions + MotoMaker.** Small (42 mm), women's (42 mm, specialized bands/materials),
  large (46 mm, the original's size). MotoMaker lets you pick **case color, bezel color, and band
  separately** — "a bezel color independent of the body, which is pretty cool" — with quick-swap lugs ("I
  did it myself with my huge hands, only took a minute").
- [2015-09-02] **The flat-tire defense (restated).** The bottom bar (housing sensors, wrapping the LCD) is
  "really not that much of an issue to me anymore… yes, a full uninterrupted circle would be cool, but it's
  either that or bigger bezels — and Motorola is really proud of the huge screen-to-bezel ratio."
