# Google Pixel Unboxing! (Best Android 7.1 Features) (@mkbhd, 2016-10-18)

## Summary
After ~4 days with the Pixel XL, five favorite Android 7.1 observations. The clone-relevant meat is his
verdict that **Google matched Apple's 3D Touch with a simple long-press** (an efficiency-beats-fancy-tech
read), his warming to software-only design choices (swipe-up app drawer, circular icons "at least all the
same size now"), and further use-based praise for the OS-baked Assistant.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-10-18] **Google matched 3D Touch with a long-press — and that's smarter.** "Long-press any icon
  to get in-app shortcuts — this reminds you of 3D Touch on the iPhone. While Apple developed a really
  technically-impressive proprietary pressure-sensing display, Google just built it into a long-press. I
  think it's cool they figured that out and baked it right in." An efficiency-over-fancy-hardware take —
  and a candid "honestly, how much do you actually use 3D Touch?" aside (echoing his own admission).
- [2016-10-18] **Circular icons: consistency is the real win.** "There are circles everywhere — not
  entirely consistent (Allo and Dropbox aren't circles), but the one thing you can say is they're now all
  the same size. That minor adjustment makes a big difference to feeling clean." Values visual consistency
  over the shape itself.
- [2016-10-18] **The swipe-up app drawer — grew to really like it.** "You swipe up to get the app drawer
  instead of pressing a button — a way bigger touch target, easier to reach, and it gives me an extra
  dock slot." A small-UX-change-that-matters observation.
- [2016-10-18] **Using the Assistant more than Now-on-tap.** "I'm actually using the assistant a bit more
  than I used Now on tap — say 'OK Google' from any screen or even with the phone off." Confirms the
  Assistant's stickiness from real use.
- [2016-10-18] **The fingerprint-swipe + gesture 'moves.'** Favorite of the new 'moves': "swipe up/down
  on the fingerprint reader to pull the notification tray from anywhere" (some accidental swipes, but a
  useful shortcut with intent), plus double-twist-to-flip-camera and double-tap-power-for-camera. Likes
  configurable physical-ish shortcuts (consistent with the "good buttons"/custom-key thread).
- [2016-10-18] **Cross-phone-switching honesty.** "I've switched phones a lot between on-screen and
  off-screen buttons — I came from the OnePlus 3, so I found myself tapping empty space to go back." A
  reviewer's-life detail (his daily driver churn).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "While Apple sort of developed this really technically impressive proprietary display technology for
> sensing pressure, Google just built it into a long press."
> "At least all the icons are the same size now — you couldn't say that before, but that minor adjustment
> actually makes a big difference to feeling clean."
