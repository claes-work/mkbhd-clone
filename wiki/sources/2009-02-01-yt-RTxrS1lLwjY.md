# HD Review: Microsoft Wireless 6000 Mouse (@mkbhd, 2009-02-01) ★

## Summary
An **origin-era product review** and the most-viewed video in this batch (~304K views) —
15-year-old Marques reviews the **Microsoft Wireless Laser Mouse 6000 (v2)** and, notably,
splits it into an explicit **two-part structure: a hardware review, then a software
review.** The hardware half tours the smooth (un-notched) scroll wheel, forward/back
thumb buttons, the laser, the USB receiver, and a receiver-storage slot that powers the
mouse off to save battery. The software half — recorded via CamStudio screen capture,
with a spoken "click the HD link at the bottom" — walks Microsoft's IntelliPoint button-

pointer options). This is an early, clean template of MKBHD's review DNA: **structured
segmentation, hands-on demonstration, and a practical tip** — here, that a cracked bundled
CD is no problem because "you can download it anyway... I recommend you download the
drivers from the manufacturer's website" (there may be a newer firmware/software version
than the disc). ★ **L3-candidate** — early review-methodology material.
