# Top 5 Google I/O Announcements! (@mkbhd, 2012-06-28)

## Summary
A same-day recap of Google I/O 2012 day one — Jelly Bean (Project Butter, actionable notifications,
Google Now's cards UI), the Nexus 7, the Nexus Q, Google+ updates, and the Google Glass skydive demo.
He frames I/O as "basically a mini WWDC in one day for Google," a symmetry that echoes his
even-handed WWDC-copying framing two weeks earlier. Mostly event coverage, but it dates his reactions
to several products he'd later review or wear.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-28] **Jelly Bean's Project Butter, with the target stated**: Google claims "the entire frame
  rate of the entire operating system at 60 frames per second… that sounds really really good to me,
  hopefully they can actually make that realistic with the devices that come out in the future." He
  credits the goal while flagging the execution risk.
- [2012-06-28] **Actionable notifications called the standard-setter**: "Android already has some of the
  best notifications of any operating system out there, period — mobile or desktop… now you can take
  action on your notifications from the notification tray without ever opening any applications." A dated
  statement of the notifications-are-Android's-strength view that recurs across his coverage.
- [2012-06-28] **Google Now framed as adaptive/anticipatory** — "designed to just figure you out": uses
  location + calendar to warn you when to leave, flags flight delays, surfaces the next bus, tracks your
  teams. He reports it as the biggest software idea, distinct from a Siri clone.
- [2012-06-28] **Nexus 7 = "watch out Kindle Fire, because this [aims] directly at you"** at $199 — his
  single most-excited announcement, and the setup for the first-impressions review three weeks later
  ([[2012-07-18-yt-eG0As9lWP7I]]). (Note the framing shift: here "directly at" the Kindle Fire; by the
  hands-on he argues it *isn't* aimed at the Fire at all — a dated evolution of the take.)
- [2012-06-28] **Nexus Q assessed skeptically on price** — the honest read on a product he likes the idea
  of: "it runs 300 bucks… it's going to be a tough sell." (The Nexus Q was indeed pulled before retail.)
- [2012-06-28] **The Google Glass skydive demo — a superlative**: "that is by far the most badass thing I
  have ever seen at any keynote from any company ever." A dated, characteristic burst of genuine
  enthusiasm, and continuous with his Project Glass interest ([[2012-04-06-yt-_hRCAg9jFu8]]).
- [2012-06-28] **I/O framed as Google's WWDC** — the symmetry: "basically a mini WWDC in one day for
  Google," consistent with the both-sides framing in [[2012-06-11-yt-nY15RGU2QJM]].
- [2012-06-28] Jelly Bean rollout dated: mid-July to the Galaxy Nexus, Nexus S, Motorola Xoom, and the
  Nexus 7; the Nexus 7 is "the first device to ship with Google Chrome as the stock browser."
