# MKBHD YouTube Tips | #3 (@mkbhd, 2011-01-20)

## Summary
Episode 3 of the "YouTube Tips" series: a budget-tiered camera/camcorder
buying guide for aspiring hardware reviewers, from sub-$100 point-and-shoots
(Flip Video/Flip Ultra) through the $100–200 "clear winner" (Sanyo Xacti
CG10), $200–500 point-and-shoots and Canon Vixia camcorders, up to $500+
DSLRs (his own Canon T2i, the then-upcoming Canon T3i, Nikon D3100, Canon
60D) and professional-tier cameras (Canon 5D Mark II). Recommends staying
under $500 for most YouTube creators and frames DSLRs as the low-light/
depth-of-field ceiling for the format.

## Key claims
- [2011-01-20] **Explicit budget guidance for aspiring creators**: "you can
  probably stay under 500 bucks for anything you want to do on YouTube and
  get away with it pretty easily — anything over $500 could be considered
  overkill, but I'd rather have overkill than underkill."
- [2011-01-20] Names his own gear and its provenance: "I use the Canon T2i,
  which came out last year in February," and flags the then-unreleased
  Canon T3i as not yet worth buying: "I wouldn't quite buy that just yet."
- [2011-01-20] States his top budget-tier recommendation with reasoning: the
  Sanyo Xacti CG10 ($100–200) for its 3" flip-out LCD, 5x optical zoom, and
  lens image stabilization — "by far my favorite camcorder in this range."
- [2011-01-20] Frames DSLRs as the video-quality ceiling for the format:
  "the DSLRs shoot the best quality video you can get, in low light and ...
  indoor/outdoors — it's just an awesome thing that you can shoot video
  with DSLRs," while noting they're "definitely not for the beginning
  YouTube user."
