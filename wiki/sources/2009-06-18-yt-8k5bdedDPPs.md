# Reviewed: UniBrain Fire-i Webcam (@mkbhd, 2009-06-18)

## Summary
Full review of the UniBrain Fire-i, a FireWire-connected digital camera he uses as a
webcam. Standard origin-era hardware-review structure: physical tour (dual FireWire
ports for daisy-chaining a multi-camera rig, manual-focus twist ring, tripod mount),
software install ("basically a matter of clicking Next Next"), then recording specs
(640×480 up to 30fps, 4.3mm focal lens, adjustable frame rate down to 3.75fps, sleep
mode, color-bar generator). Flags a real compatibility gap on his own gear — his
desktop has 6-pin FireWire, his laptop does not — and floats using the camera for
"Ustream or blog TV," reviving a platform question ("Ustream versus blog TV") he had
already put to viewers. Ends on a production note: he says he'll try to sync mic audio
to the video feed going forward, an explicit acknowledgment of an audio/video sync
problem in his current setup.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-18] **Multi-camera daisy-chain use case**: FireWire ports on both sides of
  the UniBrain Fire-i let "you can have a multi camera set up, a bunch of these
  circled around or in a line."
- [2009-06-18] **Hardware compatibility gap, self-disclosed**: his desktop has 6-pin
  FireWire but "my laptop actually does not," so he'll need a 4-pin-to-6-pin adapter to
  use it there.
- [2009-06-18] **Recording specs**: 640×480 up to 30fps, 4.3mm focal lens, selectable
  frame rates (30/15/7.5/3.75 fps), software sleep mode, color-bar generator.
- [2009-06-18] **Positions it against Ustream/blog TV**, a live-streaming question he
  says he's "been asking you guys about" — an early sign of considering a live format.
- [2009-06-18] **No score given** — unusual for an origin-era hardware review; ends on
  "you're definitely gonna want to check this out for ease of use" rather than a

- [2009-06-18] **Production self-correction announced**: "tell me if you like the new
