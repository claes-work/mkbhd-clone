# iPhone SE Review! (@mkbhd, 2016-04-05)

## Summary
Review of the iPhone SE — "the iPhone 5's body with all the same features from the iPhone 6s,"
i.e. "a 4-year-old flagship on the outside and an 8-month-old flagship on the inside." He treats it
as Apple answering the standing demand for a *small flagship with no compromises*, and comes away
more open to small phones than he expected — while dinging the recycled design, the sub-720p display,
and (loudly) the 16GB base tier.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-04-05] **The recycled-design critique.** "I still would have liked to have seen a new design
  for a brand-new compact phone in 2016 instead of a recycled one." The only external tells vs. an
  iPhone 5s are the rose-gold option and the "SE" (special edition) badge. Recycling the old body is
  also *why* it lacks 2nd-gen Touch ID and Force Touch ("not quite enough room").
- [2016-04-05] **Answering the small-flagship demand.** "People talk about it all the time — what's
  the best small smartphone that doesn't make any compromises? This phone is definitely trying to fill
  those shoes." Notes the 5s was "my first iPhone ever," so he likes the squarish, easy-to-hold design.
- [2016-04-05] **Converted (a bit) on small phones.** After years on big phones a tiny phone "gets
  swallowed up in my hands — no way I could use this every day," but "it actually turned out to be not
  that bad… now I feel like I want to see more high-end smaller Android phones too." A reachability
  argument: small phones let you reach all four corners without adjusting your grip. Still: "not saying
  I could personally do it forever — it's just not my thing, but I get it."
- [2016-04-05] **"The best camera in a small smartphone."** iPhone 6s internals → A9, 2GB RAM, 12 MP
  sensor, quick autofocus, 4K video (no OIS). Weak spot: the front camera *didn't* get upgraded — "a
  pretty weak 1.2 MP shooter, sorry selfie people." Surprisingly good battery life despite a small
  1642 mAh cell, "I'd attribute that to the A9 chip."
- [2016-04-05] **"I'd pretty much never tell anyone to buy a 16-gig phone."** At $400 "you're only
  getting 16GB, and I'd pretty much never tell anyone to buy a 16-gig phone" — so realistically it's a
  $500–600 phone you'd buy only if you *really* want small. The standing 16GB-needs-to-die stance
  applied to a verdict. (See [[../../persona/beliefs.md]].)
