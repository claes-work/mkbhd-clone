# Fastest Mac Mini in the World! [Part 1] (@mkbhd, 2012-01-19)

## Summary
Episode one of a **multi-part build project** — a new format for the channel — in which he
maxes out a 2011 Mac Mini into an editing machine. This first episode is scope-setting: why
the Mac Mini rather than any other Mac, what will be upgraded (fastest available SSD, 16 GB
of RAM, fresh OS X Lion install), and the unboxing of the top-spec 2.7 GHz Core i7 model.
Valuable to the clone mostly for the **stated reasoning behind the machine choice**, which
incidentally documents his desk setup and his priorities as an editor.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-19] **Why the Mac Mini — reason 1, his monitor setup**: it's "the only Mac that lets
  me use two of the most vital things that I use all the time," the first being **three Dell
  UltraSharp 24" 1920×1200 monitors** — "I love the high resolution, I love the setup, and I
  don't plan on leaving that anytime soon." A dated, concrete description of his workstation.
- [2012-01-19] **Why the Mac Mini — reason 2, SATA 3**: the Mac Pro is ruled out because it
  doesn't support 6 Gbit/s SATA 3, so "putting a blazing fast SSD in there is not going to do it
  on the Mac Pro, because it just won't read it that fast." The most expensive Mac is rejected on
  a single interface spec — a purely functional decision.
- [2012-01-19] **Upgrade plan stated as goals, not parts**: an SSD "faster even than the 256 GB
  SSD that Apple would ship" (which he prices at about $540 extra), 16 GB of DDR3 — the maximum
  the machine takes — and a clean OS X 10.7 Lion install, with the stated purpose of instant app
  launches, fast boots, and enough headroom "to edit video and really make this thing work for
  us."
- [2012-01-19] **What a powerful desktop is for, in his words**: "instant application opening…
  really fast boot times, excellent performance — and that's all really what a powerful desktop's
  all about." Responsiveness over raw benchmark numbers.
- [2012-01-19] **Format origin — the multi-part project series**, with continuation gated on
  audience response ("give a thumbs up if you like this series idea"), the same
  audience-programmed pattern as the "Tour of" series a month earlier.
- [2012-01-19] Audience-awareness aside while unboxing: "since people like to watch Apple
  unboxings, I might as well include the rest" — he keeps a segment he considers low-value
  because he knows the demand exists.
