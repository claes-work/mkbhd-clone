# Top 5 Fastest Discontinued Tech Products! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-04)

## Summary
A tech-history listicle of products killed fastest (Power Mac G4 Cube, Nexus Q, Microsoft
Kin, HP TouchPad, HTC First, Cisco Flip Live at #1 — cancelled the day before launch). Low
persona yield, but it opens with a clear statement of the **product-lifecycle / planned-
obsolescence** framing and includes a creator-culture nod to the Flip camera.

## Key claims
- [2013-07-04] **The product-lifecycle + planned-obsolescence framing**: "products are expected
  to come out about once a year now… some companies are accused of *planned obsolescence* —
  intentionally making products only valid until the next version, to get you to buy the next
  one." Names the concept (his incentive-lens on the release treadmill; cf. the "Too Many
  Android Phones" rant on perceptual obsolescence).
- [2013-07-04] **The lesson, stated plainly**: "what did we learn? You'd want to know a product
  is bad *before* you release it — that's the theme of all these." A wry takeaway.
- [2013-07-04] **Creator-culture nod — the Flip camera**: "those flip cameras were insanely
  popular among YouTubers and vloggers — lightweight, inexpensive, hold-it-out-and-record… Cisco
  bought the company for $590M, then cut the whole thing one day before the Flip Live launched
  (-1 days)." A creator-tooling history datum.
- [2013-07-04] **Credits a company that handled failure well**: "Sony's eVilla was cut in 3
  months, but they handled it really well — refunded everyone who returned it, and refunded the
  $21.95/month internet plan." Even-handed — credits good behavior even in a failure list.
- [2013-07-04] **Voice/humor register**: the Kin "backstory is hilariously depressing… every
  line of that Wikipedia article is cringe-worthy but hilarious"; Google Wave "lasted 75 days —
  shorter than Kim Kardashian's wedding." His internet-culture comedic register.
