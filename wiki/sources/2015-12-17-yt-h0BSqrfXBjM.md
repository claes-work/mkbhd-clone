# Tech of the Year: Tablets! (@mkbhd, 2015-12-17)

## Summary
A year-end "Tech of the Year: Tablets" comparing the Pixel C, iPad Pro, and Surface Pro 4 — unified by the
observation that **2015's tablets all "really want to be laptops"** (two "Pro" names, all with first-party
keyboards). His read: the **keyboard accessory tells the story of each**, and the **Surface Pro 4 is the
one that actually gets there** (full Windows 10, the best keyboard). The Pixel C is beautiful hardware
undercut by an awkward keyboard (and "probably never meant to run Android"); the iPad Pro is touch-first and
"not fully replacing a laptop."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-12-17] **Tablets are blurring into laptops.** "What do these three have in common? Two have 'Pro'
  in the name, they all have a first-party keyboard, and they all really want to be laptops."
- [2015-12-17] **Pixel C — beautiful, awkward.** "One of my favorite pieces of recent Google hardware"
  (unibody metal, great speakers, the always-on color bar), but the **most awkward keyboard** ("almost
  pointless… just the letters, no Android shortcut keys") and stuttering performance out of the box. Cites
  Ron Amadeo's theory that "it was probably never meant to run Android." (Bonus: strong enough magnets to
  stick to a fridge — "your smart fridge.")
- [2015-12-17] **iPad Pro — touch-first, not a laptop.** Huge, gorgeous, "obscenely fast (the A9X is
  legit)," best tablet-only battery. But "touch is the primary interaction — tons of iPad apps made for your
  hands." The Apple Pencil is "objectively a pretty good stylus (wish it had an eraser / didn't charge like
  a giant lollipop)"; the $170 keyboard adds command keys but "you're definitely not fully replacing a
  laptop."
- [2015-12-17] **Surface Pro 4 — the real laptop replacement.** "The closest to a laptop of any of them"
  — full metal, fans/vents, spec-able to Core i5/i7 + 16 GB + 1 TB SSD, full-size USB/SD, **full Windows 10
  (real desktop apps)**. Its keyboard is "by far the best of the bunch — you really shouldn't get one
  without it," with real key travel + a mechanical-click touchpad with gestures. "Really part of buying a
  Surface Pro."
