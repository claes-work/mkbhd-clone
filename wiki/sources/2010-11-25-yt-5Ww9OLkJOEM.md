# Canon EOS Rebel T2i Unboxing (@mkbhd, 2010-11-25)

## Summary
Unboxing of Marques's own Canon EOS Rebel T2i DSLR + 18-55mm kit lens, bought at a
discounted price from his "favorite online retailer" as his first DSLR ("intro to
DSLRs"), explicitly bought in preparation for the then-upcoming Canon T3i and to
replace his prior point-and-shoot (a Sanyo Xacti CG10). Walks through the box
contents (manual, software, lens strap, charger, battery, USB/component cables) and
the camera body itself, closing with a promise of a full review to follow.

## Key claims
- [2010-11-25] **Confirms the DSLR purchase as a deliberate upgrade, priced-driven**:
  "I saw the price of this... at my favorite online retailer and I was really really
  impressed, could not say no... it was a ridiculously good price" — frames the
  purchase decision explicitly around a price trigger, not just desire for the
  camera.
  - Also frames it as **entry-level DSLR positioning ahead of a successor**: "getting
    this as my intro to DSLRs... in preparation for the Canon T3i in February,"
    dating an already-anticipated successor camera as of Nov 2010.
- [2010-11-25] **States the Sanyo Xacti CG10 is being retired**: "this camera and my
  point and shoot" are being replaced by the T2i, and this is "the last of the Sanyo
  Xacti CG10 videos" on the channel — a concrete production-gear transition date,
  consistent with the same-week note in
  [[wiki/sources/2010-11-17-yt-7vZwUStVqdw]].
- [2010-11-25] **Ties the red-and-black color preference to product packaging**: "the
  rebel box is my favorite colors of course red and black" — extends the previously
  tracked red-and-black aesthetic-preference thread (see prior synthesis passes) to a
  new, unprompted context (product packaging, not branding).
