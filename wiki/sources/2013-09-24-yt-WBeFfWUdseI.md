# LG G2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-09-24)

## Summary
A review of the LG G2 — "The Almost Nexus," read as a preview of the next Nexus (LG being
the hardware partner). Persona value is a clean **great-hardware-ruined-by-bad-software**
split (his stock-Android preference again), the honest rear-buttons adaptation curve, and
recurring taste notes (big hands, glossy-plastic dislike).

## Key claims
- [2013-09-24] **"The Almost Nexus" — reads it as a next-Nexus preview**: "Google picks LG for
  the Nexus hardware, and LG adapts its current flagship into what Google wants — the Nexus 4
  came from the Optimus G, so this G2 is a peek at what we'll probably see running stock Android
  KitKat." His pattern-reading of the OEM-Nexus relationship.
- [2013-09-24] **Great hardware, ruined by the software — the split**: "a potentially incredible
  device ruined by awful LG software… the biggest downsides come from that LG skin — ugly,
  inconsistent, tacky, unnecessary animations, changes just for the sake of changes. The first
  time I pulled down the notification drawer, my jaw was on the floor at how bad it looked." His
  hardware/software separation + stock-Android preference, at full strength.
- [2013-09-24] **Copies-the-S4 observation**: "a lot of what LG did involves copying what Samsung
  did with the S4 — it's like they told their engineers 'see that S4 camera feature? build it.'
  Everything but the kitchen sink." The feature-parity-chasing read.
- [2013-09-24] **The rear-buttons adaptation curve — honest**: "day 1–2 you think it's insane;
  day 3–5 you learn where they are; day 6–10 you're totally used to it. I don't even look
  anymore." A lived, patient assessment of an unusual design.
- [2013-09-24] **Camera praise (a Nexus-hope signal)**: "the camera is a strong point — top-three
  Android cameras I've used, thanks to the sensor, glass, and OIS — which gives me a glimmer of
  hope for the next Nexus." Credits the hardware.
- [2013-09-24] **Taste notes restated**: big hands ("even if you don't have massive hands like
  me"); glossy-plastic dislike ("I'm not a fan… you can always add a case or decal like I did
  with my S4"); wishes it were "lighter, cleaner, and closer to stock like a Nexus."
