# 4K Video: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-01-18)

## Summary
An "Explained!" primer on **4K resolution**, prompted by CES 2013 — the pedagogical,
teach-the-fundamentals mode he's built a brand on. Restates the **viewing-distance-
determines-required-pixel-density** thesis with worked numbers, lays out the **two
barriers to 4K adoption (price + content scarcity)**, and makes a dated prediction about
when 4K reaches living rooms. A good, on-brand "Explained" source and a clean statement
of how he thinks about resolution and technology-adoption curves.

## Key claims
- [2013-01-18] **The viewing-distance thesis, quantified — a core framework**: pixel
  density needs scale with how far the screen sits from your face. His worked figures:
  phone ~300 PPI (held <1 ft), 15" laptop ~120 PPI (2 ft), 55" 1080p TV ~40 PPI (10–20
  ft), 55" 4K TV >100 PPI. "Sitting 10, 15, 20 feet away… you're never going to be able
  to see those pixels at 40 PPI." The same display-judging-by-viewing-distance doctrine
  ([[2012-origin]] arc), applied to 4K.
- [2013-01-18] **What 4K looks like — his signature line**: "a really good 4K video looks
  incredible… it's almost a little below that level of looking through a window — a
  surreal sort of realism, especially if you have a high frame rate." The
  window-comparison he'd use for years.
- [2013-01-18] **UHD ≠ "4K" precisely — spec precision**: "4K actually varies, there
  isn't an exact standard yet, but the closest thing we have is UHD… 3840×2160." Careful
  about a marketing term's imprecision (his standing habit).
- [2013-01-18] **Two barriers to adoption — price and content**: (1) "not a little bit
  expensive — new-car, $25,000–$30,000 expensive," and (2) "there's barely any 4K
  content… remember when we saw 3D TVs and nobody makes 3D content." The technology-is-
  ahead-of-its-ecosystem thesis applied to 4K (cf. the Nexus 10 content-optimization
  caveat, [[2012-12-17-yt-NyoM_i1jJZc]]).
- [2013-01-18] **A dated adoption-curve prediction**: "we saw them on the show floor at
  CES for the past 2 years — that's kind of where innovation starts, and maybe in another
  2 or 3 years it'll be in our living rooms." His model of how CES-floor tech diffuses to
  consumers (4K did roughly hit mainstream living rooms by ~2015–16 — a broadly-correct
  call worth tracking).
- [2013-01-18] **Web video is the real 4K hub, and a YouTube-vs-Vimeo compression take**:
  "the largest hub for 4K content is the internet, specifically web video like YouTube…
  but it gets compressed way down, ~50 Mbps tops." Recommends Vimeo for "the clearest /
  littlest compression." A dated platform-quality judgment (from a heavy uploader's seat).
- [2013-01-18] **A creator "fun fact"**: "when you upload a 4K/5K/8K video, YouTube gives
  you a special 'Original' button for anything over 1080p." Insider platform knowledge,
  dated.
- [2013-01-18] **Discloses he doesn't own the gear yet**: "I don't have a 4K TV
  personally, but I saw a ton of them at CES." Honest about the limits of his hands-on.
