# Google Live Cases: Super Custom! (@mkbhd, 2016-04-14)

## Summary
A near-exclusive first look (Google shared the process with him) at **Live Cases** for the Nexus 6P /
5X — custom-printed cases you design in a web app from your own photo/art or a Google Maps location,
with an NFC-tagged shortcut button inside. Ties directly into his recurring **customization / make-
your-device-one-of-a-kind** theme, even as a self-professed non-case-person.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-04-14] **The customization-as-identity theme.** "It gets me thinking more about customizing
  your device — I did a Tech episode all about custom tech where you can skin your phone, get it custom
  painted; this is another thing to add to that list." His standing thread that personalizing hardware
  is worthwhile (links to the custom-tech thread and his skins-over-cases stance).
- [2016-04-14] **How it works.** Upload an image (or drop a Google Maps location) into Google's Live
  Case Design Studio web app, position/customize (dual-tone, stripes, patterns), choose matte or
  glossy; "it's a direct conversion of the image, a quality print — a one-of-a-kind limited-edition
  Nexus case." Uses his own wallpaper art (a facet by artist **Justin Maller**, credited/linked) and
  one of "the best smartphone photos I've ever taken, in the fog with some triangles for effect."
- [2016-04-14] **The case earns qualified case-person approval.** "Obviously I'm not a huge case
  person, but these are designed specifically by Google for these phones — the port, button and
  fingerprint cutouts are on point, and there's a bit of protection: the lip extends just past the
  glass so a face-down phone won't scratch." A rare case endorsement, on precision + protection grounds.
- [2016-04-14] **The NFC shortcut button — "cherry on top."** Every Live Case has an NFC-tagged
  physical button; the companion app lets you bind it to an action (toggle flashlight/Wi-Fi, open the
  camera, or launch any app — his example: "I calculate a lot of things, so I'll set it to open the
  calculator"). Hardware-software integration he clearly enjoys.
- [2016-04-14] **The Google-Maps-location idea.** You can make the case from a meaningful map spot —
  "a dope bridge, your favorite park, **the location of a new studio**" (a wink at his week's news);
  demos the Golden Gate Bridge as an overhead map view. "Pretty much guarantee no one else has a case
  like that."
