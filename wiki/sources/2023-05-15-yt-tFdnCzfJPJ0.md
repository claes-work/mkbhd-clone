# This is the Dumbest Product I've Ever Reviewed (@mkbhd, 2023-05-15)

## Summary
A solo review of the **$1,000 Dyson Zone air-purifying headphones** — part ANC headphones,
part wearable air purifier with a visor that channels filtered air over the nose and mouth.
Marques buys one despite believing up front it's "a terrible product." His verdict splits:
as **headphones** they're genuinely decent (clever joystick controls, excellent
transparency mode, ~50h battery, premium all-metal build) but overpriced-sounding and
absurdly heavy (588g, "like wearing a football helmet"); as an **air purifier** the whole
premise fails — no facial seal, so he could still smell gasoline, pollen and a Porta-John;
battery drops from 50h to 2h with filtration on; and it's not HEPA- or N95-grade. The
video brings in **guest expert Doctor Mike** (physician/YouTuber) to explain the medical
flaws, and closes by reframing the product through Barbara Corcoran's **loss-leader**
concept: it exists to generate press for Dyson, not to sell. Recurring MKBHD frames: the
**two-in-one law** and "only branding explains this." Marques-fronted (persona data);
Doctor Mike's medical statements are guest-attributed and quarantined.

## Key claims
- [2023-05-15] He bought the Zone already believing it's bad but reviews it anyway: "I'm
  still here to do my job and find out anyway." The product "could only have come from
  Dyson — it makes no sense but makes all the sense in the world."
- [2023-05-15] Dyson thesis: Dyson "is a motors and filtration company" that has turned
  niche products (stick vacuums, bladeless fans, the hair dryer) into cult classics by
  charging a lot successfully — but it also has "complete misses" (the $850 floor lamp,
  the abandoned car). (Aside: he uses the Dyson hair dryer to heat dbrand skins, not his hair.)
- [2023-05-15] As headphones: 588g (vs 388g AirPods Max, 288g ATH-M50) makes them
  "feel like a football helmet"; the right-ear-cup **joystick** for volume/track/scrub is
  genuinely clever and even scrubs inside Apple Music/Spotify/podcasts; sound is "okay…
  like some two-to-three-hundred-dollar headphones" though built like $400 ones;
  **transparency mode is "the best I've heard from anything that isn't AirPods."**
- [2023-05-15] As an air purifier it fails because there is deliberately **no seal**
  ("nobody wants to walk around breathing from a literal tube"); he "could still smell a
  lot of everything," the compressors are audible on-ear, and filtration cuts battery from
  50h to 2h. Filters claim 99% of particles down to 0.1 micron but it is **not HEPA-rated**.
- [2023-05-15] **Branding is the real explanation**: "so many other things in the tech
  world can be explained by branding — it's only Dyson that could have pulled this off."
  The $1,000 price is "the easy part" given the materials/packaging.
- [2023-05-15] **Loss-leader framing** (credited to Barbara Corcoran on the Waveform
  podcast): the Zone is "a loss leader because no one should buy it and probably no one
  will, but it'll generate enough buzz that people talk about Dyson" — "you didn't even
  have to pay for an ad."
