# Samsung Galaxy Camera Hands-on! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-25)

## Summary
A hands-on with the Samsung Galaxy Camera — an Android-powered point-and-shoot — filmed at the NY
Galaxy Note 2 launch, where he says he spent his time on the side of the event because this camera
intrigued him more than the phone. A short piece, but it shows his instinct for **a genuinely new
product category** (a "shrunken Android tablet that happens to be a camera") and his interest in
**real-time capture-and-share**, foreshadowing themes in his later camera and connected-device coverage.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-25] **Drawn to the odd category, not the headline product**: "yesterday I was in New York for
  the US launch of the Galaxy Note 2, but more interestingly the Samsung Galaxy Camera — I spent a lot of
  time on the side of the event… I was just really intrigued by this camera and it really impressed me."
  His attention goes to the novel thing, not the marketed one.
- [2012-10-25] **Frames it as a new category**: "turning on that display you get a full-fledged shrunken
  Android tablet — I don't say Android *phone* because while it has a mic, it doesn't make phone calls."
  A 16 MP sensor, 23 mm f/2.8 lens, 21× zoom, 4.7" HD Super AMOLED, running Android 4.1.
- [2012-10-25] **Praises the manual-control camera app specifically**: "you can dial and adjust manual
  controls — shutter speed, aperture to f/2.8, ISO up to 3200 — with previews in real time… or flip it to
  automatic. It's basically the camera app on steroids." He values the manual control and real-time
  preview, consistent with his production sensibility.
- [2012-10-25] **Identifies the real use case — real-time sharing**: "it'll be really good at gatherings
  and party scenarios where you're taking photos and videos and sharing them as you take them, which is
  kind of like the real-time sharing that Google+ started." A dated read of the connected-camera value
  proposition.
- [2012-10-25] **Reserves final judgment pending real footage**: "I don't have a lot of hands-on time…
  I'll be giving you guys test footage and test videos of the actual camera sensor so you can get a sense
  of how good that is as soon as I get my hands on it." The review-scope discipline — hands-on ≠ full
  verdict — applied again.
- [2012-10-25] Notes concerns honestly: the 1650 mAh battery "seems a little bit small," offset by not
  needing 4G on constantly.
