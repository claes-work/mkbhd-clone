# Amazon Fire Phone: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-19)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"-format** breakdown of the Amazon Fire Phone that lands one of his sharpest
industry-commentary verdicts: **"Amazon probably should have made an app."** He walks the two headline
features (Dynamic Perspective's four face-tracking cameras — "either awesome or gimmick"; Firefly's
scan-and-buy button), then argues the whole value proposition — except Dynamic Perspective — could have
shipped as a $5–10 app, since Shazam / Google Goggles / Google Now already did the scanning. Making a
**$650 AT&T-exclusive phone** instead multiplies the work (a custom Fire OS, custom apps, no Play Store,
manufacturing) for a thin payoff. He predicts it won't last — "I'd be surprised if we saw a version two
next year." A canonical **judge-by-real-purpose / form-factor-economics** commentary.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-19] **Dynamic Perspective** = four front-facing cameras + accelerometer/gyro tracking your
  face to shift the UI for a pseudo-3D immersive effect; works in the UI and a few apps/games. "To a lot
  of people this will either scream awesome or gimmick depending on who you are."
- [2014-06-19] **Firefly** = a dedicated side button that scans whatever the camera/mic sees (a song, a
  show, an object) and surfaces an Amazon buy link — "pretty self-explanatory: this is supposed to make
  Amazon more money."
- [2014-06-19] **The core critique — it should have been an app.** "Basically all of this phone's
  features except Dynamic Perspective… could be packaged into one app." Prior art already existed
  (Shazam/SoundHound for music, Google Goggles for objects, and Google Now once had the command). "Amazon
  could have built a Firefly app, sold it on Android and iOS, and called it a day."
- [2014-06-19] **Making a phone multiplies the work for the payoff:** a phone-sized custom Fire OS,
  custom apps (no access to the Play Store or App Store), reliance on Amazon's app store, manufacturing/
  packaging (Amazon's never made a phone), and a single carrier (AT&T-exclusive) — all to sell two
  features, at $650 off-contract.
- [2014-06-19] **"Not everyone has to make a phone."** He invokes the Facebook/HTC First precedent
  ("how long did that last?") and predicts the Fire Phone won't outlast it — likely no version two.
  Contrast: the $200 Kindle Fire **tablet** made sense because it's a genuine new form factor with real
  advantages in its price bracket; the phone has "better options out there."
- [2014-06-19] **Disclosed stance + conflict-of-interest transparency:** he loves Amazon, is a Prime
  member, buys his textbooks there — "but I won't be buying this phone," and he couldn't get one early
  (his only access would be pre-ordering at $650).
