# Smartphone Awards 2021 (@mkbhd, 2021-12-22)

## Summary
The annual solo MKBHD Smartphone Awards for 2021, awarding trophies across nine categories
from the year's phones. Opens with a mock "moment of silence" for LG's exit from the phone
market (a nod to Marques's recurring pro-competition stance). Winners: Best Big Phone —
Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 3 (first folding phone he'd pick full-time); Best Small Phone — iPhone
13 Mini (his closest race, over the Z Flip 3); Best Camera — iPhone 13 Pro (runner-up Pixel
6/6 Pro; the Pixel 5a won his separate Blind Smartphone Camera Test); Best Battery — iPhone
13 Pro Max; Design — Galaxy S21 Ultra; Value — Pixel 6 ($599, made the 6 Pro "look like a
bad deal"); Bust of the Year — Microsoft Surface Duo 2; Most Improved — Galaxy S21 Ultra
(over the S20 Ultra); and MVP / Phone of the Year — Galaxy S21 Ultra, with the iPhone 13 Pro
runner-up and Pixel 6 honorable mention. Threaded through: his framing of the awards as
incentives to reward the best, the growing rarity of good small phones (a category he
created deliberately), and the closing "cheap phones are getting good" sentiment. Solo video
— all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2021-12-22] Best Big Phone → **Galaxy Z Fold 3**: "if you made me pick a folding phone to
  use right now full time, it would without a question be this one"; glad a folding phone can
  now win awards. Honorable mentions: S21 Ultra (the standard big-phone experience) and ROG
  Phone 5 (gaming).
- [2021-12-22] Best Small Phone → **iPhone 13 Mini** ("my best use of not much space"), his
  closest race; it fixed the 12 Mini's weak battery by getting slightly thicker. Runner-up Z
  Flip 3 (price dropped to $999). He notes he "created a bunch of categories" like this
  specifically to highlight phones that wouldn't otherwise win — and that he is "not a small
  phone person" himself.
- [2021-12-22] Best Camera → **iPhone 13 Pro** ("no-brainer for me… a whole package"),
  crediting the big primary sensor, photographic styles, and 4K ProRes (he shot a whole video
  on it). Runner-up Pixel 6/6 Pro (the "contrasty HDR-e computational look"); the **Pixel 5a**
  won the Blind Smartphone Camera Test.
- [2021-12-22] Best Battery → **iPhone 13 Pro Max**, "the battery experience king" — an easy
  two-day phone, and "maybe even three days" at 60 Hz; despite an on-paper modest battery
  size, Apple's silicon/optimization plus decent charging and wireless charging win it.
  Runner-up ROG Phone 5 (6,000 mAh, no wireless charging).
- [2021-12-22] Design → **Galaxy S21 Ultra** (matte black; praises Samsung "embracing the
  camera bump and actually making it look really good," corner-to-corner display, minimal
  edge curve). Runners-up: Oppo Find X3 Pro, Oppo Reno 6 (soft-touch back), and a
  "taking-a-risk" shout to the Lenovo Legion phone.
- [2021-12-22] Value → **Pixel 6** ($599 with the Tensor chip, high-refresh OLED, great
  cameras) — "made the Pixel 6 Pro look like a bad deal." His recurring line: "good phones
  might not be getting cheap, but cheap phones are getting good."
- [2021-12-22] Bust of the Year → **Microsoft Surface Duo 2** ($1,500): more expensive than
  the first "and it's worse"; wants the dual-screen idea to work but the software and cameras
  aren't there — "I would just never give a Duo to anyone."
- [2021-12-22] Most Improved → **Galaxy S21 Ultra** over the S20 Ultra (fixed the S20 Ultra's
  autofocus troubles, kept the big-number camera ambitions). Honorable mentions: Z Flip 3
  (better AND cheaper — "you don't usually see a phone make a big leap in usability and leap
  down in price") and iPhone 13 Pro (finally got ProMotion; camera + battery + screen all up).
- [2021-12-22] MVP / Phone of the Year → **Galaxy S21 Ultra**: an MVP-style pick weighing best
  phone plus impact and "a little bit of which one was my favorite"; a January phone that
  "nailed it" and dropped to ~$600–700 by year-end. Runner-up iPhone 13 Pro (the three most
  fundamental things — screen, battery, cameras — all much better). Honorable mention Pixel 6
  ("why spend a thousand dollars when you can have a phone for 5.99 that does all the same
  stuff").
- [2021-12-22] Recurring framing: nailing "the fundamentals" (camera, screen, battery)
  earns his praise; the whole judging committee "is me."
