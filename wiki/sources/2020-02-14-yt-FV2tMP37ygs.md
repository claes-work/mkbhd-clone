- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) On the 20-year record: "swung for the fences" and succeeded in global health via new vaccines that have "really gotten out"; U.S. education has been much tougher — some good curriculum/tutoring/feedback work, but dropout, graduation and preparedness are not dramatically improved.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) On missing: you hope to get more than one swing — either multiple attempts at the same problem (e.g., two or three malaria vaccine constructs raise combined odds) or working across multiple areas.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) On climate: he's involved with a company trying to make a nuclear reactor that would be very cheap and safe enough for public acceptance; he calls it high-risk ("less than 50 percent chance all that comes together") and is only involved because it would help generate electricity without greenhouse gases.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) Getting to net zero requires all countries and all sectors — not just passenger cars, but industry, agriculture, planes, long-distance trucks; the number of needed innovations is very high.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) Coins/uses the "green premium": in some sectors the clean approach still costs more than the dirty one; great innovation could take that premium to zero in a few cases, but many emissions will stay premium-priced; you must get ~194 countries (especially the big ones) to go along or the whole effort fails.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) Passenger cars are one of the most hopeful climate sectors; Tesla is the one company he'd name as having driven the shift; other carmakers (and new entrants) are now moving fast on EVs; the biggest concern is whether consumers overcome range anxiety.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) Just got a Porsche Taycan — his first electric car — describes it as very cool and premium-priced, and says he's enjoying it a lot.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) EV holdbacks: range for long trips, charging pervasiveness and time; gasoline is very dense energy (~30× denser than current lithium batteries); a ~2× range improvement would suffice for most people. EVs are <3% of global car sales today; Norway is higher; China looks to be a fast-developing market. Electricity and maintenance cost a bit less, insurance is a bit more, and repairs are costly — fixing one of these camera/sensor-laden mirrors went from ~$100 to ~$1,500.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) On YouTube/misinformation: historically newspapers/media were responsible about not letting untruths about vaccine safety spread; you don't want to lose the good thing — low barriers to entry and diversity of voices vs. traditional publishing. His own audience "probably isn't deep with the vaccine doubters." Worries that despite the internet, people don't seek out complex information that conflicts with preconceived notions; wants social media to be a force for education, especially on climate where consensus is needed.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) On engagement: amazed at rising interest in climate; hopes it's permanent, not cyclical, since the challenge needs decades of being a top issue.
- [2020-02-14] (Bill Gates — CONTEXT) On email: very PC-centric, sits at a desk with a big Surface screen; gets in early and does a lot at night; marks long emails unread and returns to give thoughtful responses; falling behind on email is his symptom of over-scheduling; mentions getting mail that day about the coronavirus outbreak and whether the foundation should step up (he was glad to give a quick yes).
