# 2026 Nissan Leaf Graduated from Glow-Up University (@AutoFocus, 2026-06-01)

## Summary
A review of the facelifted **2026 Nissan Leaf** that doubles as a mini-essay on EV design.
Marques opens with a thesis he clearly cares about: today's EVs "just look like blobs"
because battery tech forces an obsessive focus on aerodynamic drag — and when energy
density improves, designers will get to "have design again." He uses that to connect the
$40k Leaf to the $640k Ferrari Luce (people mocked the Luce by comparing it to a Leaf): both
are teardrop low-drag shapes for the same reason. The Leaf itself he now recommends — a real
"glow-up" from its infamously ugly, uncool predecessors — with one strong gripe: a confusing
"e-Step" regen that isn't true one-pedal driving. Marques-solo.

## Key claims
- 2026-06-01: The facelifted Nissan Leaf — 75 kWh battery, front-wheel drive, ~214 hp, 0–60
  in 7.2s, **~260 miles range**; starts near **$30k**, ~$40k+ in the top interior trim
  reviewed. (Marques)
- **EV design thesis** (the video's throughline): "We will be able to look back at [this era]
  as the time that electric cars just look like blobs... when battery tech gets better and
  energy density gets higher, we won't have to focus so strictly on the coefficient of drag
  ... and we can have design again." (Marques — tech-industry-commentary)
- Explicitly ties the Leaf to the **Ferrari Luce**: same smooth teardrop, no grille, smooth
  headlights — "exactly the same stuff you heard Ferrari talk about with the Luce." Argues
  Ferrari shouldn't have chased aero efficiency: a Ferrari "should look like a Ferrari...
  give it downforce, give it character," but "now it just looks like a Nissan Leaf."
  (Marques — a critical position on the Luce, dated 2026-06-01)
- The Leaf is a genuine **"glow-up"** — previous Leafs were "truly ugly," the "uncool
  electric car no one would want to be caught dead driving"; now well-equipped (wireless
  CarPlay/Android Auto, HUD, Bose ~10 speakers with headrest speakers, tinted electrochromic
  sunroof "I never expected... in a Leaf"). He'd "have no problem recommending" it now.
  (Marques)
- Main gripe: the **"e-Step" button is not real one-pedal driving** — it ramps up regen but
  holds at ~5 mph applying power instead of stopping at zero. "Nissan, let me do one-pedal
  driving. That's kind of the whole point of an EV." (Marques)
- Offers to make "a whole other video just on electric car design." (Marques)
