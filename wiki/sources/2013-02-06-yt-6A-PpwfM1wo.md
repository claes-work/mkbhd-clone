# HTC Droid DNA: Revisited! (@mkbhd, 2013-02-06)

## Summary
A "revisited" review of the HTC Droid DNA — the first US 1080p phone — that he'd
initially skipped because he was "so enthralled in the Galaxy Note 2." A clean example
of his **one-flaw-kills-an-otherwise-great-package** verdict pattern: superb build and
the best mobile display he'd seen, undone by "terrible battery life." Also restates the
**resolution-you-actually-can-see** position and calls out Beats Audio as a software
gimmick.

## Key claims
- [2013-02-06] **The one-critical-flaw verdict framework, stated explicitly**: "you can
  add this phone to a long list of devices that have all-but-awesome specs for just one
  thing on the list is bad — the Nexus 4 (no LTE), the Note 2 (too big)… this phone's
  critical bad thing is the battery life." A reusable way he resolves a near-great device
  (name the single dealbreaker).
- [2013-02-06] **Best mobile display he'd seen — and the "you can't see the difference"
  rebuttal**: "this is the best display on any mobile device, period… a lot of people
  like to say oh you'll never see the difference — yes it is all that it's cracked up to
  be, you absolutely can see the difference." 441 PPI, and he singles out brightness and
  ~90° viewing angles. Defends high-res displays against the diminishing-returns claim.
- [2013-02-06] **Terrible battery is the dealbreaker**: "I was charging it at 3 PM every
  day… I don't want to recommend this phone just because the battery life is so bad." The
  sealed body means no spare battery — ties the flaw to the design tradeoff.
- [2013-02-06] **Build quality praised, one detail excepted**: "everything about this
  screams quality… except the Beats logo on the back, that does not scream quality at
  all." Even-handed down to a single logo; prefers the DNA's build to the Note 2's
  plastic.
- [2013-02-06] **Calls out Beats Audio as an EQ gimmick — a debunking**: tested with wired
  and Bluetooth headphones, "clearly it's not an internal hardware thing, it's just
  changing an EQ, and they call it Beats Audio." Tests a marketing claim and reports it's
  hollow (consistent with his standing Beats skepticism).
- [2013-02-06] **Big-hands self-reference**: "having big hands I tend to use the Galaxy
  Note 2 with one hand, but for most people this is a two-handed device." A dated physical
  self-datum (hand size), consistent with the height thread.
- [2013-02-06] **A falsifiable prediction**: "I'm probably going to go ahead and put my
  money saying that the Galaxy S4 is going to be a 5-inch 1080p display." (Correct.) Logs
  a bet.
