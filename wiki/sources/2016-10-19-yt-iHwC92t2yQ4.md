# Tesla Model S P100D: The Options! (@mkbhd, 2016-10-19)

## Summary
Video #2 of the "Apollo" playlist — a teach-the-mechanism walkthrough of the Model S range and every
option on his car, shot (per the playlist conceit) entirely on the **Pixel XL** camera. Reinforces
"treat the car like a tech product," previews the **Model 3 "$35k"** thesis, and yields a personal-taste
biography detail (why he chose gray over a second red car).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-10-19] **"The same way I talk about smartphones… it's a tech product."** "Just hardware-wise, I
  guess the same way I talk about smartphones — it's a striking car, unique in some ways, familiar in
  others." Reaffirms reviewing-a-car-as-a-gadget, and predicts "I fully expect a P120D next year — that's
  inevitable, based on the way technology works; it's a tech product." (Tech-cadence applied to cars.)
- [2016-10-19] **Decodes the whole Model S matrix (teach-the-mechanism).** Battery sizes (60/75/90/100
  kWh = range), motors (P = performance, D = dual, the underline = Ludicrous), "so P100D essentially
  represents the peak of what Tesla's accomplished in 2016 — largest battery, highest performance." Also
  previews the Model 3: "a less expensive, slightly smaller sedan starting around **$35,000** — that's a
  completely separate video." (Ties to the EV-for-the-masses thesis → [[../topics/ev-cars/ev-cars]].)
- [2016-10-19] **Why an EV looks the way it does (efficiency).** "Gas cars need a big grille/radiator to
  cool the engine; an EV doesn't, so they cover the front — you might not like it, but it's for
  efficiency, way more aerodynamic. The door handles retract while driving for aero. This car has the
  lowest coefficient of drag of any production car." An early articulation of the "EVs are shaped by
  aero" idea that becomes his 2026 "EVs look like blobs" thesis.
- [2016-10-19] **Gray-over-red (personal-taste biography).** "This paint is Midnight Silver Metallic — I
  call it gray. I was split on red vs. gray, but I went with gray — I already had a red car, and I'm not
  trying to get pulled over; I'm trying to fly under the radar." Confirms he owned a red car before Apollo;
  a rare personal-preference tell. (Still keeps a red accent: the red brake calipers "a little bit of red
  on a non-red car" + black wheels "I don't know why more people don't get it.")
- [2016-10-19] **GPS-aware smart air suspension = his favorite detail.** "It remembers the locations where
  I manually raise the ride height and does it automatically next time — like my high driveway. The fact
  that it can do that based on GPS location is sweet." His delight in software-defined, self-learning
  hardware (the phone-reviewer's lens on a car).
- [2016-10-19] **Autopilot as an upgrade path.** Notes the sensor/radar suite and that "word on the
  street is you'll have to replace this harness for the Autopilot 2.0 hardware to legitimately drive
  itself A to B." The upgradeable-tech-product framing again.
