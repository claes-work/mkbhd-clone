# Dope Tech: Best of 2015! (@mkbhd, 2015-12-30)

## Summary
A solo year-end "Dope Tech" recap in which Marques rounds up the coolest and most significant tech of 2015, explicitly framing the video as "a hub for everything you need to know about the year that just happened in Tech." Rather than crown a single "year of the blank" theme, he walks category by category — smartphones, tablets, wearables/smartwatches, virtual reality, Force Touch / 3D Touch, USB type-C, Windows 10 hardware, and cars (electric + autonomous) — pointing to his separate dedicated videos for phones and tablets. He calls pressure sensitivity Apple's biggest innovation of the year and USB-C the port that's "here to stay." A standout segment explains how Tesla Model S Autopilot learns individual curves and shares that learning fleet-wide via software updates. He closes on the growth of tech YouTube, citing his own 2015 numbers, and looks ahead to 2016.

## Key claims
- [2015-12-30] Marques frames the video as a year-in-review hub and pushes back on reductive "2015 was the year of X" framing: too much good tech came out to "put it into a box like that."
- [2015-12-30] Smartphones broadly got better in 2015 — performance, features, and cameras — and it "would be hard to go wrong with any of the top few choices." Detailed phone coverage is deferred to his separate "Smartphone Awards 2015" video.
- [2015-12-30] Tablets had a "confused" / weird year for progress, though still with good hardware — he names iPad Air 2, iPad Pro, Google Pixel C, and Surface Pro 4 — with functionality direction being the unsettled part. Covered in a separate tablets video.
- [2015-12-30] 2015 was "the year of the second wave of smartwatches," including Apple's first smartwatch (the Apple Watch), alongside the Moto 360 v2, Moto 360 Sport, and entries from Huawei to watchmakers like TAG Heuer. His verdict: these are still smartphone accessories, not standalone devices — "and that's kind of the point." He notes it was cool to see Android Wear come to iPhone this year.
- [2015-12-30] VR advanced on two fronts: phone-display headsets (Samsung Gear VR, Google Cardboard) and standalone headsets (new Oculus Rift, Sony PlayStation VR — formerly Project Morpheus — and HTC Vive, which he got hands-on with). He judges VR still niche, with expensive hardware for limited functionality, but notes early adopters will happily "drop a few hundred bucks."
- [2015-12-30] Force Touch / 3D Touch became a big deal for Apple "out of the blue" this year; Marques calls pressure sensitivity "honestly Apple's big innovation of this year." 3D Touch (multiple pressure levels, on iPhone 6s / 6s Plus) vs Force Touch (two levels, on Apple Watch, new MacBook trackpad, MacBook Pro, Magic Trackpad 2). Prediction: pressure sensitivity on larger surfaces like an iPad next year, and possibly a big 3D Touch design change in the iPhone 7 in 2016.
- [2015-12-30] USB type-C is "new, it's awesome, it's here to stay" — reversible, more power, faster data. Seen in 2015 in phones (Nexus 6P, Nexus 5X, OnePlus 2), tablets (Google Pixel C, Asus, Dell), and most notably the new MacBook.
- [2015-12-30] Criticism of the new single-port MacBook: one "solo lonely USB-C port" plus an $80 adapter to do anything but charge "does seem kind of weird and behind." Prediction: the next-gen MacBook will have more than one port, and many more phones will adopt USB-C in 2016; later adopters likely Apple (sticking with Lightning) and Samsung (micro-USB), but "down the road USB-C… it'll happen."
- [2015-12-30] Windows 10 was a big improvement for Microsoft; the Surface lineup is "the Nexus of Windows 10 hardware" — Surface Book (laptop), Surface Pro 4 (tablet), Lumia 950 XL (highest-end Windows 10 phone), tied together by Continuum. He says his own 950 XL is in the mail and will be one of his first phones reviewed for 2016.
- [2015-12-30] On cars: the "100-year-old car industry" is "always a little bit behind" on tech, but 2015 was solid and 2016 promising. Marques states he's personally a big fan of two things — electric cars and self-driving/autonomous cars — which is why he's a big fan of the Tesla Model S.
- [2015-12-30] Tesla Model S "fun fact": it has its own internet connection and downloads over-the-air software updates that improve features and driving. Adaptive cruise control + self-steering combine into "Autopilot mode." The car learns individual curves — taking a tight curve too fast the first time (beeping for you to take the wheel), then slowing earlier and hugging it better on repeat passes until it does it "just perfectly, like a normal driver."
- [2015-12-30] The learning is fleet-wide: each Model S "treats itself like part of a fleet of every single Tesla Model S," uploading learned road data so that other owners receive those curve improvements in their next software update — so a car can already know a curve the driver has never driven. Marques's verdict: "that's pretty dope."
- [2015-12-30] Tech on YouTube "did really well again this year" — new channels, communities, and people. Self-reported metric: of the 86 videos he uploaded in the last year, "over a thousand years of human life were spent watching them," more than the 2014 numbers.
- [2015-12-30] Closing take: "2015 was pretty dope, 2016 has a tough act to follow, but I think we're up for it."
