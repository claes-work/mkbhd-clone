# Dope Tech: The Biggest Ultrawide Monitor! (@mkbhd, 2020-07-18)

## Summary
A themeless three-item Dope Tech episode — Marques explicitly calls out that the items are unrelated and turns the transitions into "a Linus-inspired game of high-tech chain reaction." Item 1: the "3X Pods," hilariously oversized Bluetooth speakers shaped like AirPods Pro (~$40) — a pure novelty flex with bad sound and cheap build; he recommends buying them only for the conversation-starter factor, not sound or build quality. Item 2: the second-gen Asus ROG Zephyrus Duo, a dual-screen laptop whose secondary 4K touch display now rises and tilts toward you on open (fixing the first gen's hard-to-see angle) while adding airflow for cooling; capable specs (8-core CPU, RTX 2080 Super Max-Q, 32GB RAM) but the familiar dual-screen-form-factor quirks remain (no palm rest, tiny trackpad, awkward power-plug placement, worse battery from driving two 4K screens). Item 3, the headline: the Samsung Odyssey G9, a 49-inch 5120×1440 ("5K-wide"), 240Hz, HDR1000 QLED, 1000R heavily-curved gaming monitor (~$700). Marques calls the gaming immersion unmatched but notes he personally prefers vertical pixels for scrolling-heavy work, so ultrawides aren't his daily driver — it earns Dope Tech for gaming.

## Key claims
- [2020-07-18] This Dope Tech has no theme — three completely unrelated items — and Marques treats the segues as a self-imposed challenge, name-checking Linus (LinusTechTips) as the inspiration for the "high-tech chain reaction." (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] The "3X Pods" are Bluetooth speakers modeled after AirPods Pro, roughly the size of a camera-and-lens or a big coffee mug; despite not fitting any ear they still ship with rubber tips. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] They pair easily over Bluetooth, have a power switch / playback controls / mic on the stem, charge over micro-USB, and can also play from FM radio, a USB flash drive, or a TF (microSD-equivalent) card. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] They sound bad — very little bass, not very loud; other same-size, same-price speakers get twice as loud and sound much better. They're ~$40 and very cheaply built (visible seams, screws, sticker "grilles"); the site claims 3D printing but Marques is skeptical it helps the build. Buy them for novelty/flex, not sound or build. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] Quirk: both units ship as *left* AirPods; Marques's suggestion is one left and one right that pair in stereo — "maybe they'll save that for the second gen." (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] The second-gen Asus ROG Zephyrus Duo fixes the first gen's main complaint (the secondary screen against the keyboard was hard to see off-axis and reflective): a simple hinge now raises and tilts the second 4K matte touchscreen toward you every time you open the lid, which also opens a rear airflow gap for cooling. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] Marques frames the dual-screen concept as "the Touch Bar on steroids" with real multitasking upside, but the form factor keeps its quirks: no built-in palm rest (keyboard pushed down), a tiny trackpad, an awkwardly-placed power plug on the left-middle, and worse battery life from powering two 4K screens (90Wh battery drains fast at high brightness on both). (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] Specs: unlocked 8-core CPU, RTX 2080 Super Max-Q, 32GB RAM, SSD storage; the port layout (power/mic/headphone + 2×USB-A left, 1×USB-C right, and USB/Ethernet/HDMI along the back middle) signals Asus expects it used as a desktop replacement. His real praise is that "Asus is listening" and iterating on the concept. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] The Samsung Odyssey G9 is a 49-inch, 5120×1440 ("5K-wide"), 240Hz, HDR1000 (QLED), 1ms-response, NVIDIA G-Sync (DisplayPort 1.4) ultrawide — the biggest monitor Marques has used and, he believes, the first he's seen combining that resolution with a 240Hz refresh. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] Its 1000R curve means the radius of the circle the panel would form is 1000mm — i.e. very heavily curved, said to match the human eye and to maximize immersion by filling peripheral vision. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] I/O: 2× DisplayPort, 1× HDMI, USB hub, audio jack; DisplayPort is the best way to get the most out of it. Marques wishes it had USB-C. Rear has RGB lighting, cable management, and a handy (if hard-to-reach) headphone holder. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] At ~$700 it's expensive — more than two separate 27-inch 240Hz monitors — but the all-in-one, bezel-free, HDR/QLED/curve package is hard to match. (Marques)
- [2020-07-18] Personal caveat: Marques has never been a big ultrawide person because most of his work needs vertical pixels for scrolling, and 1440p isn't enough vertical resolution for him — but for gaming "this thing is incredible," which is why it's Dope Tech. (Marques)
