# I Spent $40,000 to Unbox a Sealed Original iPhone! (@mkbhd, 2023-04-20)

## Summary
A solo collector/retro-tech stunt: Marques buys a **factory-sealed, unopened first-gen
(2007) iPhone** at a live auction for **~$40,000** and unboxes it on camera. Framing is
about collector-market economics — the sealed original is "the number one most sought
after piece of tech in The Collector world," so rare that scammers reseal used units and
"YouTubers have fallen victim to this." He walks through verifying authenticity (x-rays,
unregistered serial, 16-year-old shrink wrap smell, the difficulty of getting a 1.0
iPhone past the iTunes activation screen) and concludes it is legitimately unopened. The
unit had a corner break and an unexplained upside-down "lucky you" sticker that even
Apple staff couldn't identify — factors he thinks kept it below the ~$63k record. The
video also candidly notes the act of opening it destroyed most of its value. Also
functions as a companion piece to his earlier "reviewing every iPhone ever" video. Solo
— all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2023-04-20] The sealed, untouched original iPhone is "the number one most sought after
  piece of tech in The Collector world"; only ~6 million first-gen iPhones were ever
  sold, so the supply of unopened ones "will only ever go down." He thinks the value has
  likely peaked, citing an auction sale of **over $60,000** in February 2023 (a record)
  and another that hit a high figure in March.
  > ⚠️ Caption garble: the transcript renders the record as both "$60,000"/"$63,000"/
  > "$660,000"/"$555,000" in different places — the spoken figures are unreliable here;
  > treat the exact auction records as approximate, not citable.
- [2023-04-20] He won his unit at a real live-streamed gavel auction (item #203) with an
  opening bid of ~$32,000; the ~$40,000 total was bid plus "sales tax and bidding premium
  and shipping."
- [2023-04-20] Authenticity is "very much a trust exercise": x-rays only prove the
  accessories are in the box, and an unregistered serial proves it was never activated but
  "doesn't mean it was never opened." He became convinced it was genuine from the aged
  shrink wrap, the pristine inner wrap, and how hard it is to get a 1.0 iPhone past the
  original iTunes activation screen.
- [2023-04-20] Details he'd forgotten about the original iPhone: no App Store (only
  built-in apps) and no custom wallpaper in iOS 1.0, so "everyone's iPhone looked exactly
  the same"; it shipped with a 30-pin dock, a giant original-size AT&T/Cingular SIM, a
  microfiber cloth, and no SIM-ejector tool.
- [2023-04-20] Honest value reveal: opening it "is the moment that all the value just
  went from 40,000 to… dramatically less than what I paid for it, and that's okay."
- [2023-04-20] **Self-sponsorship model**: the video's "sponsor" is his own re-released
  limited-edition matte-black MKBHD wallet at shop.mkbhd.com — "now I can say that this
  video is made possible by my wallet."
