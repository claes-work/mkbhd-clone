# GoPro Hero 4 Session Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-07-24)

## Summary
A review of the cube-shaped GoPro Hero 4 Session — "I really want to like it, but…" Two recurring lenses:
**one-upped by its own sibling** (the same-$400 Hero 4 Silver has much better video and swappable
batteries) and **image-quality-over-form-factor** ("at $400 I start to look for good video quality on top
of just being well-built"). The verdict circles back to **"who is this for?"** — hard to recommend at $400
except for water sports / weight-critical uses. Also a voice tic: "not exactly Team Crispy."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-07-24] **The cube form factor: clever, but limited.** A 1.5" waterproof cube (50% smaller / 35%
  lighter than a waterproof Hero 4 Silver/Black), rotatable in its mount (no new accessories to side-mount),
  micro-USB charging, two mics — but **no real display** (you need your phone + the GoPro app to change
  settings, awkward "when you're out on an adventure without your not-waterproof phone") and **no standby
  mode** (a ~4-second on-to-recording delay to save battery).
- [2015-07-24] **Image-quality-over-form-factor (the core critique).** Max 1080p, and "it just doesn't look
  like a *good* 1080p — colors dull, dynamic range limited… **not exactly Team Crispy**." At $400 "I start
  to look for good video quality on top of just being well-built."
- [2015-07-24] **One-upped by its own sibling.** The same-price **Hero 4 Silver has much better video +
  switchable batteries**; a side-by-side (unedited) shows the Session sacrificing "detail, color, exposure,
  dynamic range." "Looking through the Session footage made me appreciate the Hero 4 Black that much more."
- [2015-07-24] **Verdict — who-is-this-for?** "I originally thought it was a beginner GoPro… but at $400,
  no. This is really just better for water sports / weight-critical uses (attaching it to a pet). For
  everything else I'd still recommend the regular GoPros or other action cameras." Not a bad product — just
  hard to recommend.
