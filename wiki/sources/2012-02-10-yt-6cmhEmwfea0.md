# Fastest Mac Mini in the World! [Part 2] (@mkbhd, 2012-02-10)

## Summary
Part two of the build: installing the SSD. Notable for three things beyond the procedure — he
**called Apple and couldn't get an answer** about their own SSD's speed, which is why he sourced his
own; he **declines to re-teach the teardown and sends viewers to another creator's tutorial** instead;
and he describes the Mac Mini's internals against his own frame of reference as a **tower-PC builder**.

## Key claims
- [2012-02-10] **Why not Apple's SSD — because Apple couldn't say**: "in terms of the Apple option, I
  actually called Apple and nobody could tell me there how fast their $500 256 gig SSD option was, so
  we're taking matters into our own hands here." A dated instance of doing the legwork and reporting
  that the manufacturer had no answer.
- [2012-02-10] **The part and its stated specs**: OCZ Vertex 3 Max IOPS Edition, SATA 3 (6 Gbit/s),
  "handles write speeds at around 500 megabytes per second and reads at around 550" — with the form
  factor admired in passing: "just the power and the SATA connector and the SSD, that's it."
- [2012-02-10] **Sends viewers to someone else's tutorial rather than duplicating it**: "I'm not going
  to explain every detail in the hardware swap process… mainly because there's already a fantastic
  step-by-step instructional video on YouTube that I used and followed to do this." Linking a
  competitor's better coverage instead of making a worse version of it.
- [2012-02-10] **Reports the job honestly, including his own limits**: about 25 minutes, "pretty
  painless," "I've never opened up a Mac Mini before so stuff was much more crammed together than I'm
  used to when I build regular tower computers — but I do have massive freaking gigantic hands, so that
  was probably the most difficult part."
- [2012-02-10] Practical tips volunteered: an 8 GB+ USB drive with the OS X Lion recovery tool to get
  Lion onto the new SSD; hold Option at boot for Disk Utility; "the screws you'll be unscrewing are
  mostly really tiny, so be prepared to have a good place to store them — like in a bottle cap."
- [2012-02-10] **PC-builder identity confirmed in passing** — "when I build regular tower computers" —
  useful context for the macOS transition documented later in the series
  ([[2012-03-02-yt-G_H6mRHS9pc]]).
