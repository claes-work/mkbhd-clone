# Android 7.0 Nougat Update! (@mkbhd, 2016-08-23)

## Summary
His "lived-with-it" take on the final Android 7.0 Nougat (after weeks on the Nexus 6P beta) — distinct
from the earlier feature-rundown, and a good example of his **daily-use-over-feature-list** review method
("this video is more my thoughts on the final version… what actually mattered in everyday use"). Honest
about which headline features he did and didn't actually use.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-08-23] **Daily-use over feature-list.** "I've already done the full feature rundown — this video
  is more my thoughts on the final version and what actually had the biggest impact in daily use." His
  method: separate the spec list from what lived experience proves matters.
- [2016-08-23] **Notifications = the biggest real-world change.** "The grouping, the more info, quick
  replies, quick settings — particularly the way notifications are organized has had the biggest impact.
  They're bundled by app now, and you interact one by one — it's like a whole new app up there." Names
  the single most impactful change from use, not marketing.
- [2016-08-23] **Honest about features he didn't use.** "The double-tap quick-switch I used a lot — you
  start to love it and miss it on other phones. But split-screen multitasking — I didn't really use it
  much; I didn't use it on the LG or Samsung phones that did it first either." Refuses to praise a feature
  he doesn't personally use.
- [2016-08-23] **Skeptical-but-fair on Doze-on-the-go.** "Doze-on-the-go should put the phone in a low-
  power state when it's not being used, like a tablet on a shelf — but I personally haven't noticed a huge
  battery boost on my 6P vs. Marshmallow. Then again it was just the public beta, so we'll keep testing."
  Withholds a verdict pending more real-world data. (The empirical-humility habit.)
- [2016-08-23] **The LG V20 note.** "At the bottom of the official release they mention the first phone
  shipping with 7.0 out of the box will be the LG V20 — not a Nexus. Looking forward to that." Tracks the
  ecosystem detail most would miss.
