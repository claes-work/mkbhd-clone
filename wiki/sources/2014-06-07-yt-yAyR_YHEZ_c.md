# LG G3 QHD Display Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-07)

## Summary
A **display-explainer** on the LG G3's 2560×1440 QHD panel that doubles as a canonical statement of
Marques's **pixel-density-vs-viewing-distance / retina-display-effect** doctrine. He walks the display
history — iPhone 3GS (165 ppi) → iPhone 4 "Retina" (326 ppi) → 720p → 1080p → the G3's ~538 ppi — to
set up the real question: **if everything north of ~300 ppi is already indistinguishable at a normal
viewing distance, why bother with QHD?** He defers the verdict (does it hurt battery/performance?) to
the full review, making this the "is the extra resolution worth it?" framing rather than the answer.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-07] **The retina threshold, restated as doctrine:** the iPhone 4's 326 ppi "Retina" display
  (4× the pixels of the 3GS) was the point past which "you literally cannot see fuzziness… cannot
  discern individual pixels at a normal viewing distance or even a super-close viewing distance."
  Anything north of ~300 ppi looks the same to the eye.
- [2014-06-07] **So QHD raises a diminishing-returns question.** "If everything north of 300 ppi is the
  same, then why even bother with quad HD?… surely it's going to hit battery life, stress your graphics,
  maybe disturb performance." He explicitly withholds the verdict for the full review.
- [2014-06-07] **The measurable benefits today are narrow** — sharp text, high-res photo/video
  playback, high-res wallpapers — but he's clear the sharpness gain over 1080p is at the edge of
  perceptibility at normal distance.
- [2014-06-07] **A production/education aside:** he tells viewers to bump the YouTube player to the
  highest resolution they can, because "even if you don't have a 4K display, YouTube streams these
  higher-resolution videos at higher bit rates, so the clarity is improved anyway." (His shot-in-4K
  workflow feeding the display argument.)
