# Top 5 LG G6 Features! (@mkbhd, 2017-02-28)

## Summary
First impressions of the LG G6 — "successor to the G5 pretty much just in the name, because everything else
is completely different." A ranked Top-5 (his order, not marketing's). The clone-relevant beats: LG walking
back from the G5's failed modularity to a conventional-but-solid flagship, the taller 18:9 display as a
signal of the bezel-less trend, and his consistent skinned-Android-update-lag caution.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-02-28] **"Successor in name only" — LG resets after the G5.** "Everything else is completely
  different." An implicit acknowledgment that the modular G5 failed and LG went conventional (the G5 was
  "barely modular" in his earlier take → [[2016-06-03-yt-96PJdbJysxQ]]).
- [2017-02-28] **The tall display = the bezel-less trend arriving.** A taller-than-16:9 (18:9) display that
  fits a big screen in a smaller body — an early mainstream step toward the bezel-less future he rated 8/10
  ([[2017-02-02-yt-J49hiExRUmQ]]).
- [2017-02-28] **The improved (less in-your-face) LG skin — the shove-vs-tuck balance.** "Still an LG skin
  on Android 7.1, but much less in-your-face than the G5/G4. There's always a balance between shoving new
  features in your face (Samsung) versus tucking them in settings for you to find later — this skin balances
  those pretty well." His recurring stock-vs-skin framing.
- [2017-02-28] **The skinned-Android caveat, restated.** "As a skin phone you inherently have a bit of a
  delay on software updates — we hope it gets them fast, but no guarantees; and you have to deal with LG's
  lately-shaky stability reputation." A consistent, source-critical caution.
- [2017-02-28] **Solid flagship spec + the fundamentals.** Snapdragon 821, 4GB RAM, twin 13 MP cameras,
  3,300 mAh, ip68, Qi wireless charging, and a microSD slot (up to 2TB) — "the numbers look great as you'd
  expect from a 2017 flagship." (Reads as ticking the five-pillars boxes conventionally.)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "This is the successor to the G5 pretty much just in the name, because everything else is completely
> different."
> "There's always a balance of shoving new features in your face — which is kind of what Samsung might do —
> versus tucking the features away in the settings and letting you find them later."
