# Nexus 6 (Shamu) Thoughts! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-05)

## Summary
A pre-announcement "ramble" about the rumored Nexus 6 (codename **Shamu** — "whales are big; this phone
is supposed to be big") — made partly because his hand-injured full reviews are taking longer. The
substantive point is that a **5.9" QHD Nexus breaks the Nexus tradition of conservative size and price**,
which he's ambivalent about. Low-yield, but a dated marker of his "Nexus should be the conservative
reference device" read and the Moto-X-2014 basis for the design.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-05] **The rumor: a 5.9" display (Shamu).** He's wary — the 5.5" iPhone 6 Plus is already
  "huge," so 5.9" "just seems massive." Mitigations: a Nexus can use thinner bezels + on-screen buttons
  (no home button), and the design is rumored to be based on the thin-bezeled 2014 Moto X.
- [2014-10-05] **It breaks Nexus tradition.** "We've traditionally had a relatively conservative design,
  a relatively conservative price for the Nexus… a 5.9" quad HD phone does not feel conservative at all —
  it feels like a side-step or a different direction." He's not sure he'd have picked this design.
- [2014-10-05] **Nexuses are always based on an existing device** (to showcase the new Android), so basing
  it on Motorola's top-tier Moto X "makes sense."
- [2014-10-05] **Android L is improving fast** — the developer preview he ran on his Nexus 5 wasn't
  complete; the final version updates far more Google apps with Material Design ("encouraging… APKs
  floating around").
- [2014-10-05] **Meta/creator note:** "the other reviews I'm working on are taking so much longer, mostly
  'cause I only have one hand to do it" — this talk-only video is what he can make one-handed.
