# 4G and LTE: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-06-04)

## Summary
An "Explained!" entry on why "4G" means almost nothing — "there's no real widely accepted definition
of 4G." He walks the generations (analog → 2G digital → 3G → 4G/LTE/WiMAX), then makes the point the
video exists to make: **carriers slap "4G" on whatever they want because no one can stop them**, with
T-Mobile's HSPA+ as the case study. He closes on a battery-physics argument that connects directly to
his earlier "better, not bigger" rant.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-04] **The definition is a marketing vacuum** — the thesis: "there's no real widely accepted
  definition of 4G." Each generation is faster but incompatible with the last ("you'll never see a 3G
  phone working on a 4G network").
- [2012-06-04] **The carrier-marketing move, named**: modern 3G like HSPA+ (~7 Mbps) can beat early 4G,
  so "instead of being left out of the 4G chatter… they just figured they'd stamp a 4G sign on their
  HSPA+ network and just declare it 4G — cuz who's going to argue?" Incentive/marketing skepticism, the
  same lens as [[2012-03-09-yt-zGXJm2QE5Do]] (ultrabooks as an Intel marketing category).
- [2012-06-04] **The battery-constraint argument** — ties to the hardware-tradeoff thread: 4G radios are
  "very, very power-hungry," which "is why in the United States we pretty much haven't seen any device,
  any smartphone at all with both quad core AND 4G — because that's just way too much power for a single
  battery to handle." The HTC One X ships quad-core overseas but dual-core in the US "so hopefully it'll
  have enough power to last through the day." Explicitly self-references the battery rant
  ([[2011-09-22-yt-XXySJoRe5zc]]): "I did a little bit of a rant on battery technology already, and
  hopefully if battery tech improves, we'll be able to see one."
- [2012-06-04] **Speed/capacity distinction**: 4G isn't just faster — "the 4G networks also tend to have
  a lot more capacity than the 3G networks, so you're going to see a lot less bandwidth caps."
- [2012-06-04] Generation figures (caption-derived): 2G ~10–200 kbps, 3G from ~384 kbps, 4G from
  ~5 Mbps; the first iPhone was 2G.
- [2012-06-04] **Format governance restated**: "if you also have anything else that you want to be
  explained, something technical that I could break down and make a fun interesting video, let me know"
  — the "Explained!" series is audience-programmed.
- [2012-06-04] Glosses the acronyms honestly, including where he doesn't know: "LTE stands for long-term
  evolution and HSPA stands for high-speed packet access — I don't really know why those are named that
  way."
