# What's on my Android Phone 2012! (@mkbhd, 2012-12-25)

## Summary
A Christmas-Eve tour of his own Galaxy Note 2 setup — made by Twitter request. The organizing idea is
**making a skinned (TouchWiz) phone look and behave like stock Android** through third-party apps, which
is the control-over-software value expressed through a home-screen. Dense with dated personal-tooling
detail, and it states a small but real value: **he pays for apps that are good.**

## Key claims
- [2012-12-25] **The whole setup is "make TouchWiz look stock"**: "everything about this is still stock,
  but it's running a ton of different apps that make it seem like it's rooted and running stock Android."
  The control-over-software instinct applied without rooting — Nova Launcher Prime + stock-Android app
  replacements.
- [2012-12-25] **"I tend to pay for apps if they're good" — a stated value**: "I tend to pay for apps if
  they're good. If it's good enough, I will pay for it" (he buys the Pro versions of Battery Widget Reborn,
  Nova Launcher, WidgetLocker, etc.). Willingness to pay for quality software, dated.
- [2012-12-25] **A single-home-screen, tightly-centralized philosophy**: "I don't actually have more than
  one home screen — it's just one home screen, everything really tightly centralized, gives me quick
  access to everything I do." The minimalism/neat value applied to his phone layout, with multiple docks
  instead of multiple screens.
- [2012-12-25] **His app stack, dated (late 2012)**: Nova Launcher Prime, WidgetLocker, Zooper Widget,
  HD Widgets; **Falcon Pro** ("probably the best Twitter app for Android right now"); the **stock Google
  Play calendar** ("so much nicer than the Samsung one"); **Spotify** ("pretty much exclusively for my
  music," synced to Facebook); Google Now (heavy use); Press for Google Reader, Pocket, Reddit News,
  Package Buddy, SwipePad, Foursquare, Google Maps, SoundHound, SoundCloud, Snapseed, Instagram, Evernote,
  Dropbox, Google Drive.
- [2012-12-25] **Replaces Samsung's apps with stock ones**: "Gallery ICS… because the Samsung gallery is
  not very good"; "the stock calendar from the Play Store because it's so much nicer than the Samsung
  one." Actively de-skinning without rooting.
- [2012-12-25] **A production/photo note**: "I don't edit photos on my phone that often" (on Snapseed) —
  consistent with editing on his desktop.
- [2012-12-25] **Request-driven**: "I asked on Twitter if you guys wanted it and resoundingly most of you
  said yes." The audience-programmed content pattern.
- [2012-12-25] **Verizon-branding gripe restated**: "I'll be putting a sticker on that home button right
  after this video."
