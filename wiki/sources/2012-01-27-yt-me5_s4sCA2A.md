# The OLED TV Rant! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-27)

## Summary
A "positive rant" — his own term for how it turned out — about the **LG 55" OLED TV**, the one
thing at an otherwise incremental CES 2012 that he says caught his eye. The argument is not
really about the TV: it's about **why he wants the category to succeed**. He wants OLED to
catch on so that competition drives prices down until OLED monitors cost what LCDs do, and he
explicitly hopes Apple enters the market — not because he'd buy an Apple TV, but because the
industry follows Apple.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-27] **CES 2012 assessed as underwhelming, from the outside**: "I kind of stayed away
  from the sort of underwhelming event that was CES 2012… a lot of the stuff was just
  incremental upgrades" — the Droid RAZR MAXX over the RAZR, the TF700T over the Transformer
  Prime. Consistent with him not attending ([[2012-01-04-yt-NspuoxVOUD4]]).
- [2012-01-27] **The display thread made explicit across his own catalog**: he chains three
  earlier videos — the standalone "why OLED rocks" explainer, the Galaxy Nexus OLED display
  video, and the Xoom HD OLED piece — and concludes "in case you haven't noticed, OLED is
  awesome." A rare moment of him naming his own recurring obsession.
- [2012-01-27] **Standing proposal restated**: every phone manufacturer should use OLED, because
  it's thin, looks good, "and it's also really low power — it doesn't light black pixels at all,
  and that saves battery life." Same mechanism he demonstrated on camera in
  [[2011-12-17-yt-snXTJWL6z4g]].
- [2012-01-27] **The economic argument — competition as the price mechanism**: "when stuff
  catches on, competition is great, and that's what's going to have companies pitting against
  each other and trying to undercut prices and deliver the best goods with the lowest price…
  until eventually we have say 24-inch OLED monitors for the same price that we have 24-inch LCD
  monitors right now."
- [2012-01-27] **The Apple-effect argument, with his own preference explicitly bracketed**: "even
  if I don't want to buy necessarily an Apple TV, I want them to introduce that to the market,
  because when Apple introduces something kind of new then other companies are just on their
  tail… and eventually you've got OLED TVs everywhere." He wants a product he wouldn't buy to
  exist, for second-order reasons.
- [2012-01-27] **3D contrasted as the failed prior fad**: 3D was "the thing to throw into TVs"
  at the previous CES and "didn't really get a very welcome response" — OLED is framed as the
  substantive successor to a gimmick.
- [2012-01-27] **Stated intent to pursue review units**: "I am definitely going to be trying my
  hardest to get some of these in-house and reviewing as many of these as possible for you guys"
  — a dated look at how the review pipeline was built, by asking.
- [2012-01-27] Price sensitivity, self-aware: the TV "is also probably for the really really
  rich," but "if Apple starts under maybe say $2,000, I might seriously consider that one."
- [2012-01-27] Sourcing note: he uses CES floor footage shot by **Austin (of Duncan33303)** —
  the peer network supplying coverage he couldn't get himself.
