# 8 Holiday Gifts for a YouTuber (@mkbhd, 2012-12-16)

## Summary
A holiday gift guide for video creators — and, incidentally, **a rich source of his own production
history**: he names the **nifty-fifty 50mm f/1.8 as his first video lens** ("where I first really
started getting creative with DSLR videos") and the **gorilla pod as one of his first creative
tools**. It also documents that he **founded a YouTubers-only Google+ community** that became one of the
top 15 on the platform (10,000+ members). A window into the gear and community that shaped early MKBHD.

## Key claims
- [2012-12-16] **The nifty-fifty was his first video lens — a production-history anchor**: on the 50mm
  f/1.8, "it was **my first video lens, and that's where I first really started getting creative with DSLR
  videos**… very inexpensive and very sharp, allows you to get a blurry background, nice depth of field."
  The origin of his shallow-depth-of-field aesthetic, named.
- [2012-12-16] **The gorilla pod was one of his first creative tools**: "this was actually one of my first
  more creative types of things that I use to make videos… to get all sorts of crazy angles… get more
  creative shots. I think that's ultimately what YouTube's all about." Dated production-craft origin.
- [2012-12-16] **He founded a top-15 Google+ YouTubers community**: "I recently created a community on
  Google+ just for YouTubers by YouTubers… surprisingly it's one of the top 15 communities on all of
  Google+ right now, over 10,000 people have joined it already." A dated, concrete community-building
  achievement and a reflection ("it reminded me how many YouTubers there are").
- [2012-12-16] **His own current gear, dated**: "I'm using a 64 gig class 10 SD card" — the fastest tier,
  for video transfer/shooting.
- [2012-12-16] **The Nexus 7 as a DSLR video monitor** — a creator hack: "with some little hackery… you
  can make this a video monitor for your Canon DSLR and control the DSLR from the tablet." Shows how he
  thinks about repurposing gear for production.
- [2012-12-16] **Studio-lighting candor**: recommends a reflector "because not all of us have studio
  lights" — a dated, humble note on his own (still modest) 2012 setup.
- [2012-12-16] **Doesn't own a YouTube shirt (joke)**: "I don't actually own one yet, so if someone I know
  is watching and wants to get me a gift…" — small window into his humor.
- [2012-12-16] Other picks: a fast/large SD card, a video-editing mouse (names the Cyborg RAT 7), and — as
  the fallback — an Amazon/B&H gift card ("the most effective thing you can get a person").
