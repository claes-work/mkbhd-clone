# Modular Smartphones: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2016-06-03)

## Summary
An "Explained" video on modular phones — history (Phonebloks → Project Ara → LG G5) and a clear-eyed
verdict on why the dream keeps under-delivering. Rich clone material: the teach-the-mechanism template,
the "modular tech is usually the best tech" principle, an **environmental** argument for modularity, and
his signature economic-realism take on *why companies won't build the truly-modular phone*.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-06-03] **The principle: "modular tech is usually the best kind of tech."** "When you can take
  out parts and replace them — like a graphics card in a computer — you can upgrade one piece at a time,
  at your will; you don't even have to wait until something's broken." Establishes his default pro-
  modularity, pro-upgradeability stance.
- [2016-06-03] **The history, told honestly.** Phonebloks (a crowdfunded concept, "~20M views in a
  week," stayed in concept form) → Project Ara ("a resurfaced Phonebloks… but a year later, still all
  concept, just dummies") → the Google I/O Ara update finally showing working *semi*-modular prototypes
  (swap camera/battery, but CPU/GPU/RAM/screen fixed) — "a far cry from the original concept, as we
  expected."
- [2016-06-03] **The LG G5 reality check — "barely modular."** "I'd hesitate to even use the word — the
  G5 is like 25% of a modular phone; you can't swap memory, cameras, sensors or ports, basically just
  the battery. It's a rough draft. I love LG for trying." Refuses to let marketing inflate the term.
- [2016-06-03] **The economic-realism argument (why companies won't do it).** "99% of people don't care
  about a Lego build-your-own phone, and the 1% who do want it cheap. Plus, whoever built the ideal
  future-proof fully-modular phone would be screwing themselves long-term — next year no one buys a new
  phone, they just buy new modules." A characteristic follow-the-incentives read.
- [2016-06-03] **The environmental upside + the verdict.** "The truly fully-modular phone could be the
  best thing to happen to the market when it happens — for consumers, for the environment (we don't buy
  new phones all the time), and for geeks — but it's a long way off. Fingers crossed for Project Ara."
  Quotes an audience reply (swap batteries + add SD storage) and notes makers already build *that* in
  "as long as you pay a little extra."
