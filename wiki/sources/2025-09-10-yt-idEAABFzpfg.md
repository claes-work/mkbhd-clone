# iPhone 17/Pro/Air Impressions: Spot the Red Flags! (@mkbhd, 2025-09-10)

## Summary
Same-day first-impressions from Apple's **September 2025 event** — iPhone 17, 17 Pro, and the
new **iPhone Air**. Marques frames the whole video around a recurring personal device: the
**"text from friends" compression test** — distilling each product into one or two digestible
sentences. Base **iPhone 17 finally gets ProMotion** (the first Pro feature to cross to non-Pro)
+ a clever **square Center Stage selfie camera**. The **17 Pro** switches **back to aluminum
unibody** with a **first-ever iPhone vapor chamber** for sustained performance, plus a bigger
battery and a 48 MP 4X tele. The **iPhone Air** is the headline skeptic's target: stunning in
hand but, "if you subtract emotions from it, it's the worst one" — a cluster of **red flags**
(thermals, durability, battery, $999). Solo → persona/voice data. Disclosed **Ridge** sponsor.

## Key claims
- [2025-09-10] **Framing device — the "texts from friends" test.** Marques says his mental task at
  events is to summarize everything into "one or two easily-digestible sentences, or just words"
  for friends texting to ask what he thinks — and structures the video as that "super compressed
  version." (A characteristic MKBHD explainer method.)
- [2025-09-10] **iPhone 17 (base) one-liner: "it got ProMotion and a better selfie camera, and the
  rest is mostly the same."** Same design/dimensions/camera-bump; new A19 (~20% faster CPU); up to
  3,000 nits; **256 GB base storage**. Key point: **ProMotion (adaptive 1–120 Hz) is now on the
  base iPhone**, "not handicapped at all" — he calls back the old "regular people don't care about
  120 Hz" argument and welcomes it being gone.
- [2025-09-10] **Center Stage selfie camera** (all iPhone 17s): a larger **24 MP square sensor**
  that shoots an 18 MP crop in portrait or landscape without rotating the phone — auto or manual.
  Loudest applause of the keynote; he initially wanted to mock it but calls it "genius" and
  possibly "the biggest upgrade to the iPhone 17."
- [2025-09-10] **iPhone 17 Pro one-liner: "they got better zoom, and more battery, and orange."**
  Three colors only (Cosmic Orange, Deep Blue, Silver); bummed there's **no black Pro** ("but then
  I remembered dbrand exists"). New **camera "plateau" bar** (Pixel-visor-like); Apple moved logic
  board/A19 Pro/storage into the bar to fit **more battery at the bottom** (39 hrs video playback
  vs 16 Pro Max's 33 → ~10% gain).
- [2025-09-10] **Big Pro design shift: back to aluminum unibody + first iPhone vapor chamber.**
  Notes the irony — the iPhone 15 Pro's "whole thing was titanium" two years ago; aluminum is
  "much better thermally." Apple claims **40% better sustained performance** (matters for long
  renders / ray-traced gaming, not bursty everyday use). New 48 MP **4X telephoto** (56% larger
  sensor, more resolution than last year's 12 MP 5X → smaller gap to main, more usable crop-zoom).
  Adds Qi2, Genlock sync, **ProRes RAW** (wants to test in an upcoming Auto Focus video).
- [2025-09-10] **iPhone Air ("no number, just Air"): the red-flag phone.** 5.6 mm at its thinnest;
  6.5" ProMotion, A19 Pro (minus one GPU core), titanium, single 48 MP Fusion camera, new **N1**
  connectivity chip (Wi-Fi 7, BT 6, Thread) + efficient **C1X modem**. $999, replacing the Plus
  slot. Verdict: **"if you subtract emotions from it, it's the worst one"** — surrounded by iPhones
  better in nearly every way except thinness/weight.
- [2025-09-10] **The Air's red flags, enumerated:** (1) **thermals** — still titanium, no room for
  a vapor chamber, so it'll likely get hotter and throttle sooner than the Pro; (2) **durability** —
  thin phones have historically bent (points viewers to JerryRigEverything's bend test), skeptical
  of Apple's "most durable iPhone ever" claim; (3) **battery** — "all signs point to it being
  trash," sold alongside a **dedicated 3,149 mAh MagSafe battery** just to match the 17 Pro's quoted
  playback; also e-SIM-only globally and no mmWave 5G.
- [2025-09-10] **Two lenses on the Air:** either Apple **"throwing something new at the wall"**
  (like the Mini, then Plus, both underwhelming in that lineup slot), or a **"visionary preview at
  the future of all phones"** — analogy to the **first MacBook Air** (underpowered, but all laptops
  eventually got that thin). Speculates the Air may be one half of a future **ultra-thin foldable
  iPhone**, drawing a parallel to Samsung's S25 Edge preceding the thin Z Fold 7.
- [2025-09-10] Also announced (to test later): **AirPods Pro 3** (2× ANC, IP57, workout heart-rate,
  same $250), **Apple Watch Series 11** (5G, possible hypertension detection), **Watch Ultra 3**
  (thinner bezels, LTPO3, longer battery).
