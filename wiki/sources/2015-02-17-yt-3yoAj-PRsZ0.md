# Audio Technica ATH-M70X Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-02-17)

## Summary
A headphone review that shows his **audio literacy** and his **judge-by-purpose** discipline. The M70x
are **studio monitors** — a deliberately **flat/neutral frequency response** for mixing, which "to the
untrained ear may sound like it's lacking bass or even a little tinny, but that flat response is exactly
what you want when editing." He's careful not to call them "better" than the beloved M50x — they're for a
*different job*. A production-craft datum (he mixes his own videos on them) and a clean know-your-domain
statement.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-02-14] **Headphone reviews are simple — just build + sound** ("no battery life or display tech to
  worry about"). Build: still hardened plastic (matte now), lighter and more comfortable than the M50x
  (which he notes ran a little tight); a fantastic seal; but they **no longer fold** (need the bigger
  included hard case), and use the twist-and-lock cable (no third-party cables).
- [2015-02-17] **Judge-by-purpose: these are studio monitors, not consumer headphones.** "These are
  studio monitors, for real — a very neutral sound signature, which is what you want if you're monitoring
  or mixing. To the untrained ear that may sound like it's lacking bass or a little tinny, but this flat
  frequency response is exactly what you want when editing." The M50x are more **colored** (more bass) and
  "will continue to sound better for everyday listening, music, movies, gaming."
- [2015-02-17] **He mixes his own videos on them** — "including actually mixing the last video I did,
  the Dell tablet review," plugged into his computer's DAC.
- [2015-02-17] **Know-your-domain + the open-back next step.** The "M" = closed-back monitors; the new
  ATH-R70x ("R" = open-back reference) is the usual next step up in audio quality ("like moving to
  speakers right next to your ears — I'll explain in a later video"). Verdict: the M70x is "the upper
  limit for closed-back production headphones you want at this price"; the M50x stays his everyday
  recommendation "for its rich, clear sound in a ridiculously good price bracket."
- [2015-02-17] **A source-critical audio aside:** "I don't know if I believe in the whole burning-in
  headphones thing."
