# YouTube's New Video Editor (@mkbhd, 2011-09-15)

## Summary
A same-era walkthrough of YouTube's rebuilt in-browser video editor, framed as a
follow-up to his earlier video on the previous editor. The headline difference he leads
with is not a feature but a **distribution consequence**: saving edits keeps the video at
the same URL with its existing views, likes and comments, instead of forcing a re-upload.
He then tours quick fixes (trim, rotate, stabilization), the lighting/color controls
(fill light, contrast, saturation, temperature), the effects tab, and the audio tab, and
tests stabilization with deliberately shaky footage.

## Key claims
- [2011-09-15] The key improvement over the previous YouTube editor is that edits save
  **in place** — "the video stays online as the same video, so no more republishing or
  re-sharing a new video… it has all the likes, views and comments of the original."
  Emphasis on retained social proof over the editing features themselves.
- [2011-09-15] Stabilization is tested against intentionally shaky footage and judged to
  work "amazingly well," with the caveat — surfaced unprompted — that it **crops/zooms in
  slightly** to hide the moving edges.
- [2011-09-15] The grainy-looking preview pane is flagged as a preview artifact only: the
  saved output "keeps full quality."
- [2011-09-15] Scope verdict: the effects tab is "really basic and meant for beginners who
  don't have a video editor yet — this isn't going to replace your iMovie or Movie Maker."
  Positive but bounded; the audio tab is called "kind of boring" with "nothing really
  groundbreaking or new."
- [2011-09-15] Useful detail noted in the audio tab: track suggestions are matched to the
  video's length so the music does not get cut off.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "Not only because of the new features, but it has one key difference: when you're done
> editing in this one and save your effects, the video stays online as the same video. So
> no more republishing or re-sharing a new video — this one has all the likes, views and
> comments of the original."

> "I wasn't sure it would work well, but I intentionally filmed shaky video, as you can
> tell in the preview windows, and this after-processes actually works amazingly well.
> Now I do have to note that it did actually zoom into my video just a little bit to cut
> out the edges."

> "This isn't going to replace your iMovie or Movie Maker or anything, but it's nice to
> see."
