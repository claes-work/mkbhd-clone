# Nokia Lumia 925 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-10)

## Summary
His review of a Windows Phone 8 flagship — explicitly **from the perspective of a heavy
Google-services user**. A clean instance of his **disclose-your-baseline / give-it-a-fair-
shot** methodology: he praises the build, display, and battery genuinely, but the
**customization ceiling** and the **app gap** are dealbreakers for him. "If this phone was
running Android, I'd carry it in a heartbeat."

## Key claims
- [2013-07-10] **Discloses his perspective up front — the methodology**: "a lot of people asked
  me to review Windows Phone from the perspective of someone like me who uses a lot of Google
  services — Google Tasks, Calendar, Gmail. I gave it a full, fair shot, and it's important to
  note where I'm coming from." His perspective-disclosure review ethic (cf. "How to Give an
  Honest Video Review").
- [2013-07-10] **Genuine praise where earned — build, display, battery**: "top-five best-built
  smartphones you can get right now, alongside the HTC One — zero flex, aluminum ring, Gorilla
  Glass 2… the AMOLED display is 334 PPI with insane contrast… and impressive battery life:
  put it to sleep at 100% and it's at 97% the next morning." Credits the hardware fully.
- [2013-07-10] **The customization ceiling — his make-it-your-own value**: "I usually like
  tinkering with the look of Android — home screens, etc. — and I got bored pretty easily in
  Windows Phone. Besides changing colors, you can only change tile size and position. If you
  like making the device your own, you'll be disappointed." His customization value as a
  dealbreaker.
- [2013-07-10] **The app gap — the real killer**: "this is what kills Windows Phone for me —
  the app support just isn't there. The top-apps list very quickly diminishes from Angry Birds
  down to flashlights and terrible mini-games. The interface is fine, but it's missing so many
  apps we're used to." Names the single decisive flaw (his one-critical-flaw pattern).
- [2013-07-10] **The honest bottom line**: "if this phone was running Android, I'd carry it in a
  heartbeat — I'm such a big fan of the design, aesthetics, and build. But there are decisions
  you have to make." Separates the hardware admiration from the platform verdict.
- [2013-07-10] **Red-and-black aside**: "if you're not into red and black like I am, you can
  change the whole OS theme with a pair of flipped switches." The recurring disclosed bias.
