# iPhone Commercials vs. Android Commercials (@mkbhd, 2011-11-15)

## Summary
A comparative teardown of smartphone advertising: he plays an iPhone 4S ad, then a Verizon
Droid Bionic ad, and narrates the difference in real time. The iPhone spot shows the phone
in the first two seconds and states a feature; the Bionic spot opens on a mystery, a woman,
a robot fight and a lightsaber before showing much of anything ("is this a Tron trailer?").
What lifts this above a rant is the second half: he **explains the difference structurally**
rather than blaming the creative. Apple controls its own ads end to end; most Android ads
aren't Google's at all — they're Verizon, AT&T or Sprint ads that must also sell the
carrier, which is why the phone gets crowded out.

## Key claims
- [2011-11-15] **The structural explanation (the core insight)**: "Apple controls everything
  that they put into the iPhone commercials — that's just an Apple commercial… With the
  Android stuff, most of those aren't Google commercials. I've seen a few Nexus ads, but all
  the rest of them are Verizon or AT&T or Sprint ads. The Bionic ad we watched was a Verizon
  ad, and they somehow have to promote Verizon in the ad too, not just the Bionic. And that's
  why you're seeing so much extra crap thrown in there."
- [2011-11-15] **Why the iPhone wins a specific audience, stated without contempt**: "this is
  the reason why the iPhone is the phone for people who aren't tech-savvy, or just don't care
  about specs, or don't really do that much on their phones. This is why the iPhone is so
  damn popular in those categories." Marketing legibility, not product superiority, is the
  proposed mechanism.
- [2011-11-15] **Self-implicating admission** — unusual for an avowed Android partisan: "I've
  never wanted to buy an iPhone, but watching iPhone commercials honestly makes me think
  about, like, what would happen if I switched to an iPhone?" He reports the ads working on
  *him*, which is the evidence for his own thesis.
- [2011-11-15] **Blunt aggregate verdict**: "I haven't seen a single phone commercial for an
  Android phone in the United States that has made me want to buy the phone if I didn't
  already want to. Not a single one."
- [2011-11-15] Self-positioning, dated: "as an Android follower, an avid Android follower for
  the past couple of years" — and an explicit acknowledgment that "I don't usually talk about
  iPhone stuff or iOS stuff in my videos," marking this as a deliberate departure.
- [2011-11-15] Method note: he narrates the Bionic ad live as a confused viewer, then
  explicitly extrapolates from himself to the general audience — "keep in mind this is from a
  fairly tech-savvy person like me; I'm guessing for the average Joe watching, that commercial
  would go a little something like this."
- [2011-11-15] The stated goal is corrective, aimed at the carrier rather than the platform:
  share it "so we can try to get the word out to Verizon that they're doing it wrong."
