# LG G Watch Hands-on Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-26)

## Summary
A hands-on with the **LG G Watch** at **Google I/O 2014** — one of the first three **Android Wear**
watches (alongside the Samsung Gear Live and the Moto 360). This is the **concrete arrival of Android
Wear**, the payoff of his standing first-gen-wearable thesis. The square, Pebble-like G Watch ($229,
36 hr always-on) is characterized bluntly: **"Google Now and your Android notification center on your
wrist — that's basically all it does."** He reads it as an existential threat to Pebble.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-26] **The three launch Android Wear watches:** the circular Moto 360 ("the one everyone's
  talking about"), the Samsung Gear Live (square), and the LG G Watch (square). The G Watch ships **today
  at $229** in the Play Store.
- [2014-06-26] **Specs/feel:** 280×280 LCD, stainless steel, black or white, a 400 mAh battery LG rates
  at ~36 hours **always-on**, no physical buttons, a 9-axis accel/gyro for raise-to-wake. Size/shape is
  "really similar to a Pebble" (a bit bigger).
- [2014-06-26] **What it actually does (the honest first-gen framing):** "you can think of this as
  Google Now and your Android notification center on your wrist — that's basically all it does" — Google
  Now cards, step count, weather, notifications, and media play/pause via swipes. Demoed in retail mode
  (no voice/phone connection), where it ran smoothly.
- [2014-06-26] **Sensor question:** unclear whether it uses its own sensors or the phone's; LG implied
  the pedometer/other sensors come from the paired phone over Bluetooth.
- [2014-06-26] **Competitive read — bad news for Pebble.** "Coming in at $229, this is less than a
  Pebble Steel… it has a lot more functionality. If I was Pebble right now, I'd be very concerned about
  this kind of stuff." Android Wear is "getting a lot more in the spotlight."
