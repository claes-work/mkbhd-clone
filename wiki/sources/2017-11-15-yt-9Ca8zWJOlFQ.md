# Apple iPhone X Review: The Best Yet! (@mkbhd, 2017-11-15)

## Summary
The full iPhone X review — "my favorite iPhone ever, by far… iconic." The clone-relevant core is the honest
tension he threads: the redesign that makes it feel genuinely new is also **the source of its sore spots** —
a real learning curve (3–4 fundamental interaction changes), the notch (he's in the "don't-notice-it" camp),
and the most-breakable-iPhone-ever glass.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-11-15] **"My favorite iPhone ever, by far… iconic — and that's also the source of the sore spots."**
  "It finally feels like a *new* iPhone — a big part of why I like it so much — but that's also where the sore
  spots come from. Every other iPhone has been home-button-rectangle-rounded-corners; you knew exactly how to
  use it. The X has three or four fundamental changes to how you use the phone — clearly still an iPhone, but
  a real learning curve unlike any other." Names the trade-off of a bold redesign directly.
- [2017-11-15] **The notch: "two camps — I'm in the don't-notice-it camp."** "Camp 1: I hate it, it's ugly.
  Camp 2: I don't notice it anymore — and like with the Essential, I fall in camp 2. When you shrink the
  bezels in 2017, you still have to put all the front sensors, the cameras, the IR blaster, and the depth
  sensor *somewhere*." Frames the notch as an engineering necessity, and reports his lived-with-it position.
- [2017-11-15] **The size + the slippery-stainless-steel caveat.** "It feels great — an awesome size (closer
  to an iPhone 8 than an 8 Plus) — but if there were a bigger iPhone X Plus, I'd totally get that. The
  stainless-steel sides are slippery — it *will* slide out of your pocket at least once — and the super-shiny
  silver finish won't stay that pristine; I usually go matte-black skin, but dbrand has a new **grip** bumper
  I'm trying." His slipperiness/skins thread, plus a wish for a larger model.
- [2017-11-15] **"Officially the most breakable iPhone ever" (glass back) — stated plainly.** "Is it the most
  breakable iPhone ever, thanks to glass on the back? Yes. Yes." Water resistance + wireless charging are "nice
  peace of mind"; no headphone jack ("welcome to 2017"); the speakers, even non-front-facing, are "pretty
  good." A well-considered, well-put-together $1,000 package.
- [2017-11-15] **The one hardware thing he really dislikes: the camera bump's new orientation.** "Not the
  usual rocking-on-a-table reason (I've accepted that) — because of the vertical orientation, a couple times I
  accidentally got my finger in the photo/video, since it's up in the corner. Takes a few days to stop." A
  specific, use-based gripe.
