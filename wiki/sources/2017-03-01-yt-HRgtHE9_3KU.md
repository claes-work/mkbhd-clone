# Dear Apple! (2017) (@mkbhd, 2017-03-01)

## Summary
A recurring "Dear Apple" wishlist video (a format he'd done ~4 years earlier) — "being a user first gives me
a good perspective." Four asks that concentrate several durable personal values: **matte-black everything**,
**USB-C the iPhone**, **refresh the Mac Pro**, and **wireless charging in the next iPhone**. A rich beliefs/
voice/appearance node, and a candid biography detail about his editing setup.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-03-01] **"Matte black all the things" — and the matte-black iPhone 7 = "the best colorway of any
  iPhone ever."** "You got it started with the matte black iPhone 7 — in my opinion the best colorway of any
  iPhone ever, I really do think that. Imagine a matte black Mac Pro, iMac, keyboard, trackpad, AirPods. I'm
  a fan of matte black — it's hard to go wrong with it." A cornerstone appearance/taste statement.
- [2017-03-01] **"USB-C the iPhone."** "You're already at the front of the USB-C line with the MacBooks —
  while you're at it, USB-C the iPhone. I know Apple makes a ton from Lightning licensing, and Lightning is
  physically smaller — but Apple users have never known the feeling of coming home with **one charger** for
  laptop and phone. USB-C is obviously the future; you took away the jack, so you're familiar with that."
  The one-cable thesis aimed squarely at Apple.
- [2017-03-01] **"Refresh the Mac Pro, please" — with a biography disclosure.** "I'm a Final Cut Pro editor
  — I switched from a PC and Premiere a couple years ago — so I have to use the most powerful Mac Apple
  makes, which is this 4-year-old 12-core trash-can Mac Pro. It's kind of a joke that this is their most
  advanced machine, and they still sell it for the launch price. I know there are only like 12 Final Cut
  editors left, but we're so hungry for an update." Why he stays: "the Mac Pro still renders/plays faster
  than the more powerful PCs I've tried, plus Final Cut plugins that don't exist in Adobe."
- [2017-03-01] **"Wireless charging in the next iPhone" (the ~4th time asking).** "If Apple's marching
  toward a completely portless iPhone, you have to get people used to wireless charging — this could be the
  killer feature of the next iPhone. Apple tends to adopt new tech late and do it better; this is that
  opportunity. This is probably the fourth video where I've said they should do this." A tracked, repeated
  forecast (wireless charging lands with the iPhone 8/X later in 2017).
- [2017-03-01] **The user-first credibility frame.** "I'm a person who uses a lot of technology — that's
  why I review it; being a user first is what gives me a good perspective." His stated basis for opinion.

## Notable verbatim quotes
