# iOS 16 Hands-On: Top 5 New Features! (@mkbhd, 2022-07-11)

## Summary
A solo hands-on run through Marques's five favorite features in the iOS 16 beta (which
he warns is not yet stable enough to daily-drive), framed as the software update that
will change any iPhone 8-or-later "for the better." Counts down: (5) edit/unsend
iMessages within a 15-minute window; (4) "live objects" — lifting a subject out of any
photo (and live text in videos), an extension of iOS 15's Live Text; (3) "the little
things" (per-hour weather from the Dark Sky acquisition, landscape Face ID, refreshed
Spotlight, Apple Card order tracking in Wallet, and a **haptic keyboard** Android had
"for a decade"); (2) **Continuity Camera** — using the iPhone as a Mac webcam, with the
"Desk View" top-down trick he calls the biggest on-stage reaction; and (1) the new
customizable **lock screen** (fonts, widgets, depth-effect clock cutouts, Focus-linked
lock screens, and Live Activities). Recurring MKBHD tell: he judges a phone's haptic
engine by typing a Wi-Fi password. Solo video — all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2022-07-11] iOS 16 will reach any iPhone 8 or later; the beta "is not stable enough to
  make your main just yet," but there's "a lot of good stuff here," some of which Apple
  did not cover on stage.
- [2022-07-11] Edit/unsend iMessages works only if the recipient is also on iOS 16 —
  otherwise they still see the original message; edited messages show they were edited
  but not the edit history.
- [2022-07-11] "Live objects" — long-press to lift any subject off its background and
  paste it elsewhere — does "a surprise amazingly good job for a literal one second
  background delete," and works on any photo from any camera, not just iPhone
  portrait-mode shots. Same object-recognition later powers the lock-screen depth effect.
- [2022-07-11] Apple only shipped a 1080p webcam on the M1 MacBooks in 2020 and it "still
  looks worse than a normal phone camera," so Continuity Camera — using the good iPhone
  camera as the Mac's webcam — is a "genius" move (needs iOS 16 + macOS Ventura).
- [2022-07-11] Desk View uses the ultra-wide to flip a top-down view of the desk in front
  of the keyboard: "seems like black magic but it works pretty well" as long as the
  object is fairly flat.
- [2022-07-11] The iPhone finally getting a **haptic keyboard** is "one of those things
  that Android has had for so many years, a decade at this point"; because "the iPhone
  literally has the best vibration motor on any phone," the feature is very good (off by
  default). His personal tell: he judges any phone's haptics by feeling the keyboard
  while typing a Wi-Fi password — good ones he leaves on, mushy ones he turns off.
- [2022-07-11] The customizable lock screen is "the biggest visual, most obvious change
  to iOS": save/switch multiple layouts like Apple Watch faces, watch-complication-style
  widgets, a depth-effect clock that nestles between wallpaper foreground and background
  (breaks once you add bottom widgets, at least in beta), and Focus-mode-linked lock
  screens. He calls it the highlight of iOS 16 because the lock screen is "the thing that
  you interact with the most on your phone," maybe 100 unlocks a day.
- [2022-07-11] Live Activities (a single notification that updates live — Uber ETA, sports
  scores, timers) and notifications now rolling in from the bottom are praised for not
  covering the customized lock screen; he prefers the new stacked notification style.
