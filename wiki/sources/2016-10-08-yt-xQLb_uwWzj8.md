# Dope Tech: Self-Lacing Nike Mag! (@mkbhd, 2016-10-08)

## Summary
A short Dope Tech hands-on with the 2016 Nike Mag, the real-world self-lacing shoe inspired by the "power laces" Nike imagined for Marty McFly's 2015 in Back to the Future Part II. Marques frames it as "a little taste of the future": the shoe reads your weight via a sensor in the sole and uses motors connected to the laces to auto-tighten, with green/red/yellow buttons for manual tightening, loosening, and lights/battery check. His through-line is that this is a badass but impractical collector piece, not a daily driver — only 89 pairs of this first kind exist and they'll be raffled off for $10 an entry, with proceeds going to the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's research. He closes by pointing to the forthcoming Nike HyperAdapt 1.0 as the actual self-lacing retail product.

## Key claims
- [2016-10-08] The Nike Mag makes real the self-lacing high-tech shoe that Back to the Future imagined for the year 2015 — "kind of."
- [2016-10-08] The technical magic is in the sole: a sensor there works with motors on the back that are connected to the laces.
- [2016-10-08] When the shoe senses your weight for the first time, the laces automatically tighten to be just snug enough to get up and go.
- [2016-10-08] Auto-tightening isn't perfect every time, so there are in-shoe buttons: green to tighten more, red to loosen (and to take them off), yellow to toggle the lights and serve as a battery check.
- [2016-10-08] The lights on the back double as a battery indicator; leaving them on all day drains the shoe, which then must be charged from a wall outlet at night "just like your phone."
- [2016-10-08] Despite housing a motor, sensors, and batteries, the shoes aren't heavy — they're actually kind of lightweight and comfortable, though big high-tops.
- [2016-10-08] The Nike Mag is more of a collector piece than day-to-day footwear, but it looks badass on the foot.
- [2016-10-08] You can't just buy them: only 89 pairs of this first kind are being made.
- [2016-10-08] The pairs are being raffled off — a $10 entry buys a chance — with all proceeds going to the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's research.
- [2016-10-08] The Nike HyperAdapt 1.0, coming later, is expected to be the real retail self-lacing product.
- [2016-10-08] Nike lent Marques the shoes to try out for a little while (they are not his to keep).
