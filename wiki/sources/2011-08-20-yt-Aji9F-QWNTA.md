# Stop It! (@mkbhd, 2011-08-20)

## Summary
A rapid-fire, tongue-in-cheek "rant" video listing tech-community pet peeves
— framed as "on behalf of a lot of other YouTubers and the tech community" —
aimed at both "fanboys and the newbies." Covers: asking questions Google
could answer, Foursquare check-ins at your own house, Blackberry-model
snobbery, using Internet Explorer, judging people by phone choice, asking
for profile-picture likes, treating every tablet as "an iPad," comparing
every thin laptop to the MacBook Air, patent-suing over touchscreens,
breathless iPhone-rumor reporting, expecting geeks to "fix everything," and
using Myspace.

## Key claims
- [2011-08-20] Establishes an early, dated example of the channel's
  opinionated/comedic commentary format — a direct precursor to the later
  "tech industry commentary" voice, delivered as a listicle-rant rather than
  a review.
- [2011-08-20] Explicit anti-Internet-Explorer stance, stated for emphasis:
  "stop using Internet Explorer — seriously, seriously, it's for your own
  good."
- [2011-08-20] Pushes back on genericizing product categories by brand name
  ("stop asking if every tablet in existence is an iPad — this is not an
  iPad") and on using the MacBook Air as the default thinness benchmark for
  every laptop — an early instance of the "compared to what?" critical
  instinct (previously tracked from later, more developed sources) showing
  up here as its inverse: objecting to lazy, un-earned comparisons.
