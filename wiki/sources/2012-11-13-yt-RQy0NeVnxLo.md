# The SuperNexus Project! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-13)

## Summary
A concept video where he builds his ideal "Nexus" by hand: a **black Verizon Galaxy S3, rooted and
running CyanogenMod 10** (near-stock Jelly Bean), plus flashed Android 4.2 extras — getting stock
Android *and* better hardware *and* LTE, the last of which the real Nexus 4 lacks. It's the fullest
expression of his **control-over-software** value: rather than accept any single shipped phone, he
assembles the exact hardware+software+network combination he wants. Confirms this rig as his daily
driver.

## Key claims
- [2012-11-13] **The premise — DIY the ideal phone**: "combining the best of the Galaxy Nexus and the
  Nexus 4 and more, this is it — this is the super Nexus." He solves the real 2012 dilemma (Nexus 4 has
  the newest Android but no LTE; the Galaxy Nexus is aging) by rolling his own.
- [2012-11-13] **The build**: a **black** Verizon Galaxy S3 (LTE), rooted, running **CyanogenMod 10**
  (stock-based Android 4.1.2) — "the clean, nice look of stock Android the way Google intended it" — with
  the black hardware, black notification bar and black wallpaper. Adds flashed Android 4.2 extras: the
  **gesture keyboard**, and the new **4.2 camera/gallery** (Photo Sphere, filters).
- [2012-11-13] **Daily driver confirmed, on Verizon**: "this is the phone that has been my daily driver
  now for a little while now, still on Verizon Wireless — it's a super Nexus." Reconfirms the S3-on-CM10
  carry from [[2012-08-30-yt-IT73da-C4sw]] and [[2012-09-21-yt-kRB1-APKUqs]].
- [2012-11-13] **Why near-stock over the shipped skin — stated benefits**: CM10's quick-setting toggles
  in the notification bar are "an even faster way to edit Wi-Fi / auto-rotate / brightness than Android
  4.2, which actually takes an extra click"; stability is fine even on nightlies, and milestone builds are
  "tailored specifically to be daily-driver material — I've had pretty much zero crashes."
- [2012-11-13] **The value made explicit — Nexus experience + better hardware + LTE**: "this delivers a
  Nexus-like experience with even more awesome hardware and add-ons all over the place… on top of the LTE
  speeds that some of us just can't live without." The control-over-software through-line, now paired with
  refusing to give up LTE.
- [2012-11-13] **Aesthetic disclosure**: "I really like the all-black design and the look of it" — the
  black-and-red / all-black taste applied to his own phone.
- [2012-11-13] Previews the 4.2 features he'll cover in a dedicated video ([[2012-11-15-yt-G43tYXIlUV4]]).
