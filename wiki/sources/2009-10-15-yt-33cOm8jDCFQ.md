# MKBHD Update 4.0 (@mkbhd, 2009-10-15)

## Summary
An **MKBHD Update** episode covering four threads: (1) discloses that his channel was
**suspended by YouTube for two weeks**, which he says was wrongful, was resolved after he
contacted YouTube, and that all videos/subscribers were restored; (2) restates he will
not reapply for YouTube Partnership until roughly 10,000 subscribers, deprioritizing the
banner/upload-length benefits in favor of continuing tutorials/unboxings/reviews; (3)
puts the new 1920×960 "mega widescreen" format to an audience vote (stretch to fill it,
or keep native 1280×720 16:9); (4) discloses he will be balancing a **varsity sport**,
AP classes, and the channel this school year, settling on a 3–5-video/week cadence; and
floats a possible "Can Your Mac Do This" series aimed at PC users, pending audience
reaction.

## Key claims
- [2009-10-15] **Channel suspended and reinstated — dated biographical incident**:
  states the channel "was actually suspended from YouTube for two long weeks," that
  people noticed via Twitter, that the suspension was wrongful, and that he contacted
  YouTube and got everything restored with subscribers intact. No cause is given in this
  video.
- [2009-10-15] **YouTube Partnership timeline restated and extended**: reaffirms he
  won't apply again "for a very long time," now anchoring the threshold at "maybe 10,000

  frames the banner/>10-min-upload benefits as not worth pursuing yet — "not really my
  goal here on YouTube."
- [2009-10-15] **Production-format decision put to a vote**: explicitly asks viewers

  native 16:9 — continuing the established pattern (echoes the earlier HQ-by-default and

- [2009-10-15] **School/channel balance disclosed**: names a varsity sport plus AP
  classes as competing time commitments for the school year, and commits to a 3–5
  video/week cadence as the resulting compromise (down from his higher earlier pace).
- [2009-10-15] **New content-series idea floated, contingent on feedback**: "Can Your Mac
  Do This" — a series aimed at PC users despite acknowledging much of his audience is Mac
  users — explicitly not yet decided, pending viewer reaction.
