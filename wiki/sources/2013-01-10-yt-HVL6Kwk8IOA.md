# Sony Xperia Z at CES 2013! (@mkbhd, 2013-01-10)

## Summary
A CES 2013 floor hands-on with the **Sony Xperia Z** — a flagship 5" 1080p phone he
singles out as one of his favorites of the show and "potentially the best phone at CES
2013." Notable mostly for an early, clearly-stated **industrial-design aesthetic** (thin
bezel, thin profile, glass build "on par with the Nexus 4") and a candid design gripe;
low new-belief yield beyond the design-taste datum and the continued CES-2013 context.

## Key claims
- [2013-01-10] **Design-first praise — an aesthetic datum**: "it's one of the better
  looking phones I've seen here… this is an exceptionally handsome phone, I think on par
  with the Nexus 4, it just looks good." He ties the verdict explicitly to build and
  internals, "not just because of the skin." Early evidence of his **design/hardware-
  aesthetic** lens (thin bezel, thin profile, glass back).
- [2013-01-10] **Even-handed within a favorite** — flags the flaws honestly: the volume
  rocker "is in a weird place… it should totally be on the left side," and the glossy
  glass back "is a fingerprint magnet." Praises the phone while naming its faults (his
  standing even-handedness).
- [2013-01-10] **Notes he's not usually a Sony-phone person**: "I've never been a big
  Sony smartphone person, but they really stepped up their game here big time" — credit
  where due, against his own prior lean (a dated bias-disclosure).
- [2013-01-10] **Spec-literacy on displays restated**: "not all 5-inch displays are
  created equal — some are 1280×720, some are 1920×1080; this one's 1080p… but it
  doesn't have the best viewing angles in the world." Praises resolution, docks viewing
  angles — resolution ≠ panel quality.
- [2013-01-10] **Water-resistant, not waterproof** — a careful distinction: "water
  resistant for up to 1 meter deep, not waterproof, but definitely useful if you happen
  to splash your phone." Precision about a marketing claim.
