# Tour of mkbHD Youtube Insight [HD] (@mkbhd, 2009-06-20)

## Summary
A full screen tour of his YouTube Insight analytics dashboard — the first source in the
corpus with concrete, dated audience numbers straight from the platform. He is
averaging roughly 1,500 views/day channel-wide; his **most-viewed video is a Safari 4.0
beta install tutorial** (~16–17k views at the time, later re-titled/annotated to cover
the final release rather than reshot, now "25% of my total video views in the last six
months"); his viewership skews heavily United States, then Canada and the UK; his
demographic is age 13–24, "vast majority... male"; and his average video rating is
"hovering just over 4.0." He asks viewers to post their own Insight tours as video
responses. ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-20] **Channel-wide pace**: "I'm hovering about one and a half thousand
  views per day" and "it's going pretty well, it's doing pretty steadily."
- [2009-06-20] **Most-viewed video named and explained**: a Safari 4.0 beta
  install tutorial — "the first video on YouTube about Safari 4 beta" — got ~16–17k
  views; when the final release shipped he **kept the video and updated title +
  annotation** rather than re-record ("the instructions for installation have remained
  the same, so I just changed the title and added an annotation letting you know the
  same instructions applied"). It is now his single most-viewed video, "25% of my total
  video views in the last six months." This is a second, independent instance of the
  redo/supersede-with-metadata-edit pattern (distinct from full reshoots).
- [2009-06-20] **Geographic breakdown**: heaviest in the United States, then Canada,
  then the UK, "a few more in Brazil and Australia."
- [2009-06-20] **Demographics**: viewers mostly under 65 ("a little creepy"), core band
  13–24, "the vast majority of my viewers are male."
- [2009-06-20] **Community engagement metrics** disclosed: average rating "hovering
  just over 4.0," with ratings, comments, and favorites all tracked and rising.
- [2009-06-20] **Explains why the tool matters commercially**: "if you're thinking
  about emailing a company... telling them you know I have a lot of viewers from this
  part of the world... you can definitely check this part out" — analytics framed
  explicitly as sponsorship-pitch material, the earliest such framing in the corpus.
- [2009-06-20] **Audience-engagement device**: asks viewers to post their own Insight
  tour as a video response, continuing the participatory-series pattern (cf. Tech
  Channel of the Week).
