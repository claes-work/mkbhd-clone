# Dope Tech: Weirdest Earbuds I've Ever Tried! (@mkbhd, 2022-03-02)

## Summary
A solo Dope Tech episode showcasing four unusual gadgets, each scored out of 10. (1) The
**X screen** for Xbox Series S — a Kickstarter accessory that clips an 11.6" 1080p 60 Hz
IPS display onto the console to turn it into a laptop form factor (must stay plugged in;
~40 min on the optional 99 Wh battery); $250; scored 7/10. (2) The **Sony LinkBuds** — the
"weirdest but coolest earbuds" he's seen: an open-ring 11 mm driver with a physical hole so
you always hear your environment (a permanent, non-adjustable transparency), plus
tap-your-skull volume/track controls via the Sony Connect app; $179, ~5.5 hr battery,
USB-C, no wireless charging; scored 8/10. (3) The **Asus ROG Flow Z13** — a full Windows
gaming tablet (up to Core i9, RTX 3050 Ti, external-GPU dock support) he admires purely for
its red-and-black aesthetic and engineering ("not meant for me but I can still respect it");
scored 9/10 for aesthetics. (4) A **USB-C power cable with a wattage readout display**,
part of his everyday carry, that "every time I link it, it sells out." Closes with the
record-setting collab giveaway with Aaron (Mr Who's The Boss) and dbrand, tied to a
donation to SolarAid. Solo video — all persona/voice data; the giveaway is a collab.

## Key claims
- [2022-03-02] Handheld gaming "is having a bit of a moment"; he's also testing the Valve
  Steam Deck. The X screen turns an Xbox Series S into a laptop form factor via HDMI+USB
  passthrough; the 11.6" 1080p panel is smaller than a TV so it's "significantly sharper"
  at higher pixel density. Weakness: you basically always need to be plugged in (~40 min on
  battery). $250; scored **7/10** "if it's actually shipping."
- [2022-03-02] The **Sony LinkBuds** are "the strangest but coolest earbuds design I've ever
  seen" — a circular driver with a hole in the middle, so you constantly hear the outside
  world (great for streets/transit). The always-on transparency is passive/physical, so it
  "doesn't take extra battery power"; not as good at amplifying environment as AirPods Pro,
  but consistent, and you can drown the outside out by turning volume up.
- [2022-03-02] LinkBuds' second trick: with no room for on-bud controls, you **tap the skin
  around your ear** (double/triple tap, mappable per side in the Sony Connect app) for
  volume, track skip, assistant, or answering calls. $179, ~5.5 hr battery, USB-C, no
  wireless charging, recycled-plastic case; sound is solid but light on bass (not sealed in
  the ear); touch controls are "finicky sometimes." Scored **8/10**.
- [2022-03-02] The **Asus ROG Flow Z13** is a full Windows gaming tablet (up to 14-core i9,
  RTX 3050 Ti, up to 1 TB SSD, vapor-chamber cooling) — "like a Surface Pro but way more
  gaming focused," with a Thunderbolt USB-C port plus an XGm port for external GPUs (up to
  RTX 3080). He's "really into this design"/aesthetic; scored "like a **9/10** just for the
  aesthetic." (References Dave2D's full review — 120 FPS in Overwatch on a tablet.)
- [2022-03-02] The **USB-C cable with a wattage display readout** has been part of his
  everyday carry since his EDC video — "lowkey just like nerdy pretty fun to plug in and see
  exactly what sort of wattage you're getting"; ties to his recurring point about peak vs.
  average charging rates. It sells out every time he links it.
- [2022-03-02] Announces "possibly the biggest tech giveaway of all time" with **Aaron (Mr
  Who's The Boss)** — who just hit 10M subscribers — and **dbrand** ("the logistics kings in
  tech"): 10 iPhone 13 Pro Maxes, 10 Galaxy S22 Ultras, 20 PS5s, 30 OLED Switches, 50 dbrand
  grip cases, 80 AirPods 3, and 100 t-shirts (including his iconic histogram shirt). Both
  creators donate 2 cents per entry to **SolarAid** (solar lights for families who otherwise
  burn dangerous fuels) — framed as a giveaway "that actually has a little bit of a positive
  impact on the planet."
