# Google Nexus 10: Revisited! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-26)

## Summary
A five-months-later revisit of the Nexus 10, his own most-used tablet. Persona value is in
a stated design principle — **great hardware "disappears" and leaves you interacting with
pure software** — plus several concrete taste data (front-facing speakers, the
polycarbonate back's grip, "I'm a pixel person") and his personal tablet app-stack.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-26] **The best-hardware-disappears principle**: "the hardware has pretty much
  disappeared and left only the software for me to focus on, and this is a good thing… the
  screen, build, materials, and design just kind of disappear into your hands and you're
  left interacting with pure Android." His idea that great industrial design becomes
  invisible in use (a durable design value).
- [2013-03-26] **"Since I'm a pixel person" — the self-label, restated**: picks the Nexus 10
  partly because "it's the highest-resolution tablet on the market, and since I'm a pixel
  person that's really what got me." The display-primacy self-identification (cf. the "I
  love pixels" iPad-3 line).
- [2013-03-26] **Front-facing speakers as an under-discussed win**: "I don't think I could
  go back to rear-firing speakers on a tablet again — front-facing just makes sense… I
  wouldn't be surprised to see more phones take this on now that the HTC One proved it makes
  sense for smartphones too." Values a detail most reviews skip, with a forward prediction.
- [2013-03-26] **Back material matters more than people think**: "the Nexus 10 back is my
  favorite material on the back of any mobile device — polycarbonate, a soft-touch feel
  that's a little grippy, so you can hold the weight in one hand… part of why the iPad can
  feel awkward is the smooth metal plates on the back." A specific ergonomics/materials
  judgment.
- [2013-03-26] **Request-driven framing**: "I get asked almost all the time like clockwork
  what tablet I use the most." The audience-programmed content pattern again.
- [2013-03-26] **His personal Nexus 10 setup (dated)**: Google Keep + Drive + Calendar
  widgets, Falcon Pro (Twitter), Feedly (post-Google-Reader), Google+ heavy, Pocket, Dolphin
  Browser ("performs better on the Nexus 10 than Chrome"), Reddit News, "Eye in the Sky"
  weather. Media-consumption-only ("videos and news"), a tight, minimal layout. Biography/
  tooling detail.
- [2013-03-26] **A Google-slacking gripe — accessories**: "Google has been slacking on
  Nexus accessories… the Nexus 10 even has magnets inside to sleep/wake the display, it's
  begging for a smart-cover accessory, and 5 months after release it's still MIA. Come on,
  Google." Criticizes a favorite maker (even-handed).
