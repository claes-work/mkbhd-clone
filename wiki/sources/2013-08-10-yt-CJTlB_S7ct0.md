# Motorola Moto X Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-08-10)

## Summary
The full Moto X review — the practical test of his "geeks vs consumers" framework. Gives
the mid-spec phone a fair shot "from a geek's perspective" and comes away impressed by the
**experience** (buttery-smooth stock-ish Android, X8 chip, touchless control, Active
Display) — while naming the **camera as "the biggest disappointment"** (one-critical-flaw).
Recommends it heavily to iPhone/first-time-Android switchers.

## Key claims
- [2013-08-10] **Experience over specs — the framework, lived**: "a geek's perspective, actually
  giving the phone a chance… it might not benchmark like a boss, but it feels and performs like
  one every day. This and the new Nexus 7 were the only devices that really glide through Chrome
  on any site." The good-real-world-performance-from-modest-specs verdict (720p AMOLED + X8 chip
  as a deliberate, good trade).
- [2013-08-10] **Resolution honesty from a self-described pixel-lover**: "take it from someone
  who absolutely loves resolution — the only time I see a difference between this 720p and a
  1080p display is side by side, and the average person never does that. A good trade-off." His
  perception-over-spec honesty, applied against his own bias.
- [2013-08-10] **One-critical-flaw — the camera**: "Motorola has never been good with cameras…
  it is not a good camera, the biggest disappointment of the entire phone — muted colors,
  consistently soft (I checked the lens plastic so many times)." Names the single dealbreaker;
  notes it's firmware-fixable.
- [2013-08-10] **Customization / make-it-your-own as the differentiator**: "for the first time
  you can really make this device your own — pick the back color, front, trim — and get it in 4
  days." His customization value again (Moto Maker).
- [2013-08-10] **The switcher recommendation — geeks-vs-consumers, restated**: "even if it's not
  appealing to us geeks / pixel junkies who love the Note's big display, I'll recommend this to a
  lot of people coming from iPhones or as first-time Android users — it's stock, easy to get used
  to, and it'll sell better than a lot of devices we've seen." Separates his own taste from the
  mass-market fit.
- [2013-08-10] **Honest about unfinished software + a muffleable speaker**: flags Wi-Fi
  notification-bar glitches and inconsistent nav-bar transparency as "rushed, but fixable with
  updates." Even-handed nitpicks inside a positive review.
