# Beyerdynamic Custom One Pro Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-04-05)

## Summary
A headphone review that marks a **gear transition**: the Beyerdynamic Custom One Pro
displaces the ATH-M50 as his daily editing/listening headphones — a "big deal" for him
given how long he'd hailed the M50s. Notable for the disclosed red-and-black bias, a
**data-driven** demonstration (a frequency-response graph for the bass port), and a peer
credit to TLDToday.

## Key claims
- [2013-04-05] **A new daily driver, displacing the M50 — disclosed with self-awareness**:
  "these just became my daily driver over the M50s, which is kind of a big deal for me — for
  a long time I've hailed the ATH-M50s as my favorite under $200… I've switched over to these
  as my full-time editing and music-listening headphones and I don't regret it." Names the
  significance of changing a long-held favorite.
- [2013-04-05] **Discloses the red-and-black bias up front — but argues past it**: "they're
  my favorites for a lot more than the fact that they have red and black on them… I'm not just
  saying this because they have a little red and black." Flags his own bias, then justifies on
  other grounds (the honesty-about-bias habit).
- [2013-04-05] **Peer credit — TLDToday**: "you can thank TLDToday for introducing me to these
  cans." Credits John/Jon Rettinger (TLDToday) again — the peer network (context).
- [2013-04-05] **Data-driven claim-checking — the bass port**: "a lot of people will cry this
  is BS… does it actually make a difference? Yes it does, and not only can I hear it — here is
  a graph of the frequency response with the different positions… halfway-open is where I keep
  it, approximately the most balanced sound, close to the M50s." Backs a subjective claim with
  a measurement (his standing data-driven habit).
- [2013-04-05] **Values a value-adding feature — removable cable / mic**: praises the removable
  cable (replaceable if broken; optional inline mic makes them gaming/Skype-capable) — "a
  value-adding feature" even though "I wouldn't use it for that." Distinguishes real added
  value from gimmick.
- [2013-04-05] **Honest caveats — burn-in and portability**: "these do have to be burned in a
  little bit; they didn't sound fantastic out of the box… they don't fold like the M50s, so
  they're not really portable." Flags the downsides of his new pick.
