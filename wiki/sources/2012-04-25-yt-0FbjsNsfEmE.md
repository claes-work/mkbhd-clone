# Google Drive vs Dropbox! (@mkbhd, 2012-04-25)

## Summary
A head-to-head between newly-launched Google Drive and incumbent Dropbox, covering desktop apps, web
apps, Android apps and pricing. Two things lift it above a feature comparison: he documents **his own
cloud-first backup workflow** ("almost my entire existence is in the cloud"), and he ends on a
**provisional, revisable verdict** — he's switching, but says he might switch back and will report if
he does.

## Key claims
- [2012-04-25] **His backup workflow, stated concretely**: "almost my entire existence is in the cloud —
  I back up videos to YouTube, I back up photos to Google+ and Picasa, I back up all sorts of other
  things like documents to Google Docs." A dated picture of how a creator with large media files
  actually stored them in 2012.
- [2012-04-25] **The explainer instinct, in one line**: cloud storage is "basically think of it as your
  flash drive in the cloud — that's how I explain it to, like, old people."
- [2012-04-25] **The decisive advantage he identifies is search, not storage**: "the biggest, biggest
  advantage of Google Drive is the web app search functionality… you can search for words that are
  *inside* of a presentation or a document." Content-search over file-name search.
- [2012-04-25] **Pricing compared with the mechanism behind each model**: Dropbox gives 2 GB free plus
  **referral-earned** storage ("some people have tons of free storage on Dropbox just from referring
  people"); Google Drive starts at 5 GB — "which would be a lot of referrals in Dropbox" — with cheap
  paid tiers. He discloses his own purchase: "**I've already upgraded my account for the 25 gigabytes,
  which is $2.50 per month.**"
- [2012-04-25] **Credits the incumbent's maturity honestly**: the Dropbox Android app "has been developed
  for a while… it's a very very well put together functional application and it's really really good,"
  while Google's is "version 1.0" and only "decent — it's not phenomenally smooth or anything."
- [2012-04-25] **A joke that is also a real prediction**: "we know Google never stays at version 1.0 —
  unless it's Google Wave."
- [2012-04-25] **Gaps named without excusing them**: no Linux client for Google Drive ("why? Hopefully
  developers are on that at Google — get on that"), and no iOS app for either at launch.
- [2012-04-25] **The verdict is scoped by ecosystem, and marked provisional**: "if you're a Google person
  or even an Android person like me, you're probably definitely going to want to give Google Drive a
  shot because it integrates so well… **I'm giving Drive a shot, I think I'm going to stick with Google
  Drive right now — and I'll keep you guys updated if that changes.** … because [I] might switch back to
  Dropbox."
