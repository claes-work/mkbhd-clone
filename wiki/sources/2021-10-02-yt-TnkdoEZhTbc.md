# iPhone 13 Pro Review: Better Than You Think! (@mkbhd, 2021-10-02)

## Summary
A solo full review of the iPhone 13 Pro / 13 Pro Max, structured around Marques's argument
that this "minor upgrade disguised in the same design" is actually a bigger leap than it
looks — concentrated in the same three areas that improved from the 12 to the 13 (screen,
battery, cameras) but larger in each. Deep dive on ProMotion: the LTPO adaptive display
ramps 10–120 Hz across 12 refresh rates; Marques's long-standing thesis is that most
first-time high-refresh users won't consciously notice it at first, but won't want to go
back to 60 Hz after weeks. Battery is called out as the standout — compounding gains from
sub-60 Hz dips, a more efficient A15, and physically larger (thicker/heavier) batteries
(~11% bigger on the Pro, ~18% on the Pro Max). Cameras: bigger sensors across all three
lenses, 3x telephoto, sensor-shift stabilization, macro (with a criticized forced
auto-switch), photographic styles (he prefers Rich Contrast), and the still-missing
ProRes video / manual-video story. Ends with a wishlist (USB-C, real fast charging,
higher-res cameras) and a Skillshare sponsor read that doubles as a mini production-craft
philosophy: incremental improvement is how you get great. Solo video — all persona/voice
data.

## Key claims
- [2021-10-02] Only three things are genuinely better on the 13 Pro vs. the regular 13 —
  screen, battery, cameras — "the same list of three things" that made the 13 better than
  the 12, but a bigger leap in all of them; "a pretty big upgrade disguised in the same
  design as last year."
- [2021-10-02] ProMotion done well is more than dropping in a 120 Hz panel: the LTPO OLED
  adapts across 12 refresh rates from 10 Hz to 120 Hz (seven more than the iPad Pro),
  matching content (24 fps video, 120 Hz gaming, 10 Hz static reading) to save battery.
- [2021-10-02] The recurring MKBHD refresh-rate thesis: not everyone notices ProMotion right
  away, "especially if they're not looking for it," but anyone used to high refresh who is
  handed back a 60 Hz phone "will notice that difference and they won't want to switch back."
- [2021-10-02] He hopes Apple doesn't ship another 60 Hz iPhone next year — "like 90 Hz at
  least."
- [2021-10-02] Battery is the year's biggest surprise; the 13 Pro battery is ~11% larger than
  the 12 Pro and the 13 Pro Max ~18% larger than the 12 Pro Max; phones are ~20 g heavier. He
  dailies the 13 Pro at 6–7 hrs screen-on-time (an "A"); David tested the Pro Max to 5 hrs SOT
  with 50% left. Puts the 13 Pro Max at the top of his 2021 battery power rankings.
- [2021-10-02] Cameras: bigger sensors on all three lenses; the big-sensor "natural bokeh"
  now on the main camera makes background blur easy and beats fake portrait-mode blur "every
  time"; 3x optical telephoto (up from 2x/2.5x) nearly matches the main sensor. He prefers
  the **Rich Contrast** photographic style.
- [2021-10-02] Criticism: at ~14 cm the phone auto-switches to the ultra-wide macro without
  telling you or letting you switch back — "the most annoying part of using this camera";
  Apple told him a manual toggle is coming in a software update.
- [2021-10-02] Still missing/late: no astrophotography mode, no built-in Pro/manual video,
  and **ProRes video not yet in the phones** (coming via software update; 4K30 ProRes needs
  ≥256 GB — the 128 GB base caps at 1080p30). Notes ProRes files are ~6 GB/min and offloading
  over Lightning is "so brutal," a segue to his USB-C complaint.
- [2021-10-02] $1,000 Pro wishlist: USB-C ("probably never going to happen but I can still
  dream"), real 50–65 W+ fast charging, higher-resolution cameras, an all-black option.
  Notes the new Sierra Blue is paler in person than in the press shots.
- [2021-10-02] Production-craft philosophy (sponsor segue): "every iPhone upgrade from here
  to the end of time… is going to be a minor upgrade," and that's fine — "that's how you
  eventually get great at something, by getting a little bit better every time you do it,"
  citing his own self-taught path to better videos. Names teammates David (battery testing)
  and Michael (animations).
