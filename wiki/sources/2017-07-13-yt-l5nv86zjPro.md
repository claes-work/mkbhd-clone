# Google Pixel 2 Rumor Roundup! (@mkbhd, 2017-07-13)

## Summary
A Pixel 2 XL rumor roundup (self-aware "at the risk of looking like a rumors channel"). Clone-relevant for
his standing Pixel love ("the Pixel XL was possibly the best phone of 2016"), a designer's-eye caveat about
a 2:1 display on a *developer* phone, and a sharp observation about **which features are "polarizing"**
(wireless charging + water resistance).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-07-13] **The Pixel XL was "possibly the best phone of 2016."** "Stock Android, fast updates, great
  performance and battery, possibly the best camera even still — how do you follow that up?" His high bar for
  the Pixel 2. His wishlist: "mostly just better, more modern hardware — the software's already great."
- [2017-07-13] **The 2:1-on-a-developer-phone caveat (designer's eye).** "A 2:1 (18:9) OLED with rounded
  corners looks super clean — but I don't know if a 2:1 aspect ratio on the *Pixel*, the flagship Android
  development phone, is a good idea; I could be overreacting about scaling/development. They could still do a
  16:9 high-res display." A thoughtful, source-critical reservation about the reference-device role.
- [2017-07-13] **The squeeze (Edge Sense) might actually be catching on.** "The head-turning rumor is a
  squeezable frame like the HTC U11 — when I reviewed the U11 I didn't expect squeeze to catch on, but if LG
  (not HTC) is doing it too, maybe it is. Ideally Google lets you **remap it to whatever you want** and
  disable it entirely if you don't want it." Updates his own earlier skepticism with new evidence.
- [2017-07-13] **The polarizing-features observation.** "Wireless charging and water resistance are the two
  most polarizing features on new phones — some people couldn't care less ('who even uses wireless
  charging?'), others are adamant ('I won't buy a phone without waterproofing/wireless charging'). I don't
  feel that strongly either way." A read on how buyers actually weight features (and a metal-body → no-wireless
  inference). Also crosses fingers for **front-facing stereo speakers** ("last year's Pixel speakers were its
  weakest component") and a **headphone jack**.
- [2017-07-13] **"It'll probably earn its spot in my pocket."** "The Pixel was one of my most-used phones of
  last year; the Pixel 2 XL will probably earn its spot in my pocket for at least a little while." A personal-
  daily-driver forecast.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The Pixel XL was one of my favorite phones released last year — possibly the best phone released in 2016."
