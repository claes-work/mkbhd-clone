# Motorola Moto G Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-12-08)

## Summary
A budget-phone landmark — the $179 Moto G, which he calls a "ridiculously good deal." A
concentrated dose of his **grade-on-the-price-tier** value doctrine, with a memorable
framing device: **the "highest-end low-end device"** (vs "lowest-end high-end device"). He
explains it by comparing every cut against the Moto X, and reasons about the camera against
a same-price point-and-shoot.

## Key claims
- [2013-12-08] **Why he breaks his no-price-in-the-review rule**: "I don't tend to talk about
  price in the review itself — prices fluctuate. But this is not one of those devices: $179
  off-contract is a ridiculously good deal and always will be." Notes his usual methodology and
  the exception.
- [2013-12-08] **"Highest-end low-end device" — the framing**: "consider the question: is this
  the highest-end low-end device or the lowest-end high-end device? If you're looking for a
  high-end device, this is not it. I see it as the highest-end low-end device — an incredible
  value that gets way better performance than any other phone in this price bracket." A
  memorable value-position framework.
- [2013-12-08] **Grade-on-the-price-tier, applied**: explains every cut ("to get a phone this
  cheap you cut every corner possible") — minimal packaging, no wall adapter, a Snapdragon 400 +
  1 GB RAM, a 720p LCD, no NFC/LTE/5 GHz Wi-Fi — while crediting the surprises ("a quad-core chip
  at this price is ridiculous; the battery lasts a full day"). Value judged on its own tier.
- [2013-12-08] **The camera reasoned against a same-price point-and-shoot**: "if you care about
  photo quality and have $179, you could buy a decent $179 point-and-shoot that might not take
  much better photos than the Moto G. I'm impressed there's even a camera at all — the first
  Nexus 7 dropped the camera entirely to hit $199." A reframe-the-comparison move.
- [2013-12-08] **Performance honesty on a curve**: "if the Moto X's responsiveness is a perfect
  10, the Moto G is about a 7 — you can tell you're working with 1 GB of RAM, things take a bit
  longer. But when you consider the huge price difference, that's something you've got to love."
  Honest, tier-adjusted.
- [2013-12-08] **The verdict + industry access**: "for $179 you cannot buy a better smartphone,
  period." Notes his **interview with Motorola CEO Dennis Woodside** (who promised KitKat by
  January) — an industry-access datum.
