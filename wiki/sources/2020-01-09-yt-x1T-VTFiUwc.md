# Dope Tech of CES 2020: Sony Made a Car?! (@mkbhd, 2020-01-09)

## Summary
Marques's "Dope Tech" roundup of the best gadgets he saw walking the CES 2020 show floor. His headline pick is the Sony Vision-S — a surprise one-off prototype electric car built as a showcase of Sony's automotive-relevant tech (camera sensors, radar/lidar, in-cabin displays/UI), assembled with help from supplier Magna — which he rates better-looking than almost any car on the road. He then covers Samsung's confusing "light flagship" phones (Galaxy S10 Lite and Note 10 Lite), the OnePlus Concept One with electrochromic color-shifting glass, two rotating TVs (TCL and the Samsung Sero), Bosch's face-tracking Virtual Visor, two real EVs he's excited about (Ford Mustang Mach-E and Rivian R1T), the Omron Forpheus ping-pong robot, Razer's tiny Tomahawk PC and suspended racing simulator, and a near-bezel-less Samsung TV. He closes with takeaways: 4K is still rising while 8K is the new stretch, electric cars are coming fast, and "you can put Alexa in pretty much anything."

## Key claims
- [2020-01-09] Marques's favorite thing at CES 2020 and the biggest surprise "no one saw coming" was the Sony car; he thinks it looks better than almost every other electric car and better than a lot of cars on the streets today. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] The Sony car is a non-shippable one-off prototype demonstrating the technologies Sony already makes for the automotive business — camera sensors (rear-facing cameras replacing rearview mirrors), radar and lidar for self-driving, and a large interior display/UI — with the rest of the car (brakes, tires, etc.) built with help from supplier Magna. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Sony car specs, as Marques was told at the booth: quad motors, 0–60 mph in 4.6 seconds, top speed 149 mph — specs he says don't really matter because you can't buy one. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Samsung showed the Galaxy Note 10 Lite and Galaxy S10 Lite; Samsung reps told Marques these are not flagships but "light flagships." (Marques, relaying booth reps)
- [2020-01-09] Both Lite phones have 6.7-inch 1080p flat OLED displays with a top-middle punch-hole selfie camera, 4500 mAh batteries, no wireless charging, and the rectangular rear camera bump expected on the upcoming flagships; the S10 Lite runs a Snapdragon 855 and the Note 10 Lite a similar Exynos 9810. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Camera differences: Note 10 Lite has three 12 MP cameras (wide, 2x telephoto, ultrawide) plus the stylus and a headphone jack but is the only one not coming to the US; S10 Lite has a 48 MP main with "super steady" video, a 12 MP ultrawide, and a 5 MP macro. Both slot in around the $650 range to tide buyers over until the Galaxy S20 and Galaxy Fold 2. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] OnePlus showed the OnePlus Concept One, whose electrochromic tinting glass on the back can hide the camera bump and act as an ND (neutral density) filter for photos and video; OnePlus also built a piano out of OnePlus 7 Pro phones. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Two rotating TVs were on the floor — one from TCL with rounded corners and the Samsung Sero — pitched to rotate between portrait and landscape to frame smartphone content without black bars; Marques quips we "could just stop shooting so much vertical video." (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Bosch showed an LED-activated sun visor (a clear panel plus a face-tracking camera) that blocks the sun from the driver's eyes while leaving the road and traffic lights visible; it worked "not perfectly" but kept his face and eyes shaded. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Two real EVs impressed him as likely future autofocus episodes: the Ford Mustang Mach-E electric SUV (Honda CR-V-ish crossover size, looked more complete in person, scheduled for 2021) and the Rivian R1T pickup (scheduled for 2020, with Rivian aiming to hit the road by summer and deliver to buyers by winter). (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] The Omron Forpheus ping-pong robot returned as a tech showcase (not for sale) and now tracks the player's emotions via cameras — reading smiling vs. frowning to sense frustration and adapt — displayed live during play. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Razer showed the Tomahawk mini PC — using an Intel NUC compute unit in a special case/motherboard setup, roughly the size of a large external GPU enclosure — and a fully suspended racing simulator with realistic pedals and dual projectors, whose most immersive part was the seatbelt pulling against his chest under braking. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] Samsung showed a TV with essentially no bezels — near 100% screen-to-body ratio — though Marques isn't sure it will ship. (Marques)
- [2020-01-09] CES 2020 takeaways: 4K is still on the rise and not everywhere yet while 8K is the new stretch; electric cars are coming quickly; and "you can put Alexa in pretty much anything." (Marques)
