# Samsung Galaxy S5 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-02-25)

## Summary
Hands-on first impressions of the Galaxy S5 (about an hour with it). Reinforcing — the
verdict is "the **Galaxy S4S**" (incremental/conservative, validating his what-to-expect
call), with an honest acknowledgment that his fingerprint-scanner prediction was **wrong**
(the S5 has one). Notable for the heart-rate-monitor "good ad" incentive read.

## Key claims
- [2014-02-25] **"The Galaxy S4S" — the incremental verdict**: "this really feels like a Galaxy
  S4S — a ton of minor conservative improvements, a weird design tweak. It's so easy to call it a
  Galaxy S4 part two. Not that that's a bad thing; people were just expecting bigger changes." His
  incremental-not-revolutionary read (validating the what-to-expect prediction).
- [2014-02-25] **His fingerprint-scanner prediction was wrong — acknowledged**: "in a very Apple-
  inspired move, Samsung put a fingerprint sensor in the home button." (He'd given this ~5% in the
  what-to-expect video — a logged miss he doesn't hide.) His says-when-wrong honesty.
- [2014-02-25] **Design skepticism — the dimpled "band-aid" back**: "they added this unsightly
  bumpiness, like dimples — reminds me of the original Nexus 7 plus a golf-ball hybrid. The gold I
  call the band-aid edition. I can't say I'm a fan right now." Honest, specific taste critique.
- [2014-02-25] **The heart-rate-monitor "good ad" incentive read**: "the heart-rate monitor — you
  hold your finger to the back and it reads your heart rate [via S Health]. Not sure how many people
  will use it, but that's Samsung: it makes a good ad, a good demo, good to show off to friends, so
  they built it in." His demoable-≠-useful / value-via-software read.
- [2014-02-25] **Even-handed on the incremental improvements**: credits the IP67 dust/water
  resistance despite the removable back, the fast-autofocus 16 MP camera + 4K video, and a flatter/
  toned-down TouchWiz — "performance feels smoother, not faster." Balanced.
- [2014-02-25] **Verdict-discipline**: "I couldn't really pixel-peep the image quality in the time
  I had — we'll take a much deeper look in the full review." Holds the verdict for hands-on testing.
