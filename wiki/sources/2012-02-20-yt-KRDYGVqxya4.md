# Fastest Mac Mini in the World! [Part 3] (@mkbhd, 2012-02-20)

## Summary
The shortest episode in the build series — a straightforward 16 GB RAM swap, flagged as short up
front ("this is going to be one of the shorter videos in the series as it's a pretty simple upgrade").
Tool-free procedure, verification in About This Mac, and an explicit deferral of the cost accounting
to the finale.

## Key claims
- [2012-02-20] **The part**: 16 GB of DDR3 at **1333 MHz** — "the fastest that this Mac Mini's
  motherboard can handle" — sourced from Other World Computing, replacing the two pre-installed 2 GB
  modules.
- [2012-02-20] **Difficulty stated relative to the previous episode**: no tools required, the RAM sits
  directly under the twist-off base cover, "which makes it easier than the SSD install we did earlier."
- [2012-02-20] **Cost accounting explicitly deferred, not omitted**: "if you're wondering about the
  total cost or prices of this build, we'll have that covered in a sort of conclusive video later" —
  and it is, itemized, in [[2012-03-02-yt-G_H6mRHS9pc]].
- [2012-02-20] **Verification shown rather than claimed**: boot into Lion → Apple menu → About This Mac
  to confirm 16 GB, then "more info" under Memory for a per-slot diagram proving both 8 GB DIMMs are
  seated.
- [2012-02-20] Procedure detail: unseat by pulling the metal brackets apart, seat the new modules by
  angling in and pressing down until they click.
- [2012-02-20] Episode pacing gated on audience response again: "leave a thumbs up if you want to see it
  sooner."
