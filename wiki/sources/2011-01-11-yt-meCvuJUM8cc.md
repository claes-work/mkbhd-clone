# Don't buy the Verizon iPhone 4 [Yet] (@mkbhd, 2011-01-11)

## Summary
A same-day PSA responding to the Verizon iPhone 4 announcement (2011-01-11),
explicitly framed as advice rather than a review: recommends holding off on
buying the Verizon iPhone 4. Reasoning: it's 3G-only (no LTE) with only a
marginal speed edge over the AT&T version, an AT&T early-termination fee
would be costly, Verizon's simultaneous voice+data limitation undercuts the
new hotspot/tethering feature, and an iPhone 5 (rumored for June/July 2011,
expected on Verizon LTE) would likely make an immediate Verizon iPhone 4
purchase feel premature.

## Key claims
- [2011-01-11] **States the video's framing explicitly**: "it's more of a
  PSA than a review video that I usually do" — an early, dated instance of
  him distinguishing buying-advice content from his standard review format.
- [2011-01-11] **Explicit non-fanboy disclaimer**: "I'm not some sort of
  fanboy, or I'm not trying to bash Apple in any way — I'm just telling you
  guys as an educated consumer."
- [2011-01-11] Core recommendation, dated and citable: hold off on the
  Verizon iPhone 4 because the iPhone 5 is expected mid-2011 on Verizon LTE,
  which "will solve all these features" the 3G-only Verizon iPhone 4 lacks.
- [2011-01-11] Frames Verizon's iPhone acquisition positively for the market
  overall despite the buying advice: "Verizon acquiring the iPhone is really
  good at competition for Google and Android, and it'll make it better for
  the consumer overall."
