# iPhone 11 Review: Too Easy! (@mkbhd, 2019-09-30)

## Summary
Marques frames the iPhone 11 as the easiest review he has had to make: it is a small, obvious set of upgrades over the iPhone XR wrapped in a familiar body, sold at a lower price, and therefore an easy phone to recommend as "the new iPhone that most people should buy." He walks through what carries over unchanged from the XR/XS lineage (same shape, notch, size, aluminum rails, Lightning port, LCD display, IP68 water resistance) and isolates what is genuinely new: the name (dropping the Roman numeral), six more pastel color options, a two-finish milled glass back, a slightly longer battery life on the more efficient A13 Bionic, a modest price drop, and — the headline — the new dual-camera system that adds an ultra-wide lens. He praises the cameras as A-plus 2019 smartphone shooters (deferring detailed image-quality analysis to his iPhone 11 Pro review) while naming the low-resolution LCD display as the phone's biggest weakness. He closes with a buyer's-guide framing: decide based on how much you care about screen quality and phone size, and consider the still-cheaper XR and iPhone 8 if budget or the second camera don't matter.

## Key claims
- [2019-09-30] The iPhone 11 is a solid improvement over the iPhone XR and, being cheaper than the Pros, is the new iPhone that most people should buy.
- [2019-09-30] The design is essentially unchanged from the XR/XS/X: same shape, same notch, same size as the XR, same mute switch, speakers, aluminum rails, Lightning port, LCD display (1792x828 resolution), and same IP68 water resistance.
- [2019-09-30] The 1792x828 LCD resolution means users can't watch true HD YouTube video on a $700 phone, and the display is the phone's biggest weakness — though many people won't notice.
- [2019-09-30] Apple dropped the Roman numeral, so "iPhone 11" is now the de-facto standard name.
- [2019-09-30] The iPhone 11 comes in six colors (black, green, yellow, purple, red, white), all more pastel; Marques personally preferred the XR's color choices, especially the blue and the unique coral, which are now gone.
- [2019-09-30] The glass back is milled from one piece of glass into two finishes — glossy across the back with the now-centered Apple logo, and a matte camera square (the opposite arrangement of the 11 Pro).
- [2019-09-30] Size, thickness, and weight are identical to the XR: 8.3mm thick and 194 grams per Apple.
- [2019-09-30] Battery life is rated one hour longer than the XR by Apple; Marques confirms it is at least equivalent to the XR, which already had some of the best iPhone battery life in a long time, aided by the more efficient A13 Bionic.
- [2019-09-30] The cameras are the biggest new thing: a new main camera plus an added ultra-wide, giving dual cameras with features like switching lenses while recording, 4K video from both, portrait mode that now works on animals and objects (not just faces), and a new wider front camera that shoots slow-motion selfies.
- [2019-09-30] The only camera omission versus the 11 Pro is the third telephoto lens, which Marques does not consider a big deal; both included cameras are A-plus 2019 smartphone cameras.
- [2019-09-30] This is the best camera in a $700 smartphone and arguably the best in any smartphone, though Marques still calls it a toss-up with the Pixel 3 (noting the 11 has an ultra-wide the Pixel 3 lacks, and the Pixel 4 is imminent).
- [2019-09-30] The iPhone 11 starts at $700 for 64GB, fitting a price gradient alongside the retained cheaper XR and iPhone 8.
- [2019-09-30] The YouTube app "lies" by letting users pick 1080p on a display that is just over 720p.
- [2019-09-30] Buyers should ask two questions: how much they care about screen quality (if a lot, pick an OLED iPhone — XS or 11 Pro) and how they feel about the size (the 11 Pro is more compact, the 11 Pro Max bigger).
- [2019-09-30] The XR at $600 is more competitive for the small group who don't care about the second camera or the camera upgrade.
- [2019-09-30] The 2020 iPhone is on the horizon, but it is too early to get excited and still a year away.
