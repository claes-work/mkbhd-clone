# Mercedes CLA EV is a Great Fighter in a Dying Sport (@AutoFocus, 2026-04-13)

## Summary
A week-long review of the **Mercedes CLA 350 EV**, an electric sedan Marques calls
"underrated" and a rare option in a shrinking segment. He walks his standard car-review
arc — exterior, front trunk, interior materials, screens/software, backseat measured
against his "6'3 driving position," then the drive (suspension, quietness, range,
charging, regen). Verdict: a genuinely solid, well-equipped, luxurious-feeling EV with a
few Mercedes-should-know-better nitpicks (no next-track button on the wheel, a fiddly
manual charge-port door, quirky window switches, cramped-ish rear seat, no one-pedal
driving). Marques-solo throughout; all content is subject-attributed.

## Key claims
- 2026-04-13: The market for a **new electric sedan under $70k is very limited** — Model 3,
  then a big gap; Lucid Air is far pricier, Model S discontinued, BMW i4 about to be
  discontinued, leaving the Polestar 2 and this CLA. (Marques)
- CLA 350 specs as reviewed: fully electric sedan, dual-motor AWD, **312-mile range**,
  ~350 hp, 0–60 in ~4.8s, starts at **$50,000** (~$65k as tested). (Marques)
- Praises the interior materials and equipment for the price (knurled controls, wood inlay,
  Burmester speakers ~$800 "totally worth it," electrochromic sunroof that tints in
  sections, front trunk large enough for a carry-on). (Marques)
- Recurring criticisms tied to his review method: (a) **no next/previous-track control on
  the steering wheel** — "you gotta be able to just go to the next song on the steering
  wheel"; (b) window switches behaved erratically once while driving; (c) the second
  charge-port door "doesn't close" from the touchscreen — you must get out; (d) B-minus
  backseat (comfortable materials, high floor/knee, not great for road trips). (Marques)
- Drive: very smooth, light steering, soft suspension that "glides over the road" and
  absorbs New Jersey potholes; very quiet without double-glazed glass or active noise
  cancellation; honest ~300 miles of range; **wishes it had full one-pedal driving**
  (only paddle-adjustable regen). Notes a two-speed rear gearbox you can feel shift around
  60–70 mph. (Marques)
- Verdict: yes, worth considering at this price — soft, luxurious, well-equipped; "a really
  solid underrated EV." Hopes it isn't discontinued like the i4/Model S. (Marques)
