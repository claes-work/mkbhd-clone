# I Tried a Disney Secret Project! (@mkbhd, 2024-04-26)

## Summary
A "Dope Tech"-style exclusive: Marques visits **Disney Imagineering's labs** and becomes the
**first person outside Disney to walk on the HoloTile** — a one-of-one 360° omnidirectional
treadmill ("moving floor") built from thousands of tiny tilted spinning discs that always
recenter you no matter which direction you walk. He explains the mechanism, tries all three
control modes (hand/"the force," a PlayStation controller from a chair, and walking it up
through a ~2-minute training ramp), and tests it with an **Apple Vision Pro** on. His most
substantive insight is the **VR motion-sickness problem**: the disagreement between what your
eyes see (moving forward) and what your inner ear feels (walking in place) is what makes VR
locomotion hard — the HoloTile is a candidate solution but still very hard to trust. Solo,
Marques-fronted → persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2024-04-26] Disney greeted him with the same walking **droids** roaming the parks — he notes
  they share "a striking amount of similarities to the **Boston Dynamics Spot** robot" he played
  with years earlier (adaptive gait, easy custom controller — here a customized Steam Deck).
- [2024-04-26] **HoloTile mechanism**: instead of one flat plane, the floor is a dense array of
  articulating cones topped with **tilted spinning discs** (only the raised edge touches your
  foot), each with its own motor — "less of a flat treadmill and more of a bunch of small blades
  rotating under your feet." They spin to always pull your foot back to center, any direction,
  any stride.
- [2024-04-26] **Three control modes**: (1) point with your hands to move an object on the floor
  ("using the force" — Star Wars theme-park potential), (2) a PS5 controller from a chair (left
  stick = position, right = rotation; fast enough to fling yourself off the edge), (3) walking,
  ramped up over ~2 minutes of training mode to 100% real-time.
- [2024-04-26] **Observations walking it**: it's **loud** (a clear thing to fix for a theme
  park/home), and you never walk a perfectly straight line — you glide over ridges unless your
  foot lands along an aligned row. Imagines a future version with far more, much smaller discs
  ("an Imagineering challenge").
- [2024-04-26] **The VR insight** (the durable idea): motion sickness comes from the mismatch
  between visual motion and vestibular stillness — same as looking at your phone in a moving car.
  With a Vision Pro on the HoloTile, "my eyes see the movement, and my legs are trying to walk
  forward… but my brain thinks I'm not supposed to be moving forward. So, it's confusing" — he
  needed a harness and took baby steps.
- [2024-04-26] Credits inventor **Lanny Smoot** — 20+ years at Disney, 70+ patents (incl. how BB-8
  rolls around the parks and the real-life retracting lightsabers), "kind of a legend" — who
  walks the HoloTile in VR confidently. Frames free-form VR exploration "in the comfort of your
  own home" as "the type of thing that feels the most like the future."
