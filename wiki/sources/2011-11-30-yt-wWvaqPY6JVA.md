# What is a Fisheye Lens? (@mkbhd, 2011-11-30)

## Summary
An explainer-review of the Canon 8–15mm f/4L fisheye, structured as a **teaching video
first and a review second** — it opens with a definition and an offer to let you leave
early ("if you want to skip this video entirely, it is an ultra wide angle lens that can
cover a landscape view of up to 180°"). The heart of it is a live demonstration filmed on
two bodies: the crop-sensor T2i he normally shoots with, then a full-frame 5D Mark II
placed in the identical spot, so the viewer sees the crop factor's effect rather than
being told about it. Both bodies came from BorrowLenses, disclosed as before.

## Key claims
- [2011-11-30] **Crop factor demonstrated, not asserted**: on the T2i the 8–15mm behaves like
  a 12–24mm (×1.6), so "to fully appreciate this lens we have the Canon 5D Mark II here" —
  same position, same framing, swapped body. He notes both can be rented together and arrive
  in the same box.
- [2011-11-30] **Provenance disclosed again**: the lens retails around $1,500, "but you can
  have it for way less… courtesy of BorrowLenses.com." Third consecutive rented-unit
  disclosure in six weeks, now an established pattern rather than a one-off.
- [2011-11-30] **Verdict — a special-effects lens, not a general one**: "it's really somewhat
  of a special effects lens, if you ask me. I think that's what I'd call it." Recommended for
  landscapes and situations where you physically cannot back up (group shots indoors, city
  buildings), and pointedly *not* for portraits — "to take a picture of a person with this
  lens you literally have to get right up to the person's face."
- [2011-11-30] **Honest limitation stated up front**: the bubble distortion means straight
  lines curve and "it's never going to be a fully true representation of the image" — presented
  as the inherent cost of the coverage, not a defect.
- [2011-11-30] **Vlogging use-case identified**: at fisheye widths, a camera held one hand's
  distance from the face looks like it's held far away — "you can give the effect that you're
  holding it far away from yourself." A creator-specific application of an odd lens.
- [2011-11-30] Physical curiosity demonstrated: at 8 mm the coverage is wide enough to see the
  inside of the lens barrel in-frame, and removing the flower-petal hood yields a complete
  circular image.
- [2011-11-30] Audience-directed programming: he asks which lens or body to review next
  (5D Mark II? telephoto? wide angle?), and teases a YouTube Tips episode "that will involve
  more of you guys."
