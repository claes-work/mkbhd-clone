# MKBHD Update 9.0! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-01)

## Summary
A ★★ **biography/FAQ landmark** — his first numbered update in nearly a year (since 8.0,
Aug 2012), opening with a behind-the-scenes **workflow/style overhaul** and then answering
the most frequent inbox/Twitter questions. Confirms his upgraded camera (**Canon 5D Mark
III**), academic status (**junior**, business/technology), his editing rig, the
**no-single-daily-driver** practice, and states clearly **why he doesn't use iPhones** and
**why he only games racing sims**.

## Key claims
- [2013-07-01] **A deliberate workflow + style overhaul**: "the gap was filled with a
  cleaning/overhaul process — I felt my video-making was a little inefficient before, so I
  added tools for a smoother workflow… and studied what style I want, watching other
  excellent people's videos on YouTube and Vimeo, downloading effects/transitions to get an
  idea of what I want my videos to look like." His deliberate craft-improvement habit.
- [2013-07-01] **Camera upgrade — the Canon 5D Mark III**: "I've been using the Canon 5D Mark
  III for a little bit now, and it's become easily the favorite tool in my bag." Pins the
  camera timeline (up from the T2i/T3i era). Recommends the Canon ELPH 300HS for starting
  YouTubers.
- [2013-07-01] **Academic status — junior, and why the major**: "I'm a junior in college this
  upcoming year, 19 years old, studying business and technology, plus marketing and
  information systems… this is the first year I really get to pick courses to be specifically
  what I'm interested in." Dated academic datum (junior; cf. sophomore in April's Five Facts).
- [2013-07-01] **Editing rig confirmed**: "I still use the 30-inch Dell UltraSharp with the
  20-inch portrait beside it, flanked by the red-and-black iKey Audio M-505 V2 studio
  monitors, and yes, the Hackintosh Pro is still running the show as my editing machine. The
  new 2013 'trash can' Mac Pro will get a full fair shot when it comes." Workstation
  continuity + the fair-shot disposition.
- [2013-07-01] **Why he doesn't use iPhones — customization**: "I don't use iOS for the same
  reason I don't use Windows Phone — I don't find the same level of customization. I like to
  make my phone my own… you look at those home screens and they all look the same." (Notes he
  *did* try the iPhone 5 briefly — "bought TLDToday's iPhone" — and plans to give iOS 7 a fair
  shot.) His stated reason, plus the fair-shot commitment.
- [2013-07-01] **Why he only games racing sims — and owns no console**: "I rarely game… when
  I do it's racing/driving simulators — Need for Speed, Forza. I don't own any gaming consoles;
  I have a TV I've played games on, but it's connected to a computer, not a console." A dated
  personal-habit datum (corroborates the not-a-gamer thread).
- [2013-07-01] **If forced to pick ONE daily driver — Galaxy Note 2**: "I'd have to go with the
  Galaxy Note 2 — it's not 1080p and I do love pixels, but it's the only phone with LTE whose
  experience I enjoy that lasts longer than I need. Battery is the key factor." A dated
  single-phone pick, reasoned.
- [2013-07-01] **The Google-edition phone he'd want (nostalgia)**: "the Samsung Galaxy S2 — I
  used it on Verizon and loved it, one of my first positive OLED experiences, back when
  TouchWiz was really bad." A biography nostalgia datum.
- [2013-07-01] **Headphone picks restated**: best in-ear under $100 = Klipsch S4; best over-ear
  ~$100 = Grado SR80i. Consistent recommendations.
