# Inside Lucid Air: The Future of Luxury? (@mkbhd, 2017-03-09)

## Summary
A day with the (then-unreleased) Lucid Air and its team — an early, in-depth look at a luxury EV startup.
Clone-relevant for his **rooting-for-competition** value (he wants challengers to Tesla to succeed so
everything gets better), a sharp read that **Tesla's weak point is its interior luxury**, and a forward
thesis: the car of the future must be a great ride/self-driving/ride-sharing experience, not just a great
*driving* car.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-03-09] **"Luxury EVs are the future of luxury" + rooting for competition.** "If electric cars are
  the future, then luxury electric cars are the future of luxury… I'm definitely rooting for companies like
  this to succeed, to provide competition and make what we already have get better." His standing
  competition-raises-the-ceiling value, applied to EVs.
- [2017-03-09] **Names Tesla's real weak spot: interior luxury.** "One of the biggest complaints with Tesla
  is the interior doesn't feel luxury enough for some people — it was never a problem for me, but people
  coming from BMW/Mercedes/Porsche were disappointed and pointed at it as a flaw for the price. Lucid is
  attacking that 100%." Own-evidence honesty (his own view vs. the market's) while crediting the challenger's
  strategy.
- [2017-03-09] **The forward thesis: the future car is a *ride*, not just a drive.** "The fact that Lucid
  exists sheds light on what people will want from a car in the future — which doesn't just have to be a good
  *driving* car, but a good ride-sharing car and a good self-driving car." Reframes the car around passenger
  experience as autonomy approaches (the reclining jet-like back seats, the glass canopy — "when it's
  driving itself in a couple years, just looking around will be that much cooler").
- [2017-03-09] **Source-critical about pre-production demos.** Flags what *didn't* work in the prototype:
  "the groundbreaking motorized headlights weren't actually motorized in our prototype (made me sad)"; the
  electrochromic tinting ceiling "was still in development, couldn't show it working." Separates the promo
  vision from the shipped reality (cf. the FF91 show-car caution).
- [2017-03-09] **Reviews the interior like a product.** Praises the smartly-laid-out three-display driver
  cockpit ("the most beautiful charging indicator I've ever seen"), a good physical-vs-touchscreen balance,
  and the Model-X-like glass canopy (the source of the "Air" name); notes the tradeoff — the reclined seats
  cost "one of the smaller trunks I've seen in a large sedan" (a bench-seat version recovers it). 1,000 hp,
  "because electric cars can do that."
