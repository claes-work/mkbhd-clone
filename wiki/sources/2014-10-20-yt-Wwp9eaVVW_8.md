# Apple iPhone 6+ Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-20)

## Summary
The full iPhone 6 Plus review — **his personal iPhone of choice** — structured as a **buy-by-your-
priorities** benefits-vs-drawbacks guide for someone deciding between the 6 and 6 Plus. The standout is
the **OIS camera** (hardware, not software, stabilization → "a low-light beast" and "some of the best
1080p video from any smartphone"), plus a **battery champ** (2915 mAh). Drawbacks: app-scaling lag,
slipperiness (the one phone he'd actually cover — with a dbrand skin), and a calm BendGate coda. A clean
instance of OIS-hardware-beats-software and his pixel-peeper camera priorities.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-20] **Everything is magnified — pros and cons.** Bigger 5.5" 1080p display = bigger keyboard/
  media/gaming and full use of 1080p content, but still "just a blown-up iPhone 6 experience — I wish they
  did a bit more" (only an extra icon row, Reachability, and home-screen rotation, "which I think is
  silly").
- [2014-10-20] **OIS is the headline — hardware beats software stabilization.** The extra body space fits
  **optical image stabilization** ("the actual lens elements move to compensate for your shaky hand,"
  unlike software stabilization "that doesn't work nearly as well"): "a low-light beast" (shutter stays
  open longer without blur → "some of the best low-light shots I've ever seen from a phone with no flash")
  and "you can take a slightly shaky shot and make it seem like it's on a tripod." "Some of the best 1080p
  video and best photos from any smartphone, period. As a pixel peeper I love that."
- [2014-10-20] **Battery champ.** 2915 mAh → "I can basically guarantee you'll never kill the whole
  battery in a single day"; 7–9 hrs screen-on, often two days of light use. Only con: slower to charge.
- [2014-10-20] **Drawbacks:** (1) **app-optimization lag** (unupdated apps look fuzzy/wonky at 1080p);
  (2) **slipperiness** — "this is the one phone where I would really recommend covering it in some way,"
  his preference being a **dbrand skin** (grip without added thickness) over Apple's leather case; (3)
  **BendGate**, included only because everyone asks — "no, mine hasn't bent and I don't expect it to
  because I'm careful… don't sit on it, don't put a lot of pressure on it" (points to his explainer).
- [2014-10-20] **His pick, honestly framed.** He got both at once, took the 6 out first, then "immediately
  knew I liked the 6 Plus more" — "whether it's the feel in the hand or just being more used to devices
  this size… I'm one of billions, but I personally like the 6 Plus more." You decide if the benefits
  outweigh the drawbacks for you.
