# Blue Yeti Pro [Review] (@mkbhd, 2011-09-02)

## Summary
A hands-on review of the Blue Yeti Pro USB/XLR microphone, following an
earlier collab video with a "product feedback" channel. Walks the physical
design (large form factor, included stand, weighted base), the connection
options (USB with included cable, analog XLR with included cable, a
no-latency monitoring headphone jack), and the four selectable pickup
patterns (stereo, cardioid, bidirectional, omnidirectional) enabled by its
triple-condenser mic array, which records up to 24-bit/192kHz stereo. Plays
a live sample at medium gain in cardioid pattern and judges the quality
"really quite good," explicitly framing the high bit-rate/file-size
trade-off as worth it "since you're already investing" in this tier of mic.
Recommends it, contrasting it favorably against his prior daily driver, the
Electro-Voice RE20 ("the editor keys sl150" — likely an auto-caption garble
of a different mic name/model; flagged, not corrected without visual
confirmation).
