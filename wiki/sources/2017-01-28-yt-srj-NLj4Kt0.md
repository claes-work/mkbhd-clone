# iPhone 7 Review: 4 Months Later! (@mkbhd, 2017-01-28)

## Summary
A **long-term review** — a format he leans into ("four months, the honeymoon period is over… you really get
to know a phone best"). Verdict: a very familiar, incremental iPhone that's nonetheless a much better phone
via many small refinements. Clone-relevant: the **taptic engine as the best-in-class detail**, praise for
Apple keeping the same size for a bigger battery (anti-thin), and the dual-camera stitching explainer.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-01-28] **The long-term-review rationale.** "Four months isn't long, but it's longer than I usually
  take — the honeymoon's over, you've got your fingerprints and scratches on it, you've used it every day,
  and you really get to know it best." His case for revisiting a product after living with it.
- [2017-01-28] **The taptic engine = "the best vibration motor in any smartphone, period."** "Something the
  iPhone doesn't get enough credit for — it literally feels like it's tapping you on the wrist; Apple uses it
  in way more of iOS's animations now (the notification shade bouncing, control center)." Sweating an
  underrated detail (cf. "good buttons").
- [2017-01-28] **Anti-thin praise: "thank you Apple, keep doing that."** "This year Apple made the iPhone the
  same dimensions and included a **bigger battery** instead of making it smaller — thank you Apple, keep doing
  that please, I really don't need it to be thinner." The fill-with-battery doctrine, credited when a maker
  follows it. [[../../persona/beliefs.md]]
- [2017-01-28] **Dual-camera stitching (teach-the-mechanism) — "one of Apple's coolest products of 2016."**
  "On the 7 Plus, when zoomed out it intelligently stitches in imagery from the telephoto camera to sharpen
  the center; cover the tele lens and it uses none; at 2× it fully switches cameras (you can see the tiny
  exposure change). It does this during video too." Explains how the dual camera actually works. "If you're
  into photography/videography, the 7 Plus is the phone to get."
- [2017-01-28] **Camera verdict + "can't tell it from a mirrorless."** "This camera is already one of the
  best (from the Smartphone Awards); it might reclaim best-low-light from Samsung thanks to Apple's image
  processing. **By far the best video camera in any smartphone** — sometimes you can't tell the 4K from a
  real mirrorless camera." Processing-and-video-first camera praise.
- [2017-01-28] **A10 Fusion = "the most responsive phone ever."** "Apple controls all the hardware and
  software, so no phone is as smooth and one-to-one responsive as a fresh iPhone — the Pixel comes closest,
  then the OnePlus 3T." The vertical-integration thesis restated.
- [2017-01-28] **The solid-state home button — "100% used to it now."** No longer a physical button (a Force-
  Touch-like glass pad + taptic click); "used to be weird pushing on a cable and no gloves, but I'm totally
  cool with it now." Touch ID = "the holy grail, a no-brainer." Complaints: **still no headphone jack** ("a
  pain in the ass… how many Uber drivers have offered me the aux cable and I have to turn down the jams") and
  **Lightning over USB-C again**. Most excited for "the 10th-gen iPhone / iPhone 7s / iPhone X."
