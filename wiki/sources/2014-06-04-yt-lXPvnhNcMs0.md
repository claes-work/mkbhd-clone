# iOS 8 Features & Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-04)

## Summary
An impressions video on **iOS 8** (announced at WWDC 2014). Marques opens with his standard even-handed
framing — an "where iOS 8 features came from" video "would have been a pretty boring video because they
all really came from some previous version of Android" — then walks the feature set (Continuity/Handoff,
SMS sync, interactive notifications, widgets, third-party keyboards, Spotlight-as-Google-Now, HealthKit,
Swift). The through-line is that **Apple is getting more open** — "removing bricks from the top of the
Walled Garden" — which he reads as a genuine attitude shift he likes. Also a quiet **wearable
prediction** reinforcement (HealthKit "could use… something wearable").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-04] **Copying goes both ways — stated flatly.** "We already know Google and Apple take
  features from each other all the time"; an "where the features came from" video would be boring
  because they came from Android, "not that that's a bad thing." His recurring anti-partisan lens.
- [2014-06-04] **iOS 8 is a feature collection, not an overhaul** — "visually basically identical" to
  iOS 7. The big umbrella is **Continuity** (Handoff, one-click hotspot, AirDrop to Mac, iMessage SMS
  sync) for people who also own a Mac. He singles out **SMS sync** as "extremely useful" and wishes
  Google's Hangouts would do the same.
- [2014-06-04] **The Android-familiar tweaks:** interactive notifications, third-party notification
  widgets, a much more powerful Spotlight ("just like Google Now"), better first-party keyboard
  prediction ("exactly like SwiftKey"), and — the big one — **third-party keyboard support** ("I would
  have never expected to see that on iOS 3 years ago").
- [2014-06-04] **HealthKit hints at wearable hardware.** The health system "seems like they could use
  an extra bit of hardware, perhaps something wearable, to really tie into that system — we'll see where
  they go with that." (Reinforces the pass-11 Apple-Watch-by-end-of-2014 prediction.)
- [2014-06-04] **Swift** — a brand-new programming language announced at WWDC, "a huge deal for
  developers."
- [2014-06-04] **The read: Apple's attitude is loosening.** "It's like they're removing bricks from
  the top of the wall in the Walled Garden" — more open, more customizable, "cracking more jokes." He's
  "digging the direction Apple's headed," while noting they'll "take jabs at Android and then adopt its
  features in the same breath — hey, it's all part of the game." He'll wait for the full release rather
  than run the beta, and is personally most excited for OS X Yosemite.
