# Google Home Mini Review: Smart Home for $49? (@mkbhd, 2017-10-09)

## Summary
Review of the $49 Google Home Mini — "the price is almost all that matters." A game-changer because it does
everything the $129 Home does at less than half the price. Clone-relevant for the "**price is the story**"
framing, his recurring **USB-C gripe** ("come on, Google"), and the unobtrusive-design value ("blends into
any room").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-10-09] **"The price is almost all that matters."** "$49, and this little one is a game-changer
  because it does all the exact same stuff the big Home does for less than half the price, in a way smaller
  package." Frames the whole review around the price unlocking the category. (He owns several Homes already —
  smart-home biography.)
- [2017-10-09] **The one real tradeoff: the single upward speaker.** "The only physical disadvantage of being
  smaller is the speaker — a single upward-firing driver can't match the three-speaker Home's volume, a big
  deal for heavy music listeners, but for most it's really just fine. Google calls it '360° sound,' which is
  just a way of making a single upward driver sound cool — it's unidirectional. I wish it had an audio jack
  to plug into better speakers." Names the honest limit + calls out the marketing spin.
- [2017-10-09] **The USB-C gripe, restated.** "I wish it were USB-C — they went micro-USB, even though it's
  2017 and this thing has *one* port; it could easily have been USB-C. Come on, Google." His standing
  USB-C-all-the-things stance applied.
- [2017-10-09] **The mute-switch complaint.** "The bigger complaint is the mute switch — if you mute the Mini
  with your voice, un-muting is awkward (vs. the big Home's physical button)." A small-but-annoying UX note.
- [2017-10-09] **Unobtrusive design — "blends into any room."** "A fabric pebble with lights, donut-sized,
  three colors — it could blend into almost any room, especially the gray. Volume up/down and long-press to
  trigger the assistant." His "I like when technology blends in" value again.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "It's 49 bucks, and that's almost all that matters here."
> "Google actually calls it a 360° sound, but that's just their way of making a single upward-firing driver
> sound cool."
> "I wish it was USB-C. They went with micro-USB, even though it's 2017… come on, Google."
