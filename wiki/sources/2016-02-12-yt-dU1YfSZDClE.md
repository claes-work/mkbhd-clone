# Dope Tech: Crazy Speakers! (@mkbhd, 2016-02-12)

## Summary
A "Dope Tech" episode where Marques argues that no good tech setup is complete without addressing audio, and that instead of playing it safe you can have just as much fun going with the crazy, extreme end of the audio market. He walks through three unconventional speakers — the Mars by Crazybaby (a two-part levitating Bluetooth speaker delivering 360° sound), the Munro Sonic Egg100 (striking red desktop monitors that are passive, ship with a custom dedicated amp, and sit in "egg nest" rests), and the Devialet Phantom (a watermelon-sized wireless powerhouse claiming 3,000 W / 105 dB / zero distortion) — plus a bonus item: the light-up Lightning cable whose LED flow speed tracks charge level. The through-line is Marques's taste for gear that is both aesthetically striking and functionally impressive, evaluated hands-on from his own desk and living-space use.

## Key claims
- [2016-02-12] No good tech collection or setup is complete without addressing audio, and going with the crazy/extreme audio gear can be just as fun as the safe, tame options.
- [2016-02-12] The Mars by Crazybaby is a levitating two-part Bluetooth speaker whose bottom half is a subwoofer and top half is a tweeter, chosen over rival floating speakers because it is the best-looking and the most functional.
- [2016-02-12] The Mars produces 360° sound because the subwoofer fires straight down and the tweeter fires straight up, so it sounds about the same from any listening angle.
- [2016-02-12] The Mars base uses magnets that push the disc into the air after power-up; it is fully wireless, runs about 8 hours on battery, and has its own app to raise and lower the disc remotely.
- [2016-02-12] The Mars has two front USB ports to charge a phone from its built-in battery and a built-in mic, making it a candidate "ultimate meeting room" device for a 360° conference call.
- [2016-02-12] The Munro Sonic Egg100s are striking both aesthetically and sonically, and no other pair of speakers can accent a setup as well as these.
- [2016-02-12] The Egg100s use a 100mm woofer for lows/mids and a 25mm driver for highs, and are unusually passive: unlike most monitors at this level (including Marques's own setup), they contain only the drivers and electronics, not internal amplifiers.
- [2016-02-12] The Egg100s ship with their own dedicated desk amp — custom cables, custom preamps, with per-channel high- and low-frequency adjustment — designed specifically to work best with the speakers, and that amp is "where the magic happens."
- [2016-02-12] The Egg100s sit in sound-isolating rubber "egg nest" rests that keep them still and let you point them in any direction, and they nearly tempted Marques away from his current Yamaha monitors.
- [2016-02-12] The Devialet Phantom's claim to fame is as much power as you can handle in a tightly packaged, roughly watermelon-sized unit, claiming 3,000 W, 105 dB, and zero distortion (a zero-distortion claim Marques won't personally vouch for, though he confirms it gets very loud).
- [2016-02-12] The Phantom has a tweeter under the end grille, a mid-size driver in the surrounding ring, and side-firing woofers that move a lot of air; its directional sound makes Marques lean toward corner placement to fill a room.
- [2016-02-12] The Phantom is wireless but keeps a power cable (no battery) with more emphasis on quality for the price, uses its own "Spark" app for iPhone/Android setup, and can also run as a plain Bluetooth speaker.
- [2016-02-12] Bonus: the light-up Lightning cable (the one from the iPhone 6s review) has a small firmware box on the USB end that speeds up the moving lights when the phone is nearly empty and slows them as the charge nears full.
