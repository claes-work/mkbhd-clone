# iPad Mini vs Nexus 7! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-22)

## Summary
A holiday-season head-to-head between the two mid-size tablets — a category, he notes, "born in the
second half of 2012 with the Nexus 7." A clean, even-handed comparison whose sharpest verdict is on the
iPad Mini's **low-resolution, non-retina display** ("I can see pixels everywhere" — the feature that
"does it down for me"), consistent with his pixel obsession. Ends with a use-case-keyed buyer's guide
rather than a single winner.

## Key claims
- [2012-11-22] **The iPad Mini's display is its biggest flaw — pixel obsession applied**: the 7.9" 1024×768
  panel is "just 163 PPI… Apple really skimped on the display, and it is noticeable even when not directly
  comparing it — I noticed pixels, especially with text. This here is the iPad's biggest flaw." (The
  Nexus 7's 216 PPI is "pretty average… decent for the price.") He predicts "the next-gen iPad Mini
  absolutely will have a retina display."
- [2012-11-22] **Ergonomics split, honestly**: the iPad Mini "takes the thinness crown" and feels
  "premium/industrial"; the Nexus 7 "feels more natural to hold, especially one-handed" thanks to soft-touch
  and rounded edges. A concrete difference each way.
- [2012-11-22] **A specific audio annoyance he actually felt**: "for some reason the Nexus 7 speaker didn't
  bother me, but the sideways audio from the iPad Mini drove me a little insane when gaming and watching
  YouTube." A lived, subjective note, disclosed as such.
- [2012-11-22] **The iPad's real app advantage, explained mechanically**: the 1024×768 aspect ratio matches
  the iPad 2/3/4, "so all the tablet-optimized apps work on the Mini with no letterboxing or stretching" —
  the app-optimization gap he flags on Android tablets, working in Apple's favor here. He states it plainly.
- [2012-11-22] **Performance framed as a tradeoff, not a winner**: "the iPad is overall smoother [better
  graphics/frame rates], but the Nexus is overall faster [better processing/load times]." Names what each
  wins rather than declaring one better.
- [2012-11-22] **The even-handed buyer's-guide close**: iPad Mini "if you're familiar with or enjoy the
  simplicity of iOS — it's like a large flattened-out iPod Touch for $30 extra"; Nexus 7 "if you enjoy
  messing around with Android, if your budget is lower, or if you do a lot of video watching on a 16:9
  display." "You can't go wrong either way." Price: Wi-Fi Nexus 7 $200 vs iPad Mini $330.
- [2012-11-22] **Credits the rival's advantage**: "I gotta hand it to Apple for giving it a 4G option,
  which is nice, while the unlocked Nexus will get you HSPA+."
- [2012-11-22] Dated context: "enjoy your turkey day if you celebrate it" — Thanksgiving 2012.
