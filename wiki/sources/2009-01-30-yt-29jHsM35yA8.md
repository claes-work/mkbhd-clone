# HQ Tutorial: Safari for Windows (@mkbhd, 2009-01-30)

## Summary
An early browser tutorial/opinion piece from age-15 Marques on **Safari for Windows** (v3.2), which
he's adopted as his main browser on **Windows Vista**. He opens by disparaging Internet Explorer 7
(slow, easily "infected" by cookies), shows where to download Safari from **apple.com** (Apple
Downloads tab), and repeatedly emphasizes its headline virtue: **raw speed** — "unbelievably fast,"
faster than Firefox, IE, Flock, and Opera, the fastest browser he's used on Windows. He notes Chrome
is comparable in speed (he installed it "for security reasons") and invites viewer comments on
Chrome vs. Safari. He praises Safari's simplicity (quick history clear, private browsing, empty
cache, bookmarking, checking Gmail) and its lack of need for a preloader (unlike his Firefox
preloader). He runs the **Acid3 test**, where Safari scores **74/100** (a link test fails) versus
Opera 10 hitting **100/100** despite being slower — conceding "A+ Safari, not so much" on standards
but insisting Safari "blows it out of the water" on speed. He's recording with screen-capture
software while uploading videos with ~100 processes running, offering it as a real-world speed test.

## Key claims
- [2009-01-30] Safari for Windows (v3.2) is his **main browser on Windows Vista**, chosen for **pure
  speed** — "the fastest internet browser I have used on Windows," faster than Firefox, IE, Flock,
  and Opera.
- [2009-01-30] Internet Explorer 7 is slow and "infects" easily via cookies; he "highly suggest[s]"
  IE users upgrade to Firefox or Safari (Opera "is decent" too).
- [2009-01-30] **Chrome is comparable to Safari in speed**; he installed Chrome "for security reasons"
  but can't call a winner and asks viewers to comment (Chrome vs. Safari).
- [2009-01-30] Standards trade-off: on the **Acid3 test** Safari scores **74/100** (a link test
  fails) while **Opera 10 reaches 100/100** despite being much slower to load — "A+ Safari, not so
  much," but he prioritizes speed over the Acid3 score.
- [2009-01-30] He likes Safari's **simplicity** — fast history clear, private browsing, empty cache,
  bookmarking, quick Gmail check — and that it needs no preloader (he uses a Firefox preloader for a
  few seconds' faster launch; wishes Opera had one).
- [2009-01-30] Firefox's edge is its **add-ons/extensions** ("make Firefox more favorable... in the
  overall market"), e.g. a colored progress bar; he still prefers Safari for speed.
- [2009-01-30] Real-world load test: he's screen-recording, uploading videos, and running ~100
  processes in Vista simultaneously — "not the best performance, but hey."
