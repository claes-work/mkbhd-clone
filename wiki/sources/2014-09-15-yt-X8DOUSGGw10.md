# Moto 360 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-15)

## Summary
The full review of the **Moto 360** — the wearable Marques had flagged as "the one to get." His two-part
verdict is the definitive early-Android-Wear stance: **"smartwatches as a whole are not ready for prime
time,"** but **"the Moto 360 is the best smartwatch you can get."** Buy it only **"if you're willing to
go form over function"** — you like the look enough to accept mediocre battery, hit-or-miss performance,
and Android-only support. He defends the "flat tire" bottom bar, is honest about the battery math, and
diagnoses the stutter as a throttling-wake issue. A clean instance of grading a first-gen product by
naming exactly what you're trading off.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-15] **The "flat tire" is defensible.** The bottom bar houses the display drivers/digitizer/
  ambient-light sensor that other watches hide in a thick bezel — "I'll take this thin profile with the
  bottom bar any day," and "your eye really starts ignoring it" with real use (most complainers "haven't
  actually used the watch past looking at it").
- [2014-09-15] **Beautiful, light, well-built** — metal, IP67 dust/water resistant, 49 g with the
  leather band. **Wrist size is polarizing** (his thin-wristed mom: "way too big"; big-wristed Lou from
  Unbox Therapy: "dainty and small") — and unlike the Apple Watch there's **only one size**.
- [2014-09-15] **The display is the weak point** — 320×290, low pixel density ("you can easily see the
  pixels"), but he approves the **IPS-over-OLED** choice for outdoor visibility.
- [2014-09-15] **Battery is "okay, not great."** A ~300–320 mAh battery on a 1.56" screen means every
  second of screen-on counts; heavy days end at ~10%. Verdict: leave **ambient mode off** and you get a
  full ~17–19 hr day, charge nightly; it charges fast (Qi wireless) and the dock "looks cool as hell."
- [2014-09-15] **Performance is hit-or-miss (~50/50)** — sometimes flawless, sometimes janky/dropped
  frames, mostly right after waking the screen. His diagnosis: the low-end TI OMAP 3 **throttles down
  when the screen's off** and hasn't ramped back up when you act quickly — "a problem with the hardware
  more than the software," though Android Wear 1.0 shares blame.
- [2014-09-15] **The two-level verdict.** "Smartwatches as a whole are not ready for prime time… that
  being said, this is the best one you can get." Recommend it **if form-over-function** fits you; he'll
  keep wearing his because the Moto 360 will be "first in line" for the updates that should fix battery/
  performance — and he still wants to test the LG G Watch R and Apple Watch before concluding the category.
