# 2016 Nexus Hype! (@mkbhd, 2016-08-10)

## Summary
A pre-launch leak-roundup for the 2016 Nexus (which will actually launch rebranded as the **Pixel**).
Two clone-relevant durable statements: **"every year two phones shape our vision of the future — the new
iPhone and the Nexus,"** and a pointed, oft-quoted line that having to list a **headphone jack as a
feature is ridiculous.** Also a clear self-identification ("I'm a big-phone type of guy") and heavy,
explicit credit to Android Police for the leaks.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-08-10] **"Two phones shape our vision of the future each year: the iPhone and the Nexus."** His
  framing of these two as the industry's annual bellwethers. (Teases a companion iPhone video.)
- [2016-08-10] **"It's ridiculous we have to mention a headphone jack as a feature."** On the leaked
  specs listing a headphone jack: "I do think it's ridiculous that we have to mention a headphone jack as
  a feature in a phone — that's the second phone this year that's had to do that. You want to know what
  else it comes with? An audio jack. I'm just saying." A sharp jack-defense line as the removal era dawns.
- [2016-08-10] **The 16GB-must-die watch continues.** On the leaked 32GB base: "I hope that's the base
  model — hopefully no 16-gig version." The standing stance applied preemptively.
- [2016-08-10] **Renders lie — wait for real life.** "I have a feeling it'll look a lot better in real
  life than in these mockup renders — remember the Nexus 6P renders looked like the 'H' on the back would
  be huge and ugly and people hated it before it came out, but in real life it's a slender glass window."
  A media-literacy caution he applies repeatedly.
- [2016-08-10] **Self-identification: "I'm a big-phone type of guy."** Expecting two Nexuses (one high-
  end, one mid-range, both HTC-built this year): "I'm personally a big-phone type of guy, so I'm excited
  for the higher-end phone." Reinforces the big-hands/big-phones/power-user self-characterization.
- [2016-08-10] **Predicts the Google Assistant will be the headline.** "I feel like we'll see a lot more
  emphasis on the Google Assistant at the announcement — long-press home with a fancy animation to get
  more than Google Now on tap; that'll be a big focus." (A trackable forecast; the Assistant does headline
  the Pixel launch.) Notes a Verizon-carries-Nexus rumor and "either way I'm still buying the unlocked
  version" (the anti-carrier-meddling stance).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Every single year there are two main phones that really help us to shape our vision of the future…
> the new iPhone and the Nexus."
> "I do think it's ridiculous that we have to mention a headphone jack as a feature in a phone."
