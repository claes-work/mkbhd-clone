# Smartphone Awards: 2014! (@mkbhd, 2014-12-08)

## Summary
Marques's annual year-end "Smartphone Awards" for 2014 — a solo buyer's-guide format that names the best phone in each category from the year's releases, framed as "your one stop shop for picking one" a daily driver to sign a two-year contract with. For each category he gives a first place, a second place (runner-up), and one or two honorable mentions, always noting these are one person's opinions. Categories covered: big phones, compact phones, smartphone cameras, budget phones (under $400 off-contract), a set of specialty awards (Most Improved, Best Windows Phone, Craziest Design, Bust of the Year), and Phone of the Year. His overall Phone of the Year is the HTC One M8, credited with kicking off the industry-wide "build quality" conversation.

## Key claims
- [2014-12-08] **Best big phone — 1st place: Samsung Galaxy Note 4.** 5.7-inch Quad HD AMOLED display he calls "arguably the best in any smartphone right now," massive battery, excellent camera, beastly specs, and the best at exploiting its size via software — TouchWiz multitasking on Android 4.4 (Lollipop hoped soon) plus the S Pen stylus. "If you're going to go huge you might as well go all the way."
- [2014-12-08] **Best big phone — 2nd: iPhone 6 Plus** (5.5-inch, Apple's first huge iPhone, "barely making the cut" but counts). **Honorable mentions:** Nexus 6 (5.9-inch Quad HD, stock/pure-Google, first in line for updates) and Samsung Galaxy S5 (standard Samsung flagship). He also flags the **LG G3** here (5.5-inch) as the big phone that doesn't feel big in the hand thanks to thin bezels and nice design.
- [2014-12-08] **Best compact phone — 1st (by far): Sony Xperia Z3 Compact.** Little brother to the Xperia Z3 but with almost no trade-offs: Snapdragon 801, 2GB RAM, 20MP Sony camera, 4.6-inch 720p display, 2600 mAh battery with quick charging, front-facing speakers, IP68. "By today's standards that does make it compact."
- [2014-12-08] **Best compact phone — 2nd: Samsung Galaxy Alpha** ("essentially a miniature version of the Galaxy Note 4," metal build, great display, high-end internals). **Honorable mention:** iPhone 6 (he prefers the 6 Plus "in basically every way" for its larger battery and OIS, but the 6 is still great for one-handed use / front-pocket users).
- [2014-12-08] **Best smartphone camera — 1st: iPhone 6 Plus; 2nd: Samsung Galaxy Note 4.** Named together because they're so similar: edge to the 6 Plus for **photos** (superior image processing, OIS, large sensor/pixels — only 8MP but great low-light, ridiculously fast even on HDR, most pleasing and accurate colors he's seen). Edge to the Note 4 for **video** — 4K from a 16MP sensor, sharp/crispy/colorful, OIS, though photos are more saturated and sharpened. 8MP limits the 6 Plus to 1080p video.
- [2014-12-08] **Best camera — honorable mention: Sony Xperia Z3** (20MP, excellent photos, 4K video); he notes smartphone cameras "deteriorate very quickly" after the top five or so.
- [2014-12-08] **Best budget phone (under $400 off-contract) — 1st (hands down): OnePlus One.** Excellent build, camera, display, fast performer, great software, incredible battery life; $350 for 16GB is "a no-brainer." Big caveat: "you can't really buy it yet" — still stuck behind the convoluted invite system months after his review.
- [2014-12-08] **Best budget — 2nd: Moto G (2014)** ("little brother to the new Moto X," stock Android, front-facing speaker, game-changing at $180). **Honorable mention:** Nexus 5 (technically a 2013 release but still under $400 and great, first in line for updates).
- [2014-12-08] **Specialty — Most Improved: BlackBerry Passport.** A surprise pick given his known stance; he still wouldn't daily a BlackBerry ("it's not that good yet," which is why he hasn't done a full review — every phone he reviews he dailies), but with 3GB RAM, Snapdragon 801, 3400 mAh battery, and the refreshed BlackBerry 10 OS on the 4.5-inch square display it got smoother and fun to use.
- [2014-12-08] **Specialty — Best Windows Phone: HTC One M8 for Windows** (the only award in that category). **Honorable mention:** Nokia Lumia 735 (also a good budget phone, straddling categories). Windows Phone suits people invested in the Microsoft/Surface/Windows ecosystem.
- [2014-12-08] **Specialty — Craziest Phone Design: Sharp Aquos Crystal.** A budget offering with a near-zero-bezel display (one bezel — a "massive chin" — on the bottom); feels super compact but packs a 5-inch display. Ties to his separate video arguing manufacturers should take more design risks.
- [2014-12-08] **Specialty — Bust of the Year (worst phone): Amazon Fire Phone.** No real reason to buy it, nothing separating it from the pack at its $650 price; he expects Amazon to "learn its lesson" and stick to Kindles.
- [2014-12-08] **Phone of the Year — 1st: HTC One M8.** An all-around winner, improved over the M7 in almost every way, and it "single-handedly got people talking about build quality" — after it, Samsung started putting metal in phones and Apple shipped two all-metal iPhones. Slightly slippery rounded metal sides (he added a carbon-fiber skin for grip); he ran the **Google Play Edition** M8 as his main-SIM daily for the longest duration of the year.
- [2014-12-08] **Phone of the Year — 2nd (very close): Moto X (2014).** Continues the legacy of the 2013 Moto X (one of his favorites), one of the best-built phones of the year (added metal band), near-stock Android, fast updates — already on Android 5.0 Lollipop — and easy to recommend to first-time Android users; it also "gave birth to the Nexus 6."
- [2014-12-08] **Phone of the Year — 3rd (two honorable mentions): iPhone 6 and LG G3.** The iPhone 6 is the one he recommends to first-time buyers / "mom buying a smartphone" on a budget, one of the best iPhones yet despite being thin with a small battery. The LG G3 was among the earliest with a Quad HD display and has an excellent thin-bezel design.
