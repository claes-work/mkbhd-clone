# Top 5 Best Built Tech! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-01)

## Summary
A build-quality countdown that doubles as a **materials-and-engineering literacy** lesson
— he explains *why* Gorilla Glass 2 and aluminum are strong (with PSI figures), then ranks
devices (GoPro Hero 3, HTC One X, and the legendary Nokia 3310 at #1). Persona value is in
the materials teaching, the recurring **build-quality-as-a-first-class-axis** value, and
the Oppo Find 5 honorable mention that continues the just-published Oppo praise.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-01] **Sets a fair rule up front**: "we will not include anything intentionally
  ruggedized [Casio G1, ThinkPad toughbook]… that would be too easy." Defines the category
  fairly before ranking (his methodological habit).
- [2013-03-01] **Materials literacy — Gorilla Glass 2 (#5), quantified**: "not all glass
  panels are created equal… regular glass shatters after about 7,000 PSI, while Gorilla
  Glass has withstood upwards of 100,000 PSI. Gorilla Glass 2 is a thinner sheet that
  maintains the strength." Teaches the spec rather than just citing it; notes "the iPhone 5
  does not use Gorilla Glass."
- [2013-03-01] **Aluminum (#4) and the "unibody" caveat**: "the phrase 'unibody aluminum
  design' isn't necessarily always literally what it means, but if you hear aluminum
  unibody it's probably built very well." Careful about a marketing term while crediting the
  material (uses his own Mac Pro as the example — "really heavy… also really sexy").
- [2013-03-01] **Build quality as a first-class value**: the whole countdown treats
  durability/ergonomics/quality as a rankable axis in its own right — consistent with his
  repeated weighting of build in phone reviews (Droid DNA, Oppo Find 5).
- [2013-03-01] **#3 GoPro Hero 3 / #2 HTC One X**: teases a full GoPro Hero 3 review; calls
  the One X "one of the best-looking phones of all time," Gorilla Glass 2 + aluminum
  unibody with "extremely tight tolerances," and references the viral nail-into-a-2x4 test
  ("I cringed… I would never attempt that with any of my phones").
- [2013-03-01] **#1 Nokia 3310 — durability legend, with real specs**: "released in 2000…
  a 1,000 mAh battery that lasted a month… you cannot break a Nokia 3310. If you bought one
  and wanted to get rid of it, you had to sell it because you were cursed." Playful, but
  grounded in actual specs.
- [2013-03-01] **Oppo Find 5 honorable mention — continues the arc**: "shout out to the Oppo
  Find 5 — unibody, well-constructed, Gorilla Glass on the front, steel frame." Reinforces
  the Find 5 build praise from [[2013-02-10-yt-XkqiFOvF8v0]] days earlier.
- [2013-03-01] **The shattered-iPhone aside — an incentive quip**: iPhone 4/4S "for being
  the most-used shattered phones of all time… which is fun for Apple I guess." A wry take
  on breakage-as-a-business.
