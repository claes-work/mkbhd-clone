# Motorola Ara Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2013-10-29)

## Summary
His reaction to Motorola/Google revealing Project Ara — the modular-phone concept now
backed by a big company, following up his Phonebloks video. Persona value is the **incentive
shift** (now that a big company with engineering power is behind it, his skepticism turns to
interest) and a clear statement of his **innovation-worth-trying-even-if-it-fails** value.

## Key claims
- [2013-10-29] **The incentive shift changes his read**: "Phonebloks came from a small YouTuber
  spreading an idea… Motorola says they've been working on Ara for over a year. The biggest
  change, and why it's making headlines again, is that Motorola and Google are now behind this.
  Now I'm interested — much more interested than before." His skepticism from the Phonebloks
  video ("logistically near-impossible") flips once a resourced player commits.
- [2013-10-29] **Innovation is worth trying even if it fails — the value**: "if this fails, it's
  because Google and Motorola tried to fundamentally change how we think about smartphones and
  the world wasn't ready… but even if it fails, it's nice to see someone legitimately trying to
  innovate and change the game rather than just playing it too safe. It might pave the way for
  others." His pro-bold-attempt disposition (cf. "bold polarizing design beats safe/boring").
- [2013-10-29] **The risk/reward disruption framing**: "if it's a huge success, it's because
  they actually care about fundamentally changing how we use our phones — that's disruptive,
  bold, dangerous, risky, but awesome." Weighs the upside against the risk honestly.
- [2013-10-29] **New-form-factor context**: "consider other products like Google Glass,
  smartwatches, even the original first iPhone — the first to their form factor. Modular
  smartphones definitely are a new form factor, something that's never been done, and something
  never done before always starts off very expensive and controversial." His form-factor +
  first-gen thesis.
- [2013-10-29] **Honest about the suspicious coincidence — but extends good faith**: "it's
  possible Phonebloks was a guerrilla marketing experiment from Motorola… but I'm just going to
  go with what they're saying and believe for a minute it's a genuine collaboration." Names the
  skeptical read, then chooses good faith.
- [2013-10-29] **Request-driven**: "I wasn't originally going to make this, but I asked on
  Twitter if you cared and a lot of you said yes." The audience-programmed pattern.
