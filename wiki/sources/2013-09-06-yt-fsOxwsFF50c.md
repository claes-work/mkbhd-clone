# Sony RX100 M2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-09-06)

## Summary
A review of the Sony RX100 II — his "secret weapon" pocket camera. Persona value is in the
**production-gear disclosure** (99% of his CES footage came from this; you can intercut it
with DSLR footage undetectably), a **practical-over-prestige gear argument** (an RX100 beats
a DSLR whose lens you never change), and a notable **methodology-evolution** aside: he's
**stopped giving numerical ratings**.

## Key claims
- [2013-09-06] **Production-gear disclosure — his real workflow**: "I use this a lot but never
  talk about it — I take all my Instagram photos on it, bring it anywhere I have an open pocket…
  99% of the footage from my CES videos came from this. You can cut between DSLR and RX100
  footage in a single YouTube video and most people won't tell the difference." A concrete
  production-gear datum (the 1" sensor + f/1.8 Carl Zeiss lens = "DSLR-like filmic look").
- [2013-09-06] **Practical-over-prestige — the gear argument**: "a lot of YouTubers buy a DSLR
  for the cinematic look, then never use the interchangeable lenses — they use the same lens all
  the time. I'd argue this is a better buy: same quality, smaller, cheaper, OIS built in, you can
  vlog with it and check your phone at the same time." His judge-gear-by-what-you-actually-do
  stance (function over prestige).
- [2013-09-06] **Methodology evolution — he's stopped scoring**: "if I was still giving ratings,
  numerical values for devices like this, this would be one of those rare 10-out-of-10s that
  doesn't have any real flaws." A dated marker that he **moved away from numerical scores** (a
  notable shift from the origin-era scoring convention).
- [2013-09-06] **The "perfect vlogging camera" framing**: "probably even more compact than a lot
  of the other YouTube cameras, and it takes much better video at arm's length." Evaluates gear
  through the creator/vlogger use-case.
- [2013-09-06] **Names the Mark I vs Mark II difference honestly**: "the Mark II adds Wi-Fi, NFC,
  a hot shoe, and an articulating LCD — if you can live without those, the still-godlike Mark I
  saves you money." A rare "buy the cheaper older one" recommendation, reasoned.
