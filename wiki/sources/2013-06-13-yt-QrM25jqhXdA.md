# Where iOS 7 Features Come From! (@mkbhd, 2013-06-13)

## Summary
A ★ **bidirectional-even-handedness** landmark: instead of piling onto the "iOS 7 copied
Android" narrative, he traces each new iOS 7 feature to its real origin — across Android,
webOS, Windows Phone, Ubuntu Phone, Vista, and Apple's own Mac — and concludes that
**everyone copies everyone, and good features are too good not to be shared**. A rich
tech-history piece and a clear statement of his refusal to take cheap partisan shots.

## Key claims
- [2013-06-13] **The thesis — copying goes every direction**: "I keep hearing 'iOS copied
  Android'… but it's not just Android, and some features are too good not to be shared. iOS
  features come from everywhere, Android features come from everywhere, Windows features come
  from everywhere — it's not even close to a blatant copy [except maybe Safari]." His refusal
  of the partisan framing.
- [2013-06-13] **Tech-history tracing (his teaching register)**: animated wallpapers →
  Windows Vista's DreamScene (2006) → Android live wallpapers → iOS 7's Parallax; the
  multitasking cards UI → webOS (2009, Palm Pre); the lateral notification-tray → Ubuntu
  Phone; lock-screen notifications & Control Center → Android 4.0/4.2. Sources each feature
  concretely.
- [2013-06-13] **Credits the cross-pollination of people**: "Matias Duarte, a webOS designer,
  moved to Android and shaped 4.1/4.2 — that's where the Nexus look came from." Shows ideas
  travel with the designers, not by theft.
- [2013-06-13] **Gives Apple design props where earned**: "Control Center is a mush of a lot
  of things, but you get to it with one finger swipe up [vs Android's two-finger/two-tap], it
  has a totally different look, and it's transparent over whatever app you're in — I give
  them design props for that." Even-handed toward the company being accused.
- [2013-06-13] **The Picasso/Jobs frame**: quotes Steve Jobs quoting Picasso — "good artists
  copy, great artists steal… we have always been shameless about stealing great ideas" — and
  adds "pretty much every major tech company's CEO has said something along the same lines, so
  enjoy it." Contextualizes copying as industry-normal.
- [2013-06-13] **The generous conclusion**: "without iOS in the first place, we wouldn't have
  had a ton of the features we see on other operating systems. The sharing goes all around.
  Welcome to the party, iOS users." Extends good faith to the platform he doesn't use.
- [2013-06-13] **"OCD like me" — recurring self-datum**: on Parallax wallpaper, "I hope you can
  turn this off, because I can see it getting annoying for people with specific wallpaper
  habits, or OCD like me." His self-described neatness/OCD again.
