# 13" MacBook Air 2013 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-06-25)

## Summary
A review of the Haswell 2013 MacBook Air — externally identical to the 2012 model, but with
a battery-doubling chip. Persona value is in the **incentive critique** (Apple withholds a
retina display to protect the MacBook Pro line), his appreciation of **PCI-based SSDs**, and
a personal datum: he uses a **retina MacBook Air** and plans to upgrade to the **Haswell
retina MacBook Pro** — his pixel-person identity applied to his own laptop.

## Key claims
- [2013-06-25] **The incremental-improvement verdict**: "everything on the outside is exactly
  the same as the 2012 model — the story is Haswell. It made too much sense for Apple: keep the
  same design and manufacturing, drop in Intel's chip for ~12 hours of battery, and reap the
  profits, because everyone loves 12 hours of battery. I love it too." Credits a well-executed
  incremental update (his standing appreciation for iteration done right).
- [2013-06-25] **The retina-withholding critique — incentive analysis**: "a lot of people, me
  included, were hoping the retina display would come to this machine, but Apple refuses to make
  a retina MacBook Air anytime soon — they're too busy protecting their retina MacBook Pro
  lineup, which sucks, but that's just Apple." Names the deliberate product-segmentation
  incentive (cf. his Canon-withholds-AF and other cannibalization reads).
- [2013-06-25] **Praises PCI-based SSDs — a long-wanted thing**: "I'm a fan of the PCI-based
  SSDs — significantly faster than SATA, something I've always really wanted in a mobile
  machine, and now we got it." A production-performance datum he cares about.
- [2013-06-25] **Personal — his own laptop and planned upgrade**: "I personally can't switch to
  this because of the display. Coming from a 2560×1600 retina MacBook Air, I can't go back to
  seeing pixels where I usually couldn't. I'll keep sticking with retina MacBooks, and I'm
  probably going to upgrade to the Haswell 13" retina MacBook Pro when it comes out." Confirms
  his personal laptop (retina MacBook Air) + upgrade intent — pixel-person identity applied to
  himself.
- [2013-06-25] **Credits Intel / Haswell broadly**: "thank Haswell for literally changing the
  way we use laptops because of that massive heat improvement — so thanks, Intel." Even-handed
  credit to the enabling component maker.
- [2013-06-25] **Peer note (context)**: "the 11-inch Haswell Air that my buddy Austin over at
  Duncan33303 is reviewing" — the peer network again.
