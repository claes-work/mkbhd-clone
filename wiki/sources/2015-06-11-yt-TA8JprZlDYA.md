# 2015 Macbook Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-06-11)

## Summary
The full 12" New MacBook review — and the definitive verdict is a Halo-product echo: **"a beautiful,
awesome machine, but you probably shouldn't buy it,"** the same thing he said about the Chromebook Pixel.
The reframe that makes it work: he stopped comparing it to laptops and started **judging it in the right
category — as a tablet replacement**, in which frame the single USB-C port and mushy keyboard stop
bothering him. Highs: the Force Touch trackpad, a Retina display, and "the best laptop speakers I've ever
heard." Lows: the butterfly keyboard, a 480p webcam, ~6 hr battery, and no upgradeability. First-gen caveat.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-06-11] **Thinness is the whole point.** 2.03 lb, 3.5–13 mm — "the most portable laptop I've ever
  used, by a long way." The two big sacrifices are the keyboard and trackpad, your main interfaces.
- [2015-06-11] **Force Touch trackpad = one of its best features.** A non-moving glass surface + vibration
  motor that convincingly simulates a click "everywhere on the glass," with pressure-based Force Touch (he
  didn't use it much). "I definitely like the trackpad."
- [2015-06-11] **Butterfly keyboard = the thing he doesn't like.** "Almost zero travel, zero clickiness —
  a very mushy, unresponsive keyboard," plus a non-standard cramped-arrow layout. "After a few weeks I got
  used to it, but that doesn't mean I enjoyed it." Also a **480p webcam** ("too thin for a normal one").
- [2015-06-11] **Highs:** a crispy Retina display (2304×1440) and **"the best speakers I've ever heard on a
  laptop — sounds 50× its size."** Fanless/silent; the internals are "about the size of an iPhone… the rest
  is just battery." Core M, ~6 hr on Chrome (Apple claims 9; Safari would help). Not upgradeable — "max the
  RAM and storage up front."
- [2015-06-11] **The reframe — judge it in the right category (tablet replacement).** "I found myself using
  this in places where I'd usually use my tablet — it fits the same compartments as my Nexus 9 and iPad,
  just as lightweight, with a way better display and a keyboard and more functionality. As a **tablet
  replacement** it's awesome — so the single port and mushy keyboard didn't really bother me, because I
  didn't use it in situations that needed more." (Judge a product by the job it actually does.)
- [2015-06-11] **The verdict — beautiful, but don't buy it.** Single **USB-C** port ("hope you like
  dongles… but it's one of the most versatile one-port solutions; I'd have liked two or three"). "If you'll
  use it as your main laptop, spend your $1,300 in a more power-efficient place — get a MacBook Air. So this
  is a beautiful, awesome machine, but you probably shouldn't buy it" — "same thing I said about the
  Chromebook Pixel." **First-gen: the second generation is undoubtedly going to be a lot better.** For his
  own heavy work (video/photo/audio editing) he needs "the word Pro at the end of the name."
