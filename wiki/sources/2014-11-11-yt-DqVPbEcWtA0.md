# Apple iPad Air 2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-11-11)

## Summary
The iPad Air 2 review — framed around **mature-category iteration slowdown**: "large tablets have slowed
down into this iteration year by year… there aren't that many drastic changes," so he reviews it as a
**list of changes vs the Air 1**. The standouts: a **fully laminated, anti-reflective display** (image
"floating right on top of the glass") and the powerful **A8X + 2 GB RAM** — undercut by his recurring
critiques that **iOS doesn't use the power** (a tablet is "a blown-up version of the phone interface") and
another **thinness-for-battery** trade (which, notably, he says he hasn't actually felt).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-11-11] **Grade a mature category by its deltas.** "These iPads and basically any large tablet
  have slowed down into this iteration year by year," so the review is a change-list. Build is "almost
  surreal" — 6.1 mm, 0.96 lb, all metal — "large tablets are hitting their stride right now."
- [2014-11-11] **Laminated display = the best improvement.** Fully laminated + anti-reflective → "it
  pretty much seemed like I was touching the pixels, like the image was floating right on top." Honest
  caveat: "I already thought the old iPad screen looked great — maybe they all look great; you probably
  won't tell without a side-by-side." (Notes a faint black gap at the edges common to laminated panels.)
- [2014-11-11] **Anti-thinness, tablet edition — but honestly hedged.** The A8X is thermally efficient
  and never heats up, but going thinner meant a **15% smaller battery** (7340 mAh). "Yes, I should be mad
  at them for sacrificing battery just to make it thinner again — but it's at the point where I haven't
  actually yet noticed the difference in battery life." Also flags the **removed mute switch** (now a
  software toggle) as "a conscious design decision."
- [2014-11-11] **The power is wasted by the software (recurring tablet critique).** A8X + first-ever 2 GB
  RAM in an iPad, but "everything feels like a blown-up version of the phone interface — it's not really
  taking advantage of all those pixels and all that power." "With 2 GB of RAM you'd think Apple would find
  some seriously advanced iPad multitasking, but it's still the same interface." Most users "won't notice
  the spec difference vs the original Air."
