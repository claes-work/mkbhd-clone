# Top 5 Best Android L Features! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-28)

## Summary
A Top-5 walkthrough of the **Android L Developer Preview** (running on a Nexus 5) from Google I/O 2014.
He's explicit it's "nowhere near a complete build… not even daily-driver material." The through-line is
**Material Design** — which he calls "probably the most important change" — a design philosophy where
animations show "where everything came from and where everything goes," targeting a smooth 60 fps. A
dated instance of his appreciation for motion/animation as a **usability** device (not decoration), plus
notes on the redesigned lockscreen, notifications, quick settings, and settings app.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-28] **#1 — the redesigned lockscreen:** swipe-up to unlock, swipe-left camera / swipe-right
  dialer, iOS-style readable notifications, a transparent status bar, and (nice touch) time-remaining
  until full charge when plugged in.
- [2014-06-28] **#2 — the notification panel + quick settings:** "legitimately redesigned," the biggest
  notification change "since the beginning" — transparent background, one-hand quick settings, a one-tap
  Chromecast cast toggle, an auto-rotate toggle "finally," a new do-not-disturb mode, and heads-up
  notifications that don't hijack a fullscreen app.
- [2014-06-28] **#3 — Material Design (the headline).** A new philosophy where "every time a new element
  appears… it slides in from somewhere or grows out of something," so "all this movement and animation
  gives you confidence about what you're doing and where things are going," at a goal of **60 fps**. "This
  is probably the most important change of this new version of Android."
- [2014-06-28] **#4 — the redesigned Settings app:** reorganized, new icon, adaptive brightness, a
  **search box** ("we've seen this in other skins before"), animated toggles.
- [2014-06-28] **#5 — the other stuff:** a new (standalone-available) keyboard, DND scheduling, new
  multitasking, and the switch to the **ART runtime**.
- [2014-06-28] **Overall read:** "Android L is mostly a visual overhaul compared to KitKat" — a
  departure from the Holo look toward a flatter aesthetic. He notes it "reminds me of HTC Sense the way
  Sense has been flattening itself out." Rollout: Nexuses first; HTC promised the One M8 within 90 days.
