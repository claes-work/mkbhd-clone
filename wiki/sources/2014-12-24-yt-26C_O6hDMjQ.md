# Motorola Droid Turbo Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-12-24)

## Summary
A "better-late-than-never" review of the Droid Turbo (Moto Maxx on other carriers) — which Marques uses
to crown **Motorola's 2014** ("killing it": best wearable, best small phone, best budget phone, best big
phone). The Turbo is essentially a near-stock Moto-X experience in a **tank-like ballistic-nylon body**
with a QHD OLED display rivaling the Note 4 and, above all, a **massive 3900 mAh battery in a
deliberately-thick 11.2 mm body** — a real-world vindication of his anti-thinness thesis (thickness buys
battery). His verdict elsewhere: the best phone you can get in a Verizon store.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-12-24] **Motorola's 2014 sweep** — one of the best wearables (Moto 360), best small phone, best
  budget phone (Moto G), and best big phone (Nexus 6 = a big Moto X); "the Turbo is one of their best
  experiences yet."
- [2014-12-24] **Near-stock software (the Moto X approach)** — quick updates, a few useful additions
  (Droid widgets/ringtones), still on KitKat pending Lollipop. His standing praise for stock restraint.
- [2014-12-24] **Tank build** — textured **ballistic nylon** (a 3D woven pattern, "more texture than any
  phone I've held in recent years"; catches lint, but he prefers grip to smooth), curved edges, textured
  buttons — "built like a tank." Bonus: **no Verizon logo stamp.**
- [2014-12-24] **Display rivals the Note 4** — 5.2" QHD OLED, 565 ppi, "razor sharp… we're not just
  saying that because of the number," bright, deep blacks, and (unlike the Nexus 6) little color-shift
  when dimmed. First phone he got 1440p YouTube on.
- [2014-12-24] **Anti-thinness vindicated — the big battery.** A **3900 mAh** cell in an 11.2 mm body
  (no dimple, "just a big flat battery"): 5.5 hr screen-on (vs ~6 on the OnePlus One) even pushing QHD
  pixels — "an awesome media experience for an extra-long time." Thickness bought the battery.
