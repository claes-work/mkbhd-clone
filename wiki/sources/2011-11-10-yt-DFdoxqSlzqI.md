# Twitter's New Activity Tab (@mkbhd, 2011-11-10)

## Summary
A short screencast of Twitter's newly-rolled-out Activity tab — a feed of what the people
you follow are following, retweeting and favoriting — framed against the bigger platform
story of late 2011: Facebook had just shipped Timeline, Google+ had launched, and "Twitter
kind of stays the same." Mostly navigation, but it closes with a **dated statement of his
own social-platform usage** that is more useful to the clone than the feature tour.

## Key claims
- [2011-11-10] **Personal platform allocation, stated on camera**: "I've moved very, very
  confidently over to Google+… basically just Twitter and Google+ now" — with Facebook
  described as de-emphasized. Consistent with the Google+ scratchpad behaviour seen in the
  2011-10-09 dream-phone video and the Google+ links in the Chrome Web Store tour.
- [2011-11-10] **Platform-velocity observation**: "we know Twitter doesn't get a whole lot of
  updates — Facebook made major changes, Google+ rolls out, and Twitter kind of stays the
  same." A dated read on relative product cadence among the three.
- [2011-11-10] Feature mechanics described: Activity surfaces follows, retweets and favorites
  in near-real-time; visible from the top tabs or the sidebar under Trends; when someone
  follows only a couple of accounts you get a follow-through link rather than just avatars.
- [2011-11-10] Mild skepticism about the feed's substance, stated as an aside: Twitter
  "doesn't have a whole lot going on besides tweets, mentions, retweets and follows," and
  he notes he "wasn't aware that people still favorited a lot of tweets."
- [2011-11-10] Handle confirmed on camera: **@MKBHD on Twitter**, with the account presented
  as his primary Twitter identity.
- [2011-11-10] Closes by asking the audience directly whether they still use Twitter and
  Facebook — the request/vote-driven engagement pattern of the era.
