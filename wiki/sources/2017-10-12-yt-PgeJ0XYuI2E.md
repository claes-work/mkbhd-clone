# DxOMark Smartphone Ratings: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2017-10-12)

# Summary
A landmark media-literacy / teach-the-mechanism explainer on DxOMark camera scores — "the holy grail" every
new phone brags about. He credits DxO's real testing while dismantling the **one-overall-number** as too
broad, exposes DxO's **consulting-conflict-of-interest**, corrects the widespread "out of 100" error, and
turns the barrel on the tech press ("stop it, get some help").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-10-12] **"One number is too broad — look past the overall score."** "Using one number to encompass
  all the complexity of a camera is a little crazy. DxO breaks it into photo/video sub-scores, but the overall
  isn't an average — it's **their own subjective weighting** (exposure/contrast weighted most, new things like
  zoom/bokeh weighted least). So the phone that's *better for what you like* can get a *lower* overall score."
  The concrete example: **Note 8 (94) vs Pixel 2 (98) — but if you shoot portraits, you'd pick the Note 8**
  (a dedicated second camera; higher zoom/bokeh sub-scores that the overall under-weights). His decide-by-your-
  own-values ethos applied to a benchmark.
- [2017-10-12] **The conflict of interest: DxO is a consulting company too.** "DxO also **consults** for
  manufacturers before launch — providing testing software/methodology so they can tune their camera to
  produce better photos, but also **to do better on DxO's own tests.** It's a blurry line — similar to
  benchmarks. Imagine if Geekbench partnered with certain manufacturers to improve performance and *then* made
  a big deal about who scored highest in Geekbench — see how weird that is?" A follow-the-incentives exposé
  (measured: "I don't think they change results based on who paid — their reputation is objectivity").
- [2017-10-12] **The correction: the scores are NOT out of 100.** "Way too many outlets report DxO ratings as
  if they're close to a perfect score because they think they're out of 100. **They're not.** The RED Helium
  8K sensor scored a **108.** Cameras will keep passing 100; the fact they're *near* 100 now is pure
  coincidence of the weighting system — that's why they get reported more (they *look* like a near-perfect
  score)." A precise, source-critical fact-check.
- [2017-10-12] **Hold the press to account — "stop it, get some help."** "My problem isn't with DxO — it's
  with the press parroting 'the new highest overall score' without looking deeper. You don't see anyone giving
  a new crown to the highest-ever *Geekbench* score every time. If you write an article every time there's a
  new highest DxO score but not for Geekbench — think about what you're doing. **Stop it. Get some help.**"
  His media-literacy / read-the-review-not-the-headline stance, sharpened into a press critique.
- [2017-10-12] **The takeaway: ratings are clean, but shop deeper — and they're all great anyway.** "Ratings
  are a nice even number to point to, but for a real purchase decision you've got to look deeper than the
  overall — and honestly, the top cameras are all pretty incredible; the bigger differences are the *modes*
  (portrait, long-exposure, panorama). Watch the blind test." Ties back to the Blind Camera Test thesis.
