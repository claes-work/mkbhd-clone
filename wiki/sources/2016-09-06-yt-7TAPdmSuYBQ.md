# Hasselblad True Zoom Camera Mod! (@mkbhd, 2016-09-06)

## Summary
Review of the Hasselblad True Zoom Moto Mod — a camera-replacement mod he calls "the textbook definition
of a proof of concept." A rare **"don't buy this"** verdict, delivered with a memorable teaching device
(the Pixar *Geri's Game* → *Toy Story 2* analogy for how a flawed gen-1 tech matures). Reaffirms his
Moto-Mods-are-the-best-modular-execution stance even while panning this specific mod.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-09-06] **Frames it via "proof of concept."** Opens with the literal definition: "a proof of
  concept is a demonstration whose purpose is to verify a concept has potential. This is definitely a
  proof of concept — the textbook definition." His teach-the-vocabulary framing.
- [2016-09-06] **The blunt verdict: the mod's camera is worse than the phone's.** "It's a great idea, but
  the one little problem is it's not that good — the more I shot with it, the more I noticed the camera
  already on the Moto Z is actually better." The only thing it does better is optical zoom
  (25–250 mm equiv.); everything else — soft detail, noise even outdoors, a fixed f/3.5–6.5 aperture that
  starves it of light, no built-in battery, plasticky build, 1080p/30 video only (no 4K) — is worse.
- [2016-09-06] **The "don't buy this" call.** "For $250, this is probably not worth it for most people.
  If you're a mobile-photo enthusiast, use the camera already on the phone — it's better. If you're more
  of a camera person, get a point-and-shoot for the same money that takes better pictures. Don't buy
  this." A clear, unhedged negative verdict.
- [2016-09-06] **The Pixar analogy (a signature teaching device).** "In 1997 Pixar made a 5-minute short,
  *Geri's Game*, to test new cloth/facial-animation techniques — not their best work, you might not have
  heard of it, but those techniques graduated to *Toy Story 2* in 1999. Moto Mods are still super
  generation-one; maybe if we give it time, we'll get our Toy Story 2." Reframes a bad product as a
  necessary step. (A characteristic cross-domain analogy — film history explaining tech maturation.)
- [2016-09-06] **Reaffirms the Moto Mods thesis despite the pan.** "I'm still thoroughly convinced Moto
  Mods are the best execution of this modular concept as far as smartphones go — they just work, and I
  think they have a future. But right now, gen one, they're not that great." Separates the platform from
  the product.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The more I shot with this, the more I noticed that the camera that's already on the Moto Z is actually
> better than the one that's in the mod."
> "Maybe if we give it some time, we'll get our Toy Story 2. But until then, don't buy this."
