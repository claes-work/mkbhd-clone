# Xiaomi Mi Mix Alpha Impressions: The Wraparound Display! (@mkbhd, 2020-02-28)

## Summary
Marques shares first impressions of a functional prototype Xiaomi gave him an early look at: the Mi Mix Alpha, a concept phone whose OLED display curves all the way around the back so you are looking at screen from the front, sides, and back. He frames it upfront as not a real shipping product with a price or release date — just a cool idea shown off, continuing the Mix line's tradition of pushing design to the limits. He walks through the high-end build (titanium-alloy frame, sapphire camera covers, ceramic camera strip) and specs (Snapdragon 855 Plus, 512GB UFS 3.0, 12GB RAM, 4050mAh battery, 40W fast charging, 5G, in-display fingerprint reader), noting the specs weren't sacrificed to achieve the form factor. The display houses the cameras in a rear strip (108MP primary, 20MP ultrawide with macro, 12MP telephoto, dual-LED flash) and the software (MIUI Alpha) moves status icons to the sides to clean up the top. He praises the standout charging animation — the whole phone lighting up like liquid power flowing in — and the immersion of holding "all screen," while being candid about the practicality trade-offs: heavy build, glitchy virtual side buttons, hit-or-miss palm rejection, a selfie flow that flips to the main camera but is feature-limited, and a case that barely protects. He flags the biggest illusion-breaker as a visible/feelable gap where three glass pieces meet at the apex of the curve (plus edge yellowing and desk wobble), and pushes back on Xiaomi's marketing math (over-100% screen-to-body ratio, 7.9-inch diagonal). His verdict: a "form over function" concept phone he's glad exists, like a concept car, that might turn into something real someday.

## Key claims
- [2020-02-28] Xiaomi gave Marques a first look at the phone a couple days earlier; it is a functional prototype, not a real phone with a price tag or shipping date — just a way to show off a cool idea.
- [2020-02-28] The core idea: take curved-edge displays and curve them all the way over the back so you see "full 110[%] screen" — this is how they arrived at the Mi Mix Alpha. (He calls "110" here; separately cites a "180.6" screen-to-body figure — both appear to be caption garbles for Xiaomi's ~180.6% marketing number.)
- [2020-02-28] The phone earns the Mix name because that line has long pushed design to the limits; the first Mi Mix was one of Marques's favorite phones for that reason.
- [2020-02-28] Build materials: titanium-alloy frame (harder than stainless steel), sapphire camera covers, and a ceramic camera strip on the back. He has no Mohs pick to verify but believes it because the phone is clearly heavy and dense with lots of extra material and glass.
- [2020-02-28] Specs: Snapdragon 855 Plus, 512GB of UFS 3.0 storage, 12GB RAM, 4050mAh battery, and 40W fast charging — high-end enough to show they didn't have to sacrifice much for the form factor; he'd have liked an even bigger battery given the screen wraps inside the phone.
- [2020-02-28] Display: a 2088 x 2250 OLED with very thin top and bottom bezels that wrap around to meet the rear camera strip.
- [2020-02-28] The rear camera strip houses a 108MP primary camera, a 20MP ultrawide with macro capabilities, a 12MP telephoto, and a dual-LED flash; the phone also has an in-display fingerprint reader and is 5G-enabled.
- [2020-02-28] MIUI Alpha software moves the battery, status-bar icons, and notification counter to the side of the phone instead of the top, leaving the top as a cleaner notification pull-down.
- [2020-02-28] Marques calls the charging animation likely the coolest part of the wraparound screen: plug it in with the screen off and the whole thing lights up and animates the battery filling like liquid power; it doesn't stay on the whole charge, and notifications glow around the screen the same way.
- [2020-02-28] In hand it's heavy but, surprisingly, less slippery than other glass phones — his finger grips the display glass better; the "all screen" immersion factor is very real.
- [2020-02-28] There is a regular speaker at the bottom, but the earpiece for calls is replaced by an electromagnetic speaker that vibrates through the glass to create sound (seen in previous Mix phones).
- [2020-02-28] There is no front selfie camera; hitting the selfie button prompts you to flip the phone around and shoot with the main 108MP camera — which in theory improves selfies, but the selfie mode oddly can't switch to ultrawide or do nearly as much as the main camera modes.
- [2020-02-28] The virtual side buttons (power and volume) are "pretty bad" on the prototype: the volume slider only worked sometimes amid palm rejection, the on-screen power/sleep button worked more often, and there's still a physical circular power button at the top that always works but is a reach.
- [2020-02-28] Palm rejection is hit or miss; Xiaomi's info sheet says it combines accelerometers, gyroscopes, pressure sensors, and software algorithms to tell holding from using — Marques calls this the biggest challenge for a wraparound phone, impressive when it works but with tons of accidental presses otherwise.
- [2020-02-28] Uses for the back display: the charging animation, moving top-bar items to the sides, the main-camera selfie viewfinder benefit, a flip-detection panel with quick-access widgets (timer, weather), and simply looking cool.
- [2020-02-28] There is a case, but it only anchors on the camera strip and barely protects the phone; Marques doesn't see the point and says to just accept it's a super-delicate phone.
- [2020-02-28] The display isn't flawless: the biggest illusion-breaker is a gap in the cover glass at the apex of the curved sides — it's three glass pieces connected, not one, so you can feel the seam and it catches light/rainbows from some angles; there's also edge yellowing (which the camera picks up less than the eye), and the phone wobbles on a desk since the camera bump is a stripe down the middle.
- [2020-02-28] Marques questions the marketing numbers: the claimed over-100% (~180.6%) screen-to-body ratio still leaves 2.15mm top and bottom bezels, and the spec sheet's 7.9-inch diagonal is measured corner-to-corner around the back — in practice the phone is more the size of a Pixel 4 or iPhone 11 Pro Max.
- [2020-02-28] Verdict: he's very impressed by the functional prototype and, in a world of crazy concept cars, is glad we also get "wild form over function concept smartphones" that might become something real someday.
