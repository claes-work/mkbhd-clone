# Google Innovation at WWDC 2012! (@mkbhd, 2012-06-11)

## Summary
A WWDC wrap-up with a twist: rather than list Apple's announcements, he points out how many of them
**echo things Google/Android already did** — iCloud documents, swipe-up-to-reply, the Safari omnibar
and tab sync, and Apple's own Maps app. But he explicitly refuses to make it a one-sided "Apple
copies" video: he predicts the reverse will happen at Google I/O in two weeks, and he hands his own
"favorite announcement" to Apple. It also quietly **resolves a prediction he got wrong** four months
earlier.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-11] **The copying goes both ways — the even-handed frame**: he lists iOS 6 features that
  "ring a bell" for Android users (documents in iCloud, swipe-up-to-answer-with-a-text, the Safari
  omnibar, cross-device tab sync, Apple Maps with satellite/turn-by-turn), then immediately: "I
  guarantee you [you're] going to be seeing some of this stuff the other way around… Google taking from
  Apple at Google I/O." Neither side is cast as the sole copier.
- [2012-06-11] **Prediction resolved — the retina MacBook Pro he "wouldn't put any money on"**: Apple
  announced the Retina MacBook Pro (2880×1800), and he calls it "pretty baller." In
  [[2012-02-26-yt-pzGsUp3yM8w]] he had said "there have been rumors about a retina display MacBook Pro,
  but I wouldn't put any money on it." Four months later it shipped and he names it his favorite
  announcement — a **documented miss he does not hide** (though he doesn't flag it as one on camera).
- [2012-06-11] **"I'm a sucker for pixels"** — his favorite pick, tied to display primacy and the
  channel name: "it's an easy pick for me, it's the new MacBook Pro… I'm a sucker for pixels, and hey
  that's a lot of freaking pixels on a 15-inch display." Consistent with "the HD means I love pixels"
  ([[2012-02-23-yt-4f6ti8k_BhY]]).
- [2012-06-11] **Credits Apple's execution generously as an Android partisan**: OS X Mountain Lion is "an
  extremely advanced operating system that I don't expect Chrome OS to be anytime close to — it's pretty
  much a completely different category," and Siri's sports/player additions are "nice."
- [2012-06-11] **Reads the calendar strategically**: "now we know why Google decided to have their 3D
  maps conference last week" — noticing Google front-ran Apple Maps, an incentive/timing observation.
- [2012-06-11] **Mac Pro anticipation restated**: "they haven't made a whole lot of fuss about their new
  Mac Pro, which I'm very very anxious to see the next version of" — the same request from the Dear Apple
  letter ([[2012-04-21-yt-a5C-pzJUMsQ]]).
