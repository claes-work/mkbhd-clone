# Curved TVs: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2014-05-24)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"-format** explainer on the curved-TV fad that appeared at CES 2014. Marques breaks the
curved-TV question into **immersion / distortion / glare**, explains that the curve radius *is* the
optimal viewing distance (~13.5 ft on the Samsung HU9000 he demos), and lands a characteristic verdict:
the curve is a real but subtle benefit that "**only becomes a problem when it's distracting**" — the
same lens by which he explains why 3D TV died. Bottom line: you have to **see one in person**, and only
the large models. A clean dated instance of the explainer format and his immersion-vs-distraction rule.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-05-24] **3D TV died because it distracted** — "they kind of give you a headache… distracting
  to wear the glasses, so 3D TV just kind of slowly went away." Curved TVs are the new CES-2014 fad;
  **4K, by contrast, "is not a fad."**
- [2014-05-24] **Curving a TV vs curving a phone are different problems.** You curve a *phone* to fit
  your hand/face; you curve a *TV* for the viewing experience, which changes along three axes:
  **immersion, distortion, glare.**
- [2014-05-24] **Distortion and glare are minor; immersion is the X-factor.** Glare is no worse than a
  flat TV; distortion only gets weird from off-axis angles (a slightly smaller ideal viewing radius).
  Immersion is what actually differentiates curved from flat.
- [2014-05-24] **The curve radius = the optimal viewing distance.** The HU9000's ~4.2 m / ~13.5 ft
  radius means you get the best experience sitting ~13.5 ft away; farther than that and "it might as
  well be a flat panel"; closer and it feels even more surrounding, the TV "seems bigger than it really
  is."
- [2014-05-24] **Immersion is welcome only until it's distracting** — more pixels (4K), thinner bezels,
  and a subtle curve all add immersion; 3D added immersion but *distracted*, so it failed. The subtle TV
  curve isn't distracting if you sit right.
- [2014-05-24] **Recommendation: see it in person, and only the big models.** Curved is more expensive;
  on smaller models "you're not going to be able to tell it's curved." He'd probably not recommend a
  curved *smartphone* yet, but a curved *TV* is "a lot more tempting."
