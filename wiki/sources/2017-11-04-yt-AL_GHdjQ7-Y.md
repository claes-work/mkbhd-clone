# iPhone X vs iPhone 8: Worth the Skip? (@mkbhd, 2017-11-04)

## Summary
A quick buyer's guide for the many people simply choosing between the two iPhones now on sale side by side.
Clone-relevant for the read that **a new design is what actually makes a phone "feel new"** — with a great
real-world anecdote (a stranger IDs the iPhone X from an Uber half a block away).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-11-04] **A new design is what makes people *feel* they have a new iPhone.** "Even if most phones are
  nearly identical, having a new design is what really makes people feel like they have a new iPhone this year
  — the bigger screen and smaller footprint finally feels very 2017. Apple also does a little something each
  year (this year the 90°-tilted camera module) so you can tell from far away it's the newest one." A read on
  the *psychology* of a redesign vs. a spec bump.
- [2017-11-04] **The IDs-from-an-Uber anecdote (design-as-signal).** "True story: shooting this outside,
  someone stuck in traffic in an Uber half a block away yelled 'is that the iPhone 10?' — and they said *ten*,
  not *X*. They could tell from way over there." Concrete evidence that the redesign reads as new from a
  distance (the notch + tilted camera as recognizable signals).
- [2017-11-04] **iPhone 8 = the familiar comfort choice.** "The 8 is nearly the exact same design as last
  year and the years before — bigger bezels, the home button and fingerprint sensor on the front. Some people
  like that and gravitate to it because they're used to it. But a lot of people have been waiting for
  something new, and that's what the X is." Segments the two by what the buyer values (novelty vs. familiarity).
- [2017-11-04] **"A really great year for phones."** Notes that beyond these two there are plenty of great
  phones this year — but "let's face it, a lot of people are just picking between these two," so the video
  serves that audience. His who-is-this-for scoping.
