# iPhone 12 Review: Just Got Real! (@mkbhd, 2020-10-25)

## Summary
Full review of the standard iPhone 12, which Marques calls his "favorite iPhone ever" — mostly for the new flat-sided design. He works through: the truly-flat industrial design and the new **Ceramic Shield** front (Apple claims 4× better drop/shatter protection), using it to explain his durability philosophy — scratch resistance is inversely proportional to shatter resistance, and he cares more about the everyday micro-scratches than a one-time drop test (deferring the scratch verdict to JerryRigEverything's Zack). He covers **MagSafe** and reads its endgame as Apple marching toward a **portless iPhone**, with the removable MagSafe wallet accessory as evidence Apple is pre-solving the "wallet-case users can't wireless charge" problem. The **5G** section is the philosophical core: he pushes back on the "5G just got real" marketing, argues 5G is real but still being built, and frames Apple's battery-saving Smart Data Mode as an example of his belief that **the best technologies are invisible** (like ProMotion ramping refresh rate up and down). He praises the new 6.1-inch 1080p OLED as a night-and-day upgrade over the old 720p LCD, calls battery life about the same as last year despite a smaller battery, notes cameras are only marginally better (video still "in a league of its own"), flags Dolby Vision HDR video as first-in-the-world but hampered by platform compatibility, and concludes that at $830 with best-in-class cameras/performance and a much better screen, "this is the one to get for most people."

## Key claims
- [2020-10-25] The iPhone 12 is his "favorite iPhone ever," mostly because of the truly-flat design (not the "2.5D" near-flat of rivals); the square sides are also the most space-efficient way to pack internals. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] New Ceramic Shield front glass claims 4× better drop protection — but Marques cares as much or more about scratch resistance, and states the principle: scratch resistance is typically *inversely proportional* to shatter resistance (softer = harder to shatter but easier to scratch, and vice versa). (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] He won't drop-test his own phone; he defers scratch/durability truth to "Zack" (JerryRigEverything). His feel: not significantly more scratch-resistant than before; drop outcomes are largely happenstance based on how it lands. His stance: use a case/screen protector if you want — "I'm just going to live my life." (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] MagSafe is optional "for now," but he reads it as Apple moving toward an eventual **portless iPhone** — MagSafe being the wireless solution once the port is gone. The removable MagSafe wallet exists to pre-solve "wallet-case users can't wireless charge." (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] The MagSafe puck charges at 15W vs 7.5W for other Qi chargers on iPhone; MagSafe still works through most thin (even non-MagSafe) cases; the faint marks MagSafe leaves wipe off and likely aren't a long-term problem. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] On "5G just got real": Marques notes he'd already reviewed ~30 phones with 5G that year, so 5G already existed; carriers and Apple push the slogan because millions of new 5G-capable iPhones will accelerate the network's development (and carriers want you on a 5G plan). (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] Smart Data Mode uses 5G only for bandwidth-heavy tasks and drops to 4G otherwise to save battery — Marques frames this as good design because **the best technologies are invisible** (analogy: iPad Pro ProMotion ramping 120Hz→24Hz as needed). His advice: don't buy the phone *for* 5G, treat it as a future bonus. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] The new 6.1-inch 1080p OLED replacing the old 720p LCD is "night and day," basically on par with the Pro (slightly lower peak brightness); it's still 60Hz, which he calls a missing feature on an $800 2020 phone but says he'd expected the *Pros* to get 120Hz (none did). (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] Battery life is good but not noticeably better than last year — impressive given a slightly larger, higher-res display and a physically smaller battery; ~6 hours screen-on with heavy use. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] Cameras (dual standard + ultrawide, standard now f/1.6) are only marginally better and mostly in edge cases (low light, night mode, fast autofocus, extreme HDR via Smart HDR 3); iPhone *video* remains "firmly in a league of its own." One knock: more low-light video flaring. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] iPhone 12 is the first/only camera to shoot 10-bit Dolby Vision HDR 4K video — great, but Dolby Vision is just one HDR format, and compatibility means it reverts to SDR once uploaded to Twitter/Instagram/TikTok/YouTube; the millions of new iPhones may pressure platforms to support HDR ("maybe the tagline should have been HDR just got real"). (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] A14 Bionic is one of the first 5nm chips (4GB RAM); the performance payoff is longevity 2–4 years out rather than a day-one difference. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] Verdict: at the real $830 price (Apple's site shows misleading carrier pricing first), with best-in-class cameras and performance and a much better screen, "this is the one to get for most people." The price is a little higher partly because 5G is built in even for people who'll never use it. (Marques)
- [2020-10-25] Aside on the "Pro" question: as a YouTuber/content creator he genuinely wonders whether he's a "pro iPhone user" — teeing up the separate Pro review. (Marques)
