# YouTuber Tip #6: Ideas! (@mkbhd, 2012-02-29)

## Summary
A deliberately "less edited" YouTube Tips episode answering the question he says he gets most often:
**how do you make better videos?** Two durable answers. First, a selection principle — make the video
*you* would want to find in your own subscription box, and specifically the one you searched for and
couldn't find. Second, a concrete editing discipline: **finish it, sleep a full day, then rewatch
before publishing** — and keep the project file so you can actually act on what you notice.

## Key claims
- [2012-02-29] **Refuses to universalize his own method first**: "I might not be the person to tell you
  how to make a better video, because *different is good* is different for everyone — so
  [one creator] might tell you one thing and [another] might tell you another thing and I might tell
  you a totally different thing. It's really all up to you."
- [2012-02-29] **The selection principle**: "when I try to make better videos, I try to make videos that
  are better *to me* — that I would actually want to watch." Operationalized as a search test: "if I
  was browsing YouTube right now and I just want to search for a random tech video, what do I want to
  see? … **that's what I would end up making a video about, 'cause I don't see it on YouTube and I want
  it to be there, and I will put it on YouTube.**"
- [2012-02-29] **The sleep-on-it rule — a real production discipline**: "let it sit there, shut down
  your laptop, go to sleep and sit on it for one day — a whole entire day. Sleep on it. And then wake
  up the next day, do things, and then come back to that video and watch it again, and you will find so
  many things that you would rather have different about that video. I swear. I do this all the time."
- [2012-02-29] **Keep the project file** so the rewatch is actionable — an awkward audio pause or a line
  you'd phrase differently can still be fixed; he prefers this to pulling a published video down.
- [2012-02-29] **Genre-specific version of the same standard**: "if you're trying to make a funny video,
  try to make *yourself* laugh. If you're trying to make a really informative video, do a lot of
  research — **put things in the video that you didn't know before you started researching.**"
- [2012-02-29] **The closing heuristic**: "make a video that you think is good, and then chances are
  it's a good video."
- [2012-02-29] Series governance, stated again: the thumbs-up "really does help me know which series you
  want me to keep going on" — formats continue on audience signal.
- [2012-02-29] Production honesty: flagged up front as "one of those sort of less edited videos."
