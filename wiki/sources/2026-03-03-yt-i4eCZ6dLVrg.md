# "There's Only 2 Problems with the Polestar 4" (@AutoFocus, 2026-03-03)

## Summary
Marques reviews the **Polestar 4**, a car he genuinely likes — and then answers his own
recurring review question, **"what's stopping me from recommending it?"** Positives run
long: he thinks the designers "cooked" (handsome fastback-ish shape between sedan and
crossover, text Easter eggs, wiper-blade-integrated washer fluid, charge port in the
"commonly accepted correct place" rear-left), a commercial-grade minimalist all-black
interior (ventilated/heated/massaging seats, clean sparse layout that's still
well-featured), and — a repeated Marques theme — **"some of the best software in any car
that is not CarPlay."** He highlights a **new Google×Polestar feature** combining Google
Maps data with the front cameras for **lane-level guidance** (tells you which lane you're
in and which to move to), plus planet-themed ambient lighting. Basics: dual-motor AWD,
100 kWh, 200 kW charging, ~280 mi rated range, 0–60 mid-3s, ~$62k. **Then the two
deal-breakers:** (1) **no rear window at all** — a camera feed replaces it, and the back
seat feels "a little claustrophobic" (worse than the normal Polestar 2 back seat); and
(2) **range** — rated 280 mi but he's seeing **under 200 mi** on a full winter charge,
which he calls "kind of unacceptable" for a $62k car when a Polestar 2 gives more. His
plea to Polestar: keep the design/interiors/materials, just deliver **more range and
efficiency** out of that 100 kWh pack. Marques-solo (off-camera crew laughter only).

## Key claims
- [2026-03-03] Frames every review around **"I really like it — so what's stopping me from
  recommending it?"** For the Polestar 4, two things do. (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] Basics: dual-motor AWD, 100 kWh battery, 200 kW charging, ~280 mi rated
  range, 0–60 in the mid-3s, ~$62k. He thinks "the designers of this car cooked." (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] Praises Polestar's minimalist design language: clean/sparse yet
  well-featured, commercial-grade all-black interior, ventilated/heated/massaging seats,
  charge port in the "commonly accepted correct place" (rear-left). Minor gripe: HVAC and
  temperature are screen-only, no physical buttons. (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] Recurring theme: **Polestar has "some of the best software... that is not
  CarPlay,"** alongside Tesla and Rivian (which don't offer CarPlay); if you skip CarPlay
  the native panel layout (Google Maps left, quick controls right) is intuitive. (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] Highlights a **new Google×Polestar collaboration**: Google Maps + front
  cameras give **lane-level guidance** (which lane you're in vs. should be in) — "subtle
  but useful," a real advantage of the built-in (non-CarPlay) software. (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] **Deal-breaker #1 — no rear window:** replaced entirely by a rear-camera
  feed; the back seat feels "weirdly a little bit claustrophobic" (low roofline, high
  knees from the floor battery), worse than the normal Polestar 2 back seat. (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] **Deal-breaker #2 — range:** rated 280 mi but he's seeing **under 200 mi**
  on a full (winter) charge — "kind of unacceptable" at $62k when a Polestar 2 delivers
  more; asks whether the aesthetics can outweigh the range for viewers. (Marques)
- [2026-03-03] Driving: soft, isolating, luxury-leaning ride on active dampers (no air
  suspension) that cancels NJ potholes well; one non-range complaint is slightly
  delayed/laggy lift-off regen. His fix for a recommendation: ~75 more miles of range
  from that 100 kWh pack. (Marques)
