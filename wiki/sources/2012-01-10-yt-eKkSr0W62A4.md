# Transformer Prime Gets Android 4.0 ICS! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-10)

## Summary
A walkthrough of the Ice Cream Sandwich update landing on the Asus Transformer Prime four
days after his first impressions — covering the new lock screen and camera access, true
multitasking with swipe-to-kill, the app drawer and widget picker, folders, the updated Gmail
and gallery, and the Roboto font on a tablet. He opens with a pointed observation about
**which device got the update first**, and takes the first opportunity to switch off Asus's
customized settings in favour of stock.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-10] **Update-order observation, delivered as a mild dig**: it's "a little bit
  surprising that it's here before the beloved Motorola Xoom — Google's very own, the first
  Honeycomb tablet." A third-party OEM shipping ICS before Google's own reference tablet is
  flagged as notable.
- [2012-01-10] **Stock-over-skin preference acted on immediately**: he goes into Asus's
  customized settings, unchecks them — "it's a little bit ugly in my opinion" — and switches to
  the stock ICS settings, "which I think is beautiful." Consistent with the stated
  stock-Android-over-TouchWiz preference from [[2011-10-09-yt-mtBHnwok3rI]], now demonstrated in
  practice.
- [2012-01-10] **True multitasking demonstrated rather than described**: he opens Calendar,
  returns home and reopens it (instant), then swipes it away in the recents view and reopens it
  (full reload) — showing that swipe-to-dismiss actually kills the process. The A/B
  demonstration habit again.
- [2012-01-10] Version specifics: Android **4.0.3** on the Prime versus 4.0.1 on his Galaxy
  Nexus at launch; the Nyan-cat Easter egg triggered by tapping the build number; the cyan
  accent colour and Roboto carried onto tablet form factor.
- [2012-01-10] **Typography noted again**: Roboto is "looking beautiful as ever" and the updated
  Gmail "looks all pretty now — very very professional looking." The word *professional* recurs
  as his term of praise for interface design, as in [[2011-10-30-yt-VuIPe93ZmiM]].
- [2012-01-10] **Battery report offered as an interim data point**: "the battery life, just so
  you know, so far has been outstanding, absolutely phenomenal" — explicitly flagged as
  preliminary, with the full review still pending.
- [2012-01-10] Benchmark caveat: he ran Quadrant but discounts it because the benchmark "is not
  Ice Cream Sandwich optimized yet" — declining to report a number he doesn't trust.
