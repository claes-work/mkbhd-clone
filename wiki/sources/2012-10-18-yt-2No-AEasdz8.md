# Samsung Galaxy Note 2 vs Galaxy S3! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-18)

## Summary
A direct comparison of the two Samsungs, made because viewers wanted the similarities and differences
laid side by side. Mostly consolidates the two reviews, but it contributes one memorable ergonomic
argument for big screens — **the "thumbs-on-screen" point** — and a dated note that he **switched his
own testing daily driver from the S3 to the Note 2**, which recalibrated his sense of phone size.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-18] **The "thumbs-on-screen" argument for larger displays** — the one genuinely new framing:
  "on the Galaxy S3 and smaller devices, while you're gaming your thumbs take up a good portion of the
  display and you can only really concentrate on the portion right in between your thumbs. On the Note
  you don't have to worry about missing content — it's almost all viewable even with your thumbs on the
  screen." A concrete, physical case for big screens that isn't just "more pixels."
- [2012-10-18] **Switched his own daily driver to the Note 2 for testing**: "I've had the Galaxy S3 for a
  while and I actually moved to the Galaxy Note 2 for my testing and review… I felt the S3 was a great
  size, but then I came back from the huge Note and the S3 actually seemed small." Dates a testing-device
  switch and the size-perception recalibration.
- [2012-10-18] **The decisive differences, itemized**: the Note 2 has a **quad-core** vs the US S3's
  **dual-core** Snapdragon S4 (both 2 GB RAM, both LTE); a **bigger, louder, clearer speaker**; a
  **3,100 mAh vs 2,100 mAh** battery; **Jelly Bean out of the box** vs the S3 "still waiting" on ICS; and
  the **S Pen**. Same 8 MP camera and 1080p video.
- [2012-10-18] **Battery as a concrete buying rule**: "if you want a phone that can last you an entire
  weekend road trip, or if you forget your charger — the Note will last you, it's got to be the Note." A
  use-case-keyed recommendation, his usual buyer's-help style.
- [2012-10-18] **Pentile vs non-pentile, restated honestly**: the S3's 4.8" Super AMOLED is pentile
  ("eh"), the Note's 5.5" is not — "things look just as sharp despite the slightly lower pixel density."
- [2012-10-18] **The S Pen framed as optional, not decisive**: "really quite useful whether you decide to
  take advantage of it or not," but "definitely establishes the Note 2 as a two-handed device."
- [2012-10-18] **Hands the decision to the viewer**: "which one would you pick if you had the choice? …I'm
  still trying to get used to the concept of thinking a 4.8-inch display is small." Buyer's-help framing
  rather than a single verdict.
