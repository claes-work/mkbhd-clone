# Lamborghini Temerario — "Perfecting the Hybrid Formula" (@AutoFocus, 2026-02-24)

## Summary
A reshoot (his opinions "changed so much since the first time I shot this") of the
**Lamborghini Temerario**, the ~$500k plug-in-hybrid Huracán replacement: mid-engine,
AWD, a new 4.0L twin-turbo V8 revving to **10,000 RPM** plus three electric motors for
~900 combined hp, 0–60 in the twos. Marques — self-admittedly "not the Lamborghini type"
— covers the aesthetics (signature hooded headlights, ~$80k carbon-fiber option package,
a rear end he calls maybe "the best back of a car they've ever made," exposed rear tires
that will sandblast anything behind it) and, in his lane, the **tech and hybrid system**:
it **starts silently in EV mode** (front-wheel-drive electric, ~4 mi range) so you can
sneak out of the neighborhood before waking neighbors with the V8; the hybrid selector
lets you run EV / Strada / hybrid / recharge (engine can recharge the battery, so you
"ideally never actually have to plug it in"). His nitpicks skew tech/usability: the
**Lamborghini in-car software "looks ancient,"** climate control is buried, there's
nowhere good to put a phone and no wireless charging — so he'd "instantly put wireless
Android Auto or Apple CarPlay over the top of it and never think about it again"; and he
**hates the steering-wheel blinkers** (both turn controls on the left side — his
"least favorite blinkers... in any car"). He explicitly contrasts the spartan Lambo
interior against the practical Corvette ZR1 he'd just reviewed. **Attribution:
co-hosted** — Marques does the intro, aesthetics, interior, and hybrid-tech walkthrough
and a brief closing; he hands the **driving/dynamics segment to co-host Miles** ("take it
away, Miles"). Only Marques's portion is persona data; **Miles's section is quarantined**.

## Key claims (Marques — persona data)
- [2026-02-24] The Temerario is the ~$500k **Huracán replacement**: mid-engine AWD, new
  4.0L twin-turbo V8 to 10,000 RPM + three e-motors, ~900 combined hp, 0–60 in the twos.
  Lamborghini "only makes hybrids now" and has gotten really good at it. (Marques)
- [2026-02-24] Design highlights: hooded signature headlights, a rear he rates possibly
  "the best back of a car they've ever made," and a pinched rear diffuser leaving exposed
  tires that throw rocks/chips at anyone behind (questions its legality); ~$80k optional
  carbon package is extensive but skippable. Decent frunk (duffel + backpack). (Marques)
- [2026-02-24] **Hybrid/EV tech (his angle):** starts silently in EV mode (front-wheel-
  drive, ~4 mi range) to leave the neighborhood quietly; drive-mode selector on the left,
  EV-mode change on the right; **recharge mode lets the engine refill the battery**, so
  you can treat it like a gas car and never plug in. He calls this genuinely useful.
  (Marques)
- [2026-02-24] **Tech nitpicks:** the Lamborghini-built infotainment "looks ancient,"
  climate control is buried and slow; no good phone spot, no wireless charging → he'd
  immediately overlay **wireless Android Auto / Apple CarPlay** and ignore the native UI.
  (Marques)
- [2026-02-24] **Hates the steering-wheel blinkers** — both turn controls on the left
  side (tap to blink 3×, hold to hold) — "my least favorite blinkers probably in any
  car." (Marques)
- [2026-02-24] Explicit contrast with the **Corvette ZR1** he'd just reviewed: the ZR1 has
  a livable, practical interior (cup holders, wireless chargers); the Temerario is far
  more raw/aggressive and spartan — "you don't have to appease me," acknowledging he's
  not the target buyer. The Temerario comps to a Ferrari 296 (vs. the bigger Revuelto).
  (Marques)
- [2026-02-24] It shoots ~4-ft flames from the factory exhaust at night; a "rowdy, raw"
  hybrid experience you rarely see — the 296 is "much more refined if it wants to be,"
  the Temerario has no refined/chill mode. (Marques)

## Quarantined — co-host Miles (NOT persona data)
