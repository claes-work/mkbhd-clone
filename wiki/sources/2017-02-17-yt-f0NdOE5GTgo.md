# The State of 4K: 2017! (in 8K) (@mkbhd, 2017-02-17)

## Summary
His annual "State of 4K," this year shot in 8K — a teach-the-mechanism update on where high-resolution
capture and display actually stand. The clone-relevant thread: **the high-tech vs. bleeding-edge
distinction** (4K has the whole pipeline; 8K has screens but almost no content), and the TV-upgrade-cycle
read ("if you're buying a 4K TV now, it should be 4K — but most people's 1080p TVs are working just fine").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-02-17] **Teach-the-mechanism: what "K" means.** "K ≈ 1,000 horizontal pixels: 1080p ≈ 2K, 4K =
  3840×2160 ≈ 4,000 wide, 5K = 5120×2880 (a tessellation of four 2560×1440 panels), 8K = 7680×4320 = 4× 4K
  = 33 megapixels." His characteristic define-the-terms opener.
- [2017-02-17] **High-tech vs. bleeding-edge — the content-pipeline gap.** "For all the high-tech 4K
  displays we have a whole ecosystem of 4K cameras. But for the bleeding-edge 8K TVs, there are almost no
  8K video cameras — so at CES demos they just play slideshows of high-res photos (you need a 33 MP photo
  to fill an 8K TV pixel-for-pixel). Less than 100 8K videos exist on all of YouTube, including this one."
  The pipeline-not-the-panel thesis applied to 8K.
- [2017-02-17] **RED as the high-res pioneer (why he can shoot 8K).** "RED has two 8K sensors in production
  now, including the one we're shooting on — that's why this video has an 8K option (sorry about your GPU
  if you clicked it)." Also: "everyone's making a consumer 4K camera except Canon." (His standing Canon jab.)
- [2017-02-17] **Why shoot 8K now — same reasons as shooting 4K on a 1080p world.** "Better sharpness,
  better reframing, more data for stabilization — a lot of minor stuff. That's why it's all at the very
  high end." A production-craft rationale for over-capturing resolution.
- [2017-02-17] **The TV-upgrade-cycle read.** "If you're buying a 4K TV now, it should be 4K — 4K is
  mainstream at Best Buy/Amazon. But go through people's living rooms and you'll find plenty of 1080p TVs
  still; the TV upgrade cycle is much slower than a phone's two-year contract — people don't buy TVs unless
  they need one." A nuanced adoption read (mainstream in stores ≠ in homes).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "That's the biggest difference between high-tech and the bleeding edge of tech."
> "There's a grand total of probably less than 100 8K videos on all of YouTube, including this one."
> "If you're buying a 4K TV now, it should be 4K — it's just that people usually don't buy TVs unless they
> need a new one."
