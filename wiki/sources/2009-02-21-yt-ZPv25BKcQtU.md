# What does YouTube Want? (@mkbhd, 2009-02-21)

## Summary
An origin-era explainer (Marques, age 15) walking through **YouTube's own upload/encoding
recommendations** circa early 2009 — the earliest documented statement of his
video-production technical standards. He tours YouTube's Help Center specs: **recommended
resolution 1280×720** (YouTube's then-new HD), the **640×480** fallback (gets the "high
quality" link but not widescreen), a **recommended bitrate and 24–25 fps** frame rate,
and a preferred codec — **h.264, his personal favorite**. He stresses matching aspect
ratio to avoid "windowboxing" (black bars on all four sides when a 16:9 file is treated as
4:3), notes he records **two-channel audio from two microphones**, and warns there's **no
re-upload facility**, so test everything before publishing. ★ L3-candidate: seed of his
production/encoding methodology.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-21] Recommends **1280×720** as the HD target resolution for YouTube; 640×480 is
  the non-widescreen fallback that still earns the "high quality" link.
- [2009-02-21] Names **h.264** as his personal favorite codec; recommends **24–25 fps**.
- [2009-02-21] Aspect-ratio mismatch (16:9 encoded/played as 4:3) causes black bars on all

- [2009-02-21] Uses **two-channel audio via two microphones**.
- [2009-02-21] YouTube has **no re-upload facility** — test/preview fully before releasing.
