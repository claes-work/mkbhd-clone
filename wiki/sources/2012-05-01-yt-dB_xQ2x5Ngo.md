# Transformer Pad 300 vs Transformer Prime! (@mkbhd, 2012-05-01)

## Summary
A head-to-head between the $399 Transformer Pad 300 and the pricier Transformer Prime he'd called
"the best tablet money can buy" three months earlier — and the interesting result is that **he
mostly prefers the cheaper one**. The plastic back he might have been expected to dismiss turns out
to fix the Prime's known Wi-Fi weakness and feel better in the hand. He ends with a three-way buying
guide that includes telling some viewers to buy *neither* and wait.

## Key claims
- [2012-05-01] **The cheaper model wins on the things that matter in the hand**: "the Prime feels a bit
  sharp on the edges while the Transformer Pad 300 feels nice and rounded… the buttons were actually a
  little bit easier to press. I really actually like the feel of the slightly less expensive Transformer
  Pad 300 — **the materials just feel a bit friendlier**."
- [2012-05-01] **The plastic back reframed as an engineering advantage** — the analytical move: "the
  biggest advantage you have in going with the cheaper 300 is actually that plastic back. You get absurd
  Wi-Fi connection strength, pretty much 100% at all times, **destroying the metal-back Transformer
  Prime and iPad** and other tablets." Directly addresses the Prime's known Wi-Fi weakness he had
  reported in [[2012-01-22-yt-WBdJ6X1hp-U]] and the shared metal-back issue from
  [[2012-03-22-yt-R3n_J7v82AM]].
- [2012-05-01] **Reports a difference he could not perceive, and says so**: hardened glass on the 300
  versus Gorilla Glass on the Prime — "did I notice a difference? **Nope.**" And on colour reproduction:
  the Prime is slightly more accurate, the 300 slightly cooler, "but you can't really notice that when
  they're not side by side."
- [2012-05-01] **Performance parity stated with a falsification test**: both run Tegra 3, benchmark
  "within a few points of each other," and "**if I didn't tell you which was which you probably couldn't
  tell which one seemed faster.**" Real-world-over-benchmark reasoning, consistent with
  [[2012-03-22-yt-R3n_J7v82AM]].
- [2012-05-01] **A three-way buying guide with a decision rule per case**: outdoors → the Prime, for its
  600-nit Super IPS+ display; on a budget → the 300 at $379/$399, "$100 cheaper… you're definitely going
  to get a lot of value out of it"; undecided → the 300, "because of the value part of it"; and for
  enthusiasts, **buy neither yet** — wait for the TF700T, which will pair the fastest Tegra 3 with a
  plastic top for "better Wi-Fi, GPS."
- [2012-05-01] **A compatibility trap flagged for buyers**: "just know that the Prime dock does not work
  with the 300 and vice versa — so make sure you get the right dock for the tablet that you choose."
- [2012-05-01] Content origin: made because "we've been asked a lot how it stacks up" — request-driven,
  as usual.
