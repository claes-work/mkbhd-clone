# Samsung Galaxy S6 Edge Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-05-03)

## Summary
The full S6 Edge review — and a clean example of a verdict **tempered by living with the phone**. In his
March impressions he said he'd pick the Edge; after using it, the curved glass reveals a real cost: **less
side to grip** (you accidentally tap the screen, or hold "a sharp metal rail" that "got uncomfortable"),
so it "feels a little bit too thin." The internals are identical to the S6, so the **$100 premium buys
mostly aesthetics** plus a few edge features he half-uses. A dated instance of impressions ≠ review.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-05-03] **Objectively, only the display and dimensions differ** from the S6 — same chip/RAM/camera;
  slightly thicker (7 mm) with a slightly larger 2600 mAh battery. **"You'd better get something out of"**
  the $100 premium.
- [2015-05-03] **The curve costs grip → feels too thin (verdict tempered).** Wrapping the glass over both
  sides "means you get less of a side to hold" — grip it wrong and you tap the screen; be too careful and
  you're holding "a metal rail," which "got uncomfortable after a while." "It does make the phone look and
  feel super thin… but this turned into a case of feeling a little bit too thin." (His anti-thinness lens,
  now applied to a design he'd initially favored.)
- [2015-05-03] **The edge features are nice-not-essential — and split by usefulness.** People Edge + Edge
  Lighting (color-coded favorite contacts, caller glow when face-down) he actually used and liked; the
  Information Stream + Night Clock (which smartly only light edge OLED pixels, no battery waste) "make the
  most sense" but are the ones he used least. Honest aside: "I didn't want to put this all-glass phone face
  down on a table that often."
- [2015-05-03] **Mostly aesthetics — with a small real benefit.** "Everything else about the edge display
  is just aesthetics — the crazy way it looks; content spills over the sides with no bezel." The curve does
  make left-swipe app drawers "more comfortable" ("probably a great phone for Tinder with all that
  swiping"). Watch-out: the curve adds **glare/distortion** a flat zero-bezel screen wouldn't.
