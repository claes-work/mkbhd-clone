# Samsung Galaxy Note 4 Hype! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-11)

## Summary
A pre-announcement **hype/spec-speculation** video on the coming Galaxy Note 4. Mostly forward-looking
enthusiasm (Snapdragon 805, QHD AMOLED, 16 MP OIS, big battery), but it carries one durable
consumer-education point — the **storage-pricing critique**: doubling a phone's storage doesn't cost the
maker anywhere near the $100 they charge for it, so a 32 GB baseline with a cheap 64 GB upgrade would be
fairer. Also restates his **thin-bezels** preference and his even-handed read of Samsung's "looks better
than it is" materials.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-11] **The Note line is "Samsung's stepchild flagship" that geeks love** because it's usually
  "the spec king." He's hyped "even if it's before anyone else is yet," as he was for the Note 3.
- [2014-06-11] **Expected specs:** Snapdragon 805 (whose "real power" hasn't been shown yet), a QHD
  2560×1440 AMOLED display (following the LG G3), a Sony-made 16 MP OIS camera "already used in a couple
  of other flagships," 3–4 GB RAM, a 3000+ mAh battery.
- [2014-06-11] **Storage-pricing critique (the durable point):** "it doesn't actually cost 100 bucks
  more to make a phone with double the storage… a 32 GB phone doesn't cost 100 bucks less than a 64 GB
  phone to make." He wants a 32 GB baseline price with 64 GB as a ~$20 option (plus expandable microSD).
- [2014-06-11] **Samsung's materials "look better than they actually are."** He expects premium-looking
  (leatherette/stitching) rather than truly premium materials — "I'm fully expecting it to look like
  something better than it actually is" — and is fine with that as long as it's not slippery plastic.
- [2014-06-11] **Size + the thin-bezel wish.** Note history trends bigger (5.3→5.5→5.7"), so ~5.9" is
  the math, "but it doesn't have to be" — he'd rather they follow the **LG G3's thin-bezel** approach to
  keep a big screen wieldy. Long-shot wishes: a Google Play edition Note 4.
