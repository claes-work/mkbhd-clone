# Dear Samsung! (@mkbhd, 2017-04-05)

## Summary
A "Dear Samsung" wishlist (a follow-up to Dear Apple — which he notes seemingly worked, as Apple confirmed a
new Mac Pro days later). Three asks concentrate durable critiques: **stop copying Google's software** (Bixby),
**make good first-party cases**, and (a parting shot) **keep the headphone jack**. A strong industry-commentary
+ comedic-voice node.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-04-05] **"Dear Apple" seemingly worked — a creator-influence beat.** "Days after my Dear Apple / please-
  refresh-the-Mac-Pro video, Apple gave rare insight that a new Mac Pro is coming. I won't say Phil Schiller
  saw my video, but the message got echoed and Apple is listening." Frames his platform as having reach.
- [2017-04-05] **"Stop competing with Google on software" — the copying critique.** "Sammy, you have a long
  history of using Android but building a Samsung version of every single thing Google makes — a huge waste
  of resources. Bixby does all the same things Google Assistant does, and it's on a phone that *already has*
  Google Assistant. **Competition is good, but not when you're just copying something and the product's
  already free.**" A durable, principled anti-redundant-copying stance.
- [2017-04-05] **The Bixby-button ask (a legit feature hiding in a misstep).** "At least let us remap the
  Bixby button to Google Assistant or any app — like the custom key on the S7 Active. That would be a
  legitimately awesome feature." Turns a complaint into a constructive product suggestion.
- [2017-04-05] **"Your first-party cases need work" (the comedic riff).** "The S8 is beautiful but breakable
  with those thin bezels; you have the dimensions first, you know the weak points — this is your chance to
  make the case market great. And *this* is the case you come up with? It looks like a bikini for your phone
  made out of Play-Doh… like you hired the guy who designed the iPhone 5C case and locked him in a room with
  crayons… a Fisher-Price case for a Fisher-Price phone." A signature escalating comedic-insult register.
- [2017-04-05] **Two types of phone owners (a recurring framing).** "There are two types: people like me who
  won't put a case on a beautiful phone because I want to show it off, and people who won't use it *without*
  a case because it's too delicate to risk on a 2-year contract." His empathy for both, and the case-market
  argument. Parting shot: "keep the headphone jack around."
