# Tech Channel of the Week #8: MacintoshTipz (@mkbhd, 2009-06-28)

## Summary
Eighth Tech Channel of the Week, spotlighting Macintosh Tipz — a two-person YouTube
Partner channel (run by "EB and scooter") with 1,552 subscribers and 33,000+ channel
views at filming, focused on Apple/Mac tutorials and reviews plus a companion website.
Unlike prior TCOTW entries, he tells a specific **discovery story**: he found the
channel while watching one of their live streams ("I was in the chat room and I was
like, wow, what is this Macintosh Tips?") and became "a proud subscriber," noting they
are "a proud subscriber, I guess, of MKBHD" in return — an explicit mutual-subscriber
relationship, not just one-way promotion. Praises their production quality (iMovie +
ScreenFlow, "no messing around on this channel") and their sponsor-relationship
maturity (a dedicated Product Reviews playlist, implying they already receive review
units from companies).

## Key claims
- [2009-06-28] **Discovery story, dated**: found Macintosh Tipz via a live stream chat
  room, "not that long ago" relative to filming (~mid-2009) — the first TCOTW entry
  with a specific discovery narrative rather than just "I came across this channel."
- [2009-06-28] **Mutual-subscriber framing**: "I'm a proud subscriber of Macintosh
  Tips, and they are a proud subscriber, I guess, of MKBHD" — evidence of a genuine
  peer relationship among small 2009 tech channels, not one-directional shoutouts.
- [2009-06-28] **Stats disclosed**: 1,552 subscribers, 33,000+ channel views, 62
  videos, YouTube Partner status confirmed by presence of autoplay/monetization
  features.
- [2009-06-28] **Production-quality praise as a review criterion**: singles out their
  use of iMovie and ScreenFlow and "they really get into the editing and they spend a
  lot of time with their videos" as the reason their most-viewed video (a Sony DCR
  camcorder unboxing/review) stands out — production effort treated as a quality
  signal independent of subject matter.
- [2009-06-28] **Notes their sponsor maturity**: a dedicated "Product Reviews"
  playlist, because "they have enough subscribers that companies are willing to send
  them products for reviews" — an explicit statement that subscriber count gates
  access to review units, a lens he applies to his own channel elsewhere.
