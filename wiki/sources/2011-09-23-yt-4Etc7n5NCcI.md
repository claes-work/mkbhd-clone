# Facebook Timeline 2011: Review / Tour (@mkbhd, 2011-09-23)

## Summary
A screencast tour of Facebook's newly-announced Timeline profile, walked through **on his
own real profile** — the onboarding tour, the cover photo (uploaded live), the
"everything in one place" info bar, the activity/privacy log, the two-pane story layout,
app integrations, and the scroll-back-to-birth timeline. Because he demonstrates on his
own account rather than a dummy, the video incidentally yields **hard dated biographical
facts**, most notably his birth date, stated on camera while scrolling the timeline to its
beginning.

## Key claims
- [2011-09-23] **Birth date stated on camera (self-reported)**: scrolling the Timeline to
  its start, he says "if you hit born, you actually go all the way back to my born day,
  which is **December 3rd 1993**." A first-person, dated confirmation of the birth date
  rather than a third-party biography claim.
- [2011-09-23] **Ultimate frisbee anchor**: the Timeline tour's auto-selected slideshow of
  photos he's been tagged in is described as "mostly frisbee stuff" — dated evidence that
  frisbee was already his dominant tagged-photo activity by Sep 2011, consistent with the
  later "lawn sports"/ultimate identity.
- [2011-09-23] **Self-reported low Facebook activity**: scrolling back, "I wasn't really
  very active on Facebook during the year 2008, or anytime for that matter, between 2008
  and the year I was born" — his platform of record was YouTube/Twitter, not Facebook.
- [2011-09-23] **Audience-response commitment restated**: "I answer all the comments and
  personal messages on YouTube" — a dated statement of the direct-reply practice that runs
  through the whole early channel.
- [2011-09-23] Privacy handling on camera: the chat sidebar and friends' photos are blurred
  in post — an early, deliberate editorial choice to protect third parties while
  demonstrating on a real account.
- [2011-09-23] Product observations: Timeline's two-pane layout is called "really the
  newest thing about this"; stories can be starred to go full-width; the info bar is
  extensible with apps (he connects SoundCloud live); check-ins can be viewed on a map.
- [2011-09-23] Closing assessment of what Timeline *is*: "this really makes it like a
  personal blog — you can really just keep track of your whole life here… and really give
  Facebook all your information." A pointed aside on the data trade, delivered flatly.
- [2011-09-23] Photography detail: he notes his uploaded cover photo "looks pretty good…
  with the bokeh of the lens that I took the photo with" — dated evidence of him shooting
  his own stills with a fast lens (consistent with the Canon T2i + Sigma 30mm f/1.4 gear
  thread).
