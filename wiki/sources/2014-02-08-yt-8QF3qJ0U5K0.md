# Samsung Galaxy S5: What to Expect! (@mkbhd, 2014-02-08)

## Summary
A pre-announcement rumor/prediction video for the Galaxy S5. Reinforcing, but a clean
instance of his **calibrated-probability** prediction habit (assigning percentages to
competing rumors) and his **incremental-vs-revolutionary** read (leaked benchmarks point to
a safe, incremental upgrade — another 1080p/Snapdragon-800 phone).

## Key claims
- [2014-02-08] **Calibrated-probability predictions**: "the display is a 50/50 — case one, another
  5" 1080p [I'd give it 65–75% based on leaked benchmarks showing 480 PPI / 1920×1080]; case two, a
  2K QHD display [less likely]. The far-fetched rumors — ditching physical buttons, a 128 GB
  version, a fingerprint/retina scanner — I'd put at ~5%." Assigns explicit probabilities rather
  than flat predictions (his calibrated-forecasting habit).
- [2014-02-08] **Incremental, not revolutionary — the safe-bet read**: "it's seeming like a
  relatively incremental upgrade from the S4, not revolutionary — a pretty safe bet for Samsung to
  sell a lot of them again." Reads the strategy (a low-risk iteration).
- [2014-02-08] **KitKat-improves-lesser-specs**: "even though the specs seem like a small
  incremental upgrade, KitKat has way lower system requirements and is better optimized, so even
  with a similar CPU/GPU it should perform better than the current phone on 4.3." The software-lifts-
  the-hardware point.
- [2014-02-08] **Leak-culture, matter-of-fact**: "I'm sure the rest of the details will leak before
  the announcement, because it's 2014 and everything leaks at this point." His unbothered read of
  the modern leak cycle.
- [2014-02-08] **Reasoned skepticism on the fingerprint-scanner rumor**: "the fingerprint scanner
  is the obvious copy of the iPhone 5s Touch ID — I don't expect it, Android phones haven't gotten
  that down, and Samsung doesn't take quite that extreme a risk." (Wrong — the S5 shipped with a
  fingerprint scanner — a logged miss.) His reasoned-but-fallible calls.
- [2014-02-08] **Design-cue pattern read**: "Samsung's S and Note lines bounce design cues off each
  other, so I expect a shrunken-down Note 3 — the leatherette back, the metal band." His pattern-
  reading of Samsung's design cadence.
