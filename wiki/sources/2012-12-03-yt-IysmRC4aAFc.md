# Google Nexus 10 Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-12-03)

## Summary
The full Nexus 10 review, and its central argument is a durable analytical framing: the tablet's
2560×1600 display is "the best-looking mobile display out there," but it's **so far ahead of its time
that the ecosystem hasn't caught up** — "content optimization" is the missing piece, and being ahead
becomes the tablet's biggest weakness. It's the clearest 2012 statement of the hardware-outran-software
/ tech-works-but-the-ecosystem-isn't-ready thesis (paired with the battery essay three days later).

## Key claims
- [2012-12-03] **"Ahead of its time" as the flaw — the thesis**: "this is an excellent tablet for an
  excellent price, **just enough ahead of its time for it to become its biggest weakness** for now."
  Being early is framed as a real, dated downside, not a compliment.
- [2012-12-03] **"Content optimization" — the named missing piece**: "it takes a couple of things to make
  such a display a good experience… you need the content to take advantage of it — apps, games, movies,
  fonts, icons all optimized for such a high-res display. Content optimization is what's missing." The
  hardware-outran-software argument, given a name.
- [2012-12-03] **Where it's optimized vs. not, honestly**: Android itself, Google apps, the keyboard and
  text (Google Books/Play Magazines) "look phenomenal," but many third-party apps still "run the phone app
  in landscape" or use "lower-resolution icons" so "you'll see pixelization." He shows the split rather
  than declaring the display a clean win.
- [2012-12-03] **Benchmarks-don't-translate restated**: "benchmarks indicate this is a mid-range device
  with solid power, but these benchmarks don't exactly translate well into real-life performance — this
  thing flies." Consistent with the anti-benchmark position ([[2012-10-12-yt-VdsMwGWvhO4]]).
- [2012-12-03] **Prefers this IPS panel for media despite lower contrast**: "this isn't an AMOLED display,
  so the contrast ratios aren't quite as high… but I'd honestly rather have this IPS display for media
  consumption than any other that exists right now." A disclosed personal preference weighed against a
  spec disadvantage.
- [2012-12-03] **Build/feature notes**: ~600 g hardened-plastic body with a "tacky" grippy back ("a super
  fingerprint magnet, [but] I still love it"); **front-facing triple-channel speakers** praised for media;
  a 9000 mAh battery lasting "days on end" (~5 hours screen-on); a magnetic panel for a future smart cover;
  a quirk where the volume buttons invert in portrait.
- [2012-12-03] **The forward-looking hold**: "the games fully optimized for the Nexus 10 have me holding
  onto this tablet and keeping it for the future of what this experience will be like." He buys into the
  trajectory while flagging the present gap.
- [2012-12-03] **Verdict scoped honestly**: "excellent tablet for an excellent price," with the ahead-of-
  its-time caveat as the one real reservation; Wi-Fi-only for now ("4G Nexus 10 might be the dream").
