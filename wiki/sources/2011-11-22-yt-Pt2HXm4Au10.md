# Canon 5D MkIII Preview (@mkbhd, 2011-11-22)

## Summary
A speculation video about a camera that did not yet exist: what Canon would change in the
5D Mark II's successor. He opens by stating the premise honestly — "this camera doesn't
exist yet, we're just going to talk about what we know about it" — then reasons feature by
feature from the Mark II's known weaknesses and from who actually buys it. The
analytically interesting move is his **prediction that autofocus will NOT be meaningfully
improved**, not because Canon can't, but because doing so would cannibalize their other
DSLRs. He also commits to a falsifiable number: a 28 MP sensor.

## Key claims
- [2011-11-22] **Product-segmentation reasoning (the notable move)**: "we were thinking that
  the 5D Mark III might have a better autofocus system, but then we realized that would kind
  of take away from reasons to buy their other DSLRs. So as much as we'd like to see better
  autofocus in the Mark III, I don't think it's going to happen this time." He predicts a
  company will withhold an improvement to protect its own line-up.
- [2011-11-22] **Falsifiable prediction, revised on camera**: "right now I'm going to put my
  estimate at a 32 megapix— no, I'm going to say 28. I'm going to say a 28 megapixel sensor in
  the Mark III." He declines to predict a release date, explicitly handing that to the
  audience.
- [2011-11-22] **Framing premise**: the 5D Mark II mattered as Canon's **first HD-video DSLR**
  and had become "a very popular just video camera — a lot of people use it just to shoot
  videos, there are TV shows that use it," so he expects a video-centric refresh.
- [2011-11-22] Specific predictions listed: a larger and possibly articulating rear LCD (like
  the 60D's), **dual card slots** (still CF), removal of the 4 GB per-clip recording limit, and
  a modest frame-rate bump from 3.9 fps to roughly 4.5–4.8 fps via a DIGIC 5 or dual DIGIC 4.
- [2011-11-22] Continuity predictions: the weather-sealed body preserved, and the 24–105mm f/4
  retained as kit — called "a great all-around walk-around lens," particularly on full frame.
- [2011-11-22] **Price-decay advice for the audience**: the Mark II launched around $2,800 and
  could be had for about $1,800 on eBay at the time of recording, with a further drop expected
  when the III lands — plus the BorrowLenses rental link again as the access path for a
  one-off shoot.
