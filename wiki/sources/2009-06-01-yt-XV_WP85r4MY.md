# Reviewed: Solo 17" Laptop Messenger Bag (@mkbhd, 2009-06-01)

## Summary
The **review** half of the Solo messenger-bag two-step (unboxing:
[[2009-05-03-yt-oVUf8Sv1pUA]], ~four weeks earlier). Notable as the earliest documented
review in the corpus that is explicitly grounded in **lived-in use rather than a first
look**: he says he traveled with the bag twice — "about a week and a half ago" and
"yesterday" — "so I do have a pretty good experience with this bag." He walks every
compartment, but the review's spine is what he actually put where (charger in the two lower
pockets, portable laptop cooler in the second sleeve, laptop in the velcro compartment) and
which pockets went **unused** — a criticism he softens rather than suppresses ("no one really
uses those pockets; I found that I didn't use them"). He also references the **annotation** he
hid in the unboxing video pointing at a secret compartment: an origin-era cross-video device
for making viewers watch both halves. ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-01] **Review sample, disclosed**: "courtesy of the kind folks at Solo laptop
  cases," with the description link and their Twitter handle ("for many updates and little
  promo codes"), closing with "thanks to Solo for sending this out to me."
- [2009-06-01] **Lived-with-it methodology (earliest clear instance)**: "I actually traveled
  with it once earlier about a week and a half ago and I actually traveled with it yesterday
  so I do have a pretty good experience with this bag."
- [2009-06-01] **Reviews are written for the person with the same problem**: "since I do have
  a 17-inch laptop I figured I would show you guys other people out there who have a 17-inch
  laptop who actually do go mobile with theirs."
- [2009-06-01] **Criticism through his own usage**: the small front compartments — "no one
  really uses those pockets; I found that I didn't use them, or I didn't actually have a long
  enough trip to use those, but I'm sure they come in handy for some people" — a negative
  finding immediately bounded by the limits of his own testing.
- [2009-06-01] **Favourite feature named**: the fully-unzipping main organizer — "this is
  where the good part is, this is what I really like about it."
- [2009-06-01] **Concrete load-out**: two lower pockets for the two halves of the charger,
  organizer compartments for phone and cell-phone charger, a velcro sleeve for the 17-inch
  laptop, and a second sleeve for "my little portable laptop cooler" (still in the bag from
  yesterday's trip).
- [2009-06-01] **Verdict**: "it is a good size and the way it holds your stuff is really
  secure... it's really a handy thing to have if you're gonna go mobile." No numeric score
  (consistent with the inconsistent origin-era scoring habit).
- [2009-06-01] **Cross-video annotation as a series device**: "you may have noticed in the
  unboxing video, if you watched closely, there was actually a secret hidden compartment
  annotation that I added to the video" — he plants a clue in video A and pays it off in
  video B (the hidden pass-through pockets behind the laptop sleeve).
- [2009-06-01] **Everyday framing of the payoff**: "you can just stick a few papers in there,
  work it out at a meeting or something."
- [2009-06-01] **Ongoing pipeline promised**: "I hope to get to you guys with more great
  product reviews."
