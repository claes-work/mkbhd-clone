# Tech Channel of the Week #3: Gigafide (@mkbhd, 2009-05-23)

## Summary
The third entry in the origin-era "Tech Channel of the Week" series: a screencast tour of
**Gigafide** (the YouTube channel behind **tinkernut.com**), a one-person tech channel with
**15,600 subscribers** at the time — far larger than MKBHD's ~900. The most useful part is
that he **states the series' selection rule and his underlying community habit** out loud:
the featured channel is a subscriber of his who makes high-quality tech videos, and he
personally visits, comments on, and often subscribes back to every channel that subscribes
to him. Not an L3-candidate on its own — the series mechanics were already captured in
entries #1 and #2 — but it adds the reciprocity habit in his own words.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-23] **Series definition, verbatim**: the featured channel "is basically a
  subscriber of me who does produce high-quality tech-related videos."
- [2009-05-23] **Reciprocity as a routine, not a campaign**: "every time someone subscribes,
  I do go over and visit their channel, leave a comment saying thanks, and oftentimes I'll
  subscribe to them seeing that they have a good channel."
- [2009-05-23] **Featured channel**: Gigafide, run (as far as he knows) by one person, with
  an associated website **tinkernut.com**; "a ton of uploads," mostly software videos, plus
  hacking, installation and tricks videos, several playlists, "most of them have thousands
  of views."
- [2009-05-23] **Their most recent video at the time**: free alternatives to paid software
  (e.g. Google Docs instead of Microsoft Word) — he cites it twice as the standout.
- [2009-05-23] **Mutual subscription is disclosed**: "I'm a subscriber of him. He's a
  subscriber of me."
- [2009-05-23] **Size asymmetry acknowledged without awkwardness**: "right now he has 15,600
  subscribers, so I'm sure he doesn't need a shout out" — he promotes a channel ~17× his
  own size anyway.
- [2009-05-23] **Framing of what makes the channel good**: "basically what he's doing is
  showing — he's just giving everybody a ton of knowledge."
- [2009-05-23] **Timestamped observation**: the channel was on YouTube's then-new "beta
  layout," and he notes upload counts are hard to read there — an incidental record of the
  2009 YouTube channel-page redesign he had reacted to earlier.
