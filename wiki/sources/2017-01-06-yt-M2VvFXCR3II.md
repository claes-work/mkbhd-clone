# Faraday Future FF91 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2017-01-06)

## Summary
A CES 2017 look at the Faraday Future FF91, compared to Tesla by popular request. He balances **show-car
skepticism** (a non-working floor car) against genuine excitement at the working prototypes' ideas — and
lands on his signature **"I'm glad someone's trying"** optimism for EV startups.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-01-06] **Show-car vs. working-prototype skepticism (source-critical).** "This is their show car —
  it's CES, this isn't a working car, and a lot of people love to point that out." He separates the
  floor-model spectacle from the two working beta prototypes he actually tested (self-driving/self-parking +
  performance). Distinguishes marketing from shipped reality.
- [2017-01-06] **"There's a lot of Tesla in the car's DNA" — but real differentiation.** "They have plenty
  of former Tesla employees, obviously — but they did legitimate work to make it different." Names what's
  genuinely new rather than dismissing it as a clone.
- [2017-01-06] **The standout new idea: rear-wheel steering.** "Despite being a big car with a long
  wheelbase, the rear wheels turn up to 3.5° — you could really feel the difference, a super-tight turning
  radius. It felt like a really big deal." Credits the specific engineering that impressed him.
- [2017-01-06] **The insane numbers (with the shipped-reality caveat).** "130 kWh, 1,000+ hp, 300+ mile
  range, 0–60 in **2.39 seconds** — which would make it the quickest production car in the world **if it
  were in production**." Reports the numbers while flagging the conditional.
- [2017-01-06] **THE VALUE: "I'm glad someone's trying."** "Most people aren't giving this a chance to
  exist, which is understandable — it's a rocky start for the company. But looking at all the good ideas, I
  actually really want it to happen. Someone's got to try, and with Tesla's success the industry is finally
  turning — so why not be optimistic? Cross your fingers for this one." The champion-the-bold-attempt value,
  applied to an EV startup (the ancestor of his 2026 EV-startup takes). Notes the wireless charging,
  camera-mirrors, and ~dozen displays as "just feels like the future."

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "This is their show car… it's CES, this isn't a working car, and a lot of people love to point that out."
> "I'm glad someone's trying. Someone's got to try, and with Tesla's success it feels like the car industry
