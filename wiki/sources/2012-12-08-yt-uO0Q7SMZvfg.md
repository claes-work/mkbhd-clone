# Rooting & Jailbreaking: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-12-08)

## Summary
An "Explained!" entry, made because the SuperNexus video generated so many "what does rooting mean?"
questions. It defines rooting (Android) and jailbreaking (iOS), what they unlock, and how to do it
safely — and it carries a compact, quotable statement of his **ownership/control** philosophy ("you
don't even really own the device until you've voided the warranty") plus a sharp observation that
**root-app features get absorbed into stock Android over time**.

## Key claims
- [2012-12-08] **The ownership/control line — quotable**: "yes, rooting does void your warranty. But as I
  like to say, **you don't even really own the device until you've voided the warranty.**" A blunt
  statement of the control-over-your-device value that underpins his stock-vs-skin arc.
- [2012-12-08] **Root-app features get absorbed into stock — a dated observation**: "it's these features
  from root-only apps that end up getting implemented into newer versions of Android. You used to need
  root to take screenshots — now that's built in. You needed root to tether — now that's built in." Reads
  the modding community as a feature pipeline for the OS. (Mirrors the iOS side: "you used to need to be
  jailbroken to add wallpapers, now that's built in.")
- [2012-12-08] **Why root — bleeding-edge control**: remove carrier bloatware, use root-only apps, back
  up/restore app data, theme, change the boot animation or the whole ROM, and "install a newer version of
  Android without waiting for your carrier to push it." The update-fragmentation frustration
  ([[2012-10-22-yt-y2d2tlnNYOg]]) as motivation to root.
- [2012-12-08] **How to root, taught simply**: Google "[your exact device model] + rooting instructions";
  he uses his own "Verizon Samsung Galaxy S3" as the worked example — confirming he's **still on Verizon**
  with the rooted S3 (the "leaving Verizon" was intent only).
- [2012-12-08] **Community as a safety net**: "if you have a device even slightly newer than the original
  Motorola Droid, there is a community behind that to back you up… and you can unroot." Credits **Adrian
  from XDA Developers**.
- [2012-12-08] **How to tell a guide is trustworthy — a verification heuristic**: "you can tell if a guide
  is trustworthy by reading the comments below it — if there are a lot of comments and success stories,
  chances are it's trustworthy." A practical, dated media-literacy tip.
- [2012-12-08] **Even-handed across the two ecosystems**: covers Android rooting and iOS jailbreaking
  equally — "it's really easy for both Android and iOS to get more control over your device."
- [2012-12-08] Cross-references the SuperNexus ([[2012-11-13-yt-RQy0NeVnxLo]]) as the payoff of rooting.
