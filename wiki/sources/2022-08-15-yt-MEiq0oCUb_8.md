# Dope Tech: The Most Extreme Gaming Monitor! (@mkbhd, 2022-08-15)

## Summary
A solo Dope Tech episode showcasing three gadgets: (1) the **Sharge (Shargeek) Storm 2 /
Storm 2 Slim** transparent power banks, which he says have replaced all his functional
battery banks because they are "the same thing but cooler," riding the 2022
transparent-tech moment (Nothing phone/earbuds); (2) the **Samsung Odyssey Ark** — a
55-inch, 1000R-curved, 165 Hz, 4K Quantum Mini-LED gaming monitor whose look Samsung
**sponsored** — which rotates 90° into a "towering" portrait "cockpit," shrinks via a
"Flex Move Screen" dial, and costs $3,500; and (3) the **Insta360 Link** gimbal webcam,
"the smartest webcam I've ever tried," which physically tracks you, does a pseudo desk
view, and snaps to a whiteboard on a peace-sign gesture. Recurring MKBHD frames here:
transparent tech as a design trend, and the comparison ladder that puts a phone's
Continuity Camera above any dedicated webcam. Solo video — all persona/voice data;
the Odyssey Ark segment is a disclosed paid sponsorship.

## Key claims
- [2022-08-15] "Most battery banks are basically the same thing… kind of boring"; the
  transparent Sharge Storm 2 line wins purely on being "the same thing but cooler," and
  "transparent tech is having a moment here in 2022" (citing the Nothing phone and
  earbuds). The Slim is 20,000 mAh / 100 W USB-C (enough to fully charge a MacBook Air)
  with a live nerdy readout screen; the larger one is 25,600 mAh; both are $200.
- [2022-08-15] The studio uses these power banks to run the MixPre-3 audio recorder
  because its battery compartment blocks the SD-card slot — a small behind-the-scenes
  production detail.
- [2022-08-15] The **Samsung Odyssey Ark** (55", corner-to-corner, 165 Hz, 1 ms, AMD
  FreeSync Premium Pro, four HDMI 2.1) is "pretty sure the only monitor in the world with
  that exact spec" and "in every way… ridiculous." A **1000R** curve = a 1000 mm radius;
  "the smaller the number the more pronounced the curve," and this one stretches into
  peripheral vision for "maximum immersion."
- [2022-08-15] Standout Ark features: the solar-powered wireless **Arc Dial** controller;
  a portrait-rotation "monstrosity" that can mimic three stacked 27-inch monitors (or
  "the world's best shorts or reels or TikTok monitor"); Flex Move Screen to shrink the
  panel to a 24/27/30-inch virtual monitor; four-corner speakers + two woofers for a
  built-in Dolby Atmos setup. Price: $3,500 — justified only as "kind of like having
  three or four monitors in one."
- [2022-08-15] The **Insta360 Link** (half-inch sensor, 4K30, phase-detect AF) is "easily
  miles better than any normal built-in webcam," and "a thousand percent faster and more
  responsive" at tracking than Apple's Center Stage crop-in. Its built-in gimbal enables
  a real desk view and a gesture-triggered whiteboard mode; it faces straight down when
  idle as a privacy feature. He calls it "the smartest webcam I've ever tried" and notes
  it makes sense that a camera company built it.
- [2022-08-15] Recurring comparison ladder: a dedicated webcam beats any built-in, but
  both the Insta360 Link and the Opal C1 are "like a step below using your literal iPhone
  as your webcam with continuity camera."
