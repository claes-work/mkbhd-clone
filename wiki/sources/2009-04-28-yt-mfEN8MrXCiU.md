# Reviewed: Adjustable Laptop Stand from HandStands (@mkbhd, 2009-04-28)

## Summary
The origin-era **full hardware review** (Marques, age 15) of the **HandStands "iDesk" laptop stand**
($39.99) he unboxed three days earlier — the stand he is actively using to shoot with. He notes it
**replaced his old NZXT Cryo LX** stand, apologizes repeatedly for **shaky camera work because he doesn't
own a tripod yet**, and does a feature rundown: raises the laptop to a typing angle (he recommends an
external keyboard as it only goes so low), a **detachable powered four-port USB 2.0 hub** (his favorite
feature), six vertical/tilt positions via a knob, sturdy plastic build, and foam pads. He flags a con
specific to his **HP Pavilion**: the front lip blocks the touchpad and sits over the fingerprint reader,
mic, and headphone jacks. He fumbles the "case" vs. "stand" wording several times and corrects himself.

## Key claims
- [2009-04-28] Review verdict: the **HandStands iDesk** is convenient and well-built (sturdy plastic, foam
  pads); the **detachable powered four-port USB 2.0 hub** is the standout feature ("I'm glad it has a USB hub").
- [2009-04-28] Named con: on an **HP Pavilion** the front lip **blocks the touchpad** and covers the
  fingerprint reader / mic / headphone jacks — a device-specific usability critique.
- [2009-04-28] Usability caveat: the lowest typing angle still favors an **external keyboard**.
- [2009-04-28] **No tripod yet**: he explicitly apologizes for shaky handheld camera work "I don't have a
  tripod yet" — a concrete, dated gap in his origin production kit.
- [2009-04-28] Gear history: this stand **replaced the NZXT Cryo LX** (which he had previously unboxed);
  shows the evolving desk/production setup.
