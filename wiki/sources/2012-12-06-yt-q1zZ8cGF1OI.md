# Retina Displays vs Battery Life! (@mkbhd, 2012-12-06)

## Summary
A "random, off-the-cuff, totally unedited" think-piece arguing that **the next big industry push should
be battery life**, not resolution. His frame: every recent flagship has a "wow" display, but "when's
the last time you saw a phone with a wow battery life?" — hardware has outrun the software/content, and
the one axis that hasn't advanced is the battery. It ties his display-primacy, "better not bigger," and
hardware-outran-software threads together into a single dated argument, and he cites his own 2011
battery rant.

## Key claims
- [2012-12-06] **The thesis — battery should be the next "wow"**: "I feel like I've wanted the next big
  thing to be **battery life**. Battery technology, batteries and mobile devices need to be the next big
  thing that's innovative… that's really what's needed." He wants the industry's yearly "next big push"
  (the thing CES orbits) to be battery.
- [2012-12-06] **Hardware has outrun software/content — the analytical spine**: "we've gone past our
  software with our hardware already. We've seen a lot of displays now that make us go *wow*." He lists a
  wall of "wow displays" (S3, Note 2, Droid DNA, One X, Nexus 4, Nexus 10) against the absence of a "wow
  battery." An early, dated statement of the tech-outran-the-ecosystem position the persona already holds.
- [2012-12-06] **The Apple-imitation dynamic, restated**: "when Apple does something really impressive,
  other companies tend to want to at least imitate or better that feature" — the iPhone 4's retina
  display kicking off the resolution race. The follow-the-leader industry pattern.
- [2012-12-06] **Cites his own 2011 battery rant** — a through-line: "I did a rant a really long time ago
  about battery technology and how it hasn't moved forward in a really long time." Explicitly connects to
  "better, not bigger" ([[2011-09-22-yt-XXySJoRe5zc]]).
- [2012-12-06] **A glimmer of hope named**: the Droid RAZR MAXX and Galaxy Note 2 as "two phones that
  actually have really big batteries and somewhat impressive wow battery lives" — but he wants
  *technology* progress, not just bigger cells (consistent with "better, not bigger").
- [2012-12-06] **Grounds it in audience demand**: "I hear people complaining about battery life all the
  time… the next big push that would really sell people should be battery life."
- [2012-12-06] **Another Oppo mention**: the "Oppo Find 5 is coming to the United States" with 1080p —
  continuing his Oppo tracking ([[2012-09-16-yt-1E6PDgIwFg4]]).
- [2012-12-06] **Format note**: "this is just a random, totally unedited video, I just felt like telling
  you guys" — a dated talk-to-camera format he floats as a style.
