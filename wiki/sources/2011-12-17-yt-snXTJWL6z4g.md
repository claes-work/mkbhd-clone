# All About the Galaxy Nexus Display (@mkbhd, 2011-12-17)

## Summary
A **single-feature deep dive** — an entire video on one component — opening with the
justification for the format: the display is "the most important and the most impressive
feature" because "it's what you use every time you use a phone." He demonstrates the OLED
black-pixel behaviour as a live test, works through gaming and media at 720p, covers typing
on a big screen two days after leaving a hardware keyboard behind, and closes by defending
the superlative against likely objections.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-17] **The display-first premise, stated as a principle**: the screen is "the most
  important and the most impressive feature… it's what you use every time you use a phone."
  The earliest clean statement in the corpus of the display-primacy view that defines his
  later reviewing.
- [2011-12-17] **OLED black-pixel demonstration and battery tip**: "every time that an OLED
  display has to display something black, it just doesn't light up the pixels at all… this is
  my little OLED display test where you can't even tell that the screen is on unless there are
  colors on it." Practical conclusion: dark apps and black backgrounds save battery.
- [2011-12-17] **Superlative restated and defended against objections**: "this is the best
  screen I have ever used. And you can argue that the HTC Rezound or the iPhone 4 and 4S or
  some other phones might have better quality displays — and I say just throw an OLED display
  in there with pitch blacks and you got yourself a hell of a screen." He names the
  counter-arguments rather than ignoring them, and answers with a stated preference for OLED
  contrast over raw panel quality.
- [2011-12-17] **Adaptation reported honestly with its timeframe**: "coming from a phone with a
  physical keyboard, it's only been about a day and I've already gotten used to it. So clearly
  they've done something right." The evidence is his own adjustment speed, and he says how long
  it took.
- [2011-12-17] **Resolution's mixed effect on games**: 1280×720 is a tablet-class resolution, so
  older games like Angry Birds "look a little bit tiny — you have to zoom in to play well,"
  while high-resolution games "look absurdly good." A benefit and its cost, stated together.
- [2011-12-17] **Media consumption named as the killer use**: "it's like having a movie in the
  palm of your hand… this by itself could be a reason to want the Galaxy Nexus. Forget Ice
  Cream Sandwich."
- [2011-12-17] Cross-reference discipline: he points to his earlier standalone video on why
  AMOLED displays are good rather than re-explaining the technology.
- [2011-12-17] Full-review deferral restated: it "will likely come after I've really thoroughly
  tested the phone," with the audience invited to request which parts to cover next.
