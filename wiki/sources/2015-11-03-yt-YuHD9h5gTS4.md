# Dope Tech #2: Customs! (@mkbhd, 2015-11-03)

## Summary
Episode 2 of Marques's "Dope Tech" series, focused on customized/personalized gear. He opens with the piece of tech he calls his favorite — the Sigma 18-35mm f/1.8 zoom lens (a filmmaking-gear aside continuing from Dope Tech #1's camera talk) — then moves to the episode's real theme: custom-colored devices in his signature red-and-black scheme. He shows a ColorWare-repainted 15-inch retina MacBook Pro (stealth matte black with a red hinge, used as his mobile editing rig), custom Sennheiser HD 800 headphones with hand-picked part colors, and dbrand vinyl skins as the cheaper, temporary alternative to a full custom paint job. Throughout he weighs craft, price, turnaround time, and the warranty/permanence trade-offs of each approach.

## Key claims
- [2015-11-03] The Sigma 18-35mm f/1.8 is Marques's favorite piece of tech he owns; he calls it a "one-of-a-kind" zoom that gets closer to prime-lens sharpness than any zoom he's seen, with top-notch build quality in line with the rest of the Sigma Art series.
- [2015-11-03] The lens combines specs "you literally do not see in any other zoom lens": an 18-35mm (wide-to-mid) range, a constant f/1.8 aperture across the whole zoom range, and a close ~11-inch minimum focus distance for near-macro shots; the image is sharp edge-to-edge.
- [2015-11-03] Roughly 75% of the shots in his last 50 videos went through that lens; it's the number-one lens he recommends when people ask about equipment. Available in Canon, Nikon and other mounts. Its one downside: crop-sensor only — he expects to miss it when he switches to a full-frame camera "in a few months."
- [2015-11-03] Red-and-black is one of his favorite color combos (alongside "pixels and dynamic range") and he likes to see it in tech when he can.
- [2015-11-03] His earlier MKBHD-edition 13-inch retina MacBook Pro (painted with logo replaced by ColorWare ~a year and a half prior) has held up fine — the paint aged well — but the laptop itself is now dated, so he sent in a fully loaded 15-inch retina MacBook Pro for the same stealth matte-black treatment with a red hinge, to serve as a mobile editing rig.
- [2015-11-03] ColorWare can now paint the trackpad too (no more silver trackpad sticking out), and does custom color work across many device types — laptops, the iMac, smartphones, accessories — with the new Apple keyboard and Magic Mouse expected soon.
- [2015-11-03] His headphones of choice are custom Sennheiser HD 800s; a normal pair is silver and black, but ColorWare's site lets you pick colors for each part (hinge, grill, etc.). His setup runs them into a Woo Audio WA7 Fireflies amp alongside a pair of Yamaha studio monitors.
- [2015-11-03] ColorWare trade-offs: not cheap and not fast — roughly 2 weeks to accept the shipment, disassemble, paint, and return it — and it typically voids the manufacturer warranty (ColorWare provides their own, but he wouldn't expect to take it to the Apple Store for repairs).
- [2015-11-03] A dbrand vinyl skin is the cheaper, less-permanent version of the ColorWare treatment — analogous to wrapping a car versus repainting it: temporary, much cheaper, and lets you change texture. It covers nearly the same device range (smartphones up to laptops and consoles). The skins are precisely cut, so the result comes down to the application process and a steady hand.
