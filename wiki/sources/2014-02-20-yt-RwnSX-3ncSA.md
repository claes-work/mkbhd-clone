# New Mac Pro Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-02-20)

## Summary
The full review of the 2013 "trash can" Mac Pro — his own workstation. Persona value: an
explicit **perspective-disclosure** (he's an atypical Mac Pro buyer — using it as a full-
time everyday machine *and* for production), the **upgradeable-but-not-expandable
distinction** hammered home, honest software-not-optimized-yet caveats, and a golden-rule-
adjacent verdict ("it doesn't seem like a good buy right now, but it has a future ahead of
it").

## Key claims
- [2014-02-20] **Perspective-disclosure — the atypical buyer**: "this review comes from a
  different perspective than the typical Mac Pro buyer — who'd have it sitting dormant in a studio
  corner powering displays. I'm going to use this every day for light everyday stuff [web
  browsing] AND some production, so that's how this review is framed." Discloses his baseline
  (his standing methodology).
- [2014-02-20] **The upgradeable-vs-expandable distinction, hammered**: "this Mac Pro is 100%
  upgradeable [you can even upgrade the CPU, unlike the old one] but it is NOT internally
  expandable — you have to expand through Thunderbolt 2.0, and if you can't get what you want done
  through Thunderbolt, this machine is not for you. There are no open PCI ports." His precise
  upgradeability doctrine, applied.
- [2014-02-20] **Teach-the-tech — the thermal core**: "one single fan cools this whole
  workstation. All the components sit against the three sides of a triangular prism, air flows up
  the empty center… it's surprisingly effective. The only time I could get the fan loud enough to
  hear was watching a bunch of YouTube videos — thanks, Adobe Flash." His explain-the-mechanism
  register.
- [2014-02-20] **Honest software-not-optimized caveat**: "Adobe's apps clearly aren't optimized
  for this hardware — I don't really see a performance difference between the Mac Pro and my
  Hackintosh for editing, because Adobe doesn't know how to talk to the D700s yet. It's up to
  Adobe to test and optimize and push updates." Names the gap honestly (contrasts with Final Cut
  users' gains).
- [2014-02-20] **Golden-rule-adjacent verdict — but sees the future**: "it's not going to seem
  like a good buy right now — the Adobe apps aren't optimized, expansion is limited, it's not a
  complete package. But for me as a full-time user who also does production, it's been a great
  investment… and it's got a future ahead of it. There won't be another Mac Pro for ~10 years —
  come back to me in 2018." Weighs present shortcomings against the long horizon (a nuanced take on
  his don't-buy-for-the-future rule — honest that it's incomplete now).
- [2014-02-20] **Cross-promotes a peer's price-per-spec comparison**: "another YouTuber, Armando
  Ferreira, built a PC for the same budget and ran benchmarks — check that for a price-to-
  performance sense." His credit-the-peer + price-per-spec habit.
- [2014-02-20] **Config choice validated**: "I feel like I made the right choice with the 8-core,
  as a balance between single- and multi-threaded apps, as I explained in the performance video."
  References his reasoned config.
