# Samsung Galaxy S8 Review: The Ultimate Smartphone? (@mkbhd, 2017-04-18)

## Summary
The full S8 review — "the ultimate smartphone? Almost." A recommend, built on the best display in any
smartphone and a stand-out design, with three honest knocks: the awkward fingerprint placement, **Bixby as a
swing and a miss**, and a battery Samsung "played safe" (post-Note-7). Notable for the Note-Edge-to-whole-
lineup lineage read and his glad-they-keep-doing-that appreciation of Samsung's boldness.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-04-18] **"The ultimate smartphone? Almost."** "Its hallmark headlining feature is how good it looks
  and how well it's designed. I love the build — pretty much everything about the outside is premium." The
  tall-display, thin-bezel design shrinks the footprint so the S8 "is a great size — even regular hands can
  reach across."
- [2017-04-18] **"The best display on any smartphone."** OLED, extremely bright, high-res (2960×1440) — "an
  awesome media/web/scrolling experience." The give-and-take of the tall aspect ratio (black bars on non-
  updated apps/videos, or punch-in and lose some frame) is "give a little, take a little — I'll take it."
- [2017-04-18] **"I legit dislike this location" (fingerprint reader).** "Even with my larger hands I reach
  up and over across the camera and often don't make it, or touch the camera glass, or only partially cover
  the reader. For most people it just makes it a two-handed phone." His workaround: a **dbrand skin** — better
  grip *and* the tiny depth difference lets you distinguish the reader from the camera by feel.
- [2017-04-18] **Bixby = "a swing and a miss" (verdict on the redundant assistant).** "I gave it a chance —
  didn't remap my Bixby button, used it a week. Voice isn't even active yet (rough start); the cards quickly
  devolve into a bunch of Samsung apps I don't use; it's working in catch-up mode to what Google already
  does. The camera image-recognition was Hit-or-Miss (thought a Bluetooth speaker was a baseball hat)." Notes
  Samsung **patched out button-remapping** — "understandably, people got angry." Fair-but-negative.
- [2017-04-18] **Camera: "one of the best you can put in your pocket" (slightly-updated sensor).** "We
  assumed it was the exact same as the S7, but it's a slightly updated sensor with newer optics — most of the
  difference is better image processing. Great detail, contrast, aggressive auto-HDR, rarely blows highlights.
  The best camera is the one you have with you." Processing-first camera read again.
- [2017-04-18] **Battery: Samsung "played it safe" (post-Note-7 read).** "This is Samsung, who had a phone
  literally start exploding last year when they pushed the battery — so I almost can't blame them. The 3,000
  mAh barely got me through the day; standby is poor (22% → 8% overnight), but it charges ridiculously fast
  (dead → 100% in about an hour). You pick your battles." Contextualizes the conservative choice.
- [2017-04-18] **The Note-Edge lineage + "glad they keep doing that stuff."** "This whole thing was born from
  the back-burner pet project that was the 2014 Galaxy Note Edge — now every phone has this edge display. You
  have to give Samsung credit for making a phone that stands out for how good it looks in an era where so many
  phones are accused of looking the same." Appreciation of the risk-taking (cf. "I love design gambles").
