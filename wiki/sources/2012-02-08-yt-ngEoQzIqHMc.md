# Chrome Beta for Android! (@mkbhd, 2012-02-08)

## Summary
Same-day coverage of Chrome Beta's arrival on Android 4.0, framed explicitly as a shareable
reference — "the definitive video to send your friends to." He tours the tab switching
(including the accelerometer-tilt stack), then argues the real case for Chrome is **sync**,
demonstrating cross-device continuity live by picking up a tab from his Transformer Prime on
his Galaxy Nexus and then verifying it in reverse. Both significant drawbacks are stated
plainly before the recommendation.

## Key claims
- [2012-02-08] **The case for Chrome is sync, not the interface**: signed in, "that'll be able to
  sync all your bookmarks, all your history, and all sorts of settings," plus password auto-sign-in
  — and the "single most important" feature, the **other devices** list.
- [2012-02-08] **Cross-device continuity demonstrated in both directions**: he opens a tab the
  Transformer Prime had open two minutes earlier on the phone, then switches to the tablet and
  confirms the phone's open tabs from the other side. A two-device live demo rather than a claim —
  the same show-don't-tell instinct documented throughout this era.
- [2012-02-08] **Both cons stated before the verdict**: no *request desktop site* option (so
  tablets get mobile layouts), and **no Adobe Flash support** — "if you were hoping to be able to
  use your desktop sites that were full of Adobe Flash stuff, that's not going to work." Notable
  because he had praised the stock browser's Flash handling in the Transformer Prime review
  seventeen days earlier ([[2012-01-22-yt-WBdJ6X1hp-U]]).
- [2012-02-08] **Tablet Chrome reads as a desktop browser**, and he anchors that judgement in his
  own usage: "it kind of resembles Chrome for Mac — actually, I can say that because **I use
  Chrome for Mac now**."
- [2012-02-08] **Dated personal-computing fact**: the "Chrome for Mac now" aside confirms he was
  by this point working on a Mac day-to-day, consistent with the Mac Mini build project begun
  three weeks earlier ([[2012-01-19-yt-eOBRNrn32tE]]) — a notable data point for a long-time PC
  builder and Android partisan.
- [2012-02-08] Interface notes: swipe left/right to change tabs; an accelerometer-driven tilt view
  of the open-tab stack (disableable in developer tools); swipe to dismiss a tab.
- [2012-02-08] **Recommendation scoped to the device tier**: "if anyone is looking forward to using
  such a very high-end tablet, you should go with a high-end browser."
- [2012-02-08] **Update commitment**: "I'll keep you updated if this is updated to a later version
  or if this changes significantly" — treating a beta as a moving target rather than a fixed
  verdict.
