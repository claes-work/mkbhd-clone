# Sony RX100 MKIII Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-08-16)

## Summary
A camera review of the **Sony RX100 III** — "**the best pocket camera ever made**" (a title he keeps
from the Mark II, on the reasoning "it still fits in my pocket and it's better than the last one"). Beyond
the review, it's a **creator-business** datum: he explicitly recommends the RX100 II/III as the **best
camera to start a YouTube channel** with (over a DSLR), and calls out the new flip-around LCD + wide fast
lens as ideal for vlogging. A window into his own gear taste and the advice he gives new creators.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-08-16] **"Best pocket camera ever made" — defined by its category.** The title is about being
  "the best camera I can fit in my pocket": it takes DSLR-adjacent images (near a Canon T5i + kit lens)
  because its sensor is much closer to DSLR size than other pocket cameras. "When you're comparing a
  pocket camera to DSLRs instead of other pocket cameras — that's how you can tell it's the best."
- [2014-08-16] **The three upgrades over the Mark I/II:** a pop-up **electronic viewfinder** (his one
  gripe: pushing it back in powers the camera off — "should have been a settings option"), a **fully
  flip-around LCD** (portrait/selfie/odd-angle shooting), and a new brighter **24–70 mm f/1.8–2.8 Zeiss
  lens** (a bit less reach than the Mark I, but a much lower aperture — "bests anything Sony's done").
- [2014-08-16] **Everything else stays great** — same 20 MP sensor, battery, build, easy Sony menus, the
  programmable control ring ("pretty genius, surprised no one else has done it"), the articulating pop-up
  flash for bounce fill, and NFC tap-to-transfer.
- [2014-08-16] **Vlogger-ready (even though he's not a vlogger)** — the wide lens + flip LCD + great
  codec make it "the highest-quality vlogs in the business… easy choice" for front-facing video.
- [2014-08-16] **Creator advice — start on this, not a DSLR.** "I would actually say go grab a Mark II or
  Mark III to start a YouTube channel instead of a DSLR" — autofocus, zoom, sharpness, a great codec, and
  60/30/24 fps + 720p120 — "you'll learn a lot about the kind of shooting you'll do later if you switch to
  a DSLR." (Not 4K, still 1080p.) Also notes the Fuji X100s takes DSLR-like images but "doesn't fit in my
  pocket, so it doesn't get the same prize."
