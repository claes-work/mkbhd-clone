# What's on my Smartphone 2016! (@mkbhd, 2016-02-21)

## Summary
A "what's on my phone" that documents his **two-SIM review workflow** and marks a biography milestone: **his
first year not a full-time student**. He runs a **primary SIM** (his real number) and a **secondary SIM**
(review phones only); when he's testing a phone, he moves everything except calls/texts to it, shoots on it,
and observes battery — and if it's "good enough," it gets promoted to his main phone with his main SIM.
Currently: main SIM in a Nexus 6P, secondary in the Nextbit Robin.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-02-21] **First year not a full-time student.** "I've done this video before, but I didn't do it over
  and over because what's on my phone was mostly the same through school. This is **the first year where I'm
  not a full-time student**, so my priorities have changed a little and the setup's a little different."
- [2016-02-21] **The two-SIM review workflow (how he tests phones).** "I have two AT&T SIMs — a primary
  (the number everyone has) and a secondary (nobody has it, just for review phones). When I review a phone,
  I move all communications except texting/calling to it, take photos and observe battery on it — and **if
  it's good enough, it moves up to become my main phone and gets my main SIM.** Repeat every time an
  interesting phone comes out." Right now: main SIM in the **Nexus 6P**, secondary in the **Nextbit Robin**.
- [2016-02-21] **Clean stock setup, minimal by design.** "Most metal phones don't really need a skin, but I
  like the way this one looks." Google Launcher, near-stock — "the better the stock apps get, the less I
  rely on third-party stuff. I keep it simple and clean since I have to quickly set it up when I move to a
  new device." (Ties to his switches-phones-often habit.)
