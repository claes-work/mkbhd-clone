# Dope Tech: STILL so good! (@mkbhd, 2025-11-21)

## Summary
A **"Dope Tech Revisited"** episode — the first Dope Tech in over a year — where every item is a
**successor to a gadget he previously featured** on the series, so he can judge whether it's still
dope. Marques notes he's made **30+ Dope Techs** showing **100+ products**. Four revisits:
**Logitech MX Master 4** (which he frames as the "Porsche 911 of computer mice"), the **Devialet
Phantom Ultimate** speaker, the **Samsung Galaxy Tab S11 Ultra** (the recurring "iPad killer"),
and the **Nikon ZR** — the first camera from the **Nikon–RED merger**, which he treats as a rare
acquisition that actually benefits consumers. Contains a notable production-craft aside about how
**MKBHD videos are shot manual-focus on RED** and his tooling philosophy. Solo → persona/voice
data. Disclosed **Google/Best Buy** (Holiday 100) sponsor mid-roll.

## Key claims
- [2025-11-21] **Series meta:** first Dope Tech in "more than a year"; he's made **30+ episodes**
  and shown **100+ products**. Format for this one: "Dope Tech Revisited" — every item is a
  follow-up to an older version previously shown on the series.
- [2025-11-21] **Logitech MX Master 4** — he's used the line since the original (and the earlier
  "Performance MX"); the MXM 3 was "the most replayed part" of a Dope Tech 6 years ago. New: an
  all-new **recycled plastic finish** (fixes the old rubberized coating that wore/got sticky), a
  higher horizontal scroll wheel (good for video editing), silent clicks, a new customizable
  front button, and the big addition — a **haptic motor** giving a convincing click on a
  non-moving silicon gesture area, configurable in **Logitech Options+** ("a fantasy land … like a
  little Grand Theft Auto weapon select but for your computer mouse").
- [2025-11-21] **"Porsche 911 of computer mice" framework:** a product that barely changes
  silhouette generation to generation, keeps its iconic shape, iterates "to the nth degree," and
  is the company's most important product so they won't change it much — "the ideal enthusiast
  daily driver," still $120 with a modest **120 Hz polling rate** (vs 8,000 Hz gaming mice) because
  "it's not a gaming mouse … just a rock-solid, pricey, but ideal daily." Now has a USB-C dongle.
- [2025-11-21] **Devialet Phantom Ultimate** — previously covered the original and Phantom 2. New
  108 dB (big) and 98 dB (small) versions with new drivers/circuitry; ~25 lb each; app adds modes
  + stereo pairing. Verdict: sounds great, huge bass, "mad" stereo separation, but "not a ton of
  change, still very expensive, and not as hype as they used to be." Fun aside: he keeps spotting
  the older ones in **Newark airport kiosks**.
- [2025-11-21] **Samsung Galaxy Tab S11 Ultra** — revisits the recurring **iPad-killer** rivalry
  (last covered the Tab S7+ in 2020, its highest-viewed part). Specs: 14.6" 3K AMOLED (bigger/
  higher-res than any iPad Pro), **5.1 mm thin** ("thinner than the iPad Pro, thinner than the
  iPhone Air"), 11,600 mAh battery, up to 16 GB RAM, microSD, up to 1 TB, new S Pen, from $1,200
  ($100 less than the big iPad Pro). Emphasizes Samsung's **window-management software** (arbitrary
  window sizes, saved desktop layouts, phone-call linking) keeping pace with iPadOS 26. Verdict:
  iPad got better, but so did these — "still dope tech," a viable alternative/killer for some.
- [2025-11-21] **Nikon ZR** — the payoff of **Nikon buying RED**. Frames it as the rare acquisition
  "that ends with everyone being happy other than people getting paid": Nikon (distant third to
  Canon/Sony in mirrorless) + RED (premium cinema, never affordable) = a **full-frame mirrorless**
  with 24 MP stacked sensor, Z-mount, 6K60, 7.5 stops IBIS, 15 stops DR, shoots **R3D (NE,
  compressed RAW)**, 4" swiveling touchscreen, ~$2,200 — an **FX3 competitor** ($4,000) rather than
  an R5 competitor. "Immediately … the smallest RED camera, and … the RED camera probably with the
  best autofocus by a mile." Quirks/asterisks: R3D NE (not identical), weak ergonomics, no EVF, no
  top handle, small grip, micro-HDMI, one CFexpress-B + a microSD slot.
- [2025-11-21] **Production-craft / tooling philosophy (persona-relevant):** "so many videos are
  actually shaped by the tools in subtle ways." **MKBHD videos are shot mostly on RED cameras and
  are therefore all manual-focus all the time** because RED "[doesn't] really have very good
  autofocus." Philosophically he thinks it should be the reverse — **the tool should enable
  whatever creative thing you want to make** — so he's drawn to flexible cameras he can shoot
  anywhere that still look great: "That's like the dream."

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "I feel like this is becoming like the Porsche 911 of computer mice ... It keeps the same silhouette, the iconic shape that everyone knows. But each time they improve a little bit and iterate over and over to the nth degree."

> "It's kind of like this little Grand Theft Auto weapon select but for your computer mouse."

> "So many videos are actually shaped by the tools in subtle ways ... Like MKBHD videos, for example, because I shoot them on mostly RED cameras, are all manual focus all the time because they don't really have very good autofocus. But philosophically, I feel like it should be the other way around. The tool should enable whatever creative thing you want to make."

> "In the long, long history of people's favorite tech companies getting purchased, how many of those are a story that ends with everyone being happy other than people getting paid? Not very many."

> "It is a result of two companies merging into one and the consumer actually getting a win out of it. I feel like that never happens."
