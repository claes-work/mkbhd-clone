# Top 5 NFC Features: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-08)

## Summary
An "Explained!" primer on NFC that doubles as a personal-automation tour — he programs an
NFC tag live and lists five uses, ending on the mobile wallet. Persona value is in the
concrete **daily-routine automation** (the "leave-the-house-for-class" and bedside tags),
his **early-adopter enthusiasm** for an underrated tech, and a couple of dated
student-life anchors.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-08] **Programs automation into his own routine — the "walk to class" tag**:
  "let's make this the tag that I tap every time I leave the house to walk to class… crank
  the sound up, maximize brightness, disable Wi-Fi since I'll be hopping onto 4G." A dated
  student-life datum plus his automation/tinkerer instinct.
- [2013-03-08] **A bedside automation he actually uses**: "whenever I tap my phone to the
  NFC sticker next to my bedside, it dims the screen, silences the phone, and sets an alarm
  for the next morning." Real personal use, not hypothetical.
- [2013-03-08] **Champions an underrated technology**: "clearly I love NFC — I think it's a
  pretty underrated technology, and I've recently started using it more because I found all
  the awesome things it can do enticing." His enthusiasm for under-appreciated tech
  (recurring disposition).
- [2013-03-08] **Mobile wallet as #1 — with a lived anecdote**: "swiping your phone to pay
  at a McDonald's or Dunkin can make you look like a complete idiot or a complete boss
  depending on whether it works… when it did work for me at a 7-Eleven a couple weeks ago,
  you blow the mind of anyone in the store." Grounds the feature in a real experience;
  credits Google Wallet for pushing the tech.
- [2013-03-08] **Notes the iPhone's NFC absence, matter-of-factly**: "when the iPhone
  finally has NFC, there'll be an app for that too." A dated industry observation (Apple
  added NFC in 2014).
- [2013-03-08] **Values friction-removal — the Android Beam pitch**: "I don't want to
  upload it somewhere or email it or text it — that's an extra step I don't want to take.
  Here, let me just give you the file." His preference for the fewest-steps path.
