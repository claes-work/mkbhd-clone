# Google Pixelbook: $1000 of Weird! (@mkbhd, 2017-11-09)

## Summary
A look at the $1,000 Pixelbook — "really weird to me": an amazing piece of hardware that's **even harder to
recommend than the Chromebook Pixel** because of Chrome OS's limits at that price. A clean instance of his
who-is-this-for / beautiful-hardware-you-probably-shouldn't-buy framing.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-11-09] **"$1,000 of weird" — amazing hardware, even-harder-to-recommend.** "I loved the Chromebook
  Pixel, but I was careful to only recommend it to a small group who knew what they were getting into. The
  Pixelbook, while an *amazing* piece of hardware, is even harder to recommend to an even smaller group,
  because of all its weirdness — a $1,000 high-end touchscreen 2-in-1 running Chrome OS." The Chromebook-Pixel
  "beautiful hardware you should never buy" halo framing, intensified.
- [2017-11-09] **Genuinely admires the hardware.** "Aesthetically it's really nice — takes after the Pixel
  phones with the glass window; all-metal, angular, boxy, and **super thin (I don't think I've held a laptop
  this thin)** — feels more like a thick tablet than a thin laptop. The soft palm rest is cool, minimal
  fingerprints, a sturdy minimal hinge." Fair about what's excellent.
- [2017-11-09] **Beefy specs + sensible I/O — that would justify $1,000 *if not for Chrome OS*.** "7th-gen
  Core i5, 8 GB RAM, 128 GB (up to i7 / 16 GB / a half-TB NVMe — nuts); a crispy 12" 4:3 2400×1600 touchscreen;
  fanless, all solid-state; two USB-C (either charges) + a headphone jack. The hardware wouldn't make you
  blink twice at $1,000 — [but the OS is the catch]." Separates the hardware value from the software limit.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "This thing is really weird to me… while it is an amazing piece of hardware, [it] is even harder to
> recommend to an even smaller group of people because of all its weirdness."
> "It's super thin — I don't even think I've ever held a laptop this thin before; it feels more like a thick
> tablet than a thin laptop."
