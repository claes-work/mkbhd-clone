# Review: BitFenix Survivor (@mkbhd, 2010-11-22)

## Summary
Review of the BitFenix Survivor mid-tower PC case, pitched at portable/LAN-party
builders (fits Micro-ATX and standard ATX boards). Covers build quality (matte
finish, durable), front I/O (2× USB 3.0, 2× USB 2.0, audio), a top carry handle, and
a con — the top/bottom/side panels need a Phillips screwdriver to remove for
internal access. Closes with an unqualified recommendation but no numeric score, and
teases an upcoming build series.

## Key claims
- [2010-11-22] **Unqualified recommendation without a numeric score**: "I would have
  absolutely no problem recommending the BitFenix Survivor to anyone who is a mobile
  gamer or who just values a good sized small case" — another origin-era instance of
  closing on a qualitative verdict rather than a score (see also
  [[wiki/sources/2010-11-08-yt-e14rw4Ap70w]]); the origin-era scoring ladder is
  evidently not applied to every review.
- [2010-11-22] **Names a specific con tied to serviceability**: "if you ever want to
  do any major upgrades you're really going to have to sit down with this case and
  unscrew a couple of things" — the case's cited weakness is the tool requirement for
  panel removal, not build quality.
- [2010-11-22] **First mention of an upcoming "how to build a computer" series**:
  "I'm not using the BitFenix Survivor for my computer build in an upcoming how to
  build a computer series" — teases a new planned content format, distinct from his
  standard single-product review/unboxing structure.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
