# Top 3 Battery Saving Tips! (@mkbhd, 2012-07-26)

## Summary
A practical battery-saving explainer built on one diagnosis: **the display is almost always the
biggest battery drain**, so most real savings come from managing the screen. He opens by killing the
myth of a magic fix (consistent with his tech-myths video), works through radios and CPU, then lands
on the display with brightness/timeout tips and the OLED-black trick. Notable for connecting his
battery-life advice to his own multi-device usage.

## Key claims
- [2012-07-26] **No magic fix — the honest opener**: "there's no magical cure or magical thing that you
  can do… there's nothing crazy you can do to get an extra million and a half hours out of it." Same
  anti-myth stance as [[2012-06-16-yt-CultO7cPnJA]] (task-killers) and [[2011-12-28-yt-hrcbcm11830]].
- [2012-07-26] **The core diagnosis — the display is the drain**: "you will almost always find that the
  number one battery killer on tablets, on smartphones, even on Wi-Fi-only laptops… is the display.
  Keeping a huge panel backlit for that long… takes the most battery out of anything. Your screen is what
  is killing your battery — I can almost guarantee that." The organizing principle, verified via
  Android's built-in per-app battery stats.
- [2012-07-26] **Display tactics, specific**: turn off aggressive auto-brightness in favor of manual
  ("auto tends to be a little aggressive… keeps it below what you think it should be"); shorten screen
  timeout to 10–15 seconds ("saves a lot more battery than people think").
- [2012-07-26] **The OLED-black trick restated**: on an OLED phone like the Galaxy S3, "all the pixels
  that are black are not lit up, so if you use a black background and **black-themed** applications — not
  gray, gray doesn't help — those pixels don't get lit up and you'll be saving a lot of battery."
  Consistent with the 2011 OLED demo ([[2011-12-17-yt-snXTJWL6z4g]]).
- [2012-07-26] **Radios ranked by draw**: 4G > 3G > Wi-Fi; keep a 4G phone on home Wi-Fi to save a lot;
  disable Bluetooth/GPS when unused. A general rule of thumb rather than a magic setting.
- [2012-07-26] **CPU wake-locks named as a "silent behind-the-scenes killer"**, with the Transformer
  Infinity's performance modes (high 1.7 / balanced 1.4 / saver ~1 GHz) as the worked example — the
  adaptive-profiles idea from his Android wishlist ([[2012-05-26-yt-haOpXNVb_9Q]]) shown in a shipping
  device.
- [2012-07-26] **The spare-device tip, from his own behavior**: "ever since I've gotten my Nexus 7 and I
  just use it around the house for checking Twitter or Maps… I notice my phone ends the day with 35–45%
  battery without any attempt to conserve. A spare device is like a spare battery." Advice grounded in
  his own multi-device usage.
- [2012-07-26] **Honest platform caveat — most of this is Android-only**: "if you're on an iPhone you can
  do almost none of the things I just said… the only thing I'd suggest is turning off auto-brightness."
  He scopes his own advice to platforms rather than pretending it generalizes.
- [2012-07-26] Recommends the app **Battery Widget Reborn** ("don't snicker at that") as a shortcut to
  Android's battery-usage screen.
