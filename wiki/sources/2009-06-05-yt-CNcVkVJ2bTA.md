# Reviewed: DS International Wireless Ergonomic Keyboard with Touchpad (@mkbhd, 2009-06-05)

## Summary
The review half of the DS International keyboard two-step (unboxing:
[[2009-05-12-yt-J6EgW9iaFM8]]). Two firsts for the corpus. (1) **Comparative framing inside a
review**: he opens by explicitly measuring this keyboard against the Matias he reviewed nine
days earlier ([[2009-05-27-yt-b7Nc78QqDs0]]) — "I did review a keyboard not too long ago and
it was not really a full-featured keyboard, but this one has a lot of features" — and returns
to the comparison for the number-pad/numlock layout at the end. (2) A **quantified break-in
claim**: "it only takes about three or four days before you can really type smoothly with
this without making mistakes," extending the softer "you do have to break in the keyboard"
of the Matias review into a number. He also states his content strategy outright: "I do
alternate back and forth between software and hardware videos." ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-05] **Comparison to his own previous review**: the Matias was "not really a full
  featured keyboard... but this one has a lot of features for me to go over."
- [2009-06-05] **Quantified break-in period**: "it does take a little bit of time to break
  into this keyboard... it only takes about 3 or 4 days before you can really type smoothly
  with this without making mistakes."
- [2009-06-05] **Key-feel vocabulary continues**: "you can hear that travel; it's not really a
  thick keyboard, the keys are not all that thick... it's a nice-sounding keyboard" — sound
  and travel treated as review criteria, as in the Matias review.
- [2009-06-05] **Setup narrated including his own failure**: "it took me like three tries to
  figure this out" — the power switch on the far side of the keyboard. He leaves his own
  fumble in rather than cutting it.
- [2009-06-05] **Wireless mechanics**: batteries in the back, USB receiver stored in the
  keyboard, "plugs into your computer, blinks a few times, sets up the drivers and it's
  plug-and-play"; height-adjustment feet.
- [2009-06-05] **Touchpad assessed with a caveat**: "a very sensitive touchpad — I found I had
  to do a little tinkering with the sensitivity... it worked almost as well as an actual mouse
  or the touchpad on the laptop."
- [2009-06-05] **Use case reasoned out, not just listed**: "say you're on a netbook and the
  touchpad won't be much bigger than this, you can actually just attach this to your netbook" —
  he identifies who the product is for.
- [2009-06-05] **Feature inventory**: internet and email-client buttons, calculator,
  back/forward, FN lock, num lock, caps lock, a **low-battery indicator** he singles out ("so
  if you ever are running low on battery you'll know that before you run out in the middle of
  an essay"), FN-mapped media/system actions along the top row (F12 → Flip 3D, F6 → eject),
  volume and transport controls, and left/right click buttons.
- [2009-06-05] **Cross-link to his own earlier video**: the media-center key is "the same
  feature as this green button on the top right hand corner of this remote which I showed you
  guys" — continuity with the Media Center remote videos.
- [2009-06-05] **Honesty about an unidentified control**: "there is — I'm not sure what that
  one is actually."
- [2009-06-05] **Layout compared, with an analogy**: no number pad; instead numlock with
  numbers "written in rows here, kind of like the BlackBerry."
- [2009-06-05] **Reliability verdict from use**: "the wireless has not been a problem for me,
  it's never disconnected randomly or anything like that; driver setting up was a breeze," and
  "I think I will definitely like using this keyboard." Again **no numeric score**.
- [2009-06-05] **Stated content strategy**: "if you have any software ideas or requests feel
  free to subscribe and message me through YouTube, cuz I do alternate back and forth between
  software and hardware videos."
- [2009-06-05] **Production self-awareness**: "I'll try to zoom in even more, though it's not
  going to be the most focused in the world" — he names his camera's macro limits on air.
