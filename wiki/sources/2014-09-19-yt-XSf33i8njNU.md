# iPhone 6 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-19)

## Summary
First impressions of the iPhone 6 — dominated by his **anti-thinness thesis**: the 6.9 mm body is
"strikingly light" but pays for it with a **protruding camera lens** (won't sit flat, first thing to
scratch) and **a small battery again**. "I don't think I can possibly be the only one who would rather
just have a slightly thicker iPhone 6 so the camera doesn't stick out and the battery is bigger." He also
notes the rounded metal makes it **slipperier** (foreshadowing BendGate), and that the display is a
modest bump (1334×750, ~same ppi as the 5S). A dated, canonical statement of the thin-obsession critique.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-19] **The dummy was accurate** — the retail iPhone 6 matches the silver dummy he covered
  earlier "almost to a tee" (ports, camera, flash, even the antenna paint lines he'd doubted).
- [2014-09-19] **The anti-thinness thesis (canonical).** Strikingly light (129 g) and thin (6.9 mm), but
  "all of this comes with the trade-off of number one a small battery again, and number two the
  protruding camera lens" — which won't sit flat, is the first thing to scratch, and "just looks unsightly.
  I'd rather just have a slightly thicker iPhone 6 so the camera doesn't stick out and the battery is
  bigger. Maybe iPhone 6s."
- [2014-09-19] **Rounded metal = slippery** (like the HTC One M8) — "a lot easier to slip or fall or
  drop without a case," vs the grippable flat edge of the 5S. (Foreshadows the BendGate video.)
- [2014-09-19] **Display is a modest improvement** — 4.7", "retina HD" 1334×750 ("just a little above
  720p, same pixel density as the 5S"); a great, bright Apple panel, but with bigger bezels than a Moto X
  (Apple needs room for the home button + matching forehead/chin).
- [2014-09-19] **iOS 8 runs smoothly** — "the first version of iOS to not stutter when I pull down into
  Spotlight." Visually near-identical to iOS 7 ("just another iPhone with a bigger screen").
- [2014-09-19] **Verdict-discipline:** it's impressions — battery and camera "I'm going to be testing a
  lot" for the full review; the 6 (not the 6 Plus) is "the one most current iPhone users are going to
  upgrade to." He'll daily-drive it.
