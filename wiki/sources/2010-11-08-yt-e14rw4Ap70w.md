# Review: Monster Jamz High Performance Earphones (@mkbhd, 2010-11-08)

## Summary
Review of Monster's "Mobile Jamz" in-ear headphones, provided by Monster Cable. Opens
with a general spending-priority statement about not skimping on headphones, then
covers build (metal earbuds, no anti-tangle cable, right/left color-coding),
durability (referencing Monster's own tractor-durability demo), sound signature
(strong bass, praised mids/highs), and closes with a comfort caveat for active use.

## Key claims
- [2010-11-08] **General spending-priority opinion, not product-specific**: "if you're
  going to go out and spend over $100... on an MP3 player... why on Earth would you
  stick with the standard earbuds that come with it" — an explicit value judgment that
  headphone spend should scale with the source device's cost, framed as general advice
  rather than a claim specific to this product.
- [2010-11-08] **Use-case-qualified verdict, not a blanket recommendation**: recommends
  the earbuds "when you're just sitting down and listening to music" but explicitly
  would not suggest them "for walking around" since they loosened their seal during
  movement — a conditional, activity-specific recommendation rather than an
  unqualified thumbs up.
- [2010-11-08] **Cites the manufacturer's own durability marketing as corroborating
  evidence**: references that Monster "ran over these with a tractor" in their own
  promotional test as support for the earbuds' durability, rather than independently
  verifying it himself.
