# ThinkGeek Unboxing (@mkbhd, 2009-05-25)

## Summary
A short origin-era unboxing/first-impressions video of a multi-product review sample
package from **ThinkGeek** (thinkgeek.com): a security-style mirror and an outdoor window
LCD thermometer. Two things make it worth a page: it is a dated instance of a **retailer
sending unsolicited samples specifically for review coverage** at ~900 subscribers, and it
is one of the first packages containing **several products at once**, which he immediately
splits into separate promised review videos of different depth. Not an L3-candidate on its
own.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-25] **Samples supplied for review**: "the kind folks at ThinkGeek... were kind
  enough to send me a few samples actually for a review video." He links the vendor in the
  description.
- [2009-05-25] **Multi-product package → per-product review plan**: the mirror gets "probably
  the quickest review video I've ever done," the window thermometer "will probably be a
  much more detailed review video" — review length allocated by how much there is to say,
  an early instance of proportional coverage.
- [2009-05-25] **Products**: a mirror ("kind of like security mirror") and an **outdoor
  window thermometer** — "a large LCD that mounts to the outside of a glass window but
  projects the actual information inside."
- [2009-05-25] **Intended real use, not staged**: "I'm probably just going to put one right
  outside the window outside my desk" — he plans to actually live with the review unit.
- [2009-05-25] **Review criteria pre-announced**: for the thermometer he promises to show
  "exactly what it entails, how you can use it, and how accurate it is" — usage plus an
  accuracy check.
- [2009-05-25] **Packaging segment kept honest**: marked "fragile / handle with care,"
  shipped "relatively quickly"; the box proves hard to open, so he narrates cutting it, and
  deduces from the rattle that "it doesn't fill up the entire box."
- [2009-05-25] **Format label used explicitly**: he calls it an "unboxing and first
  impressions video" three separate times — the format name is by now fixed vocabulary.
- [2009-05-25] **Buyer-facing sign-off**: "hopefully you'll be able to enjoy them if you do
  decide to pick them up."
