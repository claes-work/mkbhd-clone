# HTC One X Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-05-13)

## Summary
A full review of the (international, quad-core) HTC One X — "HTC's attempt to throw absolutely
everything they can at a single device." Build, display, the Tegra 3's real clocking behavior, the
Sense UI skin, and the camera. Notable for a **restated-but-tolerant stock-Android preference** and
for using a diagnostic app to report how the processor *actually* behaves rather than quoting peak
specs — the measurement-honesty instinct applied to a phone SoC.

## Key claims
- [2012-05-13] **Build quality is the headline**: "precision-milled unibody polycarbonate design, a very
  thin slightly curved body, a matte finish… an overall pretty sexy design," with milled speaker holes,
  one of which houses the notification light ("a subtle touch").
- [2012-05-13] **Performance reported from observed behavior, not peak numbers** — the method move: he
  runs CPU Spy to show the Tegra 3 "can clock all the way down to 51 MHz but spends most of its time at
  475 MHz, and when it really needs to push some pixels it hauls up to 1.4 and sometimes 1.5 GHz."
  Measuring the actual operating envelope rather than citing the 1.5 GHz headline.
- [2012-05-13] **Stock-Android preference restated — but tolerant of the skin**: "I happen to prefer
  stock Android, but hey, if you like Sense UI or if you don't mind it, that's totally cool. It just
  helps a lot if you already know what you're getting yourself into." Consistent with his own move to a
  custom ROM ([[2012-04-16-yt-fFRl2oOqDsk]]): control/preference stated, other choices respected.
- [2012-05-13] **Display verdict with an honest limitation**: the 4.7" 720p Super LCD is "topnotch" for
  detail and brightness, but "the blacks aren't as ink black as the Samsung OLED displays" — he won't
  overclaim it against his own display-primacy favorite (the Galaxy Nexus AMOLED).
- [2012-05-13] **A concrete UI criticism**: no dedicated menu button, so "when an app needs to have a
  menu button, you end up with this giant menu button at the bottom, which is kind of sad… it is what it
  is." A specific, dated complaint about the ICS on-screen-button transition.
- [2012-05-13] **Camera recommended specifically**: 8 MP, 3.3 fps burst, 1080p video — "a very very
  high-end camera for a smartphone… I can definitely recommend this if you're looking for a high-end
  camera."
- [2012-05-13] **Size judged from his own reference point**: "it doesn't feel that large to me at all —
  that may be because I'm coming from the size of the Galaxy Nexus, which I'm very used to" — the
  disclosed-baseline habit applied to ergonomics.
- [2012-05-13] **Sets up the next video explicitly**: "give this review a thumbs up if you want to see it
  compared to Samsung's quad-core flagship, the Galaxy S3" — programming the S III comparison by demand.
