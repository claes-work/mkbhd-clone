# Smartphone Awards 2020! (@mkbhd, 2020-12-23)

## Summary
Marques's annual year-end Smartphone Awards — the video he calls "probably my favorite video to do every year." Solo, hosted in front of a table full of 2020 phones, he hands out category winners (plus runners-up and honorable mentions) that together form his verdict on the smartphone year. Notable structural note: he observes that other YouTubers have started copying the awards-show format ("actually kind of flattering") and that the category timestamps are in the description. The overarching 2020 story he tells: the breakout of a "premium mid-range" ~$700 tier as the most exciting new development, and a general wish for more matte (non-fingerprint) finishes. Full winner list captured in Key claims. The MVP / phone-of-the-year goes to the Samsung Galaxy S20 FE as the emblem of that premium mid-range breakout, explicitly calling it "more of a flagship killer than a lot of the other stuff that's claimed to be over the years."

## Key claims
- [2020-12-23] Best big phone: **Xiaomi Mi 10 Ultra** — "the 120 phone" (120x zoom, 120Hz display, 120W charging, ~40% in 5 min, the fastest charging he'd seen), China-only (import asterisk). Runners-up: Galaxy Note 20 Ultra, Asus ROG Phone 3. (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Best compact phone: **iPhone 12 mini** — a full flagship shrunk down, representing "a little mini rebirth" of compact flagships. Runner-up: Galaxy Z Flip (folds smaller than the mini) — "big phone in a small body." (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Best camera: **iPhone 12 Pro Max** — "reclaimed their crown," best all-round photo+video versatility; ProRAW (added via software update in the prior weeks) "took it to another level." Runners-up: Pixel 5 (stills he often prefers — "if I had to only take one for stills photos I would take Pixel 5"), Asus Zenfone 7 Pro (2020 Blind Smartphone Camera Test winner, swivel selfie camera). (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Best battery: **Asus ROG Phone 3** — 6,000mAh + software optimization, two days even at 144Hz; "this phone just won't die." Honorable mentions: Poco M3, Samsung M51 (7,000mAh, India-only, sub-$500). (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Design award: **Galaxy Note 20 Ultra** — "easily the most subjective award this year," won on hardware/finish (satin back, boxy, flattened display, laser AF fixed the S20 Ultra's issues). His criteria this year: wanted a matte option, disliked screens curving too far over the sides, disliked ugly camera bumps. Extras: favorite back of all time = Huawei P40 Pro (soft-touch iridescent); best hinge = Microsoft Surface Duo; favorite crazy design = Xiaomi Mi Mix Alpha (wraparound display). (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Best budget phone: **Google Pixel 4a** — $349, world-class camera, clean software, headphone jack; "easily one of the best phones to recommend anywhere under 400 bucks." Runner-up: iPhone SE — but he "can't give a winner of best budget phone to a phone that got rid of the headphone jack out of principle." Also OnePlus Nord. (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Biggest bust: **Samsung Galaxy Note 20** — "wasn't that bad of a phone, it just had a horrific launch strategy and a terrible price"; $1,000 with a plastic back, 1080p 60Hz display, non-expandable storage — "the Nope 20." (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Most improved: **Galaxy Z Fold 2** — "the epitome of 2nd gen"; huge front + inner display upgrades. Honorable mention: Pixel 5, which "didn't really actually get better" (arguably worse) but "was much better marketed and it landed the right way." (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] MVP / phone of the year: **Samsung Galaxy S20 FE** — an award for its impact on and nailing of the new ~$700 premium mid-range tier; virtually indistinguishable from a flagship (120Hz OLED, SD865, IP68, 5G, wireless charging, 4500mAh); "this phone's launch made the Galaxy Note 20 look stupid." MVP honorable mentions: OnePlus 8 Pro (his steadiest phone all year) and iPhone 12 Pro (his other pocket). (Marques)
- [2020-12-23] Recurring MKBHD taste markers restated: preference for boxy/flat designs over curved-edge screens; dislike of glossy fingerprint-magnet finishes; the "flagship killer" concept; using his own Blind Smartphone Camera Test as a reference point. (Marques)
