# The iPhone 8 Model! (@mkbhd, 2017-08-07)

## Summary
A hands-on with a physical dummy of the next iPhone (built from leaks, the kind case-makers use). The
clone-relevant beats: his first real look at **the notch** (the top-sensor protrusion), reading it as a
milestone in **fixing the iPhone's long-standing screen-to-body-ratio weakness**, and the open question of
what Apple does with the software in the "ears."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-07] **First read of the notch — "still looks a little funky, but interesting."** "The display has
  awesome edge-to-edge action, but an interesting top-sensor protrusion coming down from the top (leaked via
  the HomePod firmware silhouette). In the glyph it looked weird; in real life it still looks a little funky,
  but the way the display wraps around that bar with rounded edges everywhere is really interesting. Reminds
  me of the Essential Phone renders." A measured first-notch reaction.
- [2017-08-07] **The real story: fixing the iPhone's screen-to-body-ratio weakness.** "Screen-to-body ratio
  has always been a weakness of the iPhone — it's 2017, bezels are shrinking, and the iPhone has had a massive
  forehead, chin, and side bezels forever. The 7 Plus had a 5.5" display but was one of the biggest phones."
  Frames the redesign as long-overdue catch-up on the bezel trend.
- [2017-08-07] **The open software question.** "My biggest question is what Apple does with the software up
  there — battery to one corner, signal to the other, notifications in the middle? Whatever they do, it'll be
  interesting." Reads the hardware for its software implications (a characteristic move).
- [2017-08-07] **The renders-lie caveat applied to himself.** Notes this is a dummy, not the phone — the
  usual reserve about judging from mockups (the phone tends to look better than the render).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "It still looks a little bit funky, but now that you see how the display wraps around that bar with the
> rounded edges everywhere, it still looks really interesting."
> "Screen-to-body ratio — this has always been a weakness of the iPhone. It's 2017. Bezels are shrinking."
