# Samsung Galaxy Note Edge Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-03)

## Summary
A quick impressions video on the **Galaxy Note Edge** — a Note 4 with a curved 160-px strip of display
down the right edge. Marques frames it as another of **"Samsung's crazy 'let's try everything' phones,"**
and applies his **innovation-worth-trying** lens: the original Note looked crazy too and is now
mainstream, so "maybe this looks crazy now but might look totally normal in the future." He's honest that
he **genuinely doesn't know how he feels** even after hands-on — a dated instance of leaving the
uncertainty in rather than faking a verdict.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-03] **It's a Note 4 plus an edge.** Identical internals/software/camera to the Note 4, plus a
  curved 160-px "edge screen" strip (sports scores, custom messages, Twitter trends, a dim alarm-clock
  mode). Front panel: 5.6" QHD Super AMOLED.
- [2014-09-03] **The Samsung-experiment framing.** "This is absolutely another one of Samsung's crazy
  let's-try-everything phones… when the Galaxy Note first came out it was another crazy experiment, but
  now they're everywhere." Innovation-worth-trying, even if it looks odd now.
- [2014-09-03] **The real question is whether the shape helps or hurts use** — and the make-or-break is
  the developer **SDK** ("what's really going to make this cool is Samsung giving out an SDK so developers
  can make apps that use the strip"). He notes the strip gets out of the way in fullscreen video (though
  the faint "my Galaxy Note Edge" watermark "should really be just pitch black").
- [2014-09-03] **He explicitly withholds a verdict.** "Even after watching videos on it, I honestly
  don't know how I feel. I held one, I messed around with one, I still don't know how I feel… it still
  seems really awkward and unnecessary and cumbersome, but that was only after an hour of use."
