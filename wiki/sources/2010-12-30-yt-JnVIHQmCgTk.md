# Technology of 2010 - A Year in Review (@mkbhd, 2010-12-30)

## Summary
A "state of tech" year-in-review recap of 2010: the iPad launch and the wave
of copycat tablets it triggered, the iPhone 4 (Retina Display, iOS 4) as one
of Apple's best sellers, the "superphone era," Windows Phone 7 vs. the
Kin 1/Kin 2 flop, Android's rise (Nexus One, Gingerbread 2.3 on the Nexus S,
passing iOS in US mobile-OS share), the 2010 social-networking boom
(Twitter, Facebook's 500M-user milestone, Google Buzz), CES 2010's 3D-TV
push (dismissed as a novelty), Google TV, console gaming (Kinect,
PlayStation Move), the MacBook Air refresh, and Google's CR-48 Chrome OS
netbook pilot. Closes with a self-reported channel-growth anchor: 250+
uploads on the MKBHD channel in 2010.

## Key claims
- [2010-12-30] **Self-reported channel growth anchor**: "the MKBHD Channel
  also saw more than 250 new uploads with a combination of hardware and
  software and tutorials" in 2010 — a dated, first-person upload-volume data
  point for the channel's early growth curve.
- [2010-12-30] Names the iPhone 4 as "one of their killer products this year
  and probably one of their best selling of all time," crediting the Retina
  Display and front-facing video-chat camera.
- [2010-12-30] Praises Apple's form-factor discipline on the MacBook Air
  refresh: "it's not really the specs of that notebook that are really
  impressive, it's the form factor, and that's what Apple does best" — an
  early, dated instance of his recurring form-over-specs framing.
- [2010-12-30] Assesses 3D TV (pushed hard at CES 2010) as "still pretty much
  a novelty because of the lack of content and the cumbersome glasses."
- [2010-12-30] Flags Google TV as promising but early: "it's still really
  ahead of its time, hopefully this will be big in 2011."
- [2010-12-30] Calls Google Chrome OS "revolutionary" via the CR-48 netbook,
  despite unremarkable hardware, and expresses hope to join the pilot
  program.
