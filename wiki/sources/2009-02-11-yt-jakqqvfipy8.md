# HD Tutorial: Upgrade to iTunes 8 Plus (@mkbhd, 2009-02-11)

## Summary
Origin-era how-to for upgrading to **iTunes 8 ("Plus," DRM-free)** on Mac or PC. Marques
(age 15) navigates apple.com to the iTunes Plus download, explains that installing over an
existing library preserves all songs, videos, and podcast subscriptions while stripping
DRM, and tours what's new: the **Genius sidebar/playlists** (recommends music from the
iTunes Store you don't own yet, with buy links) and — the headline benefit — **DRM-free
music playable across many devices** rather than being locked to a single iPod. He
recommends the upgrade. Incidental personal-gear mention: he references putting music on an
**LG Voyager** and an **iPod Touch**.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-11] **How-to / recommendation:** upgrading to iTunes 8 Plus is safe — it detects
  an existing install and migrates songs, videos, and podcast subscriptions while removing
  DRM; he "highly recommends" it.
- [2009-02-11] The big change is **DRM-free purchased music**: where older iTunes locked
  purchases to one device, iTunes 8 Plus lets you put purchased music on many gadgets in
  your iTunes network.
- [2009-02-11] Notes the new **Genius** sidebar builds recommended playlists and links to
  Store tracks you don't have (he declines to sign in / enable it in the demo).
