# Dope Tech: The Fastest Drone AND Car Yet! (@mkbhd, 2021-03-17)

## Summary
A Dope Tech episode themed on "small, fast, fun, lightweight objects": three products — the DJI FPV drone, the Rimac Concept One electric hypercar, and the Insta360 GO 2 tiny camera. Marques flies the DJI FPV (his fastest and "most fun I've ever had flying a drone"), takes a few laps in a borrowed Rimac Concept One (his subjective "fastest thing I've ever driven"), and uses the Insta360 GO 2 to capture the tight-cabin first-person footage — which he ties directly to his production philosophy about first-person angles. The most biographically significant beat: he announces at the end that he's **officially collaborating with Top Gear** — writing for Top Gear magazine, with Top Gear helping produce the Auto Focus car videos. Solo throughout (the drone and camera each get shout-outs to friends' reviews, but no on-camera guests).

## Key claims
- [2021-03-17] Two drone archetypes: the DJI "film tool" (Mavic/Phantom — easy, gimbal-stabilized, obstacle avoidance, "pretty good smooth 4K shot" on a first try) vs. DIY "FPV" racing quads (custom, soldering, 5–9 min battery, camera only for the pilot feed). (Marques)
- [2021-03-17] The **DJI FPV** merges both worlds: 4K gimbal-stabilized camera + obstacle avoidance from the film side, plus FPV speed/agility. Three modes — Normal (≤33 mph, ~15 min flight, obstacle avoidance on), Sport (≤~60 mph, avoidance off), Manual (fully unlocked, up to 87 mph, flips). He flew mostly in Sport; crashed it upside-down on asphalt, replaced propellers, "good to go." Full kit ~$1,299; DJI includes a flight simulator to learn manual before risking the drone. (Marques)
- [2021-03-17] Verdict on the DJI FPV: "if you asked me basically today if I could only use one drone for the rest of my life ... I think I would pick this one." Its value is accessibility — bringing the FPV experience to people who'd never build their own. (Marques)
- [2021-03-17] **Rimac Concept One**: only 8 ever made (now 7 after a Top Gear crash incident), ~$1.7M each, all unique specs/colors; the 2013 bleeding-edge electric hypercar that presaged the Tesla Roadster / Koenigsegg / Lucid mindset. Specs as narrated: four permanent-magnet motors (one per wheel), 1,200 hp, 0–60 in 2.5s, two-speed gearbox w/ carbon-fiber paddles, 90 kWh battery behind the seat, 210-mile range / 210 mph top speed; the glove box is the largest storage (no trunk/frunk). Borrowed from Manhattan Motors. (Marques, narration)
- [2021-03-17] Driving impressions: "the lowest car I've ever been in," feels "like a go-kart"; no rear-view mirror, no RPM indicator (only power indicators) so he doesn't know when to shift; a Doug DeMuro impression bit. Key subjective claim: "I know for a fact that my car is technically on paper faster to 60 than this — this feels faster ... this might not have actually technically been the fastest thing I've ever driven but it feels like the fastest thing i've ever driven." (Marques)
- [2021-03-17] **Insta360 GO 2**: "one of the neatest weirdest little cameras I've ever used" — the only camera that would fit in the Rimac's tiny cabin; best video quality Insta360 had made; built-in magnet, magnetic necklace chest-mount, and a multi-use charging case (charges ~3x, doubles as tripod). (Marques)
- [2021-03-17] Production-philosophy tie-in: he's long wanted tiny 360/action cameras to improve because they're so convenient; he uses **first-person POV** angles a lot in smartphone reviews because "it gives you that feeling like you're holding the phone in your own hand." Past hacks for the POV shot: a phone in hand, a GoPro in his mouth, holding a RED Komodo one-handed ("not exactly safe"); the GO 2 tucked in a headband solved it. Wants this for more car videos. (Marques) ★
- [2021-03-17] **Announcement — Top Gear collaboration**: "I'll be collaborating a little bit more officially with Top Gear ... I'm going to be writing for Top Gear magazine and they're going to be helping out with the videos for the Auto Focus series on the channel," with possible cross-appearances. (Marques) — biography/creator-business milestone.
