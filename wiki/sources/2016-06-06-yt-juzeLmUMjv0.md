# The Rugged Galaxy S7 Active! (@mkbhd, 2016-06-06)

## Summary
First look at the Galaxy S7 Active — "the Galaxy S7 plus the following differences": rugged build,
programmable custom key, and a big 4,000 mAh battery. He's "happy and a little disappointed at the same
time" — the disappointment is entirely about it being an **AT&T carrier exclusive**, which sets up one
of his durable industry critiques (great products buried by carrier exclusivity) and reprises the
anti-thin / fill-the-body-with-battery value.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-06-06] **"Built like a tank" — a case built into the phone.** Military-grade 810G shock
  resistance + retained ip68; a rubber rim that protects the screen face-down and beefs up the corners.
  "Someone's first reaction was 'wait, what case is that?' — it kind of looks like a thinner phone
  wearing a case, and that's essentially what it is." Skeptical of the "shatterproof" claim ("still
  Gorilla Glass 4… not sure I buy all that"). Frames it against the fragile all-glass S7: "not everyone
  can do pretty and dainty."
- [2016-06-06] **Big battery = the standout, tied to his battery value.** A 4,000 mAh cell (400 mAh
  bigger than the S7 Edge that "already lasts me all day") makes it "a power-sipping endurance champion.
  Some pretty bad phones sell anyway because their main selling point is 2 days of battery — people just
  need battery." Reinforces the fill-the-body-with-battery / battery-matters-most thesis; the thickness
  is a feature, not a flaw.
- [2016-06-06] **The programmable custom key.** A dedicated key with three programmable actions (single/
  double/long press) that can launch favorite apps even when sleeping — "pretty dope." (He likes real,
  configurable physical controls — echoes the "good buttons" value.)
- [2016-06-06] **The carrier-exclusive critique (the core takeaway).** "The biggest problem — and why I
  was disappointed — is that it's an AT&T exclusive, one carrier in one region. This could be the best
  phone Samsung makes right now, but a lot of people will never find that out. If it were unlocked or on
  multiple carriers it'd be a big player I'd recommend even over the regular S7. Instead it's tied down
  by AT&T, plus a little AT&T bloatware and branding — that's kind of a shame. Maybe next year, Samsung."
- [2016-06-06] **Everything else = the known-good S7.** Best display (Quad HD Super AMOLED), "one of
  the best if not the best cameras" (12 MP f/1.7 OIS), top specs, Android 6.0 — "the speaker still not
  that good," still TouchWiz-skinned.
