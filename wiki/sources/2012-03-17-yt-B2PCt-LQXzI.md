# Best Audio Quality Setup: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-03-17)

## Summary
A short PSA-style explainer on **external DACs** — why the digital-to-analogue converter built into a
computer is usually the weakest link in an audio setup, and why an inexpensive external one fixes it.
Third entry in the "Explained!" run. Its durable contribution is a reusable analytical concept:
**the bottleneck** — the idea that a chain performs at the level of its worst component, so upgrading
anything else is wasted until that component is fixed.

## Key claims
- [2012-03-17] **The bottleneck framing — the transferable idea**: "even if you have the greatest
  speakers in the world, the best audio setup in the world, if it's going straight through the headphone
  jack you're using the computer's internal DAC, and ultimately that's going to be pretty bad and ends up
  being what we call **a bottleneck in your audio setup**."
- [2012-03-17] **The mechanism, explained plainly**: audio files "start off as binary zeros and ones…
  and they need to be converted to an analog signal that'll actually vibrate and make you hear the
  sound." The DAC is that conversion step; the internal ones "are typically really really bad."
- [2012-03-17] **The gap he's addressing, named**: "when I watch a lot of setup tours on YouTube… a lot
  of them just forget about audio. Even tech-savvy people forget about the audio component of their
  setup." A missing-topic observation — the "I don't see it on YouTube and I want it to be there"
  selection principle from [[2012-02-29-yt-0AZ5WyD2MK4]] in action.
- [2012-03-17] **Recommendation with the reason it's now viable**: external DACs are "actually getting
  pretty inexpensive too… they've come down a lot in price." He names the **Audioengine D1** — slim,
  solid build, and it "provides power to your headphones as well through its built-in amplifier, so
  you're getting a lot for your money."
- [2012-03-17] **Reports his own A/B test and what he actually heard**: switching from his laptop's
  headphone jack to the D1 through the ATH-M50s gave "a pretty solid difference… a little punchier,
  deeper, crisper bass, a little bit wider sound stage and more of a stereo sound effect. I was having
  fun turning it on and off listening to the difference."
- [2012-03-17] **The full chain stated as a rule**: best sound requires "the highest quality audio files
  possible… and the highest quality digital-to-analogue converter possible, which is almost never the one
  that's built into your computer" — plus "a great pair of headphones usually helps too."
- [2012-03-17] **Format self-description**: "I feel like this is more of a public service announcement
  than anything" — a video made to correct an omission rather than to review a product.
