# Setup Tour Project 2014! (@mkbhd, 2014-01-29)

## Summary
A call for submissions to his community "Setup Tour Project" — creators send 15–30-second
tours of their workspaces for a compilation. Persona value is the **community-collaboration
format** ("the largest tech collab on YouTube"), his stated **learn-from-others' setups**
value (why he watches setup tours), and a small production-craft note on brevity.

## Key claims
- [2014-01-29] **Learn-from-others' setups — the stated value**: "I really like watching setup
  tours — they're a great way to get to know a better way to do your work, how someone's workflow
  works, what tools they use. That was the inspiration for my last setup tour project — I took a
  ton of ideas from the setups you submitted and incorporated them into my own." His value-
  different-perspectives / borrow-good-ideas disposition.
- [2014-01-29] **The community-collaboration format — "the largest tech collab on YouTube"**:
  "submit a mini tour of your workspace [or your mom's, brother's, friend's] — the key is brevity,
  a 15–30 second video." His community-driven content format (cf. the earlier setup-tour project and
  the tablets collab).
- [2014-01-29] **Production-craft note — wide shot + one highlight**: "try to get two things: a
  wide-angle shot of the whole workspace, and pick something really unique to highlight [monitors,
  a custom desk, cable management] — that's what gives people ideas they haven't seen." His teach-
  the-craft instinct, applied to submissions.
- [2014-01-29] **His own setup tour will follow separately**: "I'll be watching everything
  submitted, taking a lot of your ideas into account, and uploading my own setup tour likely as a
  separate video — that'll be the conclusion." Confirms the format (his definitive setup tour comes
  as a follow-up).
- [2014-01-29] **"You know me, I like pixels"**: "use anything from your phone to your DSLR — but
  obviously higher quality the better, you know me, I like pixels." The running display-primacy gag.
