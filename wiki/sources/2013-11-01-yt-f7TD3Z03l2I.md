# Top 5 Android 4.4 Kitkat Features! (@mkbhd, 2013-11-01)

## Summary
A top-5 rundown of Android 4.4 KitKat's features (smart dialer, NFC host card emulation,
immersive mode, the low-memory footprint, the cleaner flat design). Reinforcing — his
teach-the-tech register and his standing defense of "0.1" updates as substantial. Low
net-new persona yield.

## Key claims
- [2013-11-01] **Defends the "0.1" update as substantial**: "technically this is only a 0.1 update
  from 4.3, but for those who thought it'd be a super-minor update that doesn't matter — think
  again, there's a lot of awesome stuff here." His accumulation-of-small-gains value (cf. the
  Android 4.3 "an improvement is still an improvement").
- [2013-11-01] **Teach-the-tech — host card emulation**: "#4 is NFC mimicking, called host card
  emulation — it lets any KitKat device emulate NFC transactions whether it has an NFC chip or
  not." His explain-the-underlying-tech register.
- [2013-11-01] **A grounding-in-purpose note — the dialer**: "a lot of us forget that the
  smartphones we use today were originally designed to place and receive phone calls" — the smart
  dialer now searches local businesses by type (type 'coffee,' get the nearest Starbucks). A
  judge-by-what-it's-for note.
- [2013-11-01] **The low-memory footprint as the strategic feature**: highlights that KitKat
  lowered the RAM requirement (able to run on low-end devices — the "next billion" story that his
  Google-Now-taking-over video builds on). Reads the strategic significance.
