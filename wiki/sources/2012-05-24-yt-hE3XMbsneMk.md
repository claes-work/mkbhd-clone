# Sony NEX-VG20 Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-05-24)

## Summary
A review of the Sony NEX-VG20 — a camcorder body with a large DSLR sensor and interchangeable
E-mount lenses — which he frames as "the one that breaks all the rules." The technical hook is that it
delivers **DSLR shallow depth of field with reliable video autofocus**, solving the exact pain point of
his DSLR-video era. It doubles as a biography anchor: the demo footage is from a weekend of ultimate
frisbee in Wisconsin, and he says he owns/would buy the camera.

## Key claims
- [2012-05-24] **The category-breaking framing**: "a high-end DSLR sensor from Sony in a camcorder's
  body, with a DSLR mount that has interchangeable DSLR-like lenses adapted for camcorder-like video…
  I totally love it."
- [2012-05-24] **The problem it solves — autofocus + DSLR look together**: "it's basically like having
  autofocus for video on a 5D Mark III. It's absurd." His DSLR-video reviews (T2i, 24L, fisheye) had
  all required manual focus pulls; this gives the shallow-DoF look without them. Autofocus is "not fast
  but really really sharp."
- [2012-05-24] **DSLR depth of field praised specifically**: "this super soft bokeh you get in the
  background — you don't get that softness from a regular camcorder… that is the DSLR sensor really
  working." Consistent with his stated love of the f/1.4 shallow-DoF look ([[2011-10-21-yt-LpC6UwZR_Hw]]).
- [2012-05-24] **Owns it / would buy it (self-reported)**: "there hasn't been anything to stop me from
  buying one… I really really like it." The stock 18–200mm f/3.5 zoom is called "one of my favorites
  from Sony."
- [2012-05-24] **Real-use proof, not just test-object footage** — a method note: "I know what you're
  thinking: wow, this is just general typical footage from a random object, did you actually use this
  camera? Well, yes — I have footage from an entire weekend of shooting outdoors in Wisconsin." He
  pre-empts the "staged demo" objection.
- [2012-05-24] Hardware assessed in his materials-first order: Dolby 5.1 built-in mic ("goes above and
  beyond anything you'll find on other camcorders"), 16 MP stills off the DSLR sensor, ~2 hours record
  time on the stock battery, flip-out 3" touchscreen, single SD slot ("would have liked to see two").
- [2012-05-24] **Biography anchor — Wisconsin ultimate frisbee**: the outdoor demo footage is of an
  ultimate game, with an audible score callout ("2 to 1 Stevens") — dating a Stevens-affiliated ultimate
  trip and corroborating the frisbee thread ([[2011-09-23-yt-4Etc7n5NCcI]]).
- [2012-05-24] Shooting setup confirmed: he's recording this review on the **Canon T2i** at an
  equivalent 48mm.
