# Use that Search Box! (@mkbhd, 2009-06-11)

## Summary
A short "not a rant... maybe it is a rant" update in which the 15-year-old Marques builds
a **process for handling repeat questions at scale** — the earliest audience-management
system in the corpus. As the channel grows, viewers keep asking for videos he has already
made, so he (a) teaches them to use YouTube's per-channel video search box, (b) commits to
a **reply convention**: he will answer with the keyword IN ALL CAPITAL LETTERS so the
asker can paste it straight into that search box, and (c) commits to **pre-empting the
single most common question** ("where did you get that wallpaper?") by linking the exact
wallpaper in every video description from now on. It also yields two hard corpus facts:
his catalog stands at **216 videos** on 2009-06-11, and his wallpapers come from
`wall.alphacoders.com`, including "that black and red one" viewers keep asking about.
★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-11] **Catalog size, dated**: viewers tell him "I don't feel like searching
  through all 216 of your videos to find it" — 216 uploads as of 2009-06-11.
- [2009-06-11] **The problem named**: "as of late, getting more and more subscribers and
  friends... I've been getting a lot of people asking me about videos that I've already
  done. Now, this is no offense to anyone who's already asked me that."
- [2009-06-11] **Solution 1 — teach the tool**: channel-scoped search ("there's a search
  box under the my videos section of YouTube where you can search all my videos for
  keywords") finds a video whether the term is "in the title or the description or the
  tags."
- [2009-06-11] **Solution 2 — a reply convention (a standing policy)**: "I've decided that
  from now on if someone ever asks me... I'll just point you down here to the search box
  and I'm just going to answer your question saying 'I actually already did a video about
  my wallpapers,' and I'll include the keyword in your answer in all capital letters."
- [2009-06-11] **Solution 3 — pre-empt the recurring question**: "I'm probably going to
  start including a link from now on in all my videos... to the exact wallpaper I got...
  just so you guys don't have to ask anymore." An early instance of fixing a recurring
  audience question at the source rather than answering it repeatedly.
- [2009-06-11] **Wallpaper source, dated**: "most of the wallpapers I've been getting
  recently are from... wall.alphacoders.com," found via the browser search bar with the
  query "CGI"; "pretty much like eight or nine of my latest wallpapers are from this page."
- [2009-06-11] **The red-and-black wallpaper is a known viewer landmark**: "you probably
  remember that black and red one right there. Yeah. Yeah, you've asked." Corroborates the
  red-and-black colour preference dated in [[2009-06-03-yt-rgeo4FAnpu8]].
- [2009-06-11] **Back-catalog depth is already a feature**: he demonstrates the search on
  "Skype" and gets multiple hits — "a microphone for Skype, a Skype review, a tutorial on
  upgrading to Skype" — i.e. he has enough coverage per topic that a search is the right
  interface. (Cf. [[2009-06-02-yt-s1l-49rrTYc]] and [[2009-01-30-yt-8iIOsv_RJBA]].)
- [2009-06-11] **Browser setup on screen**: he exits Safari and opens Mozilla Firefox
  "which is using a Google Chrome theme" — cross-browser desktop consistent with the
  Safari-4 and Chrome tutorials of the same weeks ([[2009-05-29-yt-nH6Aix5dAdw]]).
- [2009-06-11] **Programming rule restated as a sign-off**: "MKBHD going back and forth
  between hardware and software here" — the alternation rule stated three times in two
  weeks (also [[2009-06-05-yt-CNcVkVJ2bTA]], [[2009-06-15-yt-FDASvkMxraA]]).
