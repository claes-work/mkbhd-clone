# Apple Vision Pro Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2023-06-06)

## Summary
A solo hands-on after a ~30-minute WWDC demo of the **Apple Vision Pro** ($3,500,
shipping early 2024). No cameras were allowed inside, so it's a description-driven
first-look. The standout: **eye tracking** ("the closest thing that I've experienced to
like magic") combined with pinch-to-click **hand tracking** that works with hands resting
anywhere, giving a "telepathic" UI. He organizes what it does into three made-up buckets —
**Computing, Content, Connections** — and rates the best-in-class **stereo color
pass-through**, the outward **"two-way pass-through" EyeSight** display (which he finds
creepy and doubts), Mac virtual display, FaceTime "personas" (uncanny-valley), and 3D
"spatial video" capture (which he dislikes socially, invoking Google Glass). Downsides:
no haptics, heavy metal-and-glass build, **2-hour tethered battery**, and the price.
Verdict: the best VR eye/hand tracking he's ever tried, but "not a product for the masses" —
an Apple-Watch-**series-zero** for early adopters and developers to define. Solo — all
persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2023-06-06] The Vision Pro is a standalone AR/VR computer (M2 + a dedicated **R1** chip
  for real-time sensor processing, ~4K micro-OLED per eye, its own Wi-Fi, VisionOS) with
  **no controllers at all** — controlled entirely by eyes, hands and voice.
- [2023-06-06] **Eye tracking is the highlight**: "even for a pre-release product, kind of
  unbelievable how well it does"; you look to highlight and pinch fingers to select —
  "it feels telepathic." He calls the hand tracking the best he's seen with no controllers.
- [2023-06-06] **Pass-through** (stereo, color, sharp, real-time) is "the best I've ever
  seen in a headset"; the outward-facing OLED **EyeSight** eyes are "kind of creepy… a
  bold look — I just don't think it's smart," and predicts rivals won't copy it (no budget
  for an outward screen).
- [2023-06-06] Genuinely wants the content use case: "I would pay to watch an NBA game
  courtside in the VR headset"; likes movie-watching and the digital-crown immersion dial.
  Dislikes 3D spatial-video capture socially — "the dad at the birthday party with the
  headset on… what kind of memory is that for the other people around you?" — likening it
  to why **Google Glass** failed ("ahead of its time," plus a camera on your face "just
  wasn't cool").
- [2023-06-06] Downsides: no haptics (a butterfly lands on his finger and he feels
  nothing); heavy metal/glass build (compares to AirPods Max weight); **2-hour** external
  battery ("most movies are two hours plus"); unclear fan noise; and $3,500.
- [2023-06-06] **The "compared to what?" pricing frame**: with Apple products "there is no
  direct comp"; even an identical headset "still wouldn't have FaceTime… wouldn't have
  looking at a Mac" — "this is something only Apple could pull off."
- [2023-06-06] Positioning verdict: "this feels like this series zero, like the Apple Watch…
  it's really going to be up to the first-gen early adopters and the developers to decide
  the direction," floating a future cheap "Vision SE."
