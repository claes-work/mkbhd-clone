# HD Tutorial: Make Internet Explorer Faster (@mkbhd, 2009-02-02)

## Summary
Origin-era Windows how-to for speeding up **Internet Explorer 7** on XP/Vista. Marques
edits the registry (`HKCU\…\Internet Settings`) to raise `MaxConnectionsPer1_0Server` and
`MaxConnectionsPerServer` to a decimal **99**, warns that values of 100+ have "been known
to go wrong," then clears browsing history, recommends **CCleaner**, and sets "days to keep
pages in history" to 0. The video is also an early **browser-opinion** artifact: he closes
by conceding IE is not the fastest browser and shows **Safari** loading a page much faster
for comparison.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-02] **Registry tweak:** set `MaxConnectionsPer1_0Server` and
  `MaxConnectionsPerServer` to decimal **99** to speed IE7; create them as DWORD 32-bit
  values if absent.
- [2009-02-02] **Caution:** setting the connection values to 100 or above "has been known
  to go wrong" — he deliberately caps at 99.
- [2009-02-02] **Maintenance advice:** clear browsing history weekly; recommends
  downloading **CCleaner** to automate clearing temporary internet files.
- [2009-02-02] **Browser opinion (dated):** IE7 "is not the fastest web browser";
  demonstrates **Safari** loading a page noticeably faster, and says he has a companion
  video on making Safari faster.
