# Samsung Galaxy S4 vs HTC One! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-08)

## Summary
A head-to-head between 2013's two flagship kings — and a template for his **decide-by-use-
case** comparison style: rather than crowning one, he maps each dimension (build, camera,
display, speakers, performance, OS) and gives situational verdicts. Notable for a hands-on
**flex-and-listen build-quality test**, a balanced plastic-vs-metal treatment (informed by
having interviewed Samsung), and a clear personal pick (HTC One, 8/10 times).

## Key claims
- [2013-05-08] **Hands-on evidence — the flex test**: physically flexes both phones on
  camera — "the HTC One barely budges… the Galaxy S4 at ~20% flex has all these weird
  popping and creaking noises. That's concerning to me, that there's such a big disparity in
  build quality." His demonstrate-don't-just-assert method.
- [2013-05-08] **Balanced plastic-vs-metal — and an access datum**: "Samsung has said — and
  I've interviewed people on the Samsung team — that they have their own perfectly valid
  reasons for using plastic." Lays out the trade-offs both ways (metal: build/feel/durability
  vs chipping, temperature, radio; plastic: the reverse). The **"I've interviewed the Samsung
  team"** line is an industry-access datum.
- [2013-05-08] **Decision-by-use-case — the governing frame**: doesn't declare a single
  winner — "put your money where your heart is… I wouldn't have any problem recommending
  either one." Gives situational verdicts instead: HTC One for build/speakers/speed, S4 for
  the camera.
- [2013-05-08] **BoomSound as a decisive edge**: "the S4 has a single rear speaker that a
  badly-placed finger can muffle; the HTC One has two front-facing stereo drivers that blow
  every other device out of the water." Restates the media-experience verdict.
- [2013-05-08] **Camera — the honest split**: "the S4 takes better pictures in regular light,
  the HTC One better in low light… and regular light is where I take most of my pictures. If I
  was a huge picture-taker, my pick would be the S4 every single time, and I know a lot of you
  are suckers for pixels just like I am." Situational camera verdict + the pixel-person self-
  reference.
- [2013-05-08] **Notices what others miss — wider-angle lens**: "another slight difference
  nobody ever seems to talk about: the HTC One's front and back cameras are wider-angle… fit
  more friends in a selfie." His attention to under-discussed details.
- [2013-05-08] **His personal pick, with a condition**: "8 times out of 10 I'll take the HTC
  One… but if I need to take a lot of pictures, the S4. I really hope the HTC One comes to
  Verizon — throw some stock Android on it and it's a keeper, the ultimate daily driver for
  me." His stock-Android + Verizon preference.
