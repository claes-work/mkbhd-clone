# Unboxed: Matias USB 2.0 Keyboard (@mkbhd, 2009-05-09)

## Summary
A short origin-era unboxing of a Matias-brand USB 2.0 keyboard, presented solo by a 15-year-old Marques Brownlee. The video follows the early one-product-per-video "hardware" workflow: unbox the item, walk through what's in the box, point out the standout features (a USB 2.0 dock and a rear USB 2.0 hub), and explicitly defer the verdict to a separate full review promised in one to two weeks. Content is descriptive product handling rather than opinion or analysis. Not an L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-09] **Format is unbox-now, review-later**: Marques frames this as the unboxing video, with the review video to follow "within 1 to two weeks" after he has had time to use the hardware.
- [2009-05-09] **Shipping was slow**: he notes the keyboard took "a couple couple weeks" to ship via USPS high priority mail to a PO Box.
- [2009-05-09] **Headline feature — USB dock**: per the box and the manufacturer's website, this is billed as the world's first keyboard with a USB 2.0 dock, demonstrated by plugging a USB 2.0 4 GB stick directly into the keyboard.
- [2009-05-09] **Rear USB 2.0 hub**: the keyboard also carries a two-port USB 2.0 hub in the back; he flags testing its speed for the review.
- [2009-05-09] **Two USB ports on the connecting end**: one for the dock and one for the keyboard itself.
- [2009-05-09] **Physical details**: adjustable-height stands on the underside for a more comfortable typing angle, LED indicators (num lock, caps lock, etc.), and extra media keys including volume and a mute button.
- [2009-05-09] **Deferred assessment items**: key travel, typing feel, speed, and sound are all explicitly left for the review video.
- [2009-05-09] **In-box contents**: shipping/paper info note, a quick-step setup guide (instructions noted as being in Spanish), and the keyboard in plastic wrap; "nothing really special about the Box."
