# ZTE Axon 7: Top Budget Smartphone? (@mkbhd, 2016-08-17)

## Summary
Review of the $400 ZTE Axon 7 — the clearest canonical statement of his **"five pillars of a great
smartphone"** framework, applied explicitly to a budget phone. Also his optimistic **"good phones got
cheap and cheap phones got good"** thesis, and a textbook use of **"numbers don't mean everything"** on
the camera. Lands it "firmly right behind the OnePlus 3" for best budget phone.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-08-17] **The optimistic "cheap phones got good" thesis.** "A lot of people say smartphones are
  getting stagnant or boring — I'd argue the rise of super-inexpensive, really-good smartphones is an
  exciting benefit of how good the tech has gotten. Good smartphones have gotten really cheap, and cheap
  smartphones have gotten really good." A durable, characteristic counter-narrative.
- [2016-08-17] **THE FIVE PILLARS (canonical statement).** "Consider my five pillars of a great
  smartphone, which I judge all smartphones by, whether they're $850 or $350: great build quality, great
  display, great performance, great battery life, and great camera. Nail those five core ones and you're
  set — other little things probably aren't a dealbreaker." The definitive articulation of his review
  rubric. (Reinforces the pass-13 "five pillars" doctrine.)
- [2016-08-17] **"Numbers don't mean everything" (camera).** "You get a 20 MP sensor, f/1.8, OIS — great
  numbers, but as we know, numbers don't mean everything. The pictures show pretty clearly a mid-range
  camera: a consistent warm-to-magenta cast, struggles with color reproduction and low light." His anti-
  spec-sheet stance in its purest form. Silver lining: "consistently bad the same way every time, so it's
  fixable with software."
- [2016-08-17] **Battery pillar is "flexible" — charging conveniences can substitute.** "The battery
  category is more flexible for me, because charging conveniences can make up for it — a swappable
  battery, wireless charging, or fast charging. The OnePlus 3 has decent battery but the best fast
  charging in the game, so it's totally fine." A nuanced view of how the pillars trade off.
- [2016-08-17] **Media/audio as the Axon 7's standout.** Stereo front-facing speakers + a high-quality
  DAC/amp (32-bit) = "one of the best budget phones you can get, especially if you're into audio." Dings
  the unlit nav buttons ("you literally can't see them at night") and a lock-screen notification quirk.
- [2016-08-17] **Verdict + audience-driven coverage.** "It sits firmly right behind the OnePlus 3 for
  best smartphone on a budget right now — highly recommend it. Thanks to you guys for recommending I check
  it out; you were blowing up Twitter over it." (His coverage is partly audience-directed.)
