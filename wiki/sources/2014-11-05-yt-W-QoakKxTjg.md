# Google Nexus 9 Unboxing + Hands On! (@mkbhd, 2014-11-05)

## Summary
An unboxing / first-impressions of the **white Nexus 9** (HTC-built, replacing both the Nexus 7 and Nexus
10). Marques flags the mixed reviews going around (some pre-dated a software update), keeps his own
first-impressions honest, and notes the build quirks others glossed over — a slightly **squishy/giving
back** and shallow buttons — against genuine positives (metal band, front-facing speakers, excellent
haptics). Low review-yield; a dated first-look ahead of the full review.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-11-05] **Honest-first-impressions framing.** He notes some published reviews ran before a recent
  software update, so he's giving his own fresh take from setup.
- [2014-11-05] **Build: metal band = more premium**, but the **back has "a little bit of give"** ("built
  really different from any metal tablet you've seen") and the **buttons are very shallow** ("I wish they
  were more tactile"). Light/dark models use slightly different soft-touch (the white treated to stain
  less).
- [2014-11-05] **Best haptics he's felt on a tablet** — "the tactile feedback from the vibration motor
  felt really nice, probably better than any other tablet I've ever used."
- [2014-11-05] **4:3 like the iPad mini**, with big **front-facing speakers** (to be compared against
  BoomSound), running his **first look at Lollipop on a tablet**. First impressions only; verdict deferred.
