## 11. Panels (wallpaper app, 2024–2025) — and its controversy

- **Panels** — a mobile wallpaper app curating work from artists, launched **September 2024**
  (iOS/Android). Pricing at launch: **$11.99/month or $49.99/year**; free tier required
  watching **two unskippable ads** to download one lower-res file. Brownlee said he split
  revenue **50/50 with artists.** [press-corroborated]
  (https://www.benzinga.com/24/09/41006748/...; https://decrypt.co/251013/...)
- **Backlash (immediate)**: fans slammed the **$50/yr price**, the **ad-gated free tier**, and
  especially the app's **data collection** (location, contacts, IP, device model, cookies,
  etc.) for what is essentially serving JPEGs. **13,000+ critical replies within ~20 hours**
  under his announcement. [press-corroborated]
  (https://decrypt.co/251013/...; https://www.benzinga.com/...)
- **Response**: Brownlee updated the app to address privacy concerns and **cut the price to
  ~$2/month**, but it never recovered momentum. [press-corroborated]
- **Shutdown announced December 1, 2025**; app stops working **December 31, 2025**. Reasons
  given: the **development team's makeup changed** and no right-fit replacements were found;
  the model was **unsustainable** and it was hard to build a paying market for something
  people won't normally pay for. **Annual-subscriber refunds** begin Dec 31, 2025; the **code
  to be open-sourced under Apache 2.0 in January 2026.** [press-corroborated]
  (https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/01/mkbhds-wallpaper-app-panels-is-shutting-down/;
  https://9to5google.com/2025/12/01/...; https://www.macrumors.com/2025/12/01/...)

## 12. Other controversies

- **Fisker Ocean review (February 2024)** — Brownlee titled it **"This is the Worst Car I've
  Ever Reviewed"** and called it "the single weirdest automotive reviewing experience" he'd
  had (malfunctioning key fob, faulty hill-hold letting it roll backward, broken "California
  mode," unmonitorable solar roof). He'd sourced the car from a Mitsubishi dealer because
  Fisker wouldn't supply one; Fisker urged him to wait for a software update, but he reviewed
  it because customers were already receiving un-updated cars. Widely credited with amplifying
  Fisker's collapse, **though the company was already failing** (layoffs, halted production)
  before/around the review. [press-corroborated]
  (https://insideevs.com/features/709130/fisker-ocean-worst-car-mkbhd-review/;
  https://www.teslarati.com/mkbhd-fisker-ocean-worst-car/; https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/17/...)
  > ⚠️ The brief conflated a "worst company in America" phrase with Fisker. The actual line
  > was **"the worst CAR I've ever reviewed"** (Fisker Ocean). Use the exact phrasing.
- **Humane AI Pin review (April 2024)** — his critical review ("The Worst Product I've Ever
  Reviewed... For Now") drew debate about a big creator's power to sink a struggling product;
  TechCrunch defended him ("Don't blame MKBHD for the fate of Humane AI and Fisker").
  [press-corroborated] (https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/17/mkbhd-humane-ai-review-fisker/)
- **Speeding-video incident (November 11, 2024)** — a DJI action-cam review included a POV
  clip of him **driving 96 mph in a 35 mph zone** (speedometer blurred). After criticism he
  removed the driving portion and **apologized on X**; body-cam footage of prior traffic stops
  (speeding, illegal tint, obscured plates) later circulated. [press-corroborated]
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marques_Brownlee)
- Joint "YouTube Masterclass" appearances with **MrBeast and Casey Neistat** (creator-craft
  content), and an Apple-AI-training-data flap where MKBHD's and MrBeast's videos were
  reportedly among data used — both **context, not scandals about him.** [press-corroborated]
  (https://www.dexerto.com/tech/apple-accused-of-stealing-youtube-videos-...)
