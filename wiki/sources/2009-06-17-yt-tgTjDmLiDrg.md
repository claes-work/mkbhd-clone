# YouTube Update: Download your Own .mp4 Videos (@mkbhd, 2009-06-17)

## Summary
A platform-update tutorial covering YouTube's new built-in "Download MP4" button — and,
at ~74k views, by far the **highest-viewed video of the entire 2009 origin corpus so far**
(the surrounding origin videos sit at 4k–10k). Two things make it worth keeping. (1) It is
an early, clean instance of the **quality-tradeoff verdict** that becomes his signature
analytic move: the official button is easier and preserves title/description metadata, but
"it is not in the highest possible quality, which is the only downside... it's not the
1280x720 that I would have expected YouTube to give your video in," whereas the JavaScript
method he had shown earlier gets you the full-quality file without the metadata. He lays
both options out and lets the tradeoff stand rather than declaring a winner. (2) It shows
him **superseding his own earlier video** with a newer, better method — the origin of the
"redo it when the world changes" pattern. He also gives an on-air tour of the entire 2009
YouTube creator toolbar (edit, annotations, audio swap, Insight, promote, delete) and
pre-announces two future videos from it. ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-17] **Supersedes his own earlier tutorial**: "this one's title might look a
  little bit familiar to you guys who have been subscribed for a little while... recently
  I actually did a video on downloading your own — or anyone's — YouTube videos in the MP4
  format... using a piece of JavaScript I put together that I got from one of Google's
  blogs or forums."
- [2009-06-17] **Quality tradeoff stated explicitly** (the analytic core of the video): the
  official button "is not in the highest possible quality, which is the only downside...
  it's not the 1280x720 that I would have expected YouTube to give your video in. So it
  will be a bit pixelated... you are sacrificing a bit of quality when you do it that way.
  You're not going to get the HD out of it, but you will get everything else."
- [2009-06-17] **The counterweight named**: the built-in method "saves the title and
  description of the video," so it is findable later "just by using the Windows Vista
  search box and just typing in the title of the video you downloaded"; the JavaScript
  method has no metadata "but you will have it in the highest possible quality to back up
  on your hard drive."
- [2009-06-17] **720p as his own quality reference point**: he expects 1280x720 from
  YouTube because that is what he uploads — consistent with the 720p/H.264 encoding
  standard already promoted from the origin-era production pages.
- [2009-06-17] **Archiving framed as the point**: the purpose is "for you guys to download
  and back up your own MP4 YouTube videos onto, say, an external hard drive, or backing it
  up onto some service where you can back up a certain amount of gigs."
- [2009-06-17] **Platform observation**: "YouTube's been having a lot of server troubles
  recently, but they've been actually making quite a few updates to their page if you
  didn't already notice" — the earliest instance of him tracking platform changes as a
  beat.
- [2009-06-17] **Full 2009 creator-toolbar inventory**, given in order: play, edit
  (title/description/tags), the annotations editor, audio swap ("music vendors who have
  decided to let YouTube and Google overlay their music on your video"), Insight, promote,
  delete — and now Download MP4.
- [2009-06-17] **Two future videos pre-announced**: "I was considering doing an overview or
  a tutorial on [annotations], but we'll see how that goes," and "I think I will be doing
  an Insight overview." He is already reading his own analytics tool as content.
- [2009-06-17] **Request loop restated**: "if you have any other tutorial ideas or requests,
  you can feel free to subscribe and message me... for anyone who feels that they have a
  good enough question."
- [2009-06-17] **Production honesty**: "sorry about that background noise" — left in rather
  than re-recorded, consistent with the origin-era single-take approach.
- [2009-06-17] **Self-demo detail**: the file he downloads to demonstrate is one of his own
  Tech Channel of the Week videos, opened in QuickTime Player.
