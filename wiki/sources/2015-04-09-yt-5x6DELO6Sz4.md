# HTC One M9 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-04-09)

## Summary
The full HTC One M9 review, summarized in one symmetrical line: "the M8 was a really well-built phone with
great fast software and a **letdown camera** — and so is the M9." An **if-it-ain't-broke** iteration where
the one part people considered broken (the camera) stayed broken. The standout doctrine: **more megapixels
≠ better pictures** — the 20 MP sensor has terrible dynamic range, and he **blames the hardware** (HTC used
a "random Toshiba sensor" instead of the tried-and-true Sony sensors everyone else uses). Praise: BoomSound
"is king, second place isn't even close," and Sense 7 has become "one of my favorite Android skins."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-04-09] **If-it-ain't-broke, applied.** Daily-driven since MWC; hard to tell from the M8 up front —
  "most changes are on the inside." Build/display/speakers "not broken, so no need to overhaul." One misstep:
  the **power button moved too low** (right under the volume keys — "I constantly accidentally press volume
  down"); they should have moved the volume keys to the left instead.
- [2015-04-09] **BoomSound is king.** The best speakers on any phone ("second place isn't even close"), now
  with a Dolby music/theater EQ toggle. Paired with a great 1080p LCD (still sharp at 5"), an "awesome media
  experience."
- [2015-04-09] **Sense 7 turnaround.** "I used to really not like HTC Sense, the same way I hated TouchWiz —
  now it's one of my favorite Android skins": a full theme engine, card-vs-grid multitasking choice, screen-
  off gestures. Performance a champ (Snapdragon 810, 3 GB) — it does get warm charging/gaming "but nothing to
  worry about." Battery "decent" (~3–3.5 hr screen-on; the software oddly hides the screen-on-time number).
- [2015-04-09] **The camera — "more megapixels doesn't always equal better pictures."** The 20 MP camera is
  "still not that good," mainly **terrible dynamic range** (nothing exposes correctly; HDR looks unnatural)
  plus "almost embarrassing 4K that looks like upscaled 720p." **He blames the hardware, not software** —
  "most high-end phones, even the iPhone, use a Sony sensor; HTC went with some random Toshiba-built sensor,
  and that's where I place the blame." (Image-quality/sensor-choice over megapixels.)
- [2015-04-09] **Verdict:** "an extremely well-built phone with great fast software and the best speakers on
  any phone… but if cameras/photo/video are high on your list, the M9 is probably not at the top — the iPhone
  and even some Windows Phones take better pictures." A great M7→M9 upgrade, less critical from an M8.
