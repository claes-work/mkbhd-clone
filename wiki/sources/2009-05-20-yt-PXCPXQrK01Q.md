# Reviewed: iFrogz EarPollution D33 Noise-Isolating Earbuds (@mkbhd, 2009-05-20)

## Summary
The review half of the unbox→review two-step for the iFrogz EarPollution D33 earbuds
(unboxing published 2009-04-30, [[2009-04-30-yt-uHaTlGE-UH0]]). Age 15, camera-filmed
hardware. He walks the accessories (zippered carrying
case, three eartip sizes), the asymmetric "around-the-neck" right-side cable, the 3.5 mm
jack and its device compatibility, and the 9 mm drivers — then lands a **numeric verdict:
8.5/10**, "worth every cent," with the one con being weak bass. Notable as the second dated
origin-era **scored** review and the clearest early statement of his **price-tier
grading** instinct: he explicitly refuses to judge a $19.95 earbud against "$200 noise-
cancelling over-the-ear headphones." ★ L3-candidate for the review-methodology thread.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-20] **Score: 8.5/10** — "overall 8 and 1/2 out of 10, very nice earbuds."
- [2009-05-20] **Price and recommendation band**: $19.95 at the iFrogz site; "if you're
  looking for a pair of earbuds that are $20 or less... you're going to want to take a
  look at these."
- [2009-05-20] **Grade on the price tier, not absolutely** — the review's central
  methodological move: "these again granted are not a $200 noise-cancelling over-the-ear
  headphones."
- [2009-05-20] **The one con: bass** — "the base was not the strongest of any earbuds...
  they did lack a little bit of bass."
- [2009-05-20] **The pro: clarity** — 9 mm drivers give "a really really clear sound"; "the
  crisp noises... is pretty much unparalleled for the price of these earbuds."
- [2009-05-20] **Fit and passive isolation**: three eartip sizes (he used medium); noise
  **isolating**, not cancelling — "you're not going to need extra power or anything."
- [2009-05-20] **Compatibility, checked personally**: 3.5 mm jack works with "pretty much
  any iPod model" and the **iPhone 3G but not the original iPhone** (whose recessed jack
  needed an adapter); he used a 2.5 mm adapter for his own cell phone (the Verizon LG
  Voyager era) with "no degrading in the sound of any kind."
- [2009-05-20] **He compares against his own prior daily driver**: the asymmetric cable
  style matches "the Sony earbuds that I was using before."
- [2009-05-20] **Design/color customization noted as a plus**: buyers "can customize which
  colors" on the iFrogz site.
- [2009-05-20] **Two product variants flagged**: "there are actually two versions of the
  D33 earbuds — this is the full-size version," and he links the exact product, not just
  the brand.
- [2009-05-20] **Still not a partner**: "just go ahead and subscribe, that button should be
  up there, from not a partner yet."
