# Top 5 Google I/O 2016 Recap! (@mkbhd, 2016-05-19)

## Summary
His Top-5 from the Google I/O 2016 keynote — "a pretty quiet keynote for new physical products, but a
lot of cool stuff." The clone-relevant beats: his first real reactions to the **Google Assistant** and
**Google Home** (which he frames warmly as "the future… like the computer from Star Trek"), skepticism
about Google's messaging-app sprawl, VR going mainstream (Daydream), and **Instant Apps** as his
favorite thing of the show.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-05-19] **Google Assistant + Google Home = "the future."** On the conversational assistant
  (contextual follow-up questions) and the Home speaker: "some people have mixed feelings about a
  little white box in the corner that seems to know everything about you… but it kind of seems like the
  future to me — reminds me of the computer from Star Trek." An early, optimistic ambient-computing read
  (the ancestor of his later smart-home takes).
- [2016-05-19] **#1 Instant Apps = his favorite.** "This was my favorite thing of all of Google I/O —
  the ability to open apps you don't even have installed, instantly… it's like taking the mobile web to
  the next level." Walks a B&H deep-link demo. Enthusiasm for reducing friction between web and app.
- [2016-05-19] **Skeptical of Google's messaging sprawl.** New apps Allo (smart replies, whisper/shout)
  and Duo (video, "knock knock" live preview — "seems cute at first, then a little creepy"): "why
  couldn't they all be bundled into one app? I guess they forgot about that whole unifying-messaging
  thing they were working on." A recurring critique of Google shipping overlapping products.
- [2016-05-19] **Daydream — VR baked into the OS.** "Starting with Android N, all the ingredients for
  VR are built into the core of Android," with a Daydream-ready hardware spec (sensors, SoC, display).
  Reads VR as evolving fast. (Continues his cautious-VR-optimism thread.)
- [2016-05-19] **The naming-tease + the machine-learning takeaway.** Jokes Android N still has no name
  ("I'm pretty sure they already have one and are just waiting for someone to submit it"). Big-picture
  takeaway: "VR is everywhere, messaging apps are everywhere, machine learning is incredible, and Google
  is basically Skynet at this point — in the best possible way."
