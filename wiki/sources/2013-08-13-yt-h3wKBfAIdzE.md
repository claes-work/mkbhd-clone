# The Perfect Smartphone! (2013) (@mkbhd, 2013-08-13)

## Summary
A crowdsourced "Frankenstein dream phone" thought-experiment (sparked by his own tweet
that the Lumia 1020 camera would improve any phone). Persona value is a **best-of-breed
taste snapshot** — his 2013 picks reveal what he values on each axis — and, tellingly, his
ideal way to *sell* it: **Nexus-priced, pure stock Android, no bloatware, off-contract in
the Play Store.** A "dream phone" format he returns to for years.

## Key claims
- [2013-08-13] **The best-of-breed picks (a 2013 taste snapshot):** body/design from the **HTC
  One** ("the best-designed phone out there," unibody, BoomSound, matte black); display from
  the **Oppo Find 5** ("one of the most underrated devices in a while"); camera from the
  **Lumia 1020** (41 MP, "that Ultra Pixel just isn't cutting it for a dream phone"); the
  highest-end internals (Snapdragon 800); and **Motorola for the battery** ("Motorola just
  knows batteries… the Droid RAZR Maxx has the best battery life of any smartphone"). Each pick
  is a dated statement of what he weights per axis.
- [2013-08-13] **His ideal way to sell it — the stock-Android + fair-pricing ideal**: "I'd want
  it priced like a Nexus / Google Play edition — sold with pure stock Android 4.3, no carrier
  bloatware, off-contract in the Play Store, arriving in 2 days." His stock-Android + no-carrier-
  interference + Nexus-pricing preference, stated as the ideal.
- [2013-08-13] **Bought the Lumia 1020 for the camera alone**: "if this didn't have the amazing
  camera it does, I might never have bought it — it'd just be another Windows Phone 8 device,
  and honestly that's not my thing." A dated personal-purchase + optics-love datum.
- [2013-08-13] **Personal-preference asides**: "I'm not one of those people who has to have a
  removable battery, as long as the battery is good — but I do like external storage." Names his
  own priorities honestly.
- [2013-08-13] **Crowdsourced / community-exchange format**: "this is basically a video to get
  your thoughts — leave your Frankenstein phone in the comments, thumbs-up the ones you like."
  The audience-programmed content pattern (the dream-phone format he reuses for years).
