# OnePlus X Impressions: Super Budget! (@mkbhd, 2015-10-29)

## Summary
Impressions of the **$249 OnePlus X**, framed by his value thesis: **"phones are getting good, and good
phones are getting cheap."** For $249 you get a genuinely premium-feeling **glass-and-metal** design (no
plastic) with the OnePlus 2's beloved alert slider, dual-SIM/microSD, and solid mid-range specs — a phone
that "stands out from the rest in its budget" mostly on **industrial design**. (It's slippery and a
fingerprint magnet from all the glass.)

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-10-29] **The value thesis, stated.** "Phones are getting good and good phones are getting cheap.
  Three years ago $250 wouldn't get much of a smartphone at all… that has changed a lot over the last year
  and a half, and this is the latest and greatest of that trend."
- [2015-10-29] **Design is what sets it apart at $249.** "There's no plastic here — the front and back are
  Gorilla Glass, and it has a grooved metal rail all the way around… it feels like a really tight, well-
  constructed, nearly high-end smartphone." Keeps the **alert slider** ("from the OnePlus 2 that we loved
  so much"). Downside: slippery with so much glass, and a fingerprint magnet.
- [2015-10-29] **Specs for the price:** a 5" 1080p AMOLED (441 ppi), Snapdragon 801 + Adreno 330, 3 GB, a
  13 MP f/2.2 camera, a 2525 mAh battery, and a dual-slot tray (dual-SIM or nano-SIM + microSD up to 128 GB
  more).
