# NEW YouTube Comment Sharing [November 2010] (@mkbhd, 2010-11-18)

## Summary
Second "YouTube update" video published the same day as
[[wiki/sources/2010-11-18-yt-3DdJtW72hSs]], this one demoing the new per-comment
share button (hover a comment → share to Facebook/MySpace/Twitter) plus a smaller
watch-page change (numeric like/dislike counts replacing the old hover-chart). Shows
himself tweeting a shared comment live, signed in as `@mkbhd`.

## Key claims
- [2010-11-18] **States a stated preference for the old like/dislike UI**: "I like
  this the way it is. A chart is very intuitive" — a mild critique of a YouTube UI
  change, an early instance of the "reviewing the platform itself" habit later
  central to his tech-industry commentary.
- [2010-11-18] **Confirms his personal Twitter handle on camera**: demonstrates
  sharing a comment to Twitter and narrates "this has been tweeted signed in as
  mkbhd."
- [2010-11-18] **Invites and frames viewer comments as content**: "you guys are now
  free and inspired to leave amazing comments on my videos" — an explicit instance of
  actively cultivating audience engagement as part of the channel's growth loop.
