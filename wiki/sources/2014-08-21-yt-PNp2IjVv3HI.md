# NVIDIA Shield Tablet Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-08-21)

## Summary
A review of the NVIDIA Shield Tablet from a deliberately **disclosed angle**: Marques is "**really not
much of a gamer at all**," so instead of faking authority on the gaming features, he reviews it as what's
left — "an 8-inch pure-Android sort of Nexus 8" — and **links other creators' videos** for the gaming
side. A clean instance of his **know-your-lane / disclose-what-you-don't-cover** ethic, plus two
recurring positions: **front-facing speakers should be standard**, and **Google Earth as a stock
performance benchmark**.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-08-21] **Disclosed-limitation method.** "If you know me, I'm really not much of a gamer at all… I
  didn't want to ignore this just because I don't game." He reviews the non-gaming experience and
  explicitly defers: "I'm actually going to leave a link or two… to videos that do a good job of
  showcasing these gaming features," because "I wouldn't know best about that." (His know-your-lane ethic.)
- [2014-08-21] **As a stock-Android tablet it's excellent** — the Tegra **K1** chip (2.2 GHz quad,
  192-core GPU) makes near-stock Android "fast, fluid, and smooth"; he puts the Google Experience
  Launcher on it. **Google Earth** — his long-standing informal benchmark that "lags and stutters on any
  other Android tablet" — ran with almost zero dropped frames: "might actually finally be the first
  Android tablet I can showcase this app on."
- [2014-08-21] **Front-facing speakers should be standard.** "I probably said this about just about every
  device with this, but it really should be standard, especially in tablets" — even mid-range drivers
  beat back-firing speakers for media.
- [2014-08-21] **Honest cons:** the power/volume **buttons are awful** (stiff, near-zero travel — "I
  still can't change the volume without looking at it"), and the display is only a mid-range warm-ish
  1080p panel (fine, since the point is showing off the K1).
- [2014-08-21] **The DirectStylus is a genuine extra** — a felt-tip-recognizing stylus with the NVIDIA
  Dabbler app; "the stylus recognition is as advanced as anything you'll find on any iPad or other
  tablet." NVIDIA's other additions (performance modes, HDMI/controller settings) are tucked neatly into
  Android settings.
