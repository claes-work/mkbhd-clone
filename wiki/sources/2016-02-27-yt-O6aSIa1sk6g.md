# Nextbit Robin Review: Unlimited Storage? (@mkbhd, 2016-02-27)

## Summary
A review of the Nextbit Robin — a $400 phone from a brand-new company built around **cloud-based storage**
("the smartphone that never runs out of storage"). His verdict lands on **champion-the-risk-taker**: even
though he thinks a normal $400 Android with microSD "might have been more of a hit," it's "cool to see this
type of cloud-based storage solution in action — props to Nextbit, I like it." Well-built, USB-C, and
**dual front-facing stereo speakers** ("I love this"), with an unlocked bootloader (warranty honored).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-02-27] **A new company's swing.** "Anytime you get an entire phone made by a brand-new company,
  there are decisions that have to be made to put it on the map, and the Robin is no exception." Squared-off
  design, soft-touch, well-built ("feels like you could hurt someone with it"), USB-C, and — praised —
  **dual front-facing stereo speakers** ("I love this — loud and clear"). Weak point: the small buttons.
- [2016-02-27] **The concept: cloud storage.** Marketed as "the smartphone that never runs out of storage"
  via cloud offload.
- [2016-02-27] **Enthusiast-friendly.** "The bootloader is unlocked out of the box, they'll support loading
  CyanogenMod, and they still honor the warranty" — a control-over-software plus.
- [2016-02-27] **Verdict: champion-the-risk-taker.** "If you're buying a mid-range phone specifically for
  lots of storage, get one with microSD (the OnePlus X). But if that's not hugely important and you're
  choosing between this and a Nexus 5X or OnePlus 2, it's worth considering — build/specs/performance are
  solid. **If this were a normal $400 vanilla Android with microSD instead of the Nextbit cloud stuff it
  might have been a hit — but it's cool to see cloud-based storage in action; props to Nextbit, I like it.**"
