# Top 5 Android 4.2 Jellybean Features! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-15)

## Summary
A countdown of Android 4.2's five biggest additions: the new lock screen, gesture typing, quick
settings, "the little things," and — his #1 — the new camera with Photo Sphere. Mostly feature
coverage, but it carries his recurring **polish-as-a-first-class-quality** framing, an honest "this
feels half-baked" call on a headline feature, and a genuinely useful **teaching tip** on how to shoot
a Photo Sphere correctly.

## Key claims
- [2012-11-15] **Polish as a real quality axis, restated**: "I actually said this in my Android 4.1 video —
  the little things. There are so many little things in Jelly Bean that just made it a more polished, more
  complete-feeling user experience." He treats fit-and-finish as substance, not garnish (cf.
  [[2012-06-28-yt-poSFV9_abcw]]).
- [2012-11-15] **Honest "half-baked" call on the #5 feature**: the new lock-screen widgets "feel a little
  bit half-baked right now, because you can only add one widget per swipe — so you can have a calendar,
  but if you want your clock you have to go to another widget on a new page." Names the limitation of a
  brand-new feature rather than hyping it.
- [2012-11-15] **Gesture typing praised from his own use**: "I've actually used Swype a lot before this,
  so having it in a stock Android keyboard is a big deal, and I'll definitely be using it a lot." A dated
  personal-workflow adoption.
- [2012-11-15] **Quick settings — a feature he'd wanted made stock**: "I've been talking about this in my
  Super Nexus video — this was baked into some previous ROMs like CyanogenMod and AOKP, but now it's in
  stock Android, you don't have to root." A wish (from his custom-ROM use) now shipping in stock, which
  he flags.
- [2012-11-15] **Photo Sphere as #1, chosen for "wow factor"**: 360° stitched panoramas viewable "just
  like Google Street View." He picks it over the gesture keyboard as #1 explicitly for the wow factor
  while noting he "definitely love[s] the new keyboard as well" — showing his own #1 was a close call.
- [2012-11-15] **A genuinely useful teaching tip**: "a lot of people swing the phone around while taking a
  Photo Sphere — try to keep the camera lens in the same place and pivot it around, like your head on a
  swivel. That gets the stitching a lot better." Teaches the correct technique, not just the feature.
- [2012-11-15] **Cross-platform-sharing observation, from his own posting**: Photo Spheres "don't always
  look so great on Twitter and Facebook where I've been sharing them, so if you share on Google+ on the
  desktop there's a viewer that lets you look around" — a practical note grounded in his own sharing
  habits (and Google+ advocacy).
- [2012-11-15] Notes a feature he'd skip: Daydream screensaver — "I wouldn't use it because that seems
  like a battery killer" (consistent with his display-is-the-drain view).
