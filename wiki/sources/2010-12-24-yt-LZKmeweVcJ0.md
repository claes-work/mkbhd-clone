# MKBHD YouTube Tips | #1 (@mkbhd, 2010-12-24)

## Summary
Episode 1 of the announced "YouTube Video Tips" series, and a genuine
origin-story/meta episode: walks through how he personally got started —
picking a tech-video sub-type (software / tech-news / hardware), his actual
first hardware video, and choosing "MKBHD" as the channel identity over his
own name. Explicitly frames science-and-technology as a smaller niche than
comedy on YouTube's top-subscribed list at the time.

## Key claims
- [2010-12-24] ★ **Names his literal first hardware video and how it
  happened**: he bought a laptop and a laptop cooler, then "took the laptop
  webcam, point it at the box, and just film me taking it out of the box" —
  the most concrete, self-reported account yet in the corpus of the exact
  origin moment of his hardware-review format (webcam + a purchase he already
  owned, not a review unit).
- [2010-12-24] ★ **States the channel-name-origin decision outright**: "I
  didn't really have an idea of what I was going to do on my YouTube channel
  when I started, of course I just picked Marcus Brownley cuz that's my name,
  but I did get youtube.com/MKBHD ... that channel [name] as well is much
  easier to remember." — direct, dated, first-person confirmation that the
  channel started under his real name and MKBHD was adopted later for
  memorability/branding, not chosen from day one. (Caption again renders the
  name "Marcus Brownley"; verified spelling is Marques Keith Brownlee.)
- [2010-12-24] **Three-way content taxonomy, self-stated**: software,
  tech-news, hardware — "a combination of the three ... is what I try to do."
  Software is framed as the easiest entry point (free screencasting tools);
  tech-news as the most saturated (names "Locker Nome" [likely a caption
  garble of a contemporary tech-news YouTuber] and repeats "John for Lakers"
  and "Soulja Knows Best" from the [[2010-12-08-yt-zRqfTwA0o-o|Dec 8 PSA]]);
  hardware as requiring a camera and review units, with self-owned gear as a
  viable starting point.
- [2010-12-24] **Names CamStudio as his free screencasting tool** for the
  software-review sub-type — a distinct, earlier/cheaper tool than the
  Camtasia pipeline confirmed elsewhere in this era.
- [2010-12-24] Confirms a just-finished channel-background redesign ("just
  finished redesigning that, it's embarrassing how long it took to make but
  I'm really proud of it") — a dated branding-effort anchor.
