# 2017 vs Smartphone Bezels! (@mkbhd, 2017-08-28)

## Summary
A "what is 2017 the year of?" trend piece — his answer: **the year of the disappearing bezels** (with dual
cameras a close second). Clone-relevant for the each-year-has-a-trend framing, a teach-the-mechanism on how
each maker handles the front-facing components a bezel-less phone can't lose, and the cheap-got-good thesis
(a bezel-less phone from a no-name brand).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-28] **"Every year has a trend — 2017 is the year of the disappearing bezels."** "Each year the big
  phones follow some trend — material, battery, headphone-jack related. 2017's first candidate was dual
  cameras (everywhere, with a few exceptions), but the real one is **disappearing bezels** — every phone we
  looked forward to this year minimized bezels in some way." A recurring name-the-year's-trend framing.
- [2017-08-28] **Teach-the-mechanism: no phone is truly bezel-less — what do you do with the sensors?** "As
  everyone minimizes the bezel, they each have to decide what to do with the things that *must* stay on the
  front — the selfie camera, proximity/ambient sensors, display drivers. The Essential crunched the earpiece
  into a top sliver + a display cutout for the camera; the S8/G6/Note took a symmetrical slim-bezel approach
  (all the stuff up top, matched by the bottom); the Xiaomi Mi Mix used a piezoelectric frame-speaker and
  moved sensors to the bottom chin for truly zero top/side bezels." Explains the engineering trade-offs
  behind the trend.
- [2017-08-28] **The dual-camera taxonomy (recap).** iPhone = normal + 2× telephoto (portrait/zoom); LG G6 =
  normal + super-wide (landscape/fisheye); Essential = normal + monochrome (detail/B&W). "All did it for a
  slightly different purpose." The reuse-the-explainer move.
- [2017-08-28] **"Cheap phones got good, good phones got cheap" — applied to bezel-less.** "All that got us
  here — and I've said this before: cheap phones are getting good and good phones are getting cheap." Segues
  to a bezel-less phone from a no-name brand (a Doogee Mix) as evidence the trend has trickled all the way
  down. His democratization thesis again.
