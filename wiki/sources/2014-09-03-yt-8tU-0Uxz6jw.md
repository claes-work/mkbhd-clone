# Samsung Galaxy Note 4 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-03)

## Summary
First impressions of the Galaxy Note 4 — notable as the moment Marques **turns positive on Samsung
hardware** after years of criticizing their "looks-better-than-it-is" plastic. "I am now finally
actually really impressed with a piece of Samsung hardware, and it's about damn time." The Note 4's real
metal (aluminum sides, chamfered edges), improved buttons, QHD Super AMOLED, and first-ever Samsung OIS
camera earn genuine praise; the software is "90% hardware improvements, 10% software" (basically Galaxy
S5 TouchWiz). A dated data point that his even-handed praise follows the hardware, not the brand.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-03] **The build finally impressed him — and he says the hardware, not the marketing, did it.**
  "You can watch all the promos you want, but actually holding this phone was the only thing that
  convinced me of its premium build." Real **aluminum** sides + chamfered edges (like the Galaxy Alpha /
  iPhone 5S), "not that fake plastic-looking-like-metal thing on the Galaxy S5" — "the best looking and
  feeling Galaxy smartphone Samsung has ever made."
- [2014-09-03] **He watched the keynote skeptically and changed his mind on contact** — "I watched the
  presentation just like you guys, rolling my eyes… but actually holding this metal in my hands changed
  my mind really fast." (Judge the object, not the pitch.)
- [2014-09-03] **Hardware upgrades:** Snapdragon 805, 3 GB RAM, a 5.7" QHD Super AMOLED (>500 ppi, "the
  best display on any smartphone out right now"), a **16 MP OIS camera — a first for Samsung** ("top of
  my list of best smartphones to shoot video with"), rapid charging (0→50% in 30 min), removable back +
  microSD, and much better clicky metal buttons. The fingerprint reader is still "finicky."
- [2014-09-03] **Software is ~unchanged from the S5** — TouchWiz on Android 4.4.4; the standout is
  improved windowed multitasking (up to 16 resizable app windows) and the more pressure-sensitive S Pen.
- [2014-09-03] **Disciplined about the verdict** — "a lot of these things I'm spouting are just numbers…
  I'll definitely be putting these to the test for my full review; this is just first impressions."
