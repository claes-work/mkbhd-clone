# $500 Drone vs $3000 Drone - DJI Spark vs Inspire 1! (@mkbhd, 2017-05-24)

## Summary
A price-spectrum comparison of the $500 DJI Spark vs the ~$3,000 Inspire 1. The clone-relevant core is a
clean **"film tool vs. fun tool"** framing (what you actually get for a ~10× price difference) and the
**"cheap drones got good, good drones got cheap"** thesis — his budget-tech optimism applied to a new
category.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-05-24] **"Cheap drones getting good, good drones getting cheap."** "Over the years drones have gone
  from big, expensive things to much smaller, cheaper, more accessible — to the point where cheap drones are
  getting good and good drones are getting cheap." His recurring democratization thesis, applied to drones.
- [2017-05-24] **The organizing frame: "film tool vs. fun tool."** "The differences that matter — physical
  size, camera quality, flying features — split them into two categories: the Inspire 1 as a **film tool**
  and the Spark as a **fun tool**." A reusable way-to-think-about-a-category framing (rather than 'better/
  worse').
- [2017-05-24] **What the ~10× price buys (named concretely).** The Spark's wins: pocketable ("makes the
  Mavic look huge — literally smartphone-sized"), single-operator fun features (gesture/follow/portrait
  modes, obstacle avoidance). The Inspire's wins: swappable cameras/lenses (modular), 3-axis camera control,
  stability in heavy wind, 50–60 mph to chase subjects, "people use its camera+gimbal as a handheld
  Steadicam." Camera is the biggest gap: Spark = 1080p ("I wish it shot 4K — the sensor probably can, the
  computer inside might not"), Inspire = 4K with the Zenmuse X5.
- [2017-05-24] **The Spark "will sell pretty well" — value verdict.** "It's super cheap compared to previous
  drones with the same capabilities — the fun of a drone in your pocket." A pro-accessibility read.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Cheap drones are getting good, and good drones are getting cheap."
> "Definitely a film tool versus a fun tool."
