# Google Glass Explorer Edition: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-08-30)

## Summary
A ★★ landmark wearable explainer — his hands-on with the $1,500 Google Glass Explorer
Edition. Persona value is in the **new-form-factor / new-paradigm** framing (phone →
tablet → smartwatch → glass) and the **Explorer-Edition-is-not-the-final-product**
first-gen discipline, plus the "an Android phone with no apps out of the box — the
developer army makes the category" analogy. A key early node in his long new-form-factor
evaluation thread (2012 Glass, 2024 Humane AI Pin).

## Key claims
- [2013-08-30] **New form factor = new paradigm — the framing**: "this is the beginning of a
  new paradigm. Android phones are one form factor, tablets another, a wave of smartwatches
  another — Glass, wearable tech on your face, is a totally different form factor." Situates
  the device in his form-factor taxonomy (the lens he applies to every wearable).
- [2013-08-30] **First-gen discipline — "this is the Explorer Edition, not the final
  product"**: "there will be an evolution in software and hardware before an actual consumer
  edition… this is a limited early version for people willing to explore and give feedback."
  Refuses to judge an early-access device as the shipping product (cf. "first-gen = public
  beta," "series zero").
- [2013-08-30] **The "Android phone with no apps" analogy — how a category grows**: "think of
  it as an Android phone with no apps out of the box — it can do a core set of functions, and
  that's normal. Once you add an app store and a developer army, that list gets a lot longer."
  Reasons about the ecosystem/developer flywheel (his "the tech works, the ecosystem isn't
  ready" thesis, applied).
- [2013-08-30] **Owns it and shoots with it — a production/first-person angle**: "the first-
  person view is one of the defining features… you'd never get a camera angle like this doing
  anything else, especially with both hands free." Praises the POV capture (uses Galaxy-Nexus-
  like optics, wide-angle, HDR burst).
- [2013-08-30] **Honest about the display and price**: "not an HD display — around 640×360,
  like a 25" monitor from 8 feet away," and "$1,500 for the Explorer Edition… hopefully way
  less than half that when it's mass-produced. I feel like a lot of people would max out
  around $400–500." Sets accurate expectations and reasons about the mass-market price.
- [2013-08-30] **Teaches the how — the trackpad interaction + tethering**: no cellular radio,
  so it tethers to your phone via the MyGlass app; swipe/tap on the side arm; "Okay Glass" +
  seven core functions. His explain-the-mechanism register.
