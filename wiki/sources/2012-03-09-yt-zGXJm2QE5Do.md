# Ultrabooks: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-03-09)

## Summary
The second entry in the **"Explained!" format** (after pixel density, eleven days earlier) — a
category-definition explainer answering "what actually *is* an ultrabook?" The analytically
interesting move is that he doesn't treat the category as a technical fact: he names it as
**Intel's marketing construct, created to compete with the MacBook Air**, then tests two famous
laptops against the spec list and finds that neither qualifies.

## Key claims
- [2012-03-09] **The category is named as marketing, with its motive** — the framing move: an ultrabook
  must "meet a certain set of specifications as defined by **Intel**, who pretty much came up with the
  category as a sort of a marketing project to **compete with the MacBook Air**." Incentive-structure
  reasoning applied to a product *category* rather than a product.
- [2012-03-09] **The spec list, as of March 2012**: Intel processor; ≤21 mm thick for 14"+ screens,
  ≤18 mm for 13" and smaller; ≥5 hours battery; resume from hibernation in ≤7 seconds. No storage
  minimum yet, with a next-generation expectation of Ivy Bridge and a 16 GB floor.
- [2012-03-09] **The requirements are explicitly a moving target**: they "can actually change as time
  goes on to keep them ahead of the curve, and to keep them just as sexy as the newest MacBook Air."
- [2012-03-09] **The MacBook Air fails on a technicality — and he says so, then overrides it**:
  "technically no, because Apple doesn't use the little bit of proprietary software Intel requires called
  Rapid Start… **but the MacBook Air resumes from sleep that fast anyway**, so I'd say it fits mostly all
  the specs. Close enough, Apple, close enough." Judges the substance over the certification.
- [2012-03-09] **The Chromebook fails on the merits**: "a couple of millimeters too thick," runs Chrome
  OS without Intel's Rapid Boot, and uses Atom processors — "so basically these fall into the **netbook**
  category, not the ultrabook category."
- [2012-03-09] **Audience-verification invitation**: "if you know the definition of an ultrabook, I dare
  you to leave it in a comment. I mean, obviously I can't verify that you didn't watch this video first."
- [2012-03-09] **Category saturation noted**: "I can't even count on two hands how many world's thinnest
  ultrabooks we saw crowned at CES 2012."
- [2012-03-09] **Source honesty, joked about**: "according to Wikipedia, which is a reliable source,
  teachers" — cites the source and undercuts it in the same breath rather than hiding it.
- [2012-03-09] **Dated biography marker**: "I'm going on vacation next week… taking my duties and all
  sorts of things down to the south half of the United States for a week" — a spring-break trip,
  consistent with the college-era timeline.
