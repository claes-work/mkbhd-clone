# HTC U Ultra Rant: Skip this One! (@mkbhd, 2017-03-17)

## Summary
A rare full negative verdict ("skip this one") on the $749 HTC U Ultra, built around a single devastating
refrain: **"poor use of space."** A clean example of his structured-teardown method — take one lens and
apply it relentlessly across every subsystem — and of his willingness to pan a beautiful phone that doesn't
earn its price.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-03-17] **The one-lens teardown: "poor use of space."** The U Ultra is bigger than the iPhone 7 Plus,
  Note 7, and Pixel XL, and still 8 mm thick — "so there should be room for every flagship feature." Instead,
  each subsystem fails the space test: a **big S6-era camera bump** on a huge phone; **no headphone jack**
  ("Apple removed it to save space in a *compact* interior — you have all the room in the world and removed
  it anyway"); **no water resistance** (room for the seals, didn't do it); **serious light bleed** at the top
  corner ("I hardly ever notice light bleed in a flagship"); **tiny speakers**; and **no wireless charging**
  despite a glass back and huge internal volume. "Poor use of space" repeated as the verdict's spine.
- [2017-03-17] **The price makes it a hard sell.** "$700-phone buyers are the pickiest, biggest enthusiasts
  — they want something really special, and you're not getting that here. At $750 it's a hard sell; there are
  phones lacking these things too, but they're all cheaper." Ties the verdict to who buys at that tier
  (geeks-value-specs-vs-price).
- [2017-03-17] **Fair about what's good (grade honestly).** "There's promising stuff that has me psyched for
  HTC's next phone: the 12 MP UltraPixel camera is really good — almost toe-to-toe with the Pixel — plus
  expandable storage and a useful second display (a shameless rip of the LG V20, but I like the notifications-
  at-a-glance)." Names the upside even while panning it.
- [2017-03-17] **The unhedged verdict.** "Aside from HTC's software, there's almost nothing for $750 this
  phone does that you can't get somewhere else better or cheaper — so skip this one. Wait for the HTC 11, or
  grab a Galaxy S8 when it comes out, or an LG G6, or pretty much any other $700 phone — a better use of
  space than this one." A clear "don't buy" with named alternatives (cf. the Hasselblad mod).
- [2017-03-17] **The jack-removal principle restated (source-critical).** "USB-C is great and it's the
  future, but Apple's reason for removing the jack was to save space in a compact phone — other makers keep
  the jack because they don't need that space, and the portless future isn't here yet. HTC could have kept
  it." Applies his own teach-the-mechanism read to indict an unjustified removal.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Seems like a poor use of space." (the recurring refrain)
> "There's almost nothing for 750 bucks that this phone does that you can't get somewhere else better or
> cheaper — so I'll say skip this one."
