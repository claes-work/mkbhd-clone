# Bose Companion 2 Series II Speaker Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-12)

## Summary
A review of the $99 Bose Companion 2 Series II desktop speakers — and **the single most
explicit enactment of his own review methodology found so far**. Before giving a verdict he
states his listening baselines by name and price ($600 studio monitors, these $99 Bose, $29
earbuds), i.e. exactly the disclosed-baseline rule from
[[2011-09-30-yt-dTPZ2tuyWFw]]. He then breaks the sound down by frequency range in
descending order of quality, and closes by disclosing his own **genre bias** as the reason
the bass verdict might not apply to the viewer.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-12] **Baselines disclosed by name and price — the methodology, verbatim**: "I've
  listened to a lot of hours of audio on these $99 Bose speakers. I've also listened to a lot of
  hours of audio on $600 iKey Audio studio monitors, and on $29 earbuds — right, so that's a
  pretty full range." He tells you the scale before he uses it.
- [2012-01-12] **Frequency-by-frequency verdict, ordered by quality**: "we start from the highs
  and work our way down to the lows, because that is also the order in which it falls." Highs
  "sound great… very very crisp," mids "were medium… hit or miss," lows "decent at best."
- [2012-01-12] **Personal taste disclosed as a limit on the verdict**: "I listen to a lot of
  music like house music and music with a lot of bass in it already, so when I'm not hearing it
  in the Bose and I'm used to hearing it in say my M50 headphones, then it's a little bit weird."
  He names his genre preference as the reason his bass complaint may not generalize — a
  textbook application of "perspective."
- [2012-01-12] **Conditional recommendation with a costed remedy**: "if you listen to a lot of
  music with intentionally heavy bass you might not like these as much, or you might want to go
  ahead and pick up a subwoofer for maybe an extra 100 bucks or 75 bucks to really fill in the
  sound."
- [2012-01-12] **Ownership disclosed as the basis of the review**: "during my time owning these
  speakers so far I've learned a lot about why people like them… and a lot about why they cost
  99 bucks" — a lived-with-it review, consistent with the standard set in the 2009 Solo bag
  review.
- [2012-01-12] **Honest confusion admitted rather than glossed**: the only driver is a 2.5"
  unit at the top of a much larger cabinet — "there's a whole lot of body of the speaker that's
  not being used, or maybe it's hollow, I don't really know. Maybe it helps with the sound having
  space in there, but it definitely confused me why it was so much bigger than the actual
  speaker. And I like compact speakers, so… I don't know."
- [2012-01-12] Practical notes: magnetically shielded cabinets (safe next to a monitor), two
  audio inputs, region-appropriate wall plug, high enough efficiency to run "really loud and
  really clear without an amp."
- [2012-01-12] **Reader-need stated plainly**: "how do they actually sound? That's what people
  want to know, it's why they came here to see this review."
