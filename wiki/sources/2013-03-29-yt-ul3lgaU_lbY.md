# HTC One 'Ultrapixel': Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-29)

## Summary
The definitive early statement of his **optics/pixel-size-over-megapixels** camera thesis,
occasioned by HTC's "UltraPixel" marketing. He teaches *why* the megapixel race is
misleading (megapixels = resolution; larger pixels = more light = better low-light), then
delivers an even-handed verdict on HTC's move: a genuinely good engineering idea dressed up
in "fancy marketing" — not the revolution the name implies. A ★★ camera-fundamentals
landmark.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-29] **Megapixels are just resolution — the core teaching**: "the megapixel count
  is literally just a resolution… an 8-megapixel sensor takes a 3264×2448 image, larger than
  anything our TVs or monitors can natively show. You'll only tell the difference between 8
  and 13 megapixels if you zoom, crop, or print really large." Deflates the headline number
  with the actual math.
- [2013-03-29] **Why cramming pixels hurts — the physics**: "as you cram more pixels onto
  the same size sensor, the pixels get smaller, which means less light per pixel — more
  noise, grain, and worse low-light performance." The pixel-size → light → image-quality
  chain, the foundation of the optics-over-megapixels belief.
- [2013-03-29] **HTC's bet, stated fairly**: "HTC decided to take a risk, not get involved
  in the megapixel race, and cram far fewer pixels [a 4-megapixel sensor] with much bigger
  pixels — more light, less grain, better low-light." Credits the engineering logic.
- [2013-03-29] **But calls the marketing what it is**: "what's the difference between a
  4-megapixel and a 4-'UltraPixel' camera? Nothing, really… all they've done is decrease the
  number of pixels and boast about how big the pixels now are. They took a risk that happened
  to be a good idea and dressed it up under fancy marketing and a sweet name." Separates the
  real engineering from the marketing spin — even-handed but unfooled.
- [2013-03-29] **Honest performance verdict — not the best, but not bad**: "in regular light
  the photos are pretty average… in low light it's a stellar performer [on par with the
  Lumia 920], but how many people take phone photos in low light? Overall it's not the best
  smartphone camera out there." Weighs the tradeoff realistically rather than crowning the
  gimmick.
- [2013-03-29] **Video is fine at 4 MP — scopes the claim**: "4 megapixels is still
  significantly more than 1920×1080, so there's no sharpness problem with video." Notes where
  the low count doesn't matter.
