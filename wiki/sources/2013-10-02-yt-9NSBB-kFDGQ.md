# Samsung Galaxy Note 3 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-10-02)

## Summary
The full review of the phone he'd hyped — and one of his favorites of 2013. Delivers on the
"no major flaw" hope (near-perfect, but "definitely not perfect"). Persona value: a firm
**anti-benchmark ethics** statement ("I am done showing benchmarks on Samsung devices"), the
**experience-over-spec** verdict, honest **fake-leather-is-plastic** calling-out, and the
big-phone / stock-Android threads restated.

## Key claims
- [2013-10-02] **Anti-benchmark ethics — refuses to be gamed**: "Samsung has been known to ramp
  up the CPU cores when it detects a benchmark app, so I am done showing benchmarks on Samsung
  devices. Not only is this device fast, it shows off how fast it is." A principled refusal to
  publish a gamed number (sharpens the standing anti-benchmark stance into an ethics stand).
- [2013-10-02] **Experience over spec — the verdict**: "the Note 3 isn't revolutionary or
  obviously the best phone out there, but it's pretty damn good in every department… you could
  almost not care about the flaws when you see how much space you have and how smoothly things
  run. The experience is what really matters." His real-world-over-spec conclusion.
- [2013-10-02] **Fake-leather honesty**: "a lot of people judged the look because it looks like
  leather — but this is plastic shaped to look like leather, and it doesn't even feel like
  leather, because it's plastic. So now that we're not fooled by the aesthetic…" Refuses to let
  a material's *appearance* stand in for its quality.
- [2013-10-02] **Everything-but-the-kitchen-sink critique, restated**: "Samsung threw every
  possible feature into this, just like the S4 — everything but the kitchen sink, hoping
  something gets you to like it enough to buy it. A familiar strategy." His value-via-software /
  feature-overload read.
- [2013-10-02] **Stock-Android preference + honest credit to TouchWiz**: "if you know me, you
  know I prefer stock Android over any skin — TouchWiz still has jank and stutter it shouldn't…
  but I don't want to make it sound like all garbage; the multi-window and Flipboard-style
  magazine are genuinely good." Even-handed inside his preference.
- [2013-10-02] **Big-phone / big-hands, restated**: "once you go big you never really go back…
  I came from a Note 2, so this doesn't feel all that big in my hands." The recurring preference.
- [2013-10-02] **The Note's structural weakness, named**: "a lot of the Note 3's best features
  besides the stylus come from the Galaxy S4 — so as long as the S line updates before the Note
  line every year, you want the Note's advantages (stylus, bigger display, more RAM, huge
  battery) to be very clear." A shrewd product-line-timing read.
