# MKBHD YouTube Tips | #4 (@mkbhd, 2011-08-30)

## Summary
The fourth entry in the recurring "YouTube Tips" series, done as a two-part
collab with fellow creator Austin Evans (credited on-camera by his then
channel handle "Duncan33303"): this half covers off-camera production —
Marques's half handles audio, lenses, and lighting/editing; Austin's
companion video (linked, not ingested here) covers on-camera topics.
Microphone options are laid out as three price/workflow tiers: USB (Blue
Snowball — plug-and-play, no post-sync needed), camera-mounted (Rode
VideoMic, for DSLRs/camcorders with a mic jack), and fully off-camera (Zoom
H1 — most control, but requires manual audio-sync in post). Lens
recommendation: a 50mm f/1.8 prime ("the Plastic Fantastic" on Canon; the
Nikon 50mm f/1.8 equivalent) for shallow depth of field on a budget, versus
a kit lens for convenience. Lighting: three-point lighting as the standard
setup, with an explicit "use free light before buying light kits" ordering
(windows/sun/room light first). Editing software: Corel VideoStudio Pro X4
recommended on Windows (cheaper/simpler than Sony Vegas but full-featured);
iMovie defended on Mac as underrated for free software, versus Final Cut for
advanced/paid needs.
