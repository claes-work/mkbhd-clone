# Moto Z Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2016-07-15)

## Summary
First impressions of the Moto Z / Moto Z Force — "one of the prettiest phones I've ever held," but he
withholds judgment ("you don't buy a phone just because it's pretty… you got to get to know it first").
Two clone-relevant firsts: he flags the **removed headphone jack** ("pulled an Apple before Apple pulled
an Apple") months ahead of the iPhone 7, and introduces **Moto Mods** as the most credible take yet on
the modular idea.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-07-15] **"You don't buy a phone just because it's pretty."** "This might be one of the prettiest
  phones I've ever held, but you don't buy a phone just because it's pretty… you don't want to lock into
  a two-year contract just because something was shiny in the store — you got to get to know it first."
  His review-discipline ethos stated outright (impressions ≠ verdict).
- [2016-07-15] **"They pulled an Apple before Apple pulled an Apple."** "There's no headphone jack at all
  — only one I/O on the entire phone, a single USB-C at the bottom; they included a USB-C-to-headphone
  dongle in the box. Welcome to 2016." Anticipates the iPhone 7 jack removal (still ~3 months away) and
  is already skeptical.
- [2016-07-15] **Thinness critique — the camera bump that rocks.** The regular Moto Z is 5.2 mm thin but
  "the Force at 7 mm actually feels thick next to it, which is ridiculous"; both have "a big old camera
  bump that rocks if you type on a table" and awkward top-corner buttons. The anti-thin thread: chasing
  thinness produces the bump + a small battery.
- [2016-07-15] **Moto Mods introduced (modular done differently).** Magnet-and-pin accessories that snap
  on with no reboot/battery-pull: style shells, a 2,200 mAh battery pack (shrinks the camera hump), a JBL
  speaker pack, an Insta-Share projector. "They don't really use the word modular, but you get the idea…
  I'll make a dedicated video, I have a lot to say." (Follows his modular-smartphones explainer
  [[2016-06-03-yt-96PJdbJysxQ]].)
- [2016-07-15] **Clean software, spoiled by carrier exclusivity.** Praises "classic Motorola stockish
  Android," but "since it's a Verizon exclusive for the first couple weeks it becomes a Droid Edition and
  gets this lovely Verizon bloatware." The carrier-exclusive gripe again ([[2016-06-06-yt-juzeLmUMjv0]]).
