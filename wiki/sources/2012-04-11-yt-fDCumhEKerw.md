# TokyoFlash Night Vision Watch! (@mkbhd, 2012-04-11)

## Summary
A short review of a TokyoFlash LED watch whose display has to be *decoded* rather than read — twelve
perimeter bars for the hour, centre LEDs for the minutes. He addresses the obvious objection first
(why own a watch at all when you have a phone), then teaches the reading system and immediately
**quizzes the audience on camera**, asking them to tell the time in the comments. An early example of
the aspirational/novelty-gadget coverage that later becomes the Dope Tech franchise.

## Key claims
- [2012-04-11] **Answers the category objection up front**: "I've never been a watch person, and I'm
  guessing you aren't either — because after all, you can just whip your phone out your pocket and tell
  what time it is. But there's something special about this watch that makes it worth having more than a
  regular watch."
- [2012-04-11] **Teaches the display, then tests the viewer**: twelve bars around the edge for the hour,
  centre LEDs for minutes — "the third one's blinking, it's the third hour… 18 minutes, so it's 3:18.
  **No math required.**" Then: "now you've got enough practice, you should be able to tell the time at a
  glance, right? So here's your practice… let me know if you can tell the time in the comment section
  below."
- [2012-04-11] **Second mode explained by the same logic**: a lower button shows the date using the same
  encoding — twelve bars for months, centre LEDs for the day.
- [2012-04-11] **Build assessed in his usual materials-first order**: "built like a tank… completely
  metal exterior, water resistant up to 3 m, glass face, metal links… you can barely even scratch this
  thing," plus an aesthetic read — "this looks classy, like formal-attire classy."
- [2012-04-11] **Practical ownership details volunteered**: a rechargeable battery via a USB port on the
  case, "the battery lasts about a month per charge."
- [2012-04-11] Notes the brand is "apparently pretty popular already" — flagging that the audience may
  know it better than he does.
