# Asus Transformer Pad Infinity Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-07-23)

## Summary
A review of the Asus Transformer Pad Infinity (TF700) — "the new and improved Transformer Prime." He
calls the hardware "the best of any tablet I have ever held," but the review turns on a
**hardware/software mismatch**: on Android 4.0 the Tegra 3 and the OS "are kind of like strangers,"
producing inconsistent performance. His verdict is therefore **conditional on the pending Jelly Bean
update** — a clean instance of judging what ships today while naming exactly what would change the
verdict.

## Key claims
- [2012-07-23] **Hardware praised at the top of the class**: 1.6 GHz Tegra 3, DDR3 RAM, 1920×1200
  Super IPS Gorilla Glass display (224 ppi), a metal-with-plastic-top back that "improves GPS and Wi-Fi
  reception a lot over the all-metal Transformer Prime" while keeping a premium feel — "the best of any
  tablet I have ever held." The plastic-strip-for-radios lesson from the TF300/Nexus 7 applied to the
  premium model.
- [2012-07-23] **The hardware/software mismatch, explained as the core flaw** — the notable analysis: on
  Android 4.0, "the Tegra 3 chip and Android are kind of like strangers… sometimes Android says 'let me
  take over performance,' and other times the Tegra 3 says 'let me control performance' — the result is a
  mixed bag of sometimes buttery-smooth and other times choppy" for light tasks. High-end tasks are
  great because both "agreed to push performance to the max"; low-end tasks stutter because the chip
  relaxes. A precise, mechanism-level account of inconsistent performance.
- [2012-07-23] **The verdict is conditional and forward-dated, but grounded in evidence** — the method
  move: "when this tablet gets Android 4.1.1 Jelly Bean, it is going to be the best Android tablet money
  can buy, hands down." Crucially he **doesn't take the update on faith**: Jelly Bean already runs
  beautifully on the Nexus 7, "which is also an Asus Tegra 3 tablet, which means Android 4.1 will likely
  perform even more amazingly on this faster Tegra 3." A conditional verdict backed by a real data point,
  not a promise.
- [2012-07-23] **"A story of amazing hardware and decent software"** — the summary framing: "they both
  kind of have to complement each other to complete the package." The hardware/software-must-match theme
  that runs through his tablet coverage this year.
- [2012-07-23] **Display spec-sheet enthusiasm, checked**: "did Asus leave anything off that list? That
  is a pretty hot spec sheet for a display" — but he still subordinates it to the performance
  inconsistency, not letting the spec sheet carry the verdict.
- [2012-07-23] Ergonomic detail noted as an improvement: the power/volume buttons repositioned to the top
  with "a deeper travel… they feel better to press."
- [2012-07-23] **Dock continuity**: works with the existing Transformer Prime dock; the keyboard dock's
  value (extra battery, full-size SD and USB) referenced back to his dedicated dock review
  ([[2012-02-18-yt-fQqdHfUVOiI]]).
