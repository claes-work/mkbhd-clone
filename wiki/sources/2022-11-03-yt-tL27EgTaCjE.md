## Summary
The panel dissects Apple's then-new 10th-generation iPad and argues it is "the most Tim
Cook iPad ever" — a product engineered to sit as a perfect middle rung on Apple's
pricing ladder, nudging buyers to keep spending upward. They walk through the ladder:
the aging base iPad held at $329, the new 10th-gen iPad at ~$449 (USB-C, "all-screen"
look) but only 64GB, whose 256GB upgrade lands at ~$599 — the same price as the base
iPad Air, which itself is only 64GB, pushing shoppers toward the iPad Pro and beyond.
They mock the awkward accessory story (a first-gen Apple Pencil that needs a USB-C
dongle to charge; a new two-piece $250 Magic Keyboard with a function row exclusive to
this model). Verdict: the product is fine but weird in its price bracket and execution;
it would have been better to leave the base iPad at $329 and just give it the new look.
