# Reviewing EVERY iPhone Ever! (@mkbhd, 2021-10-12)

## Summary
A solo retrospective walking through all 33 iPhone models released from the original (2007)
to the iPhone 13 lineup (2021), one generation at a time, followed by a data-visualization
segment ("big graph guy") charting storage, RAM/performance, camera megapixels, screen
size, starting price, and sales across 14 years. It doubles as a compact history of the
smartphone era and of Apple's recurring patterns: the "gate" controversies (Antennagate on
the iPhone 4, Bendgate on the 6 Plus, the 3GS white-discoloration issue), the steady price
inflation culminating in the $999 iPhone X psychological barrier, screen-size inflation
driven by Android pressure, the S-cycle refinement cadence, the arrival and death of
features (headphone jack removed on the 7, 3D Touch added on 6s and killed by the 11 Pro,
Touch ID → Face ID on the X, Lightning replacing the 30-pin on the 5), and Apple's habit of
announcing camera features (ProRAW, ProRes) that ship later via software update. Rich as a
source of dated MKBHD judgments on individual iPhones and on industry trends. Solo video —
all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2021-10-12] The original iPhone (announced Jan 9, 2007; shipped June 2007) was
  "legendary" and an "obvious inflection point," despite launching with no App Store, no
  custom wallpaper, a 3.5" display, a 2 MP camera, and a $499 starting price — it stood out
  enough to have "such an impact."
- [2021-10-12] The iPhone 3G brought the App Store (after Steve Jobs initially wanted only
  Safari web apps) starting at 500 apps; the 3GS was the first "S" (speed) update and the
  first iPhone that could record video.
- [2021-10-12] The iPhone 4 was Apple's "best new design and maybe their best design ever" —
  Retina display, first Apple-branded chip (A4), first front camera / FaceTime, glass-and-
  steel flat sides — but its external antenna caused **Antennagate** (grip-induced signal
  drop; the free-bumper-case remedy) and was the last iPhone Steve Jobs unveiled.
- [2021-10-12] The iPhone 5 killed the 30-pin connector for **Lightning**, brought the first
  screen-size bump (3.5" → 4"), first LTE, and 1 GB RAM; Apple Maps launched "absolutely
  terrible." The 5S was his first personally-owned/reviewed iPhone and added Touch ID; the 5C
  ("cheap, colorful") showed the pressure Apple felt from Android.
- [2021-10-12] The iPhone 6/6 Plus are "not my favorite designs" yet among the best-selling
  smartphones ever (~220M+ combined); Marques calls the 6/6 Plus "the best-selling smartphone
  of all time." They introduced the first camera bump and suffered **Bendgate** (he believes
  Unbox Therapy's bend video may be "the most viewed tech gadget video of all time").
- [2021-10-12] The 6s introduced 3D Touch + the Taptic Engine and the 12 MP camera every
  subsequent iPhone has kept; Marques "really liked 3D Touch and I miss it now" (it was later
  killed).
- [2021-10-12] The iPhone 7 is "one of the most important iPhone updates ever": first dual
  camera (Plus), first solid-state (non-clicking) home button, and the removal of the
  headphone jack (the "courage" moment) alongside AirPods; also the start of Apple branching
  a more-"pro" larger model.
- [2021-10-12] The iPhone X was "the true beginning of the future of the iPhone" — OLED,
  near-bezel-less, home button gone, Face ID — and its **$999 price** "opened Pandora's box,"
  proving people would pay $1,000 for a phone; it was also "the fastest discontinued iPhone
  of all time" (~10 months).
- [2021-10-12] The 1st-gen iPhone SE (2016) began "the beginning of the end of small phones";
  the 2nd-gen SE (2020, $399) is "the best red they've ever made" and shows how inflated
  prices have become. He repeatedly flags that a genuinely good small/mini iPhone may not
  last — "get one while you can."
- [2021-10-12] Data-viz takeaways: max storage went from 4/8 GB to over 1 TB; camera
  megapixels plateaued at 12 MP since the 6s (he predicts a jump to ~30–33 MP for 8K video);
  starting prices ramped steadily (the $999 X "wasn't actually that monumental a jump… it was
  just such a mental hurdle" of adding a digit); the most expensive current iPhone is $1,599
  vs. $599 at the start; iPhone sales surged ~2011–2015 and are now "plateauing… closer to
  saturated," which is why Apple grows services and accessories.
- [2021-10-12] Sign-off wish for the iPhone 14: "I hope it has a port."
