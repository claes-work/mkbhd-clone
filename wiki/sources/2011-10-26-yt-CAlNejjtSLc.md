# New Chrome Web Store Review (@mkbhd, 2011-10-26)

## Summary
A screencast tour of the redesigned Chrome Web Store: the new-tab entry point, the
featured large/small panel grid, hover-to-install, in-listing screenshots and embedded
video, user reviews, infinite scroll, category and collection browsing. Light on opinion
and heavy on navigation — a platform-explainer in the same mold as his YouTube-feature
tours, notable mainly for a **restated affinity for Google's design direction** and for
being the setup video for the app-recommendation list ten days later.

## Key claims
- [2011-10-26] **Stated design affinity**: "I love the redesigns that Google's putting
  through all their web apps — it's really looking good." A dated data point in the
  long-running Google-favorability thread (cf. the 2010 "Google is one of my favorite
  companies" line in the Chrome 7 beta video).
- [2011-10-26] Store mechanics described: featured apps shown as mixed large/small panels
  "just like in Google+"; hover to add to Chrome directly or open a detail pane with
  screenshots, an optional developer-embedded YouTube video, and user reviews you can
  contribute to after installing.
- [2011-10-26] **Infinite scroll noted with a dry caveat** — convenient "if you just want to
  waste time in the Chrome Web Store"; panels shrink to a denser grid far enough down.
- [2011-10-26] Categories include extensions alongside apps (social/communication, fun,
  news and weather); "collections" group apps thematically (a money collection is
  demonstrated).
- [2011-10-26] Incidental workflow detail: he demonstrates searching the store **by Google
  Voice Search in Chrome** before switching to typing — an early instance of him surfacing
  a capability the viewer didn't ask about.
- [2011-10-26] Cross-promotion pattern of the era: Google+ profile linked in the
  description and a +1 gesture demonstrated live, consistent with Google+ being his
  active secondary platform in late 2011.
