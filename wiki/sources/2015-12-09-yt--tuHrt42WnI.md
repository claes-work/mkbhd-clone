# iPhone 6s Battery Case: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-12-09)

## Summary
A short take on Apple's much-mocked $99 iPhone 6s Smart Battery Case — the one with the awkward "bulge." He
explains why people are unhappy (the design, and especially the **$99 price** against a better-designed $99
Mophie), while fairly noting its real **first-party advantages** (Lightning charging instead of micro-USB;
both battery levels shown in the notification tray). A value-mismatch read on an Apple accessory.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-12-09] **The basics.** An 1877 mAh case (~one full charge; Apple claims +18 hr LTE browsing / +20 hr
  video), slides on over the Lightning connector, two colors. "Pretty basic, super-simple look, not a lot of
  extra features."
- [2015-12-09] **Why everyone's hating on it: design + price.** The **bulge** — "kind of looks like there's
  another iPod under the skin; other cases look similar but most look better" (maybe meant as a finger rest,
  "I don't know if I'd buy that"). But the **main gripe is the $99 price** — "that only matters because of
  what else you can get for $99: the Mophie Juice Pack Air, the most popular battery case maker, which is
  designed a lot better." (Value-mismatch.)
- [2015-12-09] **The real first-party advantages (fair credit).** "This is probably the only iPhone battery
  case that plugs in via **Lightning** (most use micro-USB) — an advantage of being first-party. And in the
  notification tray it shows **both** the iPhone's and the case's battery levels" (it talks to the phone).
