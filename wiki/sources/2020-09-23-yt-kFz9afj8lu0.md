# Can You Actually Game in 8K? (RTX 3090 Gameplay!) (@mkbhd, 2020-09-23)

## Summary
Marques answers "can you game in 8K, and would you even want to?" — in a video shot entirely in 8K — using the first on-camera look at NVIDIA's new flagship GeForce RTX 3090 (top of the RTX 3000 series). The hook ties into the PS5 / Xbox Series X hype cycle: the PC community's argument that "the real power still comes from the real computer." He runs the 3090 inside a Maingear F131 custom PC (Core i9-10900K, 32GB RAM, NVMe SSDs) driving an 88-inch 8K OLED TV over a single thick HDMI 2.1 cable at 7680×4320. He explicitly frames this as a gaming demo, not a card review (no benchmarks — he defers those to Linus Tech Tips and Hardware Canucks). Testing Forza Horizon 4 (locked 8K/60, dips to ~50fps in busy scenes) and Control Ultimate Edition (where pushing internal render resolution to 5K collapses to ~21fps, forcing 2K render for a usable ~36fps), his conclusion: yes, you can game in 8K with a 3090, but the missing piece is displays — 8K gaming *monitors* basically don't exist yet, so most people have no reason to do it in 2020. A recurring MKBHD theme lands: frame rate is king, but for maximum immersion you also want resolution.
