# The Chromebook Pixel: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-02-22)

## Summary
An "Explained!" analysis of the just-announced **Chromebook Pixel** — and one of his
strongest early **business-strategy / incentive** reads. Rather than dismiss a $1,300
"web browser," he argues the Pixel is a deliberate **Halo product**: Google addressing
*hardware first* (on its own, not via OEMs) to break the "Chromebooks = cheap plastic"
association and shift the burden to Chrome OS + developers. A ★★ for the strategic-
reasoning method, and it connects directly to his earlier **"dream Chromebook"** wish.

## Key claims
- [2013-02-22] **The Halo-product thesis — the core strategic read**: "this is the Nexus 10
  and Nexus 4 of Chromebooks… the inspiration for the next generation of high-end
  Chromebooks. In its creation it's not even really made for the typical consumer, not
  made to sell well, not a flagship — it's just a Chromebook." Reads the product's PURPOSE
  (seed a category) rather than scoring it on unit sales — the same loss-leader/
  inspire-the-ecosystem logic he used for the Nexus 7.
- [2013-02-22] **Hardware-first sequencing, and why**: "Google has chosen to address
  hardware first… doing so on its own, not relying on Samsung or Acer or HP, and doing so
  boldly. No longer are Chromebooks synonymous with budget hardware… specs are no longer an
  excuse. Now Chrome OS really needs to step up." Maps the deliberate order of moves and
  who has to act next (developers, the web store).
- [2013-02-22] **Names the two standing Chromebook complaints and how the Pixel answers one**:
  "the two biggest complaints — one, cheap hardware; two, it lacks powerful apps… What
  today shows is Google chose to address hardware first." Decomposes the problem before
  judging the move.
- [2013-02-22] **The 3:2 aspect ratio, defended by use-case**: "you might think 3×2 is
  weird for media consumption, but it's a Chromebook — you'll be web-browsing, and pretty
  much every website scrolls down, so extra vertical pixels are better." Judges a spec by
  what the device is actually for (his standing method).
- [2013-02-22] **Viewing-distance/PPI literacy restated**: 2560×1700, 239 PPI, "the number
  one highest pixel density on any laptop display," compared against the 13" Retina
  MacBook Pro. His display framework applied again.
- [2013-02-22] **The touchscreen is why it's expensive — and his standing gripe**: "my
  biggest problem with touchscreen laptops was actually pressing it — the hinge would bend
  and wobble. This needs to be a great touchscreen to justify $1,300." Names his own bias
  and the bar the product must clear.
- [2013-02-22] **Withholds verdict until hands-on**: "all that being said, I still haven't
  held one yet… expect a video when I get my hands on one." His don't-conclude-before-
  touching discipline.
