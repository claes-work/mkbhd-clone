# Porsche Macan Turbo EV - Worth 3 Model Ys? (@AutoFocus, 2026-07-13)

## Summary
A review of the **Porsche Macan Turbo (electric)** built around one question: can a
~$150k-as-tested electric crossover justify costing two-to-three times a Tesla Model Y
Performance or Hyundai Ioniq 5N? Marques handles the static review — exterior, key, front
trunk, the (excellent) interior materials and HUD, screens, backseat — and is openly
smitten with the cabin, while pushing back on the price and Porsche's lack of one-pedal
driving. **The driving section is handed to a co-host, "Miles,"** whose analysis is
attribution-gated (see quarantine section). This is the first Auto Focus source page in the
corpus with a **non-Marques speaker**, so attribution matters here.

## Key claims (Marques — subject-attributed)
- 2026-07-13: Macan Turbo EV specs — dual-motor AWD, **630 hp, 0–60 ~3.1s, ~280-mile
  rating, ~100 kWh battery**, base ~$112k, **~$150k as tested.** Competes on paper with a
  Model Y Performance / Ioniq 5N but at 2–3× the price. (Marques)
- Loves the **interior**: rock-solid build, excellent materials, extremely well equipped
  (three front screens, carbon-fiber trim, Burmester ~$4–5k option, heated/ventilated active
  seats). Calls the **HUD "maybe the best... I've ever seen in a car"** — large, bright,
  with AR object-tracking that floats a hazard marker onto a too-close car ahead. (Marques)
- Nitpicks consistent with his method: the piano-black center console ("I hate this little
  bit"), pressure-sensitive climate strip, no rear-view camera (just a mirror); real-world
  range ~250–275 miles and only ~100 kW charging on a non-ideal (non-NACS) charger, so "not
  the ideal road-tripping car." (Marques)
- Strong recurring position: **the Macan EV has no one-pedal driving** and no setting
  reveals it — "Porsche, please, please give us the option for one-pedal driving." Notes
  Porsche blends heavy motor regen into the brake pedal (since the Taycan) but still wants
  the option. (Marques)
- Value verdict framing: within the Porsche lineup it's "kind of a bargain" (cheapest way to
  600+ hp from a new Porsche), but against the market it's hard to justify the "tax you pay
  to get that Porsche badge"; suggests buying used near the depreciation floor (under ~$100k).
  (Marques — though the sharpest value take is delivered by the co-host; see below.)
