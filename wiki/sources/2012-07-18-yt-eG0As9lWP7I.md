# Nexus 7 First Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2012-07-18)

## Summary
First impressions of the $199 Nexus 7 — and a **form-factor-defines-the-category argument**: he
insists the Nexus 7 isn't a Kindle Fire competitor at all but "**the first real 7-inch Android
tablet**," a device that "created a new market." The through-line is that what makes it matter is not
its specs but its *shape and how you hold it* — a one-handed, portrait-default, paperback-sized tablet
— which is a tablet-scale restatement of his display/experience-first instinct.

## Key claims
- [2012-07-18] **Form factor is the whole point**, stated as the thesis: "what really makes the Nexus 7
  unique from other tablets is the form factor… a 7-inch tablet, extremely lightweight like a paperback
  book, a wide-screen aspect ratio, a thinner-than-average bezel, and a default portrait orientation
  instead of landscape. All of this is new — we haven't seen this in other Android tablets before." Prior
  Android tablets were "10-inch widescreen tablets you hold sideways with two hands"; this "you basically
  always use as a smaller one-handed more portable tablet."
- [2012-07-18] **It created a market, rather than entering one**: "this isn't even really aimed at the
  Kindle Fire… this isn't aimed at any particular tablet at all. This is the first real 7-inch Android
  tablet, and in that Google has created a new market with this device… this is the definitive Android
  tablet right now."
- [2012-07-18] **"League of its own"** — a signature phrase, dated here: "this guy is in a league of its
  own." (The persona already documents "league of its own" as a signature construction; this is an early
  instance.)
- [2012-07-18] **The one-sided comparison stated bluntly**: "calling the Nexus 7 a Kindle Fire competitor
  would be an insult to the Nexus 7 — it's way beyond that," itemizing thinner/lighter/2× RAM/quad-core/
  front camera/GPS/NFC/higher PPI/larger app store/"virtually guaranteed the next few years of Android
  updates."
- [2012-07-18] **Reassures on the price point — no hidden shortcuts**: "if you had any thoughts about
  Google taking massive shortcuts to hit that magical $200 price point, you can rest easy." The only real
  hitches: no rear camera ("you're not taking any pictures with this tablet, so that's one off the list")
  and no expandable storage.
- [2012-07-18] **The cloud-first framing of the low storage**: "Google really ramps up the Google Play
  cloud experience, so you're really streaming more things from the cloud instead of having everything
  locally. I have the 8 gig version and right now I might not even ever need to upgrade." Consistent with
  his own cloud-first workflow ([[2012-04-25-yt-0FbjsNsfEmE]], [[2012-05-17-yt-_aemBh_vC-Y]]).
- [2012-07-18] **A dated update-cynicism jab at the rivals**: "forget the pathetic 7-inch Samsung Galaxy
  Tab… forget the Amazon Kindle Fire with who knows whatever version of Android it'll never be updated."
  Update-support is treated as a first-class buying criterion.
- [2012-07-18] Specs: Tegra 3 quad-core, 1280×800, first device to ship with **Chrome as the stock
  browser** — the merged-Chrome outcome he'd wished for in [[2012-05-26-yt-haOpXNVb_9Q]].
