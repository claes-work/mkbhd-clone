# iPhone 6 Sapphire: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2014-07-19)

## Summary
The **methodology-correcting follow-up** to the 07-07 sapphire video — and a strong instance of Marques
**upgrading his own test with better science**. He introduces the **Mohs hardness scale** to explain why
knife/key scratch tests are invalid (steel ≈ 5.5, Gorilla Glass ≈ 6.8, pure sapphire ≈ 9 — metal is
*softer* than any phone glass), then runs a rigorous **sandpaper test** (garnet ≈ 7, emery ≈ 8) that
reveals the real answer: the iPhone 6 display is **harder than Gorilla Glass but NOT pure sapphire** —
it's a sapphire *composite/laminate* (per an Apple patent), because a pure-sapphire slab would be too
expensive and couldn't flex. Consumer-education + a public correction of his own prior method.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-07-19] **Knife/key scratch tests are invalid — teach the mechanism.** On the Mohs scale a mineral
  scratches anything below it, not above. His steel blade ≈ 5.5, Gorilla Glass ≈ 6.8, pure sapphire ≈ 9
  (the second-hardest naturally-occurring material). "A steel knife blade, no matter how hard I try, will
  never scratch my phone's display… let's say goodbye to knife-blade scratch tests."
- [2014-07-19] **The better test: sandpaper.** Garnet (≈7, harder than Gorilla Glass, softer than
  sapphire) and emery (≈8). Garnet scratched the 5S's Gorilla Glass immediately but the iPhone 6 panel
  less (different sound) — proving the iPhone 6 panel is **not pure sapphire** (a softer garnet scratched
  it). The 5S's **pure-sapphire home button** was flawless — "the home button was damaging the garnet."
- [2014-07-19] **Two conclusions:** (1) the iPhone 6 display is harder than Gorilla Glass and resists
  scratching/bending much better than the 5S; (2) it's **not pure sapphire** — "using a massive panel of
  pure sapphire would be a little bit stupid… much more expensive, and it wouldn't be able to bend, and
  you actually kind of need your phone to flex a little bit."
- [2014-07-19] **The engineering explanation** — Apple has patented a sapphire *fusion/laminate* process
  (multiple planes) for superior hardness AND strength; it's a composite, "a lot stronger than the
  gorilla glass of previous iPhones." He links the exact patent.
- [2014-07-19] **Verdict, precise not hyped:** "as long as you're not carrying high-quality sandpaper in
  your pocket you should be good without a screen protector" — and pre-empts Apple's likely keynote
  hyperbole: "I would expect a lot of talk from Apple about how strong and indestructible this is — well,
  not really indestructible."
