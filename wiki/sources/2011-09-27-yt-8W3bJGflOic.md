# The DROID [Retro Review] (@mkbhd, 2011-09-27)

## Summary
Marques revisits the original Motorola Droid **two years into using it as his own
on-contract daily driver**, explicitly framing the video as the first time he has done a
retro review ("if I've ever gotten close to doing a retro review video, this is probably
the closest I've ever gotten"). Half hardware-condition report — how an all-metal slider
survives two years of heavy real use — and half software teardown of the mods keeping it
fast: a Simply Stunning Gingerbread custom ROM on an ultra-low-voltage 1.25 GHz kernel,
SetCPU for governor timing, LauncherPro Plus as the home screen. Filmed in his **dorm
room**, where he states a **minimalist setup philosophy** and applies it to phone
maintenance as well as desk layout. A giveaway of an unlocked Droid is gated on 800 likes.

## Key claims
- [2011-09-27] **Format origin — the retro review.** Named on camera as his first: "you've
  just finished watching my first retro review video." Establishes the
  revisit-after-long-term-use format that the channel keeps returning to.
- [2011-09-27] **Dated personal-device fact**: the original Motorola Droid has been "my on
  contract phone ever since" his original review at the end of 2009 — a continuous two-year
  daily driver, reviewed originally on this same channel.
- [2011-09-27] **Long-term durability verdict**: all-metal build, "dropped, opened and
  closed hundreds of times, slid across surfaces," still holding together "without a single
  replacement." Battery has "degraded slightly" but still yields a full day for a heavy
  user. The slider keyboard "started off really flat and 2 years later it feels even
  flatter."
- [2011-09-27] **Gadget-care claim (self-reported)**: "I take really good care of my gadgets
  and my phone is no exception" — offered as the reason a two-year-old device looks the way
  it does, and as a caveat on the durability verdict.
- [2011-09-27] **Software mod stack, stated in detail**: Simply Stunning Gingerbread ROM
  (Android 2.3.4 base), ultra-low-voltage kernel at 1.25 GHz on the single core, SetCPU to
  manage timing with underclocking to 125 MHz when the screen is off "saving a ton of
  battery life," LauncherPro Plus home screen replacement.
- [2011-09-27] **Honest limit on the mod**: overclocking fixes speed but not RAM — "it
  doesn't have nearly as much RAM as the other zippy phones out there, so apps don't stay
  loaded." He separates what a mod can and cannot fix rather than declaring the phone
  as-good-as-new.
- [2011-09-27] **Minimalism stated as a general principle and applied to devices**: "just
  like the rest of my desk setup and office setup here in my dorm room, I have relatively
  minimalist style, so I keep the applications I use and need and only the applications I
  use and need — nothing, no extra parts in this machine. This is how you keep a phone
  running smooth and fast for a long long time."
- [2011-09-27] **Dorm room confirmed as the filming location** in Sep 2011 — a dated
  biography/production-setting anchor for the college era.
- [2011-09-27] **Upgrade intent restated**: still "holding out for the Nexus Prime," which
  will not have a keyboard; he notes he uses the physical keyboard heavily for long
  messages and expects OG Droid users to move to all-touchscreen with him. (Consistent with
  the 2011-09-09 Forum Friday statement.)
- [2011-09-27] Android identity framing: the Droid is "the phone that started it all… the
  one that Android fans like me really identified themselves by when Android first gained
  that identity" — a self-placement inside the Android-fan identity, dated.
