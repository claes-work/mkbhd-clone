# Smartphone Awards 2023! (@mkbhd, 2023-12-19)

## Summary
The **2023 edition of MKBHD's flagship year-end Smartphone Awards** — one of his signature
recurring formats, with custom physical trophies (which winning companies now request and
display in their HQs). Solo, opinion-forward: he hands out category winners, runners-up, and
honorable mentions across Best Big / Best Compact / Best Camera / Best Value / Best Battery /
Best Design / **new-for-2023 Best Foldable** / Most Improved / Bust of the Year / and the MVP
(Phone of the Year). Recurring theme: phones have plateaued — screen size topped out ~7",
truly small phones are dying, and it's "genuinely hard now to get a bad phone." Solo → all
persona/voice/opinion data.

## Key claims (all Marques's 2023 picks)
- [2023-12-19] **Best Big Phone: Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra** — the "boring," understated phone
  that "holds steady the entire year"; its standout is the massive zoom, which "still feels
  unmatched." Runner-up **OnePlus Open**; HMs Asus ROG Phone 7, Oppo Find N3 Pro.
- [2023-12-19] **Best Compact: Asus Zenfone 10** (5.9") — packs flagship features into a truly
  one-handable phone; "if you're in the age of wanting a mini phone, speak now, or forever hold
  your cash," because small phones "are going away." HM Samsung Z Flip 5.
- [2023-12-19] **Best Camera: iPhone 15 Pro** — "my camera king… no regrets." Concedes some
  phones beat it on specific stills (esp. zoom) but the iPhone wins on **totality, consistency,
  and video** "by a long way." HM Galaxy S23 Ultra. Also credits his own **scientific blind
  smartphone camera test**: standard-photo winner **Pixel 7A**, low-light **iPhone 15 Pro**,
  portrait **Pixel 8 Pro** — i.e., his personal pick and the crowd's blind pick diverge.
- [2023-12-19] **Best Value: Samsung Galaxy A54** (~under $400) — "everything you need, and
  nothing you don't"; the "it was fine" phone. Notes base prices crept up across the industry.
  HMs Moto G Play ($169), Pixel 7A (now $500).
- [2023-12-19] **Best Battery: iPhone 15 Plus** — big battery + 60 Hz display = "just goes and
  goes." HMs ROG Phone 7 (6,000 mAh) and, "wizard-like," the Zenfone 10.
- [2023-12-19] **Best Design: Honor Magic V2** — "legitimately shifted the way I think about
  phone hardware": a foldable under 10 mm closed (as thin as a normal phone) with a 5,000 mAh
  battery. "A lot of us didn't even realize that you could make a phone like this."
- [2023-12-19] **Best Foldable (new category): OnePlus Open** — the folding phone he'd most
  likely daily-drive; feels most like a normal phone closed, nearly-square ~8" inner screen,
  minimal crease. Runner-up Pixel Fold (best to use *closed*); HMs Z Flip 5 (Samsung's build/
  QC "at the top of the heap") and Honor Magic V2.
- [2023-12-19] **Most Improved: Nothing Phone 2** — hardware + Nothing OS 2.0/2.5 story; "don't
  buy last year's phone… get the Phone 2." Notes Nothing now has to "stabilize and become a
  real company."
- [2023-12-19] **Bust of the Year: Solana Saga crypto phone** — "there is actually no price
  that I would recommend anyone buys this phone… buying this phone is like buying a timeshare."
- [2023-12-19] **MVP / Phone of the Year: Google Pixel 8** — great camera, dramatically better
  120 Hz 2000-nit display, the Pro's Tensor G3, deep software, and **7 years of updates**, all
  for hundreds less than the Pro. Runner-up **Zenfone 10**; HM **iPhone 15 Pro** for feeling
  like "peak iPhone… this just feels like a finished phone."
- [2023-12-19] Cross-cutting theses: screens have stopped growing (agreed ~7" ceiling); small
  phones are an endangered species; foldables are "quite on the doorstep" but still thicker/
  pricier than normal phones; it's hard to buy a genuinely bad phone in 2023.
