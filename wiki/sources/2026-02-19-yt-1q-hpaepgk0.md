# "The Model S Plaid Walked so This Car Could Run" — Corvette C8 ZR1 (@AutoFocus, 2026-02-19)

## Summary
A Corvette **C8 ZR1** review told deliberately through Marques's **"tech angle"**: he
frames the ~$200k, 1,000+ hp, twin-turbo flat-plane-crank V8 mid-engine ZR1 as the
supercar-world equivalent of the **Tesla Model S Plaid** — an American-made success story
that makes 1,000-hp performance suddenly attainable and, crucially, **daily-usable**.
His comparison beats: both are "performance bargains" that match/exceed cars several times
their price; both are practical (he fits a duffel, backpack, hard-shell carry-on, or a
full set of golf clubs in the ZR1's rear trunk behind the LT7, reaching around the fixed
carbon wing); both pair huge mechanical grip (345-section rear Pilot Sport 4S) with a
livable interior (improved HVAC button row, wireless Android Auto). He notes the ZR1's
aggressive carbon aero does things "no Tesla ever has," the front radiator all but
eliminates a usable frunk (heat spreader will "light on fire" anything stored there), and
at low RPM/low speed it's shockingly docile — bringing thousand-horsepower performance to
"a place we've kind of never seen it before." **Attribution: co-hosted.** Marques delivers
the intro, walk-around, interior, and the Model S Plaid comparison; he then explicitly
hands the **driving/performance segment to co-host Miles** ("I'm going to hand this over
to Miles... he's going to go over driving"). Only Marques's portion is persona data;
**Miles's driving section is quarantined** below.

## Key claims (Marques — persona data)
- [2026-02-19] Deliberately reviews the ZR1 through the **"tech angle"** — heavy
  comparison to the Tesla Model S Plaid — because that's Auto Focus's lane. (Marques)
- [2026-02-19] The ZR1 is a **"performance bargain"**: ~$200k+ for 1,000+ hp (5.5L
  flat-plane-crank twin-turbo V8, rear-wheel drive) delivering performance that matches
  or exceeds cars 2–5× the price — the same accessibility jump the Model S Plaid brought
  to 1,000-hp street cars. (Marques)
- [2026-02-19] Reprises his (self-described "hot take") that the **Model S Plaid is an
  American muscle-car success story**, and extends it: the C8 is likewise an American
  (Chevy) success story that built out its mid-engine platform up the lineup. (Marques)
- [2026-02-19] Like the Plaid, the ZR1 is **ridiculously practical for its performance**:
  the rear deck-lid trunk behind the engine swallows a duffel + backpack + hard-shell
  carry-on, or a full set of golf clubs (reaching around the fixed carbon wing, whose
  downforce he'll happily trade for). The front "trunk" is unusable — a heat spreader
  sits right there. (Marques)
- [2026-02-19] Huge mechanical grip from 345-section rear Pilot Sport 4S ("grip amazing,
  just like they did on my Plaid"); interior is track-focused-racy yet still a livable
  Corvette — improved HVAC buttons, wireless Android Auto, twin wireless chargers (one
  hinged to stop the phone flying out under 1,000-hp acceleration), camera rearview.
  (Marques)
- [2026-02-19] It's a **"split personality"** car: under ~3,000 RPM shockingly smooth,
  docile, and easy to drive — thousand-hp cars 10 years ago never were — while the
  flat-plane crank gets Ferrari-like loud up top; the ZR1 "brings this level of
  performance down to a place we've kind of never seen it before." (Marques)
