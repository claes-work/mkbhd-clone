# iPhone 8 Review: Skip this Great Phone! (@mkbhd, 2017-09-30)

## Summary
A deliberately paradoxical verdict: the iPhone 8 is genuinely a really great phone — and he tells you to
**probably skip it**, only because the iPhone X is right around the corner and just better. The clone-relevant
craft is **reviewing-in-a-vacuum** (judging it as if the X didn't exist) and a sharp "**nice, not first, but
nice**" take on Apple adopting Qi wireless charging.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-09-30] **The paradox: "a really great phone… probably skip it."** "This is actually a really great
  phone in a lot of ways, but it'll go under the radar — and the only reason I'm telling you to probably skip
  it is because of the iPhone X; it's just better. But I'll try to **review this phone in a vacuum, as if we
  don't know it's about to be one-upped.**" A distinctive review discipline (judge the thing on its own, then
  contextualize).
- [2017-09-30] **"Nice, not first, but nice" — Apple adopting Qi.** "The glass back's main reason is
  wireless charging — Qi, so any pad at Starbucks / IKEA tables / a Samsung user's bedside works. Clearly not
  first, but still nice — it doesn't have to be first to be nice. And it's really nice it's the *Qi standard*
  and not proprietary, so we'll see Qi accessories everywhere. **If the iPhone can make the Apple Watch the
  most popular watch in the world, it can make Qi popular too. Thanks Apple.**" His late-but-mainstreams-it
  read of Apple's role in standards adoption.
- [2017-09-30] **The display disappointment — "still 1080p IPS in 2017."** "The displays are mostly the same:
  still IPS, same resolution, now with True Tone (adjusts color temp to the room — technically less accurate
  but looks better; I leave it on unless I'm editing/color matters). But **no ProMotion, no OLED, no
  resolution bump — still 1080p in 2017. You could say I'm disappointed with the (lack of a) display upgrade.**
  Still, I guess, the best IPS display in a smartphone." A pointed complaint even in a positive review.
- [2017-09-30] **The glass-back tradeoffs, honestly.** "Heavier, grippier, more fingerprinty, and more
  breakable than aluminum — even the strongest glass ever is glass. Everything's now under the glass (the
  logo, text, flash — all smooth). Space gray is a fingerprint magnet → a dbrand skin might look better than
  the glossy anyway." The recurring glass-slipperiness/skins thread. Speakers "25% louder — more a clarity
  than loudness difference."

## Notable verbatim quotes
