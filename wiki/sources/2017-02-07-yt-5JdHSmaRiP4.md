# LG UltraFine 5K Review: 15 Million Pixels! (@mkbhd, 2017-02-07)

## Summary
The full review of the LG UltraFine 5K (the display Apple co-developed after killing the Cinema Display).
Verdict: **"it depends on who you are"** — a great panel wrapped in caveats. A clean example of his
segmented-verdict method (name exactly which buckets of buyers should and shouldn't get it), plus honest
reporting of two real flaws (under-powered charging while editing; router-proximity flickering).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-02-07] **"It depends on who you are" — the segmented verdict.** "Is this a good enough replacement
  for the Cinema Display legacy? My answer is it depends on who you are — let me explain." Refuses a
  one-size verdict and instead maps it to buyer buckets. The panel itself: "objectively, undeniably awesome"
  (5K, 218 ppi, P3, 500 nits) — "the rest can be forgiven when the panel looks so good."
- [2017-02-07] **Who should NOT buy it (named honestly).** (1) "If you don't have a 2016/2017 MacBook Pro,
  even with the right adapter you cap at ~4K — you bought a 5K display, get your money's worth." (2) "If you
  **edit video on your laptop**" — and the under-powered-charging flaw: "editing in Final Cut with the
  laptop 'charging' the whole time, the battery still drained from 100% to 4% by the time the render
  finished; it says 85W but Dave2D measured ~60W." (3) "If your setup is within 2–3 ft of a Wi-Fi router —
  poor shielding causes flickering/reboots; LG says they're fixing it and will retrofit." Reports flaws
  others surfaced (credits Dave2D) plus his own ("even I've had blinking problems a reboot fixed").
- [2017-02-07] **Who SHOULD buy it.** "If you have a new MacBook Pro and just want one monitor to plug in
  with one cable and get way more real estate — this is an awesome pick; the panel is amazing." The clean
  one-cable (Thunderbolt 3: video down, power back up) setup is the appeal.
- [2017-02-07] **His own setup (production).** "I'm coming from a pair of ASUS 32" 4K monitors — I've always
  been a dual-monitor person — and I run **two of these on the MacBook Pro, all three screens full-time**."
  A production-workflow datapoint (the Mac-Pro-replacement experiment continuing).
- [2017-02-07] **The honest bottom line.** "I wish I could say it's an Apple-logo away from being the next
  Cinema Display, but it's not quite there — a great panel, hit-or-miss surrounding hardware, and whether you
  fit those buckets decides it." (Signs off: "you could say it's ultra-fine.")
