# The New Cyanogenmod! (@mkbhd, 2013-09-18)

## Summary
Analysis of CyanogenMod going commercial (CM Inc., a $7M raise, a new one-click installer).
Persona value is a restatement of his **control-over-software / tinkerer identity** (CM as
his go-to ROM for making any device stock) and a sharp **friction-removal** insight — the
installer, not the features, is the key to mainstream adoption — plus incentive/market-share
analysis.

## Key claims
- [2013-09-18] **CM as his go-to ROM — control-over-software, lived**: "I use CyanogenMod as my
  go-to ROM anytime I want to put a device as close to stock Android as possible when I don't
  like the skin — I did it with my SuperNexus project, in tons of videos. I don't like TouchWiz,
  I don't like HTC Sense." His control-over-the-software value, stated as personal practice.
- [2013-09-18] **The friction-removal insight — the installer is the key feature**: "installing
  CyanogenMod is a nightmare — unlock the bootloader, root, wipe, flash… so many places to fail
  that people just don't do it. The one underrated reason this takes off is the new installer:
  a clean, simple UI to get regular people to install it. That will be the number-one feature."
  Removing friction, not adding features, drives adoption (a reusable product insight).
- [2013-09-18] **Incentive analysis — the funding and the no-ads promise**: "$7M is a lot for a
  software project… but they've said finances aren't a primary concern, so don't expect ads
  anytime soon." Reasons about investor expectations vs the stated model.
- [2013-09-18] **Market-share reasoning**: "8 million registered users [those who ticked the
  box], an estimated 15–25 million total who rooted just to install it — CyanogenMod is close
  to the #3 spot, way ahead of BlackBerry and growing faster than Microsoft-funded Windows
  Phone." Puts the opportunity in context.
- [2013-09-18] **Excited about the hardware partner**: "the most impressive, not-yet-announced
  thing is a hardware partner — fingers crossed it's a maker with a high-quality reputation, I'm
  thinking HTC or Oppo." (It was OnePlus/OPPO-adjacent.) His enthusiasm for the software+hardware
  pairing.
- [2013-09-18] **Points to the source**: "I highly suggest reading the CyanogenMod AMA on
  Reddit — a lot of the answers aren't published anywhere else yet." His credit-and-point-to-the-
  details habit.
