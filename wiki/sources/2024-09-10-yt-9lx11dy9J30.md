# iPhone 16/Pro Impressions: The Great Separation! (@mkbhd, 2024-09-10)

## Summary
Marques's same-day first impressions from Apple's September 2024 event (he flew in from
Australia that morning), covering iPhone 16 / 16 Pro, Apple Watch Series 10, and the new AirPods.
The framing thesis — **"The Great Separation"** — is that the physical phone each year is now
just a minor iterative bump, while the real reason to buy (Apple Intelligence) **ships later,
separately**, via software updates the phone launches *without*. He invokes one of his **golden
rules — never buy something based on the promise of future software updates** — which makes this
year's pairing awkward. Big hardware talking points: the new **Camera Control** button and the
persistent **60 Hz display on an $800 flagship**. He also skewers the **AirPods Max "fake
upgrade"** (new colors + USB-C, nothing else). Solo → persona/voice data. Disclosed **Anker**
sponsor read.

## Key claims
- [2024-09-10] **The Great Separation** (headline thesis): "each year of the new physical phone…
  is just a small improvement… but on the other side is this rapidly improving ball of all this
  AI stuff that is not launching on the phone but that is the totally separate really big reason
  to get the phone." The iPhone 16 ships on iOS 18.0 with **no Apple Intelligence features** —
  the "shiny brand new iPhone" boots with "the old Siri"; features trickle in from 18.1 onward.
- [2024-09-10] Invokes his **golden rule**: "**never buy something based on the promise of future
  software updates**" — which is exactly the bind this launch creates.
- [2024-09-10] **iPhone 16** (base): like the 15 with new colors; cameras re-stacked vertically
  to enable **spatial video/photo**; A16→A18 (~20–30% gains, 2× neural engine, 8 GB RAM for AI);
  larger battery but **Apple stated zero numbers** on stage (compare page implies 20→22 hrs
  video). Gains the **Action Button** and the new **Camera Control**.
- [2024-09-10] **Camera Control** (his other big highlight): a capacitive, pressure-sensitive
  Sapphire "tiny trackpad" with a taptic two-stage click; opens the camera, shutter, swipe to
  change settings; third-party apps (Snapchat/Instagram) can use it. Technically impressive, but
  he's "curious how many people actually use this regularly" (echoes his Action Button skepticism),
  and notes case-makers now must design around it. Also flags the two-stage shutter press won't
  arrive in the camera app until a later software update — "that's just weird too."
- [2024-09-10] Recurring complaint: **still a 60 Hz display on an $800 flagship in 2024** — "that's
  really disappointing for me personally." Suggests the **base model is probably the better buy for
  most people** over the Pro.
- [2024-09-10] **iPhone 16 Pro**: slightly bigger (6.3" / 6.9") via thinner bezels; A18 Pro
  (~20–30% gains), USB 3 speeds, longest-ever iPhone battery life (unquantified). New/updated
  cameras: 48 MP main, 48 MP ultrawide, and a **5X tele now on the smaller Pro too**; **4K 120 fps**
  slow-mo straight from the camera — which "totally proves to me that they could handle processing
  enough to shoot 8K 30… but they're just choosing not to."
- [2024-09-10] **Apple Watch Series 10**: "mostly slightly new again" — 10% thinner/lighter, larger
  wide-angle OLED, S10 chip, titanium replaces stainless; new depth/tide apps; **gyroscope-only
  sleep-apnea detection** (pending FDA). Plus a satin-black **Ultra 2** he admits he'll buy for no
  real reason ("suddenly they have my shipping address and credit card info").
- [2024-09-10] **AirPods**: AirPods 4 (redesigned, $129, or $179 with ANC + speaker case) — good
  sub-$200 ANC target. AirPods Pro 2 gains **clinical-grade hearing-aid** certification. **AirPods
  Max = "the fake upgrade of the day"**: after 4 years and still $550, they get new colors and
  finally **USB-C** — but keep the H1 chip, no on/off button, and "that stupid case." "Did the
  entire AirPods Max team go into a coma for 4 years?" (He'll still upgrade — tired of Lightning.)
