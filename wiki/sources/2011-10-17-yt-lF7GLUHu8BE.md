# The Blue Snowball [Review] (@mkbhd, 2011-10-17)

## Summary
A review of the Blue Snowball USB microphone that doubles as a **giveaway video sponsored
by Blue** — disclosed in the second sentence, with the prize shipped directly by the
manufacturer to a randomly-picked commenter. The review itself is the first in the corpus
to show the **relative-rating methodology from three weeks earlier applied mechanically**:
he scores the mic on three separate axes (audio quality, construction, ease of use), each
with its own justification, before arriving at an overall number — and he lets the axes
disagree sharply.

## Key claims
- [2011-10-17] **Sponsorship disclosed up front**: "this video is actually a giveaway video
  sponsored by Blue… one random comment will be picked to win their own Blue Snowball in
  their color of choice directly from Blue." Dated evidence of a manufacturer-sponsored
  giveaway with the sponsor named immediately rather than buried.
- [2011-10-17] **Sub-scores, stated separately**: audio quality **7/10** ("for sound quality
  it could be better, but then again this is better than most other USB microphones"),
  construction **7.5/10** (plastic — "but it's fantastic plastic"), ease of use **10/10**
  ("hands down one of the easiest microphones to use"), **overall 8–8.5/10**.
- [2011-10-17] The scoring is explicitly **scoped to a budget and an audience**: "this is a
  great mic for beginners on YouTube," "especially if you're looking for professional
  sounding audio on a tight budget" — the disclosed-baseline rule from
  [[2011-09-30-yt-dTPZ2tuyWFw]] in practice, 17 days later.
- [2011-10-17] **Claim consistency, self-noted**: he calls it "easily the best USB
  microphone in this budget, as you might remember I've said that in a previous video" —
  explicitly tying the verdict back to his prior reviewed set rather than restating it as
  fresh.
- [2011-10-17] **Demonstration-as-evidence**: the review is voiced *through* the reviewed
  mic — "what you're listening to right now is unedited audio from the Blue Snowball…
  plug and play, no enhancements whatsoever." The product's own output is the test.
- [2011-10-17] Practical verdict details: three switchable polar patterns (cardioid,
  −10 dB padded cardioid, omnidirectional) described as more than a beginner needs but "a
  great place to start and start learning how these things work"; the bundled plastic
  tripod judged adequate enough that no stand upgrade is required.
