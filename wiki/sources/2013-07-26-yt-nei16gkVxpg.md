# Ubuntu Edge: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-26)

## Summary
An explainer on Canonical's record-breaking Indiegogo campaign for the Ubuntu Edge — a
crowdfunded **convergence** phone (Android in your pocket, full Ubuntu desktop when docked).
Persona value is in his continued **convergence / phone-as-desktop fascination** and his
reuse of the **Halo-product / "Nexus of Ubuntu"** framing. Dated (the campaign failed).

## Key claims
- [2013-07-26] **Convergence fascination, restated**: "in your pocket it runs a normal phone OS
  like Android; plug it into a display and keyboard and it runs a full-fledged desktop Ubuntu,
  non-stripped-down. The two OSes talk to each other via 'Convergence' — search contacts in the
  Ubuntu desktop and you're searching your Android phone's contacts." His recurring one-device-
  becomes-your-desktop interest (cf. the 2013 Ubuntu Phone video).
- [2013-07-26] **The Halo-product framing again**: "consider the Ubuntu Edge the *Nexus of Ubuntu
  OS* — a limited-edition high-end phone that shows what the experience should be, for other OEMs
  to follow. It's not meant to be an iPhone rival or sell well; it's for the enthusiasts." His
  reusable Halo-product / direction-setting-device lens.
- [2013-07-26] **Puts the crowdfunding ask in perspective**: "a record $32 million sounds insane,
  but compared to the actual cost of making a smartphone, and for a limited-edition device, it's
  not all that much." Reasons about the number rather than reacting to it.
- [2013-07-26] **Genuine enthusiasm — "where can I give you my money?"**: praises the spec sheet
  (sapphire crystal, 4 GB RAM, 128 GB, silicon-anode battery) and the single-piece-metal design
  ("drop-dead gorgeous… looks a lot like the Oppo Find 5"). His excitement for ambitious hardware.
- [2013-07-26] **Constructive critique + points to the source**: wishes for a real physical dock
  and more prototyping, and links Mark Shuttleworth's Reddit AMA "about the business and
  technology behind the scenes." His credit-your-sources / point-to-the-details habit.
