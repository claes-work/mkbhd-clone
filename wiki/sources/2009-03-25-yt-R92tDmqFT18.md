# HD Tutorial: Callouts in Camtasia Studio 6 (@mkbhd, 2009-03-25)

## Summary
A production-tooling screencast (Marques, age 15) walking through **callouts** — Camtasia
Studio 6's annotation system — which he uses heavily to make his tutorials "look professional."
He tours the full callout menu: **arrows** (single/two-sided/indented), **blur** callouts
(stacked to hide personal information), **highlighter** and **highlight-rectangle**,
**transparent hotspot** (which he skips because it "doesn't work in YouTube," only PowerPoint),
**notepad**, **bubble/square/spotlight**, and the **text callout** — his most-used one. He then
demonstrates **custom callouts**, importing an image he captured with the **Windows Vista Snip
tool** as a PNG/JPEG overlay. He closes by showing his signature move: **tilting the video to
one side with zoom-and-pan and placing the callout on the opposite side**. Ends with his
standard **request-driven** call to action (PM/subscribe → he'll make a request video and link
your channel) and teases an upcoming **music video** and more Camtasia tutorials.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-25] In **Camtasia Studio 6**, callouts are the annotation system; the **text
  callout** is the one Marques uses most, and he prefers the font **Calibri**.
- [2009-03-25] Practical uses: **stacked blur callouts** to redact personal info; **spotlight**
  to gray out everything except a focal point; **highlighter** for on-screen text.
- [2009-03-25] The **transparent hotspot** callout is deliberately avoided because it "doesn't
  work in YouTube" (PowerPoint-only).
- [2009-03-25] **Custom callouts** can be any imported image (e.g. a **Vista Snip** screenshot
  saved as PNG/JPEG), enabling arbitrary overlays.
- [2009-03-25] His signature composition: **tilt the clip via zoom-and-pan and offset the
  callout to the opposite side** of the frame.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "the callouts are basically a little text boxes or different things that you can put over a video ... they'll work very very well and make your video look professional"
