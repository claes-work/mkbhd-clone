# Xiaomi Mi Mix 2: The Bezel-less Sequel! (@mkbhd, 2017-09-11)

## Summary
A look at the Mi Mix 2 — the sequel to the concept phone he loved (his 2nd-most-viewed video of the year).
The clone-relevant read: the Mi Mix 1 **moved the industry's needle but didn't sell** (unwieldy, unfamiliar
ROM), so every Mi Mix 2 change is about making the vision **approachable and viable** — the concept brought
down to earth.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-09-11] **The Mi Mix 1 moved the needle but didn't sell — the sequel fixes that.** "As beautiful as
  the original was on video, that didn't translate to sales — it's a huge, unwieldy phone running an
  unfamiliar ROM; people weren't rushing to import it. Every change from Mix 1 to Mix 2 is to make it a more
  **approachable, more viable** sequel." A concept-vs-commercial-viability read (impact ≠ sales).
- [2017-09-11] **"Bringing the extreme design down to earth."** "They did a once-over to tame anything too
  extreme: the piezoelectric frame-speaker is replaced by a **real front-facing earpiece** (more practical,
  directional sound); smaller 6" body (same-ish display as the new iPhone but way easier to grip one-handed);
  the selfie camera's chin glass is darkened to blend in; the fingerprint reader is now super-fast and in the
  ideal middle-of-the-back spot." Values the practical refinements over the showpiece.
- [2017-09-11] **Ceramic body (the Essential comparison) + upgraded camera.** "Ceramic back, aluminum sides —
  a dense, premium material (like the Essential Phone), still shiny/slippery/fingerprinty and heavier. The
  camera's upgraded (12 MP Sony IMX386, OIS) — a real improvement, if not quite Samsung/Apple level; no dual
  cameras." Fair, comparative read.
- [2017-09-11] **The real question is the MIUI ROM — and the update-speed benchmark.** "With hardware this
  impressive, it comes down to whether you can stand the MIUI software on top of Android; they've made it more
  familiar (split-screen, the quick-ball). It's on 7.1.1 — **the benchmark is how fast they move to Android
  8.0; I'm keeping tabs on all the big phones for that.**" His software-update-speed metric.
- [2017-09-11] **The goal is global viability.** "The changes are more to put it on a global scale — 37 bands
  (22 LTE), dual-SIM — so you can put your SIM in it anywhere and it works." Reads the strategy behind the
  refinements. (A special edition adds a ceramic unibody + 8 GB RAM.)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Even though it had an awesome super-thin-bezel form factor that helped move the needle for the entire
> smartphone industry… people weren't exactly rushing to import this and make it their daily driver."
> "Basically a once-over of the entire phone to take anything that was a little bit extreme… and bring it
> down to earth."
