# Nokia Lumia 1020 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-08-20)

## Summary
The full review of the 41 MP PureView Lumia 1020 — the camera phone he bought (yellow) for
its camera alone. Persona value: his **optics love** (calls it the best smartphone camera
period), a **teach-the-camera** breakdown (manual Pro Cam controls, 38 MP + 5 MP
oversampling, reframe-after-the-fact), the **display-shows-pixels** disappointment (one-
critical-flaw + pixel-person), and the honest **Windows-Phone tough-sell** from his
disclosed Google-user perspective.

## Key claims
- [2013-08-20] **"The best camera on any smartphone right now, period"**: "one of those cameras
  that makes you want to get out and take pictures because you know it's going to turn out well
  every time." His optics-first enthusiasm (real Xenon flash, Carl Zeiss glass, OIS).
- [2013-08-20] **Teach-the-camera — manual controls + oversampling**: praises the Pro Cam app's
  manual exposure/shutter/ISO/focus/WB and shutter-priority mode ("the viewfinder is super
  smart"); explains the **38 MP full-res + 5 MP oversampled** dual-save and the **crop-and-
  reframe-after-the-fact** trick. His explain-the-mechanism register on cameras.
- [2013-08-20] **Honest weaknesses — color + workflow**: "the biggest strength is sharpness…
  the biggest weakness is color — auto white balance was hit or miss, sometimes way wrong,
  even more apparent in video"; plus a big between-shot delay, no burst, no HDR, and "a 41 MP
  sensor with a 720p viewfinder" (line-skipping/moiré). Balanced even about a camera he loves.
- [2013-08-20] **Display-shows-pixels — one-critical-flaw + pixel-person**: "the highest-end
  Windows Phone you can buy and you can see pixels on the display — disappointing, considering
  I've seen much better displays in much less expensive devices." His display standard, applied.
- [2013-08-20] **The Windows-Phone tough-sell — perspective-disclosure**: "as a person who uses
  a lot of Google services, this is a tough sell — Microsoft and Google aren't friends, and
  there's plenty missing from Windows Phone I'd love to have. You have to decide if you want the
  best camera badly enough to switch. If I were already a Windows Phone person, I'd be all over
  this." His disclose-your-baseline honesty (cf. the Lumia 925 review).
- [2013-08-20] **The camera-hump ergonomic honesty**: "the hump is so big it affects using the
  phone — it never sits flat on a table, so count out texting while it's lying down." A lived,
  specific downside.
