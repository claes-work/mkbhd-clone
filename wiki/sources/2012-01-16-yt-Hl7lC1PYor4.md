# Grado SR80i Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-16)

## Summary
A review of the $99 Grado SR80i that doubles as a **teaching video on open-back headphones**
— what the design does, who it's for, and the one large cost that comes with it. He explains
soundstage in concrete spatial terms, argues that the SR80i's cheap-feeling parts are a
deliberate cost allocation toward sound, and then does the thing that makes the page worth
keeping: **demonstrates the downside on camera** by letting the viewer hear how much these
leak.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-16] **Open-back explained by what you hear, not by physics**: open-back gives "a much
  bigger sound stage, which means you can hear the cymbals behind you, the trumpet to your right,
  the saxophone to your left — and you can snap to the beat just like you're in the room with the
  musicians recording."
- [2012-01-16] **Category claim with a mechanism**: "as an open back pair of headphones, of
  course these sound much better than closed back headphones for the same price" — contrasted
  explicitly with Beats by Dre and V-MODA Crossfade LPs, which seal and isolate.
- [2012-01-16] **Genre-scoped verdict**: excellent for vocals, jazz and orchestral music and
  "natural sounds," but "it might not have the best sound for synths, because those aren't
  natural sounds and it's hard to sound like you're in a room next to a synth." The verdict is
  bounded by content type rather than stated absolutely.
- [2012-01-16] **The downside demonstrated, not just disclosed**: open-backs "are going to sound
  like portable speakers to the person next to you. They leak a lot — let's have a listen," and
  he plays it. Consistent with the show-don't-claim pattern documented across this era.
- [2012-01-16] **Cost-allocation reading of the design** — the analytical move: the headband
  isn't padded and the adjustment has no notches, but "you can see where they saved money on some
  parts of the headphones and put that money saved into the sound quality." He reads a
  spec sheet as a set of deliberate tradeoffs rather than as defects.
- [2012-01-16] **Audience scoped explicitly**: "really good for beginning audiophiles or people
  just starting to get into audio and music and headphones," and "absolutely perfect for someone
  who doesn't necessarily consider themselves an audiophile yet."
- [2012-01-16] Design candour: the "vintage kind of look" is called "a point of contention for
  some people and even a buying point for others" — he declines to arbitrate taste. The split
  Y-cable earns a shrug: "I'm not really sure why they decided to do this, but it's not a deal
  breaker at all."
- [2012-01-16] **Follow-up comparison promised in response to requests**: "I've been asked to
  compare these to some higher-end headphones and I will attempt that."
- [2012-01-16] Likes-gated giveaway at 800, the same mechanic as the ATH-M50 review and payoff.
