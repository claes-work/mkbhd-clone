# Dope Tech: The Hottest Laptop Design! (@mkbhd, 2022-09-30)

## Summary
A solo "Techtember" Dope Tech episode with three items: (1) the **Dell XPS 13 Plus**,
which he calls "probably the hottest laptop design I have ever seen" and, in the same
breath, "probably the purest example of form over function I've ever seen" — an
edge-to-edge keyboard, an invisible haptic trackpad with no visual boundary, a
capacitive touch function row instead of physical F-keys, only two USB-C ports and no
headphone jack, and no thumb notch to open it; (2) the **EcoFlow Delta Pro / Delta II**
portable power stations (this video's **sponsor**), used behind the scenes to power
studio shoots and, in the demo, to add a couple of miles of charge to a stranded EV
(their Rivian) — his answer to the recurring "what if you run out of charge in an EV?"
question; and (3) prototype **haptic VR gloves** on a Quest 2 that let you feel triggers
and grip, which he frames as the bleeding edge of VR immersion. Running MKBHD frame here:
"form over function" as a design-critique lens. Solo video — all persona/voice data;
the EcoFlow segment is a disclosed sponsorship.

## Key claims
- [2022-09-30] The Dell XPS 13 Plus is "probably the hottest laptop design I have ever
  seen" and simultaneously "the purest example of form over function I've ever seen":
  the beautiful design directly makes it "a little bit more difficult to use."
- [2022-09-30] Specific form-over-function trade-offs: the invisible one-piece haptic
  trackpad has no defined boundary, so "when your finger leaves the sensitive surface
  area the mouse just stops working" (other makers visually define the pad for a reason);
  the capacitive touch function row "is an even worse version of a touch bar because it's
  not a screen" — no visual confirmation, smaller targets, no haptics; only two USB-C
  ports, no headphone jack; and no thumb indent to open the lid, sacrificed for a clean
  sharp edge. His takeaway: this is "a better understanding of why more laptops don't
  look just like this one." (Defers benchmark depth to a linked Dave2D review.)
- [2022-09-30] The **EcoFlow** stations power full studio shoots (camera, audio device,
  lights) — e.g. the Ioniq 5 review and this outdoor shoot. The Delta II is ~1 kWh
  (expandable to 3 kWh, daisy-chained), 15 devices at once, 1800 W AC, 100 W USB-C,
  0→80% in ~50 min, rated 3,000 cycles ("like a decade"), five-year warranty, "same types
  of batteries that are in Tesla's vehicles." The Delta Pro is 3.6 kWh / 3,600 W, can back
  up a home, and charges via solar or a Level 2 EV charger.
- [2022-09-30] Recurring EV question answered: unlike a gas car you can refill from a can,
  a dead EV can now be topped up by bringing an actual battery — an EcoFlow Pro plugs into
  the car's charge port ("drawing nearly a thousand watts… add one or two miles real quick
  just to get to the nearest fast charger"). He argues "every roadside assistance vehicle
  should just have one of these now" given how popular EVs are getting.
- [2022-09-30] The prototype **haptic VR gloves** (sense gloves on a Quest 2) deliver up
  to 20 newtons of resistance per finger plus fingertip/palm vibration, letting you feel
  triggers and grip inside VR — but only outward squeezing force, "you don't actually feel
  the object in your hands," and there's no weight simulation. Sold to enterprises
  (Volkswagen, the European Space Agency) at ~€4,500. He predicts other headset makers
  will work on this immersion problem and imagines driving-wheel and ping-pong-spin
  feedback as "the future… endless."
