# iPad Pro Second Impressions! [Apple Pencil] (@mkbhd, 2015-11-15)

## Summary
His "second first impressions" of the iPad Pro — now in-house with the **Apple Pencil** and Smart Keyboard.
Two recurring lenses: **"who is this for?" / "can it replace your laptop?"** (his answer: right now he's
"loving it as a huge iPad" for *consumption*; *production* is a work-in-progress), and a **consumption-vs-
production** split. The Pencil has near-zero lag and good palm rejection but is "a very specific tool a
very small group will buy"; the Smart Keyboard is "definitely no Surface Book." An A9X monster.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-11-15] **Massive, with the big-device benefits.** 12.9" 2732×2048 — "more than a 12"/13"/15"
  MacBook." Bigger battery (10,300 mAh), and **stereo speakers on both sides** ("the stereo image is
  something else since they're so far apart"). But it's heavy (1.57 lb), tiring to hold one-handed, and the
  **home screen looks "a little comical"** with no widgets to fill the space ("the distance between icons is
  more pixels than the entire original iPhone screen").
- [2015-11-15] **A9X monster.** "Benchmarks off the charts… performance so good it removes any lag — iOS at
  its finest."
- [2015-11-15] **Can it replace your laptop? (the pro question).** "If they'd called it the iPad XL, that'd
  be the end of the conversation — but they call it the Pro." The **Smart Keyboard** (Smart Connector,
  MacBook-like short-throw keys) is good for people who type a lot "but it's definitely no Surface Book" and
  lacks a laptop's ergonomics.
- [2015-11-15] **The Apple Pencil.** Charges by sticking out of the Lightning port ("an accident waiting to
  happen"); near-zero lag, good palm rejection, a thinner precise tip — "but I wish it had an eraser… a very
  specific tool that a very small group of people will buy for the extra $100; cross your fingers it gets
  more functional as apps support it."
- [2015-11-15] **Verdict-so-far: consumption yes, production TBD.** "If you're wondering who this is for,
  you're not alone — right now I'm loving it as a huge iPad. Consumption (videos, YouTube, reading) is
  fantastic; production is a work in progress but will get better." (Voice: he opens by vowing to avoid
  "that's what she said" jokes about its size.)
