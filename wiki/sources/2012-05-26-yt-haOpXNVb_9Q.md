# Android 5.0 Feature Wishlist! (@mkbhd, 2012-05-26)

## Summary
A crowdsourced wishlist for the next Android — "I asked a question on Twitter earlier and I got a lot
of interesting responses." He walks his own most-wanted features and, notably, **predicts the next
version will be a point release (4.1) rather than 5.0**, correcting his own video title's premise
mid-video. Several of the requests (a system dark/light theme, adaptive performance profiles) landed
in Android years later, making this a rich dated-prediction source.

## Key claims
- [2012-05-26] **Crowdsourced premise**, stated up front — the wishlist is built from a Twitter prompt
  plus his own picks, with comment additions solicited.
- [2012-05-26] **A system-wide dark/light theme with a master switch** — a request that anticipates
  Android's system dark theme by ~7 years: "I'd like to have the ability to either set a manual master
  switch everything dark or a master switch for everything light… have the whole operating system go
  along with the way you feel about it." He notes the inconsistency of the era (Gmail light-themed, the
  dialer dark-themed, the People app "sticks out like a sore thumb").
- [2012-05-26] **Built-in adaptive performance profiles** — anticipates battery/performance modes:
  "a nighttime mode which underclocks your processor… a worktime mode… a gaming mode which uses more
  processing power," switchable "manually or automatically — maybe by location, or by the Wi-Fi network
  you're on, or by how much battery you have left." A battery/performance tradeoff idea consistent with
  the "better, not bigger" thread.
- [2012-05-26] **Chrome as the stock browser, out of beta** — "it's already one of the best mobile
  browsers out there" (tab/password/bookmark sync); wants it merged into the OS.
- [2012-05-26] **A self-correcting prediction on version numbering** — the notable honesty: despite the
  "5.0" title, "Ice Cream Sandwich is only on about 5% of Android devices right now, still most Android
  devices are on Gingerbread… so maybe the next version of Android will be 4.1, seems a little bit more
  realistic." (Android 4.1 Jelly Bean did follow — the point-release call was right.)
- [2012-05-26] **Praises the multitasking UI with a specific comparison**: "I think it's one of the best
  on any mobile platform, right next to webOS" — crediting a rival platform's card-based multitasking.
- [2012-05-26] **Names a favorite app while criticizing a specific flaw**: "Gmail is easily the best app
  on Android, one of the most amazing apps on any mobile platform — but yet you can't zoom in on an image
  in Gmail, it's just a little bit weird." Love plus a precise nitpick.
- [2012-05-26] **Fragmentation datum**: ICS at ~5% adoption, most devices still on Gingerbread — the
  concrete evidence behind the fragmentation argument he made in [[2012-02-05-yt-Ktoalz6PbXc]].
