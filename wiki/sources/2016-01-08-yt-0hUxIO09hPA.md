# Dope Tech: CES 2016! (@mkbhd, 2016-01-08)

## Summary
A solo Dope Tech episode filmed on the CES 2016 show floor in Las Vegas — Marques's roundup of the coolest gadgets he saw amid the "chaos." He runs through smartphones (few new ones; Huawei's gold Nexus 6P and Mate S), Jaybird's Freedom/X3 Bluetooth earbuds with firmware-baked EQ presets, a wave of 4K cameras (Nikon D500, Panasonic 4K point-and-shoot, Nikon's 360° action cam), the ubiquity of 4K and rare 8K TVs (LG's 98-inch 8K, LG G6 OLED), the $50,000 Sennheiser Orpheus headphone system, drones (DJI black Inspire One, Phantom 3 4K), Griffin's MagSafe USB-C charger, and cars (Chevy Bolt as a real Tesla rival, BMW i8 Spyder, Mercedes and Faraday Future concepts). The through-line is his framing of what CES is actually *for*: not the gadget you'll buy tomorrow, but a glimpse of the future.

## Key claims
- [2016-01-08] There are not a lot of new smartphones at CES anymore; the most noteworthy phone news was Huawei showing up with a subtly-gold Nexus 6P and the Huawei Mate S (6-inch flagship, Huawei Android skin, claimed 2-day battery — which he says he needs to test before trusting the number).
- [2016-01-08] Jaybird's Freedom and X3 Bluetooth earbuds' standout feature is adjustable sound: the MySound app lets you set an EQ preset, but the preset is written into the earbuds' firmware — so once set you no longer need the app and can pair the buds to any other device and keep that sound. He calls this "pretty sick."
- [2016-01-08] The Nikon D500 (4K video, articulating LCD, mic input, HDMI out) is "a huge improvement for them in video" and he sees it as potential YouTube gear; it's a "great distraction" from the pricier D5, which he calls disappointing because it shoots 4K for only up to 3 minutes and doesn't even use the full frame — "that is not dope tech."
- [2016-01-08] Panasonic's 4K point-and-shoot (viewfinder, articulating LCD, programmable lens ring) reminds him of the Sony RX100 he has raved about; he thinks it could be just as good a run-and-gun camera, especially if it doesn't overheat in 4K like the RX100, and for people needing zoom.
- [2016-01-08] 4K is "everywhere" — everyone who makes a screen has a 4K TV; only a few have 8K, and one 8K set he saw "didn't even look that good," probably because they were showing upscaled 1080p with no real 8K content. His highlight was the LG 98-inch 8K TV, which looked incredibly detailed even as just a slideshow.
- [2016-01-08] Many CES items had no price and no release date, and for him that was fine — "the desire is real." He's untroubled by aspirational, unbuyable hardware at a trade show.
- [2016-01-08] The Sennheiser Orpheus ($50,000) is "the 8K TV of audio": he doubts the sound is meaningfully better than a $5,000 pair, but the *experience* is the point — nested in solid marble, tube-amp tubes that rise and glow as they warm up, rope-like cables, huge-but-light headphones. His one-word summary of the listening experience: "clean." He calls it the "8K TV of audio" because it needs content that doesn't really exist yet to be fully worth it.
- [2016-01-08] Best TV at CES in his opinion was the LG G6: clean modern design, electronics housed in the stand, thin bezels, incredible 4K OLED image.
- [2016-01-08] Other show-floor picks: Audio-Technica ATH-A990Z (only heard ~30 seconds, needs his own music to judge), tablets from normal to an 18-inch Samsung "borderline TV-size" tablet, high-tech appliances (he's personally in the market for a refrigerator), a Kodak Super 8 film camera reviving the film-shooting process, DJI's stealthy black Inspire One and a 4K Phantom 3, and Griffin's MagSafe USB-C charger.
- [2016-01-08] On the Griffin MagSafe USB-C charger: 12-inch MacBook users will think "this is exactly what Apple should have included in the packaging," it works great, and you can add MagSafe to other USB-C devices that lacked it.
- [2016-01-08] On cars: the Chevy Bolt is, in his opinion, "the first actual rival to Tesla" — a real vehicle being made, under $40,000, with ~200-mile electric range — as opposed to headline names like Faraday Future. He puts less stock in concept cars (crazy self-driving Mercedes concept, doorless BMW i8 Spyder, the Faraday Future car).
- [2016-01-08] His framing of the event: "You don't really go to CES to see the gadget you're going to buy in the store tomorrow — you go to CES to catch a glimpse of the future, and sometimes you spot some dope tech along the way."
