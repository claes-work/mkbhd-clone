# Smartphone Awards 2024! (@mkbhd, 2024-12-19)

## Summary
The **10th annual** Smartphone Awards — MKBHD's signature year-end format, staged on a set
in front of an audience — handing custom red trophies across 10 categories for phones
released in calendar-year 2024. The throughline this year: **phones have all gotten big**
(the "small" flagship is now bigger than the "big" phone winner of a decade ago), the
**camera race is the closest ever** but the **iPhone still wins**, and the overall
**Phone of the Year is the "boring but does everything right" Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra** —
the phone Marques says he personally daily-drove ~9 months of the year. Solo → persona/voice
data. Notable meta-signal: Marques openly muses about **retiring the Best Small Phone
category** because small phones are effectively extinct.

## Key claims
- [2024-12-19] **10th anniversary** of the Smartphone Awards; format unchanged — 10 categories,
  honorable mentions/runner-ups where deserved, one custom trophy per category winner. He notes
  the awards "skew" toward phones that launched later in the calendar year.
- [2024-12-19] **Best Big Phone → Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra.** Praises the anti-reflective screen
  coating, cameras that don't protrude much, big battery + stylus, One UI 7 / Android 15 beta.
  Runner-ups: ROG Phone 9, Vivo X200 Pro. Notes it launched in January and still deserves it.
- [2024-12-19] **Best Small Phone → iPhone 16** — "a kind of sad one this year" because small
  flagships (iPhone mini, Zenfone) "are all dead now." A 6.1"/6.2" phone now counts as small; he
  points out the 6.2" S24 is bigger than the **5.7" Galaxy Note 4** that won Best Big Phone 10
  years earlier. Praises the base iPhone 16's matte rails, color variety, near-Pro capability —
  though still "mad personally about the lack of high refresh rate."
- [2024-12-19] **Best Camera → iPhone 16 Pro** ("the streak continues"), despite being "just as
  tired of it as you guys are." **No blind camera test this year.** Reasoning: for anyone who
  *relies* on their phone for photos/videos, the answer "just keeps coming back" to iPhone —
  easy, simple, reliable, consistent look, unparalleled 4K30/stabilized/slow-mo video. Strong
  runner-up **Vivo X200 Pro** "comes the closest to an actual real camera shooting experience"
  (50MP main, 50MP ultrawide, 200MP periscope). He wants something to finally "knock it off its
  post" and says we're "closer than ever."
- [2024-12-19] **Best Value → Nothing Phone 2A** ($350): 120 Hz screen with uniform bezels,
  5,000 mAh + 45 W, serviceable 50 MP camera, smooth optimized software, glyphs. Shout-outs:
  **CMF Phone 1** ($200, fun/modular) and Pixel 8a ($500). Frames value via **law of diminishing
  returns** — "How can an $800 phone give you twice as much value as a $400 phone? It just can't."
- [2024-12-19] **Best Battery → Red Magic 10 Pro** (gaming phone): **Snapdragon 8 Elite** + a
  **7,050 mAh battery** = a comfortable two-day phone at 144 Hz, 100 W charging (~0–100 in ~30
  min), plus battery-maintenance features (80% cap, bypass charging). He flags the **Snapdragon 8
  Elite as "dramatically more efficient"** than the prior gen (asks for thumbs-up for a dedicated
  video). Beat out S24 Ultra, Oppo Find X8 Pro (5,900 mAh/80 W), Vivo X200 Pro (6,000 mAh/90 W).
- [2024-12-19] **Best Design → Huawei Mate XT (tri-fold).** Awards audacity/execution over
  flaw-free-but-boring options (S24, Pixel 9). Acknowledges likely durability issues and possible
  covered-up bad press ("probably true"), but a real company shipping a working triple-folding
  phone at volume earns it.
- [2024-12-19] **Best Foldable → Pixel 9 Pro Fold** — the most polished folding-Android experience,
  came close to daily-driving it. Honor Magic V3 gets best raw foldable *hardware* (thin, no
  crease, sturdy hinge). Z Flip 6 still "carries the torch" among flips.
- [2024-12-19] **Most Improved → Pixel 9 Pro Fold** — "a glow up in every single way" over the
  first Pixel Fold (which "didn't even fold flat"). Base iPhone 16 was a rare contender here.
- [2024-12-19] **Bust of the Year → Asus Zenfone 11 Ultra** — notably *not* a bad phone ("it's
  fine"), but it represents a loss: Asus turned the beloved genuinely-small one-handable Zenfone
  into "just another generic big phone." Uses it to lament the death of the small flagship.
- [2024-12-19] **MVP / Phone of the Year → Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra.** "Rewarding overall
  greatness." Runner-ups Vivo X200 Pro and Pixel 9 Pro Fold. Analogy: the S24 Ultra is **"2024
  Scottie Scheffler"** — boring precisely because it's elite at everything (screen, chip, battery,
  cameras, software, support, durability, even the vibration motor and speakers). Says it's the
  phone he "come[s] back to after reviewing a phone more than any other," ~9 months of the year,
  even though he doesn't use the stylus. Separately notes he was **daily-driving a beat-up Pixel
  9 Pro XL** for the extra battery.
