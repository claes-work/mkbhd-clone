# Samsung Galaxy S10 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2019-02-20)

## Summary
Marques's hands-on first impressions of Samsung's Galaxy S10 lineup, which he frames as "the most anticipated phone of 2019 so far" that turns out to be three phones: the Galaxy S10, S10+, and S10e. He focuses on the two flagships (S10 and S10+) and identifies three headline improvements — a next-gen "Infinity-O" hole-punch display (HDR10+ certified, 90%+ screen-to-body), a first move to an under-glass ultrasonic fingerprint reader, and a rear triple-camera system with three focal lengths (main, telephoto, ultra-wide). He runs through the smaller additions Samsung stacks on (bigger batteries, Snapdragon 855, up to 12GB RAM / 1TB storage plus microSD expansion, ceramic-back option, Wi-Fi 6, retained headphone jack and Bixby button, IP68, fast/wireless charging), plus Wireless PowerShare reverse charging and the redesigned One UI software. He notes the cheaper S10e ($750, iPhone XR answer) has a separate video, and mentions a barely-shown S10 5G that Samsung wouldn't let him turn on or hold. Overall he's impressed and "pretty into it" at $900/$1000, while repeatedly deferring verdicts to the full review.

## Key claims
- [2019-02-20] The Galaxy S10 launch is actually three phones: Galaxy S10, Galaxy S10+, and Galaxy S10e; this video focuses on the two flagship S10 and S10+.
- [2019-02-20] The three main improvements on the S10 are the display, the fingerprint sensor, and the cameras — plus many small ones.
- [2019-02-20] Display sizes are 6.1-inch on the S10 and 6.4-inch on the S10+; Samsung continues its multi-year run of having the best displays in any phone.
- [2019-02-20] The display has a 90%+ screen-to-body ratio, high resolution, a 19:9 tall aspect ratio, and is now HDR10+ certified; Samsung renamed it the "next-level Infinity display."
- [2019-02-20] The front camera cutout is a laser-cut hole in the AMOLED panel; the S10 has a single 10MP selfie camera, and the S10+ adds a depth sensor (dual cutout). There is no ultra-wide selfie camera.
- [2019-02-20] Samsung's hole-punch cutout looks slightly smaller than Honor's (which was on the left; Samsung's is top-right); Samsung ships wallpapers darkened in the top-right corner and a front-camera animation to make it less jarring — meaning Samsung can now claim it has "technically never done a notch."
- [2019-02-20] The S10 officially moves to an ultrasonic under-the-glass fingerprint reader, unlike prior optical in-display sensors (e.g. OnePlus 6T); ultrasonic advantages: faster, no bright light needed (better in dim light/at night), and it can read fingerprints before the display wakes.
- [2019-02-20] The S10 adds a rear triple camera with three focal lengths — main, telephoto, and super-wide — which Marques has been waiting for and hopes Apple adopts next.
- [2019-02-20] Camera specs: 12MP main with dual aperture f/1.5 or f/2.4 and OIS; telephoto at f/2.4 with OIS; ultra-wide at f/2.2 with a 123-degree field of view and no OIS. Software lets you seamlessly zoom and auto-switch between cameras (as Apple, Huawei, LG do), and it keeps intelligent scene recognition.
- [2019-02-20] Batteries grew: S10 is 3400 mAh and S10+ is 4100 mAh.
- [2019-02-20] Snapdragon 855 is the one constant across the lineup.
- [2019-02-20] Pricing/config: S10 starts at $900 (8GB RAM / 128GB, also an 8/512 option); S10+ starts at $1000 (8GB/128GB), can be specced to a ceramic back, an 8/512 version, or a 1TB model with 12GB RAM; Samsung retains microSD expansion (up to 1.5TB total combining 1TB internal + 512GB card).
- [2019-02-20] The S10e is Samsung's answer to the iPhone XR, with trade-offs (side-mounted regular fingerprint sensor, slightly smaller display) landing at $750; covered in a separate video.
- [2019-02-20] Samsung also revealed a Galaxy S10 5G — a larger S10 with a 6.7-inch screen, huge battery, and higher price — but wouldn't let Marques turn it on or hold it, leading him to suspect the unit shown may have been a non-functional model; allegedly launching Q2 2019.
- [2019-02-20] The lineup retains IP68, fast charging, fast wireless charging, the headphone jack, Wi-Fi 6, and the Bixby button.
- [2019-02-20] New "Wireless PowerShare" (reverse wireless charging) delivers slow 4.5W Qi charging to other devices (like the Huawei Mate 20 Pro did); Marques calls it gimmicky, useful mainly for accessories like the new wireless Galaxy Buds or emergencies. Word on the street is Apple may add it for new AirPods with a wireless case.
- [2019-02-20] The phones run a redesigned One UI that is visually quite different from prior Samsung skins, moving menu items, quick toggles, and controls to the bottom for reachability on large phones; Marques finds it more well-designed overall, though some elements are "cartoonishly huge" and he'd prefer them smaller/subtler.
- [2019-02-20] New color options; Marques finds none amazing (green is "kind of cool") and expects to pick black; all are glass and will show fingerprints.
