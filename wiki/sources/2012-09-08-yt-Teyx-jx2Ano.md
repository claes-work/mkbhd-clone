# Asus Zenbook Prime Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-09-08)

## Summary
A review of the Asus Zenbook Prime UX32VD — the successor to the UX31 he'd praised in March — and the
laptop he uses himself for taking notes in class. The review turns on a single, cleanly-stated verdict
principle: **there is exactly one real reason to buy it over the previous model, and it's the 1080p
display**; everything else is a bonus. Also notable for calling out a rare ultrabook feature
(upgradeable RAM and storage) and for grounding the recommendation in his own student use.

## Key claims
- [2012-09-08] **"There is only one real reason you buy this over the UX31"** — the review's spine:
  "it's not the massive specs, it's not the 6-hour battery, it's not even the backlit keyboard… it's the
  display. This 13-inch ultrabook is rocking a 1920×1080 IPS display with a matte finish — it's
  beautiful, very color-accurate, very bright." Reduces the buying decision to the single differentiator
  (and, characteristically, that differentiator is the screen).
- [2012-09-08] **Praises a rare-for-the-class feature**: "with most ultrabooks the RAM and storage are
  soldered to the motherboard so they're not upgradeable, but they are here" — 8 GB upgradeable RAM plus
  an SSD-for-OS and a 500 GB 7 mm drive. Repairability/upgradeability flagged as a genuine plus, an early
  dated instance of a value that recurs in his later coverage.
- [2012-09-08] **Own-gear, student-use grounding**: "I happen to use this guy for note-taking in school,
  so it goes everywhere with me, fits in my backpack, and I don't have to worry about it bumping into
  textbooks with that magnesium case." He reviews the machine he actually carries to class, and pitches
  it "for college, or if you guys are starting university."
- [2012-09-08] **Honest about gaming scope**: "you can get away with some light to mid-range gaming — I
  wouldn't say Crysis, but it can handle Minecraft, World of Warcraft, a little Need for Speed." Bounds
  the capability rather than overclaiming the dedicated GPU (Nvidia GT 620M, 1 GB).
- [2012-09-08] **The build praised specifically**: 3.2 lb magnesium-alloy body, USB 3.0, full-size media
  card reader and HDMI, and the side "vents" that are actually high-quality speakers — the same
  spun-metal finish as the high-end Asus Transformer tablets.
- [2012-09-08] **Topical framing**: made "in honor of recent events that have the word HD in them" — the
  Motorola Razr HD and Kindle Fire HD announcements that week — a small dated instance of him hooking a
  review to the news cycle (and to his own "HD" identity).
- [2012-09-08] Recommendation stated broadly but honestly scoped: "a laptop I can pretty much recommend
  for anyone… thin, light, really good for school."
