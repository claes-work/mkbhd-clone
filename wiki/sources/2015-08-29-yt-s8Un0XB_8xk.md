# OnePlus 2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-08-29)

## Summary
The full OnePlus 2 review after ~3 weeks as his daily driver — "ignore the flagship-killer hype, look at
the device." A rich beliefs source: an explicit **anti-thin / fill-the-body-with-battery** plea, his
**"five pillars of a great smartphone package"** (performance, camera, display, battery, build), a
measured **USB-C-is-the-future-but-inconvenient-now** take, and a sharp **NFC-omission critique**. He also
calls back his own "Perfect Smartphone" video ("we measure phones by what's wrong with them") and shows
the full-time-user's-eye for flaws reviews miss. At $389, "pretty damn impressive."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-08-29] **Anti-thin — fill the body with battery (stated as a plea).** "I like that they didn't try
  to go super thin. Dear every smartphone manufacturer: if you're using a large camera module — which you
  probably are — **feel free to fill out the rest of the phone's body with battery**. It won't make the
  phone too heavy or thick, and everyone will appreciate the extra battery. Thank you."
- [2015-08-29] **The five pillars framework.** "When judging the OnePlus 2 as a complete package, it
  satisfies my five pillars of a great smartphone: **great performance, great camera, great display, great
  battery life, and great build quality** — and it nails all of those." (The Big Four + build.)
- [2015-08-29] **Measure-by-what's-wrong (self-callback).** "Like I mentioned in that Perfect Smartphone
  video, we tend to measure smartphones by what's wrong with them — the worst parts here are the weak
  (mono) speaker and the lack of wireless charging and NFC."
- [2015-08-29] **The NFC critique (a signature analogy).** "Not including a super-cheap feature like NFC in
  a new smartphone because people don't use it is kind of like building a car and not including reverse
  lights because people usually drive forward. Technically true, but come on." (A dealbreaker for mobile-
  payment users; not for him.)
- [2015-08-29] **USB-C: future, but inconvenient now.** "It totally is the future, but that means in the
  present it's a little inconvenient — unless you have a 2015 MacBook/Chromebook Pixel, you don't have
  USB-C chargers lying around… I kind of miss fast charging, but the battery's good enough that I'm not
  charging mid-day, so I'm down with it."
- [2015-08-29] **The 1080p guessing game.** OnePlus sent it with no specs, so he tried to guess 1080p-vs-QHD
  by eye — "I said quad HD… took a screenshot later, it's 1080p." "A great 1080p display, one of the best
  in the business" — and 1080p helps battery/performance (Snapdragon 810, 4 GB; "never got hot on me once").
- [2015-08-29] **Full-time-user's eye + control-over-software.** Battery "B+" (a huge jump from the "D+"
  S6); notices things reviews miss (a too-weak vibration motor made him miss calls); praises deep Oxygen OS
  customization ("make this phone work exactly the way you want"). "The worst thing about this phone is how
  hard it is to buy one."
