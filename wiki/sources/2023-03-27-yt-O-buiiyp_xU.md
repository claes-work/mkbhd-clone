# Dope Tech: Better than Expected! (@mkbhd, 2023-03-27)

## Summary
A themed solo Dope Tech: three products he had "rock bottom super low expectations for"
that each cleared that bar in some way — while he explicitly does **not** recommend
buying any of them. (1) **Amazon Astro**, "basically an Alexa speaker on wheels," which
he wouldn't buy but which impressed him with confident navigation, a decent boombox
speaker, and uncanny voice-triangulation face-pointing (4/10). (2) The **Huawei Watch
Buds** (~$550), a smartwatch that flips open to reveal tiny earbuds — a vehicle for his
recurring **two-in-one theory** ("anytime you combine two product categories into one
product it has to be worse at… one of those things"); here it's a good watch with weak
earbuds, sold as pure convenience (7/10). (3) The **Shift Moonwalker** motorized "world's
fastest shoes" (robotic rollerblades, $1,400), which work as advertised but are heavy,
loud, and beaten by hoverboards/electric skateboards on top speed and energy (5/10).
Running frames: the low-expectations-exceeded structure and the two-in-one compromise law.
Solo video — all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2023-03-27] Episode thesis: all three items had "rock bottom super low expectations"
  and "managed to exceed those expectations in some way" — but "it doesn't mean they're
  good products; in fact I don't even recommend buying any of the things… in this video."
- [2023-03-27] **Amazon Astro** is "basically an Alexa speaker on wheels with a 10-inch
  screen" plus a camera/mic security-cam. Three genuine surprises: (a) confident
  maneuvering — "dramatically better than any Roomba or robot vacuum" (he wishes it had a
  vacuum attachment); (b) a decent "mini boombox on wheels" speaker; (c) shockingly good
  **voice triangulation** — it points its expressive "face" screen right at whoever said
  its name. Long list of reasons not to buy: setup bugs, bad facial recognition (mistakes
  him for others), shin-height video calls (max 42" telescoping camera), laggy steering,
  and the discomfort of "an Amazon camera and microphone follow you around your house."
  Score: **4/10**.
- [2023-03-27] The **Huawei Watch Buds** (~$550) is a premium smartwatch that opens to
  reveal two truly tiny, orientation-agnostic earbuds (magnetic strip; a two-nod gesture
  calibrates left/right). It states his **two-in-one theory**: "anytime you combine two
  product categories into one product it has to be worse at something at one of those
  things." Verdict: "it's mostly bad earbuds" — the watch still gets ~3 days battery and
  feels uncompromised, but the buds have barely-working ANC and a 3-hour battery; pure
  convenience for grabbing a call, "you'll never lose your earbuds — or I mean, I guess
  you could just lose your whole watch." Score: **7/10**.
- [2023-03-27] The **Shift Moonwalker** "world's fastest shoes" are motorized robotic
  rollerblades (eight wheels per foot) that accelerate a normal walking stride by up to
  250% to ~5–7 mph. They "work as advertised" (a controlled, more-stable rollerblading
  feel) but are heavy, loud ("Clump Clump… you can hear someone coming from a long way
  away"), and hard to turn. His comparison verdict: a hoverboard or electric skateboard
  (e.g. a boosted board) beats them on top speed and energy, so the only edge is keeping
  them on more often and the natural walking motion. USB-C charged, water-resistant, 5–7
  mi range, $1,400. Score: **5/10**.
