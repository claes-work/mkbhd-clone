# iPhone 6s Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-09-10)

## Summary
First impressions of the iPhone 6s/6s Plus from the Apple event — same design as the 6 (plus an "s" badge
and a rose-gold-that's-"really more of a pale pink"), with a subtle **BendGate fix** (a stronger 7000-
series aluminum, a couple grams heavier). Three real changes: the A9 chip, a **new 12 MP camera that
finally does 4K** ("this smartphone is now capable of hanging with the heavy hitters… we may have a new
number-one best smartphone camera"), and **3D Touch** (pressure-sensitive context menus). A dated
camera-ranking-watch marker.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-09-10] **Same design + a quiet BendGate fix.** Identical to the iPhone 6 externally (an "s" badge;
  rose gold "really more of a pale pink"), but the 6s Plus is a couple grams heavier — "we can thank
  BendGate for that, since Apple is now using a **7000-series aluminum alloy** to strengthen the body." (A
  callback to his own BendGate explainer.)
- [2015-09-10] **The camera catches up — 12 MP + 4K.** Up from 8 MP; "crispier photos, and 12 MP now means
  a large enough sensor for **4K video** — so this smartphone can hang with the heavy hitters (Galaxy S6,
  Note 5, LG G4) that shoot 4K." OIS now works in video (6s Plus). "If the demo photos are any indication,
  we may have a new number-one best smartphone camera." A 5 MP front camera with a display-flash.
- [2015-09-10] **Live Photos** — captures 1.5 s before/after a shot, viewed by pressing on it.
- [2015-09-10] **3D Touch (= Force Touch on the phone).** A pressure-sensitive display with multiple
  levels — "kind of like an expand / more-info button": 3D-touch a home-screen icon for quick actions, an
  address for a Maps preview, or a message thread to peek in, then press harder to fully expand.
- [2015-09-10] **Everything else stayed the same** — still 1 GB RAM, a non-removable battery, 16 GB base.
