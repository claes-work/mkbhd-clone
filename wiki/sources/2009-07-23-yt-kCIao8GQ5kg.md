# Youtube Insight 2.0 [2009] (@mkbhd, 2009-07-23)

## Summary
HD tutorial touring the newly-updated YouTube Insight 2.0 analytics dashboard, posted as a
video response to his original YouTube Insight tutorial. Walks through per-video statistics
using his Safari 4 video as the live example: comments/favorites/ratings graphs, referral-
source breakdown (embed views, Google/YouTube search terms), and viewer demographics
(age/gender/country).

## Key claims
- [2009-07-23] **Dated Safari-4-video analytics snapshot**: 274 comments; average rating
  holds at 4.4; referral sources include 1,182 views from a single external embed, 4,400
  views from people Google-searching "Safari 4," plus additional YouTube/Google search and
  "other/viral" referral buckets — a concrete, dated citation upgrade for the analytics-as-
  sponsor-pitch framing already logged.
- [2009-07-23] **Dated demographic read on his own audience (via the Safari 4 video)**:
  most popular with males ages 13–17, then 45–54, then 35–44; popularity skews toward the
  US, then other countries by volume — the most granular self-reported audience-demographic
  statement found so far in the origin corpus.
- [2009-07-23] **Privacy/sharing mechanic explained**: notes creators can make per-video or
  channel-wide statistics public or private via account settings, and that he keeps his own
  visible ("I'm going to let all my videos have all these... open").
- [2009-07-23] **Cross-reference example**: uses Susan Boyle's video (~71M views) as an
  example of statistics visible on very high-view videos when a creator hasn't disabled
  sharing.
