# Dope Tech: The $3500 Bluetooth Speaker! (@mkbhd, 2020-06-09)

## Summary
Quarantine ("still around the studio") edition of Marques's recurring Dope Tech segment, a montage of four items rather than one focus piece. The headline item is the Devialet Phantom Reactor wireless speaker (matte black), which Marques praises for being astonishingly loud and bass-heavy relative to its very compact, dense cabinet — he runs a stereo pair on Spotify and calls it "a tiny portable noise complaint." The transcript's "$3500" title refers to one configuration; Marques clarifies the lineup has multiple wattage tiers and the top-end version is a different, larger ~$4,000 model. The other three items: the Sisyphus kinetic art table (a Wi-Fi-connected robot arm moves a magnet under a sand bed to draw and erase patterns with a steel marble); a rehoused Helios 44-2 vintage Russian lens (Iron Glass rehousing of the swirly-bokeh 58mm lens Marques has used in his videos, giving it better build, longer focus throw, follow-focus gears and filter threads); and a 1TB SD card as a "did you know this exists now" fun fact. His take: some of these are barely "tech," but they're impressive, fun, or useful enough to share.

## Key claims
- [2020-06-09] This is a quarantine edition of Dope Tech built from tech already around the studio; some episodes are single-item deep dives, some are montages like this one. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] The Sisyphus table draws patterns via a steel marble rolling through a thin layer of silica sand (included with the table), moved by a rotating magnet on a Wi-Fi-connected robot arm underneath. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] The table has an LED ring for an evenly lit dramatic look and a companion Sisyphus app with playlists of user-generated patterns; you can attempt your own designs in the app but it's very hard to match the pre-made ones. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] He frames the table as internet-connected moving art more than "tech" — mesmerizing to watch it draw, erase, and redraw perpetually, "just like the myth itself." (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] The Phantom Reactor speaker is roughly the size of an iPad mini — about 8 inches long, 6 inches wide — and weighs 4.3 kg / about 10 pounds ("like a bowling ball"). (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] Its design is a small full-range driver on the front plus two side-firing woofers that move a lot of air, with heat-sink fins on the back; the dense, compact cabinet is what produces the loud, punchy, rich bass. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] Two identical units can be paired as a left-right stereo pair; Spotify then sees them as one speaker and splits the channels appropriately. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] The speaker contains a small computer — an ARM processor, half a gig of RAM and a Wi-Fi chip — for its internet-connected features; he notes every speaker has some computer inside. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] It gets very loud with surprisingly little distortion at high volume, but is bass-heavy with a V-shaped-EQ feel because there's no mid-size driver dedicated to the mids — not a big deal for filling a room or outdoor space. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] On pricing: the title's ~$3,500 figure is one version; the Reactor line has a 600W and a 900W version (this is the 900W compact), and there's a separate bigger Phantom at 2,000W and 4,500W that peaks over 100 dB and costs about $4,000 — so this compact one is "a little safer than that." (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] The original Helios 44-2 is a vintage used Russian 58mm lens grabbable on Amazon or eBay for about $60, known for its swirly bokeh and fun optical characteristics; it's the source of the swirly backgrounds in past MKBHD videos. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] Most modern/cinema lenses are clinical, rectilinear, and optimized for maximum sharpness and minimum distortion — accurate but sometimes you want a more fun look instead. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] The original Helios isn't particularly sharp and is old, poorly built, and not easy to use. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] Rehousing (common in the industry) puts the same glass into a better barrel — better build quality, longer life, longer focus throw for easier focusing, follow-focus gears, and filter threads; this rehoused Helios is made by Iron Glass. (Marques)
- [2020-06-09] 1TB SD cards and 1TB microSD cards now exist; this one is $350 — expensive for an SD card but not insane for a terabyte — and he'd feel nervous putting that much data on a card you could snap in half. (Marques)
