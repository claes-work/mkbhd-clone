# Moto X Pure (2015) Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-09-25)

## Summary
The full Moto X Pure Edition review (= Moto X Style abroad). A familiar 2014-Moto-X body, but the two big
upgrades are **stereo front-facing speakers** ("right up there next to HTC's BoomSound — the best-sounding
speaker in any smartphone") and a QHD IPS display. Notable: the **grippy rubberized Moto Maker back means
"you pretty much don't need any cases or skins"** (his no-cases stance satisfied by grip), plus a combo
nano-SIM/microSD tray and turbocharging (his fast-charging love). A strong near-stock, competitively-priced
package.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-09-25] **Grippy back = no cases needed (no-cases, satisfied by design).** With the rubberized Moto
  Maker back, "you pretty much don't need any cases or skins — it is one of the grippiest phones out there,
  really easy to hold despite being bigger." (You can also opt for wood/leather.)
- [2015-09-25] **Stereo front-facing speakers are back.** "Last year the Moto X switched to a top-only
  speaker; this year we get the stereo upgrade back — right up there as the best-sounding speaker in any
  smartphone, next to the HTC One's BoomSound." Loud, front-facing, low distortion.
- [2015-09-25] **Fast charging love.** Non-removable battery, but **turbocharging** — "I'm a really big fan
  of fast charging: even if the phone lasts all day, plugging in for a few minutes for a massive charge is
  super useful."
- [2015-09-25] **The rest:** a combo **nano-SIM + microSD** tray, the returning dimple, QHD IPS display,
  high-end specs, and near-stock Android with Moto gestures.
