# Top 5 Galaxy Note 7 Replacements! (@mkbhd, 2016-10-12)

## Summary
The video that **retracts** his Note 7 review's "green-battery replacements are safe" verdict — the
replacements also caught fire, Samsung killed the phone worldwide, and he says return ALL of them ("old
or new, green or white battery"). Then a by-similarity buyer's guide to the best alternatives. A model of
updating-in-public when the facts change.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-10-12] **THE RETRACTION (contradiction resolved).** "I mentioned at the end of my Note 7 review
  that if you have a post-recall green-battery one you're good — then we saw a replacement Note 7 catch
  fire on a plane… then another, and another. The bottom line: the Note 7 is not safe. If you have a Note
  7 — old or new, green or white battery, original or replacement — for your safety and those around you,
  return it. Samsung has stopped selling it worldwide. RIP Note 7." A clean public reversal of his own
  earlier call.
- [2016-10-12] **Mourns a genuinely great phone.** "It was a great phone — great display, camera,
  features, specs, build. What's not to love? It was nice enough to get people to spend a lot of money on
  it, before it started exploding." Separates the product's quality from its fatal defect.
- [2016-10-12] **The by-similarity replacement ranking.** #1 **Galaxy S7 Edge** ("as close as you'll
  get — like a brother in the lineup; loses only the iris scanner/secure folder/stylus, but gets a bigger
  battery"). #2 regular S7 ("a further drop-off; almost no reason over the Edge unless your hands are too
  small"). #3 **iPhone 7 Plus** ("outside the family, but a top-notch large phone — great battery, camera,
  water resistance; the best 1080p display in any smartphone"). #4 **LG V20** ("was competing directly
  against the Note 7 — removable battery, expandable storage, quad-DAC, the second screen"). #5 **Pixel
  XL** ("a bit of a wild card, perfect timing, pure stock Android 7.1 — but not out yet, no water
  resistance, no expandable storage, unproven camera; more of a distant cousin"). Plus: "if you *have* to
  have the S Pen, go back to the Note 5 — no other phone has that stylus experience."
