# LG G4 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-04-28)

## Summary
First impressions of the LG G4 (from LG's NYC announcement). Two notable reads: the G4 is **"a bit of a rare
breed"** — it still has a **removable battery AND microSD** ("becoming less and less common… might be one of
the last major flagships with both"), which he flags as increasingly precious; and he applies healthy
**"can it walk the walk?" skepticism** to LG's "DSLR replacement" camera claim, while conceding his own test
shots looked genuinely good. Also a top-five display and a functionally-pointless leather-seam design note.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-04-28] **A rare removable-battery + microSD flagship.** "This is one of those traits that makes the
  G4 a bit of a rare breed — it still has a removable back, a removable 3000 mAh battery, and expandable
  storage via microSD. This is becoming less and less common; it might just be one of the last major flagships
  to have both." (You get wireless but not quick charging — so just swap batteries.)
- [2015-04-28] **Design: slight curve + leather backs.** A slightly curved (non-flexing) display glass,
  glossy metal sides, and back options (plastic diamond pattern, glossy ceramic, or leather in several
  colors/textures — he likes the plain black leather). The **stitched center seam "doesn't serve any
  functional purpose at all"** — a decorative choice you'll like or not.
- [2015-04-28] **Top-five display.** A 5.5" IPS "Quantum" QHD — "super bright, visible outdoors, colorful,
  very sharp… right up there in the top five displays on any smartphone right now."
- [2015-04-28] **Camera: talk-vs-walk skepticism, then impressed.** LG pitched it as a "DSLR replacement" —
  "they can talk the talk, but can this camera walk the walk?" His caution about sample photos ("they always
  look good") gives way to genuine early praise from his own shots: f/1.8, 16 MP, 1/2.6" sensor, 3-axis OIS,
  laser AF, and a new **color-spectrum sensor** for better white balance made it "really quick" with "good
  colors and dynamic range." Snapdragon 808 (not 810), 3 GB.
