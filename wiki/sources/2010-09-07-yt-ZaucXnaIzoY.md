# Solar Sound 2 Speaker System Review (@mkbhd, 2010-09-07)

## Summary
A combined unbox-and-review ("live look," no separate unboxing video) of the Devotec
Solar Sound 2 Bluetooth stereo speaker, covering its solar/USB/wall charging options,
Bluetooth + 3.5mm line-in/line-out, touch-sensitive controls, and portability, with a
qualitative (no numeric score) audio-quality assessment.

## Key claims
- [2010-09-07] **Combines unbox + review into one video explicitly**: "I didn't do an
  unboxing of it but I'll show you right now here everything that comes in the speaker
  box" — a counterpoint data point to the separately-established unbox/review-firewall
  rule from other 2009-era sources: here he collapses the two steps for a compact
  portable product rather than running the two-video pattern.
- [2010-09-07] **Repeats the royalty-free-audio playback constraint** already
  established in [[wiki/sources/2010-09-03-yt-S_IOXncPeuY]]: "I don't really have any
  audio that is royalty-free for me to play for you guys," so quality is described
  verbally instead of demonstrated.
- [2010-09-07] **Qualitative audio verdict, no numeric score**: "the audio quality is
  not as bad as you would think... pretty good highs, not really a lot of mids or
  lows," comparable to "laptop sound quality" — framed as reasonable for a portable
  Bluetooth speaker, not top-tier.
- [2010-09-07] **Practical caution about touch controls**: flags that the front
  buttons are touch-sensitive with no physical depth, recommending the speaker be
  powered off before being placed in a bag to avoid accidental activation.
- [2010-09-07] **Value framing for the price tier**: "if you're willing to pay for it
  you're definitely going to get what you want, you're not going to overpay" —
  a price-to-value judgment without a number attached.
