# HD Tutorial: Eliminate Menu Show Delay (@mkbhd, 2009-02-04)

## Summary
Origin-era Vista snappiness tweak via the registry editor (regedit). Marques removes the
built-in menu-open delay by editing `HKEY_CURRENT_USER → Control Panel → Desktop →
MenuShowDelay` and lowering its value. His careful instruction — **set it low but not to
zero** — recurs across his early tweaking videos and is an early marker of measured advice.
He notes he first learned the delay existed from a YouTube video, an early hint of his own
learning-from-the-platform loop.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-04] **How-to:** eliminate the Vista menu-show delay by editing the registry
  key `HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Control Panel\Desktop\MenuShowDelay` in regedit.
- [2009-02-04] **Careful-tweak guidance (dated):** set the value **as low as you're
  comfortable with but not to zero** (recurring caution in his 2009 tweaks).
- [2009-02-04] **Learning loop:** says he discovered the built-in delay from a YouTube
  video — an early self-taught-via-the-platform tell.
