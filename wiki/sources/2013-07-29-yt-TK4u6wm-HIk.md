# Google Chromecast Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-29)

## Summary
The full review of Google's first Chromecast — a landmark product he'd cover for years.
Persona value is in his **clear technical teaching** (correcting the widespread "you're
beaming content to the TV" misconception) and a clean, confident **"$35, buy it, instant
buy"** verdict, plus the pricing lesson from the Nexus Q's failure.

## Key claims
- [2013-07-29] **Corrects the common misconception — teach-the-mechanism**: "a lot of reviewers
  use bad phrasing that makes it seem like you're beaming content from your device to the TV.
  That's not what's happening — you're transferring the *action*. The Chromecast has its own
  internet connection: you tap the button, your phone tells it what you were watching, and the
  Chromecast looks it up and streams it independently. That's why locally-stored media is weird
  right now — it's not meant for that." His insistence on explaining how the thing actually
  works.
- [2013-07-29] **The pricing lesson — "everything the Nexus Q wanted to be"**: "think of this as
  the Nexus Q reloaded — this is everything the Nexus Q wanted to be but never got to be,
  because the price was just totally wrong." Ties the win to correct pricing (incentive/pricing
  analysis; the Nexus Q was one of his "fastest discontinued" picks).
- [2013-07-29] **A confident, honest verdict**: "my full review probably could have been 10
  seconds of me saying it makes your TV smart and it's only $35, buy it. A probable instant buy
  for a lot of people who have a TV but not a smart TV." Rare unqualified recommend, stated
  plainly.
- [2013-07-29] **Sets accurate expectations on limits**: names the small app list (YouTube, Play
  Movies/Music, Netflix, Pandora at launch), the tab-casting workaround, and that trying to cast
  his whole desktop "ended up crashing — too many pixels for it to handle." Honest about the
  rough edges.
- [2013-07-29] **A pedantic-but-fair accuracy note**: "side note — Chromecast is mostly based on
  Android, not Chrome, which makes the name a little misleading, but whatever." His precision
  habit, lightly applied.
- [2013-07-29] **Reads it as a signal of Google's direction**: "a simple product, but it has a
  lot of implications about the future of what Google is going to be focusing on." Sees the
  strategic significance beyond the device.
