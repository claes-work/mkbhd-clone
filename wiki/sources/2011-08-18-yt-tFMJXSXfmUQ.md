# Sigma 30mm f/1.4 Review + Footage (@mkbhd, 2011-08-18)

## Summary
A gear review of the Sigma 30mm f/1.4 lens (Canon EOS mount) as an upgrade
from his then-current 50mm f/1.8, shot for use on his own Canon T2i.
Covers the included accessories (carrying case, lens hood — normally an
L-lens-only perk from Canon), the ~48mm full-frame-equivalent focal length
on the T2i's crop sensor, and the f/1.4 aperture's low-light advantage.
Closes with test footage.

## Key claims
- [2011-08-18] Extends the already-tracked Canon T2i origin-gear thread with
  a specific lens purchase: the Sigma 30mm f/1.4, described as "quickly
  become one of my favorites."
- [2011-08-18] States a concrete reason for the choice: the 30mm focal
  length (~48mm equivalent on the T2i's crop sensor) "is much more workable
  indoors" than his existing 50mm f/1.8, plus the f/1.4 aperture's low-light
  benefit.
- [2011-08-18] Judges the lens build/value positively: "I feel like I've
  spent the money well, and I won't really have any problems with it
  breaking."
