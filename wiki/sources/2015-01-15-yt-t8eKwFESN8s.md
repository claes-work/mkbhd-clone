# CES 2015 Highlights! (@mkbhd, 2015-01-15)

## Summary
His CES 2015 wrap-up, notable for three durable positions: **OLED is his next TV** ("of all the new
display tech, my next TV upgrade is going to have to be OLED"), **2015 is "the year of 4K"** — now
because it got easy to *shoot* 4K, not just watch it — and a substantial look at the **Tesla Model X**
prototype (falcon-wing doors, three rows, a refined interior answering the Model S's biggest complaint).
Continues the car-beat interest and the 4K-adoption thesis.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-01-15] **TVs dominate CES; OLED wins his vote.** 4K everywhere ("the pixel lover inside me very
  happy"), multiple **8K** sets ("looked absolutely insane… even better than current 4K"), 3D mostly dead
  ("I'm kind of glad"). His favorite was Sony's ~5 mm-thin 4K Android TV, but "**my next TV upgrade is
  going to have to be OLED.**"
- [2015-01-15] **"The year of 4K" — because shooting got easy.** Beyond all the 4K TVs, "more than ever
  we're seeing more ways to *shoot* 4K" — cheaper Sony 4K handycams, 4K unlockable via external recorder,
  4K action cams and the new GoPro. "Shooting 4K no longer has to be an ordeal with a huge camera — anyone
  can do it. This is going to be the year of 4K." (The 4K-adoption thesis advancing.)
- [2015-01-15] **Tesla Model X (car-beat continues).** "I've talked a lot about Tesla since I showed the
  Model S in this booth last year." The Model X prototype: **falcon-wing doors** (fold to need little
  vertical space, open by button), three rows (tight middle/third row), and — answering the Model S's
  interior criticism — "everything looked more refined… a much cleaner, more luxurious interior," a nicer
  screen bezel, and **touchscreens on either side of the steering wheel** replacing knobs. Notes it now
  has side mirrors (an earlier version had cameras).
- [2015-01-15] **LG G Flex 2** — the standout phone: a much-improved 5.5" 1080p (less-curved) OLED,
  LG-G3-like back buttons + laser AF, still flexible with a self-healing back (he'll test it).
- [2015-01-15] **Sharp's bezelless / formless displays** — "one of the best booths at all of CES," using
  the same tech as the Aquos Crystal, plus displays "you could make whatever shape you want" (his
  champion-the-risk-taker interest again).
