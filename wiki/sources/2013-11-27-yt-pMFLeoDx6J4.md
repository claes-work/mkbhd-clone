# Google Nexus 5 vs Moto X! ($350) (@mkbhd, 2013-11-27)

## Summary
A holiday buyer's-guide comparison of the two $350 pure-Android phones. Reinforcing — a
clean **decide-by-use-case** breakdown (Moto X for ergonomics/battery/first-Android-
recommendation; Nexus 5 for specs/display/updates) — plus the recurring Stormtrooper-Nexus-5
love, a rolling-release payoff note, and a small music-taste corroboration.

## Key claims
- [2013-11-27] **Decide-by-use-case — the breakdown**: "the pros/cons are remarkably similar. The
  better-feeling, better-ergonomics, more-compact device is the Moto X — the form factor I'd
  recommend, especially for someone moving from an iPhone. The Nexus 5 wins on paper specs
  (Snapdragon 800, bigger/brighter 1080p display) and gets substantially worse battery. Camera:
  speed to the Moto X, quality to the Nexus 5." Situational verdicts, not one winner.
- [2013-11-27] **Stormtrooper-Nexus-5 love + Moto Maker**: "I happen to be a huge fan of the
  Stormtrooper Nexus 5, but the customization factor puts the Moto X ahead — with Moto Maker you
  get a rainbow of backs/accents/engraving, and it ships first even customized." The recurring
  aesthetic word + make-it-your-own value.
- [2013-11-27] **Rolling-release payoff — the Moto X camera**: "since my Moto X review, the camera
  got a substantial update and they moved the camera app to the Google Play Store, so it updates
  quickly and everyone's Moto X gets the improvement." The Play-Store-decoupling benefit, realized.
- [2013-11-27] **The one-and-the-same incentive note**: "Google and Motorola are technically one
  and the same company right now, so the experience is very similar — but the Nexus is the Android
  flagship, so you get software advantages [the Google Experience Launcher, slightly smoother, and
  first in line for updates]." His incentive/ownership read.
- [2013-11-27] **Availability as a deciding factor**: "the Nexus 5 is only in the Play Store where
  Google sells; the Moto X is in way more areas and on Verizon." A practical decision axis.
- [2013-11-27] **Music-taste corroboration + deal-tweeting**: "I'm wearing an Overwerk shirt
  because his new EP dropped today" (an electronic-music artist — corroborates the house/electronic
  taste), and "I'll be tweeting the best Black Friday/Cyber Monday tech deals I find." Dated
  taste + a creator-service habit.
