# Dope Tech #1: Weapon! (@mkbhd, 2015-10-09)

## Summary
The inaugural episode of Marques Brownlee's signature "Dope Tech" series, a recurring format built to showcase the coolest and most exotic tech that crosses his path, with the only qualification being that he thinks it's "dope" (his rule: "anything with an on button is game"). Episode one covers two consumer items — the 2015 Chromecast and Chromecast Audio — and then pivots to his own production gear: the RED Weapon Dragon cinema camera he uses to shoot his videos. He explains RED's modular body/sensor naming (Epic vs. Weapon body, shared 6K Dragon sensor), why he shoots RAW despite it being overkill for YouTube, and closes by noting the series is inspired by TLD's "Coolest Tech on the Planet." He also plugs the MKBHD v2 t-shirt.

## Key claims
- [2015-10-09] "Dope Tech" is a brand-new recurring series designed to showcase the best and coolest tech Marques encounters, whether used once or daily; the only qualification is that he thinks it's dope. (Marques — series thesis)
- [2015-10-09] The 2015 Chromecast is an HDMI dongle (now with a built-in short HDMI cable and a magnet to store it) that turns a "not so smart TV" into a useful one; the original Chromecast was one of the best-selling electronics of the prior year. (Marques)
- [2015-10-09] New 2015 Chromecast features: 5 GHz Wi-Fi / 802.11ac support and multiple internal antennas that switch to get the best signal behind the TV; comes in multiple colors. It still does NOT support 4K — everything, including gaming, stays 1080p (he speculates Google wants to push people to Android TV for 4K). (Marques)
- [2015-10-09] Chromecast Audio is "Chromecast for speakers" — it plugs into any speakers via the included 3.5 mm mini cable, or Optical or RCA, and lets devices stream music to them. He frames it as better than Bluetooth because more than one person can join and add to the stream on the fly. (Marques)
- [2015-10-09] Both Chromecast devices are $35 each and get his thumbs up. (Marques — pricing)
- [2015-10-09] The camera he uses to make his videos is the RED Weapon Dragon (unit "number 41"). He describes it as easier to think of as "a small computer with a sensor attached than a camera." (Marques — production/filmmaking)
- [2015-10-09] RED's naming: the body he used before was the Red Epic Dragon (Epic = body, Dragon = the 6K sensor). The new camera is the Red Weapon Dragon — new body, same Dragon sensor. A full-frame 8K sensor is coming as a later upgrade that year, so he may be shooting 8K later in the year. (Marques)
- [2015-10-09] He would NOT go around recommending the RED — he's fully aware it's overkill for YouTube / web video; the files look far better on his computer than after YouTube's compression, but he shoots RED because he enjoys it and learns from the RAW workflow daily. (Marques — opinion/production stance)
- [2015-10-09] Hardware details: forged carbon fiber body (a different process than normal carbon fiber, making it stronger and lighter), SSD reader on the side, customizable buttons on the front/sides, and a pair of decent stereo mics for scratch audio on the front. (Marques)
- [2015-10-09] Because the camera is named "Weapon," RED offers a stealth badge option (hiding the word "weapon") for people who travel through airport security with their cameras — though Marques says he still gets stopped almost every time, mostly because of the huge batteries in his bag. (Marques — anecdote)
- [2015-10-09] The big differentiating factor of RED cameras is modularity: he runs a minimal rig as a one-man crew (battery mount, battery, monitor, soon a top handle), whereas most RED setups are rigged much more heavily for multi-person crews. (Marques)
- [2015-10-09] The series is inspired entirely by TLD's "Coolest Tech on the Planet" series; he credits and recommends subscribing to TLD, and invites viewers to suggest future Dope Tech episodes in the comments. (Marques — attribution/credit)
