# Tesla Solar Roof Review: Was It Worth It? (@mkbhd, 2023-07-29)

## Summary
A solo, data-heavy **one-year review of his Tesla Solar Roof + Powerwall** home setup —
one of his recurring "**was it worth it?**" long-term reviews. Hook: "I have not paid for
electricity in a year" while running appliances, AC and charging an EV at home. He explains
solar-plus-battery fundamentals (net metering, going off-grid), why he chose the all-Tesla
integrated **solar tiles** (premium price for simplicity + coverage + curb appeal, despite
not needing a new roof), and the 8-month install. Then a **season-by-season data
breakdown** from the Tesla app: huge summer generation (~200–300 kWh/day), best excess in
spring/fall, brutal cloudy Northeast winters (net-positive grid use Nov–Jan), with **car
charging** the #1 draw and **AC** #2. Verdict: bleeding-edge, expensive but "worth it" for
early adopters; ~9.6-year payback; wouldn't change a thing except timing for a bigger tax
credit. Includes a disclosed **Eight Sleep** sponsor read. Solo — persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2023-07-29] Framing hook: "I have not paid for electricity in a year" — appliances,
  computers, consoles, TVs, AC and a home-charged EV, "$0 bill." He waited a full year to
  review "specifically because now I've seen all four seasons."
- [2023-07-29] Chose **all-Tesla** (Solar Roof tiles + 3× Powerwall + one app) for
  "simplicity and integration," explicitly paying a price premium over a cheaper mixed
  panel/battery setup. Chose **solar tiles** over bolt-on panels for maximum coverage
  across his many roof pitches and aesthetics — "a crazy option" since he didn't need a
  new roof. Install: signed Nov 2021 → activated July 2022 (~8 months).
  > ⚠️ Caption garble: the spoken specs are unreliable — the array reads as "29,313 kW"/
  > "29.3 kW" (the ~29.3 kW figure is the plausible one) and Powerwall totals as "40½ kWh"
  > with "13½ kWh each" and a "152 kW" max output that is almost certainly ~15.2 kW.
  > Treat exact kW/kWh numbers as approximate.
- [2023-07-29] Learned home-usage baseline ~400W idle; TVs ~100W, computers 300–500W;
  biggest spikes from microwave/toaster, but the two dominant draws are **#2 air
  conditioning** and **#1 EV charging "by a country mile."**
- [2023-07-29] Seasonal pattern: summer ~200–300 kWh/day (net ~0 to −50 after heavy AC +
  car); fall/spring produced the **most excess** (many net-negative days); winter is brutal
  in the Northeast (short days, ~1-in-10 sunny, EVs far less efficient in cold), giving
  **net-positive grid months for Nov/Dec/Jan**. His single best month for excess was **May**.
- [2023-07-29] Money: total system ~$122–133k (caption-garbled), reduced ~$30k by the ~30%
  federal/NJ solar tax credit to net ~$93k; **~9.6-year payback** vs a "good" 6–10-year
  range, with panels warrantied 25 years. "Am I mad at that? Not really… I don't think I
  would change a thing" except waiting ~6 months for a larger credit.
- [2023-07-29] Quirks: net-metering credits roll over monthly but reset yearly (how he kept
  $0 bills despite net-positive winter months); power outages he "didn't even notice"
  (Tesla app + Storm Watch); solar tiles stay just above freezing so light snow melts, but
  heavy snow slides off the roof in sheets ("a loud booming noise… piles up around the
  door"); and the "$0 bill" is really ~$57.5/month in fixed grid-connection charges.
- [2023-07-29] Forward look: hopes Tesla ships **bidirectional charging** (mentioned for
  ~2025) so an EV battery (one Model S ≈ 7–8 Powerwalls) could back up a house; notes the
  F-150 Lightning already can with the right hardware. Broader civic point: "any strain we
  can take off the grid is a win."
