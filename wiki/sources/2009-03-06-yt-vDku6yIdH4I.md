# 2009 Mac Desktop Refresh: iMac (@mkbhd, 2009-03-06)

## Summary
Part **two** of the 2009 Apple desktop-refresh series — a spec-and-price walkthrough of the
new **iMac** (Marques, age 15). He positions the iMac as Apple's "ultimate all-in-one" for
the average consumer, above the pared-down Mac mini, and calls it his **favorite Mac**
despite owning a Mac Pro. He tours the 20-inch and 24-inch models, the Nvidia GeForce
9400M vs. ATI Radeon HD graphics options ("six times the performance" on the top 24"),
up to **8 GB DDR3 RAM**, 1 TB storage, and prices from **$1,200 (20") to ~$1,500–$2,200
(24")**, configuring a ~$3,270 high-end unit live. Also a notable **origin-era production
detail**: he says he is **still recording/broadcasting from a PC, not the Mac Pro yet**,
and plans to get **ScreenFlow** to start making Mac OS X screencasts.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-06] Prefers the **iMac as his favorite Mac** (over the Mac Pro he owns);
  positions it as the all-in-one for the average consumer, a step up from the Mac mini.
- [2009-03-06] iMac specs: 20"/24" sizes, Nvidia GeForce 9400M or ATI Radeon HD (up to
  ~6× graphics on the top 24"), up to **8 GB DDR3**, up to 1 TB; **$1,200** (20") to
  **~$1,500–$2,200** (24"), bundled with iLife '09 and OS X Leopard, Boot Camp for Windows.
- [2009-03-06] **Production setup (self-reported):** still **screencasting from a PC**, not

- [2009-03-06] Repeats his anticipation for **Windows 7** ("I can't wait for Windows 7").
