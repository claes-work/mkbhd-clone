# HD Tutorial: PIP Effect in Camtasia Studio 6 (@mkbhd, 2009-04-12)

## Summary
An origin-era **production-methodology tutorial** (Marques, age 15) walking through the
**picture-in-picture (PIP) webcam effect in Camtasia Studio 6** — the on-screen "face in the
corner" that his early screencasts used. He shows the pre-record menu recognizing his **HP laptop's
built-in webcam**, then edits a recorded clip: importing media, choosing his **HD preset (1280×720)**,
and opening `Edit → Picture-in-Picture → Modify selected PIP clip` to resize/reposition the webcam via
**keyframes** (large at the start, shrunk to the bottom-right later), plus **opacity/fade** (usable as a
watermark or a fade-in), a **colored border** (thickness + palette), and a **drop shadow**. He
cross-promotes his earlier well-rated **HD-preset (1280×720) tutorial**.

## Key claims
- [2009-04-12] The early MKBHD screencast look — **webcam PIP in the corner over a screen recording** —
  was produced in **Camtasia Studio 6**, with the webcam and screen/audio captured simultaneously.
- [2009-04-12] He standardizes on a **1280×720 HD preset** ("I like to make all my videos in 1280×720")
  and points viewers to his separate, highly-rated preset tutorial — reinforcing the **720p HD standard**
  documented across his 2009 origin thread.
- [2009-04-12] PIP customization taught: **keyframed size/position**, **opacity/fade** (watermark or
  reveal), **border color + thickness**, and **drop shadow** — the granular polish choices of the
  origin production style.
