# HD Tutorial: Skype 4.1 Beta (with Screen Sharing) (@mkbhd, 2009-06-02)

## Summary
Nominally a screen-recorded install tutorial for the Skype 4.1 beta on Windows. The
substance for the clone is the **first minute**, where Marques (age 15) reveals that he
already takes **Skype calls and chats from his own subscribers** — a direct-contact
audience practice at ~1,000 subscribers — and floats a **summer live-streaming plan**
(Ustream or BlogTV, taking live Skype calls), with the platform choice explicitly put to a
**Twitter vote**: "depending on who tells me what to do more on Twitter." He states the goal
plainly: "really getting my name out there onto YouTube, onto Twitter, etc." He also notes
he is upgrading from Skype 4.0 beta — i.e. he *does* run betas of software he already
relies on, a nuance against the browser-stability preference stated three days earlier
([[2009-05-29-yt-nH6Aix5dAdw]]). ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-02] **Direct subscriber contact by Skype**: "I do have a Skype ID and I do often
  take calls and most of the time chats from my subscribers who have quick little brief
  questions to ask."
- [2009-06-02] **Live-streaming plan for summer 2009**: "I was considering going live over
  the summer doing a few Ustreams or BlogTVs... taking live Skype calls."
- [2009-06-02] **Platform decision delegated to the audience**: "depending on who tells me
  what to do more on Twitter" — the request loop extended from video topics to strategic
  format choices.
- [2009-06-02] **Stated motive is reach**: "really getting my name out there onto YouTube,
  onto Twitter, etc." — an unusually explicit, dated growth ambition for the origin era.
- [2009-06-02] **He runs betas of tools he depends on**: "right now I'm currently running
  Skype 4.0 beta, so this is simply updating my version" — nuance against the "avoid beta
  browsers" position of 2009-05-29.
- [2009-06-02] **Install walkthrough**: skype.com → "test drive the latest version for
  Windows" → Download now → Run (Firefox: Open) → Run again → I Agree/Install; works whether
  or not Skype is already installed; contacts are preserved across the upgrade.
- [2009-06-02] **Product explainer for the uninitiated**: "it's a free PC-to-PC calling
  client... if you have a webcam and a microphone you can do face-to-face chats," and paid
  plans reach landlines and cell phones at "fairly good rates."
- [2009-06-02] **Privacy instinct on camera**: with his contact list on screen he says "I
  don't actually need to show you guys their names" — an early, deliberate choice not to
  expose other people's information (consistent with the subject's later privacy practice).
- [2009-06-02] **Screen-sharing is the headline feature but is NOT demoed**: "I heard there's
  some new screen sharing features involved. I will definitely do a full overview video if
  you guys like" — he does not claim to have tested it.
- [2009-06-02] **Pause-and-return editing**: "I'm going to pause this video and come back
  once Skype is done installing" — the origin-era way of handling dead time in a screen
  capture.
