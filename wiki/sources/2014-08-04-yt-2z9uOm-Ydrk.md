# Android Wear Review! (Smartwatches) (@mkbhd, 2014-08-04)

## Summary
The **full Android Wear category review** (on the LG G Watch) — the definitive early statement of his
first-gen-wearable thesis in practice. He walks what Android Wear *is* ("Google Now cards and your
phone's notifications on your wrist" + standalone voice features), and lands the honest verdict: **"for a
real geek like me this stuff is awesome… but I don't even really think the average person is going to
like this yet."** Battery (one day) is the biggest downside; the pedometer is unreliable; and his advice
is to **wait for the Moto 360 and for the Apple option** — Apple Watch anticipation again. A canonical
geek-vs-mainstream, "it's the beginning of a proof of concept" wearable read.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-08-04] **What it is:** information at a glance — Google Now cards + phone notifications on the
  wrist (swipe/card UI, expand to act: play/pause, archive), synced to the phone over Bluetooth (dismiss
  on one → dismissed on the other), customizable watch faces, and standalone voice actions (take a note,
  set a reminder/timer/alarm, dictate a text/email, navigate, launch a third-party app).
- [2014-08-04] **The geek-vs-mainstream verdict (canonical).** "Even though it may seem awkward and geeky
  to tap your watch and talk to it, it actually works pretty well… but this is the first version of
  Android Wear — I don't even really think the average person is going to like this stuff yet. For a real
  geek, a tech head like me, this stuff is awesome." It's "a bit of a geek toy instead of a mainstream
  gadget" right now.
- [2014-08-04] **Real daily value + a party trick:** not pulling the phone out constantly; hands-free
  texting while driving; a Google Now **boarding pass** he scanned off his wrist to board a flight ("that
  kind of stuff blows people's minds").
- [2014-08-04] **Honest downsides.** (1) **Battery** — the G Watch's 36 hrs is really one day of real
  use, "charge it every night." (2) **App ecosystem** is thin — "we have to wait for more dedicated watch
  apps." (3) **Pedometer is unreliable** — it's in the watch, and wrist motion (eating, brushing teeth,
  writing) fakes steps: "I wouldn't base my fitness routine on the pedometer of any Android smartwatch,
  at least for now."
- [2014-08-04] **The recommendation = wait.** "Wait for the Moto 360 — I definitely think that's going
  to be the one to get… wait for the Apple option, of course, and those that follow it." He expects "a
  lot more awesome smartwatches" in H2 2014 / H1 2015. Notes it's Android-only (no iOS/Windows Phone).
- [2014-08-04] **The category problem, named:** "it's still kind of hard to convince the masses to buy a
  smartwatch… people who don't wear watches in the first place aren't really flocking to buy any."
