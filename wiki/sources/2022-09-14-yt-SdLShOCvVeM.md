# iPhone 14 Pro Review: This Will Be Copied! (@mkbhd, 2022-09-14)

## Summary
A solo full review of the iPhone 14 Pro / 14 Pro Max, built around Marques's argument
that the phone looks "exactly the same as the iPhone 13 Pro" and so the story is three
areas: internals, display, and cameras. Internals: the A16 Bionic (only slightly above
the A15, hence Apple compared it to the A13 on stage), new Crash Detection sensors, the
US-only **e-SIM-only** switch, and satellite connectivity. Display: the new **always-on
display** (a "classic Apple late-mover strategy" he'll turn off because it dents battery)
and — his favorite — the **Dynamic Island**, which he predicts "will be one of the most
copied features in the smartphone world in like 18 months" (the title's thesis) while
noting LG did a top-screen version on the V10 seven years earlier. Cameras: the first
**48-megapixel** main sensor since iPhones stuck at 12 MP, enabling a lossless 2x crop,
Action mode, and ProRAW; he crowns it "the camera King again" for the fourth year but
repeats his standing critique that the selfie camera "still has a tendency to overexpose
people with dark skin constantly — I would know," plus the recurring USB-C/ProRes-offload
rant. Solo video — all persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2022-09-14] The 14 Pro "looks exactly the same as the iPhone 13 Pro" — he walked
  around with the NDA unit and "nobody even blinked an eye"; three things are actually
  new: internals, display, cameras.
- [2022-09-14] The A16 Bionic (4 nm, 6 GB RAM) benchmarks "very similar to an A15 Bionic,
  just slightly above"; a tell that it's a minor bump is that Apple compared it on stage
  to the two-years-older A13.
- [2022-09-14] Crash Detection fuses GPS speed drop, a loud-noise mic reading, barometric
  pressure change from airbags, and a hard gyroscope force; he won't test it. New this
  year and US-only: **e-SIM-only** (pro: fewer water-ingress points/moving parts; con:
  grabbing a local SIM abroad is no longer easy) — he expects Apple to expand it to all
  iPhones. Satellite connectivity is an "edge case… you're honestly hoping you never ever
  have to use."
- [2022-09-14] The **always-on display** is "the perfect example of the classic Apple
  late-mover strategy" — a feature on hundreds of phones for years, now done "the Apple
  way" (full color, album art, smooth fade), but he sees "no advantages to this Apple
  way" beyond looks, it lacks customization, and it measurably hurts battery, so he's
  turning it off.
- [2022-09-14] The **Dynamic Island** turns the notch cutouts into a software surface with
  ~30 fluid, physics-y states and a two-slot "multitasking app switcher." He calls the
  name terrible ("what a terrible name; they felt the need to name the notch") but
  predicts "this will be one of the most copied features in the smartphone world in like
  18 months, pretty much guarantee it," while crediting LG for a similar top-screen idea
  on the V10 "seven years ago." Not, in his view, a reason to buy the phone.
- [2022-09-14] The primary camera is Apple's first **48-megapixel** sensor (65% larger
  than the 12 Pro's, f/1.78, second-gen sensor-shift OIS), the first jump off the 12 MP
  plateau held since the iPhone 6s. It bins to 12 MP by default, gives natural shallow
  depth of field "more than we've ever seen from an iPhone," enables a near-lossless
  optical-quality **2x** crop, an **Action mode** (heavy-crop stabilization up to 2.8K),
  and 50–70 MB ProRAW DNGs; notably **no 8K video** despite crossing the resolution
  threshold.
- [2022-09-14] He calls it "the camera King again" — the iPhone's fourth straight year
  atop his camera rankings — mainly on video (ProRes, color consistency, autofocus) which
  he shoots the new Auto Focus car channel with. Standing critique repeated: the selfie
  camera "still has a tendency to overexpose people with dark skin constantly — I would
  know." Also flags over-baked HDR in harsh light and, again, the missing astrophotography
  mode.
- [2022-09-14] Recurring USB-C/offload rant: 5–6 GB-per-minute ProRes files "take forever
  to get off the iPhone via this Lightning port… they should probably just switch to
  USB-C." Notes a sustainability win: the iPhone 14 line has slightly better repairability
  (back glass now separates from the components).
- [2022-09-14] Verdict/"is it worth it": at $999/$1,099 it's competitive with best camera
  and one of the best displays, but there's "nothing folding in half… no super fast
  charging." Recurring upgrade doctrine: "if you have an iPhone 11 or later… the new iOS
  16 update… is gonna make just as big of a difference… as getting the new phone."
