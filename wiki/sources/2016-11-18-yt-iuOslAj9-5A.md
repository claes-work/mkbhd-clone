# The Google Popup Store in NYC! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-18)

## Summary
A short, photogenic walkthrough of Google's NYC pop-up store (display/demo only, nothing for sale). Light
content, but two clone-relevant notes: his **producer's eye for a photogenic space** (he reviews the
*store* like a product, A−), and consistent product opinions — Google Photos as underrated, and Daydream VR
as his favorite area (the demo-democratizes-VR point).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-18] **Reviews the store like a product — with a producer's eye.** "If I'm reviewing this thing,
  I'm giving it an A− — I was really happy with how photogenic it is; this LED beam in the middle doubles
  as a huge softbox, so the lighting was on point." His filmmaker's instinct applied to a physical space.
- [2016-11-18] **Google Photos = "low-key one of Google's best products right now."** On the tablet
  photo-sphere demoing searchability: "Google Photos is low-key one of Google's best products right now."
  A recurring elevate-the-underrated-Google-product take.
- [2016-11-18] **Daydream VR was his favorite area (demos democratize VR).** "The VR section for Daydream
  View was my personal favorite — most people still haven't tried good VR; even I haven't tried all the
  ones I want. This was an easy way to walk in, try the headset, and decide if they're into it." Values
  hands-on demos for a category people only hear about.
- [2016-11-18] **The interactive block wall — "the most pointless but coolest damn thing."** Sensors move
  the Google-colored blocks to follow you: "about the most pointless but coolest damn thing I've seen in a
  while." Delight in playful, well-executed interactive tech.
- [2016-11-18] **Reiterates the Pixel "have to use it" thesis.** At the Pixel demo desk: "remember how I
  said this is a phone you actually have to use to understand how good it is? Here you can pick one up." A
  callback to the review's central point ([[2016-11-14-yt-LR708uA4zQ8]]).
