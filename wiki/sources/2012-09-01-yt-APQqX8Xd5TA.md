# The Galaxy Nexus 2 Wishlist! (@mkbhd, 2012-09-01)

## Summary
A predictions/wishlist video for the next Nexus phone, built from the current Galaxy Nexus's
shortcomings. Several calls are analytically interesting — he predicts a **multi-manufacturer Nexus
launch** and reasons about **Google's Motorola acquisition and vertical integration** — and one is a
personal easter egg: his guessed release date is "December 3rd… it's just a date I happen to like"
(his own birthday). It also states plainly why he switched to the S3: the camera.

## Key claims
- [2012-09-01] **Predicts a multi-manufacturer Nexus launch** — a notable, correct-ish call: "there's a
  rumor that there's going to be multiple nexuses launched by different carriers and manufacturers at the
  same time, and this rumor has some nice credibility — there could be an LG Nexus, a Samsung Nexus, maybe
  an HTC Nexus… all with stock Android, no skins, updates pushed from Google. If I had to guess right now,
  that's what's going to happen." (Google did move toward this; the Nexus 4 was LG.)
- [2012-09-01] **Reasons from OEM strengths — an incentive/structure argument**: Google picks the Nexus
  maker by who's dominating that category — "a lot of the reasons Google picked Samsung is because they
  dominated Android phones the year before… Asus makes great dominating tablets, so they picked Asus for
  the [Nexus 7] tablet." A structural read of Google's partner choices, not a guess.
- [2012-09-01] **Anticipates vertical integration via Motorola**: "the next-next Nexus will be made when
  Google vertically integrates and takes advantage of their purchase of Motorola Mobility." An early,
  dated read of the Google-owns-Motorola dynamic.
- [2012-09-01] **States plainly why he switched to the S3 — the camera**: "one of the big reasons I've
  used the Galaxy S3 over the Galaxy Nexus is the significantly better camera. I share a lot of media
  pictures, and while [the Nexus] takes great video and okay photos, it's a 5 megapixel sensor." A
  concrete, personal reason behind the June daily-driver switch ([[2012-06-26-yt-4JEZ2rrf2N4]]).
- [2012-09-01] **Design praise for the current Nexus, as a bar to keep**: "this is probably the
  best-looking phone out there right now, at least in my opinion — no branding whatsoever on the front and
  the back… the front is perfect." The clean/no-branding aesthetic he values.
- [2012-09-01] **Refuses the pointless-incremental-upgrade idea, via incentives**: on a rumored "Galaxy
  Nexus Plus," "there's literally no point for Google to do that — I know some other companies like to do
  that, but there's no point for Google." He reasons from what serves Google's interest, not the rumor.
- [2012-09-01] **The birthday easter egg**: "when will it come out? …my guess is **December 3rd — it's
  just a date I happen to like.**" His own birthday ([[2011-12-03-yt-a6YEI-bOe-E]]) slipped in as a joke
  release-date guess.
- [2012-09-01] **Answers the hold-off buying question**: "a lot of people were wondering if they should
  hold off on buying a Galaxy Nexus… I think you have about three more months before the next Nexus."
