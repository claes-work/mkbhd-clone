# HD First Look: SRWare Iron (@mkbhd, 2009-02-09)

## Summary
Origin-era **browser first-look/review** — and one of the clearest early examples of
Marques' review instinct. Age-15 Marques reviews **SRWare Iron** (a Chromium-derived,
privacy-focused browser) after a **viewer named "butter chicken" requested it in a comment**.
He installs it live, notes it "looks a lot like Google Chrome" (correctly inferring it is
built from Chrome/WebKit source), and then **benchmarks it methodically**: runs the
**Acid3 test** (100/100, but flags a "link test failed" glitch) and — most notably —
opens Task Manager to **compare RAM/CPU usage** across browsers, finding Iron uses only
~15–16 MB (spiking to ~20 MB streaming a video) versus Firefox's preloader ~102 MB and
Safari climbing to ~40 MB. He recommends Iron for users without much RAM. The
compare-the-numbers, test-it-yourself methodology and the "reply to audience requests"
loop both prefigure the mature MKBHD review format.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-09] **Review verdict (dated):** likes SRWare Iron; recommends it especially for
  **low-RAM machines** because of light resource usage.
- [2009-02-09] **Benchmark method:** compares browsers by (a) **Acid3** score and (b)
  **RAM/CPU** in Task Manager — Iron ~15–16 MB vs Firefox preloader ~102 MB vs Safari ~40 MB.
- [2009-02-09] Correctly identifies Iron as **built from Google Chrome / WebKit source**
  from its layout and behavior (ships with Google Gears, saves passwords like Chrome).
- [2009-02-09] The video exists **because a viewer requested it**; he invites more requests
  via personal message — an early audience-driven content loop.
- [2009-02-09] Reports having already tried **Opera 10 Alpha, a WebKit "chameleon" Safari
  build, Safari, and now Iron** — self-positions as a serial browser tester.
