# Samsung Galaxy Note 2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-12)

## Summary
A review of the 5.5" Galaxy Note 2 — the "phablet" that made him rethink what phone size he could get
used to. Two durable items: a clear **anti-benchmark, real-world-performance** statement ("I never
really put a lot of weight on benchmarks… I might do a video about why"), and an on-camera **change of
mind about the stylus** ("I never ever thought I would say this, but I kind of like the stylus").
Also a standout battery result and a dated size-perception shift.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-12] **Anti-benchmark stance, stated outright**: "I never really put a lot of weight on
  benchmarks — you guys know that — and I might do a video about why in the near future… in the end it's
  really more about the experience that these high-performance internals can deliver." He runs Quadrant
  only to confirm the quad-core+2GB "held up," then pivots to lived experience: "real-world performance
  here is a 10 out of 10." A dated, explicit articulation of the real-world-over-synthetic position that
  recurs across his career.
- [2012-10-12] **Changed his mind about the stylus, on camera**: "I never ever ever ever thought I would
  say this, but I kind of like the stylus… to me it feels like a part of the experience of using this
  phone." He's careful to scope it — "you don't have to like it too, you don't even have to use it" — and
  singles out handwriting recognition as "straight up one of the most impressive things I've ever seen on
  a phone, next to Google Now." A documented reversal of an expectation, stated as such.
- [2012-10-12] **Stock-Android preference restated, with a size caveat**: "you guys know I prefer stock
  Android of the Nexus line any day of the week, but with a screen size like this you kind of do need some
  adjustments" — he tolerates TouchWiz here for a functional reason, consistent with the control/
  best-experience through-line.
- [2012-10-12] **Standout battery result, reported as a genuine test**: the 3,100 mAh battery — "I
  actually tried to kill it, I tried for a full day unsuccessfully: constant use, 6.5 hours of screen-on
  time, 22 hours on, still 10% left at 11:25 PM. So I called it quits. Very very impressive." A concrete,
  adversarial battery test with numbers.
- [2012-10-12] **A dated size-perception shift**: "I never thought I'd get used to it… but now this seems
  phone-sized to me, which makes the Galaxy S3 seem small, which makes the Galaxy Nexus seem really
  small." Reports his own adaptation honestly, with the timeframe.
- [2012-10-12] **Non-pentile display flagged as a plus**; the split-screen multitasking praised
  specifically ("you could watch a video and fact-check at the same time… I don't even use it a lot, but
  when I do, holy hell it is useful").
- [2012-10-12] **A candid hardware nitpick kept**: "the Note 2 rocks when you press the right-hand side —
  where the stylus sits — I don't know if it's mine or if it's lopsided, but it's really annoying when
  typing on a flat surface." Names a small defect rather than omitting it.
- [2012-10-12] **Verdict**: "arguably the best phone you can get right now," gated on tolerance for the
  size ("the only thing about it is the really large size, which would make someone either consider it or
  throw it down the drain").
