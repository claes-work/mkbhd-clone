# Nexus 6 & Nexus 9 Wishlist! (@mkbhd, 2014-08-03)

## Summary
A wishlist for the next Nexus phone/tablet that surfaces two durable analytical points: **stock Android's
chronic weakness at camera image-processing** (a software, not hardware, deficit), and a **"don't skimp
on graphics"** argument tied to Android L's 60 fps goal — the combination that could finally let a Nexus
"compete with the silly buttery smoothness of iOS." Also a dated **daily-driver** datum: he's switched
his SIM back to a Nexus 5 running the Android L developer preview "because it's that good."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-08-03] **The Nexus program will (and must) continue** — Google needs a hardware partner to show
  off each new Android and "lead the charge" for hardware direction (e.g. on-screen buttons debuting on a
  Nexus, then adopted by OEMs). Said despite the "Android Silver" rebranding rumors.
- [2014-08-03] **Wish #1 — camera image-processing (a software fix).** The Nexus 5's 8 MP Sony sensor is
  good hardware, but "other phones using the same sensor take better pictures, because **stock Android has
  always been really poor in the image-processing department**" — Google over-invests in the camera UI,
  under-invests in processing. A recurring, specific critique.
- [2014-08-03] **Wish #2 — battery.** The Android L preview already extends his Nexus 5 battery
  noticeably; a 15–20% bigger battery + Android L + the ART runtime could deliver real all-day life, even
  at the cost of a little thickness.
- [2014-08-03] **Wish #3 — "don't skimp on the graphics."** Android L targets a constant **60 fps**
  (which even Project Butter didn't hit); paired with a high-end GPU, "this new Nexus [could be] the first
  Android phone to compete with the silly buttery smoothness of iOS."
- [2014-08-03] **What he does NOT expect** (reading the trend lines): microSD, a removable battery (three
  gens gone, wireless charging now), another Verizon Nexus, or a QHD display (cost + graphics strain —
  "we'd have to wait for the next-next Nexus"). Aside: the Nexus 10 is still so good "we might not even
  need an upgrade."
- [2014-08-03] **Daily-driver datum:** he'd been running a GPe HTC One M8, but switched his SIM back to
  the **Nexus 5 on the Android L developer preview** — "not even a beta… but it's that good."
