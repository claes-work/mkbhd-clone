# AirPods Pro Unboxing & Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2019-10-29)

## Summary
Marques delivers a first-impressions unboxing of Apple's AirPods Pro roughly a day after Apple "quietly dropped" them on its site, ahead of the in-store availability he places at October 30th and explicitly framing this as a preview rather than the full review. He walks through everything new: a smaller, shorter-stemmed design (which he defends against the internet's rapid memes comparing it to a Pokémon, a hair dryer or Pingu), IPX4 water/sweat resistance, and three sizes of silicone rubber tips with the mediums attached out of the box. The headline addition is active noise cancellation — a rumor he says he was "on the fence" about — which uses the earbuds' microphones for cancellation beyond passive isolation; his early read is that it is "pretty decent," roughly on par with the on-ear Beats Solo Pro if you get a good seal, and better than he expected from earbuds, though he is unsure he'd wear them on a plane. He highlights a black grille that vents air so the in-ear seal doesn't feel "clogged up," Transparency mode to hear surroundings, and multiple ways to toggle cancellation (iOS 13.2 quick settings, a pressure-sensitive stem squeeze, or the Apple Watch). He praises a novel iOS "ear tip fit test" that plays a sound and uses the internal mics to confirm seal, saying he hadn't seen it before and it seems to work well. On sound he reserves judgment — "fine," better with ANC on — and covers the wider H1-chip case with wireless charging, still Lightning, and 24 hours of total battery. He questions why they're called "Pro" (noting no audio engineers are waiting to use them in studios) but concludes the package looks fairly complete and, crucially, appears to solve his two biggest gripes with the original AirPods: mediocre sound and falling out of his ears.

## Key claims
- [2019-10-29] AirPods Pro cost $250, which Marques frames as high-end pricing that puts them in competition with other nice headphones and earbuds at that price.
- [2019-10-29] Apple "quietly dropped" the AirPods Pro on its site the day before, and Marques had been using them for about a day at the time of publishing.
- [2019-10-29] The new design is smaller with a shorter stem, which Marques considers less "toothbrushy" and an improvement over weird competitors like the Microsoft Surface earbuds.
- [2019-10-29] A black pair was rumored but they ship only in glossy white; Marques wishes they came in more colors.
- [2019-10-29] AirPods Pro are officially IPX4 rated for water/sweat resistance, good for light rain.
- [2019-10-29] They ship with three sizes of silicone rubber tips (small, medium, large), with the mediums attached out of the box.
- [2019-10-29] They still use Apple's H1 chip, pairing by being near an iPhone and opening the case, and surface a setup tutorial covering media controls, Siri reading messages (iOS 13.2), and noise cancellation.
- [2019-10-29] AirPods Pro add active noise cancellation using the earbud microphones, beyond the passive isolation of any sealed earbud — a feature Marques says he was on the fence about being included.
- [2019-10-29] First-impression ANC is "pretty decent," about on par with the on-ear Beats Solo Pro given a good seal, and better than Marques expected from earbuds, though he's unsure he'd wear them on a plane.
- [2019-10-29] A larger black grille vents air (air pass-through) so the in-ear seal doesn't feel clogged, and Transparency mode lets you hear surroundings.
- [2019-10-29] Noise cancellation can be toggled via iOS 13.2 quick settings, a ~1-second squeeze of the now pressure-sensitive stem, or the Apple Watch, with a chime on activation.
- [2019-10-29] iOS includes an "ear tip fit test" that plays a sound and uses the internal mics to detect a proper seal and recommend adjusting tip size; Marques says he hadn't seen it before and it works well.
- [2019-10-29] Sound quality is only "fine" on first listen — better with ANC on, okay without — with full judgment deferred to the review.
- [2019-10-29] The case is slightly bigger/wider than the previous AirPods case, still supports wireless charging (like gen 2), stays pocketable, uses Lightning, and gives 24 hours of total battery life.
- [2019-10-29] Marques questions the "Pro" branding, noting "Pro" is now attached to anything high-end and that no audio engineers are waiting to use AirPods Pro in their studios.
- [2019-10-29] AirPods Pro go on sale in stores October 30th; Marques believes they're available today or tomorrow online.
- [2019-10-29] In theory the rubber tips solve Marques's two biggest problems with the original AirPods: poor sound and falling out of his ears after only a short run.
