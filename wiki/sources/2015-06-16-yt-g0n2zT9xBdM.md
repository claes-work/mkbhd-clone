# Top 5 Smartphones Under $300! (2015) (@mkbhd, 2015-06-16)

## Summary
A budget-phone roundup built on his **value-compresses-over-time thesis**: budget phones "take awesome tech
from the higher-end flagships and bring it down one or two years later," so "a lot of the smartphones we
can get for $300 today rival the experience of the phones we'd have spent $600–700 on three or four years
ago." His **#1 is the OnePlus One** (second year running). Includes a dated spec-skepticism aside — he
"hasn't personally noticed the difference" between the ZenFone 2's 4 GB RAM and 3 GB.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-06-16] **The budget thesis.** Budget phones inherit flagship tech ~1–2 years later; "$300 phones
  today rival the experience of $600–700 phones three or four years ago — something to be pretty happy
  about."
- [2015-06-16] **#1 OnePlus One ($300, now invite-free).** "Won best budget smartphone for 2014 from me
  last year… it's kind of funny because it's not built off another flagship — it *is* its own flagship."
  Industry-leading battery life, a good 13 MP camera "that competes with much higher-end flagships in image
  quality" (rare at this price).
- [2015-06-16] **The rest:** #5 Xiaomi Mi4i (best-designed budget, import-inflated); #4 **Moto G 2015
  ($180)** — near-stock, front-facing speakers, removable back + microSD, "feels premium" (shout-out to the
  even-cheaper Moto E); #3 Alcatel Idol 3 (front-facing speakers, reversible UI); #2 **Asus ZenFone 2** —
  "first phone with 4 GB of RAM, which is great, but **I haven't personally noticed the difference vs 3 GB**"
  (spec-skepticism), with a power button "in the worst imaginable spot" (top-middle).
- [2015-06-16] **Honorable mentions:** Sony Xperia M2 (budget waterproofing), Lumia 640 (best cheap Windows
  Phone), and the Sharp Aquos Crystal ("best-designed budget smartphone by far").
