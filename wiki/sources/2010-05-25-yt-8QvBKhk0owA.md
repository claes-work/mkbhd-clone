# Google Moderator Module (@mkbhd, 2010-05-25)

## Summary
Announces adoption of "Google Moderator," a widget embedded above his videos that lets
viewers submit and upvote content suggestions (software/Android videos, screencasts,
reviews, hardware requests). The top-voted suggestion each week (voting closes Fridays
at midnight) becomes that week's video, with a shout-out to the submitter.

## Key claims
- [2010-05-25] **A second, more formal audience-driven content-selection system**:
  distinct from the informal YouTube Group submissions covered in
  [[wiki/sources/2010-05-07-yt-zoF2a0BAIec]] — this one runs on a fixed weekly voting
  cadence with explicit rules (250-character cap, one attached video, moderation of
  inappropriate submissions, a shout-out reward). A more codified precursor to the
  "Use that Search Box!" audience-management system already promoted in synthesis
  pass 5.
- [2010-05-25] **Content moderation stated as policy from day one**: "obviously things
  that are inappropriate will be deleted," an explicit content-moderation rule attached
  to the launch.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "The suggestion on the top of the moderator widget on my channel that has the most
> thumbs up is going to win — that's the video that I'll be doing for that week."
