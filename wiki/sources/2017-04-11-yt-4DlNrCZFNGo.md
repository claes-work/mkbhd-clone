# Moto G5 Plus: Budget Smartphone King? (@mkbhd, 2017-04-11)

## Summary
Review of the $230 Moto G5 Plus — which he crowns the budget king. The clone-relevant core is the **"build up,
not cut down"** framing for budget phones and a restatement of his **"good phones got cheap, cheap phones got
good"** thesis. Also a useful read on the budget-flagship tier drifting upmarket (OnePlus creeping to $400+).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-04-11] **"Build up, not cut down" — the right way to make a budget phone.** "You default to thinking
  a way-cheaper phone is inferior in every way, but the Moto G5/G5 Plus is the result of Motorola not cutting
  *down* from flagships but **building up as high as they can for a price** — and they're still killing it in
  the budget game." A durable framing for how to judge affordable tech.
- [2017-04-11] **"I keep coming back to: good phones are getting cheap and cheap phones are getting good."**
  The optimistic budget thesis restated as a personal refrain (cf. ZTE Axon 7 → [[2016-08-17-yt-ptni6tRrU54]]).
- [2017-04-11] **The camera is the biggest, quickest drop-off (name where budget cuts show).** "On paper a
  12 MP f/1.7 camera, but no OIS — good shots in good light, but as light drops you see noise, chromatic
  aberration, less dynamic range, less natural color. Motorola has always struggled with cameras even in
  their best phones; this isn't the phone you buy for the camera." Names exactly where the price shows (the
  value-forces-visible-tradeoffs lens).
- [2017-04-11] **Near-stock Moto software = a plus.** "Classic Motorola — mostly pure stock Android with
  enhancements they integrate to take advantage of the hardware (the home-button gestures turning the reader
  into a trackpad, chop-for-flashlight, twist-for-camera). No heavy skin, no redundant apps. I really like
  that about them." His stock-Android-restraint value applied.
- [2017-04-11] **The budget-flagship tier is drifting up.** "The budget-flagship crown used to go to the
  OnePlus 3/3T for me, but those are creeping into the $400–450 range now, edging up to compete with
  flagships — so for the best phone under $300, it's a no-brainer: the Moto G holds the crown. If you're
  balling on a budget, this is the one." A market-segmentation read (the mid-tier moving upmarket).
- [2017-04-11] **Honest about the cost-cutting.** Notes the tradeoffs: still micro-USB not USB-C ("would've
  liked USB-C"), no NFC (matters if you use mobile payments), a mono front-facing speaker, and a big camera
  bump with "no reason for it" (no Moto Mods on this one). Grades on the value tier ("at the top of the heap
  for the price").

## Notable verbatim quotes
