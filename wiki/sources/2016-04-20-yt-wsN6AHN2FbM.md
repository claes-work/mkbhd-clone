# NextDesk Review: Motorized Desk! (@mkbhd, 2016-04-20)

## Summary
A gear video on his motorized NextDesk sit-stand desk — prompted by questions after he posted an
updated gear list on Kit. Doubles as a "tech going into the new studio" node. Notable for a concrete
ergonomics rule (standing height ~46–47" so arms are parallel to the desk), his cable-management
obsession, and a characteristic filmmaking aside (the motorized desk doubles as a camera-motion tool).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-04-20] **Owns it a while; makes the video because there aren't many.** "I've owned one for a
  while, there's been one in the background of plenty of my videos, and I got a lot of questions after
  I posted my updated gear list on Kit — there aren't many videos about it." (Gear-recommendation
  driven by audience demand.)
- [2016-04-20] **The ergonomics rule.** "My standing height is usually ~46–47 inches, because that's
  the height where your arms are parallel to the desk while using the keyboard and mouse — the
  ergonomically correct way to line it up." Health framing is dismissed lightly: "supposed to be
  healthier, blah blah blah — I just feel better standing up once in a while."
- [2016-04-20] **Cable-management as the selling point.** The high-end touches that beat "just a plain
  table": a rear cable cover + a built-in under-desk power strip so "you now only have one cable coming
  off the back of the desk." A recurring personal value (clean cabling).
- [2016-04-20] **Filmmaker's-eye aside.** The smooth hydraulic lift "can double as a video tool to get
  some motion into shots if you want to, instead of getting fancy with a tripod." Characteristic:
  seeing production uses in everyday objects.
- [2016-04-20] **Buyer's-guide honesty (flex).** The extended two-leg Terra has "a slight bowing in
  the middle" under heavy weight — "the worst-case scenario for flex"; the four-leg Pro / metal Air Pro
  "do not flex at all." Names the specific weakness and who should avoid it.
- [2016-04-20] **Studio-setup thread.** "Just wanted to give you guys a sneak peek of some of the tech
  going into the new studio… anything tech-related as I set it up, I'll probably make a video about it."
  BTS on Twitter/Snapchat. (Continues the studio arc from [[2016-04-08-yt-K_34vZ9haCk]].)
