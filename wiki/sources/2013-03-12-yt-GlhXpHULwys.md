# Sony Xperia Z Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-12)

## Summary
The full review of the Xperia Z he'd praised at CES — and he flips: "the most mixed bag
of all time," great and terrible in equal measure on every axis. The review is a clean
statement of his **buy-according-to-your-own-values** framework for a polarizing device,
plus the **"1080p is becoming the new megapixel count"** spec-inflation warning and a
notable **methodology disclaimer** about a factory screen protector affecting his display
verdict.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-12] **"The biggest mixed bag ever" — and how to decide on one**: "if it was all
  bad or all good it would be easy to recommend or not… but you really have to pay
  attention to the goods and the bads, pick what you like, and if they match up with your
  values you might still go for it. For me they don't align with what I've gotten used to
  enjoying." His decide-by-your-own-priorities framework, stated explicitly.
- [2013-03-12] **Spec-inflation warning — "1080p is becoming the megapixel count"**: "that
  440 PPI is kind of becoming the megapixel-count number… it's like having a 37-megapixel
  camera that doesn't take great photos." Warns that a headline spec (resolution) is
  becoming a marketing number divorced from real quality — a display-side restatement of
  his optics-over-megapixels thesis.
- [2013-03-12] **Viewing angles matter more than peak sharpness**: "head-on it's extremely
  sharp and beautiful, but a few degrees off-axis the viewing angles, color, and contrast
  go straight downhill… you don't even need a side-by-side to notice this display is
  subpar." Judges the display by real use, not the spec.
- [2013-03-12] **A methodology disclaimer — the factory screen protector**: "a bit of a
  disclaimer: this phone comes with a very subtle screen protector already applied [the
  Sony branding is on it]… that's probably the cause of some of the display issues I have,
  but I wouldn't take it off because that would expose the display." Flags a confound in his
  own testing rather than hiding it — a fidelity-conscious habit.
- [2013-03-12] **The design-vs-rugged contradiction, named**: "is this a slick designer
  phone or a tough rugged water-resistant phone? It's really hard to be both, and Sony
  tried to be both — that's where the contradictions come from." Diagnoses the product's
  root tension.
- [2013-03-12] **Even-handed close — credits what he can't**: "performance flies, I can't
  take anything away from it… on par with the Note 2." Praises the genuinely-good axes even
  in a phone he won't recommend.
