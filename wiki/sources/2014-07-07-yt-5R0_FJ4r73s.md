# iPhone 6 Sapphire Crystal Display! (@mkbhd, 2014-07-07)

## Summary
Leak coverage of an **actual off-the-assembly-line iPhone 6 front glass panel** (via leaker Sonny
Dickson) — confirming a 4.7" iPhone 6 with a **sapphire** (not just Gorilla Glass) front. Marques does a
hands-on **durability test** (knife, keys, stabbing, bending) and can't scratch or break it, and marvels
mostly at the **material clarity**. It's a genuine primary-source scoop, but the testing here is
methodologically loose — which he corrects in the very next video (the Mohs-scale follow-up). Mid-yield;
best read as the setup for the 07-19 methodology correction.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-07-07] **Confirms the 4.7" sapphire front** — "because of its existence we can basically confirm
  there will be an iPhone 6 with a 4.7" display," and it has sapphire, debunking the rumor that only a
  5.5" model would. (Apple already uses sapphire on the 5S camera lens and home button.)
- [2014-07-07] **Material quality is what excites him most** — paper-thin, ~0% opacity, no color shift or
  distortion looking through it: "about as good quality as the glass I'm using to record this video."
  Predicts Apple will make a big marketing deal of it.
- [2014-07-07] **Ad-hoc durability test:** knife (deep pressure), keys, a hard stab-and-drag, and
  bending/flexing — "I couldn't get the surface to even scratch a little bit," "no way I can break this
  under my own power." Concludes "you don't need a screen protector here."
- [2014-07-07] **Honest about the test's limits** — the panel isn't attached to a weighted phone, so he
  couldn't do a drop or corner-impact test ("the important stuff to see if it would shatter").
