# Top 5 WWDC 2017 Announcements! (@mkbhd, 2017-06-06)

## Summary
His Top-5 from WWDC 2017 — an unusually hardware-heavy Apple keynote. The clone-relevant beats: his hands-on
**HomePod-beats-Sonos-and-Alexa** first listen, the surprise **iMac Pro** ("an elaborate trap set by Jony
Ive… I really want one"), the **iPad Pro's 120Hz ProMotion** flagged as the underrated announcement, and the
recurring smartwatch-ambivalence ("maybe it'll get mine off the shelf").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-06-06] **HomePod: sounds better than Sonos and Alexa (first listen) — and a naming quibble.** "In a
  quiet area I compared it to an Amazon Alexa speaker and a Sonos — it sounded a lot better than the Alexa
  and decently better than the Sonos; the mids were really full and legible where they weren't on the Sonos.
  It's a premium hi-fi speaker first, smart features second." Notes he'd want to test it with his own music,
  and whether it works with Google/Spotify/as a Bluetooth speaker. Aside: "I'd have gone with Apple Home or
  Siri Speaker over HomePod, but iPod branding is still strong."
- [2017-06-06] **iMac Pro = "an elaborate trap set by Jony Ive, preying on content creators whose Mac Pros
  are on their last breaths."** "Up to 18-core Xeons, Vega GPUs, 128 GB RAM, 4 TB SSD, 10 GbE — an absolute
  monster for a Final Cut user — but totally not upgradeable. Who buys a $5,000 workstation-grade all-in-one
  that you can't upgrade? Maybe I'm not learning my lesson from the 2013 trash-can Mac Pro, but I really want
  one." The buying-logic tension, argued at length in [[2017-06-08-yt-RvXmktAurSQ]].
- [2017-06-06] **The underrated announcement: the iPad Pro's 120Hz ProMotion display.** "They casually
  dropped it — a display that dynamically switches refresh rates, up to 120 Hz (60 fps constant) not just for
  gaming but for *all* the swiping/typing/drawing. It's like butter, like water pulling your hand across the
  UI. Hard to show in 30 fps video, but trust me, it's very different — people should be excited." His
  find-the-underrated-announcement instinct.
- [2017-06-06] **iOS 11 (#2) reframes the iPad as a possible primary computer.** "The biggest iPad weakness
  was that it claimed to replace a PC but couldn't do basic multitasking/productivity. iOS 11 adds a real
  dock, drag-and-drop between apps, a Files app, and Apple-Pencil handwriting-to-searchable-text — it made me
  reconsider the iPad as a first/primary computer for more people." (Its own deep dive to come.)
- [2017-06-06] **macOS High Sierra — "the nerd in me" loves h.265 + eGPU + VR.** Refinement release; but
  "hardware-accelerated h.265 built into Final Cut — let's go"; native Unity/Unreal VR support; and an
  external-GPU dev kit (Radeon Pro in a Thunderbolt 3 enclosure) "I kind of want to get." His production/
  bleeding-edge enthusiasms.
- [2017-06-06] **watchOS 4 (#5) — the recurring smartwatch ambivalence.** "I'm not a big smartwatch person;
  nothing here would make a non-owner get one — it's more improvements to make someone who owns one and
  stopped wearing it try it again. Maybe it'll do that for me — I'll try it." His standing wearable-fatigue.
