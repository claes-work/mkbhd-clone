# YouTube Tech Bundle GIVEAWAY! (@mkbhd, 2011-12-04)

## Summary
A holiday **collaboration giveaway with fellow tech YouTuber Duncan33303**, structured as a
two-video cross-entry: each creator posts a video, one grand prize is drawn from one
comment section and the second prize from the other, so entering both doubles your chances.
The prizes are deliberately chosen as **starter kit for aspiring YouTubers** — a Canon
ELPH 300 HS with SD card, mini tripod and Corel VideoStudio Pro as first prize; a Kodak
PlayFull pocket camcorder as second. The framing throughout is "helping you guys out with
YouTube," and the second prize carries a small biographical detail: it's very similar to the
camcorder he himself started on.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-04] **Named collaboration**: "a little collab between Duncan33303, fellow tech
  YouTuber, and I" — a dated instance of the peer-network cross-promotion running through the
  early channel (Duncan33303 also appears in the 2010 Beats Studio review and the 2011
  YouTube Tips #4 collab with Austin Evans).
- [2011-12-04] **Cross-entry mechanic**: two videos, two comment sections, one prize drawn from
  each, with the audience explicitly told to enter both — a designed traffic exchange between
  two channels rather than a simple shoutout.
- [2011-12-04] **Biography detail (self-reported)**: the second-prize Kodak PlayFull is "a very
  similar camcorder to the one I started off with when I first started YouTube. It's a 720p HD
  camcorder" — dating his first video camera as a cheap 720p pocket camcorder of that class.
- [2011-12-04] **Prize selection reasoning**: the Canon 300 HS is picked because it's "very
  very popular both for vlogging and YouTube videos," bundled with a card, a tripod that
  "works very well with the camera," and an editing suite "for those of you guys who want to
  get started." The bundle is assembled as a working setup, not a headline item.
- [2011-12-04] Audience framing: aimed at beginners — "if you're already YouTubing, good for
  you, this is going to make you better" — and at people the creators expect to see making
  videos with the gear afterwards.
- [2011-12-04] **Purchase intent restated**: "hopefully Galaxy Nexus video this week… I really
  want that phone," five weeks after the day-one intent stated in
