# Editors Keys SL150 Microphone [HD] (@mkbhd, 2010-12-05)

## Summary
Review of the Editors Keys SL150 USB studio microphone — notably, the exact
microphone he records all his own videos with (screencasts, hardware reviews, and
voiceovers, including this one), mounted on a Samson MD5 stand unboxed previously on
the channel. Covers build (aluminum top, hard-plastic body), portability (included
leather case), plug-and-play USB 2.0 operation (no drivers), and audio specs (22mm
gold-diaphragm condenser, hypercardioid, 20-18,000kHz). Closes with an explicit
pros/cons list and an 8.5/10 score.

## Key claims
- [2010-12-05] **Discloses this is his own recording equipment**: "the microphone
  you're looking at here is actually... a microphone that I record all my videos
  with, both screencast and hardware videos when I do voiceovers, including this
  video here" — a direct, dated confirmation of his production audio chain as of
  Dec 2010.
- [2010-12-05] **8.5/10, itemized pros/cons format**: pros = "great audio quality for
  the price," "pretty solid build construction," "plug-and-play... with a really long
  USB cord easy to set up"; cons = doesn't include the stand, no pop filter included
  (which he uses anyway) — "I think Editor Keys can do a lot better in a couple of
  certain areas about this mic but overall it's a very very good value recording
  studio microphone."
- [2010-12-05] **Value framing tied explicitly to price bracket**: "it's the highest
  quality USB studio microphone on the market for under $150 in the United States" —
  a price-tier-bounded superlative claim, not an unqualified best-in-class claim.
