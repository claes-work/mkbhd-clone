# Beats X Review: Best Beats Ever? (@mkbhd, 2017-02-22)

## Summary
Review of the $150 BeatsX — "the cheapest wireless Beats, cheaper than AirPods, and supposedly sounds
better." Verdict: yes, "the best Beats ever" for bang-for-buck — but the clone-relevant point is his
**know-who-it's-for** buying framework and a pointed critique of a "glaring omission" (not sweatproof, so
useless for the workouts Beats are associated with).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-02-22] **"The best Beats ever" — in the bang-for-buck-for-Beats category.** "Are these the best
  Beats ever? Sure — at least in the bang-for-your-buck-for-Beats category. But that doesn't mean you should
  get them; you've got to know who Beats are made for." Qualifies the superlative carefully.
- [2017-02-22] **The W1 chip + Android-friendliness (a genuine advantage over AirPods).** "Same W1 chip as
  AirPods — power on and your iPhone pairs instantly to all your Apple devices. But *unlike* AirPods, you
  can connect these to an Android phone and keep all the functionality (inline volume, mic) — connect
  AirPods to Android and you lose everything (auto-pause, Siri, volume)." Credits the physical inline
  controls as more portable across platforms.
- [2017-02-22] **The "glaring omission": not sweatproof — and the cynical read of why.** "Durability-wise
  these aren't sweatproof or water-resistant in any way — a glaring omission. People smarter than me will
  tell you that's just so Beats can point you toward another pair they sell just for working out." Names the
  business incentive behind a product gap.
- [2017-02-22] **The wing-tip seal beats AirPods for his ears.** "They make a really good seal thanks to
  the wing tip — even better than the Jaybird X3s — so the noise isolation is excellent for my ear shape.
  Some people can put AirPods in and run around; I'm not one of those people, my ears don't work with that."
  Personal-fit honesty (a recurring theme).
- [2017-02-22] **Made-for-iPhone-first (the Lightning tell).** "They charge via Lightning — so if you're an
  Android person you'd carry two chargers. This reinforces they're made for iPhone users first." Reads the
  target user from the charging port.
- [2017-02-22] **The buyer segmentation.** "Know who Beats are for: active people who sweat want sweatproof
  (get the Jaybird X3); people who want *no* wires at all get AirPods; people who want *Beats* can now
  justify these." The segment-by-buyer verdict (cf. the LG 5K "it depends who you are").
