# OnePlus 2 Camera Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-07-15)

## Summary
A pre-launch camera-only review of the OnePlus 2 — notable for **disclosure ethics** ("OnePlus sent me one
early to test the camera; I'm not going to be promoting it in any way, literally just figuring out if it's
any good or not") and for **foreshadowing his big multi-phone camera comparison** (a Smartphone-Awards /
Blind-Camera-Test precursor: he shoots the OnePlus 2 head-to-head against the LG G4, Galaxy S6, iPhone 6
Plus, and OnePlus One). Verdict: solid, balanced exposure, a B+/A- camera — but "definitely not the best;
that's still the Galaxy S6, closely followed by the LG G4."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-07-15] **Disclosure up front.** "They sent me one of the phones early and I'm tasked with testing
  this camera — I'm not going to be promoting it in any way, literally just figuring out if it's any good."
  (He won't reveal any other specs about the unreleased phone.)
- [2015-07-15] **The camera:** 13 MP, ~f/2.0 max aperture, laser autofocus. Solid one-shot auto results —
  "sharpness is good with plenty of light, contrast on point, colors accurate, and dynamic range actually
  pretty healthy without HDR." Notably, **it picks a balanced exposure** between highlights and shadows —
  "unlike some phones like the M9, this doesn't blow things out." 4K video "looks good — not muddy upscaled
  1080p; it gets a pass." Tends to err slightly underexposed (vs others that overexpose).
- [2015-07-15] **The multi-phone comparison (the seed).** He shoots the same scenes on the **OnePlus One,
  iPhone 6 Plus, LG G4, Galaxy S6, and OnePlus 2** and shows them side by side ("watch in 4K to see the
  differences"). Explicitly flags a future "**giant comparison of all the smartphone cameras**" once the
  Note 5 / new iPhone / Moto X / Nexus land. Encourages viewers to **pixel-peep** the full-size links.
- [2015-07-15] **Verdict: a good-not-best camera.** "Image processing is definitely a step up from last
  year's model… B+ to A-. Definitely not the best camera I've used — that's still hands-down the Galaxy S6,
  followed closely by the LG G4." Caveat: it's a v1.0 camera app; processing could improve before launch.
