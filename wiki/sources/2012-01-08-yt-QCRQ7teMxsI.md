# Tour of my Nexus (@mkbhd, 2012-01-08)

## Summary
The second entry in the "Tour of" series, and the most **personally revealing** video in
this batch: a full walkthrough of his actual Galaxy Nexus setup — home screens, icon pack,
widgets, and the specific apps he uses daily. Explicitly made by request ("this was requested
so much"), and he keeps a running tally of what viewers want toured next. Beyond the format
note, it's a dense source of dated personal facts: what he uses to organize his life, how
much data he burns, and a couple of hobbies mentioned in passing.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-08] **Personal app stack, stated on camera**: gTasks for task management, Foursquare,
  Dropbox, Google Maps/Navigation ("has saved me a couple times already"), a Twitter client he
  calls "Twi"/Tweakdeck-adjacent, a Google Reader–style news app, YouTube, Evernote, Google Docs
  (with InSync, reviewed a week earlier in [[2012-01-01-yt-p0nlU7E_xs8]]), Spotify, SoundCloud,
  Amazon + Amazon Price Check + Google Shopper, and a speed-test app.
- [2012-01-08] **Spotify verdict — service vs. app split**: "Spotify, which I love. The app
  could use some work, but the service is great." The same hardware/software-style split
  applied to a subscription product.
- [2012-01-08] **Dated data-consumption figure**: since getting the phone (~3.5 weeks) he'd used
  **6.6 GB of 4G and 3.92 GB of Wi-Fi** — "a pretty decent amount of data, not enough to get
  throttled though, thank gosh." A concrete, dated glimpse of his usage intensity and of the
  throttling-era anxiety.
- [2012-01-08] **Golf mentioned in passing**: "Sky Droid, by the way, is what I use when I'm
  golfing" — a dated hobby marker, complementing the ultimate-frisbee thread from
  [[2011-09-23-yt-4Etc7n5NCcI]] and the later "lawn sports" identity.
- [2012-01-08] **Self-reported non-gamer**: "I'm not a big gamer, but the games I have are Angry
  Birds, Fruit Ninja and Zombie Dash — and I guess Words With Friends could be considered a game
  too."
- [2012-01-08] **Minimalism, fourth dated instance**: "the setup as it is — I don't have a lot of
  widgets, as you can see."
- [2012-01-08] **Stock-software preference restated with a reason**: he uses the stock lock screen
  for a specific function (swipe up to text back a missed caller) and the stock browser "because
  it's fast and it syncs with Chrome" — preference argued from function, not ideology.
- [2012-01-08] Customization detail: a Google-coloured icon set distributed via his Google+
  profile, applied with Desktop Visualizer; a circular battery widget; an extended-controls
  widget for Wi-Fi/Bluetooth/brightness/airplane mode; Beautiful Widgets for weather.
- [2012-01-08] **HD-viewing instruction to the audience**: "make sure you watch this in HD so you
  can read everything" — production awareness of how the video will actually be consumed.
- [2012-01-08] Privacy handling on camera, consistent with the Facebook Timeline tour: he
  deliberately skips opening the people and messaging apps, and blurs/avoids a phone number.
