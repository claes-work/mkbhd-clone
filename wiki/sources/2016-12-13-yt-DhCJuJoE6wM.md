# MacBook Pro with Touch Bar Review! Worth it? (@mkbhd, 2016-12-13)

## Summary
The full Touch Bar MacBook Pro review — home to one of his most durable evaluation principles: **the line
between a gimmick and a useful feature**. Also the definitive statement of the **"dongles are our bridge to
the future and they suck"** framing (with the follow-the-incentives USB-C-accessory argument), and a candid
bleeding-edge verdict: "not worth the price… would I buy it again? Yeah."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-12-13] **THE GIMMICK-VS-USEFUL PRINCIPLE.** "This is the difference between a gimmick and an actually
  useful feature: to not just be a gimmick, these Touch Bar buttons have to do things I couldn't already do
  with a keyboard shortcut — and pros use keyboard shortcuts. The new-tab button in Safari is cool, but is
  reaching up for it faster than Command-T? Probably not. The best thing about the Touch Bar is when I can
  quickly do things I couldn't ordinarily do with the mouse or a shortcut" — e.g. Final Cut audio-fade
  trimming, Safari/QuickTime video scrubbing. A reusable, durable test for any new feature.
- [2016-12-13] **"Dongles are our bridge to the future, and they suck."** "USB-C is the future — that's why
  we get hyped for phones to have it. Laptops are a bit further down that road. These dongles are our bridge
  to the future and they suck — ugly, an extra expense, you lose them — but if we want a future where every
  laptop has this high-throughput reversible port, we have to cross that bridge eventually." The nuanced
  future-vs-present framing at its fullest.
- [2016-12-13] **Follow-the-incentives on USB-C accessories.** "This is Apple, so these laptops will be
  everywhere — so if you're an accessory maker, it's in your best interest to make as many good USB-C
  accessories as possible. We're already seeing good ones (Samsung T3 SSD, the LG 5K, USB-C card readers,
  even USB-C headphones)." The market-analysis lens ([[2016-09-18-yt-XMAwwpYQd7E]]).
- [2016-12-13] **The bleeding-edge verdict.** "Is it worth the price? No, it's definitely overpriced. Would I
  still buy it again? Yeah, I really like it — but I'm not the average person; the average person doesn't make
  a financial decision like this, and I don't think it's a smart one right now. As someone on the bleeding
  edge of tech, I'm willing to make it work." Explicitly separates his own bias from the general
  recommendation ("maybe hold out for the 2017 version and skip over the bridge").
- [2016-12-13] **The SD-card omission gripe + honest QC note.** "The omission of an SD card slot is terrible
  — so many cameras and pro accessories use SD cards. That's my only real gripe." And, source-critically:
  "I'd have expected a little better quality control — none of it happened to my machines, but I've seen a
  lot on Twitter, from unresponsive Touch Bar graphics to full graphics meltdowns." Reports problems he
  didn't personally hit, attributed to others.
- [2016-12-13] **Touch ID + display praise; keyboard-travel acceptance.** Touch ID in the power button = "this
  is awesome, makes typing a password seem inconvenient"; the 500-nit P3 display "maybe my favorite part."
  On the 2nd-gen butterfly keyboard: "I originally preferred the deeper-travel chiclet keys, but after a few
  weeks I've gotten super used to these and type just as fast" — a rare walk-back of an ergonomic complaint.
  Laments MagSafe again ("rip MagSafe, it was great while we had it").
