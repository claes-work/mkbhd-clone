# YouTube Gear #2 | Carbon Slider! (@mkbhd, 2016-01-29)

## Summary
Episode 2 of the **"YouTube Gear"** franchise — the **iFootage Shark Slider S1**, "one of the most
underrated things I use," bought in 2013 and used constantly for 3 years without ever talking about it. It's
the piece of gear behind his **signature moving/tracking/revolving shots**. A production/gear datum that
also restates the content-first-but-gear-helps principle.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-01-29] **What a slider does + why it matters.** "A slider gets the camera moving on a smooth track…
  a way to add motion to make videos more interesting without the shake of handheld." On a tripod "you can
  only do so much with panning and tilting — this takes it to the next level."
- [2016-01-29] **His exact rig (workflow).** The **belt-driven, inertia-driven Shark Slider S1** (carbon-
  fiber rails — "I can be like a DJ over here and not even touch the camera"), and — key — "**I don't put
  the camera directly on the slider; I put a Manfrotto 502HD video head on it and control the camera on the
  head**," which lets him get tracking shots and revolve around a subject. "That all comes from getting good
  at using this slider." He's used it since 2013.
- [2016-01-29] **Content-first-but-gear-helps (restated).** "The videos you make are the most important
  part, but it helps to have some dope gear." (He gives the slider away on Twitter, as he promises for every
  YouTube Gear item.)
