# PlayStation 5 Unboxing & Accessories! (@mkbhd, 2020-10-27)

## Summary
A hardware-only unboxing (under a Sony embargo that permits only packaging/hardware, not gameplay or performance) of the final retail PlayStation 5 and its first-party accessories. Marques unboxes the disc-drive PS5, notes its enormous size (dwarfs the Xbox Series X he's been testing and his PS4 Pro), the required stand/dock for horizontal placement, and the tiny embossed PlayStation-shape (X/O/square/triangle) texture on the console and controller. He's genuinely impressed by the **DualSense** controller — bigger, more solid, more comfortable than the DualShock 4, USB-C rechargeable, light relocated behind the trackpad — calling it "a pretty huge win" while noting he can't yet demo its haptics/adaptive triggers (embargo). He runs through the DualSense charging station (wishes it were heavier and used magnets), the Pulse 3D wireless headset (unique dual-band design, comfy, but he doubts he'll use it), and the HD Camera (for streamers). Recurring MKBHD tell: he'd have loved MagSafe-style magnets in the controller dock.

## Key claims
- [2020-10-27] This is the *final retail* PS5 hardware, unlike the *preview* Xbox Series X he'd been testing; the disc (optical-drive) version, box claims 8K / 4K120 / HDR, 825GB storage. (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] The PS5 is "gigantic" — bigger in every dimension and heavier than both the Xbox Series X (which it "dwarfs") and his PS4 Pro; the height/footprint is real and could restrict airflow under a TV. (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] It ships with a power cable (no brick), one DualSense controller, paperwork, an HDMI cable, and a separate stand/dock required to sit it flat on its side ("don't throw this out"). (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] Design details: matte-white outer shells, glossy black inner; the inner surface is textured with tiny embossed PlayStation-button shapes (X, O, square, triangle) in a seemingly random pattern; a lot of the visible bulk is heatsink / thermal management. (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] His unveiling-era "alien spaceship" first impression softened in person — he likes the build now, thinks it looks like a "fancy building." (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] The DualSense is "a pretty huge win": bigger, more solid, more comfortable, longer stems than the DualShock 4, still lightweight; built-in 1500mAh rechargeable battery, USB-C charging, light moved to behind the trackpad, same face-button layout. (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] He previously found the DualShock 4 merely "fine" and actually preferred the Xbox Elite controller for its size/quality — so he was hoping for a bigger, more solid PS5 controller, "and that's exactly what we got." (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] Embargo limits this to unboxing/hardware — he can't yet show haptic feedback, adaptive triggers, or responsiveness. (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] The DualSense charging station charges two controllers via pins; he wishes it were heavier/more solid and had used magnets — "they should have done magnets ... if they had MagSafe technology in the PS5 dock." (Marques)
- [2020-10-27] The Pulse 3D headset (3D audio, dual mics, ~12h battery, USB-C, unique sliding dual-band fit, mostly plastic, comfy thick cushions) looks great but he suspects he won't use it; the 1080p HD Camera is for streamers. (Marques)
