# LG G4 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-05-24)

## Summary
The full LG G4 review, run in his **pros-and-cons format** — and "it's mostly pros; there's not a lot
wrong with this phone at all." Standouts: a **removable battery + microSD** ("an endangered species of
smartphones"), a great **Quantum IPS QHD display** (not quite the S6's), and a **camera a lot of people
are calling the most impressive feature**. His least-favorite part is the build (the G3's thin bezels gave
way to a returned forehead + a plastic back).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-05-24] **High-end across the board.** Snapdragon 808 (benchmarks a bit under the 810 "but not a
  big deal — still an excellent performer"), 3 GB; smooth gaming/browsing/multitasking on Android 5.1 + LG's
  UI ("a refined G3 UI… copied some good stuff from rivals, but I still prefer stock").
- [2015-05-24] **Build is his least-favorite part.** A 5.5" slightly-curved (non-flex) phone that "grew
  its forehead back from the G3"; glossy metal sides + a plastic (diamond-pattern) back — "not the prettiest
  phone." He'd prefer the leather backs over the plastic.
- [2015-05-24] **Removable battery + microSD = an endangered species.** "The G4 is part of an endangered
  species of smartphones that still have a removable back" → swap the 3000 mAh battery + expand storage.
  (Post-launch it also gained Qualcomm Quick Charge, still no wireless.) Battery life "great" (~4 hr screen-
  on, sleep at 100% → wake at 98%).
- [2015-05-24] **Display: great, not quite the S6.** A 5.5" **Quantum IPS** QHD (not OLED, "not to be
  confused with quantum-dot TVs") — bright/sharp/vibrant, "top-five displays," but he "still hands the crown
  to the Galaxy S6" (a bit brighter outdoors). Dinged by a mediocre rear-firing speaker "that does the
  display a disservice."
- [2015-05-24] **The camera is the headline.** "One of the most talked-about, most impressive features…
  blowing more people away than anything else, and rightfully so": 16 MP, 1/2.6" sensor, **f/1.8 (the
  widest on any smartphone so far)**, laser AF + a color-spectrum sensor for white balance. (LG's "DSLR
  replacement" pitch, from the impressions, largely holds up.)
