# Top 5 Reasons Google+ is Awesome! (@mkbhd, 2012-09-29)

## Summary
A defense of Google+ against its "wasteland" reputation — five reasons it's actually good: the +1
button, an engaged 100M-daily-active user base, deep Google-services integration (Google+ as the hub),
free 10-person Hangouts, and a best-in-class mobile app. Heavily date-scoped (Google+ was shut down in
2019), but it documents his **active, sustained Google+ advocacy** and a recurring rhetorical move:
*if a network feels like a wasteland to you, you're probably using it wrong.*

## Key claims
- [2012-09-29] **The "you're doing it wrong" argument** — the reusable move: "if you say Google+ is a
  wasteland from your own personal experience, you're probably doing it wrong — like if I were to follow
  zero people on Twitter or have zero Facebook friends, I'd think those sites are wastelands too." A
  network's value depends on curating your circle, not the platform alone.
- [2012-09-29] **Cites engagement data, not just totals**: Google+ "recently passed 400 million users to
  sign up," and against the "Google forces signups so none are active" objection, "one in four of them —
  about 100 million people — are active on Google+ every single day, and I'm one of them." He answers the
  skeptic's framing with a specific active-user figure.
- [2012-09-29] **Google+ as the hub of Google's services** — the structural point: "a very high-end Google
  VP described it as a hub for the rest of the Google services… Maps, Gmail, contacts, calendar,
  everything depends on your Google+ profile." He reads Google+ as connective infrastructure, not a
  standalone Facebook rival — which reframes the whole "is it a good social network?" question.
- [2012-09-29] **Hangouts as a genuine advantage**: free, built-in, up to 10-person video chat — contrasted
  with FaceTime ("only two people, and Wi-Fi-only before"). A concrete capability comparison.
- [2012-09-29] **The mobile app as the standout**: "the Google+ app is fast, it's responsive, it has just
  about every single feature you can use on the desktop — which is something not all the other services
  offer," versus the "really badly" sucking Windows Phone/Myspace apps. Feature-parity between mobile and
  desktop treated as the real quality bar.
- [2012-09-29] **Discloses his own platform stake**: "I'm one of them [the daily actives]… circle me, my
  link is down below." A dated confirmation of his active Google+ presence, consistent with the 2011–2012
  platform-allocation thread.
- [2012-09-29] **A best-place-to-meet-strangers framing**: "Google+ is probably the best social network to
  just start meeting new people… it might seem awkward at first because barely any of your Facebook
  friends are there, but it's a great community." A dated read of Google+'s interest-graph character.
