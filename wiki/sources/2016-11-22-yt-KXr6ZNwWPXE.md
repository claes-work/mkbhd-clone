# Tesla Model S P100D: The App! (@mkbhd, 2016-11-22)

## Summary
Video #3 of the "Apollo" playlist — a walkthrough of the Tesla mobile app (shot on the OnePlus 3T front
camera). Reinforces the **"it's a tech product / LTE-connected car"** framing, delivers pointed criticism
of the app's dated, crash-prone software (a direct-to-Elon aside), and yields personal-ownership details:
his **charge-to-85–90% battery-health habit** and the Summon demo.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-11-22] **"It's an LTE-connected car — a tech product."** "There's a lot going on… it's an LTE-
  connected car, it's a tech product." The consistent frame that the car is a software platform, reviewed
  like a gadget.
- [2016-11-22] **Criticizes the app's software directly ("hey Elon…").** "The app is super outdated in the
  way it looks — very old-iOS, no iOS 10 look. And it's crashing on basic functions like this, which is
  weird; it actually works better on iOS than Android. Hey Elon, if you're watching, this app update is
  long overdue." Holds a beloved product to account; notes Musk "pretty much announces all new Tesla
  products" and has tweeted an update is coming. (Software-critic-even-of-favorites; cf. the Note 7.)
- [2016-11-22] **Battery-health habit (personal ownership).** "For Teslas and their large lithium-ion
  batteries, you don't want to charge to 100% every time — that'd be ~319 miles. Unless I'm going on a road
  trip, I charge to about 85–90% (~280 mi) and set that as my charge limit every night." A concrete
  ownership behavior + a battery-longevity principle. (Charges in his garage.)
- [2016-11-22] **"Summon is the most modern version of ghost-riding the whip."** Demos Summon — the car
  drives itself forward/back out of a tight garage using its sensors, meeting you with the handles out, no
  one inside — plus HomeLink opening/closing the garage door behind it: "no one's in the car, no one's
  controlling it — that is the future." Delight in autonomy features.
- [2016-11-22] **Names concrete app wishlist items (a reviewer's suggestions).** Wants: nearby open
  Superchargers in-app, a trunk-open-too-long alert, closing the windows remotely when rain's coming, and a
  material-design Android redesign. Turns the review into constructive product feedback.
- [2016-11-22] **Valet mode + convenience features.** Notes valet mode (limits to 70 mph, hides personal
  info, PIN-protected), remote climate pre-conditioning, and the auto-cabin-cooling software update — the
  car-as-updatable-software-platform theme again.
