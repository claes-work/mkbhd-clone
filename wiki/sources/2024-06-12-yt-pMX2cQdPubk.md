## Quarantined — guest (Tim Cook), NOT persona data
_Cook is the interview guest; his statements are context only and are excluded from persona/
voice/beliefs. Attributed to Cook from the interview exchange._
- (Cook) Apple has "been executing with AI for a long time" — machine learning underpins Watch
  features like crash/fall detection and AFib; what changed is that **generative AI** "captured
  people's imagination," which Apple embraced and branded **Apple Intelligence**.
- (Cook) Apple leads with **user benefit**, not the underlying technology; it branded Apple
  Intelligence because "people wanted to know our views on generative AI in particular."
- (Cook) Generative AI "was never off the table" — the aim was to pursue it "in a thoughtful
  kind of way," integrated deeply into apps you already use, with **privacy as a fundamental
  human right**.
- (Cook) **Private Cloud Compute** and the **OpenAI/ChatGPT** arrangement are different things:
  PCC uses the same basic Apple-silicon architecture and software as the iPhone to keep off-device
  compute "as safe and secure and private… as the device"; world-knowledge requests may go to
  ChatGPT, but only with **explicit per-use user consent** — "you decide."
- (Cook) Apple's "model is not one that needs engagement to succeed" — it builds tools to empower
  users (Screen Time, notification counts, Focus modes), and smarter Apple Intelligence could let
  tasks "take less time now."
- (Cook) On legacy/succession: doesn't think about legacy ("a legacy is… defined by other people"),
  stays "focused on the forward, not the rear-view mirror" — "You won't find a museum here." At
  some point there will be another CEO and his focus will be "on making them successful."
- (Cook) On Vision Pro: uses it for entertainment and multitasking; "half of the Fortune 100 is
  now buying Vision Pro for some project"; making old photos 3D in visionOS 2 is "a big, big
  thing" — poignant after losing both parents before it launched.
