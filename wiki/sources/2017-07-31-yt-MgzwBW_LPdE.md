# Tesla Model 3 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2017-07-31)

## Summary
A landmark EV node: **Tesla invited him (as a Tesla owner/YouTuber) to drive the Model 3 and meet Elon Musk
and Franz von Holzhausen** — fulfilling his 2016 "Model 3 = the EV for the masses" prediction. Rich with the
Tesla master-plan context, the **minimalist-dream-car / simplicity-serves-manufacturing** read, and a sharp
button-vs-touchscreen critique.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-07-31] **THE PREDICTION VINDICATED — "we finally have a great affordable electric car."** Frames it
  with Musk's 2006 master plan (sports car → affordable car → even-more-affordable car). Direct Elon quote
  from their meeting: **"We've had great electric cars, we've had affordable electric cars, but they never
  really overlapped. This is that."** The literal payoff of his 2016 Model-3-as-EV-for-the-masses forecast
  ([[2016-02-07-yt-itInMEcvjYc]]). "Really exciting for Tesla and for the industry as a whole."
- [2017-07-31] **The trust-based pre-orders (an industry observation).** "Hundreds of thousands put down
  deposits without ever sitting in or test-driving one — most people test-drive before buying. That shows how
  much trust people have in the company." A read on brand trust as a market force.
- [2017-07-31] **The core read: "a minimalist dream car" — simplicity serves manufacturing.** "Everything —
  interior, exterior, and most importantly the *manufacturing process* — is as simple as possible. Tesla
  isn't offering many configs, because minimizing versions (as close to one SKU as possible) is the only way
  to hit the production volume they need." Connects the design minimalism to the production challenge.
- [2017-07-31] **The button-vs-touchscreen "pushing the barrier" critique.** "100% of the controls are on the
  15" touchscreen or two steering-wheel dials — even opening the glove box is two taps, and the AC vent
  direction is set on-screen. There's a line between making something futuristic and where it'd just be better
  with a button or knob, and I think this really pushes that barrier." A design-taste boundary (physical
  controls still matter — cf. "good buttons"). The rationale: "it's *made for* autonomy — most of your time
  in the driver's seat won't be driving, so there's no screen behind the wheel."
- [2017-07-31] **"It drives like a Tesla" + the value verdict.** "Coming from a Tesla owner, the Model 3
  feels/accelerates like a Tesla, at home — not as fast as the Model S, but very responsive, quiet on smaller
  wheels. $44k for 310 miles is the **best cost-to-mile ratio in an EV today**, with all the clout Tesla
  already has." Names what got cut vs the Model S (no falcon greeting handles, coil not air suspension, no
  second screen, phone-as-key). "The biggest challenge now is just manufacturing enough."
- [2017-07-31] **Meta: rising access — "they invited a YouTuber, which is kind of funny."** "No photos/videos
  allowed, so they invited a YouTuber to do this — kind of funny." Notes his growing industry standing (an
  early-access daytime drive + an Elon/Franz Q&A).
