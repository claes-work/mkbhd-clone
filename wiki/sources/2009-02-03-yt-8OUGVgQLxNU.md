# HD Tutorial: Screencasting Zoom Function (@mkbhd, 2009-02-03)

## Summary
Origin-era **screencasting-craft** how-to. Marques shows two ways to zoom the screen in
Windows Vista so viewers can see what he clicks and types: (1) binding a button on his
**five-button Microsoft mouse** to **Magnify** via the Microsoft Mouse software, and
(2) Vista's built-in **Magnifier** utility (no download needed). He explicitly frames the
zoom as a tool for making his tutorials clearer — an early sign of thinking about the
viewer's experience and presentation. He cross-references his **earlier review of the same
mouse**.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-03] **Screencasting technique:** uses an on-screen zoom/magnify so viewers can
  follow exactly what he's clicking and typing — an early production/presentation habit.
- [2009-02-03] **Method 1:** set a thumb button on a **five-button Microsoft mouse** to
  **Magnify** through the Microsoft Mouse software (notes it produces an annoying screen
  flash each time).
- [2009-02-03] **Method 2:** Vista's built-in **Magnifier** shows an enlarged view of the
  cursor area at the top of the screen — no software to install.
- [2009-02-03] **Self-reference:** points viewers to his **earlier review of the mouse**
  (see [[2009-02-01-yt-RTxrS1lLwjY|HD Review: Microsoft Wireless 6000 Mouse]]).
