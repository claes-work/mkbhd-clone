# HD Tutorial: Shrink + Convert Large Files (@mkbhd, 2009-05-18)

## Summary
An origin-era freeware tutorial that is really a document of the **pre-Partner upload
constraints** he was engineering around. Non-partner accounts were capped at 10 minutes
and 1 GB per upload; his 1280×720 unboxing exports were hitting **1.37 GB**. His fix:
convert the finished HD file to **FLV** with DVDVideoSoft's Free Video to Flash Converter,
keeping 1280×720 at 30 fps and the highest audio bitrate — which took a 0.98 GB AVI down
to **68.7 MB**, "and it's still HD quality." He also notes FLV uploads process faster
because YouTube transcodes everything to FLV anyway. ★ L3-candidate: this is a concrete,
dated addition to the origin encoding-standards thread and to the economics of being a
non-partner creator.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-18] **The pre-Partner ceiling, stated exactly**: "if you're not a YouTube
  partner, you have a maximum video upload length to YouTube of 10 minutes and a maximum
  file size of one gig."
- [2009-05-18] **The problem was real, not hypothetical**: a recent unboxing "went up to
  1.37 gigs when I produced it in 1280 x 720 HD."
- [2009-05-18] **Tool**: DVDVideoSoft's free studio suite; specifically Free Video to Flash
  Converter 4.1.3 (10.6 MB download), linked in the description. He downloaded only two of
  the ~20 utilities in the suite (that one plus YouTube-to-MP3).
- [2009-05-18] **Settings he specifies**: FLV output, "ultra high" LAN/Wi-Fi bandwidth
  preset, dimensions forced back to 1280×720, frame rate to 30 fps, stereo audio at "pretty
  much the highest bitrate you need" — i.e. compress the container, never the resolution.
- [2009-05-18] **Bandwidth-preset guidance scales with source size**: "if your videos are
  really really high, like 30, 40 gigs, you can go from high to very high bandwidth."
- [2009-05-18] **Result**: 0.98 GB source → **68.7 MB** FLV, "still HD quality." Upload
  becomes near-instant: "will be uploaded to YouTube in a matter of seconds and it's all
  about a matter of putting in the description fast enough."
- [2009-05-18] **Why FLV specifically**: "YouTube processes videos to turn them all into
  FLV format. So if you're uploading an FLV video, it won't take quite as long to process."
- [2009-05-18] **Housekeeping detail**: the converter emits several files; keep only the
  `.flv` and delete the rest.
- [2009-05-18] **Working method**: he converts a file (the Matias keyboard unboxing) that
  "actually I have not uploaded yet" and sets the process to high priority to speed it up —
  showing the real backlog-and-batch workflow rather than a staged demo.
- [2009-05-18] **Request pipeline restated**: "if you do have any other tutorial ideas or
  requests, go ahead and subscribe and message me through YouTube. I read all my emails."
