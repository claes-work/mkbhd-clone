# iPhone 7 with no Headphone Jack?! (@mkbhd, 2016-01-23)

## Summary
A commentary on the iPhone-7-removes-the-headphone-jack rumor — a **teach-the-mechanism, understand-Apple's-
logic-even-if-it-seems-insane** piece (months before the jack was actually removed). His frame: the 3.5 mm
jack is **"the grandfather of legacy I/O"** (everything else went digital — HDMI/Thunderbolt/USB-C), and
it takes up a lot of internal space, so Apple's logical move is to drop it and route audio through
**Lightning** (with digital-port advantages). Crucially: **"it's not about thinness"** (the iPod Touch is
thinner and keeps the jack) — it's about **fitting more stuff**, and Apple has a history of sacrificing
legacy for progress (the 30-pin → Lightning transition).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-01-23] **The jack is the last legacy I/O.** "The 3.5 mm audio jack is one of the oldest I/O ports
  still around — you don't see Ethernet, FireWire, or VGA much anymore; they've all been replaced by digital
  ports (HDMI, Thunderbolt, USB-C). But everything still has the audio jack — the grandfather of legacy I/O."
- [2016-01-23] **It's not about thinness — it's about fitting more stuff.** "The iPod Touch is already ~10%
  thinner than the iPhone and still has a jack, so it's not about thinness — there's just so much stuff
  packed into an iPhone." The jack "takes up a lot" of internal space; "a couple of millimeters can be
  important."
- [2016-01-23] **The logical solution + the 30-pin precedent.** "Apple's solution seems to point to: get
  rid of the jack, and plug headphones in through Lightning. It seems insane because it kind of is — but you
  can understand it from Apple's perspective." Precedent: **the 30-pin → Lightning switch** "made sense for
  Apple but, in typical Apple fashion, screwed over everyone using the previous accessories — that was the
  sacrifice Apple was willing to make to fit more stuff."
- [2016-01-23] **The digital-port upside.** Lightning is digital, so Apple could add features — music app
  auto-opening on plug-in, headphones setting an EQ, or **noise-cancelling headphones tapping the iPhone's
  power via Lightning (like the Apple Pencil does on the iPad Pro)** instead of their own battery.
