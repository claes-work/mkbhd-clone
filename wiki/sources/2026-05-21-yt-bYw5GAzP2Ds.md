# The Closest thing to a Tesla 'Model 2' - And it's Great (@AutoFocus, 2026-05-21)

## Summary
Marques reviews the **2026 Chevy Bolt** as the real-world answer to the long-rumored
"affordable Tesla Model 2": a sub-$30k EV that "genuinely doesn't suck." The video's thesis
is a benchmark question — can you make a brand-new EV under $30k in 2026 that's actually
good? — and the answer is yes. He praises how little Chevy skimped (NACS port, one-pedal
driving, real HVAC buttons, ample storage, comfortable ride), with two main gripes: **no
Apple CarPlay/Android Auto** (a GM brand decision) and no play/pause on the wheel.
Marques-solo. Contains a **sponsor read (Chemical Guys)**.

## Key claims
- 2026-05-21: Chevy Bolt as tested — single-motor **front-wheel drive**, 65 kWh battery,
  262-mile rating, ~210 hp, 0–60 in 6.8s, ~3,800 lb, **under $30,000 as tested**. (Marques)
- Thesis/benchmark: "Is it possible in 2026 to make an electric car for under 30 grand brand
  new that doesn't suck? And the answer is yes." Cars like this are "a benchmark to how far
  we've come with electric cars." (Marques)
- Praises Chevy for **not skimping**: NACS port (Supercharge without an adapter), real door
  handles, rear wiper, hydraulic-assisted trunk + sub-trunk, real physical HVAC buttons,
  abundant storage, one-pedal driving, and a genuinely usable rear seat behind his 6'3
  driving position. (Marques)
- Main criticism: **GM's decision to drop Apple CarPlay/Android Auto** across Chevy/Cadillac
  ("that might be a deal breaker for some people") — the car runs Google built-in with the
  Play Store instead. Also wishes a wheel button did play/pause. (Marques)
- Range was "real": 250–270 miles honest; a 20–30 mile commute showed 20–30 miles used.
  Sport mode changed "not a single other thing" he could notice. (Marques)
- Verdict: "I'm proud of the Bolt." Wonders aloud whether they could push a good EV down to
  ~$23k. (Marques)
- **Sponsor**: Chemical Guys "Raging Rhino All-in-One Exterior Detailer" (waterless
  clean/shine/protect, $49.99). Disclosed as a paid sponsor. (Marques — ad read)
