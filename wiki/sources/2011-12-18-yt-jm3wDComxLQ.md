# Galaxy Nexus Camera Review (@mkbhd, 2011-12-18)

## Summary
A dedicated camera review made because it was **the most frequently asked question** he'd
received since buying the phone — and because his own first-impressions verdict had been
mixed. Before any test shots, he states the principle the whole review rests on: **megapixel
count is not the thing that matters; optics are.** He then works through the camera software,
video at 720p/1080p indoors and outdoors, the front-facing camera, and stills, landing on a
verdict that separates hardware from software: he's "a big fan of the camera software rather
than the camera hardware."

## Key claims
- [2011-12-18] **The megapixels-don't-matter principle, stated up front**: "there are 3
  megapixel sensors, there are 5 megapixel sensors, there are 8, 10 and 12 megapixel sensors
  all in smartphones — but it's really all about the quality of the optics, which is basically
  the sensor and the glass." The earliest clear articulation in the corpus of a position he is
  still known for.
- [2011-12-18] **Verdict split cleanly along hardware/software**: "I'm really a big fan of the
  camera software rather than the camera hardware." Instant shutter, tap-to-focus and
  lock-screen access are praised; the 5 MP sensor's output is "pretty average… nothing to write
  home about."
- [2011-12-18] **Scoped numeric verdict for video**: "on the smartphone scale this guy takes
  around an eight for me. I definitely would use this to take video and send it to people" —
  the score is explicitly bounded to the smartphone class, exactly as the 09-30 methodology
  requires.
- [2011-12-18] **Effort disclosed as part of the evidence**: "I walked around in 25 degree
  weather — that's Fahrenheit — to get these shots for you." The test conditions are stated,
  not just the results.
- [2011-12-18] **Honest failure mode named**: stills are "literally hit or miss — if you press
  the shutter button when the item is out of focus, you will get that out-of-focus picture, so
  I always tap to focus just to make sure." The instant-shutter benefit and its cost described
  as two sides of one design choice.
- [2011-12-18] **Convenience weighed against quality explicitly**: "the convenience factor of
  having instant access from the lock screen and instant shutter really does make up for it, in
  my opinion." The tradeoff is named and the judgement flagged as opinion.
- [2011-12-18] Technical observations: single LED flash preferred over dual for video
  illumination ("dual LED was actually a little bit too bright"); 720p chosen for test footage
  to match his upload standard; front camera is 720p with face tracking and rated good enough
  for Google Chat and Skype; low-light autofocus is slower "like any other camera"; zooming
  while recording is called out as something "you just don't want to do."
- [2011-12-18] **Vocabulary marker**: "a 5 megapixel rear facing shooter — I like saying shooter
  instead of camera."
