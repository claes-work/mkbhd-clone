# Top 5 Tesla Model S Features! (@mkbhd, 2014-05-03)

## Summary
A ★★ **long-arc landmark — his first dedicated car video** (Tesla loaned him a Model S for
the day). This is the origin of the car-review beat that becomes AutoFocus: he reviews the
car exactly the way he reviews phones (a tech-feature walkthrough ending in the drive),
frames the 17" touchscreen and instant torque as "feeling like the future," and states he's
**never owned a car**. He signals "more car stuff" is coming.

## Key claims
- [2014-05-03] **"My first car video" — the AutoFocus origin**: "obviously I'm not a car reviewer,
  I've never really shown that I'm interested in cars any more than the super-sweet ones anyone
  likes — but I do appreciate a good car, and when Tesla offered me the Model S for the day, I
  couldn't say no. It's my first car video." The documented start of his car-review career.
- [2014-05-03] **Never owned a car — a biography datum**: "spoiler: I've never actually owned a
  car. I've always lived close enough to the school I go to my whole life that I've never needed
  one." A dated personal fact (no car; lives near campus).
- [2014-05-03] **Reviews a car like a phone — the AutoFocus method origin**: structures it as a
  Top-5 tech-feature walkthrough (sound system → design → the key → the electric motor → the 17"
  touchscreen) ending in the drive — the "fixed walkthrough ending in the drive" format he later
  formalizes on AutoFocus.
- [2014-05-03] **The instant-torque drive — "feels like a roller-coaster launch"**: "I got to
  drive the 85 kWh Performance Plus — 0–60 in 4.2s, all the torque available immediately as soon as
  you touch the 'go pedal.' The best way to describe the instant torque is it feels like a roller-
  coaster launch when the tires stick." His earliest EV-driving impressions.
- [2014-05-03] **The 17" touchscreen center console = "the future"**: "#1 is the 17" touchscreen
  that replaces all the buttons and dials, running a custom Tesla OS that gets free OTA updates
  over 3G — it added memory seats in an update. It's an internet-connected car with a giant tablet
  in the center. It's just one of those things like front-facing speakers on a phone where it feels
  like the future, and I kind of wish everyone else would adopt it." His tech-first read of the
  car (+ the OTA-updates-as-value connection).
- [2014-05-03] **Design-with-a-purpose read**: "a striking car — some Jaguar, Aston Martin,
  Maserati in it — but it's pretty for a reason: the 0.24 drag coefficient [lowest of any car],
  flat bottom, batteries on the floor, all to maximize range." Reads the engineering behind the
  aesthetics.
- [2014-05-03] **Names the con + foreshadows more car content**: "the biggest con for me is the
  lack of an audio jack… I also have more stuff coming up with Tesla and hopefully other car stuff
  too." His verdict-honesty + the explicit AutoFocus foreshadowing.
