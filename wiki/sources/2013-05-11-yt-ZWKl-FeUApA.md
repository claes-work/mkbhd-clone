# Motorola X-Phone Rumor Rant! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-11)

## Summary
A media-criticism rant about **rumor culture and consumer entitlement** toward tech
companies, hung on the endlessly-rumored "Motorola X phone." Persona value is in a clearly-
stated belief: **companies don't owe you the phone of your imagination** — enjoy the great
products that already exist instead of resenting the ones that don't. A good early instance
of his push-back against hype-cycle journalism.

## Key claims
- [2013-05-11] **The core critique — the X phone is a projection, not a product**: "the
  Motorola X phone is a figment of our imaginations, a manifestation of what we want to see in
  a smartphone… it can't be everything. When a Google-Motorola collaboration finally happens,
  it won't have all the rumored features, and some people are inevitably going to be
  disappointed." Diagnoses the hype-cycle's built-in letdown.
- [2013-05-11] **Anti-entitlement — companies don't owe you**: quotes Eric Ravenscraft
  (Android Police) approvingly — "I love imagining what's coming and even predicting and being
  wrong… when it turns dark is when we suddenly start acting like Google owes us something" —
  and adds "yes, that is exactly what I get pissed about sometimes." His stated frustration
  with entitlement toward companies.
- [2013-05-11] **Enjoy what exists — the prescription**: "let's relax and let the evolution of
  smartphones take place… enjoy the flagships that already do exist. There are some awesome
  phones we should be picking between now, instead of worrying about the X phone." Redirects
  from speculation to the real products (cf. his "which of these should I get?" buyer's guides).
- [2013-05-11] **The buyer's-frustration he lives**: "I get questions every single day — I
  have the S3, should I get the S4, the HTC One, or wait for the X phone? That's not a thing."
  Content driven by a real, recurring reader question.
- [2013-05-11] **Cites a concrete cautionary example**: "Project Roadrunner — a completely
  made-up 4.2 battery-life leak someone published as a thing that would happen; it never did,
  and people got mad at Google for not doing it." Grounds the critique in a real fabricated-
  rumor case.
