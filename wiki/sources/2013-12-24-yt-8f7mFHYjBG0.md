# Nexus 5: Revisited! (What's on my Phone) (@mkbhd, 2013-12-24)

## Summary
A two-months-later revisit of his Nexus 5 daily driver, doubling as a "what's on my phone."
Persona value is the **rolling-release payoff** (software updates fixed the two biggest
flaws — camera speed and battery), a personal home-screen tour, and confirmation that the
Nexus 5 is **his benchmark** — the phone every other device gets compared to.

## Key claims
- [2013-12-24] **The rolling-release payoff — updates fixed the flaws**: "two software updates
  brought it to 4.4.2, and the biggest change is the camera — shooting speed shrank the between-
  shot time to almost zero, HDR+ is now as fast as regular shots used to be. It's still not the
  best camera, but at least it's not a pain to shoot with anymore." The Play-Store-updates-fix-it
  thesis, realized on his own phone (and the battery "consistently got better and better… like I
  had to break it in").
- [2013-12-24] **The Nexus 5 is his benchmark**: "this is the phone I keep coming back to after
  testing others… a lot of the other phones I get are just compared to the Nexus 5 — it's the
  benchmark." Confirms his reference device.
- [2013-12-24] **His personal home-screen setup (dated)**: "a pretty simple setup — three home
  screens plus the Google Now page, two of them completely widgets. DashClock is my default [with
  plugins for glanceable info], the Today Agenda widget because my life kind of relies on Google
  Tasks and Google Calendar, and a grid of my daily-used apps I open on every phone I test." A
  concrete tooling snapshot (Google-services-centric).
- [2013-12-24] **Glad he chose the Stormtrooper white**: "I'm so glad I made the decision of the
  Stormtrooper white/Panda over the matte black — my biggest concern was whether it'd get dirty,
  and it hasn't; it's the same bright white as day one, and the two-tone makes it look a bit
  thinner." His aesthetic satisfaction + care-for-my-gear note.
- [2013-12-24] **Turned on ART runtime + carries the RX100**: "yes, I've turned on the ART Android
  runtime and I'm loving it," and "I carry it often with the Sony RX100 in the other pocket when I
  want to take actually-good photos." His tinkerer + production-gear habits.
- [2013-12-24] **Still recommends the Moto X to switchers**: "even though I carry the Nexus 5, I
  still recommend the Moto X to a lot of Android switchers because it fits people's hands better."
  Separates his own carry from the general recommendation.
