# Tour of the New Twitter (@mkbhd, 2011-12-08)

## Summary
A walkthrough of Twitter's cross-platform redesign — desktop (via Twitter's own launch
video), then the Android app demonstrated **on his aging OG Motorola Droid**, with a note
that the iPhone equivalents look near-identical. The Discover tab is called the biggest
change. Method note worth keeping: he deliberately demos on old hardware "just so you can be
pretty sure that this app will work on just about any other phone in existence" — testing
for the audience's device rather than his own.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-08] **Discover named as the substantive change**: it "categorizes the people you
  tweet and lets you discover things based on hashtags," with much more image and video
  content — "a more visually pleasing experience."
- [2011-12-08] **Deliberate old-hardware demo**: the Android app is shown on the two-year-old
  OG Droid specifically to establish a compatibility floor for viewers, not because it's his
  preferred device.
- [2011-12-08] **Gap identified**: no Honeycomb/tablet-optimized Android version shipped —
  "they forgot to optimize a version for Honeycomb," and the phone app looks "clunky" stretched
  on a tablet.
- [2011-12-08] **He does not switch, and says so**: despite complimenting the redesign, "I'm
  not going to switch to it yet, I still use another client called Tweakdeck, which is pretty
  much the most badass thing out there — it's really fast and really clean." Praise and
  personal adoption are kept separate.
- [2011-12-08] **Creator's-eye observation**: he singles out the in-timeline "view video"
  button and hopes it persists for YouTube links "so when I tweet out my new videos you guys
  will be able to watch it right on Twitter" — while immediately noting he'd still want the
  comment on YouTube. A distribution-vs-engagement tradeoff noticed in passing.
- [2011-12-08] Platform-cadence callback: "this is the first major update Twitter's rolled out
  in a long long long long time," consistent with his 11-10 observation about Twitter's slow
  product velocity.
- [2011-12-08] Anticipation marker: "I'm really excited to use this on my future Galaxy Nexus"
  — a week before he buys it.
