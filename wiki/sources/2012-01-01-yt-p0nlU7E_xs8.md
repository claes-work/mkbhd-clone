# InSync: Dropbox for Google Docs! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-01)

## Summary
A New Year's Day software review of InSync, a desktop client that syncs a local folder to
Google Docs the way Dropbox syncs to its own cloud. He opens by reacting to the product's
marketing — its tagline was "it's okay to break up with Dropbox," which he pushes back on
("whoa whoa whoa, who do these guys think they are? I love Dropbox") — then walks the web
and desktop interfaces and lands the verdict **on price per gigabyte**, not on features.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-01] **Verdict is a storage-economics call, stated as the short answer**: "why would
  you choose this over Dropbox? Both are on Mac and Windows — what's the difference? The short
  answer is storage. Both Google and Dropbox offer the first gigabyte free, but after that
  Google is way cheaper… I'm talking like 20 gigs for five bucks a year. It's a no-brainer."
- [2012-01-01] **Defends the incumbent before assessing the challenger**: his instinctive
  reaction to the anti-Dropbox marketing is to defend a tool he likes, and he explains Dropbox
  from scratch for viewers who don't know it — assessment separated from the pitch.
- [2012-01-01] Mechanics described: an InSync folder on the desktop behaves like any file
  manager; changes sync to Google Docs "really really ridiculously fast"; the web view mirrors
  Dropbox's structure with per-file sharing links, version history and viewer counts; `.txt`
  files can't be previewed and must be downloaded.
- [2012-01-01] **Google-design affinity restated** (third dated instance in three months):
  "when you go to Google Docs on your web browser you have the beautiful Google design and the
  neat organization."
- [2012-01-01] Personal-stack continuity: this tool joins the Dropbox / Google Docs / Evernote
  set he confirms using a week later in [[2012-01-08-yt-QCRQ7teMxsI]].
