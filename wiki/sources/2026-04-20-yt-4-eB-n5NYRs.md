# Subaru Doesn't Want to Make EVs (@AutoFocus, 2026-04-20)

## Summary
A review of the **Subaru Uncharted** (GT trim), framed as a story about "wasted
potential." Marques's thesis: it's a fine car hamstrung by the company behind it —
essentially a **rebadged Toyota bZ** (as the earlier Solterra was a rebadged bZ4X), and
Toyota "doesn't want to make EVs; they love hybrids." He likes the styling but argues the
value proposition is weak: mediocre range, slow-ish charging, and no standout advantage
over a cheaper Chevy Bolt. Marques-solo; all subject-attributed.

## Key claims
- 2026-04-20: The Subaru Uncharted is a dual-motor AWD electric crossover, 75 kWh battery,
  338 hp, ~$45k as tested (GT), starting closer to **$35k**. (Marques)
- **Rebadge critique**: it's "essentially the same car again as Toyota's next-gen EV, the
  bZ," just as the Subaru Solterra was the Toyota bZ4X "minus the badges." The only visible
  Subaru tell is the color and a tiny "Subaru" etched in the headlight. (Marques)
- Recurring industry take: **"Toyota doesn't like making EVs. They love hybrids"** — they
  pour effort into "incredible" hybrids (Corolla, Camry, SUVs) and make EVs as if they "feel
  like they have to." (Marques — tech-industry-commentary)
- Value verdict: for ~$35k you could get a **Chevy Bolt** that's "probably better," or step
  up to a Model Y; hard to recommend the Subaru/Toyota version. "Maybe if it was 5K cheaper."
  (Marques)
- Real-world range roughly **250 miles** on a full charge (less at 80–90% daily charging);
  not the fastest charger, so "you can scratch out road trips." (Marques)
- Praise where due: he likes the looks (dark grey wheels, sporty fastback), the interior
  isolation and ride quality over New Jersey potholes ("almost like a pleasant surprise"),
  and a physical temperature dial. Drive modes barely differ and are only indicated in a
  tiny gauge-cluster readout. (Marques)
