# BMW Tech at CES 2015! (@mkbhd, 2015-01-11)

## Summary
An early **car-tech** video from CES 2015 — part of the through-line from the 2014 Tesla Model S video to
the eventual Auto Focus channel. His framing: "**who would have thought the coolest booth at CES 2015
would be a car company?**" He walks BMW's booth — the i8 hybrid, **inductive wireless charging** for the
car, OLED taillights + laser headlights on the M4, and, most strikingly, an **i3 that summons itself and
drives autonomously from a smartwatch**. A dated node in his growing car-beat interest (he teases a
future i8 video).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-01-11] **Cars as the CES surprise.** "When you think of high-tech you rarely think of the ancient
  100-year-old car industry… but BMW this year had a legitimately awesome booth." Follows his 2014 Tesla
  coverage.
- [2015-01-11] **BMW i8 (hybrid) — "totally dope looking car,"** with more coverage teased.
- [2015-01-11] **Wireless car charging.** "Leave it to BMW to introduce inductive wireless charging in
  their car" — drive onto an inductive mat in your garage and charge "at almost the same rate as if
  physically plugged in." (He connects it to phone wireless charging he's covered.)
- [2015-01-11] **M4 concept lighting:** **OLED taillights** ("basically using your taillights as a
  display… any color, any pattern, any frame rate" — but he questions daylight brightness) and **laser
  headlights** with intelligent beam-shaping (light 650 yd, sensors carve out oncoming traffic to avoid
  blinding them) — "basically the way headlights should be" (though not yet US-legal).
- [2015-01-11] **The i3 stole the show — self-driving demos.** A crash-avoidance demo (the car stops
  itself before an obstacle even if you floor it) and **summoning the car from a smartwatch** — "pretty
  surreal sitting in the backseat of a car that was driving itself with no one else in it." Plus gesture
  controls, a backseat climate tablet, and an i8 key fob showing charge status.
