# Top 5 Smartphones of 2013! (@mkbhd, 2013-12-21)

## Summary
His year-end best-phones list — an early **Smartphone Awards precursor**. Notable for the
deliberately **unordered** format ("I didn't feel right picking a number one"), for judging
each phone by **what it *changed* about 2013**, and for a daily-driver reveal (the
Stormtrooper white Nexus 5). Also a clean statement of his platform-agnostic breadth of
taste.

## Key claims
- [2013-12-21] **Unordered list, judged by impact — the awards-format sensibility**: "this is an
  unordered list — I didn't feel right picking a number one. I'm taking a retrospective look at
  five phones that really changed 2013." An early instance of the annual-awards / judge-by-what-
  it-changed approach that becomes the Smartphone Awards.
- [2013-12-21] **The picks, each for what it changed**: Moto X (form factor + quick updates,
  great first Android phone), Lumia 1020 ("changed the way we look at cameras in smartphones for
  good" — 41 MP), HTC One (build quality + BoomSound speakers — "you never looked at the S4 the
  same after"), Galaxy Note 3 ("the first real phone with pretty much no compromises across the
  board"), Nexus 5 (price-to-performance). Each pick is a dated impact judgment.
- [2013-12-21] **Daily-driver reveal**: "a lot of you ask what my daily driver is — this is the
  closest thing: I've carried the Stormtrooper 32-gig white Nexus 5 for the past month or so."
  Confirms his current carry (the Stormtrooper Nexus 5).
- [2013-12-21] **Breadth-of-taste — platform-agnostic**: honorable mentions span the Galaxy S4
  (best-seller), the Lumia 1520 (best Windows Phone), the BlackBerry Z30 ("their best"), and the
  Moto G (price-to-performance). He values the diversity across platforms.
- [2013-12-21] **Cross-promotes a peer's differing list**: "check out TLDToday's best-five phones
  going up today — we're both tech people but have different tastes, so his list is different from
  mine." His credit-the-peer / value-different-perspectives habit.
