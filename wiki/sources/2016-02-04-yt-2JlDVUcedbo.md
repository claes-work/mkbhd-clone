# Are Tablets Dead? (@mkbhd, 2016-02-04)

## Summary
A commentary essay on the tablet market — his canonical **"tablets aren't dead, they're a niche"** thesis.
Tablets filled the phone-laptop void and spiked with the iPad, but **sales are declining** (24M → 16M
units, Q4'14 → Q4'15, −20%), squeezed by convergence (phones getting bigger, laptops getting thinner). His
diagnosis: (1) people **don't upgrade tablets** as often as phones, and (2) a genuine lack of exciting new
features. Conclusion: **"they're not dying, they're just more focused — a niche… tablets aren't for
everyone, and that's exactly what they need."**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-02-04] **Convergence squeezes tablets.** "In 2010 the industry standard big phone had a 4.3"
  display — today that's compact." Phones do more and get bigger; laptops get "nearly as thin and light as
  tablets" (plus convertibles) — "that's pushed tablets into a specialty category."
- [2016-02-04] **On paper, a dying breed — the sales data.** "By volume, tablet purchases have started
  *declining*, not leveling off: **24 million shipped in Q4 2014, 16 million in Q4 2015 — a −20% drop.**"
- [2016-02-04] **Two reasons people buy fewer.** (1) **Upgrade cycle** — "phones get upgraded every 12–18
  months; you're not upgrading your tablet every one or two years — casual gaming/browsing doesn't need a
  new model, so you buy one and let it sit." (2) **A genuine lack of exciting new features** in the last
  couple of years (see the Tech-of-the-Year Tablets video — they push against laptops but stay in the
  middle).
- [2016-02-04] **The verdict: a niche, not dead.** "Tablets aren't dying, they're just more focused —
  that's exactly what they need. They're not for everyone; they're a niche — for the parent buying for a
  kid, the casual gamer who wants a bigger screen but not a laptop, the graphic designer who wants a good
  stylus. These pockets of dedicated users aren't dying; they're discovering themselves."
