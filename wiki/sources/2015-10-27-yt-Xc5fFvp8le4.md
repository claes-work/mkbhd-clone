# Nexus 6P Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-10-27)

## Summary
The full Nexus 6P review — "P apparently stands for premium, which I think is fitting." After nearly a
month, it's **"quickly become one of my favorite smartphones,"** and — notably — **"it has lived up to a
lot of the hype, which is hard to do for a smartphone nowadays."** A premium all-metal build with flat
grip-friendly sides, an excellent rear **Nexus Imprint** fingerprint reader, a defensible camera hump, and
top-notch stock-Android hardware. He's source-critical about a slightly-misaligned panel on his review
unit (his retail units were fine).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-10-27] **Lived up to the hype (rare praise).** "One of the most anticipated smartphones of the
  year… after using it for nearly a month, it has lived up to a lot of the hype, which is hard to do for a
  smartphone nowadays — quickly one of my favorite phones."
- [2015-10-27] **Big but manageable, premium build.** The successor to "the huge Nexus 6," but a slightly
  smaller 5.7" display + a thinner, flatter body with **flat sides that help grip a lot**. All-metal,
  chamfered, nice weight, ridged clicky buttons — "top-notch industrial design and material choices, always
  awesome to see in a Nexus." His pick is the stealth graphite (though the biggest fingerprint magnet; the
  white keeps the traditional Nexus look — "or rock a skin").
- [2015-10-27] **The camera hump is fine.** It spans the full width (like the old Droid X) so "it still
  sits flat enough on a table to type without rocking" and "helps tell which side is up when grabbing it."
- [2015-10-27] **Nexus Imprint (rear fingerprint) is really good** — quick touch-to-unlock, unsurprising
  given Huawei's other readers.
- [2015-10-27] **Source-critical about the review unit.** "The only weird part was the bottom back panel
  felt slightly more raised on one side on my review unit — but my retail units were just fine." (Reports
  the flaw, distinguishes review-vs-retail.)
