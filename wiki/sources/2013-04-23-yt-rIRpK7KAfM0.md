# Google Babel: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-04-23)

## Summary
A preview/rumor analysis of "Google Babel" (the messaging consolidation that shipped as
Hangouts). Its most persona-relevant thread is a **personal-pain-point-as-content** opener
— his own experience of Google's fragmented, non-syncing chat apps — plus a clean
availability-based comparison to iMessage. Dated, but a good instance of his critique of
Google's product sprawl.

## Key claims
- [2013-04-23] **Personal pain point → the video's thesis**: walks through his own experience —
  a Google Talk message answered on his phone still shows unread on his tablet and again in a
  Gmail tab — "that is just one of the problems Google now has… the real problem is Google has
  multiple chat services that don't talk to each other, notifications don't sync, and every
  time you have to pick how to communicate." Content driven by a real frustration he lives.
- [2013-04-23] **Critique of Google's fragmentation**: "Google Talk, Google Voice, Google+
  Messenger, Gmail chat, Hangouts… they all look different and they all don't talk to each
  other. It's a bit of a mess right now." A recurring criticism of Google's overlapping-product
  sprawl (aimed at a company he otherwise favors — even-handed).
- [2013-04-23] **The iMessage comparison, on availability**: "the main difference between Babel
  and iMessage is availability — I can't use iMessage without a Mac or iOS device… the idea of
  Babel is to use it everywhere, on every device, even a web client." Reasons about platform
  reach (the same everywhere-availability value he applied to Chrome OS / cross-platform apps).
- [2013-04-23] **Predicts timing**: "expect it alongside the next version of Android [Key Lime
  Pie] at Google I/O this year." (Hangouts launched at Google I/O 2013, as predicted.)
- [2013-04-23] **Signature ad-inventory quip**: "if you want bonus points, Google, throw
  chat-ads in there — I love chat-ads." A wry recurring joke about Google's ad-monetization
  instinct (voice + his incentive-awareness).
