## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
### Marques (subject — persona-eligible)
- [2017-05-06] **The premise: the G6 is easy to overlook, and that's unfair.** "The S8 is everywhere —
  Samsung has a massive marketing budget AND it's a great phone — which makes it easy to overlook the LG G6,
  which is LG's best phone ever." Champions the overshadowed challenger even while backing the S8.
- [2017-05-06] **His S8 case: best display + best design.** "The S8 has the best display on any smartphone
  (OLED, wider range, brighter, slightly higher-res, taller) — in a class of its own; the edge-glass gives
  an immersion factor. And the industrial design: the S8 looks like a final copy while the G6 looks like a
  rough draft — the seamless glass-to-metal feels like holding a smooth stone." Plus Snapdragon 835 vs 821,
  the jack at the bottom, no carrier branding, and a slightly better screen-to-body ratio.
- [2017-05-06] **The honest framing: phones are personal.** "The S8 is a tremendous phone, but smartphones
  are very personal things, and for some people the G6 might be even better." Refuses to declare a universal
  winner (consistent with the Blind-Test / decide-by-your-own-values ethos).

### Michael Fisher / MrMobile (GUEST — context only, NOT persona data)
- [2017-05-06] (MrMobile) Argues the G6's two big wins are projected durability (MIL-STD-810G, 14 tests
  including salt-fog, with LG disclosing its lab/procedures) and grip; frames the S8 Active workaround as
  likely AT&T-exclusive. *(Attributed to MrMobile; excluded from persona.)*
