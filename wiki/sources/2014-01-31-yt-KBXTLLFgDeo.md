# Google Sells Motorola: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2014-01-31)

## Summary
A rich incentive/business analysis of Lenovo buying Motorola from Google ($2.91B, after
Google's $12.5B purchase in 2012). Persona value is his **three-perspectives** structured
method (Lenovo / Motorola / Google), sharp reads on **brand equity**, the **favored-OEM
tension** (Motorola-as-Google's-favorite pushed Samsung away from Android), and the
**patents-were-the-real-prize** insight — plus a clip from his **Motorola-CEO interview**
(industry access).

## Key claims
- [2014-01-31] **The three-perspectives method**: "there are three players, so three perspectives:
  Lenovo's, Motorola's, and Google's." He works each — Lenovo (a US-smartphone entry via Motorola's
  carrier relationships, designers, and reputation = "brand equity" — "if they're smart, they keep
  the Motorola name up front"); Motorola (a deal is a deal); Google (the interesting one). His
  structured analytical habit.
- [2014-01-31] **Patents were the real prize — the incentive insight**: "Google bought Motorola for
  $12.5B mainly for the patent portfolio [15,000+ patents to protect Android], kept those and the
  ATAP division [Project Ara], and sold the rest to Lenovo for $2.91B — still a steal for Lenovo.
  Google valued those patents at billions: 'try suing us now, Apple.'" Reasons from what Google
  kept vs sold.
- [2014-01-31] **The favored-OEM tension — a sharp read**: "ever since the acquisition, it seemed
  to a lot of vendors that Motorola was the favored OEM, got first dibs — and that made Samsung
  uncomfortable and pushed them to prepare otherwise [you can see it in TouchWiz and the magazine
  UI]. Now that Google has sold Motorola, there's no favorite; the playing field is even, and Google
  asked Samsung to dial back the TouchWiz customizations in return." An incentive-structure read of
  the ecosystem dynamics.
- [2014-01-31] **Industry access — the CEO interview**: plays a clip from "the interview I had with
  the CEO of Motorola about two months ago" (on Google/Motorola operating independently) and notes
  "I've been in contact with Motorola, and I'll continue to stay in touch." His industry-access +
  disclose-what's-private discipline ("I'm not going to disclose anything that's supposed to be
  private").
- [2014-01-31] **Reads the Nest contrast**: "Google also bought Nest for $3B — and Nest already
  seems to integrate with Google way more than Motorola ever did, so they seem to be much more of a
  hardware partner." Reads Google's real hardware strategy.
- [2014-01-31] **Consumer-reassurance on the Moto X/G**: "if Lenovo is smart they won't touch a
  thing — business as usual — and slowly take advantage of Motorola's resources later. This doesn't
  affect the day-to-day Moto X/G stuff right now." Grounds the analysis in what it means for buyers.
