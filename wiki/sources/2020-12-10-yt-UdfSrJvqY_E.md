# AirPods Max Unboxing & Impressions: $550?! (@mkbhd, 2020-12-10)

## Summary
First impressions / "top five things you should know" on Apple's surprise $550 over-ear wireless noise-cancelling AirPods Max (this is the impressions video; the full review followed six days later). Marques unboxes the mint-green pair, notes the premium stainless-steel/aluminum build (cold metal, no creaks), the USB-C-to-Lightning cable with no brick and no audio cable, and walks his five points: (1) materials/build — excellent but heavy at 386g vs 250g Sony XM4; (2) magnetic, replaceable ear cups ($69/set) — smart but "the most Apple thing"; (3) controls — a borrowed Apple Watch Digital Crown plus a noise-control button, which he prefers over finicky touch controls; (4) the Smart Case — which he mocks as looking like a purse/bra with "no redeeming quality," though it's also the only off-switch (it triggers ultra-low-power mode); (5) smart features — dual H1 chips, nine mics, Apple-ecosystem integration. The throughline is the pricing puzzle: he doesn't yet know what to compare them to (on paper the $350 Sony XM4 beats them on every metric), so maybe the right frame is high-end/reference audio — TBD until the review.

## Key claims
- [2020-12-10] AirPods Max are $550 over-ear wireless noise-cancelling headphones, a surprise "one more thing on top of their one more thing" for the 2020 holiday lineup. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] Build: stainless-steel flexible frame, aluminum ear cups (cold to the touch), telescoping arms with ball joints (>90° rotation), no creaks/gaps — "it just doesn't get much better built than this," but no sweat/water resistance. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] They're heavy — 386g vs his 250g Sony XM4 — mitigated by a soft, breathable mesh top band that distributes weight; long-term durability of the band is unproven. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] Ear cups attach magnetically and pop off to be replaced — smart, since people rarely replace worn cushions; Apple sells replacements at $69/set (mix-and-match colors), which he calls "the most Apple thing you've ever heard." (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] Controls borrow the Apple Watch Digital Crown (rotate for volume, press for media, reversible direction in iOS 14.3) plus a dedicated noise-cancellation/transparency button — he prefers a physical control over finicky touch controls. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] The included Smart Case is bad — "no redeeming quality," looks like a purse/bra, covers the durable part while exposing the delicate mesh band, doesn't compact the headphones. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] There is no on/off button — putting them in the Smart Case (magnet detected) triggers ultra-low-power mode; outside the case they drain for ~2 hours before auto low-power. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] Smart features: dual H1 chips (one per ear), nine microphones for ANC/transparency, and Apple-ecosystem perks (audio sharing, Siri reading texts, instant pairing). First impression: sound is very good; ANC is about on par with AirPods Pro (great with white noise). (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] Comparison problem: on paper the Sony XM4 ($350, 250g, 30h battery, folds, real case + audio cable) beats them on nearly every metric — but he suspects buyers won't use that comparison; maybe the right frame is ultra-high-end reference gear (his $2,400 Sennheisers, Drop THX Panda, HD 650). TBD in the review. (Marques)
- [2020-12-10] They sold out already, so "there's an audience for it," regardless of his verdict. (Marques)
