# Moto Z/Force Review! (@mkbhd, 2016-07-29)

## Summary
The full Moto Z / Z Force verdict: "a great idea with great execution, but not a complete package." He
crowns **Moto Mods "the best execution of a smartphone mod I have ever seen"** (redefining it as
"modification," not modular), lands his clearest **"I hate carrier exclusives"** line, notes Motorola's
perennial camera weakness, and delivers a memorable functional-design observation — the first camera bump
"with a purpose."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-07-29] **"I hate carrier exclusives."** "Unfortunately these are both exclusive to Verizon right
  now; the unlocked versions come later. I hate carrier exclusives — I've talked about that in the past —
  but what can you do." The critique from the S7 Active crystallized into a flat statement of principle.
- [2016-07-29] **Moto Mods = "the best execution of a smartphone mod I've ever seen" — and it's
  *modification*, not modular.** "I don't even know if I'd call this modular, because you're not swapping
  anything off the phone — I'd call Moto Mods modification: the quickest, easiest way to add functionality,
  snap it in in two seconds, no reboot, no buttons." Praises the color-swap backs, battery packs, JBL
  speaker, and projector. Directly extends his modular explainer's "barely modular" critique of the G5.
- [2016-07-29] **"The first phone to ever have a camera hump with a purpose."** The thicker Z Force keeps
  the same camera bump because it anchors the Moto Mods in place — "probably the first phone to have a
  camera hump with a *function*." A characteristic "design detail with a reason" observation.
- [2016-07-29] **Build quality is his favorite thing.** "All metal and glass, no chassis flex or creaking,
  tight tolerances — materially this could be one of the best Moto phones ever built." But the worst thing:
  "a pretty constant fingerprint magnet" (→ dbrand skin or Moto color-swap) and awkward top-corner volume
  buttons ("I have big hands and it's still a stretch").
- [2016-07-29] **Jack removal — "premature," not necessary.** "A single USB-C, which is an interesting
  choice but maybe a bit premature — I don't think this is a phone where you *have* to get rid of the
  headphone jack, but they did anyway. I still miss it a lot." (Consistent with the impressions video.)
- [2016-07-29] **Motorola's camera weakness, stated as fact.** "Cameras on smartphones has never really
  been a strong point for Motorola — it's just true. Hit or miss, a little more miss than you'd like in a
  daily phone; falls apart quick at night." Praises the de-duped Google stock apps (the HTC-pioneered
  move) but notes Verizon bloat nullifies it. Battery "really super average" — the battery Mod is the fix.
- [2016-07-29] **The "million-dollar idea" aside (voice).** "Here's a million-dollar idea: a Moto Mod with
  a high-quality DAC and a headphone jack and a little battery. That would be awesome. You're welcome, I'll
  take 10%." A characteristic playful-inventor beat.
