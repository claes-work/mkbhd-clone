# iPhone 7 Headphone Roundup! (@mkbhd, 2016-09-18)

## Summary
A practical roundup of listening options after the iPhone 7 killed the headphone jack — Lightning
headphones vs. wireless. The clone-relevant meat is the industry-analysis framing (**Apple just created
a huge new market for headphone makers**), his flat **anti-dongle** stance, and a memorable closing wish
(**two Lightning / two USB-C ports** to fix charge-and-listen).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-09-18] **The market-analysis framing (follow the incentives).** "Apple dropping the jack is
  actually great news for one group: headphone manufacturers — they just got a huge new market that
  didn't exist before, iPhone 7 buyers. The accessory market is young, so if you're early to the phone,
  you've got to be early to the accessories." His habit of reading a product decision through who profits.
- [2016-09-18] **The trade, stated plainly.** "We can thank Apple for the huge new market to sell
  headphones to; we can thank Apple for the inability to charge and listen to wired music at the same
  time." A balanced two-edged verdict on the jack removal.
- [2016-09-18] **Anti-dongle.** "There's that $9 dongle — I'm personally against dongles; I don't want to
  have to think about an adapter every time I plug my headphones in. Worst case, plug it in and leave it
  on the end of your current pair permanently so you don't lose it." A durable personal stance.
- [2016-09-18] **Wireless-honesty + battery-life is what to look for.** "The thing about wireless is
  you're never quite going to match wired sound quality — what you want is a really long battery life to
  alleviate the charging inconvenience." Praises his Jaybird X2/Freedom (~8 hr, "sound phenomenal, won't
  fall out"), the Bose QC35 (~20 hr, excellent NC), and notes the Beats Solo3's 40-hr battery ("but
  they're still priced like Beats and still sound like Beats").
- [2016-09-18] **Measured take on AirPods.** The $159 AirPods "get a lot of flack for not being worth it,
  but there's actually a lot going on — the W1 chip for super-simple pairing, auto-sync across your Apple
  devices, mics for Siri, touch sensitivity." Reserves judgment pending a review — refuses the easy dunk.
- [2016-09-18] **The closing wish — two ports.** "Here's what I'd like to see in the next iPhone: two
  Lightning ports. And if someone wants to get in early, make an Android phone with two USB-C ports — then
  I wouldn't care about the lack of a headphone jack as much." (Echoes the dual-USB-C MacBook wish; the
  charge-and-listen problem is the real objection, not the jack itself.)
