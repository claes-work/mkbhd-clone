# Top 5 Upcoming Tech: 2016! (@mkbhd, 2016-02-07)

## Summary
A subjective list of what he's most excited about in 2016. Two takeaways matter most: **4K OLED TVs**
("plenty of crispy 4K, none OLED; plenty of beautiful OLED, none 4K — this year we get both; the LG 4K OLED
is easily the best-looking TV I've ever seen"), and the **Tesla Model 3** — "**the endgame**" for Tesla's
mission to bring EVs to the masses affordably (a $35k car, enabled by the gigafactory). Also the S7,
iPhone 7 (new design, no jack, maybe rethink the home button), and the next Nexus.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-02-07] **#4 — 4K OLED = the best TV he's seen.** "My last few years of TVs: plenty of crispy 4K,
  none OLED; plenty of beautiful OLED, none 4K. This year we start to see 4K OLED — I got to look at the LG
  4K OLED, and it's **easily the best-looking TV I've ever seen.**" (His OLED-is-the-future thesis
  culminating.)
- [2016-02-07] **#5 — the Tesla Model 3 (EV for the masses).** "The Model S starts at ~$70k, the Model X at
  ~$80k; the Model 3 is supposed to start at **$35k** — for a high-end EV that's important. Getting the
  price down is big for Tesla and for EVs in general, enabled by the new **gigafactory**. **One of Tesla's
  biggest goals was to bring EVs to the masses affordably** — the Model S got everyone's attention, the
  Model 3 is the one people can actually get; it's seen as the endgame." (Design/spec reveal this year, buy
  in ~2017.)
- [2016-02-07] **#1–#3 — S7 / iPhone 7 / next Nexus.** S7 (Snapdragon 820, IMX300 sensor, an oddball new
  feature — USB-C/microSD/pressure display); **iPhone 7 = a new design year** (cleaner, removed antenna
  lines, maybe no camera bump, no headphone jack — could free space for a bigger battery/speaker; maybe
  "rethink the home button entirely" given Force Touch), plus a rumored smaller iPhone 5 SE; the next Nexus
  as the Android N showcase.
- [2016-02-07] **Studio note.** Shout-out to "the desk setup behind me" (his new studio) with a link.
