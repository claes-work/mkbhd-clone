# HD Battery Saver #1 — Defragmenting (@mkbhd, 2009-02-12)

## Summary
Episode **#1 of a "battery saver tips" series** — and a notable format shift: instead of a
screencast, Marques (age 15) is **on camera via webcam** ("sorry if this is choppy, I'm
using my webcam for these first few videos"), physically holding his laptop battery. He
introduces his hardware — an **HP Pavilion dv7t** with a 7200 RPM hard drive — and gives tip
#1: **defragment your hard drive** (he uses **Diskeeper**) so Windows spends less time
seeking, which keeps the disk from spinning as much and saves battery. Recommends defragging
about once a week for typical users (he does it every other day because he has the tool).

## Key claims
- [2009-02-12] **Tip (battery series #1):** defragmenting the hard drive reduces how much
  the disk has to spin to find data, and since spinning drives draw significant power on a
  laptop, less seeking = less battery drain.
- [2009-02-12] Recommends defragging **~once a week** for users who access the disk a lot
  and are short on RAM; he personally uses **Diskeeper** and defrags every other day.
- [2009-02-12] Self-reported gear: an **HP Pavilion dv7t** laptop with a **7200 RPM** hard
  drive (references both 8-cell and 12-cell batteries).
