# Review: Azza Hurricane 2000 (@mkbhd, 2010-10-27)

## Summary
Review of the Azza Hurricane 2000 full-tower ATX PC case, described as one of his own
top choices when building his personal PC. Detailed hardware tour (hot-swappable
drive bays, cable-management routing, dual 230mm side fans, dual 200mm top fans, seven
PCI slots, dust filters), then an explicit pros/cons close. No numeric score given —
verdict delivered as a conditional recommendation instead.

## Key claims
- [2010-10-27] **Discloses his own PC-building habit and case-selection process**:
  "this is a computer case that is definitely going to be used by a lot of enthusiasts
  who are building their own PCs like me, and this was actually one of my top choices
  when it came to picking a case" — a direct biographical disclosure that he builds
  his own PCs, not just reviews others' hardware.
- [2010-10-27] **Explicit named pros/cons structure**: "so the pros of this case, I'll
  start off with the pros... and the cons of the case" — states the pros/cons framing
  as a deliberate review structure rather than an unstructured wrap-up.
- [2010-10-27] **Conditional, audience-segmented verdict instead of a number score**:
  recommends the case specifically for high-airflow/high-performance builds ("if you
  really don't have a size limit and you really want to go all out with a high airflow
  high performance case then may I suggest...") while flagging its large footprint as a
  dealbreaker for space-constrained builders — an early instance of a "worth it for
  whom" conditional verdict rather than a single absolute score.
- [2010-10-27] **Personal aesthetic preference stated in passing**: notes the interior's
  black finish "if you have a nice uh black and brown setup going on like I do" —
  a rare mention of a black-and-brown (not red-and-black) personal setup color scheme,
  distinct from the red-and-black branding preference tracked elsewhere.
