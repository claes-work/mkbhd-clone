# Fat Laptops - HP 8560W Review (@mkbhd, 2011-08-24)

## Summary
A review-as-rant coining a category label — "fat laptops" — for oversized
mobile-workstation machines, using the HP 8560W as the case study. Proposes
an explicit weight threshold (under 6 lb = "a laptop"; heavier = a "fatty"/
mobile workstation) and a matching performance bar: if a laptop markets
itself as a desktop replacement, it has to actually match desktop-class
features. The HP passes on raw specs (quad-core i7, 8GB DDR3, Quadro 2000)
but is marked down for two concrete shortfalls: a spinning hard drive that
Windows Experience Index flags as the bottleneck, and a "pathetic" 1600x900
panel that some 13" notebooks already beat. Closes by pointing to the Alienware
M17x and MacBook Pro as laptops that do match desktop-class expectations, and
asks viewers what kind of laptop they use.

## Key claims
- [2011-08-24] Establishes an explicit **category-naming + threshold
  criterion** for laptop reviews: <6 lb = normal laptop, ≥6 lb = "fatty"/
  mobile-workstation category, each judged by a different bar — an early,
  concrete instance of grading products by "what is this actually trying to
  be?" rather than one universal standard.
- [2011-08-24] States the review's core standard explicitly: a laptop
  marketed as a desktop replacement must have desktop-class features to
  justify the size/weight trade-off, not just "pretty good" specs.
- [2011-08-24] Names the specific bottleneck methodology used: cites the
  Windows Experience Index sub-score to identify the weak component (HDD)
  rather than a subjective impression.
- [2011-08-24] Comparative framing again reaches for the Alienware M17x and
  MacBook Pro as the standard other big laptops should be held to — the same
  benchmark-by-comparison instinct tracked in nearby sources.
