# USB Type-C: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-03-13)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"** follow-up to the New MacBook, unpacking USB-C for viewers unsure whether the single-
port move mattered. His clear thesis: **"the key to USB-C is the U — it's Universal"** (not proprietary
like Thunderbolt/Lightning), and **it's the future — it's going to be everywhere.** He praises the
connector (reversible, 100 W power, 10 Gbps, backwards-compatible) and reads Apple's single-port move as
"super aggressive, very Apple" — while making a **prediction**: the second-gen MacBook will have more than
one USB-C port ("quote me").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-03-13] **"The key to USB-C is the U — it's Universal."** Unlike Thunderbolt (Intel, peripherals)
  or Lightning (Apple, low-power accessories), USB is non-proprietary — "usable by anyone, anywhere,"
  which is why it's on nearly every Android phone. USB-C = USB 3.1 in a small reversible connector.
- [2015-03-13] **Why it's better:** smaller/thinner (fits the thinnest devices and normal ones), very
  versatile (**up to 100 W** power → charge full-size electronics; **10 Gbps** data → run a 4K monitor or
  a fast SSD RAID), backwards-compatible with USB 3.0/2.0, and **reversible** ("plug it in the right way
  the first time, every single time").
- [2015-03-13] **It's the future — and Apple is being aggressive about it.** "The New MacBook might be one
  of the first to use USB-C, but it will definitely not be the only one… Apple being super aggressive,
  very Apple, making a statement that USB-C is the future by making it the *only* port."
- [2015-03-13] **A dated prediction.** "I can pretty much guarantee the second generation of this thin
  MacBook will have more than one USB-C port — quote me." (Contrasts the Chromebook Pixel's two USB-C
  ports as "really convenient" — plug power on either side.)
- [2015-03-13] **Where you'll see it:** laptops, tablets, phones, monitors — "anywhere you see a USB port
  now… expect that to turn into a USB-C port pretty soon," from Apple, Google, Samsung, Motorola, Asus
  (the Nokia N1 already has one; the next Nexus will too). Early one/two-port machines are netbooks/
  Chromebooks not meant as powerhouses — "and trust me, they're willing to sell you lots of adapters."
