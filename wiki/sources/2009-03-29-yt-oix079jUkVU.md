# HD Tutorial: Create A Channel Icon Using Gimp (@mkbhd, 2009-03-29)

## Summary
★ A production/branding screencast (Marques, age 15) showing how he built his **channel icon
in GIMP** — and, notably, **how the original MKBHD logo itself was made**. He creates a square
canvas (**200×200**, or 400×400 for crispness), uses the **blend/gradient tool** to fade
**black into red** (the earliest documented appearance of the **red-on-black** MKBHD color
identity), then adds text layers. He reveals his logo is the letters **"MKB"** — his
**initials, all capitals** — with **"HD"** added, and that the finished mark has **three text
layers**, including a **giant "X"** set to **very low opacity** behind everything (viewers had
asked how he made it). He exports as **channel.png**. He also notes his current channel icon is
only **200×200** and he's **redoing it larger/HD** because "my entire channel isn't HD so I
might as well make my logo HD." Ends with the usual **request/subscribe** CTA and a teaser for a
**"how to make an intro in Sony Vegas 8"** tutorial.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-29] The **MKBHD logo/channel icon** was created in **GIMP**: a **black-to-red
  gradient** background with **three text layers** — **"MKB"** (his initials, capitalized) +
  **"HD"**, plus a **large low-opacity "X"** behind the text.
- [2009-03-29] The original icon was **200×200**; he was **remaking it at higher resolution
  (400×400)** so the logo would be "HD" like the channel.
- [2009-03-29] Earliest documented statement of the **red + black** MKBHD visual identity
  (here as a simple GIMP gradient), predating the later crimson-red studio aesthetic.
- [2009-03-29] Reinforces the **"HD" brand promise** as a core early identity ("my entire
  channel isn't HD so I might as well make my logo HD").
