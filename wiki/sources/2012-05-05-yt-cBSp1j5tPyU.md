# Samsung Galaxy S III Design: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-05-05)

## Summary
**The sharpest single piece of analysis in the 2012 material.** The Galaxy S III had just been
revealed to widespread complaints about how it looked. Rather than join in, he reads Apple's April
2011 lawsuit against Samsung — quoting the infringement claims *verbatim* — and shows that the S III's
design is a **point-by-point legal workaround of every claim in that filing**. The conclusion is the
title of the argument: "the Samsung Galaxy S3, **designed by lawyers in California**." He also
explicitly withholds his own aesthetic verdict until he can hold the device.

## Key claims
- [2012-05-05] **The thesis**: the S III looks the way it does because of litigation, not taste — "you
  can thank Apple for this one… you can see the work of Samsung lawyers just crawling all over this
  device."
- [2012-05-05] **Apple's claims quoted verbatim** from the 2011-04-18 filing: a rectangular product
  shape with all four corners uniformly rounded; a front surface dominated by a screen with black
  borders; substantial black borders above and below the screen of roughly equal width, narrower ones at
  the sides; a grid of colourful icons with uniformly rounded corners; and a persistent bottom row of
  square icons.
- [2012-05-05] **The workaround, mapped claim by claim** — the body of the argument:
  - *rectangular with uniformly rounded corners* → "Samsung has clearly made it more of an oval… and the
    corners aren't even uniformly rounded anymore, the bottom corners are tighter than the top."
  - *screen with black borders* → "no black borders here, just Pebble Blue and Marble White."
  - *equal-width top and bottom borders* → "Samsung lawyers said 'we got this' and they gave it a chin."
  - *colourful square icons* → "the newest version of TouchWiz has done away with those icon backgrounds
    that made them all square and colourful."
  - *persistent square dock row* → "that is gone too in TouchWiz."
- [2012-05-05] **Judgment explicitly withheld pending hands-on** — review discipline stated on camera:
  "I'm going to reserve my own personal judgments about how it looks and how it feels in the hand for
  the day that I'm actually holding it."
- [2012-05-05] **Redirects the audience's complaint to its actual cause**: "make sure you share this
  video so people actually know what to be complaining about — maybe you don't want to complain to
  Samsung as much as you want to complain to that other company."
- [2012-05-05] Specs noted in passing: quad-core, Android 4.0 with TouchWiz, 4.8" display.
- [2012-05-05] The **"Explained!" format** continues — fourth entry in ten weeks, and the first applied
  to a design/legal question rather than a technical one.
