# The Steam Machine is Over $1000?! (@Waveform, 2026-06-26)

## Summary
An ensemble Waveform episode (Marques Brownlee with co-hosts Andrew Manganelli, David Imel, plus Adam and Ellis) built almost entirely around pricing news. The hosts react to Valve's Steam Machine landing at $1,049+ (over the ~$700 everyone expected), the ~$25,000 modular electric Slate Truck that Marques drove, the newly announced cheaper Nothing Phone 4B, GTA 6's $80/$100 tiered pricing, and Meta's rebranded "Meta Glasses" that drop the Ray-Ban name. Recurring themes are RAM-driven price inflation ("Ramageddon"), buy-cheap-upfront-vs-pay-over-time economics, and privacy concerns around always-on camera glasses. ## Key claims
- [2026-06-26] Valve's Steam Machine is priced at $1,049 for the 512GB model (no controller), $1,128 for the 512GB bundle with controller, up to $1,428 for the 2TB bundle; release date June 29. Performance is described as roughly PS5-level (some games worse), while a 6-year-old PS5 is ~$650 and a PS5 Pro ~$900 — so the price-to-performance is called "a stinker." (panel / uncertain — narrated, likely David)
- [2026-06-26] Valve is deliberately not subsidizing the Steam Machine; the hosts read Valve's statement that subsidizing hardware "doesn't align with our beliefs of how healthy ecosystems are built" and works against open PC systems. (panel — reading Valve's statement)
- [2026-06-26] The Steam Machine's strengths are form factor, quietness, and living-room cohesion (HDMI-CEC, separate Wi-Fi/Bluetooth antennas, magnetic face plates); it runs SteamOS/Proton (a Windows-to-Linux translation layer likened to Rosetta 2). (panel / uncertain — largely David)
- [2026-06-26] The Slate Truck starts just under $25,000: rear-wheel drive, 65 kWh battery, ~180 hp, 0–60 in ~8s, ~90 mph top speed, ~2,000 lb tow, 5-ft bed, two-seater with lockable frunk, no paint/speakers/power windows by default. Range is now ~205 miles (up from an earlier ~160-mile promise); the 240-mile option was dropped, leaving one drivetrain. (Marques — presenting the vehicle he drove)
- [2026-06-26] Slate module pricing (per a Speed Sport Life leak): vinyl wraps from ~$500 up to ~$1,500–$1,600 (materials only, DIY install); integrated speakers ~$150 (front L/R) + $250 (center); durable bed liner ~$750. Wiring/pins for power and data are pre-run so accessories snap on. (panel / uncertain — David and Andrew discussing)
- [2026-06-26] The Slate is polarizing: some call $25k too expensive vs a ~$28–30k Ford Maverick that includes speakers/windows/etc.; others call it impressive as the cheapest new truck in the US and an EV. Big caveat: the factory isn't finished, so it's TBD until it actually ships (Q4 target). (panel consensus)
- [2026-06-26] Nothing announced the Nothing Phone 4B, a cheaper tier; a Nothing employee's convoluted tweet ("most premium line below our flagship") is mocked as marketing-speak for "cheaper." The CMF 3A Pro was cancelled because RAM would have been ~half the phone's cost. (panel; the branding critique is group)
- [2026-06-26] Marques reiterates his own prior review take: Nothing can't make a flagship phone because they lack the volume to get part prices low enough to compete/make money, so they're expanding downward instead. (Marques)
- [2026-06-26] GTA 6 is priced $80 standard / $100 deluxe, releasing November; per Reddit/Radio Times, certain in-game locations, vehicle mod shops, and car collections are locked behind the ultimate edition — criticized as Fortnite-style monetization on a paid game. (panel / uncertain)
- [2026-06-26] Meta launched three new "Meta Glasses" (Fury, Adventure, and a Kylie Jenner edition), ~$80 cheaper (~$299 start; Kylie edition $399), dropping Ray-Ban branding but still made by Luxottica (who owns Ray-Ban). They include a new Meta Superintelligence Labs model called "Muse Spark." (panel)
- [2026-06-26] The panel raises serious privacy concerns: people drilling out the recording indicator light (Joanna Stern documented a paid service for it), venues banning smart glasses (e.g., the F1 paddock), and the general unease that anyone in glasses could be recording; Japan's mandatory loud camera-shutter law is cited as a contrast. (panel)
- [2026-06-26] iOS 27 beta 2 adds partial RCS 2.7 support including edit/unsend messages, narrowing the gap with iMessage down toward mainly the bubble color. (uncertain — RCS enthusiast, could be Marques or Andrew)
