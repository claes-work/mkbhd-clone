# 2013 Mac Pro: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-06-11)

## Summary
Analysis of the radically redesigned "trash can" 2013 Mac Pro — and a capstone to his own
Mac-Pro arc (the Dear Apple video begging for an update, the overpriced-tech pick, the
5-part Hackintosh Pro built as an alternative). He's genuinely impressed by the specs and
the thermal-core design, but sharpens his **upgradeability critique** (everything soldered;
Thunderbolt-as-expansion is external clutter) and reads the cylinder shape as a marketing
move.

## Key claims
- [2013-06-11] **Closes his own Mac-Pro arc**: "I made a Dear Apple video begging them to
  update the aging Mac Pro, put it in a most-overpriced-tech video, and did a 5-part
  Hackintosh Pro series to create an alternative to the machine Apple wouldn't update — and
  here in 2013 they finally did it." Ties the announcement to his documented history.
- [2013-06-11] **Impressed by the specs + thermal core**: "2–3× faster than the previous
  highest-end Mac Pro… dual Xeons, dual FirePro GPUs, PCI-based storage at 1.25 GB/s [vs my
  600–700 MB/s SATA SSDs]… the tiny size is made possible by a single triangular 'thermal
  core' heat sink everything connects to." Credits the engineering generously.
- [2013-06-11] **The upgradeability critique, sharpened**: "the whole point of a desktop is
  to expand and upgrade it as parts get better… for years the Mac Pro was the last upgradeable
  Apple machine. Now the CPU, GPU, storage, memory are basically soldered or glued to the
  board — pretty much no expandability. Apple says 'Thunderbolt,' but that means all your
  storage is external — your clean setup goes from looking like this to a mess of external
  drives. You can't just shove a 2 TB drive in like the old Mac Pros." His core upgradeability
  belief, applied to a machine he otherwise admires.
- [2013-06-11] **Reads the cylinder as a marketing move**: "your room has square corners and
  your square computer sits against a square wall under a desk; this cylinder forces itself to
  not be against a wall — it's forcing itself onto your desk, on display. That's kind of what
  Apple wants." Design-as-strategy analysis (plus the "Darth Vader's trash can" quip).
- [2013-06-11] **Honest aesthetic nitpick**: "I don't love that it's glossy — the inside is
  matte black, but the cover is glossy on the outside, doesn't look all that Pro to me. I'll
  probably put some designs on it anyway." Flags a taste objection (his customize-it instinct).
- [2013-06-11] **Names the setup-of-the-future**: "the specs to edit 4K video while rendering
  effects in the background, connected to three 4K monitors — that's the setup of the future."
  His forward-looking framing (ties to the 4K-Explained thesis).
