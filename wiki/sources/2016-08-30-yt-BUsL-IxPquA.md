# Dope Tech: Boosted Board 2! (@mkbhd, 2016-08-30)

## Summary
A Dope Tech episode reviewing the newly-announced second-generation Boosted Board, an electric skateboard controlled by a Bluetooth pistol-grip remote, batteries, and electric motors. Marques frames the appeal as spending less of your own energy so you can go farther and faster, walks the incremental-but-real upgrades over the original (quick/user-replaceable battery, new range tiers, dual Bluetooth radios, water-sealed internals, better wheels/trucks/motors, smoother acceleration), and positions electric skateboards as a legitimate mode of transportation in a dense city like New York. His through-line ties the board's instant torque to his enthusiasm for electric cars, while candidly flagging himself as a beginner rider and crediting his friend Sam and Casey Neistat as the real experts.

## Key claims
- [2016-08-30] An electric skateboard like a Boosted Board replaces kicking and pushing with a remote, batteries, and electric motors, using less of your energy so you can go farther and faster.
- [2016-08-30] Boosted announced the second-generation board on this day; it looks a lot like the first, but frequent riders will notice the differences.
- [2016-08-30] The remote is a mostly-unchanged small, lightweight pistol grip: hold the safety switch and rotate the throttle to go forward or backward, and it connects to the board over Bluetooth.
- [2016-08-30] The remote's side display shows board battery level, connection status, remote battery level, and current mode (modes 1–4 each reach different speeds).
- [2016-08-30] The Boosted Board 2 adds quick charging, fast-charging to 85% in about 30 minutes.
- [2016-08-30] The battery is now user-replaceable, so you can carry a spare and a screwdriver, swap it, and effectively double your range.
- [2016-08-30] It offers two battery options: a standard 99Wh battery good for 6–7 miles, or an extended 199Wh battery good for 12–14 miles, while keeping roughly the same ~15 lb weight and size.
- [2016-08-30] It adds a bottom battery-indicator light and two Bluetooth radios (so the smartphone app and remote can connect simultaneously) — both absent on the first board, which had only one radio.
- [2016-08-30] A new front accessory port supports upcoming modular add-ons like headlamps or LEDs.
- [2016-08-30] The internals are now water-sealed in the housing — not fully waterproof, but comfortable enough for riding through puddles or in the rain.
- [2016-08-30] Marques found the Boosted Board 2 easier and smoother to ride, crediting better wheels, trucks, more efficient motors, and a smoother acceleration curve — while cautioning he's a beginner with only ~2 miles on the original.
- [2016-08-30] The board delivers instant torque like an electric car, accelerating very fast; in Mode 4 it reaches up to 22 mph, far faster than an analog skateboard.
- [2016-08-30] Electric skateboards are a more portable alternative to a bicycle for commuting the same distance in a dense city.
