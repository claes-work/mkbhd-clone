# HD Tutorial: Produce any Video in 16:9 HD (@mkbhd, 2009-05-01)

## Summary
★ An origin-era **production/encoding tutorial** (Marques, age 15) showing how to render a **4:3 source
video into 16:9 720p HD** using the free tool **MPEG Streamclip** — for creators who shoot on a non-HD 4:3
camera and don't mind the stretch (he notes he does have an HD camera). He imports several AVI clips
(intro + numbered clips), then exports to **MPEG-4 / H.264** with specific highest-quality settings:
**de-interlace, 30 fps, 1280×720 (720p HDTV), and the quality slider at ~80**. Crucially he ties quality
directly to **file size and upload cost**: a video he uploaded the day before was **985 MB and took ~1.5
hours to upload**, and he warns you generally can't upload over ~1 GB — so he caps the slider around 80 to
balance quality against size. He frames this as the non-HD-camera counterpart to his earlier **Camtasia
Studio 6** HD tutorial.
