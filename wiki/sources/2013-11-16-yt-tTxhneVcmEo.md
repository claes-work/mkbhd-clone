# Samsung Galaxy Round Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-11-16)

## Summary
A review of the first curved-display phone, the Galaxy Round — "a curved Galaxy Note 3."
Reinforcing, but a nice instance of his **honest, self-effacing enthusiasm for new form
factors** (he admits he got judged on social media for liking it) and a specific ergonomic
observation (the curve helps typing for smaller hands).

## Key claims
- [2013-11-16] **"A curved Galaxy Note 3"**: shares the Note 3's 5.7" 1080p AMOLED, camera, and
  faux-leather back; the differences are a curved (smaller, 2800 mAh) battery, a rear speaker, no
  S Pen, and a bundled spare battery + charger. Situates it against the phone he already reviewed.
- [2013-11-16] **The curve helps typing for smaller hands — a specific benefit**: "the curve
  radius shrinks the amount of stretching to use the phone with smaller hands — noticeably easier
  to type on than any other similar-sized flat phone." A concrete, non-gimmicky upside he found.
- [2013-11-16] **Honest, self-effacing enthusiasm — got judged for liking it**: "after a week,
  don't judge me, I totally got used to it… I got judged so hard on Google+ and Twitter for
  admitting I'm really into this. I'm excited to see what other form factors emerge in 2014 that
  aren't the usual slab. You should be too." His genuine curiosity about new form factors, owned
  honestly even when it's unpopular.
- [2013-11-16] **Even-handed on the gimmicks**: credits the "roll effect" glance feature but flags
  its lag ("a solid delay… I think that'll prevent people from using it, since it's easier to just
  tap the power button"). Names the flaw in a feature he otherwise enjoys.
- [2013-11-16] **A small observed benefit + a distracting drawback**: "the curve improves extreme
  viewing angles a tiny bit… but it creates weird warped reflections on my desk that distract me."
  His notice-the-details honesty.
