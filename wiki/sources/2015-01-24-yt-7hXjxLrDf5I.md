# HTC One M9: What to Expect! (@mkbhd, 2015-01-24)

## Summary
A pre-launch preview of the HTC One M9 (from trustworthy leaks), notable for two doctrine restatements:
**"if it ain't broke, don't fix it"** (the M8's aluminum unibody + BoomSound front-facing speakers stay;
the main change is moving all buttons to the right side), and **image-quality-over-megapixels** — HTC
finally ditches UltraPixels for a 20 MP Sony sensor, but Marques says he'd have preferred a **bigger 8 MP
sensor with big pixels** over the megapixel jump. Also a no-cases/skin note.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-01-24] **"If it ain't broke, don't fix it."** The M8 drove the build-quality conversation
  (aluminum unibody, "my favorite, the BoomSound front-facing speakers — dope as usual"); the M9 keeps it,
  with the main design change being **all buttons moved to the right side** (easier one-handed reach on a
  phone made large by the speakers). "If you already like the current design, which a lot of people did"
  — a good thing.
- [2015-01-24] **The camera fixes the M8's biggest weakness.** The UltraPixel 4 MP camera (M7/M8) was
  "one of the most polarizing cameras in smartphones" — OIS + low-light promise but "hit or miss / meh."
  "Goodbye UltraPixels — it was a nice run, but things weren't clicking." The M9's rumored **20.7 MP** Sony
  sensor (Xperia-class) should produce nice photos + 4K video.
- [2015-01-24] **Image-quality-over-megapixels (his preference).** "I personally would have liked to see
  maybe an 8 MP larger sensor with big pixels, but I'm okay with them going full 20 MP — hopefully it's
  really good. I just kind of want the pictures to look good." (Camera-module looks "don't really matter.")
- [2015-01-24] **Predicts a 1080p (not QHD) display** — "I wouldn't be surprised to see them omit Quad HD
  in favor of better battery life" (his display-diminishing-returns / battery-first instinct). No-cases:
  "if it is slippery again, I will be putting a skin on the back like I have with other slippery metal
  phones."
