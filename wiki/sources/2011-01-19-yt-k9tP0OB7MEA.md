# How to make a YouTube Mobile Banner [2011] (@mkbhd, 2011-01-19)

## Summary
A screencast tutorial on designing a YouTube mobile channel banner (new
YouTube-partner feature at the time), built in GIMP ("the gnu image
manipulation program," used explicitly by name here). Walks through the
320×50px portrait-banner spec, a red-to-black/red-to-darker-red gradient
background, a text box (font named as "Babas Neue," from dafont.com), and
his PNG watermark placed in the corner — then shows the upload flow on
youtube.com and the final rendered banners (portrait: "subscribe";
landscape: "subscribe today") on his own channel, viewed live on a Droid
device.

## Key claims
- [2011-01-19] **Names GIMP explicitly by its full name**: "for this
  project, for the portrait channel banner, I'm using ... the GNU image
  manipulation program — it's free and multiplatform, but if you have
  Photoshop that will do as well." This resolves the caption-garbled tool

- [2011-01-19] Reiterates the red/black brand-gradient choice in a concrete
  applied example: "maybe your channel has a sort of a red and black theme,
  so you can choose a red-to-black gradient" — a second, dated instance of
  the red/black/white palette being treated as his established brand
  identity by January 2011.
- [2011-01-19] Names the specific display font used across his branding
  graphics: "Babas Neue," sourced from dafont.com.
- [2011-01-19] Demonstrates practical, iterative brand application: the
  final shipped banner differs from the tutorial demo (a white-to-gray
  gradient plus his logo, rather than the red/black example built on
  screen) — shows brand execution as adapted per-surface rather than
  rigidly templated.
