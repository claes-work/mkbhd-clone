# Using Google Now: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-12-30)

## Summary
His last video of 2012 — an "Explained!" deep-dive on Google Now, which he calls his "number one favorite
thing that was released in 2012." Mostly enthusiastic feature coverage, but it carries a **dated 2012
privacy stance** worth flagging: he acknowledges the data-collection unease and dismisses it with a
pragmatic tradeoff ("you're being tracked anyway; at least Google gives you something back, and it's
free"). Notable to record because his later privacy positions may contrast with this.

## Key claims
- [2012-12-30] **The privacy tradeoff, stated plainly — a date-scoped position**: "a lot of people might
  think it's weird that Google knows all this about you… but **you're being tracked by a bunch of different
  companies anyway that you might not even know about, so it's nice that at least Google gives you something
  back for it, and it's free.**" A pragmatic 2012 stance on personal-data collection.
- [2012-12-30] **Google Now framed as a step beyond Siri — the pre-search**: "what separates Google Now
  from Siri or S Voice is the **pre-search** — what shows up in the cards UI *before* you even search…
  information relevant to you based on what it knows [Gmail, search, calendar]." The anticipatory-computing
  idea, praised.
- [2012-12-30] **His favorite feature is the commute card** (and the package tracker): "if your device
  picks up that you commute somewhere frequently based on GPS, it'll show a card in the morning with how
  long it takes and a notification ~10 minutes before you need to leave based on traffic… it's just genius."
  Also "my favorite feature is the package tracker — every time I get an Amazon email it shows up in Google
  Now."
- [2012-12-30] **Daily user, disclosed**: "I do use it all the time, pretty much every day… I use this more
  than most people do." Consistent with his heavy Google-services usage.
- [2012-12-30] **Notes what's coming — OS control functions**: "I asked it to turn off Wi-Fi and it said
  system functions are coming but not yet implemented — keyword *yet*." A dated feature-gap observation.
- [2012-12-30] **Type-not-just-voice option praised for context**: "if you're in a quiet area and don't
  want to look crazy talking to your phone, you can type your question and get the same card." A practical,
  socially-aware note (echoing the Google Glass social-legibility concern).
- [2012-12-30] **Star Trek trivia (voice data / humor)**: Google Now's dev codename was "Majel," after
  Majel Barrett, the voice of the Star Trek computer — delivered with a Lieutenant-Commander-Data riff.
