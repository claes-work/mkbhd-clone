# Moto 360 V2 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-10-13)

## Summary
The full review of the 2nd-gen Moto 360 — "a really minor upgrade in pretty much every way," but the one
he keeps coming back to as his "de-facto smartwatch." He credits the original with "starting the circular
revolution and the focus on design." The standout feature is **MotoMaker customization** (band/body/bezel
built-to-order). Notable is a candid **wearable-fatigue** admission: **"I'm less of a smartwatch guy than I
was a year ago — I don't wear one every day anymore."**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-10-13] **His de-facto smartwatch, minor upgrade.** "I keep coming back to this 360… I still give
  it credit for starting the circular revolution and the focus on design." Improvements: easier quick-swap
  lugs, a Snapdragon 400 (fixing the original's slow OMAP), and — the best feature — **MotoMaker** (build
  your own band/body/bezel combo out of the box; he went all-black, which "hides the bar at the bottom").
- [2015-10-13] **Not the smoothest, but good for Android Wear.** He still gives the pure-smoothness edge to
  the Apple Watch and Samsung Gear S2, "but among Android Wear watches this is about as good as it gets."
- [2015-10-13] **Battery.** Ambient mode on by default; still makes a full day (>30% left), or **two days**
  with ambient off; fast wireless charging (dead → full in under an hour).
- [2015-10-13] **Works with the iPhone now** (Android Wear iOS support), "which also makes it a competitor
  to the Apple Watch for some buyers."
- [2015-10-13] **Wearable-fatigue (the candid part).** "Bottom line: I'm less of a smartwatch guy than I
  was about a year ago — I don't wear one every day anymore. But it's nice to see improvements in these
  neat little pocket computers on your wrist, especially in design." Worth upgrading from the original for
  the battery/performance.
