# Oppo Find 5 Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-02-10)

## Summary
The full review of the **Oppo Find 5** — the phone he'd flagged five months earlier
([[2012-09-XX-super-retina-smartphone]] / the Sep-2012 "Super Retina Smartphone" preview)
as one to "write down, it's going to be big." A **★★ LANDMARK on the long Oppo arc**: he
calls it flatly **"the best smartphone you can buy"** on build quality, applies his
signature **"Stormtrooper"** descriptor to a phone, and champions an under-the-radar
maker most viewers had never heard of — the same Oppo that, per the 2026 material, ends up
breaking iPhone camera dominance. Also a **promise→delivery** instance and a couple of
concrete daily-life data.

## Key claims
- [2013-02-10] **"The best smartphone you can buy" — on build**: "in the build quality
  department this is the best smartphone you can buy. Step aside Droid DNA, step aside
  Samsung plastic, step aside iPhone 5, step aside Lumia 920… this is a Stormtrooper of a
  smartphone, an absolute boss." The **"Stormtrooper"** aesthetic word (his matched
  black/white palette, cf. the Hackintosh case) applied to a phone; steel frame, edge-to-
  edge glass.
- [2013-02-10] **Champions an underdog/unknown maker — the long Oppo arc's origin (full
  review)**: "Oppo, a company more well-known for its pricey Blu-ray players… this is
  Oppo's way of saying hey, we can make world-class smartphones too, and they've proven
  it." His disposition to elevate innovative outsiders — the earliest **full** Oppo review
  in the corpus, connecting forward to the 2026 Oppo-camera-dominance thread.
- [2013-02-10] **Skins can add value, not just reskin — the nuanced skin position**: praises
  Oppo's Android 4.1 skin for adding real features (notification-bar toggles, 3-app
  multitasking with free-RAM readout) "not just to make it look different, but they
  actually add value… changed with the intent of making it better." Distinguishes
  value-adding skins from cosmetic ones (a refinement of his stock-leaning position — and
  he notes you can always throw on Nova Launcher, so Android's flexibility defuses the
  objection).
- [2013-02-10] **Camera = "hit or miss," honest despite the spec sheet**: "13 megapixels…
  but it really just performs like any other average 8-megapixel smartphone camera." Spec
  ≠ result again; most impressed by the 1080p HDR video and the front camera.
- [2013-02-10] **A packaging-as-craft aside**: "some US manufacturers could really learn
  from this — very simple, clean, compact packaging… even the Oppo-logo SIM ejector tool
  feels high quality." Values unboxing/packaging as part of the product (recurring).
- [2013-02-10] **Daily-routine self-datum**: "the battery often lasts me when I get up at
  5:30 in the morning, don't ask, till around 11 or 12 at night, which qualifies as a full
  day in my book." A dated personal-schedule datum (early riser) + his "power user, hours
  of screen-on time" self-description.
- [2013-02-10] **Promise→delivery**: opens "it's about time we gave the full review
  treatment to the Oppo Find 5," closing the thread he opened in Sept 2012. Another
  documented preview→full-review cycle.
