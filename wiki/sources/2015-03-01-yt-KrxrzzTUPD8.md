# Samsung Galaxy S6 Edge Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-03-01)

## Summary
First impressions of the Galaxy S6 Edge — the dual-curved-display sibling to the S6. The key read: the
edge display has **gone "from a weird experiment to a full-fledged flagship"** (versus the one-sided Note
Edge). The edge software features (People Edge, Edge Lighting) are "not one of those things that blows my
mind" but the **immersion** of the curved-under-glass display genuinely impressed him — "I couldn't stop
looking at this damn display." His call: given the choice, **he'd pick the Edge over the regular S6.**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-03-01] **Same phone, different shape.** Identical internals/software/camera/materials to the S6,
  a slightly larger 2600 mAh battery, and the 5.1" display now curving over both sides — "a real head-
  turner, but not in an overly distracting way."
- [2015-03-01] **Curved-under-glass takes the iPhone trick further.** Where the iPhone curves the glass
  edge for a smoother transition, "this takes it to the next level by curving the display under the glass
  also."
- [2015-03-01] **Edge features are nice-not-essential.** People Edge (swipe-in favorite contacts — "I
  really wish they'd let us put anything there, like five favorite apps"), Edge Lighting (color-coded
  caller glow when face-down), plus Note-Edge carryovers (information stream, night clock). "Not one of
  those things that blows my mind, but at least it's good-looking."
- [2015-03-01] **The real win is immersion.** "The edge display is all about immersion — that's what
  impressed me most." A "dope side effect": it's oddly perfect for the left-swipe app drawers common in
  new Android apps.
- [2015-03-01] **Experiment → flagship, and his pick.** "It's gone from being a weird experiment to a
  full-fledged flagship… honestly, if I was picking between the S6 and the S6 Edge right now, I'd go for
  the Edge." (Note: cases for it "are going to be pretty awkward.")
