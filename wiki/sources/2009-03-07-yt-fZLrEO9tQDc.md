# HD Tutorial: Enable Advanced Disk Performance (@mkbhd, 2009-03-07)

## Summary
A Windows-optimization tutorial (Marques, age 15) showing how to enable **write caching /
"advanced performance"** on drives in XP or Vista via **Device Manager → disk drive
properties → Policies tab**. He demonstrates on a USB flash drive plugged into his hub,
explaining the trade-off between "optimize for quick removal" (caching off, safe to yank)
and "optimize for performance" (caching on, faster but requires a proper eject). He gives a
clear **safety caveat**: don't enable advanced performance on a laptop's internal drive
unless on battery, because a sudden power loss can corrupt data or the drive. Continues the
origin-era stream of small PC-tuning how-tos.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-07] Enable disk write caching via **Device Manager → [drive] → Properties →
  Policies → "optimize for performance"** (works in Windows XP and Vista); "quick removal"
  disables caching for safe unplugging.
- [2009-03-07] **Caveat:** enabling advanced performance on a laptop's internal drive is
  risky off-battery — a power loss "might actually lose data or completely have an unusable
  drive"; restart after changing the setting.
- [2009-03-07] Origin-era hardware detail: mentions a **7200 RPM 250 GB internal drive** and
  external/USB drives on his rig (self-reported).

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "if you do lose battery or power for some reason... you might actually lose data or completely have an unusable drive"
