# iKey Audio HDR7 Dual Microphone (@mkbhd, 2010-11-17)

## Summary
HD review of the iKey Audio HDR7, a handheld dual-condenser portable audio recorder
with an LCD screen and tripod mount, aimed at reporters/video producers recording
audio off-camera. Covers the interface (jog wheel, menu/transport buttons, input
gain), a stand-far-away/still-clean-audio demo, and closes noting this is one of the
last videos NOT filmed with his newly acquired Canon T2i — signaling an imminent gear
transition.

## Key claims
- [2010-11-17] **9/10 rating, framed around recording structure + interface**:
  "I have to give this product a 9 out of 10 for just a phenomenal recording
  structure ... the wav format it records to ... the customization of the interface
  and the really powerful microphone" — negatives limited to battery life and
  file-deletion/shutdown speed.
- [2010-11-17] **Positions the HDR7 as a fix for weak on-camera mics**: recommends it
  specifically for "people who are recording things that are far away or want a
  solution to the microphone on top of their camera that simply doesn't pick up their
  voice well enough" — even after using an external mic (Rode video mic) directly on
  the camera.
- [2010-11-17] **Confirms the Canon T2i camera transition**: "I recently purchased the
  Canon T2i and this is actually one of the last videos you will see that is not
  filmed with the Canon T2i possibly the last" — dates the equipment switch to
  mid-November 2010, consistent with the T2i unboxing the following week
  ([[wiki/sources/2010-11-25-yt-5Ww9OLkJOEM]]).
- [2010-11-17] **Names an aspirational "editor's choice" standard**: "if I could give
  it an editor's choice if I was actually an editor of a website well I definitely
  would" — an early instance of borrowing tech-publication review vocabulary/criteria.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "This thing is a beast — you can be standing 15 ft away from it and if you turn
> input gain all the way up it'll sound like I'm sitting right next to you talking
> into your ear."

> "If I could give it an editor's choice if I was actually an editor of a website,
> well, I definitely would."
