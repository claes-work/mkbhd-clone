# The Ultimate iOS 14 Homescreen Setup Guide! (@mkbhd, 2020-09-25)

## Summary
A how-to riding the September 2020 wave of iPhone home-screen customization that iOS 14 unlocked. Marques structures it as three escalating "levels" of customization — Level 1 "beginner" (for "mom and dad"): stock Apple widgets (weather, battery), hiding home-screen pages via the App Library, a minimalist dock-only layout; Level 2 "enthusiast": third-party widget apps (Widgetsmith, Color Widgets, Widgeridoo, Time Page, HayWeather, the Google widget with Lens), and a note that these are usually free at a basic tier and paywall deeper customization; Level 3 "former Android user, full tilt": faux custom icons built from Siri Shortcuts (with the tradeoff that each launch has to bounce through the Shortcuts app, adds ~¼–½ second every time, and loses notification badges). A running framing throughout: everything iOS 14 "finally" enables — widgets, launcher-style customization, hidden pages — has been available on Android for a decade, but it "does feel a little bit new again now that Apple's finally doing it."

## Key claims
- [2020-09-25] People have been customizing phone home screens for "10 plus years"; iOS 14 making it possible on iPhone feels new even though Android has had it for years. (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Level 1: the point of widgets is "beautiful glanceable information"; best starter widgets are Apple's stock weather and battery widgets. (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] iOS 14's App Library lets you hide whole home-screen pages and — unlike any Android launcher he's used — *saves* the hidden pages so you can bring them back (e.g. a "weekend home screen"). (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Pro tip: you can now have a single home screen with no apps at all, just the dock, for a minimalist look. (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Level 2: third-party developers are building dedicated widget apps (Widgetsmith, Color Widgets, Widgeridoo, Time Page, HayWeather) — an "entire ecosystem" of apps whose only function is widget customization. (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Caveat: many of these apps are free at a basic level but charge (subscription or one-time ~$4) once you customize colors or add functions. (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Level 3: you can't actually set custom app icons on iOS yet — the "custom icon" home screens on Twitter are Siri Shortcuts, made via Shortcuts → "Open App" action → "Add to Home Screen." (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Downside of the Shortcuts workaround: every launch must open the Shortcuts app first (~¼–½ second, and "it never gets faster over time"), and you lose notification badges — so a whole home screen of them isn't ideal. (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Pro tip: leave the shortcut's name field blank for a cleaner look — "something I've been doing with Android launchers for years." (Marques)
- [2020-09-25] Verdict: iOS 14 home-screen customization "isn't much if you've seen more than this before," but it's far more than the last 13 versions of iOS offered. (Marques)
