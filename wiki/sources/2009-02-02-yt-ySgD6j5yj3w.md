# HD Tutorial: Get High-Resolution Wallpapers Quickly (@mkbhd, 2009-02-02)

## Summary
Origin-era how-to: 15-year-old Marques shows a quick way to find desktop wallpapers that
match your screen resolution exactly. The trick is to Google-image-search your own
resolution (he uses **1440×900**), pick an image whose listed dimensions match, then use
**Firefox's built-in "Set As Desktop Background"** right-click tool. He argues an
exactly-fitting image loads faster and won't stretch, and warns that some results are
merely *titled* with a resolution without actually being that size. A small personal tell:
he says he changes his wallpaper constantly.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-02] **How-to:** to get a perfectly-fitting wallpaper, Google-image-search your
  screen resolution (e.g. `1440 by 900`) and pick a result whose stated size matches.
- [2009-02-02] **Reasoning:** an image at your native resolution loads faster and fits
  without stretching; an overly high-res image is slower and a smaller one won't fit.
- [2009-02-02] **Caveat:** some results are titled with a resolution but are not actually
  that size — "be wary of those."
- [2009-02-02] **Tooling:** demonstrates Firefox's right-click **Set As Desktop
  Background** and Windows Vista's Personalize → Desktop Background to revert.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
