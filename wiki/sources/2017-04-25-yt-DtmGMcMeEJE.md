# Top 5 Smartphone Cameras: The Blind Test! (@mkbhd, 2017-04-25)

## Summary
**The origin of the Blind Camera Test franchise** — the first time he runs the top phone cameras as a
blind A/B/C/D/E comparison and lets the audience pick without knowing the brands. The thesis: cameras are
now so good the difference is **personal preference, not objective performance**, and head-to-head
comparisons are distorted by **confirmation bias**. The direct realization of the camera-method he'd been
building since 2014 ("you decide by how much you like the picture").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-04-25] **Why blind, why now — cameras got too good for objective wins.** "I used to do very
  objective, numerical camera evaluations — but that was 2014. In 2017 the top-5 cameras are so good the
  difference is way less about objective performance (dynamic range, megapixels) and more about personal
  preference — which hue/colors/software you happen to like." The premise of the whole format.
- [2017-04-25] **The method: identical shots, labeled A–E, you pick honestly.** Same angles across all
  five (a backpack, a gray car, portraits/skin tones, a shallow-DoF shot, a low-light Groot, an Empire State
  landscape, a car interior for dynamic range). "Don't skip to the end. Be honest with yourself. Watch in
  the highest resolution you can (1440p/4K)." Notes the biggest differentiator across the board is **color
  temperature / white balance**, not sharpness (all in focus).
- [2017-04-25] **THE INSIGHT: confirmation bias distorts every head-to-head.** "We go into these comparisons
  knowing which one we *want* to win — so we justify every little thing that looks better about phone A and
  ignore what's good about phone B. It's basic confirmation bias; we assume the new one is better even when
  we don't know that. A lot of people say 'I hate the iPhone, I'd never pick it' — and then a lot of you pick
  the iPhone." A durable media-literacy point about how people actually judge cameras.
- [2017-04-25] **"They're all really damn good" — the reassuring conclusion.** "Your winner probably doesn't
  surprise you, because these are among the best cameras — they were chosen *because* they're good and all
  very similar. Hopefully this sheds light on the fact that they're all really damn good." Reframes the
  contest as proof of parity.
- [2017-04-25] **Scopes what the test covers (honest limits).** "This is just raw back-camera stills — other
  things matter too: autofocus speed, software features, time-lapse, stabilization for video, the selfie
  camera." Names what a blind stills test *doesn't* capture.
