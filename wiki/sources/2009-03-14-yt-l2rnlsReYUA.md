# Abbreviated iPhone 3.0 Wishlist (@mkbhd, 2009-03-14)

## Summary
A webcam-recorded opinion video (Marques, age 15) laying out the **software features he wants
from iPhone OS 3.0** ahead of its expected reveal on **Tuesday, March 17, 2009** — explicitly
"not my predictions but what we actually want." His wishlist: **copy/paste**, **SMS forwarding
and MMS (picture) messaging**, sending **calendar appointments to contacts**, saving an **email
as a draft**, a **weekly calendar view**, and **Adobe Flash support** in Mobile Safari.
Throughout he benchmarks the iPhone against his own **Verizon LG Voyager** (and an older
Verizon flip phone), revealing he is **not an iPhone owner** at this point — a lifelong Verizon
user who says he wouldn't mind an iPhone if Apple went beyond AT&T. This is the earliest
sustained smartphone-software critique in the corpus and shows the analytical instincts that
became his signature.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-14] Wants iPhone OS 3.0 to add **copy/paste**, **SMS forwarding + MMS picture
  messaging**, **send calendar appointment to a contact**, **save email as draft**, a **weekly
  calendar view**, and **Flash support** in the browser.
- [2009-03-14] Frames these as gaps versus feature-phones — repeatedly citing his **Verizon
  LG Voyager** as already doing copy/paste, message forwarding, and appointment-sharing.
- [2009-03-14] Self-reported: a **lifelong Verizon customer**, **not yet an iPhone owner**;
  argues Apple would "get a lot more money" by putting the iPhone on more than one carrier
  (i.e., beyond AT&T exclusivity).
- [2009-03-14] Records on a **webcam** and openly asks viewers to recommend an **HD camcorder**
  he should buy — the same gear hunt as his same-day HD-test uploads.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "cut and paste has been a big thing missing from the previous generation iPhone"

> "flash support on the iPhone what everyone wants their iPhone 3G to support Flash"

> "if Apple were to put the iPhone on more than one network they would definitely get a lot more money out of it"
