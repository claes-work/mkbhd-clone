# Camstudio Clarity (@mkbhd, 2009-01-30)

## Summary
A short follow-up/correction from age-15 Marques: he re-records to prove that **CamStudio does record
in good ("HQ") quality**, after his earlier CamStudio HQ tutorial ironically "came out in crap
quality." He diagnoses the cause: he'd run the good-quality recording through **Windows Movie Maker**
and, when he published/exported it to **720p**, it got "stretched out" from his native recording
resolution up to 1280×720, which "ruined it." His takeaway is a pointed early production lesson —
**don't use Windows Movie Maker** — and he tells viewers to click the "HQ" link at the bottom of the
video. He confirms CamStudio can capture at a perfectly watchable ~10–15 frames per second for
YouTube and promises to cover a better (non–Movie Maker) freeware tool later.

## Key claims
- [2009-01-30] **CamStudio does record in good/HQ quality** — this video exists to prove the point
  after his prior CamStudio tutorial rendered in poor quality; viewers should click the **"HQ" link**
  at the bottom of the screen.
- [2009-01-30] Root cause of the earlier bad quality: he processed the recording in **Windows Movie
  Maker** and exporting/"publishing" it to **720p** stretched the native recording up to 1280×720 and
  degraded it — "that ruined it."
- [2009-01-30] Production verdict: **"I would not recommend ... Windows Movie Maker is not good"** —
  download a different freeware program instead (he promises a future tutorial on a better tool).
- [2009-01-30] CamStudio captures ~**10–15 frames per second**, "perfectly suitable for watching
  YouTube videos."
