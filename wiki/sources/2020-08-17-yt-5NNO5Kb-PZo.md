# Dope Tech: The iPad Pro Killer?! (@mkbhd, 2020-08-17)

## Summary
A three-item Dope Tech mixing "great stuff and weird stuff." Item 1: Xiaomi's smart 20W wireless charging pad (launched alongside the Mi 10 Ultra) — an oversized pad whose one gimmick is a motorized coil that physically moves inside to find and align with whatever device you drop anywhere on it; Marques likes it despite it charging only one device at a time (it moves to a second device once the first finishes). Item 2: the Hachi Infinite M1, an openly-sponsored projector Marques calls the smartest he's ever seen — a Snapdragon 670 / Android 9 "InfiniteOS" tablet-projector that turns any wall or flat surface into a responsive 10-point multi-touch screen, with AI/camera features (spelling recognition, a guided "future kitchen," a form-correcting fitness app), a battery for portability, and multiplayer surface games; ~$999. Item 3, the headline "iPad Pro killer": the Samsung Galaxy Tab S7+ — which he admits is "a bit generous" to call a killer, but which earns Dope Tech almost entirely on its screen: a 12-inch 16:10 120Hz Super AMOLED at full quad-HD resolution, the best screen on any tablet including the iPad. He praises the thin metal design, landscape-centered webcam, side fingerprint reader, and the S Pen (while ribbing Samsung for "copying Apple's homework" with a weaker magnetic attach), but flags the usual unoptimized-Android-tablet-apps caveat.

## Key claims
- [2020-08-17] Xiaomi released the smart wireless charging pad alongside the Mi 10 Ultra (which Marques notes is "also a mirror"). (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] The pad's single gimmick: it's a 20W wireless charger whose internal coil motorizes to find and align to a device placed anywhere on the large pad — you hear the motors move it into position, removing the pain of aligning a charger manually. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] It does not charge multiple devices simultaneously; the only "multi-device" behavior is that after finishing the first device it moves to a second one, so it can eventually top up several items left on it overnight. Marques still likes it and leaves it on his desk for quick top-ups. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] Sponsorship disclosed on-screen: "Hachi reached out and they wanted to sponsor a spot in a Dope Tech episode" — the Hachi Infinite M1 segment is a paid placement, stated plainly by Marques. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] The Hachi Infinite M1 is "the smartest projector I have ever seen": a Snapdragon 670 with up to 6GB RAM and 128GB storage running Android 9 customized as "InfiniteOS" — effectively a whole Android tablet inside a projector, with cameras, front/top sensors, and a gyroscope. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] It projects onto a wall or down onto a desk/table, auto-calibrates and sharpens, and turns the projection into a responsive 10-point multi-touch surface — more responsive than Marques expected; the main limitation is not casting a shadow on the button you're touching (approach from the light side). (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] AI/camera-driven apps: a spelling-education mode that reads physical plastic letters via image recognition; a "future kitchen" that projects recipes onto a counter/cutting board and recognizes scanned ingredients; a fitness app that projects a workout on one side while the camera corrects form and counts reps. It has a built-in battery for portability, and supports multiplayer surface games (tic-tac-toe, Pong, air hockey). ~$999. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] The Samsung Galaxy Tab S7+ is the bigger of the S7 / S7+ pair; calling it an "iPad Pro killer" is "a bit generous," but Marques notes premium Android tablets have little competition now, and this one has the goods. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] Specs/design: Snapdragon 865+ (5G-capable), up to 8GB RAM and 512GB storage, a very thin metal body with sharp edges, quad speakers, and a large battery. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] Thoughtful design choices vs the iPad: the front camera sits centered on the *landscape* edge (where you actually use a tablet), and the fingerprint reader is on the right for sideways double-tap unlock; bezels are thin but not too thin, leaving room for thumbs. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] The S Pen (9ms response, low latency, Note-series tech) is a favorite quirk, but Marques says Samsung "copied Apple's homework but changed it up just a little bit": it attaches/charges only in one orientation, the magnets aren't as strong as the iPad's (can't attach it blind), and it props the tablet up when laid flat. He recommends the case as a top accessory. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] The reason it makes Dope Tech is the screen: a 12-inch 16:10 120Hz Super AMOLED running full quad-HD resolution at 120Hz, very bright with excellent colors — "the best screen on any tablet including the iPad" and the ideal media machine. (Marques)
- [2020-08-17] Caveat: it's still an Android tablet, so expect partially unoptimized, oddly-vertical apps — worth it if you'll tolerate that "part of the Android tablet experience," but the screen is the draw. (Marques)
