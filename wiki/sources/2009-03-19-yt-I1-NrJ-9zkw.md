# Media Center Remote Part 2 (@mkbhd, 2009-03-19)

## Summary
A follow-up (Marques, age 15) to an earlier video on the **HP Media Center remote**, this time
shot **on a real camera** ("HQ") rather than a screencast, so he can film the laptop screen
while pressing the remote's buttons — something the built-in webcam couldn't show. Running
**Windows Vista Home Premium 64-bit** on his **HP Pavilion dv7t** laptop, he presses the green
Media Center button (picked up by the laptop's IR receiver) and tours **Windows Media Center**:
the music library (searchable, CoverFlow-like animated browsing, internet radio), the
pictures/video library (folder-aware, full-screen viewing), and a **Sports** section that pulls
**live scores from Fox Sports without a TV tuner**, plus an **online media** guide of streamable
shows. He walks the remote's transport/navigation buttons and mentions **HP QuickPlay** as a
separate future video. He repeatedly apologizes for shake (no tripod yet) and mic quality —
early signs of his hardware-and-production upgrade path.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-19] Demos **Windows Media Center** on an **HP Pavilion dv7t** (Vista Home Premium
  64-bit) driven by the **HP IR Media Center remote**: music, photos/video, and a Sports panel.
- [2009-03-19] **Windows Media Center pulls live sports scores (Fox Sports) and online media
  without a TV tuner** — a feature he highlights as genuinely useful and free/built-in to Vista.
- [2009-03-19] **Production shift:** he is now filming hardware **with a camera** (not a
  screencast), explicitly to show on-screen results of physical button presses — but notes he
  has **no tripod yet** and the mic/quality is rough (early gear-limitation era).

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
