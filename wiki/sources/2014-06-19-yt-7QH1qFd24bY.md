# LG G3 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-06-19)

## Summary
The **full LG G3 review** — the third and concluding video in his G3 arc (Impressions → QHD Display
Review → this). It **answers the diminishing-returns question** he'd set up: pushing 2560×1440 does
**not** meaningfully hurt battery life or performance (battery "actually excellent," performance
identical to 1080p flagships), while the QHD sharpness is "a couple of years out" from having content
that uses it. Praises the design (thin bezels, rear buttons, laser AF), dings a **thermal
brightness/shutdown throttle**, and lands a verdict sharpened by an honest **taste-disclosure**: he'd
have left the G3 off his best-of-2014 list at the start of the year (not an LG-skin fan), but "now that
this is out… it's clearly easily one of the best phones that's come out this year, if not the best."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-06-19] **The QHD verdict — no performance/battery penalty.** "There were concerns about whether
  this display would have an impact on battery life… as far as I can tell, no." Battery was excellent
  (4.5 hrs screen-on on a road trip; superb standby), gaming/scrolling/frame-rates identical to 1080p
  flagships. The extra sharpness's real payoff is "a couple of years out" until apps/games use it — "right
  now it's just kind of a spec line to get you to look at the phone."
- [2014-06-19] **Design is the real draw** — a handsome plastic-that-fakes-metal body, a comfortable
  curved back, intuitive rear buttons (with asleep-shortcuts), and **thin bezels** that make a 5.5" phone
  compact. Honest aside: "you could've given me a 1080p display here with this thin a bezel and I'd
  probably still be just as happy."
- [2014-06-19] **Laser autofocus is a standout** — "one of the fastest focusing cameras I've used in a
  smartphone"; simple camera UI (no manual modes), fist-gesture selfie, up to 4K.
- [2014-06-19] **Software = his standing skin critique, applied fairly.** The LG skin is "okay… better
  than the G2's"; he praises the keyboard, the multi-item clipboard ("every phone should adopt this"),
  and knock-code, but dings the cluttered notification bar and the "awful to navigate" settings app.
  Discloses his bias ("you know how I feel about Android skins already").
- [2014-06-19] **The one real hardware con: thermal throttling.** Screenshots of the phone refusing to
  brighten (and once threatening to shut down in Google Maps navigation) to cool itself — "sometimes it
  does heat up a little bit more than other phones I've used."
- [2014-06-19] **The taste-disclosure verdict.** "If you'd asked me at the beginning of this year what
  the best phones of 2014 would be, I might have left the LG G3 off the list" (he'd have gone Galaxy/Note/
  HTC One) — "but now that this is out… it's clearly easily one of the best phones that's come out this
  year, if not the best, especially because of the display." A dated instance of updating a stated
  position with evidence and naming his prior bias.
