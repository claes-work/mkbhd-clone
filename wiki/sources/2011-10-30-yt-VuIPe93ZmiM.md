# Top 5 Features of Android 4.0 [Ice Cream Sandwich] (@mkbhd, 2011-10-30)

## Summary
A countdown of his five most-anticipated features in Android 4.0, published before the
Galaxy Nexus shipped. Ranked: **5.** the UI overhaul and the new Roboto font, **4.** the
rebuilt browser with desktop bookmark sync and 16-tab browsing, **3.** Face Unlock,
**2.** Android Beam (NFC tap-to-share), **1.** the new camera (zero shutter lag, built-in
panorama, 1080p recording, in-gallery editing), with hardware acceleration as an honorable
mention. The framing throughout is anticipation rather than review — he has not yet used
the device, states he will buy it on day one, and repeatedly says what he's "looking
forward to."

## Key claims
- [2011-10-30] **Purchase intent, dated and specific**: "I'm definitely gonna be reviewing
  the device and I will be picking it up on day one at the Verizon store two blocks that
  way" — Verizon Galaxy Nexus, day one, bought (not seeded), and geographically near his
  campus.
- [2011-10-30] **UI/typography opinion**: the black-and-cyan high-contrast scheme is "very
  easy on the eyes" and the Roboto font gives "a very relaxed professional layout"; the
  word he returns to for the whole ICS aesthetic is **professional**. An early instance of
  him treating typography and visual consistency as reviewable substance.
- [2011-10-30] **Face Unlock assessed by second-order consequence, not the feature itself**:
  he's interested less in unlocking than in "the possibility of the new API — what
  developers can do with that front facing camera." Also flags a usability regression: he
  doesn't think you keep the direct camera-from-lockscreen shortcut with it.
- [2011-10-30] Browser: bookmark/favorite sync from desktop and up-to-16 tabs land in stock
  Android for the first time; he notes this is "not Chrome for Android yet" but reads the
  direction of travel correctly.
- [2011-10-30] Camera ranked #1 with specifics: **zero shutter lag** ("tap tap tap tap…
  no shutter lag, no animations to slow you down"), built-in panorama with 360 capability,
  and **1080p recording finally standard** for any ICS phone with a capable sensor, plus a
  gallery photo editor.
- [2011-10-30] **Dated college marker**: he plans to test Android Beam "with some of my
  Android 4.0 buddies down the hall" — consistent with the dorm-room setting stated three
  days earlier in [[2011-09-27-yt-8W3bJGflOic]].
- [2011-10-30] **Audience even-handedness restated**: "I know some of you guys are looking
  at me like, well, I use an iPhone, so I'm not looking forward to it — and that's fine."
  Same posture as the 2011-10-09 dream-phone video.
- [2011-10-30] **Possible external-channel contribution (uncertain)**: he references "the
  video I did for TLD today where I explain all the new features of ICS." Caption-derived;
  most plausibly TLDToday (Jon Rettinger's channel), which would date an early
  guest/contributor appearance off his own channel. **Not confirmed** — flagged for
  verification before any biography use.
