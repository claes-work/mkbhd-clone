# Asus Transformer Prime Dock! (@mkbhd, 2012-02-18)

## Summary
A follow-up made because the review generated one recurring question — "it's called the *Transformer*
after all, so what exactly does it transform into?" He reviews the keyboard dock, then spends the
back half on the question underneath it: **can a tablet plus a keyboard replace a laptop?** His answer
is a use-case framework rather than a yes/no, and he lands on a comparison that reframes the whole
product: the Transformer Prime is "really a quad-core Chromebook."

## Key claims
- [2012-02-18] **The answer is a framework, not a verdict**: "look at what you do on your laptop. If you
  do pretty light consumption right now — watching YouTube videos, answering emails, word processing,
  sometimes some light photo editing — you're gonna be just fine." Then the boundary: "you won't have
  full-size apps like Microsoft Word or Photoshop… but if you don't need that, look in the Android
  market, and if you find the apps that you want and they actually work for you, you'll be set." Same
  shape as the feature-first phone-buying framework from [[2012-02-05-yt-Ktoalz6PbXc]].
- [2012-02-18] **The reframing comparison**: "the laptop the Transformer Prime most closely resembles is
  the Samsung Chromebook. I mean, if you think about it, the Transformer Prime is really a **quad-core
  Chromebook**."
- [2012-02-18] **Measured against the right rival, feature by feature**: instant-on (ultrabooks with SSDs
  resume in 2–4 seconds; the Prime "in maybe less than one second"), thinness and weight parity with
  ultrabooks, and price — "the Transformer Prime and the dock put together still probably cost less than
  the machine you're watching this on."
- [2012-02-18] **The dock's genuinely good parts, specifically**: a **full-size USB port** ("yes,
  full size — I'm looking at you, other tablet"), Android-specific keys (home, search, menu, arrows), a
  top row of toggles (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, trackpad lock, brightness, camera, browser, settings, media),
  and a **second internal battery that discharges first** — "use a tablet for hours while it's plugged
  in, pop off the keyboard and still have 100% battery left on the tablet."
- [2012-02-18] **The nitpick he admits is a nitpick — and keeps anyway**: "laptops built to be laptops
  are weighted and built just right to open up just like in the commercials. That is sexy. Tablets that
  aren't built to be laptops, not so much. I mean, maybe I'm nitpicking now, but that happened to bug me
  a lot." A physical-interaction complaint no spec sheet captures, flagged for what it is.
- [2012-02-18] **Reports his own expectations being exceeded**: "I found myself flying through the UI…
  using the hardware keys in a way I wouldn't have even thought of before having this keyboard,"
  with multi-touch trackpad gestures on ICS called "especially impressive," and the ability to use
  touchscreen and keyboard simultaneously described as "a very dynamic, wicked, one-of-a-kind
  experience."
- [2012-02-18] Content origin stated twice: made from comment volume after the full review, and "thanks
  to all of your comments, I was actually wondering to myself: can this replace a laptop?"
