# Blackberry KEYOne: 2017 Comeback? (@mkbhd, 2017-05-11)

## Summary
Review of the BlackBerry KEYone — "the best smartphone with a physical keyboard, and the best BlackBerry ever
made." A good example of his **judge-it-for-its-audience** discipline (he's "not in the target demographic"
but reviews it fairly), a five-pillars snapshot, the **"poor use of space"** callback, and appreciation of
the keyboard's deep customization.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-05-11] **"Not in the target demographic" — but judge it fairly.** "This is a phone for people who
  really want a physical keyboard or really want a BlackBerry — I'm neither, but I gave it a shot anyway."
  His who-is-this-for discipline (separate personal taste from the verdict).
- [2017-05-11] **Built like a tank — and the "poor use of space" callback.** "Build quality is top-notch,
  all-metal, tight tolerances, chamfered — more drop-resistant than the S8 or G6. But no water resistance,
  and no wireless charging: in a 9 mm-thick phone they probably could've found space for that — you know
  what I'm going to say already, **poor use of space.**" His HTC-U-Ultra refrain, reused.
- [2017-05-11] **The five-pillars snapshot.** "Here's how it does on my five pillars: not spectacular, but
  not bad for a $550 phone — upper-mid-tier. But 'not bad' doesn't sell anyone; a phone like this needs an
  over-the-top specialty." Applies the rubric, then names what a niche phone needs.
- [2017-05-11] **The specialty is customization (via the keyboard).** "Its over-the-top feature is
  customization: the remappable Convenience key (I set mine to Google Assistant), and — thanks to the
  physical keyboard — you can map *any letter* to launch any app on a short *or* long press. Short-press 'Y'
  → YouTube; a totally clean home screen. Plus the keyboard doubles as a trackpad (scroll without your finger
  covering content), real Ctrl-C/Ctrl-V shortcuts, swipe-to-autocomplete, swipe-back-to-delete-a-word, and
  the space bar is the fingerprint reader — good use of space there." Genuinely impressed by the depth.
- [2017-05-11] **The honest verdict.** "This is the best keyboard phone and the best BlackBerry ever — but
  for me the customization doesn't outweigh giving up that much screen to a keyboard I probably won't use.
  How bad do you want a physical keyboard or a BlackBerry? It's on you." A decide-by-your-own-values close.
  Notes the display is 1080p "in a Quad HD world" and dim — but "fits the $550 price."
