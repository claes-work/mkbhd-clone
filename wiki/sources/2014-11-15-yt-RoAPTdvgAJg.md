# Google Nexus 6 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-11-15)

## Summary
The full Nexus 6 review — "a really really big **Moto X running stock Android**," and the phone he makes
his daily driver "at least for now." It's a clean anchor for several doctrines: **benchmarks-higher ≠
performs-better** (anti-benchmark), **grade-on-a-curve** (an explicit battery letter grade: C+ absolute /
B+ among QHD phones), and **fair-to-fixable** ("I tend to be less harsh on things I believe can be fixed
with software updates"). Verdict: not the best QHD phone (the Note 4 is), but the best if what you want is
**pure stock Android 5.0** at a big size.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-11-15] **"A really really big Moto X running stock Android."** Best hardware for the new software:
  the Moto-X metal band + improved dimple/buttons, a display that "melts over the sides" (drag on/off for
  Lollipop's edge gestures) with a grippable non-slippery band. Downside: the round back "won't sit still
  on a flat surface, which drives me crazy — I text with my phone flat on a desk all the time."
- [2014-11-15] **Dual front-facing speakers** (an upgrade over the Moto X's single) — loud, clear,
  directional, "not quite BoomSound" but far better than rear/downward-firing.
- [2014-11-15] **Display good, not best** — 5.96" QHD AMOLED, 493 ppi, bright/sharp/saturated, "one of the
  best but not the best — I'd still give that to the Note 4." Honest flaw: it goes "noticeably super pink"
  at low brightness ("no, that's not a feature — it's just not as color-accurate as some panels").
- [2014-11-15] **Anti-benchmark, restated.** "Even though it benchmarks higher than a lot of other
  phones, it doesn't perform better than a lot of those phones" — because it's pushing QHD pixels; "don't
  expect it to blow your mind just because the benchmark score is so high." First-gen-QHD stutter (like
  the first 1080p wave).
- [2014-11-15] **Grade-on-a-curve, made explicit.** Battery (3200 mAh, ~3–3.5 hr screen-on vs ~4–4.5 on a
  1080p OnePlus One): "**battery grade I'd give it something like a C+**… but if you grade it on a curve
  vs all the QHD phones, Note 4 gets an A+ and the Nexus 6 gets a B+." Turbocharging is the real remedy.
- [2014-11-15] **Fair-to-fixable.** "I tend to be less harsh on things that I believe can be fixed with
  software updates — and since it's a Nexus, it's first in line for updates." (A dated statement of the
  principle.)
- [2014-11-15] **Camera = "most improved."** The Moto-X 13 MP + OIS with fast stock-camera shutter — "this
  isn't a disappointing [Nexus] camera anymore at all," though tap-to-expose is aggressive (limited
  dynamic range) and low-light focus hunts. Shoots 4K with OIS.
- [2014-11-15] **Verdict: for the Android purists.** "This isn't the best QHD phone — I'd give that to the
  Note 4 (better display, better battery)… but you have to deal with TouchWiz. This is a phone for the
  Android heads who want pure stock 5.0 and don't mind the size — if you're into it for that, this is the
  best phone you can buy right now." $649 to start; his daily driver "at least for now."
