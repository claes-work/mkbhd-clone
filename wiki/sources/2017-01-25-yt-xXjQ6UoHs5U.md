# My First YouTube Camera! (@mkbhd, 2017-01-25)

## Summary
Prompted by a Reddit-AMA question about gear, he tracks down and revisits his **first YouTube camera** —
the Sanyo Xacti CG10 — and lays out his **complete camera lineage**. The clone-relevant core is his
signature **anti-gear creator advice**: "it's not about the equipment — start making videos with what you
have now, and the rest will come." A high-value biography + creator-business node.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-01-25] **The complete camera lineage (biography).** From a Reddit AMA gear question: "I don't
  recommend what I use to most people — a RED Weapon or any cine camera is total overkill for the web."
  The lineage, newest to oldest: **RED Weapon Helium (8K) → RED Epic (6K) → RED Scarlet → Canon C100 (the
  college apartment) → Canon 5D Mark II → Canon 60D → Canon T2i (his first DSLR) → the Sanyo Xacti CG10
  (720p, his first YouTube camera).** A definitive gear-history record.
- [2017-01-25] **THE CREATOR-ADVICE THESIS: "start with what you have."** "The moral of this tiny story is
  it's not all about the quality of the video — it's about what's in the video itself. People trying to get
  started don't have to buy a great camera; you could start shooting on the phone you're watching this on.
  The best advice I can give is not about what equipment to buy — it's just to **start making videos with
  what you have now, and the rest will come. Get out there and create.**" His core, oft-repeated creator
  philosophy, stated cleanly.
- [2017-01-25] **"I use this stuff because I love it" — not because you should.** "I use the RED Weapon
  because I love it (I made a Dope Tech video about it), but for most people it's not a good choice." The
  bleeding-edge-honesty / separate-my-bias-from-the-recommendation trait applied to his own gear.
- [2017-01-25] **Digital zoom is fake (restated).** The CG10 "advertised 60× digital zoom, but we all know
  how I feel about digital zoom — it's about as fake as the leather on the side of this camera. Optical zoom
  is real and useful." A durable camera-literacy stance.
- [2017-01-25] **The blur-background trick (teach-the-mechanism).** "Zoom in as far as you can while getting
  as close to your subject as possible — it works for any lens, even the CG10." A practical craft tip.
