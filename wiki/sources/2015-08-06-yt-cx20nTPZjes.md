# Sony RX100 IV Review: Pocket 4K! (@mkbhd, 2015-08-06)

## Summary
A review of the Sony RX100 IV — "the sequel to the best pocket camera ever made, two years running" — and
a **production/gear** landmark for its **4K video** ("seriously beautiful — none of that upscaled 1080p or
oversharpened smartphone video"). He literally shot his Moto X Style video on it (S-Log, low light). But
he's honest about a real flaw: **4K is limited to 5 minutes because it overheats** (it shut down on him at
the Motorola event), plus slow video autofocus and no audio input. Verdict: **the best hybrid pocket
photo/video camera in the world, but not the "perfect YouTube camera."**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-08-06] **The 4K is the headline.** "I love the 4K from the RX100 IV — seriously beautiful, none of
  that upscaled 1080p or oversharpened smartphone video." OIS, good dynamic range, shallow depth of field —
  "the best-looking videos, the best-looking vlogs in the business." He shot his last video (the Moto X
  Style impressions) on it in S-Log for grading latitude in low light.
- [2015-08-06] **Honest about the overheating flaw.** "You're limited to 5 minutes of 4K at a time — not
  for processing reasons, but heat… in the middle of my third clip it shut down from overheating and
  refused to turn on for 10 minutes" (it happened live at the Motorola event). Also **slow video autofocus**
  (a recurring RX100 gripe — it refused to focus on a non-face subject) and **no audio input / a mediocre
  built-in mic**.
- [2015-08-06] **Not "the perfect YouTube camera" — and you don't need one.** "A lot of people say this is
  basically the perfect camera to start a YouTube channel — **you don't have to buy a $1,000 camera to
  start**. If I'm nitpicking, it isn't the perfect YouTube camera because of the slow AF, the 5-min 4K
  overheating limit, and no audio input… if you want to shoot 4K for long periods you might as well get the
  Mark III [1080p]." Verdict: **"the best hybrid pocket photo/video camera in the world," "one generation
  away from something really incredible"** (he predicts the Mark V will fix overheating + add audio-in).
