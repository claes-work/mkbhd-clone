# Five OnePlus 2 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-07-28)

## Summary
Pre-launch impressions after two weeks with the OnePlus 2 (using it alongside his S6 Edge daily driver).
Five takeaways: the home-button fingerprint reader (a non-clicking touchpad, >90% accurate); **going 1080p
was a smart battery move** ("may disappoint some members of Team Crispy, but it's a really good move for
battery" — it outlasts his S6 Edge); the **USB-C tradeoff** (no quick charging → ~3 hr charge); the
**Snapdragon 810 doesn't overheat** (warm during heavy gaming, but never throttled); and a clean, near-
stock Oxygen OS 2 (with a handy alert-slider and per-app permissions).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-07-28] **Fingerprint reader — right spot, touchpad not button.** On the home button (like the
  iPhone/S6), "the most convenient place," but a non-moving touchpad (like the M9+). >90% accurate; can
  wake the phone from off; you can remap long-press/double-tap of any bottom button ("which is **dope**").
- [2015-07-28] **1080p was a smart battery choice.** "All the hoopla about the Snapdragon 810… going with
  a 1080p display, while it may disappoint some members of **Team Crispy**, is actually a really good move
  for battery." ~4.5 hr screen-on, 2-day standby — "it is lasting longer on battery than the Galaxy S6."
  (A benchmark showed the OnePlus One still king; the 2 gets "a B-minus for now.")
- [2015-07-28] **USB-C: the quick-charge tradeoff.** One of the first phones with USB-C (see his USB-C
  explainer) — new charger needed, and **no quick charging**: ~3 hr from 25% to full (he used his Chromebook
  Pixel charger). "Spoiled by the S6's Quick Charge 2.0… but it shouldn't matter if I'm only charging once
  a day."
- [2015-07-28] **The Snapdragon 810 thermals — no problem.** "Does it overheat, is it going to burn me?
  So far, nope." Warm after long gaming (his S6 gets just as warm), but "never limited performance or
  functionality" — unlike the LG G3, which throttled brightness when hot.
- [2015-07-28] **Oxygen OS 2 is clean.** Near-stock + a physical **alert slider** (all/priority/DND, "I use
  it at least once a day"), rearrangeable quick-settings, a system-wide dark theme, and Android-M-style
  **per-app permissions** even though it's not on M.
