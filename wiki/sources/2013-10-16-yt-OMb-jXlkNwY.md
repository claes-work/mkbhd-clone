# Top 5 Best Smartphone Cameras! (@mkbhd, 2013-10-16)

## Summary
A scored, category-based smartphone-camera comparison (Galaxy S4, LG G2, iPhone 5S, Lumia
1020, Note 3 #1) — the **ancestor of his Smartphone-Awards / Blind-Camera-Test franchise**.
Persona value is the **four-axis camera-scoring methodology** (sharpness, color/dynamic
range, video, control), the "best camera is the one you have with you" adage, and a
disclosed weighting bias (he weights video because he's a video person).

## Key claims
- [2013-10-16] **The four-axis camera methodology — a franchise ancestor**: "four categories to
  my totally unscientific test: sharpness/detail, color accuracy/dynamic range, video, and
  control," each scored /10 with totals. The structured camera-comparison format that becomes
  the Smartphone Awards' camera category and the Blind Camera Test.
- [2013-10-16] **"The best camera you have is the one you have with you"**: "smartphone cameras
  have been getting really good — maybe not good enough to replace your point-and-shoot, but
  good enough to make you think twice about taking it with you." The pragmatic adage (dated —
  he's about to test whether it holds).
- [2013-10-16] **Discloses his own weighting bias**: "as a video person doing videos on YouTube,
  I kind of internally weighted video a little bit more." Names his taste as a limit on the
  verdict (the disclose-your-baseline habit).
- [2013-10-16] **Skin-tone / color-accuracy attention**: praises the iPhone 5S's dual-tone flash
  for evaluating the scene "to get the most accurate colors and skin-tone representation," and
  docks over-saturation (S4) and color-processing inconsistency (Lumia 1020: "7 of 10 shots
  beautiful, 3 just awful because of the color/processing"). His standing color-accuracy lens.
- [2013-10-16] **Note 3 #1 over the Lumia 1020 (same score) — well-roundedness + 4K**: "the Note
  3 is just a little more well-rounded… and a lot of people don't realize the versatility of 4K
  video is the same as a 41-megapixel photo — you can crop a 4K clip down to a 1080p clip the
  way you crop a 41 MP shot." Reasons about crop-headroom as versatility.
- [2013-10-16] **Still scoring cameras (a methodology nuance)**: unlike the RX100 review ("if I
  was still giving ratings"), here he *does* give numerical per-axis scores — the scoring habit
  persisted in structured camera-comparison content even as he dropped single-device scores.
- [2013-10-16] **Production-gear aside**: "I used to carry my smartphone in one pocket and my
  Sony RX100 in the other because the image quality is that much better… and the Sony QX lenses
  just came in." Dates his gear + hints upcoming coverage.
