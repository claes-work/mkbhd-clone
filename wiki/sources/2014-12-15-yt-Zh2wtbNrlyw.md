# Top 5 Wearable Tech! (Late 2014) (@mkbhd, 2014-12-15)

## Summary
A year-end wearables ranking that bookends his early-2014 list and marks how far the category moved:
where first-gen wearables could only *tell* you about a notification, **Android Wear now lets you take
action**. His **#1 is the Moto 360** ("might be the best-looking smartwatch, period"). The video also
carries the **Apple-drives-competition** point — the Apple Watch (announced, not yet shipping) is still a
question mark, but "once Apple gets into things, that's what really gets the competition going and makes
better products for us."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-12-15] **The category's step forward:** early-2014 wearables couldn't act on notifications; **the
  arrival of Android Wear** (plus several software updates since Google I/O) is the main difference now.
- [2014-12-15] **The list:** #5 **Samsung Gear S** (Tizen, big/bold, notably works untethered — even has
  a web browser); #4 **Pebble / Pebble Steel** (a returner — 7-day battery, focused notifications, Steel
  more premium); #3 **Asus ZenWatch** (best square — "strikes the balance best" between watch and gadget);
  #2 **LG G Watch R** (round, notches + numbers, "looks a lot closer to a normal watch"); **#1 Moto 360**
  (round, metal option, near-zero bezel — the one he fully reviewed; "might be the best-looking smartwatch
  period," the bottom bar "melts away" after a day).
- [2014-12-15] **Apple-drives-competition (the durable point).** The Apple Watch "is a question mark for
  me in terms of function… but we all know that once Apple tends to get into things, that's what really
  gets the competition going, and that makes better products for us." (Even-handed: competition raises the
  whole field.)
- [2014-12-15] **Google Glass watch:** dropped off from the early-2014 list; a cheaper consumer version
  "we just straight up never saw" ($1,500 still), possibly resurrected in 2015 with Intel internals.
