# Driving Tesla Cybertruck: Everything You Need to Know! (@mkbhd, 2023-12-01)

## Summary
Marques's **second impressions and first-ever drive** of a near-final Tesla Cybertruck — he
was one of the "prototype" trucks people spotted testing, for a day. Explicitly **not a
review**: he hasn't towed, off-roaded, road-tripped, or lived with it, but he has an order
and plans to. He walks the whole truck — specs, the stainless-steel body and panel-gap
lottery, the lined bed with onboard power, the handle-less doors, the world's-largest
windshield and wiper, the all-new Cyber UI software — then drives it. His headline take:
the **steer-by-wire system with rear-axle steering** is "the craziest thing about the
Cybertruck," crazier than the design. Verdict-in-progress: impressed, with the two real
downsides being the polarizing looks and that it's **more expensive than expected** (~$100k
triple-motor, no longer the price advantage people assumed). Solo, Marques-fronted → persona/
voice data. Companion to his earlier Auto Focus Cybertruck-reveal video. Disclosed **Ridge**
sponsor read.

## Key claims
- [2023-12-01] Frames it as **second impressions / first drive, NOT a review** — hasn't towed,
  hauled, off-roaded, or road-tripped; has an order and will do the full review later. A
  recurring MKBHD discipline: separate a hands-on impression from a lived-with verdict.
- [2023-12-01] **Specs**: three configs — triple-motor "Beast" (2 rear + 1 front motors,
  0–60 in 2.7s, ~845 hp, ~$100k), dual-motor (~$75–85k, 600+ hp), later single-motor RWD.
  ~340-mile max range; ~224 in bumper-to-bumper (~5% smaller than the show truck). Calls it
  the fastest production pickup ever.
- [2023-12-01] **Price is the story**: "I am driving a hundred thousand dollars truck right
  now… it's no longer an advantage that we might have thought it was gonna be." The cheaper-
  than-rivals pitch evaporated.
- [2023-12-01] **Stainless-steel body**: giant flat panels, near-zero curves, sharp corners,
  heavy fingerprints. Panel gaps vary truck-to-truck — his test unit "actually looks great"
  but he "can't promise that for every truck." Engineers explained the spring-back problem of
  stamping stainless.
- [2023-12-01] **Bed/utility**: 6-ft lined bed (Tesla lined it after construction workers said
  they'd spray-line the bare steel anyway), powered tonneau (rated ~300 lb, he stands on it),
  sub-trunk with drain plug, 120V + 240V outlets and up to 11 kW out (can power tools / charge
  another EV / theoretically a house). No spare tire standard.
- [2023-12-01] **Doors have NO handles** — a button push-presents the door ~2 inches; Tesla
  claims it breaks through up to an inch of ice (he's skeptical: "this is a California company
  that tells me these things… I hope that's true"). Predicts lots of fingerprinted Cybertrucks.
- [2023-12-01] **Software**: all-new Cyber UI on an 18.5-inch center screen (biggest in any
  Tesla/car). Real-time car visualization on "cyber terrain," live ride-height/window/light
  animation. His standing view: "**Tesla has the best software in the car industry that's not
  CarPlay and Android Auto.**"
- [2023-12-01] **Steering is the standout**: first Tesla with **steer-by-wire**, fixed ratio,
  **less than 180° lock-to-lock** (~170°) plus up to 10° of **rear-wheel steer** (opposite
  phase under 40 mph to shrink the wheelbase; same phase above 40 for stability). "Crazier than
  the design… crazier than everything else about it is the steering." Requires a muscle-memory
  remap (as every recent Tesla has).
- [2023-12-01] Other notes: 800V architecture (faster V4 supercharging, thinner/lighter wiring)
  + 48V low-voltage system (4× the normal 12V); optional add-on bed battery adds ~120 mi
  (~400–450 mi total) at a big weight cost; laminated, shatterproof, allegedly bulletproof
  windows he declines to test; no camera-feed rear-view mirror, so with the tonneau closed you
  glance at the always-on screen instead.
- [2023-12-01] **Pros**: sheer straight-line force; the tight, nimble steering. **Cons**: the
  looks will polarize ("stainless steel refrigerator on wheels"); the price.
