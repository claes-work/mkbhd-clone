# LG G Flex Self Healing Demo! (@mkbhd, 2013-11-18)

## Summary
A focused hands-on test of the LG G Flex's self-healing back (after LG's viral demo). Low
persona yield — but a clean instance of his **test-the-claim-realistically** methodology:
he rejects LG's unrealistic brass-brush demo and re-tests with keys, the real-world
scenario. Filed at L2 for chronology and the methodology datum.

## Key claims
- [2013-11-18] **Skeptical of the vendor's demo — test it realistically**: "in their video they
  scratched it with a brass brush rubbing up and down with super-even strokes — I'm not convinced
  that translates to real life unless you walk around with a brillo pad or steel wool in your
  pocket. So I did a full test of my own, the way I see it being applicable: against your keys,
  because that's all I can think of that goes in the same pocket." His don't-take-the-marketing-
  demo-at-face-value habit, re-run against a real scenario.
- [2013-11-18] **Discloses the personal cost of the test**: "I really don't like intentionally
  scratching your devices, so I did this for you guys — thumbs up for that." A viewer-service +
  honest-about-the-testing aside.
- [2013-11-18] **Context — the curved-phone he was more excited about**: frames the G Flex as "quite
  possibly the most interesting phone in the world right now," the LG counterpart to the Galaxy
  Round he'd just covered. His new-form-factor curiosity.
