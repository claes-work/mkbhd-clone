# OnePlus X Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-12-01)

## Summary
The full OnePlus X review — **"the best really cheap smartphone you can buy right now."** For $250, OnePlus
delivers a genuinely premium metal-and-glass feel (chamfered rails, 2.5D curved glass front/back like the
iPhone 4S / Nexus 4), the beloved alert slider, and a **5" 1080p AMOLED that's "the best feature of the
phone by far"** (he "always loves OLED displays"). The glass makes it slippery and a fingerprint magnet —
so a skin (OnePlus even bundles a clear case, acknowledging it).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-12-01] **Best cheap phone — worth knowing exists.** "In my opinion the best really cheap smartphone
  you can buy right now… even if you're not going to buy it, it's still worth knowing a phone like this
  exists." The whole point is a premium feel at a low price.
- [2015-12-01] **Premium metal-and-glass, but slippery.** Ridged metal rails, chamfered edges, 2.5D curved
  glass front/back — "feels really nice, has some weight, doesn't bend — but glass is one of the slipperiest
  materials, so you'll probably want to slap a skin on it" (OnePlus bundles a clear case; a white version
  hides smudges).
- [2015-12-01] **Classic OnePlus hardware + the alert slider.** Snapdragon 801, Adreno 330, 3 GB ("awesome
  for a budget phone"), the power/volume on the right and the **alert slider on the left** ("really happy to
  see that"). A decent-but-distorts-at-max mono speaker.
- [2015-12-01] **The display is the standout.** A 5" 1080p **AMOLED** — "the best feature by far. I always
  love OLED displays; inky blacks, super-contrasty, vivid saturated colors, and pretty bright too — usually
  you see a big drop-off in the display at this price."
