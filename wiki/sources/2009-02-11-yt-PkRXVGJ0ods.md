# HD Review: CamStudio (@mkbhd, 2009-02-11)

## Summary
Origin-era software review of **CamStudio**, the free screen-recorder Marques (age 15)
was using to make his own HD tutorials — an explicitly self-referential "review of the
tool I'm using right now." He pitches it as a **stripped-down free alternative to
Camtasia Studio 6** with the basics (pause, light editing, annotations) and walks the
full options panel: region modes (regular / fixed / full-screen, recording 640×360 with
auto-pan; full screen captures his 1680×1050 monitor), the **DivX 6.8.5 codec** he calls
"pretty much the best HD recording codec for CamStudio" set to a **720 HD profile at
quality 100** to enable HD, auto-pan speed (set to 90 of a 0–200 range), cursor options,
mic/speaker audio with sync, screen annotations, and the three UI views. Notable as a
**primary-source record of MKBHD's actual early production stack**.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-11] **Review / recommendation:** CamStudio is a free, stripped-down
  alternative to Camtasia Studio 6 that still covers the basics (pause, light editing,
  annotations) for screen-recording YouTube tutorials.
- [2009-02-11] **Production tooling (self-reported):** records at 640×360 with auto-pan
  on; the **DivX 6.8.5 codec** at a 720 HD profile, quality 100, is what makes his videos
  look good when viewers hit the HD button; calls DivX 6.8.5 "the best HD recording codec
  for CamStudio."
- [2009-02-11] Recommends leaving "save settings on exit" checked and setting recording
  thread priority to normal so screen capture doesn't starve other programs' CPU.
