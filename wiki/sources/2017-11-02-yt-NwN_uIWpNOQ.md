# Space Gray Tesla Model S Wrap! (@mkbhd, 2017-11-02)

## Summary
He wraps his own Tesla Model S ("Apollo") in a space-gray/matte finish — a biography/appearance node in the
ongoing Apollo series, shot on the iPhone 8/8 Plus/X cameras. Explains **why wrap a car** (protection +
restyling, "like a dbrand skin but for the whole car") and continues weaving smartphone-camera testing into
his car content.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-11-02] **Why wrap a car (teach-the-mechanism).** "Two valid reasons: (1) protection — the Northeast
  roads aren't nice (gravel, potholes, rock chips, winter salt), so people put a clear 'clear bra' on the
  front where most damage happens; (2) a full-body wrap for **restyling / changing the paint color** — like
  what I've done with dbrand skins on phones, but a much larger surface, and it adds a protection layer you can
  remove whenever." A phones-to-cars analogy (his skins knowledge scaled up).
- [2017-11-02] **A biography/appearance detail — his car, restyled.** He got his Tesla wrapped after ~25,000
  miles (most people wrap right when they get the car); the new look is a matte space-gray finish. A dated
  personal-taste/ownership node (extends the "Apollo" arc; the matte-gray choice echoes his matte-black
  identity). The wrap can be removed anytime.
- [2017-11-02] **Continues the phone-camera-test-in-the-car-series conceit (production).** "Another video in
  the Tesla series — the one shot entirely on smartphones. This one's on the iPhone 8 / 8 Plus / X cameras
  (same back sensors; the front-facing stuff is from the iPhone X). I like including the front-facing camera
  more often — people want to see the selfie camera, and you can hear the audio quality, which we also have to
  talk about in smartphone video." His format device (test cameras while showing the car) + a note on judging
  a phone's *video audio*, not just stills.
