# Reviewed: Griffin Elevator [HD] (@mkbhd, 2009-06-26)

## Summary
Full review of the Griffin Elevator laptop stand, closing the unbox→review pair opened
by [[2009-05-30-yt-3qbUOlftiX8]] roughly **4 weeks earlier** — again well past his
stated 1–2-week unbox-to-review goal, continuing the recurring pattern flagged on prior
review pages. Corrects a "common misconception" that the stand is Mac-only (he's tested
it with both a 15" Sony VAIO and a 17" HP Pavilion, and Griffin's own site confirms
cross-platform support). Covers build (brushed aluminum, rubber grips top and bottom),
functional benefits (minor passive cooling from the airflow gap, raises the laptop to
external-monitor eye level), and a real trade-off named against himself: his 17" laptop
causes "a little bit of that bend" in the stand, which he calls minor. Scores it **8/10**
and confirms it will likely go into the pre-announced summer giveaway
([[2009-06-24-yt-T2SF1XmhsGM]]).

## Key claims
- [2009-06-26] **Corrects a stated misconception**: "a common misconception is that you
  can use the Griffin Elevator with a MacBook" only — he confirms cross-platform use
  with a 15" Sony VAIO and 17" HP Pavilion, and cites Griffin's own website ("you can
  use this with any laptop, Mac or PC").
- [2009-06-26] **Names a real limitation against his own unit**: his 17" laptop
  produces "a little bit of that bend" in the aluminum arm; he calls it "not a big
  issue" rather than hiding it.
- [2009-06-26] **Two secondary benefits identified**: passive cooling from airflow
  under the elevated laptop, and eye-level alignment with an external monitor when
  used with external mouse/keyboard.
- [2009-06-26] **Score: 8/10** — "which is pretty good for a hardware review from
  MKBHD," extending the origin scoring ladder (Alto Edge 9/10 → iFrogz D33 8.5/10 →
  GelaSkins 8/10 → Griffin Elevator 8/10).
- [2009-06-26] **Confirms it's giveaway stock**: "this will likely be a part of my
  humongous giveaway that is coming up in honor of my summer vacation" — direct
  follow-through on the giveaway announced two days earlier.
- [2009-06-26] **Unbox-to-review gap**: unboxed 2009-05-30, reviewed 2009-06-26 (~4
  weeks) — again exceeds the "1–2 week" goal stated on other unboxing pages.
