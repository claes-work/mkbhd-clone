# Samsung Galaxy Nexus Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-04-16)

## Summary
**The full review promised in December — delivered fifteen weeks later**, and the deferral is the
point. He opens by dating it precisely ("a little more than 15 weeks ago in December 2011… it's been
my primary handset since then") and calls it "the 100% honest review of the Android phone that I
waited so long for." It answers exactly the questions the first-impressions video said it couldn't:
long-term durability, real battery life, and how the device holds up after months of daily use. It
also documents a change of position — he abandoned stock Android for a custom ROM — and reports the
instability that came with it rather than hiding it.

## Key claims
- [2012-04-16] **The deferral honoured at fifteen weeks.** On 2011-12-15 he said the full review would
  come "in a couple weeks when I really get to know everything about this phone"
  ([[2011-12-15-yt-pVIWkvXvK98]]). It arrives at fifteen — later than promised, but with genuine
  long-term evidence, and the elapsed time is stated up front as the review's credential.
- [2012-04-16] **Long-term durability answers the launch criticism directly**: "the only real concern
  that critics of this phone had at launch — and also the Android haters — was Samsung's plastic back,
  and I'm happy to report that it's still really durable. It's not a problem. None of the glass on the
  back is cracked, it's still in excellent condition."
- [2012-04-16] **Display confirmed as the best feature after months of use**, consistent with display
  primacy: the 4.65" 720p Super AMOLED "has proved to be the best feature of the whole device…
  absolutely astounding… it amazes me literally every day. Some days I find myself using the Nexus to
  catch up on my YouTube subscriptions more than I do on my tablet or on my computer."
- [2012-04-16] **A documented position change — stock → custom ROM**: "I realized it was downright silly
  for me, someone who likes to take advantage of the customization of Android, to stay with stock for so
  long." Running Android 4.0.4 with the **AOKP ROM** and a **Franco kernel overclocked to 1.3 GHz**,
  giving notification-bar toggles and a customized navigation bar. Reverses the pure-stock preference he
  stated in Oct 2011 ([[2011-10-09-yt-mtBHnwok3rI]]) — see contradiction note below.
- [2012-04-16] **The cost of that choice, reported honestly**: "this isn't a stable CyanogenMod or
  anything, but you will notice the occasional force close… it opens apps quickly, it closes them
  quickly, **even crashes quicker**." He recommends the setup while naming its instability.
- [2012-04-16] **Hardware modification disclosed**: he bought a 2100 mAh extended battery shortly after
  launch, "just ever so slightly thicker than the stock 1800 mAh… but the difference is definitely worth
  it" — so his battery-life verdict ("all day, every day") is scoped to a modified device.
- [2012-04-16] **A personal size framework, from his own hands**: "I have big hands. I think anything
  over 4.8 inches might be a little too big. I've enjoyed as small as 4 inches on the Incredible 2 and
  as large as 4.7 on the Titan, but 4.65 is comfortable" — a preference bounded by named reference
  devices rather than asserted.
- [2012-04-16] **Audience-scoped software verdict**: ICS is "really easy to fall in love with" for
  existing Android users, "but if you're coming from something silly simple like Windows Phone or iOS
  then yeah, you might think this is a little bit confusing sometimes — so give it a few days and you'll
  get through the learning curve."
- [2012-04-16] **Self-knowledge stated plainly**: "I'm not a huge phone gamer at all, I'm more a
  productivity and utility person" — and Google Maps walking directions singled out as "really helpful
  for me, someone who goes a lot of places on foot."
- [2012-04-16] **Buying recommendation with a current price**: "even if you were to buy one today
  unlocked for somewhere around $450 — absolutely recommend it," with the stated reason being that a
  Nexus is easy to unlock, root and customize.
