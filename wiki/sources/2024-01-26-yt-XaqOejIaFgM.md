# Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra Review: Why Buy Anything Else? (@mkbhd, 2024-01-26)

## Summary
Marques's full solo review of the **$1,300 Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra**, structured around one
rhetorical device: for each thing a buyer might prioritize (screen, battery, performance,
stylus, cameras, software/AI, updates), he asks **"why get anything else?"** because this phone
maxes out every category. Central thesis: it "sets the tone for 2024" as the everything-phone /
"peak slab phone." Two big through-lines: (1) the deep new **Galaxy AI** suite (mostly built
*with Google* — a "weird like thing where they need each other"), and (2) Samsung openly
**copying the iPhone** (titanium, flat screen, boxier corners, 5X tele, always-on wallpaper,
object-cutout stickers, lock-screen widgets) — which he frames as the two giants making each
other better. Solo → persona/voice data. Disclosed **Ridge** sponsor read.

## Key claims
- [2024-01-26] Thesis: the Ultra formula has evolved from "most numbers" → +S Pen (replacing
  the Note) → "just everything in one place." "The more you use this phone, the more you look
  for weaknesses, the more impressive it gets."
- [2024-01-26] **Screen** (his standout): flat 6.8" QHD LTPO 120 Hz, up to **2,600 nits**, new
  anti-reflective coating on Gorilla Glass Armor, thinnest bezels yet, fast ultrasonic
  fingerprint reader. "If you're a screen snob, it doesn't really get better than this."
- [2024-01-26] **Battery/performance**: 5,000 mAh (thinner body, still has S Pen), 6+ hrs SOT,
  babied to two full days; Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 + 12 GB RAM, ray tracing, 90% larger vapor
  chamber — "an A plus."
- [2024-01-26] **Cameras** — his two-axis framework: **utility** (technical, on-paper) vs
  **aesthetics** (subjective art). Utility-wise this system "checks every single box." New this
  year: 50 MP **5X** telephoto replaces the 10 MP 10X — shorter optical reach but more
  resolution, so 5–10X shots look sharper and cropped 10X beats last year. Aesthetically still
  Samsung's "extra process-looking" contrasty/bright rendering, so you can still prefer Pixel/
  iPhone.
- [2024-01-26] Notes a long-standing reason he's **carried an iPhone for years**: third-party-app
  (Instagram/Snapchat) photo/video quality matched the native camera. Samsung now partnered with
  Instagram/Snapchat for native-quality pipelines + shoots Ultra HDR by default — so "now you can
  actually say that is true" of this phone.
- [2024-01-26] **Galaxy AI**: Generative Edit / Magic Editor ("feels exactly like… the Pixel
  minus any restrictions"), Erase Reflections, move/erase objects, generative-fill horizon tilt
  (his underrated favorite), portrait blur from non-portrait shots, plus live call translation,
  transcription/summaries, and **Circle to Search** ("makes it one step… so useful"). Flags the
  AI-manipulation **watermark** as responsible — then notes you can immediately erase that
  watermark with the object-removal tool.
- [2024-01-26] Recurring **"they need each other"** observation: Samsung leans on Google for AI
  features to compete with the iPhone, at some cost to Pixel's own differentiation — "fascinating
  and probably worth its own separate video."
- [2024-01-26] **Updates**: **7 years / 7 generations** of OS + security updates → up to date in
  2031; erases the old "go iPhone/Pixel for longevity" wisdom.
- [2024-01-26] **The one real counter-argument**: "the best argument not to buy this $1,300 phone
  is the fact that it's a $1,300 phone." You can get ~98% of it for less (S24+ / base S24). But
  if you're buying an "Ultra," this is the one to get.
