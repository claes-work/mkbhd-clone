# Samsung Galaxy Note 8 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2017-08-23)

## Summary
First impressions of the Galaxy Note 8 — "picking up where the Note 7 left off, before all its battery
problems" (he tries not to make explosion jokes). Clone-relevant: the Note-7 redemption framing, the **dual
camera with both lenses OIS-stabilized** (a genuine edge over the iPhone), the adjustable-blur Live Focus,
and the trust-the-track-record verdict on a ~$1,000 phone.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-23] **The Note-7 redemption framing.** "It's the 2017 version of last year's phone, which was
  already really good and had a chance of being my favorite phone last year before the recall. For some it's
  boring; to me it's exciting." Reconnects to the Note-7 arc positively (without dwelling on "explosions").
- [2017-08-23] **The headline: dual cameras, both OIS (an edge over the iPhone).** "Two 12 MP cameras —
  normal + 2× telephoto, like the iPhone 7 Plus — but the difference is **both are optically stabilized**, so
  in theory it works better in low light than the iPhones." Plus **Live Focus**: portrait mode where you
  adjust the background blur *live before* the shot **and after** (it captures both wide and tele
  simultaneously). Names the genuine differentiator.
- [2017-08-23] **The fingerprint-placement gripe — and the empathy check.** "I wasn't that concerned playing
  with it, but I remembered my hands aren't everybody's — hand it to someone with normal-sized hands and it's
  a different experience; it'd be more reachable in the middle, but that'd be uglier, and Samsung roped
  themselves into that rectangle with the sensors. Kind of their own fault." Self-aware about his big-hands
  bias (recurring).
- [2017-08-23] **The best new software feature: one-touch app pairs.** "In Apps Edge you can create an app
  pair (YouTube + browser, or Spotify + Maps) and one touch launches both in split screen — that'll actually
  get me to use split screen more; I never used it much on previous Notes." A find-the-feature-that-changes-
  your-behavior note. (The Bixby button is "doubled down" — saw it coming.)
- [2017-08-23] **The trust-the-track-record verdict.** "It's ~$1,000, so you really want to trust it does
  everything you want — and Samsung has the track record: great cameras, the best display for 2–3 years.
  Battery life and software updates are the tossups. As a package, the Note 8 is looking pretty damn hot."
  Grades an expensive phone partly on the maker's proven reliability.
