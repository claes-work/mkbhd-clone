# Tech Channel of the Week #6: ComputerHelpGuy1 (@mkbhd, 2009-06-15)

## Summary
The sixth channel spotlight (previous: #5 [[2009-06-08-yt-oVf0uw3vJFA]]), and the most
analytically interesting one so far, because Marques **states a growth thesis outright**:
volume of useful, request-driven content is *the* strength of a channel. Of the spotlighted
Scottish tutorial creator's 194 videos he says, "this is why I like his YouTube channel...
he really gets his content out there... he's answered a lot of questions already, he's done
request videos, and that's how he has so many subscribers." He also **states his own
selection rule** for the series in full ("your best chance of becoming the next Tech Channel
of the Week is obviously being a subscriber and producing quality tech videos that deserve a
look"), **names his own role as a curator for his audience** ("I'm just showing my audience
things they might like to check out"), discloses that his channel layout is a **customized
YT Layouts template** ("I used to use this layout in the earlier days of my channel before I
took a different one and edited it to be my own"), reveals he now runs a **Flickr photostream
for high-resolution stills** linked from his video descriptions, and covers Windows 7
beta/RC coverage as a forward-looking content bet. ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-06-15] **Volume-as-strategy stated as a thesis**: 194 videos "is the strength of
  this channel... he's answered a lot of questions already, he's done request videos, and
  that's how he has so many subscribers."
- [2009-06-15] **His own catalog benchmarked against it**: 194 tech videos is "almost as
  much as me" — consistent with the 216-video count he gives four days earlier in
  [[2009-06-11-yt-rgbTIXqFo8o]].
- [2009-06-15] **Selection rule for the series, stated in full**: "your best chance of
  becoming the next Tech Channel of the Week is obviously being a subscriber and producing
  quality tech videos that deserve a look at."
- [2009-06-15] **His role framed as curation**: "I'm just showing my audience things they
  might like to check out."
- [2009-06-15] **Channel design history disclosed**: the spotlighted channel uses "a YT
  Layouts layout — I used to use this layout in the earlier days of my channel before I
  took a different one and edited it to be my own." An early dated data point on his
  branding evolution (cf. the GIMP-built MKBHD wordmark already in the wiki).
- [2009-06-15] **New distribution surface: Flickr**: "I posted the link to the
  high-resolution photos of the GelaSkins installation in the description of the GelaSkins
  video, so I do have a Flickr photostream" — stills published alongside video, linked from
  the description. Directly follows through on the description-linking commitment made in
  [[2009-06-11-yt-rgbTIXqFo8o]], and attaches to [[2009-06-13-yt-ClRH1aQuP_Q]].
- [2009-06-15] **Windows 7 coverage read as a bet on timing**: "obviously not a lot of
  people are running Windows 7 at the moment, but I'm sure when Windows 7 is released in
  its full version, that's going to be a very popular playlist." Early evidence of him
  reasoning about a topic's *future* audience, not its current one. (Windows 7 was in RC
  in June 2009; general release followed 2009-10-22.)
- [2009-06-15] **Quality assessed against his own work**: the other creator's Windows 7
  videos have "really been as smooth as anything — as smooth as my Windows Vista tutorials"
  — he uses his own output as the yardstick (cf. his Vista tutorials, e.g.
  [[2009-05-02-yt-yqm1cmoZe88]]).
- [2009-06-15] **Tooling noticed**: "he uses, I'm pretty sure, Camtasia Studio 6 as his
  screen recorder" — the same tool Marques uses, recognized on sight.
- [2009-06-15] **Playlists and series read as structure**: he singles out a "dual booting"
  playlist and a three-video Windows 7 tips series as signs of deliberate organization —
  the same curation habit he applies to his own channel.
- [2009-06-15] **Reciprocity in the community**: "he has subscribed to me... he does rate my
  videos five stars and comments, and I really appreciate him being a part of this community."
- [2009-06-15] **Alternation rule restated**: "thanks for watching this software video —
  I'm alternating back and forth between software and hardware here."
