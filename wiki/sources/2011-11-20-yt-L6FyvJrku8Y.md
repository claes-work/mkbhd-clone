# YouTuber Tip #5: Pancakes (@mkbhd, 2011-11-20)

## Summary
A YouTube Tips episode built entirely on one analogy. He describes a real morning in his
**campus cafeteria**: a short-order cook working a huge grill, ten people deep, taking every
order at once and turning food out fast. His pancakes arrived in two minutes — and were
terrible. The lesson he draws is a **quality-over-throughput thesis for content**: better to
make the audience wait for something good than to hand out half-baked work quickly. Notable
as one of the clearest early statements of the production philosophy that later defines the
channel's release cadence.

## Key claims
- [2011-11-20] **Core thesis, stated plainly**: "I would rather have the audience — or the
  consumers — waiting for great content, rather than just pumping out half-baked content all
  the time."
- [2011-11-20] **Speed is a non-worry if quality holds**: "if you make great content you
  really don't have to worry about how fast you're making it, because you can be confident
  that it's going to make some people happy, or it's going to satisfy your goal." Output rate
  is explicitly subordinated to satisfaction of the goal.
- [2011-11-20] **The failure mode named**: working five things at once with "no personal
  connection to it — you just kind of make it" is called out as the wrong approach. **Personal
  connection to the individual piece** is the variable he isolates, not effort or time.
- [2011-11-20] **The success mode named**: one project at a time, finished well, handed to
  people who are genuinely happy with it — "then you can have a really successful YouTube
  channel."
- [2011-11-20] Dated setting: the story is set on campus in the university cafeteria,
  reinforcing the college-era context (cf. the dorm room in [[2011-09-27-yt-8W3bJGflOic]] and
  "buddies down the hall" in [[2011-10-30-yt-VuIPe93ZmiM]]).
- [2011-11-20] Teaching method: an entire advice episode carried by a single concrete personal
  anecdote rather than a list — a structural contrast with the numbered-listicle tips of the
  same series.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "I got up and I ordered pancakes, and two minutes later my pancakes were done. So I was
> like, all right, I guess that's pretty good. So I brought them back to my table — and they
> were terrible."

> "I would rather have the audience, or the consumers, waiting for great content rather than
> just pumping out half-baked content all the time."

> "If you find yourself behind the grill with just one project you're working on, and you
> really make sure that project is done well, and the people who you give it out to are really
> happy with it — then you can have a really successful YouTube channel. And maybe you can
> open up your own pancake store someday."
