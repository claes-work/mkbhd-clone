# USB-C All The Things! (Pt 2) (@mkbhd, 2017-08-16)

## Summary
A sequel to his USB-C accessory guide — but this time **a PSA aimed at manufacturers**: just go USB-C
already. Clone-relevant for a sharp follow-the-incentives read on *why* some categories adopt fast (phones,
fierce competition) and others lag (mice, no pressure) — and a call for makers to be the ones who push the
standard forward.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-16] **The PSA: makers, "USB-C all the things" now.** "It's 2017, an awkward middle phase — not
  quite a no-brainer to go USB-C on a new device, even though you totally should. You might worry about
  confusing customers who still have the old port elsewhere — but you can be the ones who push into this
  future and make it *less* confusing." Turns his consumer thesis into an industry call.
- [2017-08-16] **Apple as the model: declare it, remove the old port entirely.** "Apple basically decided
  USB-C is the future and took away all full-size USB-A on the entire MacBook lineup — no downsides, way less
  confusion, because you went straight from the old port to the new one. That's a much easier mental hurdle
  than mixing both." Credits the rip-the-bandaid approach (even while he's griped about the dongles).
- [2017-08-16] **Follow-the-incentives: competition drives adoption.** "Phones went USB-C across the board
  (except the iPhone) — a great example of moving fast *because of heavy competition*. But other categories
  don't have that stiff competition, so they're not racing to adopt. Take the Logitech MX Master 2S — one of
  my favorite mice ever — it refreshed with a new sensor and battery but kept **micro-USB**; Logitech
  probably rightly figured it doesn't matter (no space crunch, the cable's only for charging, why pay for a
  new chipset). But if we want to move faster to USB-C everywhere, this is the kind of thing that has to
  change." A precise read of where and why standards adoption stalls.

## Notable verbatim quotes
