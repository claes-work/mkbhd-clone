# Smartphone Awards 2018! (@mkbhd, 2018-12-19)

## Summary
The annual Smartphone Awards — a signature recurring MKBHD year-end format — where Marques breaks down every notable phone launched across 2018 (from CES in January through December) and rewards the best in each category, with each category naming an overall winner plus a runner-up (second place) and, where warranted, an honorable mention. His 2018 slate: **best big phone → Samsung Galaxy Note 9**; **best small phone → iPhone XS** (his "closest contested category"); **best camera → Pixel 3 / Pixel 3 XL**; **best battery life → Huawei Mate 20 Pro** (the best he'd ever tested); **best design → Oppo Find X**; **best budget phone → Xiaomi Pocophone F1**; **biggest bust → Red Hydrogen One**; **most improved → Razer Phone 2**; and the top prize, **MVP / Smartphone of the Year → OnePlus 6T**. His closing thesis: despite the "every phone looks the same / peak smartphone" sentiment of 2018, there has "never been a better selection," with a phone for every priority — photography, video, budget, compact, or battery.

## Key claims
- [2018-12-19] Awards format: every category gets an overall winner, plus a runner-up ("second place") if worthy, plus an honorable mention "if there's some more good stuff."
- [2018-12-19] **Best big smartphone → Samsung Galaxy Note 9** (winner). Reasoning: "one of the best big phone feature sets you can possibly get" — best display in any phone (a 6-inch AMOLED), 4000 mAh battery, headphone jack, expandable storage, dual cameras, no notch, and a stylus that doubles as a remote; plus software (S-Pen features, multi-window) that "takes advantage of it all." Calls it "a power user's default."
- [2018-12-19] Best big smartphone **runner-up → Huawei Mate 20 Pro** — the last phone he reviewed in 2018, "snuck in at the buzzer." 6.4-inch AMOLED, 4200 mAh battery, triple cameras at different focal lengths; no pen (fine for most), no headphone jack, and it has a notch — but "one of the most complete big phones of the year."
- [2018-12-19] **Best small smartphone → iPhone XS** (winner), 5.8-inch display. Called the "closest contested category." Reasoning: to win, the small phone must do everything the big phone does, and the XS "truly does pretty much everything the iPhone XS Max does" with just a smaller display and battery — A12 (one of the fastest chips), same dual cameras and RAM as the bigger phone. Caption said "iphone 10s / 10s max."
- [2018-12-19] Best small smartphone **runner-up → Pixel 3** (5.5-inch display) — "even smaller," and "some might even say the Pixel 3 is an even more appealing buy than the bigger brother the Pixel 3 XL"; you only lose display and battery size, but keep the stereo front-facing speakers and dual front-facing cameras.
- [2018-12-19] Best small smartphone **second runner-up → Samsung Galaxy S9** (back to 5.8-inch). Great design, 3000 mAh battery; the only points docked are one fewer camera than the S9 Plus and slightly less RAM — "both pretty small" issues.
- [2018-12-19] **Best camera → Pixel 3 / Pixel 3 XL** (winner). "Come out crystal clear." One camera, but Google's software magic (HDR+, Night Sight) makes it "clear and far ahead as the favorite camera ever in any smartphone." Marques notes he personally takes a lot of photos on his phone, so he'd spent a lot of time analyzing this category.
- [2018-12-19] Best camera **second place → iPhone XS** — "slightly lesser photos but... much better video and audio." "iPhones have been sort of the king of video on smartphones for a little while now"; if you do a lot of video, "you should get the iPhone."
- [2018-12-19] Best camera **honorable mention → Huawei Mate 20 Pro** — for excellent use of three cameras at three unique focal lengths; he "really like[s] the super wide angle camera." Notes LG and others also did triple cameras, but the Mate 20 Pro "happens to be the best quality of those three cameras."
- [2018-12-19] **Best battery life → Huawei Mate 20 Pro** (winner) — the best battery life he'd "actually ever [had] in a phone." 4200 mAh, 6.4-inch display; aggressive memory management + actively-changing resolution + fast + wireless charging meant he "literally never think[s] about battery" — could use it six straight hours in a day and end with 40% left. Extra credit in this category goes for fast and wireless charging.
- [2018-12-19] Best battery life **runner-up → iPhone XR** — combining the A12 with a "particularly low resolution is a recipe for a super long lasting battery life"; the longest-lasting iPhone he'd tested, plus fun colors. Caption said "iphone 10r."
- [2018-12-19] Best battery life **honorable mention → Red Hydrogen One** — thick, but packs a 4500 mAh cell, "officially the biggest battery cell I've ever held... in a smartphone." Fast charging, no wireless charging, but "lasts for an eternity."
- [2018-12-19] **Best design → Oppo Find X** (winner) — "what a head turner." A full motorized slider holding the front camera, IR sensors, and full facial-recognition system that pops up on every unlock. Nobody can speak to long-term durability of such a system "yet," but "a really fascinatingly beautiful phone that could totally end up in a museum." Caption garbled the name as "oppo find 10." Context: 2018 spawned a genre of boutique manufacturers chasing bezel-less, no-notch designs by hiding the camera/earpiece/sensors — racing to beat Apple to a notch-less phone.
- [2018-12-19] Best design **honorable mentions → Xiaomi Mi Mix 3** (also a slider) and **Vivo NEX S** (single pop-up camera). Caption said "nex s."
- [2018-12-19] **Best budget phone → Xiaomi Pocophone F1** (winner) — "turned a lot of heads," not available everywhere. Snapdragon 845, 6 GB RAM, 4000 mAh battery, well built, "around 300 bucks give or take with a bit of shipping." Skin wasn't too overwhelming (and you can flash another ROM), and it "took down the iPhone X in our blind camera test." Caption said "iphone 10."
- [2018-12-19] Broader budget-phone claim: nearly every phone launched in 2018 was more expensive at launch than its predecessor. His old line "good phones are getting cheap and cheap phones are getting good" — now "half of that is still true," and a great budget phone "is becoming more and more rare."
- [2018-12-19] Best budget phone **honorable mentions → Nokia 7 and Honor 8X** (jokes "get it, honor"). [Caption ambiguity noted below.]
- [2018-12-19] **Biggest bust of 2018 → Red Hydrogen One** — "no doubt." "The whole video... is just let down after letdown," which stung because he loves so many other RED products as a company. Still "crossing my fingers for those modules or Hydrogen 2, whichever comes first."
- [2018-12-19] **Most improved → Razer Phone 2** — earned it in the "pretty new gaming phone category" by fixing the weaknesses of the first Razer Phone while keeping its strengths: added glass back (for wireless charging), glowing RGB logo, improved cameras, kept front-facing speakers, kept the 120 Hz display but made it brighter, and made the whole thing water resistant. "Like going from a prototype car to a full finished production version." Repositioned "from a hardcore gaming phone to a flagship that happened to be great at media and gaming."
- [2018-12-19] Aside on the industry's "tick tock design strategy": "the new iPhone looks like the old iPhone, the new Samsung looks like the old Samsung," and not many 2018 phones improved enough to warrant their price increases.
- [2018-12-19] **MVP / Smartphone of the Year → OnePlus 6T** (winner). Emphasizes "there's only one voter and that's me." It's "not really the best at anything" (arguably the fastest Android phone, but there's no award for that) — it "does pretty much everything very well" and makes "a couple sacrifices in the right place to bring the price way down." In a world of $900–$1100 phones, it brings flagship performance and design "down to literally half the price"; now in stores, it's a phone he recommends widely.
- [2018-12-19] OnePlus cadence observation: they'll have a tough act to follow, "but we kind of always see it coming — they release [a phone] early in the year, then a couple months later there's the T version that's way better."
- [2018-12-19] MVP **runner-up → Samsung Galaxy Note 9** — "the most complete phone of the year." "The only flagship left with no notch, with a headphone jack, with a stylus built in," plus incredible display, variable-aperture camera, and all-around high-end specs — "it's checking every box," even at $1000.
- [2018-12-19] MVP **honorable mention → Huawei Mate 20 Pro** — still not available in the US and "not the best reputation of a company in the US," but best battery life he'd ever used, versatile triple cameras, a colored power button, great design and performance — "easily my favorite Huawei phone of all time."
- [2018-12-19] Closing thesis: despite the "we're all getting the same smartphone over and over" sentiment, "there has never been a better selection" — "something for everyone," and even non-winning phones on the table would have been "incredible choices" six years ago. "It's kind of a good time to be alive."
