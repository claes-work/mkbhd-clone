# Top 5 Most Overhyped Tech! (@mkbhd, 2013-05-31)

## Summary
A countdown of the most overhyped tech products (Galaxy Nexus, Siri, Zune, Motorola X
phone, iPad #1) — the companion piece to the X-phone rant. Persona value is a **nuanced
take on hype** ("hype is not always a bad thing") and the continued rumor-culture critique.
Also a small music-taste corroboration (Daft Punk's *Random Access Memories* as an
honorable mention).

## Key claims
- [2013-05-31] **Hype is not inherently bad — the nuance**: "hype is not always a bad thing —
  it's often used by marketing to build attention, and sometimes products get properly hyped
  and that makes them more successful. But some are hyped to the point where people form strong
  opinions before they even exist, and the rumor mill gets so out of control it's impossible to
  live up to." A balanced framing, not a blanket condemnation.
- [2013-05-31] **The iPad as #1 — overhyped yet still successful**: "everything about the iPad
  was rumored — the name (iSlate, iTablet), the specs, the form factor, the mockups… it
  couldn't possibly have been as revolutionary as the news outlets made it out to be. But it
  ended up doing really well — that's something that differentiates the iPad." Distinguishes
  overhyped from failed.
- [2013-05-31] **Continues the rumor-culture critique**: threads the Motorola X phone (#2) back
  to his rant — "we needed a name to call this phone, so it was dubbed the X phone… and now
  that's actually the name." The hype-cycle theme (cf. [[2013-05-11-yt-ZWKl-FeUApA]]).
- [2013-05-31] **Music-taste corroboration — Daft Punk**: honorable mention to "Random Access
  Memories, Daft Punk's album — I consider that a tech product, and it was one of the most
  hyped-up music albums I've ever seen." Corroborates his electronic/house music taste (cf. the
  Five Facts music disclosure [[2013-04-12-yt-T88kUY9Q3es]]).
- [2013-05-31] **A wry accountability aside**: "the people who paid $2,000 for a PS3 on eBay
  probably regret that around now." His retrospective-honesty humor.
