# On the 'Perfect Smartphone' (@mkbhd, 2015-08-23)

## Summary
A short **philosophical essay** — the canonical statement of his **one-critical-flaw doctrine** and the
idea that we now **measure smartphones by what's wrong with them, not what's right.** His thesis: phones
have gotten so good (cameras replacing point-and-shoots, screens eclipsing laptops) that every one has a
single "but" — a critical flaw that affects the buying decision — and **the perfect smartphone doesn't
exist and never will**, because the definition keeps evolving. He formalizes the baseline as **"the Big
Four": performance, battery, display, camera** — and one missing piece is all it takes to make you
reconsider.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-08-23] **Phones are incredible, but each has one critical flaw.** "There's always a *but* to deter
  someone from buying a smartphone — one critical flaw with basically every phone out there that can affect
  a buying decision." (He's constantly asked to recommend one.)
- [2015-08-23] **The recommend-a-phone skit (the flaw ladder).** S6 (great build/camera/screen, **but** bad
  sealed battery, no expandable storage, not water-resistant) → LG G4 (great, **but** poor software + slow
  updates) → Nexus 6 (stock + always updated, **but** way too big) → Moto X Play (huge battery, **but** not
  in the US) → OnePlus 2 (**but** no NFC) → Note 5 (**but** huge, no SD, no IR blaster) → "you know what,
  I'll just get an iPhone."
- [2015-08-23] **The perfect smartphone doesn't exist — the definition evolves.** "I don't think there will
  ever be anything we can call the perfect smartphone, mostly because the definition is changing — what we
  expect in a top-tier phone evolves every year, even month to month."
- [2015-08-23] **The Big Four framework.** "There's a good baseline we all agree on — I call it **the Big
  Four: good performance, good battery, good display, and good camera.** Have all four and you're probably a
  good buy. But one little flaw, one missing piece, makes us reconsider the complete package."
- [2015-08-23] **We measure phones by what's wrong with them.** "They get so much right on the top end that
  there are entire articles about why the Note 5 doesn't have an IR blaster or why the OnePlus 2 doesn't
  have NFC — not defending those decisions, just: if you get enough right, those are the only things left to
  write about." This drives hype, comparison videos, reviews, rumor roundups, and **dream-phone videos**
  ("a Frankenstein of the parts we like").
