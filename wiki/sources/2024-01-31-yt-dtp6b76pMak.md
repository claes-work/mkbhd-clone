# Using Apple Vision Pro: What It's Actually Like! (@mkbhd, 2024-01-31)

## Summary
Marques's week-one **hands-on breakdown of the Apple Vision Pro** — deliberately the "how it
works / what it's like to use" video, with a broader, "more existential" full review promised
separately (this is **part 1 of 2**). He argues the honest label is a very high-end **VR
headset** (Apple's "spatial computing" is largely semantics), walks the hardware (metal/glass
build, ~638 g front-heavy, external battery, ~2–4 hr life, M2 + R1 chips), and the eyes+hands
input model of visionOS. Standout praises: best-in-class **passthrough** (<12 ms latency),
rock-solid **spatial placement lock**, and the **Mac Virtual Display** as the most compelling,
"futuristic" use. The defining and weirdest feature is **Personas** — impressive but "right at
the edge of the uncanny valley," with a note that darker skin tones (like his) barely show on
the external eyes display. Solo → persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2024-01-31] Honest framing: "at its core… it is a VR headset. Apple would never say that…
  they're calling it spatial computing instead." Calls it "one of Apple's craziest, most
  radical, possibly dystopian products of all time."
- [2024-01-31] **Hardware**: machined aluminum + big front glass, downward speakers (some audio
  bleed), external OLED front display; ~638 g on the solo-knit band (front-weighted because the
  battery is off-head); external 3,366 mAh battery on a locking 4-ft cable, **~2–4 hr** life
  (in line with other VR headsets), no on/off button — you unplug to power off.
- [2024-01-31] **Input = eyes + hands**: look at a target, pinch anywhere in the ~180° bubble to
  select. Biggest adjustment: "only being able to control exactly what you're looking at" — you
  don't realize how often you interact with things you're not directly looking at.
- [2024-01-31] **Passthrough** is "the best… of any VR headset I've ever used, and it's not even
  that close" — R1 keeps latency under 12 ms; he could catch objects and play ping pong. Limits:
  very close/fine text is blurry, but you can text and read notifications with it on.
- [2024-01-31] **Spatial lock**: windows stay "absolutely locked" in 3D space with shadows in
  correct Z-space — hard to convey on YouTube ("Reviewing VR headsets is hard").
- [2024-01-31] **App situation**: two kinds — native visionOS apps (glassy, immersive: Apple
  Music, Sky Guide, JigSpace, Keynote's Steve Jobs Theater rehearsal, 180° immersive video with
  Alicia Keys) and compatible-but-unmodified iPad apps. Notable **opt-outs at launch: no Netflix,
  YouTube, or Spotify apps** (developer friction with Apple) — browser fallback works. Verdict on
  the "killer app" question: "**Not really… I don't think we have that yet.**" The real killer
  app is **the ecosystem** — logging in with an Apple ID pulls everything in.
- [2024-01-31] **Mac Virtual Display** is his favorite/"biggest game changer": one 4K resizable
  virtual monitor, keyboard/trackpad flow between it and floating visionOS apps; ideal on a plane.
  Limitation: **one virtual monitor only** (no dual-display).
- [2024-01-31] **Personas** (beta): a scanned digital you for FaceTime and the external eyes.
  "Both incredibly impressive and slightly unsettling… right at the edge of the uncanny valley."
  FaceTime is "technically… incredible" — angle-correct eye contact and hand gestures, strong
  spatial audio; weakest point is hair ("a frozen lump of hair"). **The external eyes barely show
  for darker skin tones**: "if you have a darker skin tone like me, just don't expect the eyes to
  show up very brightly." (Notable first-person observation from his own experience.)
- [2024-01-31] Upsides list: immersion, placement lock, eye/hand control, passthrough, ecosystem,
  spatial audio. Downsides: weight/comfort, the external eyes, current app selection, battery
  life, price. Broader point: it's exciting precisely because it's a **young, risky category** —
  "we're so used to slow, boring iteration in mature categories… now they're actually jumping
  into something risky, and it's actually fun."
