# Top 5 Android N Features! (Dev Preview) (@mkbhd, 2016-03-10)

## Summary
A Top-5 walkthrough of the Android N developer preview (loaded on a Nexus 6P), released earlier
than usual — before Google I/O. His read: "a lot of smaller stuff… it feels like this is going to
be something like Android 6.1," dominated by redesigns that finally take advantage of bigger,
higher-res screens. The five: redesigned Settings (info shown before you tap in), redesigned
notifications (wider, per-item inline actions/replies), redesigned Quick Settings (single-pull
toggles), redesigned multitasking (his favorite), and a functional dark/night mode.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-03-10] **Framed as an incremental ".1" release.** "No version number I can see, but it
  feels like Android 6.1 — a lot of what you'll see is redesigns." A characteristic move: naming
  the *magnitude* of an update, not just listing features.
- [2016-03-10] **The recurring "take advantage of bigger screens" lens.** Settings previews info
  inline (battery %, storage, what Wi-Fi/BT you're connected to) before you tap; notifications go
  full-width with more info — "again, really taking advantage of the bigger high-res displays we
  have." A consistent evaluation frame this era.
- [2016-03-10] **#4 multitasking = his favorite change.** Bigger cards; hitting the button drops
  the current app down and surfaces your second-most-recent app; the **double-tap quick app
  switcher** ("it's awesome… handy for quick copy-paste or going back and forth between two apps");
  and native **split-screen** — "for a developer preview this is really smooth, I didn't expect it
  to be this good."
- [2016-03-10] **"Skins did it first, now it's stock."** Repeatedly notes features long seen in
  TouchWiz/HTC Sense (single-pull quick toggles, split-screen) are now coming to stock Android —
  his standing framing of stock-vs-skin convergence.
- [2016-03-10] **Dark/night mode + developer-preview timing logic.** A functional night mode
  (reverses colors in core apps, auto-on at sunset). Explains *why* an early preview matters: app
  devs get months to build in split-screen so "when the new Nexus comes out, almost everyone's apps
  already work."
