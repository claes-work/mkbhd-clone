# What's on my Smartphone: 2017! (@mkbhd, 2017-01-20)

## Summary
The annual "what's on my phone" home-screen tour — a concrete snapshot of his real daily setup and app
taste in early 2017. Confirms the **Pixel XL as his primary / iPhone 7 Plus as secondary**, his "find what
I like and keep it stable" philosophy, and a **growing smart-home footprint** (Nest, Philips Hue, Google
Home).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-01-20] **Daily driver: Pixel XL primary, iPhone 7 Plus secondary.** "My main SIM (the number most
  people have) is in the Pixel XL on AT&T; the iPhone 7 Plus has a different SIM as my secondary." The
  two-phone system, current state. (His favorite-phone-experience verdict on the Pixel, lived out.)
- [2017-01-20] **"I find what I like and go all in — I keep it clean."** "A lot of the setup looks like last
  year's — I keep a lot of the apps the same over a long period because I get to know them really well and
  they work for what I do." A stability-over-novelty personal-taste tell (consistent with his anti-churn
  thread).
- [2017-01-20] **The more Google builds in, the fewer third-party apps he needs.** "Last year I had a
  separate weather widget; this year it's built into the Google launcher in Android 7.0 — perfect." Values
  first-party integration reducing app clutter.
- [2017-01-20] **A growing smart-home setup.** "I have Philips Hue bulbs all over the studio now (works with
  Google Home — I can just talk to the bulbs), and the Nest app is new for me — I have a couple of Nest
  thermostats and cameras now." A dated marker of his smart-home adoption. Also uses the **Before You Fly**
  app for drone-legality checks.
- [2017-01-20] **His app taste (specifics).** Phoenix ("still the best Twitter client for Android"), Spotify
  (most-used), Dark Sky weather, Google Keep/Tasks "for my own sanity," MyFitnessPal "to count nutrition,"
  YouTube Studio to monitor comments, the Tesla app. Keeps a camera shortcut in the dock "even though I don't
  need it." (Practical, uncluttered.)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I just kind of find what I like and I go all in with it — I keep it pretty clean and I don't keep that
> many extra apps."
> "The more Google builds features into their stock apps and launcher, the less I have to add with
> third-party apps."
