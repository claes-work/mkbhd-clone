# MacBook Pro vs Alienware M17x? [Pricing] (@mkbhd, 2009-10-19)

## Summary
A **video response** to another creator's ("Apple Soldier"/"Meeks") comparison video, which
he says didn't clearly show its math. He redoes the comparison himself: configures a 17"
MacBook Pro and a Dell/Alienware M17x to matching specs (same CPU, same 8GB 1066 RAM, same
256GB SSD) step by step and reads off the running price gap, ending around a $1,000
difference in the M17x's favor once software (iLife/iWork/Office) is factored out.

## Key claims
- [2009-10-19] **Spec-for-spec comparison method, stated as a rebuttal.** Explicitly
  reconstructs another creator's argument line-by-line ("I'm just doing this for myself")
  because he couldn't follow the original video's math — an early instance of doing the
  comparison work transparently on-screen rather than asserting a conclusion.
- [2009-10-19] **Base config**: 17" MacBook Pro starts at $2,500 vs. Dell/Alienware M17x at
  $1,799 for matching 2.8GHz Core 2 Duo specs.
- [2009-10-19] **RAM upgrade cost gap**: 8GB of 1066 RAM costs $1,100 on the MacBook Pro vs.
  $700 on the M17x for "the same exact RAM."
- [2009-10-19] **SSD upgrade cost gap**: a 256GB SSD is a $650 upgrade on the MacBook Pro vs.
  a $400 upgrade on the M17x.
- [2009-10-19] **Software bundling caveat**: notes the MacBook Pro's base price does *not*
  include iLife/iWork/FCE/Office, while the M17x ships with open-source alternatives —
  factors this into the final ~$1,000 gap (MacBook ~$4,100+ vs. M17x ~$3,100 at matched
  specs).
- [2009-10-19] **Explicit price-vs-value verdict**: states he wouldn't personally pay the
  premium for "an aluminum casing and an operating system" — an early, direct statement of
  price-to-value reasoning that anticipates his later "grade on the right curve" framework.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)

> "I mean if you can — I was trying to see what you were saying in your video but I didn't really see it 100%... just let me know what your thoughts are."
