# Curved Display Smartphones: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-10-19)

## Summary
An "Explained" primer on curved-display phones (the Galaxy Round, LG G Flex). Persona value
is his **teach-the-mechanism** register (the curved-glass-vs-curved-display distinction, and
curved ≠ flexible), the recurring **"vote with your wallet" / product-as-experiment**
framing, and the **new-tech-in-its-infancy-is-expensive** thesis.

## Key claims
- [2013-10-19] **Teach the tech — curved glass vs curved display**: "there's a difference: the
  display sits beneath the glass. Older 'curved' phones had curved *glass* over a flat panel, and
  if you curve the glass too much you get distortion and touch issues. Curving the *display* right
  under the glass opens up a whole new world of shapes." His explain-the-underlying-tech habit.
- [2013-10-19] **Curved ≠ flexible — a careful distinction**: "I haven't said 'flexible' once —
  curved and flexible are different. Flexible phones are still a long way off, because even if the
  display is wobbly, the battery, CPU, and RAM are still rigid — that's why you always see
  components hanging off a bendable display demo." Corrects a common conflation.
- [2013-10-19] **"Vote with your wallet" / product-as-experiment, restated**: "the Galaxy Round is
  an experiment — Samsung's not afraid to take chances, because the Galaxy Note line started as an
  experiment too and did quite well. We're voting with our wallets; if we buy it, they'll work
  much harder on it." His market-analysis framing (cf. the Galaxy Mega "vote with your wallet").
- [2013-10-19] **New-tech-in-its-infancy-is-expensive**: "when a technology is still in its
  infancy, it's still pretty expensive — the Galaxy Round retails ~$1,000 off-contract in Korea.
  When it matures we'll see pretty awesome things." His standing adoption-curve thesis (cf. 4K,
  Google Glass, curved).
- [2013-10-19] **Honest about the disadvantages**: battery ("cells don't curve, so you get a thick
  phone or a small battery"), video-viewing distortion, and durability ("you could literally damage
  your phone by sitting down the wrong way"). Names the real trade-offs.
- [2013-10-19] **Wait-for-gen-2 instinct**: "I think I'll wait for the Galaxy Round 2 in a couple
  years." His first-gen skepticism.
