# Apple iPad Air Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-11-14)

## Summary
His review of the iPad Air — a clean **even-handed Apple review** from an Android/Google
power user. Persona value is the **iOS-vs-Android-tablet-apps contrast** (iOS wins on
optimized tablet apps, Android on home-screen customization — "I'm waiting for middle
ground"), the recurring front-facing-speakers preference, and a teasing nod to his
skin-tone / camera-test methodology.

## Key claims
- [2013-11-14] **The iOS-vs-Android tablet-apps contrast — the core analysis**: "iPads have the
  opposite problem of Android tablets: on an iPad every home screen is the same blown-up phone
  interface [no widgets, no glanceable info], but the App Store has hundreds of thousands of
  apps *optimized for the iPad* that use the whole display. On Android, the home screen is where
  you go to town on customization/widgets, but the Play Store lacks optimized tablet apps —
  using them feels wasteful. I'm waiting for middle ground." His balanced, both-sides analysis.
- [2013-11-14] **Even-handed on the hardware**: "the iPad keeps doing build quality really well —
  beautifully crafted, screams quality at every corner. Asus, if you're still working on the new
  Nexus 10, take notes." Credits Apple fully and nudges the Android side.
- [2013-11-14] **Skin-tone / camera-test tease**: "the iPad Air has the best camera I've ever
  used in a tablet, by a lot — and that's all I'm going to say. Not going to tell you why it's
  good or how I figure that out." A deliberate tease of his (later-famous) camera-test
  methodology.
- [2013-11-14] **Front-facing-speakers preference, restated**: "my only real gripe is the
  speakers — plenty loud and crisp, but all on the bottom, so they fire sideways in landscape,
  which drives me insane, especially since I've gotten used to the Nexus 10's front-facing
  speakers." His consistent taste.
- [2013-11-14] **Big-hands aside**: "just for the record, I don't have the normal hand size — but
  this is the first full-size iPad I've been able to hold in one hand." The recurring datum.
- [2013-11-14] **"It's an iPad, and people like that" — the verdict**: "you're not going to see
  crazy interface differences or huge performance boosts — everything performs exactly as you'd
  expect, because it's an iPad, and that's good; people like that." Honest about the appeal of
  predictability.
- [2013-11-14] **Personal — prefers the small tablet**: "I'm not much of a large-tablet person —
  I love my 2013 Nexus 7, and if I had to buy an iPad I'd want a smaller one, which is why the
  retina iPad Mini is in the mail." His own-usage disclosure.
