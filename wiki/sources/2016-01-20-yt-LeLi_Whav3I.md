# The State of 4K: Early 2016! (@mkbhd, 2016-01-20)

## Summary
The annual **"State of 4K"** — now with data. Google Analytics shows **only ~0.11% (a tenth of a percent)**
of his viewers even *have* a 4K display, yet he argues the state of 4K is "very strong," tracked across
three axes — **recording, streaming, and displaying.** **Recording 4K has never been easier** (it's in
phones/DSLRs/point-and-shoots — "the number of 4K cameras in people's pockets has never been higher");
**displaying** is getting there (affordable 4K TVs everywhere; premium 4K laptops; exactly one 4K phone,
the Z5 Premium, which isn't full-time 4K). The 4K-adoption thesis advancing with concrete numbers.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-01-20] **The data: almost nobody watches in 4K yet.** Google Analytics now shows viewer screen
  resolutions — the most common is 1920×1080 (25%), and "you have to go all the way down to 36th to find the
  first 4K display, at **0.11%** — just a tenth of a percent of you even have the ability to watch in 4K."
- [2016-01-20] **But the state of 4K is strong (three axes).** **Recording** — "4K cameras everywhere, from
  high-end production down to DSLRs, mirrorless, point-and-shoots, and smartphones; with all the iPhone 6s's
  out there, the number of 4K cameras in pockets has never been higher — pretty much anyone can record 4K."
  **Displaying** — "pretty much everyone who makes a TV now has an affordable 4K panel; the price gap over
  1080p is shrinking fast," plus premium 4K laptops (Dell XPS 15, Razer, etc.) and the 5K iMac / 4K desktop
  displays.
- [2016-01-20] **The smaller you go, the rarer 4K is.** "Exactly one 4K smartphone exists (the Z5 Premium),
  and I've explained why it's not even a full-time 4K phone. It's still pretty rare to carry a 4K display in
  your pocket, but as you go bigger — laptops, then the 5K iMac and 4K TVs — it gets common."
