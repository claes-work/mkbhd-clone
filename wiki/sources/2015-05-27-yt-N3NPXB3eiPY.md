# 10 Apple Watch Questions: Answered! (@mkbhd, 2015-05-27)

## Summary
A Q&A follow-up to the Apple Watch review (filmed from a San Francisco hotel, ahead of Google I/O),
answering the ten questions people ask him when they spot the watch. The most substantive: **the apps are
slow because they're not native** (resources stream from the iPhone on each launch; native WatchKit apps
"coming next year"). Plus practical answers — it's water-*resistant* (IPX7) not waterproof, ~1.5-day
battery, it scratches a little (like his Moto 360), and — consistent with his stance — **"you don't need a
case for the Apple Watch."**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-05-27] **Why the apps are slow: not native.** "The resources for the apps are not stored on the
  watch — they're on your iPhone, and when you tap to open, it streams them to the watch," which is why it
  lags. "Native Apple Watch apps are coming next year [via WatchKit]. Should it have been that way to begin
  with? Probably — but at least they're getting better."
- [2015-05-27] **Favorite apps:** Shazam (one-tap song ID), Deliveries (package alerts — "got used to it on
  Android Wear"), Maps turn-by-turn, and the activity/fitness tracker (great with the Sport).
- [2015-05-27] **Water resistance:** IPX7 (a precaution — fine in rain/splashes) but **not waterproof** —
  "I wouldn't recommend" showering/submerging even though videos show it surviving.
- [2015-05-27] **Battery:** ~1.5 days ("never killed it in a single day, close to two days frequently");
  Power Reserve is excellent (97% → still 97% after a 6-hour flight); charges in ~1.5–2 hr.
- [2015-05-27] **It scratches a little** (stainless steel — a hairline or two, "like my Moto 360; that's
  just the way they are").
- [2015-05-27] **No-cases, restated.** On accessories: docks (Apple's charging method "isn't exactly
  pretty") and screen protectors make sense, but **"you don't need a case for the Apple Watch."**
- [2015-05-27] **The mystery diagnostic port** behind the band clip accepts power — one company is building
  a battery-band around it.
