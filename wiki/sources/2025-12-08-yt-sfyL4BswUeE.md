# Smartphone Awards 2025! (@mkbhd, 2025-12-08)

## Summary
The annual **Smartphone Awards** for calendar-year 2025, staged before an audience with custom
red trophies across the usual categories. The dominant storyline: **the base iPhone 17 finally
became "a complete phone"** and sweeps **Most Improved AND MVP/Phone of the Year** — while the
**iPhone 16 (the stripped-down $599 entry model) wins Bust of the Year**, a rare split where Apple
takes both the top prize and the booby prize. Other throughlines: **silicon-carbon batteries
created a "bimodal distribution"** (7,000–8,000 mAh haves vs. 5,000-5,500 mAh have-nots), Oppo
finally **breaks the iPhone camera streak**, and the **iPhone Air wins Design** despite being
"beautiful and controversial." Solo → persona/voice data. No sponsor read in this one.

## Key claims
- [2025-12-08] **Framing:** despite AI devices, smart glasses, and XR headsets, "the smartphone is
  still OP… still the center of our personal computing lives." Standard format: one trophy-winner
  per category + honorable mentions; "these are of course my awards… the ones I deem worthy."
- [2025-12-08] **Best Big Phone → Xiaomi 17 Pro Max** (6.9", Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 = "the most
  powerful chip of the year," 7,500 mAh silicon-carbon, triple 50 MP, ~3,000-nit rear screen used
  as viewfinder/notifications). Runner-up **Oppo Find X9 Pro** (200 MP tele, 7,500 mAh Si-C).
- [2025-12-08] **Best Small Phone → Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 7** — a deliberate "statement" pick:
  small flagships went extinct, so he rewards the phone actually *trying* to be small. Praises the
  4.1" cover screen for **triaging notifications without doom-scrolling**, with a full 6.9" phone
  "lurking underneath." Beat OnePlus 13S ("S for small"), iPhone 17/17 Pro. Argues flip is easier
  to recommend than fold for the average person and cheaper.
- [2025-12-08] **Best Camera → Oppo Find X9 Pro** — **breaks the iPhone streak.** (Playful bit: he
  starts to say "the i—" then reveals Oppo.) 50 MP main + 50 MP ultrawide + 200 MP tele + true-
  color 4th sensor; quick app, full 50 MP shots (not binned 12 MP), 4K120 Dolby Vision + log, plus
  a **Hasselblad 10x telephoto attachment**. Runner-up **iPhone 17 Pro** — still "the undisputed
  king of video… not even up for debate" (cites Genlock support as proof the pro-video demographic
  is locked in). Shout-out Vivo X300 Pro (great sensors, bokeh a bit off).
- [2025-12-08] **Best Value → CMF Phone 2 Pro** ($279, "modular, cheap, and fun") — he notes he'd
  literally **titled his May review "budget phone of the year"** ("intense foreshadowing"). Shout-
  outs: Moto G Play (best under $200), Pixel 9a (compact, no camera bump), and the **base iPhone
  17** as "finally a good deal."
- [2025-12-08] **Best Battery → OnePlus 15** (7,300 mAh Si-C) — "the best battery I have ever used
  in a phone," got him **three full days / 10+ hrs screen-on**, 120 W wired + 50 W wireless. Frames
  **2025 as the year batteries finally took a meaningful leap**, started by the Jan OnePlus 13's
  6,000 mAh Si-C; now a **"bimodal distribution"** between adopters and Apple/Samsung/Motorola
  (capped ~5,000-5,500 mAh). Shout-outs: Xiaomi 17 Pro Max (7,500 mAh, 22.5 W reverse wireless),
  Doogee S200 Ultra (11,000 mAh, didn't fit on the table).
- [2025-12-08] **Best Design → iPhone Air** ("most beautiful and controversial product of the
  year"). Acknowledges it flopped commercially and is "clearly compromised" (worse battery, single
  camera), but "makes other very normal phones feel like tanks" and "appeals to the minimalist in
  me." Personal note: **"I want to be the type of person who could live with these sacrifices…
  I tried. I can't"** — needs dual cameras + a Si-C battery. Runner-ups: Xiaomi 17 Pro Max (usable
  rear screen), Galaxy S25 Edge (brutalist thin), Fairphone 6 ("most repairable/sustainable/modular
  phone basically of all time").
- [2025-12-08] **Best Foldable → Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7** — "if you deleted all the slab phones…
  and made me daily drive something that folds, it would be this one," not for specs but for being
  "the most rock solid across the board." The year's foldables "got way thinner," which matters for
  closed use. Runner-up Pixel 10 Pro Fold (he daily-drove it a while; a bit thicker). Notes the
  Galaxy Z TriFold announced but ships next year → eligible for 2026.
- [2025-12-08] **Most Improved → iPhone 17.** "Apple just finally went ahead and did what they
  should have done years ago… made the base iPhone a complete phone": new chip + **high-refresh 120
  Hz**, doubled base storage, upgraded selfie camera → from "the base placeholder iPhone I kind of
  hated recommending" to "all the iPhone anyone really ever needs." Runner-up OnePlus 15 (skipped
  "14").
- [2025-12-08] **Bust of the Year → iPhone 16** (the new $599 entry model). Apple stripped the base
  phone down — A18, 60 Hz notched display, **single camera**, black only, no Wi-Fi 6E/7, no UWB, no
  mmWave, no Camera Control, no MagSafe — yet it starts at **$599** (the review unit's storage tier
  hit $900). "It is the cheapest new iPhone, but it is not a cheap iPhone." Runner-up Nothing Phone
  3 (disappointed its passionate fanbase; nuked the glyph into a tiny rear display).
- [2025-12-08] **MVP / Phone of the Year → iPhone 17.** Explicitly **not** the "best phone /
  highest benchmark" award — "this is more of the one that had the story that represents the year."
  The base iPhone at ~"8 out of 10 in every category," priced under base S25/Pixel. Runner-up
  **Xiaomi 17 Pro Max** — "probably my favorite phone release of the year," which "almost
  shamelessly rode on the back of the iPhone 17 Pro Max," copied the name, then one-upped design/
  battery/power/price. Personal daily drivers this year: Galaxy S25 Ultra → Pixel 10 Pro → **OnePlus
  15** currently.
