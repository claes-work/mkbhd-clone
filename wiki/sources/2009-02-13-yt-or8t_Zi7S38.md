# Could Windows 7 Be a $50 Upgrade? (@mkbhd, 2009-02-13)

## Summary
Marques's (age 15) **first video about Windows 7** — a short, camera-only opinion piece
rather than a tutorial ("it's not going to be widescreen, but it's going to be HD"). He
relays a rumor he read on **CNET** that Microsoft is considering a **$50 upgrade for Windows
Vista users** and possibly shipping Windows 7 in **2009**, and reacts enthusiastically. He
contrasts this with the poor value he felt upgrading from XP to Vista, notes Vista shipped in
**six versions**, and guesses the **"Premium" tier** would suit him best. He promises many
more Windows 7 reviews once it launches, citing anticipated **performance boosts**.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-13] **Opinion:** if Microsoft offers a **$50 Vista→Windows 7 upgrade**, he "will be
  upgrading instantly, no doubt about it." (Datable early-adopter opinion.)
- [2009-02-13] Sourcing: he read on **cnet.com** that Windows 7 might release in **2009** at a
  **$50 upgrade price** for Vista users. (Reported rumor, attributed to CNET — not verified by him.)
- [2009-02-13] **Opinion:** upgrading XP→Vista "was not exactly the cheapest thing" and "wasn't
  worth as much money as you may have spent" — an early value-for-money judgment.
- [2009-02-13] He expects the **Premium** edition of Windows 7 to be the best fit for him, given
  Vista's six-version lineup.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "if that is the case, I will be upgrading to Windows 7 instantly. No doubt about it."

> "upgrading from Windows XP to Windows Vista has not exactly been the cheapest thing in the world, and it wasn't exactly worth as much money as you may have spent"
