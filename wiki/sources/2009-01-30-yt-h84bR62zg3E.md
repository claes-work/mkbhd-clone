# HQ Tutorial: Rocket Dock Application (@mkbhd, 2009-01-30)

## Summary
A detailed setup/customization tutorial from age-15 Marques on **RocketDock**, the free "smoothly
animated, Alpha-Blended application launcher" he runs at the top of his screen on **Windows Vista
Home Premium** (he pulls up his specs on camera: 2.2 GHz, 3 GB RAM, 512 MB graphics). He shows where
to download it (Google → rocketdock.com → Download tab, latest v1.3.5), then walks essentially every
settings tab: language, run-at-startup, running-application indicators, item locking, icon quality
(affects load/buffer time), opacity and zoom-opaque effects (transparency "takes a little CPU"), icon
size and zoom, dock position (he keeps his at the top; others use bottom "like the Mac 10 dock," left,
or right), styles/themes (with downloadable extras), fonts, and click behavior (bounce vs. darken). He
covers add-ons from rocketdock.com's "get apps": custom icon packs and **docklets** — demoing a clock
docklet and, more usefully, a **stack docklet** he points at a folder (e.g. his music/"18 Wheels of
Steel") with a rocket icon and a fly-out "grid" or "fan" animation borrowed from OS X. He notes
RocketDock is "developed by Punk Software" (rendered "Fanboy and fan draw" in captions) "on OSX, well
that explains this look." His pitch: it's faster than Start-menu hunting for apps like Safari or
Thunderbird. He closes asking viewers to "comment, rate, and subscribe."

## Key claims
- [2009-01-30] **RocketDock** is a free "smoothly animated, Alpha-Blended application launcher"; he
  runs **v1.3.5** at the top of his screen and recommends the latest version from rocketdock.com's
  Download tab.
- [2009-01-30] His machine (dated origin spec): **Windows Vista Home Premium, 2.2 GHz processor, 3 GB
  RAM, 512 MB graphics card.**
- [2009-01-30] Settings walkthrough: running-application indicators (the dot), lock-items to prevent
  accidental drag-out, icon quality trades load/buffer time, **opacity/transparency effects "take a
  little CPU,"** adjustable icon size and zoom, and dock position (top/bottom/left/right; he keeps
  top because he sometimes runs the Vista sidebar).
- [2009-01-30] Customization: many bundled themes plus downloadable ones, changeable fonts, and click
  behavior (bounce or darken). Add-ons via "get apps" include **custom icon packs** (OpenOffice,
  CCleaner icons) and **docklets**.
- [2009-01-30] **Stack docklets** point a custom icon (e.g. a rocket) at any folder and fly the
  contents out in a **grid or fan animation** ("basically one of OS 10's animations"), with an
  "open in folder" fallback — he uses one for quick access to his music.
- [2009-01-30] RocketDock is developed "on OSX" (macOS), which he says "explains this look" — i.e.
  it emulates the Mac Dock on Windows.
- [2009-01-30] Value pitch: it's **faster than hunting through the Start menu** for apps like Safari
  or Thunderbird — "it's just faster."
