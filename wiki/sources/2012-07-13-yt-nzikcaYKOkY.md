# Galaxy SIII vs One X vs Galaxy Nexus! (@mkbhd, 2012-07-13)

## Summary
A **buyer's-guide/decision-framework video** — made because "ever since I reviewed the third of those
three, the Galaxy S3, I get this one very specific question almost every day: which one should I get?"
He resolves a three-way comparison not with a single winner but with **a decision tree keyed to what
the buyer values** — hardware vs. software vs. budget — the clearest early instance of the buyer's-
guide format that later becomes the annual Smartphone Awards.

## Key claims
- [2012-07-13] **The organizing axes**: "the biggest difference between these three phones is really the
  newness and the price." He reduces the choice to a small set of decision rules rather than declaring a
  best phone.
- [2012-07-13] **The core decision rule — hardware vs. software**: "if you want hardware you go with the
  Galaxy S3. But if you're a real Android person, or if you value software, you're going to want to go
  with the Samsung Galaxy Nexus." The S III wins the spec sheet ("higher resolution and better camera,
  louder speaker, larger display, twice the RAM, twice the cores, faster clock"); the Nexus wins on
  **guaranteed updates** ("that's Google's baby right now… Android 4.1 and counting").
- [2012-07-13] **S III vs One X reduces to the skin**: "you're really picking between Sense UI and
  TouchWiz" — the hardware is near-identical (both quad-core, both 8 MP), so **update cadence and the
  skin** decide it. He flags that "the way HTC has handled their updates is probably not the best."
- [2012-07-13] **Price-to-value made explicit with numbers**: the Galaxy Nexus has fallen to "about $350
  buying it directly from Google and the Google Play Store, whereas an unlocked Galaxy S3 is going to
  cost close to twice that." The older Nexus is framed as the value pick — "bang for your buck."
- [2012-07-13] **A stated default when someone is truly stuck** — a personal preference surfaced as a
  tie-breaker: between the One X and the Galaxy Nexus, "I always just say go with the Galaxy Nexus…
  I have a hard time recommending the 1X over a lot of things right now." An honest thumb-on-the-scale,
  disclosed as his default rather than an objective verdict.
- [2012-07-13] **Names the audience segments explicitly** — he partitions viewers into groups (S3-vs-1X,
  1X-vs-Nexus, S3-vs-Nexus, budget) and gives each a rule, so every viewer can locate themselves. A
  structural approach to buying advice.
- [2012-07-13] **Promises the deeper comparison**: "I'll definitely be going in a much more in-depth
  video comparing some of the hardware I mentioned" — the review-scope discipline (this is the decision
  layer; the spec deep-dive is a separate video).
