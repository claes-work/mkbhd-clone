# The Setup Tour Project 2012 [call for submissions] (@mkbhd, 2012-09-13)

## Summary
The kickoff/call-for-submissions video for the Setup Tour Project — "a project to create the largest
ever collaborative setup-tour video on YouTube." He asks viewers to submit 10–20-second clips of what's
unique about their own setups, to be compiled into one massive community video (the payoff is
[[2012-09-21-yt-kRB1-APKUqs]]). Short and organizational, but it states clearly **why he values setup
tours** — you learn things to bring back to your own — and documents an early large-scale audience
collaboration.

## Key claims
- [2012-09-13] **The rationale — learn from others' setups**: "part of the reason why I love watching
  setup-tour videos so much is that you actually **learn things from other people's setups to bring back
  to your own**." Not a vanity format; a knowledge-exchange one.
- [2012-09-13] **A stated working habit — his setup never stays put**: "my setup back here actually
  changes all the time, it almost never stays the same for more than about a week or two, and that's
  because I'm constantly getting ideas from outside sources — from other articles, from other YouTube
  videos, and from other people's setups." Continuous iteration on his own workspace, sourced externally.
- [2012-09-13] **Setup ≠ just the desk** — a definition: "setup tours are a little bit more than just
  what's on your desk. It surrounds a whole workspace and how you use that workspace for your workflow
  and getting things done." Workflow-first framing.
- [2012-09-13] **The submission mechanics**: a 10–20-second wide-angle clip of one unique/instructive
  thing, posted as a video response (or emailed via a large-file service); everyone who submits via
  YouTube gets a shoutout.
- [2012-09-13] **Personal context — recently moved**: "a lot of you might already know I moved recently,
  and one of the types of videos I watched a lot when I moved in was setup-tour videos." The new-apartment
  move ([[2012-08-10-yt-_hhEz4Uo79k]]) is the motivation for the project.
- [2012-09-13] **Teases the reveal**: "brace yourself for a setup tour of epic proportions, including my
  own… the half of the desk that you guys haven't seen here yet."
