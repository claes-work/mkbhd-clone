# 2026 Rivian R2 First Drive — "Rivian's Model Y Fighter" (@AutoFocus, 2026-02-10)

## Summary
Marques's **first drive** of a camouflaged near-final **Rivian R2** prototype, on public
roads in California ahead of the reveal (other YouTubers present, incl. Zack from
JerryRigEverything off-roading one). He frames the R2 as Rivian's scale-up play — the
same strategic move Tesla made with the Model 3/Y after the low-volume Model S/X (which
Tesla had just announced it would discontinue): take the R1S/R1T DNA and deliver it at
roughly **half the price (~$45k start)**. The R2 achieves this by being a bit smaller
(two-row SUV vs. the R1S's three rows) and **simpler — fewer parts, less wiring** (one
example: a single piece combines the yellow accent, badge, and turn indicator that were
three separate parts on the R1S). Key simplification: **no active air suspension** —
passive coils + semi-active dampers, so all drive-mode differences come through damping,
throttle response, and steering weight. Specs: ~300 mi range, 0–60 in 3.5s, 650+ hp,
609 lb-ft, NACS port moved to the rear-left corner. Driving impressions: it "feels just
like an R1S" (his repeated theme), softer/cushier than Gen-1 Rivians in all-purpose mode
(rear-wheel drive by default for efficiency, re-engaging the front motor under load),
firmer and heavier-steering in sport. Standout: **~5 mi/kWh efficiency** — nearly double
what he sees on his R1T — despite little aero change. He geeks out over the new
**haptic dials** (motorized, trackpad/PS5-controller-like fake detents and end-stops)
first shown in the studio R2 video with RJ. Honest verdict: if Rivian can actually
deliver the base $45k car with 300 mi and this character at good margins, it can be
"their Model Y"; the $45–55k band is very compelling. Marques-solo (Rivian staff
non-speaking).

## Key claims
- [2026-02-10] The R2 is Rivian's scale-up moment, mirroring Tesla's Model 3/Y after the
  low-volume Model S/X (which Tesla just announced it's discontinuing) — R1S/R1T DNA at
  ~half price. (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] Base R2 targets **~$45,000** (rising with options), ~300 mi range, 0–60 in
  **3.5s**, 650+ hp, 609 lb-ft, NACS port relocated to the rear-left corner. (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] Cost is cut two ways: (1) smaller (two-row SUV, ~1 inch shorter than a
  Model Y, similar height/width) and (2) **simpler — fewer parts and less wiring**; a
  single component now combines what were three separate pieces on the R1S. (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] **No active air suspension** (a major R1 simplification): passive coils +
  semi-active dampers, so drive-mode character must come through damping, throttle, and
  steering weight. All-purpose mode is cushy/soft (his preference; contrasts early Model
  Y being "too firm and crashy"); sport is firmer with heavier steering. (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] All-purpose mode defaults to **rear-wheel drive for efficiency**,
  re-engaging the front motor under harder throttle; sport is AWD all the time (like
  other Rivians). (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] **Efficiency standout:** ~5 mi/kWh over 15 minutes of mixed driving —
  nearly twice what he normally sees on his R1T — despite minimal aerodynamic change from
  the R1's flat-windshield/square-front shape. (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] The new **haptic dials** (right = temp/fan/view, left = volume/media/
  efficiency/tire-pressure cards) use motors + haptics to fake left/right/forward
  detents and even end-of-travel resistance — "really clever... really intuitive,"
  MacBook-trackpad/PS5-controller-like. First shown in the studio R2 video with RJ.
  (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] It repeatedly "feels just like an R1S" — driving position, visibility,
  packaging — with *more* rear legroom than his R1T. Minor concern: the capacitive
  (non-button) front-trunk release "feels like it could ice over." (Marques)
- [2026-02-10] Verdict: if Rivian nails the base $45k car with 300 mi, this character,
  and good margins, "this can be their Model Y" — their real mass-market entry; the
  $45–55k band is very compelling and he hasn't driven much he prefers in that price in
  the past year. (Marques)
