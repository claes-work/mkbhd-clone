# The iPhone 6 Model! (@mkbhd, 2014-05-25)

## Summary
A quick, explicitly-derivative rumor-coverage video handling the **iPhone 6 "dummy" body** (a
case-maker's mockup built from leaked specs, via leaker Sonny Dickson) months before the phone's
announcement. Marques flags up front that it's "been covered in depth by a lot of people already," then
gives the three set-in-stone takeaways — **rounded edges (M8-like), a bigger 4.7" display with biggish
bezels, and extreme thinness (~6 mm)** — and turns the thinness into his recurring **battery-life
question**. Low net-new yield; useful mainly as a dated marker of his even-handed, source-critical
stance toward hype and leaks.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-05-25] **What the object actually is:** not an Apple prototype but the case-industry dummy —
  "basically made up of all your rumors, all your speculation" — used to start producing cases before
  the phone ships.
- [2014-05-25] **Rounded edges** — a departure from the flat iPhone 4→5 era; "this new dummy really
  reminds me of the HTC One M8 at a glance."
- [2014-05-25] **Bigger display, biggish bezels** — a 4.7" display (rumored 1704×960), "the biggest
  phone Apple will ever make," but "the bezels seem to be kind of big compared to a lot of other phones,"
  which "makes it look a little ugly." Compared against the 4.7" Moto X (much smaller overall body).
- [2014-05-25] **Extreme thinness → the battery question.** ~6 mm thin in a metal unibody ("the same
  thinness now as the iPod touch"); "I always think about battery life when I see a phone as thin as
  this." Notes the relocated **side power button** makes sense on a bigger phone.
- [2014-05-25] **A dummy flatters or flops depending on execution** — "it tends to look a lot better
  when it's an actual working phone." He punts the "would you buy a big iPhone?" question to viewers and
  links another creator's man-on-the-street version.
