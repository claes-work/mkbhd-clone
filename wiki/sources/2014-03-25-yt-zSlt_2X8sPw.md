# HTC One M8 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-03-25)

## Summary
Hands-on first impressions of the HTC One M8 (from the NY launch event). Reinforcing —
validates his onscreen-buttons prediction, reads **design as the phone's main focus** (HTC
doubling down on what won them awards), and honestly flags the **duo-camera refocus feature
as not working well** ("super artificial"). Verdict held for the full review.

## Key claims
- [2014-03-25] **Design is the main focus — HTC doubling down**: "it's clear the main focus of this
  phone is design. HTC knows the One was one of the best-designed phones of 2013 — that's what won
  all the awards — so they figured the best way to capitalize is to keep focusing on design, a phone
  that feels good, looks good, and works well." His read of the maker's strategy.
- [2014-03-25] **Duo-camera skepticism — honest**: "the duo camera is supposed to let you refocus a
  picture after you take it — but from my limited experience, this feature doesn't even really work
  very well, it looks super artificial in the finished results. At least it's software-fixable and
  the API is public." Names the flaw plainly, credits the software path.
- [2014-03-25] **UltraPixel continued — as predicted**: "we're sticking with that 4-megapixel
  UltraPixel, like I said we would in the what-to-expect video — a lot of people are still bummed.
  Better in low light, no OIS anymore. The viewfinder looked great [fast shutter, fast AF], but the
  finished photos resemble the old One — okay, not high-res." Validates his prediction + honest
  camera read.
- [2014-03-25] **BoomSound as the identity feature**: "no other phone really does those front-facing
  BoomSound speakers yet — it's a big part of why it looks so similar to the old One." Restates the
  speaker esteem.
- [2014-03-25] **The three main changes**: bigger 5" 1080p display + onscreen buttons (validating his
  prediction), a taller body, and a more rounded all-metal shape ("screams quality… more sleek, more
  graceful"). A clean physical rundown.
- [2014-03-25] **Verdict-discipline**: "I still have battery life to test, accessories, and a Google
  Play edition on the way — leave a comment on what you want covered in the full review." Holds the
  verdict for testing.
