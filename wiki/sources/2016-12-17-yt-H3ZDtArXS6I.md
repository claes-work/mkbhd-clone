# Dope Tech: Custom LED Clock! (@mkbhd, 2016-12-17)

## Summary
A short "Dope Tech"-style gadget video in which Marques shows off the LaMetric Time — a matte-black, app-connected pixel-art LED clock (that also happens to be a Bluetooth speaker) which he found and shared on Snapchat before enough viewer curiosity prompted a quick video. Despite the title's "Custom LED Clock," this is a purchased off-the-shelf connected clock, not a bespoke build; there is no maker/builder — Marques configures it himself via the companion app. His through-line/POV: it isn't the best-sounding or best-built option, but the pixel-art look and customization make it his new favorite clock. He walks the iOS/Android app's per-face "mini apps" (clock, weather, stopwatch, stocks, custom messages, Twitter mentions), the ability to fix one face or auto-rotate through several, IFTTT compatibility, and his favorite use — a live YouTube subscriber count that updates far faster than YouTube's own site — which he calls addicting and motivating and plans to leave running full-time in the studio. He flags the $200 price as steep but argues the huge free mini-app store earns it over time, and points viewers to a cheaper similar product ("AA box") credited to another creator.

## Key claims
- [2016-12-17] The matte-black box behind him is the LaMetric Time, a customizable connected clock that also functions as a Bluetooth speaker.
- [2016-12-17] He first shared it on Snapchat, and enough viewer curiosity is what made it worth a quick standalone video.
- [2016-12-17] There are other products like it, but in his opinion this is one of if not the best-looking — even if maybe not the best-sounding or best-built.
- [2016-12-17] The appeal is the pixel-art aesthetic plus a matte-black shape that can blend into a bedside table, studio, or office.
- [2016-12-17] It has an iOS and Android app that lets you customize exactly what shows on the clock, and it is very responsive.
- [2016-12-17] Each "face" is a mini app with its own parameters — e.g. the weather mini app lets you pick which properties (temperature, wind, pressure, precipitation) to display.
- [2016-12-17] You can pin one face permanently, auto-rotate through faces (weather, then stocks, etc.), or step through them manually with the button on top.
- [2016-12-17] It has IFTTT ("if this then that") compatibility, enabling more creative use cases like light control.
- [2016-12-17] His favorite mini app is a live YouTube subscriber count — genuinely live, updating roughly every couple of minutes versus YouTube's own site, which he says updates maybe twice an hour.
- [2016-12-17] He finds the live sub-count strangely addicting and a little motivating, and plans to leave that one on his studio desk full-time.
- [2016-12-17] A large store of free mini apps (custom messages, latest Twitter mentions, occupancy counts) is what makes the different placements useful; he's considering buying another for home.
- [2016-12-17] The $200 price is "kind of steep" and he wasn't sure he'd get his money's worth, but the huge amount of apps and functionality make it worth it over time.
- [2016-12-17] He links a similar, cheaper pixel-art app-controlled clock — the "AA box" — heavily discounted for the holidays, crediting another creator for finding it.
