# Ferrari Luce is the Most Controversial Ferrari Ever (@AutoFocus, 2026-05-25)

## Summary
A **world-exclusive first walk-around** of the **Ferrari Luce**, Ferrari's first fully
electric car, shot in Italy on a borrowed DJI Osmo. Marques frames the Luce as a collision
of two opposing design pressures — a Ferrari (lightweight, aero, sports car) vs. an EV
(smooth low-drag "blob," heavy, spacious) — resolved with the help of **LoveFrom (Jony
Ive's company)**. He withholds the driving impressions for a separate video and instead
does a detailed exterior + interior tour, raving about the tactile, metal/glass/leather
interior UI (physical vent bezels, cutout displays over real gauges, satisfying manettino
clicks) and calling it "the best back seat experience of any Ferrari ever." Marques-solo.

## Key claims
- 2026-05-25: The Ferrari Luce is a **quad-motor fully electric** car, 120+ kWh battery,
  **300+ miles of range**, five seats (three in the rear), priced very high "because it's a
  Ferrari." (Marques — presenting the reveal)
- Design story: LoveFrom (Jony Ive) collaborated on the design, which is "why this looks
  nothing like any previous Ferraris"; the EV/Ferrari tension pushes the shape toward a
  smooth low-drag form. Notes exterior-edge windshield wipers (no cowl to hide them), active
  aero gills, a huge front air gap/splitter feeding a canopy for downforce. (Marques)
- Interior UI praise (a recurring MKBHD lens — physical + digital done well): everything you
  touch is **metal, aluminum, glass, or leather**; cutouts in the displays reveal a real
  physical tachometer needle and secondary indented screens; **rotating metal vent bezels**
  that visibly open/close; a glass gear-selector; a magnetic key that slots in to "start"
  the (silent) car; a launch-control pull that's "the most satisfying piece of hardware."
  (Marques)
- Power figures (as he'd expect on Ferrari's site): range mode ~530 km / 320 kW; tour mode
  70% power (460 kW); **performance mode = full 1040 hp / 725 kW, 0–60 in 2.5s.** (Marques)
- Packaging: EV layout pushes the driver forward and rear passengers back, yielding "the
  best back seat experience of any Ferrari ever" and possibly the best of any five-seater
  EV; three rear seats, real legroom, auto-opening doors. (Marques)
- Verdict framing: no real comp (performance-ish like a Lucid Air, size-wise like a
  Purosangue), predicts the design will be **polarizing**, hopes the interior UI ideas
  "trickle down" to other cars. Driving review to come separately. (Marques)
