# HD Tutorial: Take Screenshots in Windows Vista (@mkbhd, 2009-02-10)

## Summary
Origin-era how-to. Marques (age 15, on a 17-inch laptop) demonstrates two built-in ways to
capture the screen in **Windows Vista**: (1) the **Snipping Tool** (launched by typing
"snip" in the Start menu) — click-drag to capture a region, then save as JPEG/image, email
it, copy it, or mark it up with the built-in pen/highlighter/eraser; and (2) the
**Print Screen (PrtSc)** key to grab the full desktop, then paste into Paint to crop and
edit. He frames the Snipping Tool's region capture as the more useful of the two.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-10] **How-to:** Windows Vista's **Snipping Tool** captures a dragged region and
  supports save/email/copy plus basic pen/highlighter/eraser annotation.
- [2009-02-10] **How-to:** **Print Screen** grabs the whole desktop; paste into Paint (or
  Photoshop) to crop/edit.
- [2009-02-10] Prefers region capture for grabbing "a particular image" over full-screen
  Print Screen.
