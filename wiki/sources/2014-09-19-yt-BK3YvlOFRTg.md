# Moto X (2014) Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-19)

## Summary
The full review of the 2014 Moto X — a phone that lets Marques restate two core doctrines. First, **the
experience-over-specs thesis** the original Moto X made famous ("it made us realize that not everything
is about specs — you can make a really great phone that doesn't have the highest-end specs"). Second,
**stock-Android restraint as a virtue**: Motorola avoids bloatware and "change for the sake of change,"
which is exactly why the mid-spec Moto X's performance lands in his **top three** of all Android phones.
Its weak points (battery, camera) he frames as honest **trade-offs of a $99-on-contract price**, not
flaws — his grade-on-the-price-tier method.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-19] **Experience over specs (the thesis restated).** The original Moto X "made us realize that
  not everything is about specs"; the 2014 model takes the same approach — not the highest-end or most
  expensive, but "does a lot of things about the experience right."
- [2014-09-19] **Build upgraded with real metal** — an aluminum frame makes it "so much more solid,"
  Moto Maker customization returns. Buyer caution: **avoid the leather back** — every leather Moto X he
  handled was badly worn; "leather is a softer material, it wears much more easily, and it's not friendly
  with water." The soft-touch plastic is nearly as nice.
- [2014-09-19] **Stock-Android restraint = a virtue (the durable point).** "So many companies are trying
  to add value via software… but the adjustments they make are sometimes just **change for the sake of
  change**, or crazy features people will never use. Motorola has done basically none of these" — no
  bloatware, fast updates, useful-only tweaks (custom "OK Jarvis" hotword, multi-notification peek, Moto
  Assist/Actions/Display working quietly in the background).
- [2014-09-19] **Near-stock → excellent performance despite mid specs.** Snapdragon 801 + 2 GB, but "the
  performance is right up there with the best… top three with the GPe HTC One M8 and the OnePlus One, even
  beating the Nexus 5." Evidence for his real-world-performance-over-raw-specs and stock-is-fast beliefs.
- [2014-09-19] **The weak points are trade-offs of the price, not flaws (grade-on-the-tier).** "Even
  though this is technically Motorola's flagship, it's still only a $99-on-contract / $499-off phone." The
  battery is "meh" (2300 mAh, ~2.5 hr screen-on vs 3–4 on other flagships, sealed) — "it's not a problem,
  it's a trade-off." The 5.2" 1080p AMOLED "pops" (saturated, "makes images look better than they are").
- [2014-09-19] **Front-facing "speaker" caveat** — the top grille is only the earpiece; just one bottom
  speaker, so no true stereo (still better than rear-firing).
