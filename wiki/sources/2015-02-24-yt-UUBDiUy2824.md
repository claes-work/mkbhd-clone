# OnePlus One & Cyanogenmod 12! (@mkbhd, 2015-02-24)

## Summary
An impressions video that is the **purest statement of his control-over-software doctrine**. The OnePlus
One's "lives or dies by software" verdict hit a snag — **OnePlus and Cyanogen split** (OnePlus is building
its own Oxygen OS) — so rather than wait for updates, Marques **flashed CyanogenMod 12 (Lollipop) onto the
phone himself**. His takeaway: "that's the beauty of Android — if you get impatient or want to change
something, you can just go out and do it."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-02-24] **The verdict tested.** "Every time I talk about this phone I mention it lives or dies by
  the software" — Cyanogen did well for a while (constant updates), "but then it hit a hiccup… OnePlus and
  Cyanogen split, so they're no longer working together."
- [2015-02-24] **The fallout for owners:** OnePlus is building its own **Oxygen OS** (talented hires,
  due March, presumably Lollipop-based); in the meantime, no Cyanogen updates.
- [2015-02-24] **So he flashed CM12 himself (control-over-software).** Impatient, he flashed the latest
  **CyanogenMod 12** (Android 5.0) onto his OnePlus One (root + a Windows machine + USB), documenting the
  steps on Snapchat first. "That's kind of the beauty of Android — if you get impatient with something, or
  you really want to change the UI or feature set, you can just go out and do it."
- [2015-02-24] **What a custom ROM buys you** (over stock): on-screen vs off-screen nav-button choice,
  fully customizable quick-settings tiles, screen-off gestures (draw a circle for the camera — though it
  "activated in my pocket"), a built-in file manager, and theming (he keeps it near-stock — "I'm boring
  like that," e.g. reverting the circular battery icon to the stock one). Kept all of Lollipop's Material
  Design animations.
- [2015-02-24] **He also flashed the Franco kernel** for better battery ("second day full-time, pretty
  good so far"). Aside: the OnePlus One is now easier to buy (invite-free every Tuesday), though you could
  also wait for the coming HTC One M9 / Galaxy S6.
