# On Apple's New 2012 Desktops! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-23)

## Summary
A reaction to Apple's Oct 2012 event, focused on one argument: **what actually counts as a "true
desktop."** He classes the Mac Mini and the new ultra-thin iMac as "glorified MacBook Pros" — mobile
motherboards, mobile CPUs, mobile GPUs in desktop bodies, barely user-upgradeable — and argues only
the Mac Pro qualifies as a real desktop because its components (GPU, RAM, drives) are swappable. It's
his **upgradeability/repairability value stated at length**, plus a concrete two-tier Mac Pro proposal.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-23] **The "true desktop" definition — the thesis**: only the Mac Pro "has a desktop
  motherboard, desktop chip, desktop CPU, desktop graphics card and desktop available upgraded
  components — which means I can go in and swap out the RAM, I can change out the graphics card at any
  time." Upgradeability is the criterion, not size or power.
- [2012-10-23] **The Mac Mini and iMac downgraded to "glorified MacBook Pros"**: both use mobile
  internals in a desktop chassis. The new iMac "is absurdly thin… beautiful by all accounts, no one can
  disagree — but so thin that the only thing you can upgrade (27" only) is the RAM; you need to take
  apart the 21.5" iMac just to upgrade the RAM. I can't really qualify these as true thoroughbred
  desktops." Beauty and efficiency conceded; upgradeability withheld.
- [2012-10-23] **The value is stated as loss, not preference**: the thinness that makes the iMac elegant
  is exactly what costs it upgradeability — a dated, explicit articulation of the thin-vs-serviceable
  tradeoff that recurs in his later repairability positions.
- [2012-10-23] **A concrete two-tier Mac Pro proposal**: rather than kill the Mac Pro, "totally revamp it
  like they did the iMac and keep the full true desktop form factor with upgradeable components" — a
  smaller **mid-range tower** (Ivy Bridge, user-swappable GPU/SSD/HDD) starting "around $1,800," plus a
  **high-end tower** keeping the Pro name for "the pros doing video editing in Hollywood and After Effects"
  at ~$2,400. Design thinking, not just critique.
- [2012-10-23] **Own-stakes disclosure**: "my personal favorite was probably the new iMac… but I won't be
  getting one because it's not quite a true desktop — I have a Mac Pro down here and I'll be waiting for
  the next Mac Pro." He credits the product he admires while explaining why it doesn't fit his needs.
- [2012-10-23] **Real-time event coverage, dated**: "I did some tweeting throughout the entire event
  giving my impressions" — a dated instance of live-tweeting keynotes, and he links peers' recap videos:
  "people like **TLDToday**, **Soldier Knows Best**."
- [2012-10-23] **Even-handed close**: "Apple did a great job today, they introduced a lot of great new
  products" — praise for the event overall despite the desktop-form-factor gripe.
