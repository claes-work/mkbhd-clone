# Google Assistant vs Siri! (2016) (@mkbhd, 2016-10-22)

## Summary
A head-to-head voice-assistant comparison (Pixel XL / Android 7.1 Google Assistant vs. iPhone 7 Plus /
iOS 10 Siri). The clone-relevant value is his **judging criterion for assistants** ("how much like a
robot you have to talk to it") and a dated verdict — **Google has now passed Siri** on conversational
context — plus a self-aware note that this flips Siri's original 2012 strength.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-10-22] **THE criterion: "how much like a robot you have to talk to it."** "The way I judge these
  voice assistants is how much like a robot you have to talk to it to get it to understand you — you
  shouldn't have to ask the same question three different ways. I think Google has passed Siri in that
  department." A reusable, human-centered evaluation frame.
- [2016-10-22] **Dated verdict: Google is more conversational/capable now (a role reversal).** "Google is
  a bit more capable and conversational now, especially vs. Siri in the past — because of its AI learning
  it can do 'show me pictures of a cat I took at my house last week.' There's more conversational flow;
  you can ask about previous answers. That actually used to be Siri's biggest strength vs. Google Now."
  A clean before/after industry read (2012 → 2016).
- [2016-10-22] **The read-it-out vs. show-it-prettier trade — with a use-case argument.** "Google reads
  the answer out more; Siri says 'here you go' and displays it, often more beautifully. But when I'm using
  a voice assistant it's because I *can't* look at my phone — maybe I'm driving — so I like when it reads
  it out more." Judges by the actual use context, not screenshots.
- [2016-10-22] **Context/conversation demonstrated concretely.** Notes Google chains follow-ups ("how
  tall is he?" after "who is the president?") while Siri often needs the full question re-asked, and that
  Google surfaces *your own* Google Photos ("show my photos from yesterday") where Siri falls back to a
  Bing web search. Evidence-based, not vibes.
- [2016-10-22] **Even-handed on Siri's remaining edges.** Credits Siri for richer visual answers (a
  Yahoo Finance stock graph, a full box score) — "Siri gave me more than I asked for." Refuses a one-sided
  verdict despite naming a clear winner.
- [2016-10-22] **The long-view framing.** "Interesting to see it now vs. 2012, and I'll be interested to
  see it in 2020." Explicitly situating the comparison on a multi-year arc (the assistants-improve-over-
  time throughline).
