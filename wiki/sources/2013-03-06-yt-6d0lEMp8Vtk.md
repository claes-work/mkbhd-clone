# Chromebook Pixel Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-03-06)

## Summary
The promised full review — closing the Pixel arc opened by the Explained + unboxing
videos. Its verdict is one of his cleaner paradox-verdicts: **"a beautiful piece of
engineering… that you should never buy,"** because the hardware is the best in the
business but Chrome OS can't yet justify it. Restates the Halo-product framing and makes a
prescient **"give it Android apps and it'll sell out"** prediction.

## Key claims
- [2013-03-06] **The paradox verdict — best hardware you shouldn't buy**: "as a summary,
  this is a beautiful piece of engineering, a really good-looking, solidly built piece of
  hardware that you should never buy… it's the definition of a Halo product." Separates the
  quality of the object from the buy recommendation (a distinct, honest review move).
- [2013-03-06] **The $1,300 bar — and it clears it**: "if I spend $1,300 on a laptop, it
  better have a nice keyboard, a nice trackpad, a nice display, nice build — I expect the
  most when you go over [$1,000], and the Pixel actually does deliver that." Sets the
  price-justified-expectation and grades against it.
- [2013-03-06] **Where Chrome OS falls short — native apps**: "apps like Photoshop and
  video-editing apps and native software are really irreplaceable still… most people would
  opt for Windows or OS X over a Chromebook every time because of the ability to run native
  apps." Names the exact gap (his software-must-catch-up-to-hardware thesis).
- [2013-03-06] **A prescient prediction — Android apps would unlock it**: "Google, if you
  want to make a little money… if you give the Chromebook Pixel the ability to run Android
  apps, prepare yourself to sell out — that would be a real unlocker of the potential."
  (Chromebooks gained Android-app support years later.) Logs a falsifiable, later-correct
  call.
- [2013-03-06] **The touchscreen-laptop gripe, restated concretely**: "my number one
  problem is how much the hinge wobbles when I try to touch the screen — the Pixel is no
  exception; I found myself holding the back of the display while I use the touch screen."
  His consistent, use-tested objection.
- [2013-03-06] **Benchmarks placed honestly**: scores "a lot higher on BrowserMark than the
  cheaper Chromebooks, but still less powerful than the Hackintosh Pro running Chrome." Puts
  the number in context against his own machine.
- [2013-03-06] **Speakers/trackpad praised specifically**: "the speakers are mind-blowingly
  good… hidden beneath the keyboard," the trackpad "magnificent." Credits the details.
