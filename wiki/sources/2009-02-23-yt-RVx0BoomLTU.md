# HD Tutorial: Complete Rocketdock Walkthrough (@mkbhd, 2009-02-23)

## Summary
A full walkthrough (Marques, age 15) of the **RocketDock** application dock for Windows —
the freeware that gives Windows a Mac OS X-style dock. He tours his own dock layout
(browsers, Office, Photoshop, comms incl. Skype + webcam, tools incl. CamStudio/Camtasia,
media) and then the settings tabs: run-at-startup, portable INI, minimize-windows-to-dock,
running-app indicators, icon quality/opacity/size, hover effects (zoom bubble / plateau /
zoom flat), position, and auto-hide. Notable persona signal: he keeps **zero icons on his
desktop** and runs everything through the dock — an early marker of the clean, minimalist
setup aesthetic. He mentions the recording machine is a high-end gaming laptop, which is why
he can afford "high quality" icon rendering.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-23] Runs a **completely icon-free desktop**, using RocketDock as the launcher —
  "that's why I use a rocket dock in the first place."
- [2009-02-23] The tutorial machine is a "pretty intense gaming machine... a laptop but
  it's very good," which is why he leaves icon quality on High.
- [2009-02-23] Chooses **zoom bubble** hover and top-edge, centered, flush placement; runs
  full opacity so icons are always visible; disables extra CPU-costing effects (icon bounce,
  Über icon effects) on principle — "that just takes a tiny bit more CPU and I'd rather not."
- [2009-02-23] The version shown is RocketDock 1.3.5 (Punk Software).

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "you may have noticed I have absolutely no icons on my whole desktop so that is why I use a rocket dock in first place"

> "I'd rather not the Uber icon effects I have not found that to work very well"
