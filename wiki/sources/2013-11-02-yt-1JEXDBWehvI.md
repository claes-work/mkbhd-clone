# How Google Now is Taking Over! (@mkbhd, 2013-11-02)

## Summary
An analytical/prediction piece: Google Now is becoming the anticipatory-assistant "face"
that will spread across every Android form factor (phones, glass, smartwatches, even
refrigerators), with the lightweight Android 4.4 as the backbone. A ★ **prescient ambient-
computing / assistant-everywhere prediction** — an early node on his AI-interest thread.

## Key claims
- [2013-11-02] **The prediction — Google Now as the cross-form-factor "face"**: "as Android
  spreads into new form factors and markets, so will Google Now. When there are smartwatches
  running Android in 2014, people won't want full home screens on their wrists — just Google Now
  cards. When there are refrigerators running Android in 2015, same thing. Android 4.4 can be the
  skeleton behind the scenes, and Google Now and its cards can be the face." A prescient ambient-
  computing / assistant-everywhere thesis (predates the smart-assistant era).
- [2013-11-02] **Traces Google Now's evolution — a timeline**: from a persistent search box →
  the predictive-cards app (Jelly Bean, July 2012, Nexus-only) → Gmail/package/boarding-pass
  integration → "Okay Google" → the dedicated left-swipe panel on the Nexus 5. His teach-the-
  history register.
- [2013-11-02] **Frames it as an emerging ecosystem**: "it's almost become its own little
  operating system — its own ecosystem of things it plugs into and things that plug into it, not
  just Google services but third-party restaurants, shipping, stores, events." Reads the
  strategic significance.
- [2013-11-02] **Grounds it in the company's stated mission**: quotes Sundar Pichai — "as we
  reach the next billion people we want to do it on the latest version of Android" — and ties it
  to KitKat's low memory footprint (able to run on low-end devices *and* wearables/appliances).
  His incentive/strategy reading.
- [2013-11-02] **Honest about the gaps**: "Google Now isn't perfect — it won't do hardware
  control ('Okay Google, turn off Wi-Fi' returns a 'not supported' card, so they thought about it
  and it just doesn't work), and camera-search totally disappeared in the 2.5 update. Please
  bring that back." Names the real limitations.
