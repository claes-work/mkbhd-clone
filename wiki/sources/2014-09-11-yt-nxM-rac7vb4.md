# Apple Watch Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-11)

## Summary
First impressions of the just-announced **Apple Watch** — the **payoff of his 2013–14 prediction** that
Apple would enter wearables by end of 2014 (announced 2014-09-09, ships early 2015). His verdict is
notably **mixed/skeptical**, and it contains one of his most quotable **anti-brand-halo / even-handed**
statements: strip the Apple logo off and imagine Samsung shipping a square, knob-having, phone-locked
watch — "you might have laughed it off the stage." He praises the personalization (three tiers, two
sizes, the removable-band standard) but is worried about the **digital crown** and a less finger-friendly
UI than Android Wear. A landmark for the even-handedness doctrine and the wearable thread.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-11] **Prediction validated (he didn't get hands-on, and says so).** "I wasn't cool enough to
  get some hands-on time with the watch, so I'll link some" — he grounds impressions in others' footage
  and is explicit about the limit. (He'd predicted an Apple wearable by end of 2014.)
- [2014-09-11] **Praise — personalization + bands.** Three versions (Watch / Sport / Edition), **two
  sizes** for every variant (1.5" / 1.65") — "Apple seems to be realizing one size does not fit all." The
  **removable-band standard** is the sleeper feature: "that's going to make the bands huge… third-party
  manufacturers now making bands the same way they make cases for the iPhone." The magnetic leather loop
  is "brilliant."
- [2014-09-11] **Skepticism — the digital crown.** Apple makes "a huge deal" of the knob (zoom/pan/
  scroll without covering the display), but he's "really concerned" — accidental bumps/crossed arms could
  scroll, and "it reminds me of the BlackBerry Pearl, which had a knob because it needed it" (not a
  touchscreen). He expects most people to use it as a touchscreen watch anyway.
- [2014-09-11] **The UI is less finger-friendly than Android Wear** — cluttered app-grid home screen vs
  the big, simple swipe-through cards of Android Wear.
- [2014-09-11] **The anti-brand-halo bottom line (canonical).** "If you take the Apple logo off this and
  imagine it was made by Samsung… you might have laughed it off the stage. But because it's an Apple
  Watch, we're willing to give it a shot." He explicitly names the brand halo as the reason it gets a
  fair hearing that a competitor's identical product wouldn't.
- [2014-09-11] **The category verdict:** "I'm not convinced yet — smartwatches as a whole have a lot of
  convincing to do." Reserves final judgment for hands-on and the early-2015 release.
