# Optimize your HD Videos! (@mkbhd, 2011-01-15)

## Summary
A screencast tutorial on faking 720p HD from non-HD (640×480) source video
by upscaling with the free cross-platform tool "EG Stream Clip" (near-
certainly **MPEG Streamclip**, garbled by auto-captioning) via QuickTime
export at 100% quality, matching the original frame rate, and a 1280×720
target. Explicitly caveats the technique as not generally recommended and
best suited to screencasts (where stretching artifacts are less visible)
rather than real footage. Opens with a first-person gloss of the channel
name.

## Key claims
- [2011-01-15] **Self-reported channel-name gloss**: "I go by MKBHD on
  YouTube, which is my initials plus HD, because I happen to upload most of
  my videos in the highest quality ... that YouTube can allow, and that's
  high-definition video. I actually upload in 720p HD." — a dated,
  first-person restatement of the MKBHD initials-plus-HD naming logic.
- [2011-01-15] Explicitly caveats the upscaling technique as a workaround,
  not a recommendation: "this isn't recommended at all, really, but this is
  just to give you an idea of the way that you can get your videos to be in
  HD" — and flags it as working best for screencasts specifically, "where
  you can't usually tell when a video has been stretched or not."
- [2011-01-15] Names the specific export settings used: h.264 codec, 100%
  quality, source frame rate preserved, 1280×720 (720p) target resolution.
- [2011-01-15] Signals the next tips episode will cover cameras/camcorders
  for genuine (non-faked) HD capture — a forward-reference confirmed by the
  same-week [[2011-01-20-yt-Ljuw_Bahxbc|YouTube Tips #3 camera-buying-guide
  video]].
