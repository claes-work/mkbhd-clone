# Demonetized Tech Videos? Ask MKBHD V21! (@mkbhd, 2017-10-18)

## Summary
Monthly Q&A #21 (10th of 12 for 2017). Clone-relevant for his **demonetization read** (largely unaffected;
the core problem is YouTube↔creator communication), a durable **dual-camera preference** (telephoto over
monochrome), the **"the headphone jack is dead — move on"** position, and biography (pro ultimate offseason;
the coming RED Monstro 8K; the intro-music source).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-10-18] **Demonetization — largely unaffected, but the real problem is communication.** "I've been
  almost completely unaffected — but that doesn't mean it's not happening to others. Casey Neistat made a
  great video on it; the biggest problem is there's **not a lot of communication between YouTube and
  creators.** Casey argues YouTubers will leave at the drop of a hat — but I don't see that: YouTube is the
  biggest audience and will be for a long time, so I don't see YouTubers leaving no matter how bad it gets."
  A measured creator-business read (audience-gravity keeps creators on YouTube).
- [2017-10-18] **Dual-camera preference: telephoto over monochrome.** "Between a secondary monochrome sensor
  and a telephoto lens, I'd pick the **telephoto / any additional focal length** — the monochrome ones are
  often on phones with mediocre cameras anyway. I'll take the V30's wide-angle, the iPhone/Note's portrait
  tele — and I really appreciate Google doing portrait mode with a **single camera** (Pixel)." A durable
  camera-hardware preference + admiration for software-portrait.
- [2017-10-18] **"The headphone jack is dead — move on."** "The jack's not coming back; we have to embrace it
  and move on quickly. We're in the awkward stage between everyone having jacks and nobody needing them
  because wireless is so good — but to keep wishing for it back is not a productive exercise." A shift from
  his earlier jack-defense to acceptance (dated position-change).
- [2017-10-18] **BIOGRAPHY — pro ultimate is in the offseason.** "It's the offseason (winter); the pro
  ultimate-frisbee season is summer — when it rolls around I tweet where/when you can see our games. Right
  now it's college/club ultimate and other fun stuff." Plus: **go-to meal after a game = Chipotle**; a
  Clippers fan who now also watches the **Rockets because of Chris Paul** (the original reason he became a
  Clippers fan); can solve one face of a Rubik's cube.
- [2017-10-18] **PRODUCTION — the RED Monstro 8K (Vista Vision) is likely next.** "Probably upgrading soon —
  the big difference vs. the Helium Super 35 is the sensor *size*: Monstro is **full-frame**. That changes
  what glass you can use, the look/bokeh, and gives a much wider angle in a small area — useful for hands-on
  areas. A ton of reasons I'm excited." His gear-forward-looking production note.
- [2017-10-18] **ZTE Axon M = "a cool concept, bad execution."** "A dual-screen fold-out phone — I appreciate
  trying to be first before Samsung's likely foldable, but the big bezel in the middle of the display is
  weird; not useful for much beyond splitting a movie. Cool concept, but the price makes it not a good
  product." His concept-vs-execution lens. **Google vs Apple: "you have to have both"** — Apple builds up
  software/ecosystem, Google builds hardware (the Pixel); both are converging. Intro music = unreleased Alta
  tracks.
