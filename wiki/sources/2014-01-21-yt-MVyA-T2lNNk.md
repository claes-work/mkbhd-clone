# New Mac Pro Performance Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-01-21)

## Summary
A benchmark-driven performance deep-dive on his new Mac Pro. Persona value is the config-
choice reasoning (**8-core over 12-core** for a balance of single-threaded everyday speed
and multi-threaded editing power — the van-vs-18-wheeler analogy), the continuity with his
past workstation projects (Fastest Mac Mini, Hackintosh Pro), and a notable methodology
nuance: **he uses benchmarks freely here** (a workstation, where they're not gamed) — in
contrast to refusing them on Samsung phones.

## Key claims
- [2014-01-21] **Why 8-core over 12-core — the single-vs-multi-thread balance**: "each time you
  move up in cores you move down in clock speed [3.7 → 3.5 → 3.0 → 2.7 GHz]. Higher clocks mean
  better single-core for everyday apps; more cores mean better multi-core for the big apps. As
  someone using this as both a beast video-editing rig AND a day-to-day machine, I want the
  balance — all 8 cores kick in for Premiere/Photoshop, but at 3 GHz single-core, Chrome and
  browsing stay quick." His reasoned config choice (the "van vs 18-wheeler" analogy).
- [2014-01-21] **Benchmarks used freely — the methodology contrast**: runs Blackmagic Disk Speed,
  Geekbench 3, Novabench, and Cinebench "the benchmarking apps you guys shared with me on
  Twitter and that I've used in past projects." He treats benchmarks as valid for a workstation
  (vs the Note-3 review, where he refused Samsung's *gamed* phone benchmarks — benchmarks are a
  tool, the objection is to manipulation).
- [2014-01-21] **Project continuity — compares to his own past builds**: "the Fastest Mac Mini
  scored ~10k on Geekbench, the Hackintosh Pro 15,800; this Mac Pro kicks out 26,000 multi-
  threaded… the PCI-based SSDs blow away my old single Vertex 4 and the Hackintosh's RAID-0
  pair." Anchors the numbers to his documented projects.
- [2014-01-21] **Credits the workstation GPUs honestly**: "the dual AMD FirePro D700s topped
  Cinebench at ~86 fps — this isn't gaming performance [they're not gaming cards], but a
  representation of how much power production suites can take advantage of." Scopes the claim.
- [2014-01-21] **Editing workflow named**: "when I'm rendering out videos in Adobe Premiere or
  projects in Photoshop, all eight cores kick in." Confirms his editing software (Premiere).
- [2014-01-21] **8-core surprisingly close to 12-core on CPU**: "the 8-core scored 1,223 on
  Cinebench CPU, surprisingly not far behind the 12-core — that impressed me most." Validates his
  choice with data.
