# Chrome OS: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-05-17)

## Summary
An "Explained!" entry making a thesis argument, not just a definition: **Chrome OS is "a little bit
ahead of its time."** He explains what it is (the browser *is* the whole OS), why it's not selling
(cloud-only computing before people were ready), and why Google Drive is what makes the low-spec
Chromebook viable as an ecosystem. He closes by describing his own dream Chromebook — which reads as a
near-exact prediction of the Chromebook Pixel that Google shipped the following year.

## Key claims
- [2012-05-17] **The thesis**: "I think Chrome OS is a little bit ahead of its time — it's a little bit
  too far into the future and it's trying to make its way here in the present, and that's why it's not
  being so successful right now." A structural explanation for a commercial failure, not a dismissal.
- [2012-05-17] **The ecosystem argument**: cheap low-spec Chromebooks (~8–16 GB storage) only make sense
  because Google Drive backfills them — "suddenly you have 25 gigs of external storage on Google's cloud
  servers… it's a genius system." He grounds it in his own disclosed 25 GB Drive tier
  ([[2012-04-25-yt-0FbjsNsfEmE]]).
- [2012-05-17] **The dream-Chromebook description — a near-exact prediction**: "a MacBook Air form factor
  running Chrome OS, a massive battery, maybe 8 gigs of internal storage, a massive amount of stuff in
  Google Drive, ultra-low-voltage processor, and have it say Google on the back… make it maybe matte
  black." Google shipped the Chromebook Pixel (premium aluminum, Google-branded) in Feb 2013 — a dated,
  checkable forecast.
- [2012-05-17] **Bounds the use case honestly**: "obviously you can't replace your full desktop because
  video editing apps and Photoshop… can't really be replaced in the cloud completely yet… I think
  Chromebooks are a definitely awesome option for a **secondary machine**, and it's what I'm using mine
  for right now."
- [2012-05-17] **A retail-reality observation**: "I bet you didn't know you could buy Chromebooks in your
  local Best Buy — have you even seen one on a shelf? I have, but I look for them." Naming a distribution
  problem behind the sales problem.
- [2012-05-17] **Notes Chrome web store apps maturing** from "glorified websites or shortcuts" into real
  applications with native-feeling interfaces.
- [2012-05-17] **Long-standing Chrome OS interest confirmed**: he references the CR-48 pilot (he had
  applied to the Chrome OS pilot program in Dec 2010) — a multi-year through-line.
