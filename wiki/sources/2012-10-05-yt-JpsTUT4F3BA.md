# LG Nexus Rumors! (@mkbhd, 2012-10-05)

## Summary
A rumor round-up predicting the next Nexus phone will be an LG-built, stock-Android version of the LG
Optimus G. The analytically interesting bit is his read on **LG's pattern — consistently good hardware
held back by a poor skin** — which is exactly the argument for why an LG Nexus (great hardware + stock
software) would be strong. The prediction landed: the Nexus 4 was that phone.

## Key claims
- [2012-10-05] **The prediction — an LG-built Nexus from the Optimus G**: "a ton of details are emerging
  suggesting the next Nexus will be made by LG, a pure vanilla Android version of the LG Optimus G." The
  Nexus 4 (announced later that month) was exactly this — a **correct, dated call**.
- [2012-10-05] **The "good hardware, bad skin" thesis about LG** — his structural read: "people don't
  really notice this, but LG has consistently put really good hardware in their devices — it's just kind
  of held back by their software. TouchWiz and Sense are fine, but LG's skin has been clunky and a little
  unintuitive for a while." Therefore "combining that excellent LG hardware with amazing stock Android
  should produce a pretty sweet Nexus." A clean hardware/software-separation argument applied to an OEM.
- [2012-10-05] **Reasons about Google's partner logic**: "Google has a habit every year of picking a
  top-of-the-line manufacturer to produce the once-per-year flagship Nexus" — the same
  who-dominates-the-category structural read as the wishlist video ([[2012-09-01-yt-APQqX8Xd5TA]]).
- [2012-10-05] **Excited about the chip specifically**: the rumored **Snapdragon S4 Pro** is "pretty much
  hands down the best mobile chip out there right now… I'm excited to see the first S4 Pro phones in the
  first place." Rumored specs recited: 4.7" 1280×768 IPS, 2 GB RAM, 8 MP, 16/32 GB sealed storage, NFC,
  and — flagged as a long shot — wireless charging (which shipped).
- [2012-10-05] **Playful naming riff**: it can't be "Galaxy Nexus 2" because Galaxy is a Samsung brand;
  he floats "LG Optimus Nexus" / "Optimus Nexus." A small window into his humor.
- [2012-10-05] **A self-aware annotation joke** tying to the YouTube-app critique: "if you want to check
  out my wishlist video, that's an annotation — it's not on mobile yet, I should rant about that" (he had,
  three days later — [[2012-10-08-yt-eFcpRIwPqRc]]).
