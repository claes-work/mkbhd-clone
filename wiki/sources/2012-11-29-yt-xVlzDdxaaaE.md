# Nexus 4 vs Galaxy S3 vs Galaxy Nexus! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-29)

## Summary
A three-way holiday buyer's comparison across the Nexus 4, Galaxy S3, and Galaxy Nexus — "geared to
help you make your decision." Consolidates the individual reviews, but contributes two dated,
reusable framings: **"think about what you do on your phone"** as the way to resolve the LTE-vs-HSPA+
tradeoff, and the explicit **"root the S3 into a SuperNexus"** advice — turning a comparison into a
build recommendation.

## Key claims
- [2012-11-29] **"Think about what you do on your phone" — the decision heuristic for LTE**: "if you're
  thinking about ending your Verizon contract, think about what you do — do you do a lot of web browsing,
  video watching, things that require a lot of data? Then stick with a Verizon phone. But if you mostly do
  social networking, Twitter, Google+, share photos… the HSPA+ on the Nexus 4 will do you well." Resolve
  a spec tradeoff by the buyer's actual usage — his signature buyer's-help move.
- [2012-11-29] **"Root the S3 into a SuperNexus" — a build recommendation inside a comparison**: "you can
  root [the Galaxy S3] and put stock Android on it… you'll get basically an upgraded Nexus 4 — the AMOLED
  display, the larger display, the nice camera, the removable back — if you're willing to go through the
  software process." He recommends *making* the ideal phone, not just buying one
  ([[2012-11-13-yt-RQy0NeVnxLo]]).
- [2012-11-29] **Nexus 4 speed superlative, honestly hedged**: "the fastest, snappiest Android experience
  of any phone I have ever used" — with acceptable (not spectacular) battery, consistent with the review.
- [2012-11-29] **AMOLED-vs-IPS tradeoff, even-handed**: the Galaxy Nexus's AMOLED gives "deeper blacks,
  higher contrast, punchier colors" and a truly black notification bar; the Nexus 4's IPS "gets a lot
  brighter, super easy to read in broad daylight." He names who each display suits ("if you've had trouble
  reading AMOLED outdoors… consider the IPS").
- [2012-11-29] **Camera and speaker judged consistently with the reviews**: the Nexus 4's 8 MP "on par
  with the Galaxy S3" and a real upgrade over the Galaxy Nexus's 5 MP; neither Nexus speaker is "particularly
  good."
- [2012-11-29] **The generalization**: "you can compare [the S3] to basically any of the high-end phones —
  the One X, the Note 2… you get up-to-date hardware, and if you root, up-to-date software." The
  root-for-stock-software logic extended to the whole flagship class.
- [2012-11-29] Purpose stated up front: "this video is geared to help you make your decision — which one
  will you pick?" Buyer's-help framing, not a single verdict.
