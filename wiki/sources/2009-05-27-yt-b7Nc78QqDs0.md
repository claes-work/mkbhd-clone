# Reviewed: Matias USB 2.0 Keyboard (@mkbhd, 2009-05-27)

## Summary
The review half of the Matias USB 2.0 keyboard-with-dock two-step (unboxing:
[[2009-05-09-yt-gRzaNjetlIg]], "about two, two and a half weeks ago"). Camera-filmed
hardware review at age 15. He frames it explicitly as "final impressions" versus the
unboxing's "first impressions," and posts it as a **video response** to the unboxing — the
origin-era mechanism for chaining a product's two videos together. The review itself
contains his earliest documented **key-feel critique** ("very soft travel... my typing was
slowed down"), grounded in real use (typing school essays for language arts). He closes by
announcing the **1,000-subscriber giveaway** as "coming very soon." ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-27] **Two-step structure named explicitly**: "that was just my first impressions
  video; this is going to be my final impressions and basically overall what I think."
- [2009-05-27] **Video responses link the pair**: "I'll try to post this as a video response
  to the unboxing of this keyboard" — a period-specific YouTube feature used as series glue.
- [2009-05-27] **Unboxing→review gap measured**: the unboxing went up "about two, two and a
  half weeks ago," matching his stated 1–2-week promise (slightly over).
- [2009-05-27] **Verdict, no numeric score**: "a very simple, very very well thought out and
  well-built keyboard" with "a very very nice solid build" — a positive close without the
  X/10 he used for the iFrogz earbuds a week earlier, showing the scoring habit was not yet
  uniform.
- [2009-05-27] **Con #1 — two USB cables**: "unfortunately it does take two USB cables to
  plug into your computer," which he then discounts for himself because he already runs
  several USB hubs; he separates "a con" from "a con *for me*."
- [2009-05-27] **Con #2 — soft key travel, the earliest key-feel judgment**: "the only real
  con... is the fact that it has very soft travel. I was actually typing one of my essays
  with this and it was just really really soft and my typing was slowed down a bit." He
  adds that "you do have to break in the keyboard — it does take a few days or a few
  papers."
- [2009-05-27] **Feature walkthrough**: plug-and-play driver; volume up/down/mute keys
  working under Windows Vista; the headline **USB 2.0 dock** on top; a rear USB 2.0 hub with
  three ports out; height/tilt adjustment; full number pad and function keys; a very long
  cable he tucks under the desk.
- [2009-05-27] **He tests against his own desk**: "it fits nicely with my mouse over here on
  the right hand side with the mouse pad."
- [2009-05-27] **Unresolved detail left honest**: an unfamiliar key near the Windows Start
  key — "one interesting thing that I'm still experimenting with... I'm not sure."
- [2009-05-27] **Buying guidance**: link in the description; "there is a version for the Mac
  and for the PC."
- [2009-05-27] **1,000-subscriber giveaway announced**: "like it is written on my channel,
  my 1000 subscriber giveaway is coming very soon" — the first dated mention of MKBHD
  running his own giveaway (previously he had *won* one, see Tech Channel of the Week #1).
- [2009-05-27] **High-school marker**: the essays were "heading for language arts" —
  consistent with age 15 in 2009.
