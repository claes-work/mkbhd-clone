# Prime Phones: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2014-08-09)

## Summary
An **"Explained!"** demystifier of the "Prime" phones (Galaxy S5 LTE-A, LG G3 Cat 6) that were sparking
"are we getting screwed over?" anxiety. Marques's answer: they're **sidestep variants**, not upgrades —
their real reason for existing is **Category 6 LTE-Advanced** for one specific market (South Korea's SK
Telecom), with a spec bump (Snapdragon 805, +1 GB RAM, sometimes a sharper display) as a bonus. Not sold
in the US, hard to import, and you couldn't use the LTE-A anyway. A clean **consumer-education /
don't-panic-at-the-marketing** piece.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-08-09] **The real purpose is LTE-Advanced (Cat 6), not "better phone."** US markets run Cat 4
  LTE (~75 Mbps typical, 150 Mbps ceiling); the Prime/LTE-A variants target SK Telecom's advanced network
  (~225 Mbps, 300 Mbps ceiling). "This is actually the main purpose of the existence of this device."
- [2014-08-09] **The differences are modest + market-specific.** Galaxy S5 LTE-A: same body/materials,
  a gold trim + LTE-A logo, a 2.5K display (sharper than the LG G3), Snapdragon 801→805, 2→3 GB RAM. The
  "LG G3 Prime" (really the Cat 6 version): same screen/build, just 805 + 3 GB + Cat 6 (and "for some
  reason, a lot of bloatware").
- [2014-08-09] **Why not release it everywhere?** Samsung *could* have, "but that would have pissed off a
  lot of current Galaxy S5 owners, and LTE-A really isn't available much anywhere yet." Samsung always
  ships variants (S5 Active/Zoom/Mini) — "think of this as another one of them, a sidestep variant."
- [2014-08-09] **Buyer takeaway:** only worth importing (from a specialty site) "if you're desperate for
  a 2.5K display on your S5 or absolutely must have the 805/extra RAM" — and even then you can't use the
  LTE-A. Aside: a rumored HTC One M8 Prime would have been "the first Prime to actually change almost
  everything" (new design + camera), but those plans died.
