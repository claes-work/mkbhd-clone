# Dear Apple! (@mkbhd, 2012-04-21)

## Summary
An **open-letter format** — a new format for the channel — addressed directly to Apple, alternating
genuine praise with six specific complaints: patent litigation, the absence of black hardware, the
quality of Apple's mice, the un-refreshed Mac Pro, iOS stagnation, and technical support. The
support point is not asserted but **evidenced**: he plays recorded audio of his own call to Apple
sales, in which the representative cannot answer a basic spec question. That call is also the
primary-source proof of the "I called Apple" claim from the Mac Mini build two months earlier.

## Key claims
- [2012-04-21] **The frame is genuinely warm, not sarcastic**: "you guys are a great company… we got a
  great system going on: we give you money, you give us great stuff, people love you. It's wonderful."
  He returns to it at the close: "the bottom line is, Apple, you're doing a great job."
- [2012-04-21] **Patent litigation named first**: "why are you guys patent trolls?" Left as a question,
  and directly relevant to his S III design analysis two weeks later
  ([[2012-05-05-yt-cBSp1j5tPyU]]).
- [2012-04-21] **A colour request with a market precedent**: "remember the black MacBook? Remember how
  well that did, remember how much people liked that? Imagine a black Mac Pro, a black iMac, a new black
  Mac Mini." (Consistent with his long-documented red-and-black preference.)
- [2012-04-21] **Apple's mice condemned across generations**: "a long, gruesome history of awful,
  terrible, pathetic mice… not ergonomic at all, not comfortable at all," ending with "the Mighty Mouse
  and the Magic Mouse — two of the worst mice I've ever seen from such a big company." Consistent with
  the Mac Mini finale ("the Magic Mouse, they suck") and his choice of the Cyborg R.A.T. 7.
- [2012-04-21] **"Please refresh the Mac Pro"** — the request he cares most about, restated at the close;
  he had already cited the missing refresh as the reason he built a Mac Mini instead
  ([[2012-01-19-yt-eOBRNrn32tE]]).
- [2012-04-21] **The iOS-stagnation argument, with his own motto attached**: "when are you guys going to
  take a risk with iOS? … people have been using the iPhone since the first generation, three or four
  years with the same boring OS. It's gotten stale. Even someone like me, who's been using iOS for about
  a month, is bored with it… The mantra is kind of 'if it ain't broke don't fix it' — **my motto is kind
  of 'if it ain't broke don't break it. But you can still make it better.'**"
- [2012-04-21] **Support critique, then the evidence**: "your technical support isn't very technical" —
  followed by recorded call audio in which the Apple representative cannot supply a rewrite-speed figure
  ("we haven't published any rewrite speeds — it's truly not that important") and is uncertain whether
  a 30" 2560×1600 Dell can be driven, before confirming a dual-link adapter is required.
- [2012-04-21] **The call corroborates the Mac Mini series**: on the recording he tells the rep "I'm
  trying to build actually the world's fastest Mac Mini" — primary-source confirmation of the
  "I actually called Apple and nobody could tell me" claim in [[2012-02-10-yt-6cmhEmwfea0]].
- [2012-04-21] **Invites the audience to extend the list**: "if you have anything else you'd like to see
  from Apple… we can get maybe like a list going of all the greatest stuff Apple hasn't done yet."
