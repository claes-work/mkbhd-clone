# Unboxed: Griffin Elevator Laptop Stand (@mkbhd, 2009-05-30)

## Summary
Unboxing and first impressions of the Griffin Elevator laptop stand, **sent to him by
Griffin Technology** — another origin-era manufacturer sample, disclosed on camera in the
first fifteen seconds ("this was kindly sent out to me by Griffin Technologies and of course
their link will be in the description"). He assembles the three-piece stand live on camera,
narrates materials as he handles them, and explicitly **declines to guess** on the top
panel's material ("it looks like glass but I do honestly think it's plastic... it's light
enough to be plastic"). He closes with the standing two-step promise: "I'll definitely get
back to you guys with a full review video on it." Two small notes for the setup thread: he
mentions the desk laptop he is testing on (an NZXT Cryo LX cooler, caption-garbled) and he
praises the stand's **restraint in branding** — an early aesthetic preference.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-30] **Review sample, disclosed up front**: "this was kindly sent out to me by
  Griffin Technologies and of course their link will be in the description" — the sample
  disclosure + description-link habit is already routine by May 2009.
- [2009-05-30] **Format label stated**: "welcome to another unboxing and first impressions
  video" — unboxing = first impressions, review = final impressions (the split he named
  three days earlier in [[2009-05-27-yt-b7Nc78QqDs0]]).
- [2009-05-30] **Two-step promise repeated**: "I'll definitely get back to you guys with a
  full review video on it."
- [2009-05-30] **Logistics noticed as part of the product experience**: "this did ship kind
  of quickly, this was a UPS package, so it was decent timing."
- [2009-05-30] **Live assembly as the demo**: he builds the stand on camera — glass-or-plastic
  top panel plus two metal side pieces that slide into H-slots — because the packaging picture
  alone would not show it ("hopefully I can show you guys in this video what it'll look like").
- [2009-05-30] **Material honesty**: "it looks like glass but I do honestly think it's
  plastic... it's light enough to be plastic" — he reports uncertainty rather than asserting a
  spec.
- [2009-05-30] **Ergonomic point**: the assembled stand leaves "this side flat to the ground
  and this side will actually be angled a little bit down to make it easier for typing."
- [2009-05-30] **Not a Mac-only accessory**: "this is not only for MacBooks but you can
  definitely use this for your PC, for your little 15-inch laptop, whatever you may have" —
  he corrects the Apple-accessory assumption for a PC-owning audience.
- [2009-05-30] **Earliest documented pro-restraint branding preference**: "I like the fact
  that not a whole kind of branding when you look at it, just the Griffin logo up front...
  overall it's very very clean, very nice looking. I do like the simplicity of it."
- [2009-05-30] **Grip/finish details**: "grippy kind of substance... kind of tacky, pretty
  good" on the metal top, "very grippy material on the bottom" of the panel.
- [2009-05-30] **Desk-setup marker**: he says he is testing with a laptop cooler on the desk
  — "I'm using the [NZXT] Cryo LX over there" (caption reads "mzx cryo LX"), tying this to
  the earlier NZXT hardware unboxing thread.
- [2009-05-30] **Unscripted register**: the take includes an unedited teenage aside ("that's
  what she said") — origin-era videos were single-take and lightly edited.
