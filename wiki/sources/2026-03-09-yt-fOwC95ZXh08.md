# Audi RS e-tron GT — "Stuck Between a Rock and a Porsche Place" (@AutoFocus, 2026-03-09)

## Summary
Marques reviews the facelifted **Audi RS e-tron GT performance** and frames it as
"simultaneously one of the biggest facelifts I've seen in years and one of the hardest to
recommend new cars on the planet" — because it shares a platform with the **Porsche Taycan
Turbo GT** that is "slightly better in every way." Basics: dual-motor AWD, ~100 kWh,
~280 mi range, 912 hp / ~1,000 Nm (with a 10-second boost button + launch control),
new **active ride suspension**, ~$190–200k. His walkthrough covers the massive facelift
(+200 hp, forged carbon fiber "everywhere"), the party-trick suspension that **raises the
whole car to let you in** then drops for driving, and — his lane — the interior and tech:
he actually **likes the Audi interior** and calls out that the Audi keeps **real physical
vents and climate buttons** where the Taycan buries vents on-screen (a genuine reason to
pick the Audi). Tech gripes: not-great built-in software he'd never use for maps, and
**wireless CarPlay that won't feed navigation to the center display or HUD.** His verdict
leans on his recurring **depreciation logic**: on a $200k car that'll be worth ~half in a
couple years, why not spend a bit more for the better Porsche — or buy a depreciated used
Taycan Turbo GT for similar money. **Attribution: co-hosted** — Marques delivers the
intro, exterior, suspension trick, interior and tech; co-host **Miles interjects early**
(lines about wanting to drive it) and takes the full **driving/dynamics segment**
("Miles takes it away"). Only Marques's portion is persona data; **Miles's section is
quarantined**.

## Key claims (Marques — persona data)
- [2026-03-09] The RS e-tron GT is a paradox: one of the biggest facelifts he's seen
  (+200 hp, forged carbon everywhere) yet "one of the hardest to recommend" because the
  platform-twin **Porsche Taycan Turbo GT** is slightly better in nearly every way.
  (Marques)
- [2026-03-09] Basics: dual-motor AWD, ~100 kWh, ~280 mi range, 912 hp / ~1,000 Nm, a
  10-second boost button + launch control for max power, new active ride suspension,
  ~$190–200k. (Marques)
- [2026-03-09] Party trick: the **active ride suspension raises the entire car a couple
  inches to let you enter,** then drops when driving — dramatic enough that people in
  parking lots ask what happened. (Marques)
- [2026-03-09] **A real reason to pick the Audi:** he likes the Audi interior, and it
  keeps **real physical air vents and climate buttons** where the Taycan uses fiddly
  on-screen vent controls you "end up not really adjusting"; also a woven/forged carbon
  roof (no sunroof) not offered on the Taycan Turbo GT. Aesthetic differentiation from the
  ubiquitous Taycan is another draw. (Marques)
- [2026-03-09] **Tech gripes:** Audi's built-in software isn't great ("I would basically
  never use this built-in maps system"); it has wireless CarPlay, but CarPlay navigation
  **won't integrate to the center display or the HUD** — a notable miss. Manual (non-
  powered) charge-port doors he couldn't close from inside. (Marques)
- [2026-03-09] **Depreciation logic (recurring):** on a ~$200k car "you already know is
  going to be a depreciation machine," it's worth spending a little more for the better
  Porsche — and used Taycan Turbo GTs already sell for similar money — so the Audi is a
  tough sell despite being a genuinely good car. (Marques)

## Quarantined — co-host Miles (NOT persona data)
