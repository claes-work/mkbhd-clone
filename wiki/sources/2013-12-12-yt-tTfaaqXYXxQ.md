# Google Play Editions: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2013-12-12)

## Summary
An incentive-analysis explainer on why Google Play edition phones exist despite near-zero
sales. His answer: they're **not meant to sell** — they're effectively **premium developer
editions** (latest Android, fast updates, unlocked bootloaders) that help with
fragmentation and keep developers happy. A clean instance of his "judge a product by its
real purpose, not its unit sales" reasoning (Halo-product-adjacent).

## Key claims
- [2013-12-12] **The incentive puzzle, then the answer**: "if I were Samsung spending millions
  marketing the Galaxy S4, what incentive would I have to make an alternate edition to sell in a
  different store? Wouldn't it compete with my sales? The truth is Google Play editions don't
  sell — very low numbers, zero advertising. It's clear Google just does not care about the sales
  numbers." Reasons from incentives, then resolves the puzzle with the sales reality.
- [2013-12-12] **The real purpose — "premium developer editions"**: "a better name might be the
  premium developer edition — latest Android, fast updates, unlocked bootloaders, way more
  developer-friendly. More hardware options to develop on can make it easier to develop for more
  hardware options." Judges the product by its actual purpose, not its retail performance
  (Halo-product-adjacent reasoning; cf. the Chromebook Pixel).
- [2013-12-12] **Ties it to fragmentation**: "fragmentation is thrown around a lot — an app
  developed and tested on one Android phone can literally not work on another because of screen
  size, resolution, SoC, or GPU. Giving developers more hardware to test on could help." His
  recurring fragmentation thread.
- [2013-12-12] **Reads the signal, not just the product**: "just the fact that there are any more
  Google Play editions [now the LG G Pad 8.3 and Sony Z Ultra] means Google hasn't forgotten it
  and still thinks this is the right way to keep developers happy." Reads the strategic intent.
- [2013-12-12] **"Geeks like me love them" — the honest upside**: "it also happens to have the
  great upside that geeks like me and possibly you love using them." His stock-Android-loving-geek
  self-identification.
- [2013-12-12] **Dream-edition list — a taste tease**: names realistic wishes (a Google Play
  edition LG G2, Note 3) and far-fetched ones (Lumia 1020, iPhone). His dream-device format again.
