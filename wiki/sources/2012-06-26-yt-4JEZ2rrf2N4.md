# Samsung Galaxy SIII Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-06-26)

## Summary
The full review of the year's most-anticipated Android phone — and a **personal turning point**: at
the end he states that the Galaxy S III is "actually enough to get me to **switch from the Nexus to
the Galaxy S3 on Verizon as my full-time carry daily device**." Three durable claims sit inside it: a
**TouchWiz-over-stock concession** (the first Android skin he'd pick over pure Android), an early
**camera-replaces-your-point-and-shoot** thesis, and a **pentile-doesn't-matter** perception argument.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-26] **Daily-driver switch — the biography headline**: "for me it's actually enough to get me
  to switch from the Nexus to the Galaxy S3 on Verizon as my full-time carry daily device." He'd called
  the Galaxy Nexus "one of the best phones I've ever used" ten weeks earlier
  ([[2012-04-16-yt-fFRl2oOqDsk]]); the S III displaces it.
- [2012-06-26] **TouchWiz over stock — a notable concession from a self-described purist**: the S III's
  UI fluidity "is literally unmatched by any other Android phone… this is the one reason why if I had to
  pick an Android skin over pure Android it would be TouchWiz. It's the fastest, it's the lightest of
  them all." Continues the stock-vs-skin evolution: stock-only (Oct 2011) → custom ROM (Apr 2012) → now
  preferring a specific OEM skin for performance.
- [2012-06-26] **"It's not the pentile, it's whether you can tell"** — perception over spec sheet: the
  4.8" 720p Super AMOLED is pentile, "a lot of people toss around the word pentile as if that means
  instant death to a display, but honestly you really can't tell unless you're looking at a much lower
  resolution pentile display first." Same real-world-over-spec instinct as
  [[2012-03-22-yt-R3n_J7v82AM]].
- [2012-06-26] **The camera-replaces-a-dedicated-camera thesis, early and explicit**: the 8 MP shooter
  "is totally capable of replacing your point and shoot — you no longer need to bring another camera on
  vacation, you can literally just bring your phone. I'm really happy it's gotten to this level of
  quality." A dated milestone in the phone-camera-vs-real-camera arc the wiki tracks into the present.
- [2012-06-26] **A precise, minor UI criticism kept anyway**: the notification-tray toggles slide in
  from the left on every pull-down, "so that prevents me from being able to immediately toggle Wi-Fi… a
  little bit annoying, but something definitely worth mentioning." Names a small friction rather than
  rounding it away.
- [2012-06-26] **A complaint he refuses to make** — pushing back on received wisdom: "I never understand
  why people complain about the battery door being flexible, because you should just put it back on the
  phone. I've never seen one break under normal use."
- [2012-06-26] **Attended the US launch event** in New York and filmed it — including **Samsung mocking
  Apple** from the stage ("one of our primary competitors, who likes to make a big deal about all the
  technology they've crammed into their device, has done a little more than launch a software upgrade").
  First time he saw US carrier variants of the S III in person.
- [2012-06-26] Specs (US version): 1.5 GHz Snapdragon S4 dual-core, 2 GB RAM, 4G, microSD, 8.6 mm;
  Android 4.0.4 + TouchWiz; all-day battery "miles better than the Galaxy Nexus."
- [2012-06-26] **Gimmick-skepticism, stated plainly**: most of the extra TouchWiz features (Smart Stay,
  S Voice — "a freaking Siri clone… can be just as useless as Siri sometimes") are "pretty gimmicky."
