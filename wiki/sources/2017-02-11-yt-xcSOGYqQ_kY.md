# Tesla P100D App v3.0 Review! (@mkbhd, 2017-02-11)

## Summary
A quick look at the redesigned Tesla mobile app (v3.0) — the overhaul he'd predicted was overdue in the v1
video. Clone-relevant mostly as a follow-up node: **the redesign is "a fresh coat of paint" with no new
functionality**, adds fingerprint auth, and — importantly — **resolves the power-steering-problems arc**:
"the car's been perfect since."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-02-11] **"A fresh coat of paint" — beautiful, but no new functionality.** "The icon and splash are
  flatter, there's an accurate live representation of the car at the top (lights, wheels, paint, an open
  door closes in the app), and it's cleaner and faster — but there's pretty much no functionality added; it
  does all the same stuff." Honest that a redesign ≠ new features.
- [2017-02-11] **Rare simultaneous iOS + Android release, noted.** "Both iOS and Android got the refresh at
  pretty much the same time, which is pretty rare as far as apps go." (Earlier he'd criticized the app as
  worse on Android — this addresses that.)
- [2017-02-11] **The one genuinely new thing: fingerprint auth.** "The one thing they added, new on both
  platforms, is fingerprint recognition — Touch ID on iPhone, fingerprint on Android." Notes the useful
  security addition.
- [2017-02-11] **RESOLVES the power-steering-problems arc — "the car's been perfect since."** "For those
  wondering: the car itself has been awesome these last couple weeks. You remember the last Tesla video was
  because it was having issues — this is to update you that **it's been perfect since then.**" Closes the
  loop on the reliability story ([[2016-12-19-yt-QM5z0dW60KE]]) — update-in-public, the good-news direction.
- [2017-02-11] **Walks the retained features.** Live climate pre-conditioning, remote lock/unlock, vent
  roof, valet mode, charge status/limit, and **Summon** (demos it pulling toward him) — all carried over
  from the v1 app. Consistent car-as-updatable-software-platform framing.
