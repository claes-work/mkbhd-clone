# mkbHD Update 2.3 (@mkbhd, 2009-05-14)

## Summary
The fifth "Update" channel-status video of the origin era (age 15). Marques announces a
short upload pause (away Friday–Sunday for a "trip/tournament"), reports crossing **~900
subscribers**, restates his public goal of **1,000 subscribers by summer** and his promise
to **apply for the YouTube Partner program** at that mark, explains why the long-promised
Sony Vegas intro tutorial keeps failing the pre-Partner **10-minute upload cap**, and
pitches Twitter (`twitter.com/MKBHD`) as the channel's faster, less-planned update feed.
Also a self-aware production note: this update is deliberately *not* HD because it was
shot on a non-HD camera on a mini tripod, which he says is fine for a non-tutorial video.
★ L3-candidate: dated subscriber timeline, the Partner promise, and the first mention of
companies approaching him via Twitter.

## Key claims
- [2009-05-14] **~900 subscribers, "a couple of days ago"**: "I just reached 900
  subscribers a couple of days ago or two days ago." This extends the dated origin
  subscriber timeline (74 → 95 → 260 → 450 → ~900 in the first months of 2009).
- [2009-05-14] **The 1,000-subscribers-by-summer goal was publicly doubted**: "people
  thought I was crazy when I said I wanted to get to 1,000 subscribers by summer — well
  it might actually happen."
- [2009-05-14] **YouTube Partner promise is conditional and public**: he had promised to
  apply for a YouTube partnership "when I got to 1,000 subscribers or by summer,
  whichever came first," and frames audience growth as the audience's own self-interest
  (Partner status lifts the 10-minute cap, which unlocks the requested intro tutorial).
- [2009-05-14] **The Sony Vegas intro tutorial is blocked by the 10-minute cap, not the
  1 GB cap**: he solved file size with the compression tutorial, but the shortest cut he
  could make ran "about 18, 19 minutes."
- [2009-05-14] **Deliberate quality tiering by video type**: this update is shot on a
  non-HD camera on a mini tripod, and he says that is acceptable "because this isn't a
  hardware or software tutorial or unboxing or review" — an explicit early rule that
  format determines production standard.
- [2009-05-14] **Twitter is the high-frequency channel**: "maybe once or twice a day"
  updates, and "there's a few companies who are actually on Twitter who are interested in
  exactly what I'm doing" — the earliest evidence of inbound company interest arriving
  via Twitter rather than email.
- [2009-05-14] **Content is pre-recorded in batches**: he has "recorded several unboxing
  videos" plus tutorials and reviews queued, so the pause is a publishing pause, not a
  production pause.
- [2009-05-14] **The update videos are a findable series**: "go to my channel page and
  search under the videos 'update'" — self-curated series navigation before playlists
  carried the load.
- [2009-05-14] **New microphone settings dialed in**: "I hope you like the new mic
  quality, I am very very pleased with it so far... I think I've got the settings pretty
  much down packed for the tutorial videos" — follow-through on the Alto-Edge GN3 mic
  reviewed a week earlier.
- [2009-05-14] **Category recognition matters to him**: "I love when I get honors for the
  Science and Tech category" (YouTube's since-retired "honors" ranking).
