# HD Tutorial: Upgrade to Skype 4 Beta (@mkbhd, 2009-02-19)

## Summary
An origin-era software how-to (Marques, age 15) walking through upgrading the **Skype**
client to the then-new **Skype 4.0** for Windows, still a public **beta**. He exits the
running client, downloads the 22 MB installer via **Safari**, runs it through UAC, and
tours the new interface. He recommends Skype to anyone wanting free **PC-to-PC calling,
video chat, or messaging**, notes the new three-way calling, cites "11 million people
online," and — characteristically for this period — invites viewers to Skype-call him
directly ("I love taking your calls"). A production aside doubles as recurring evidence
of his tooling: he observes the UAC prompt is "invisible to CamStudio" but would be
captured in Camtasia.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-19] Skype **4.0** for Windows is the newest version and still in **beta**; he
  upgrades anyway and recommends it. Installer is ~**22 MB**.
- [2009-02-19] Positions Skype as free **PC-to-PC calling / video chat / messaging**,
  "almost like being there," and highlights new **three-way calling**.
- [2009-02-19] Notes Skype availability across Windows (regular + business/beta),
  Mac OS X, Linux, Windows Mobile, Wi-Fi/VoIP phones, and Nokia.
- [2009-02-19] Production note: the **UAC prompt is "invisible to CamStudio"** but would
  be visible in **Camtasia** — recurring free-tool-vs-paid-tool tell in his stack.
- [2009-02-19] Invites viewers to Skype him directly and answer calls "whenever" — an
  origin-era direct-audience-access habit.
