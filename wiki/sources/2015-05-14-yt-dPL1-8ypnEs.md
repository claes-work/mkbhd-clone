# Apple Watch Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-05-14)

## Summary
The full Apple Watch review — after the Sept-2014 impressions. His verdict lands on a clean frame: **it's
an accessory for your iPhone and should be treated as exactly that** (like a battery case or a lens). It's
genuinely best at **notifications** (a clean tap on the wrist so you use your phone less), with a great
display/haptics/gyroscope, but the **apps are "smooth but not fast"** (so slow you might as well pull out
your phone), there are **no third-party watch faces** ("classic Apple lockdown mentality"), and at his
$700 config it doesn't "stick out from any other smartwatch in a crazy way — we might have to wait till
version 2."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-05-14] **Three versions differ only in materials/bands; two sizes (38/42 mm).** His 42 mm classic-
  leather is **$700 — "as much as an iPhone"**, so "for $700 you need to stand out and be noticeably better
  than the rest — but does it?" The interchangeable band is one of its best features.
- [2015-05-14] **Notifications are the core, and it nails them.** "Really good at just quietly tapping me
  on the wrist… I end up using my phone less, save a little battery and time" — "that's what smartwatches
  should be good at." The **haptic engine is the best he's used** ("almost like a tap"), driving stand
  reminders and Force Touch (pressure-sensitive extra options).
- [2015-05-14] **Apps are smooth but not fast.** They're iPhone-app counterparts, not standalone — "so
  slow that you might as well take out your phone and do the same action at the same speed on a bigger
  screen. You really have to *want* to use the watch to use those apps." The best ones are Apple's built-ins.
- [2015-05-14] **No third-party watch faces — the Apple lockdown.** "That sucks — that's like half the fun
  of Android Wear… classic Apple lockdown mentality. Maybe version 2." He settles on the modular face.
- [2015-05-14] **The good hardware:** a bright/sharp LCD (290 ppi, surprisingly black blacks), very
  comfortable, ~1.5-day battery ("never a full two days, never dead after one"), and the best gyroscope he's
  used (raise-to-wake).
- [2015-05-14] **The verdict — it's an accessory.** "Just like a battery case or a camera lens, it's an
  accessory for your iPhone and should be treated as exactly that. The base price doesn't really stick out
  from any other smartwatch in a crazy way — we might just have to wait till version 2."
