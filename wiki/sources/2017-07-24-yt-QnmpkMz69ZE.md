# Siri vs the Ads: Does It Hold Up? (@mkbhd, 2017-07-24)

## Summary
A fact-check video: he tests whether Siri (iOS 11) can actually do everything The Rock asks of it in Apple's
splashy ad. A clean example of his **hold-marketing-to-account** instinct — the ad mostly works, but only if
you live entirely inside Apple's apps (Mail, Music), and several commands are subtly staged.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-07-24] **The premise: hold the ad to account.** "Apple released an adventure ad with The Rock
  featuring Siri — it made me wonder if Siri can actually do all the things he asks. I realize this is
  probably exactly what Apple wants me to do (it's basically an ad for Siri), but let's test it." His
  fact-check / media-literacy instinct applied to marketing.
- [2017-07-24] **The catch: most of it only works inside Apple's own apps.** "Read me my last email' won't
  work if you use Gmail/Yahoo/any third-party mail — you have to use Apple Mail (apparently The Rock does).
  Same with 'play my playlist' — only Apple Music, not Spotify/SoundCloud/Tidal." Names the walled-garden
  assumption behind the demo. A follow-the-fine-print critique.
- [2017-07-24] **Several commands are subtly staged.** "'Show me photos of my fashion line' — Siri just
  **Bing-searches** it (random clothes), but in the ad the phone shows his gallery; the language he uses
  isn't quite what would actually trigger that. 'Take a selfie' opens the front camera but **doesn't press
  the shutter** — you still tap the on-screen button. 'Text Dwayne' works, but requires a follow-up
  confirmation not shown." Names exactly where the ad glosses over reality.
- [2017-07-24] **Fair about what does work.** Weather cards, unit conversions ("ounces in a centiliter"),
  in-app actions in supported apps (an Uber VIP), sending texts — all genuinely work. Balanced, not a hit
  piece: separates the real from the staged.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> have to use Apple Mail."
