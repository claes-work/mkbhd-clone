# Getting Inspired (@mkbhd, 2011-12-14)

## Summary
A contest-entry video — and, incidentally, **the most valuable biographical document in this
batch**. He opens with a plain self-introduction that states his university and his exact
course of study, then explains what he finds compelling about business as a field, names
**consumer behavior** as the most interesting thing he has studied, and closes with career
advice built around passion. Filmed during finals week, three weeks after turning 18.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-14] **University and major, stated first-person (the key finding)**: "my name is
  Marques and I'm a student at Stevens Tech, and I'm studying **business and technology,
  information systems and marketing**." A direct, dated confirmation of **Stevens Institute of
  Technology** and — new to the wiki — the specific field combination. Self-reported per
  fidelity rule 7, but first-person and specific.
- [2011-12-14] **Self-description of his work, dated**: "as many of you guys already know me,
  I'm a **YouTube video producer**" — the term he chose for himself at 18, before "reviewer"
  or "creator" had settled as the label.
- [2011-12-14] **Why business, stated as a worldview**: "everything that you work hard to do or
  everything that you surround yourself with, if you think about it, is all financially
  inspired… 99% of the products you see on the store shelves around you, of the websites that
  you visit and the hard work that goes on around you, ultimately has some sort of financial
  background." A dated statement of the follow-the-economics instinct that later shows up
  constantly in his industry analysis.
- [2011-12-14] **Consumer behavior named as the standout subject**: "one of the coolest things
  I've learned about so far is what's called consumer behavior — it's how marketing affects the
  choices that people make, and it affects millions of people all around the world, literally
  every day." Directly relevant context for the ad-teardown video a month earlier
  ([[2011-11-15-yt-sK_M5gRMP1I]]) — he was analyzing advertising while formally studying how it
  works.
- [2011-12-14] **Career advice — the passion-as-edge argument**: "what's your dream job? Shoot
  for that through what interests you. If you're passionate about something then you have a
  much better chance of success in a field against someone who's not really as passionate about
  it. So when you're passionate you put in extra work, and that's what gets you the advantage."
  The mechanism he identifies is effort differential, not talent.
- [2011-12-14] **"Dent in the universe" phrasing**: "even leave your own little dent in the
  universe" — a Steve Jobs allusion, notable in the mouth of an avowed Android partisan.
- [2011-12-14] Context markers: this is an entry in a contest requiring viewer votes, and he
  signs off "excuse me while I go study for finals" — dating it to the end of his fall term.
