# iPhone 15 Pro Review: The Good, The Bad, & The Ugly! (@mkbhd, 2023-09-28)

## Summary
Marques's full solo review of the **iPhone 15 Pro** after ~2 weeks. His central frame: when
there aren't many changes, "it really puts a magnifying glass on the few changes there are,"
which he unpacks via an extended **Porsche 911 analogy** — a decades-refined formula where
small year-over-year improvements compound over time. He argues against the annual-upgrade
reflex ("there's no world where you should upgrade… just for the titanium"). Four Pro-only
areas: **build** (softer corners, slimmer bezels, lighter titanium — he's picking natural
titanium, his first non-black iPhone), **USB-C** (welcome but Apple "did nothing" with it
beyond one-cable — no faster charging, USB 3 data, external-drive recording), the **A17 Pro**
(first 3nm chip, near-M1 benchmarks, console-grade games) with a candid investigation of
**battery + overheating** issues, and the **cameras** (new 24MP default pipeline, Log video,
haloing flaw). Verdict: "the same phone as last year with USB, a faster chip, brighter
screen, thinner bezels and better cameras — decide how much of that matters to you." Includes
a disclosed **Eight Sleep** sponsor read. Solo — persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2023-09-28] Thesis on iterative refinement, via the **Porsche 911 analogy**: reviewers of
  a barely-changed 911 always call it "an engineering masterpiece perfected over generations,"
  and the iPhone is the same — "fix anything that's wrong or bad, and then find clever,
  good, interesting ways to just get a little bit better every year… over a long time it
  adds up to a bigger, more significant change." Pushes back on upgrade-every-year pressure.
- [2023-09-28] **Build**: softened corners with a slight front-glass edge curve, ~1mm
  smaller diagonally with slimmer bezels, and **grade-5 titanium** rails — "noticeably
  lighter… 10% on paper but it feels like more." Picks **natural titanium** (best color, his
  first non-black iPhone); cites JerryRigEverything confirming light-color scratch concerns.
- [2023-09-28] **USB-C**: a big deal for multi-device people ("I just bring one charger, it
  works with everything") but he's disappointed Apple "did more with this port" than
  one-cable: still 25W charging, no DeX/desktop mode. Pros do gain USB 3.0 transfer, 4K60
  monitor mirroring, and **recording video directly to an external drive**. Notes the
  in-box braided cable is only USB 2.0 ("that's very normal… it's a charging cable").
  Credits the **EU** for forcing the switch.
- [2023-09-28] **A17 Pro**: first 3nm phone chip, benchmarks "approaching M1 levels," ~10–20%
  faster CPU/GPU. But "more power is like more horsepower — only useful if it lets you do more,"
  and the only place you notice it is gaming (console ports like Resident Evil: Village,
  Assassin's Creed — defers gaming commentary to **Dave2D**).
- [2023-09-28] **Battery + overheating investigation** (why the review ran late): controlled
  benchmarks last longer (slightly bigger battery + 3nm), but he's had no "amazing" 9-hour
  screen-on days and saw random hot-and-drain episodes with "no rhyme or reason" (fine at a
  100°F golf tournament with GPS; hot for 5 min on a plane in Airplane Mode). Best theory: a
  more powerful chip drains faster under load, plus iOS 17.0.x bugs; expects battery to "even
  out to be the same as last year."
- [2023-09-28] **Cameras**: new 48MP main + improved ultrawide/macro; a new pipeline outputs
  **24MP by default** (merging a 48MP detail shot with a 12MP quad-binned light shot via
  neural net) — "slightly better," visible only when pixel-peeping. Pro Max-only **5X folding
  telephoto** (15X→25X max zoom) helps a "very specific crowd" but makes 4X photos worse and
  trails Samsung's 10X reach; Apple's edge is stabilization.
- [2023-09-28] **Log video** is "literally a Pro camera feature" (flat log, own LUTs, gradeable),
  though "annoyingly buried" in Settings — "really not very Pro." Praises world-class 4K auto
  video (the Auto Focus Rivian van review was shot on it) and AV1 support; flags **haloing
  from point light sources** as "pretty bad… the worst on the iPhone of any other phone."
  Borrows a line from **Mr. Mobile**: camera differences between flagships are now so
  software-driven "it's hardly even consistent anymore… a lot of it is gonna be taste."
- [2023-09-28] **Action Button** (Pro only): "I kind of love it" (mute, focus, camera, voice
  recorder, or any Siri Shortcut) and hopes it gets copied, but concedes many will "play with
  it for three or four days and forget about it" because it's hard to reach one-handed.
- [2023-09-28] **Verdict**: "it's the same phone as last year with USB, a little bit of a
  faster chip, a little bit of a brighter screen, a little bit of thinner bezels, and better
  cameras… decide how much of that is important to you. Honestly, USB might be the most
  important thing about the new iPhone, despite what Apple was hoping."
