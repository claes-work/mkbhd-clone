# Driving Xiaomi's Electric Car: Are we Cooked? (@mkbhd, 2025-12-05)

## Summary
Marques tests the **Xiaomi SU7 Max** in the US for two weeks to interrogate the common hot take
that **Chinese EVs would "cook" everything if they came to America**. Verdict: **"Not yet,
clearly… but you can see how we could be soon."** The SU7 Max is a **$42,000** (≈299,000 CNY)
electric sedan he repeatedly says **feels like a $75,000 US car** — great software (from a phone
company), a **modular interior**, strong build/isolation, split-personality driving, and working
self-driving in China. His central analytical point: **nothing inside is un-replicable bleeding-
edge tech — the achievement is putting it all together in one car at that price.** Solo →
persona/voice data. Cross-promotes an **Auto Focus** companion video. Disclosed **Ridge** sponsor.

## Key claims
- [2025-12-05] **Premise/format:** built to test the recurring comment-section/hot-take that
  Chinese EVs are "too good, too much tech, too cheap to not dominate." He got one to "actually
  figure out if that's true or not." Conclusion up front: "you could say there's a little
  something to it."
- [2025-12-05] **The car:** Xiaomi SU7 Max — Xiaomi (phones, tablets, vacuums, appliances) makes
  it; trims mirror phone naming (SU7 / Pro / Max / Ultra). **~299,000 CNY ≈ $42,000 US.** Specs:
  101 kWh battery, dual-motor AWD, **673 hp**, 320 mi range — "on paper on the level of a Tesla
  Model 3 Performance," which costs ~$55,000.
- [2025-12-05] **Design — freedom of not being a car company:** with no heritage lines to honor,
  Xiaomi borrows liberally — McLaren-ish headlights, Lotus hood lines, Porsche Taycan side
  profile, Polestar bezels, Mercedes-AMG GT active wing — melded into one car ("less prestige, yes,
  but also a little bit more freedom"). Unique purple color.
- [2025-12-05] **Interior:** "This is a nice car" — emphatically *not* "nice for a $45k car" or
  "nice for a brand you've never heard of." Heated/ventilated leather seats, Porsche-like heated
  wheel, auto-aligning HUD, 16" center display, air suspension, Alcantara headliner, 25 speakers,
  album-art-reactive ambient lighting, air-cooled **50 W wireless chargers** (matched to Xiaomi
  phones), rear Xiaomi-tablet screens. "Already feeling like a 60, $65,000 car" before driving.
- [2025-12-05] **Standout feature #1 — software** ("what Apple might've done if they'd made an
  Apple car"): deep **Xiaomi ecosystem** integration (screen mirroring, smart-home cameras, texts/
  maps syncing), plus wireless CarPlay as backup ("the biggest, nicest window of wireless CarPlay I
  have ever seen"). His favorite touch: **navigation voice is isolated to the driver's headrest
  speaker** so it never interrupts the music for passengers — "my favorite software feature I've
  seen in a car in a while."
- [2025-12-05] **Standout feature #2 — modular interior:** hidden mounting points around the cabin
  take Xiaomi accessories (extra speedo/trip display, magnetic phone mount, powered USB hub,
  karaoke mics, even long-range walkie-talkies for camera-car comms). "You can make it maximalist…
  minimalist… as much or as little as you want," which flexes the price. "What other car does this?"
- [2025-12-05] **Driving — EV "multiple personalities":** soft, Lucid-level air-suspension comfort
  mode with a **power-delivery slider** (full RWD ↔ full FWD); Sport+ mode firms everything with a
  G-force meter; a boost button; **0–60 in under 3 s** (later says ~2.5 s). Handles like a Model 3
  Performance / BMW i4 — "a wolf in sheep's clothes." Not Porsche Taycan-sporty, "one notch below."
  Active seat bolsters (G-Wagon-popularized) hug you through turns.
- [2025-12-05] **Build quality & self-driving:** genuinely quiet/isolated + ANC — "feels nothing
  like the $42,000 price tag." Lidar-based **Pilot Pro/Max self-driving** works well even on US
  roads it wasn't designed for ("never really done anything to give me pause"). He struggles to
  name a negative — the usual downside (bad tech/software) doesn't apply because Xiaomi is a tech
  company, and the EV fundamentals (range, fast charging, dual personality) are all present.
- [2025-12-05] **Thesis — "are we cooked?" → not yet, but the ceiling is rising:** "a $42,000 car
  that feels like a $75,000 car if it were made here." Key insight: **no un-replicable tech inside**
  — "a car made of normal car parts, but it's the putting them all together… that's so impressive."
  Its US absence is "inherently political" (tariffs/import fees); **Europe reportedly gets it in
  2027**, and "the competition over there is at least in a little bit of trouble." Frames strong
  global competition as **raising the ceiling for all EVs**.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "This is a nice car, not just in like a nice with an asterisk type of way. Like oh this is nice for a $45,000 car ... No, this, this is a nice car."

> "This feels like a preview kind of of like what Apple might've done if they'd made an Apple car ... it's a car made by someone who makes a phone."

> "This is a $42,000 car that feels like a $75,000 car if it were made here in the US, and that's the thing that's most impressive about the Xiaomi SU7."

> "If you took this car apart, you wouldn't find anything inside ... that's impossible to replicate. This is a car made of normal car parts, but it's the putting them all together in one car that's so impressive."

> "Are we cooked? Not yet, clearly. This car is not available here in the US but you can see how we could be soon."

> "The idea that the competition is getting stronger ... really exciting and heightens the ceiling for all EVs."
