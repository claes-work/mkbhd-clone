# Cyborg R.A.T. 7 Unboxing [HD] (@mkbhd, 2010-08-26)

## Summary
A live, largely-uncut unboxing of the Cyborg R.A.T. 7 gaming mouse (Mad Catz),
explicitly done without narration-over-editing because viewers responded well to the
similarly-live [[wiki/sources/2010-08-16-yt-7XdczEd26dU|Dell UltraSharp monitor
unboxing]]. Walks through the R.A.T. product line tiers (3/5/7/9), the packaging,
included adjustment accessories, and the mouse's customizable weight/grip system, and
references an ongoing "custom build" desktop project.

## Key claims
- [2010-08-26] **Names live-unboxing as an audience-requested format, continuing from
  the prior video**: "you guys seem to like that with the monitor unboxing so I'll be
  doing it with this video... you guys ask for it more live unboxings" — a second
  data point in the audience-request-gated format-adoption pattern (see
  [[wiki/sources/2010-08-16-yt-7XdczEd26dU]]), this time acted on quickly rather than
  merely floated.
- [2010-08-26] **Positions the R.A.T. 7 within its product line**: distinguishes
  R.A.T. 3/5/7/9, noting the 9 is the wireless variant of the 7 and this is the
  "newest high-end," not the top-tier model.
- [2010-08-26] **Ties the mouse to the same in-progress "custom build" thread**
  referenced in the prior video, and reveals it includes a RAID setup with
  Velociraptor hard drives (visible in the background of an earlier giveaway video),
  plus that he uses the mouse for Photoshop CS5 graphic work as well as gaming.
- [2010-08-26] **Discloses a self-funded purchase with a shipping/cost detail**: "this
  mouse uh took just about an eternity to ship through Amazon... it was only about $75
  in the very end with shipping" — not a PR-seeded review unit.
