# Top 5 Most Overpriced Tech! (@mkbhd, 2013-02-13)

## Summary
A countdown of the five most overpriced things in tech — a concentrated dose of his
**bang-for-buck / price-must-match-value** reasoning, applied with first-principles
arguments (why each is overpriced, not just that it is). Notable for distinguishing
justified premiums (bleeding-edge 4K TVs, studio monitors) from value-mismatches, and for
two persona through-lines: the **Beats headphones critique** (#1) and the **Mac Pro is
overpriced** case built on his own Hackintosh.

## Key claims
- [2013-02-13] **The governing principle — price must match value**: "there are some things
  that are actually justifiably expensive… bleeding-edge tech that actually deserves the
  price for now. But then there are things where the price just doesn't match the value —
  the bang-for-buck ratio is totally out of whack." His explicit value framework, and it's
  even-handed (defends the $20k 4K TVs and $2k studio monitors as justified).
- [2013-02-13] **#5 AudioQuest/Monster HDMI cables — a first-principles debunk**: "it's a
  digital signal going through a wire that is not prone to interference at any short
  distance… transferring the same exact signal that a $3.50 cable from Monoprice
  provides." Argues from how the technology works, not vibes.
- [2013-02-13] **#4 printer ink — an industry indictment**: "there are printers that won't
  let me print black-and-white because I ran out of magenta ink… it's more cost-effective
  to buy several new printers than to refill." Uses the absurdity to indict the razor-and-
  blades model.
- [2013-02-13] **#3 cellphone text plans — the bandwidth math**: computes that at $0.20 /
  160-byte text, "cell phone companies are charging $1.25 million per gigabyte of data for
  text messaging," and adds that carriers charge more for 4G "even though it costs less to
  implement than 3G." Quantified incentive-structure critique of US carriers.
- [2013-02-13] **#2 the Mac Pro — built on his own Hackintosh**: "I built a Hackintosh for
  less than $2,200 with 32 GB RAM, 3 SSDs, a 6-core CPU, GTX 670… the baseline Mac Pro at
  $2,500 has 6 GB RAM, a regular hard drive, a 3-year-old 5870… spec a Mac Pro to my
  build's specs and you'll give Apple ~$6,000." His price-per-spec doctrine, evidenced by
  the just-finished Hackintosh series.
- [2013-02-13] **#1 Beats Pro headphones — the sound-vs-price / fashion critique**: "$400…
  they're made of metal but they sound like they're only made of metal," part of "a long
  string of celebrity-endorsed audio products (Lady Gaga, Justin Bieber) that don't sound
  all that great," when the ATH-M50 costs half as much. His standing Beats/fashion-over-
  fidelity critique (predates the famous 2014 Beats video).
- [2013-02-13] **Student marker (honorable mention)**: "shout-outs to my macroeconomics
  teacher who has a PDF version of his textbook for $200." Dated college-student datum.
