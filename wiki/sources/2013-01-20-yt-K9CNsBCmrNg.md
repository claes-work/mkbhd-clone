# Hackintosh Pro Project! [Part 2: The Parts] (@mkbhd, 2013-01-20)

## Summary
Part 2 of the 5-part Hackintosh Pro build — the parts walkthrough for his **2013 video-
editing workstation**. Rich with dated workstation specs and, more durably, two stated
build doctrines: **expansion/upgradeability is why he builds instead of buying a Mac Pro**,
and **"overkill is better than underkill."** A production-side companion to the smartphone
reviews; his editing rig is itself a subject of content.

## Key claims
- [2013-01-20] **The build's purpose — a video-editing/rendering beast**: "the project to
  build a beast of a video editing machine for 2013… able to dual-boot Mac OS X Mountain
  Lion and Windows 8." His workflow named explicitly: "for someone like me with a workflow
  in Premiere and After Effects, this is an absolute beast." Pins his 2013 editing stack.
- [2013-01-20] **"Overkill is better than underkill" — a stated build value**: on the
  1000W PSU, "I would rather have way more power than I need than just barely more than I
  need… Overkill is better than underkill for pretty much everything inside a build like
  this." A dated doctrine (headroom over just-enough).
- [2013-01-20] **Expansion is the reason to build, not buy — the upgradeability doctrine**:
  "part of the reason I'm doing this over a Mac Pro is if I want to put more storage later,
  add graphics cards later, change out the CPU… I won't have to change this part." The
  build-your-own-for-future-flexibility argument (the same upgradeability value seen in
  the 2012 desktop material).
- [2013-01-20] **The core specs, dated (Jan 2013)**: Intel Core i7-3930K 6-core (socket
  2011, to be overclocked), Corsair H80i closed-loop cooler, ASUS Sabertooth X79 board,
  32 GB Corsair Vengeance RAM (with 4 slots left for a future 64 GB), Gigabyte GTX 670
  WindForce OC (chosen for CUDA cores Adobe can offload to), 3× OCZ Vertex 4 128 GB SSDs
  (RAID teased), fully-modular ~1000W Fractal Newton PSU, Fractal Design Define R4 case.
- [2013-01-20] **"I'm not much of a gamer" — a recurring self-label**: picks the GTX 670
  primarily for video editing (CUDA offload), noting "I'm not much of a gamer… I might do
  some light gaming." The not-a-gamer disclosure again (dated).
- [2013-01-20] **The "why 32 GB" reasoning — feed the software**: "the more RAM you can
  throw at these programs [Premiere + After Effects] the better." His heuristic of speccing
  to the software's appetite.
- [2013-01-20] **Case taste — minimalist + functional**: "the Define is an incredibly
  good-looking case, a nice minimalist-plus-functional case." His industrial-design taste
  applied to his own gear.
