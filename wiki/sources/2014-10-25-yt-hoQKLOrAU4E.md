# Samsung Galaxy Note 4 Review! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-25)

## Summary
The full Galaxy Note 4 review, after **two weeks as his daily driver** — the payoff of the "finally
impressed with Samsung hardware" turn from his impressions. The metal frame delivers ("increased hopes for
durability"), and the **5.7" QHD Super AMOLED is "the only [display] left that still impresses me every
time I turn it on… so crispy… the best display on any smartphone right now."** He restates the
**hardware-ahead-of-content** point (QHD mostly still scaling from 1080p; only web text really benefits),
his **control-over-software** habit (swapped in the Google Now launcher over TouchWiz), and praises the
windowed-multitasking, with the usual "occasional TouchWiz jank" caveat.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-25] **The metal build confirmed in daily use.** A chamfered black metal band replaces the old
  plastic — "coming from the super-rounded, super-seamless iPhone 6 Plus, the Note 4 feels kind of sharp
  at the corners, but honestly easier to hold because of the flat sides," and metal gives him "increased
  hopes for durability." The faux-leather back ditched the fake stitching (now one smooth removable piece,
  for battery + microSD access).
- [2014-10-25] **The display is why he loves it.** 5.7" QHD Super AMOLED — big, bright, super-sharp,
  great outdoor visibility and viewing angles. "Extremely saturated, not totally accurate… but I love this
  display because it's the only one left that still impresses me every time I turn it on. So crispy." "The
  best display on any smartphone right now."
- [2014-10-25] **Hardware-ahead-of-content, restated.** Few apps/games use all the QHD pixels — most
  still scale from 1080p; "we're still waiting for that." The one clear beneficiary is **web browsing**
  (sharper text/images).
- [2014-10-25] **Control-over-software habit.** "I got very tired of Samsung's launcher and keyboard, so
  I installed the Google Now launcher and a few keyboards" — everything else stock. TouchWiz (on Android
  4.4, Lollipop promised soon) still has the S5's quirks.
- [2014-10-25] **Performance: fast, with the familiar TouchWiz caveat.** Snapdragon 805 + 3 GB + high-end
  GPU → "most stuff flies," but a consistent **pause on the multitasking button** and "occasional TouchWiz
  jank" pulling the tray too fast.
- [2014-10-25] **Multitasking is the Note's differentiator** — improved split-screen (copy/paste between
  panes) plus new resizable, minimizable **windowed apps** ("pretty nuts"), though supported-app lists are
  still short.
- [2014-10-25] **Size context:** roughly the same vertical height as the iPhone 6 Plus but thicker (huge
  battery) and wider — yet "the Note felt kind of compact despite the larger display."
