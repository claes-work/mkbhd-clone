# HD Tutorial: Encode any Video in HD for Youtube (@mkbhd, 2009-02-18)

## Summary
An origin-era **production how-to** (Marques, age 15) showing his workflow for exporting HD video
for YouTube using **Windows Movie Maker** — which he cheerfully calls "unbelievably stupid" and
"extremely easy." He drags a clip to the timeline, hits **Publish Movie → to This Computer**,
and in the settings dialog picks **720p** via the "More settings" dropdown (rather than the
default "best quality for playback"). Crucially he shares his **YouTube encoding rationale and
bitrate figures**: he uploads **all his videos in 720p** because it is (in his framing at the
time) the highest quality YouTube will play back, and going higher won't "translate." A concrete,
datable snapshot of his origin-era export pipeline and his early understanding of YouTube's HD
bitrate ceilings.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-18] His export tool is **Windows Movie Maker**: Publish Movie → to This Computer →
  More settings → **720p**.
- [2009-02-18] He **uploads all his own videos in 720p**, framing it as the highest-quality
  playback YouTube supported at the time and saying higher resolutions "won't translate."
  (Datable snapshot of his 2009 understanding — later superseded as YouTube added 1080p/4K.)
- [2009-02-18] Quotes bitrate/format figures for the WMM presets: **720p** → 1280×720, 16:9,
  ~25fps; **Xbox 360 HD** preset ≈ **6.9 MB/s**; **1080p** ≈ **7.7 MB/s** at 1440×1080.
  (Numbers as narrated — likely his rough recollection, not authoritative specs.)
- [2009-02-18] Acknowledges a known issue viewers raised — WMM output files coming out very large —
  and says he is "still looking into" a fix. Early responsiveness to audience feedback.
