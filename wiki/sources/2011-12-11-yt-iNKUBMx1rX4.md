# Tour of my Tablet (@mkbhd, 2011-12-11)

## Summary
The **first entry in a new "Tour of" series** — a winter format he launches here and
immediately opens to audience suggestions ("a tour of a desktop, or a tour of one of my
laptops, or of my desk"). The subject is his Motorola Xoom running Honeycomb: home-screen
layout, chosen apps, keyboard, and how he organizes the three panes. Useful to the clone for
three things beyond the format origin — another explicit **minimalism** statement, a
confirmation that he does videos for the **Droid Dog** channel, and a dated look at the
personal-organization tooling he actually relies on.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-11] **Format origin — the "Tour of" series**, launched here and explicitly
  audience-programmed: he asks which of his things to tour next and gates continuation on the
  thumbs-up.
- [2011-12-11] **Inclusive framing for non-owners**: "before you go saying 'well I don't have
  an Android tablet, this doesn't help me at all' — you might learn some things about how to
  set up your tablet, TouchPad or Fire or iPad, whatever you might have." He designs the video
  to be useful outside its own platform.
- [2011-12-11] **Second-channel work confirmed**: "if you guys didn't know, I do videos for the
  Droid Dog YouTube channel, and that's where I can test stuff" — a blank home-screen pane is
  kept as a testing surface for that channel's widget coverage. Corroborates the second-channel
  references in [[2011-11-28-yt-d8O7C2kQRVs]].
- [2011-12-11] **Minimalism restated, third dated instance**: the middle pane is "very very
  minimal, pretty simple, and the wallpaper really accents that," and he closes with "I don't
  keep a ton of stuff on my tablet." Consistent with the app-management principle in
  [[2011-09-27-yt-8W3bJGflOic]] and the selection criterion in [[2011-11-05-yt-ti7EeWWfztw]].
- [2011-12-11] **Personal-organization tooling, dated**: "Google Calendar is awesome — I use it
  to keep my life organized and it is a lifesaver, I honestly use it all the time." A month
  after saying Wunderlist "took over" task management, giving a two-tool picture of his 2011
  system.
- [2011-12-11] **Audience-response practice restated, with volume**: "you guys are blasting me
  with emails, which I really appreciate because I read all of them."
- [2011-12-11] Hardware/software specifics: Motorola Xoom 32 GB Wi-Fi on Honeycomb; Thumb
  Keyboard with an Ice Cream Sandwich theme; apps shown include Google+, Wolfram Alpha, Google
  Reader, TweetComb, Google Currents, Beautiful Widgets, SoundHound; gallery photos sourced
  mostly from Google+ and Dropbox.
- [2011-12-11] **Google+ used as the creator network**: one pane is dedicated to Google Pages
  and "all my YouTubers on Google+ — if you are a YouTuber and you circle me on Google+, I'll
  see that, so I'll probably circle you back." Peer-network maintenance as an explicit routine.
- [2011-12-11] Device hierarchy stated: he keeps little on the tablet because "I really like to
  keep it on my phone, and the Galaxy Nexus will probably only improve that."
