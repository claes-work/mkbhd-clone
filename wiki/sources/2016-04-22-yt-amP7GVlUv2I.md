# 2016 MacBook: Rose Gold Refresh! (@mkbhd, 2016-04-22)

## Summary
Review of the 2016 12" MacBook refresh — which he frames as barely a new generation ("a version 1.2…
essentially just a spec bump"). Skylake Core-m chips, faster SSD, marginally denser battery, and one
new rose-gold color; everything else identical. The clone-relevant meat is his two returning pet peeves
— **the single port** and **the 480p webcam** — both argued from his standing principles.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-04-22] **"Hardly a second generation… a version 1.2."** "There's not a whole lot of difference
  between this and last year's first gen — same paper-thin dimensions, same butterfly keyboard, same
  Force Touch trackpad, same great speaker, same 12" retina display. The only outside difference is the
  new rose-gold color." The "name the magnitude of the update" tic again — this is a quiet refresh.
- [2016-04-22] **The single-port pet peeve (argued from the Chromebook Pixel).** "You'd think they
  could at least throw in one more USB-C port now that it's later in the year and it's becoming
  mainstream… the Chromebook Pixel a whole year ago had it right with a USB-C port on each side — it
  makes a big deal when you want to use an accessory while charging. You'll still need to carry
  adapters, and that kind of defeats the purpose of having such a thin, light, beautiful computer."
- [2016-04-22] **The 480p-webcam pet peeve (argued from his own phone).** "It's 2016 — if the
  smartphone in my pocket can have a huge 4K sensor with OIS in this thin body, you could have fit a
  720p sensor in the new MacBook, just saying." A characteristic cross-device consistency argument.
- [2016-04-22] **Spec-bump upside, honestly stated.** Skylake Core m3 (1.1 GHz) up to m7 (1.3 GHz,
  turbo 3.1 GHz); SSD ~400 MB/s write, ~800 MB/s read; ~5% denser battery — "better performance and
  better battery life without getting thicker or heavier: that's a win-win." (The anti-thin value
  satisfied when gains come *without* thinning.)
- [2016-04-22] **Verdict / buying logic.** "It won't change your mind if you weren't already going to
  get one… but if you were on the fence about last year's, this one's better. I'd still recommend the
  13" MacBook Pro to people looking for more performance, but for a thin-and-light it doesn't get much
  better." Segments the recommendation by need.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I'd hardly call this a second-generation product at all — it's essentially just a spec bump inside,
> it's like a version 1.2 of the 12-inch MacBook."
> "If the smartphone in my pocket can have this huge 4K sensor with optical image stabilization in this
> thin body, you could have fit a 720p sensor in the new MacBook. Just saying."
