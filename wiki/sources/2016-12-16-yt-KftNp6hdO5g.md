# Apple AirPods Review: Fully Wireless! (@mkbhd, 2016-12-16)

## Summary
Review of the long-delayed Apple AirPods. He comes away **liking them more than he expected** while still
preferring his Jaybird X3s — crediting the **W1 chip** and the sensor-driven auto-play/pause "smart tricks."
Yields a characteristic reframe: **the wire on normal earbuds is secretly a convenience feature.**

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-12-16] **The W1 chip is the actual story.** "If you can get past the jokes about how they look, the
  W1 chip is what's special — open the case near your iPhone, one press, and they're paired to *all* your
  Apple devices (iPhone, iPad, MacBook Pro) without signing in again." Looks past the meme to the substance.
- [2016-12-16] **The sensor-driven "smart tricks."** Accelerometer + proximity sensors + a touch sensor:
  "take them out of the case, they turn on and connect; put one in your ear and audio auto-plays; take one
  out and it auto-pauses; single-tap for play/pause, double-tap for Siri (with the built-in mics)." Values
  the convenience/integration over raw sound.
- [2016-12-16] **The reframe: "the wire is actually a convenience feature."** "It might sound weird, but the
  wire between the earbuds of normal wireless earbuds is actually a convenience feature — if one falls out,
  you don't have to go looking for it, it's attached. That's kind of what this is teaching us." A
  characteristic see-the-hidden-value insight.
- [2016-12-16] **The honest comparative verdict.** "Would I recommend these over a similar pair like my
  Jaybird X3s? No, I'd still prefer those — I don't really like these that much. But I like them more than I
  thought I would, and that's a good sign — because of all the tech inside (mics, proximity sensors, auto
  play/pause)." Names his preference while crediting what's genuinely good.
- [2016-12-16] **Battery + the case-as-the-point.** "5 hours per charge — less than the X3s' ~8 — but the
  wireless-charging case is the big convenience: ~3 hours of listening in 15 minutes, ~24 total in the case.
  Commute an hour each way and you can go a week without charging." The case is the real feature.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The wire in between the earbuds of normal wireless earbuds is actually like a convenience feature… it's a
> strange thing to think of it that way, but that's kind of what this is teaching us."
> "Would I recommend these over my Jaybird X3s? No… but I like them more than I thought I would, and that's
