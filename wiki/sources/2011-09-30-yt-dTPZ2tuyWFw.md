# How to Give an Honest Video Review (@mkbhd, 2011-09-30)

## Summary
**YouTube Tips episode 5, and the clearest statement of MKBHD review methodology in the
2011 material.** Marques answers a question he says he's been getting repeatedly since he
started doing real product reviews: not how to *get* products to review, but how to review
them *honestly*. His answer is a single word — **perspective** — developed into a concrete
rule: a score is only meaningful relative to a disclosed baseline, so the reviewer must
tell the audience what they were using before. He argues a 10/10 from someone upgrading
from $20 iPod earbuds can be a genuinely honest 10/10, provided that baseline is stated;
without it, the same score is not honest but uninformative. Illustrated with two of his own
reviewed products (Beats by Dre Solo HD, Blue Yeti Pro) and closed with a commitment about
his own scale.

## Key claims
- [2011-09-30] **Content beats production, explicitly**: "you can have all the most fancy
  camera work in the world and all the greatest equipment for making the best video
  possible, but if the content of the video is bad then it doesn't mean anything." A
  striking statement from a creator whose channel is built on production quality.
- [2011-09-30] **The core rule — a rating is relative and the baseline must be disclosed.**
  "If you give them a 10, you have to be able to give some sort of perspective of what
  you've given them a 10 relative to." The dishonesty he targets is not the number but the
  **missing denominator**.
- [2011-09-30] **Worked example 1 (Beats by Dre Solo HD)**: someone coming off $20 iPod
  headphones can honestly call them 10/10 *for them* — while he states flatly that "the
  Beats Solo HDs are not a 10 out of 10 pair of headphones" in absolute terms. Both
  statements are held simultaneously; the disclosure is what reconciles them.
- [2011-09-30] **Worked example 2 (Blue Yeti Pro)**: coming from a built-in laptop mic it
  can be a 10/10 "in your budget," while "there are amazing microphones out there that
  would make the Yeti Pro look like a three out of 10." Rating is scoped to budget and to
  the reviewer's own reviewed set.
- [2011-09-30] **Commitment about his own scale**: "whenever you see an 8 and a half out of
  10 from me, I'll let you know what that's relative to, and what is actually a 10 out of 10
  on my scale." The scores across the 2009–2011 review catalog are therefore to be read as
  **relative to his own previously-reviewed set**, not as absolute industry rankings.
- [2011-09-30] Dated context for the question itself: he places it "in the recent days or
  months that I've been giving actual product reviews," and names the Beats by Dre and
  ATH-M50 reviews as the examples prompting it — the ATH-M50 review is 18 days earlier
  ([[2011-09-12-yt-Krdb8lceRoI]]).
- [2011-09-30] Format note: delivered "totally uncut" and self-described as a quick video —
  the methodology statement is deliberately unpolished, in contrast to his product reviews.
