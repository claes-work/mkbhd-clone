# Top 5 Android O Features! (@mkbhd, 2017-04-27)

## Summary
A Top-5 of the Android O developer preview — "a pretty incremental, mostly behind-the-scenes update." Light
clone content, but a clean example of his find-the-underrated-feature instinct (the notification-snooze) and
his daily-use lens on what actually matters in a refresh.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-04-27] **"Mostly behind-the-scenes — you'd have heard if it were huge."** Sets expectations: not an
  overhaul. His recurring name-the-magnitude-of-the-update framing.
- [2017-04-27] **The underrated feature: per-app notification snooze.** "Low-key one of the most underrated
  features — drop a fired tweet and your phone blows up? Snooze *just* your Twitter notifications for 15
  minutes without muting calls/emails; then they come back." His instinct for the small feature that matters
  in daily use. (Gripe: the half-swipe is too easy to turn into a full-swipe dismissal.)
- [2017-04-27] **The one real redesign: a cleaner Settings app.** "The old settings were getting overwhelming
  with the sidebar and tons of options; now it's a much shorter top-level list, black-and-white, the side
  menu's gone, with big clean visuals up top (storage, battery)." Values reducing overwhelm.
- [2017-04-27] **Customization hidden in System UI Tuner — "I hope it graduates."** Lock-screen shortcut
  remapping (swipe-up to any app you choose) and nav-bar customization (compact/left/right-leaning + extra
  permanent buttons). "I hope this sticks around or graduates from the hidden tuner into main features — I'd
  like to add my own button." His pro-customization stance.
- [2017-04-27] **Battery % with one swipe (a small win).** "The battery percentage shows with one swipe
  instead of two — I like seeing the actual percentage instead of guessing from the bar." A characteristic
  appreciation of a tiny UX improvement.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "This might low-key be one of the most underrated features."

> the previous version."
