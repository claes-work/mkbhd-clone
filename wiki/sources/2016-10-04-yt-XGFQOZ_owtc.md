# Google Pixel Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2016-10-04)

## Summary
First impressions of the Google Pixel / Pixel XL — "picking up where Nexus left off," but the **first
phone made entirely by Google inside and out** ("RIP Nexus"). The clone-relevant beats: the "made by
Google" significance, the Google Assistant baked into the OS as the headline (his Nexus-hype prediction
landing), a pointed **chin** design complaint, and skepticism of yet another "best camera by DxOMark"
claim.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-10-04] **"The first phone made entirely by Google — RIP Nexus."** "While Nexus was Google working
  with HTC/LG/Huawei, the Pixel is the first phone made entirely by Google, inside and out. The G logo
  now replaces the Nexus branding." Marks a strategic shift he flags as significant.
- [2016-10-04] **The chin complaint (design critique).** "I can't seriously be the first to mention that
  chin — wow. Is it really necessary to keep the phone symmetrical? I actually liked the Nexus 6's smaller
  chin; the fingerprint reader's on the back and the nav buttons are on-screen, so I'd have loved a
  smaller chin here." A sharp, specific industrial-design gripe.
- [2016-10-04] **Google Assistant baked into the OS = the headline (prediction lands).** "This launches
  with Android 7.1 — the main new feature is the Google Assistant, now built into the OS itself,
  replacing Google Now on tap; long-press home anywhere to ask it anything." His Nexus-hype forecast
  ([[2016-08-10-yt-C2p5aREEWvk]]) that the Assistant would headline — confirmed.
- [2016-10-04] **Skeptical of the "best camera by DxOMark" claim.** "The camera claims to be the best on
  any smartphone by their pre-production DxOMark ranking — I can't even keep track of how many phones have
  claimed that anymore, but it is promising." (12 MP, big 1.55-micron pixels, "basically UltraPixels,"
  f/2.0, near-zero shutter lag.) Reserves judgment for the real review under proper lighting.
- [2016-10-04] **"It still has a headphone jack — feels crazy every time I say that."** Notes the jack's
  presence as remarkable now, right after the iPhone 7 dropped it. The jack-defense thread continues.
- [2016-10-04] **The black-phone preference + premium-price caution.** "Of the three colors, the black
  one's my favorite — not a surprise, it's kind of my thing (the black version hides the sensor cluster
  better)." "$649 unlocked makes sense for the build and specs, but you're going to have to really like
  it." Impressions are "a bit of a mixed bag."
