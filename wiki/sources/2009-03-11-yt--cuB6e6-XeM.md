# 2009 iPod Shuffle 3G (@mkbhd, 2009-03-11)

## Summary
A same-day **product-reaction** video (Marques, age 15) filed to his "2009 Mac releases"
playlist, covering Apple's newly announced **third-generation iPod Shuffle**. It is
explicitly **not** a hands-on — he does not own the device and walks through Apple's own
site, photos and interactive 3D viewer rather than the hardware. He explains the redesign:
the Shuffle loses all its buttons, moving playback/volume control to an inline remote on the
earphones, adds **VoiceOver** (hold the remote to have it read out song/playlist names), and
becomes the **first Shuffle with playlist support**. He praises the pricing and size.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-11] The 3rd-gen iPod Shuffle is **buttonless** — all control moves to an inline
  remote on the bundled earphones; a 3-position switch handles off / in-order / shuffle.
- [2009-03-11] It is the **first iPod Shuffle with built-in playlists**, navigated via
  **VoiceOver**, which reads out track and playlist names aloud.
- [2009-03-11] Pricing: **4 GB for $79** (silver or black; ~1,000 songs); the older 1 GB
  Shuffle stays at **$49**. He frames the move as Apple slimming down the price for buyers
  who don't want to spend much.
- [2009-03-11] Reads it as a size/storage story — "the only thing they're changing is the
  size" — noting storage is cheap.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "I love the pricing you can get a 4 gig iPod Shuffle for $79 so that's a great price"

> "storage is cheap so you can [have] as many gigs as you want"
