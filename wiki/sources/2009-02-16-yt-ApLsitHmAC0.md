# HD Battery Saver #4 - Power Options (@mkbhd, 2009-02-16)

## Summary
Episode **#4** of Marques's (age 15) origin-era **"Battery Saver" / "Power Saver" HD tutorial
series**, screencast over his laptop. This installment covers the built-in **Windows power
management** as the "by far the easiest way" to extend laptop battery life: switching the
**Windows Vista** power plan (High Performance / Balanced / Power Saver) via the battery/plug
tray icon, and the different route in **Windows XP** (Control Panel → Power Options). He notes
Power Saver auto-dims the screen, tones down the CPU and graphics, and does not change screen
resolution. He mentions his machine is an **HP Pavilion** (which adds an "HP recommended" plan)
and that he keeps it on **High Performance** while docked at his desk working, gaming, etc.
Part of a recurring numbered series — evidence of early serialized, request-driven programming.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-16] Frames Windows' built-in power options as "by far the easiest way" to maximize
  laptop battery life — the theme of the whole battery-saver series.
- [2009-02-16] Vista power plans: **High Performance** (max performance, most drain),
  **Balanced** (the default), **Power Saver** (auto-dims screen, tones down CPU/graphics, keeps
  resolution) — recommends Power Saver on battery for basic tasks (music, email, Skype calls).
- [2009-02-16] He personally runs **High Performance** while docked ("sitting at my desk doing
  my work, Playing Games"). Datable early self-description of his workflow as a
  desktop-replacement laptop user.
- [2009-02-16] Windows XP uses a different path (Control Panel → Power Options); desktops always
  run max power, so this only matters on a laptop.
- [2009-02-16] Hardware detail: his origin-era machine is an **HP Pavilion** laptop with an
  8-cell battery. (Self-reported.)
