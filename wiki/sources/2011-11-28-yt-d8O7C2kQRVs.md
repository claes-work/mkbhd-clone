# Cyber Monday GIVEAWAY: ATH-M50 Headphones! [Closed] (@mkbhd, 2011-11-28)

## Summary
A short Cyber Monday video making good on the giveaway promised in the ATH-M50 review ten
weeks earlier: that video needed 800 likes, it got there, so he ships a pair. Two things
make it worth a page beyond the housekeeping — a **dated 20,000-subscriber milestone**, and
an explicit **giveaway-integrity practice**: the draw itself will be filmed and posted to
his second channel "so you guys know it's legit."

## Key claims
- [2011-11-28] **Milestone, dated**: "I just hit also 20,000 subscribers on YouTube, which is
  like a crazy milestone. I didn't really expect to get this far, but it's been a crazy
  journey." Slots into the subscriber timeline after the 15,000 stretch goal of Dec 2010.
- [2011-11-28] **Promise-keeping stated as the reason for the video**: the M50 review promised
  a giveaway at 800 likes; "it actually finally did hit 800 likes, so I'm going to keep my
  promise and actually give away a pair."
- [2011-11-28] **Engagement data point**: the M50 review took "about 500 likes on the first
  day, which is crazy and really awesome" — a rare dated glimpse of first-day performance on
  the 2011 channel.
- [2011-11-28] **Giveaway integrity practice**: no separate winner-announcement video on the
  main channel; the winner is announced on Twitter, and "I'll put up on my second channel just
  a quick video of me drawing a winner so you guys know it's legit." Verifiability is the
  stated purpose, and the second channel is used as the overflow surface.
- [2011-11-28] **Format testing, asked explicitly**: "if you like this giveaway format, by the
  way, just give it a thumbs up" — the audience is polled on the format itself, not just the
  content, continuing the vote-driven pattern.
- [2011-11-28] Entry mechanic: one week of comments on this video, one comment drawn as
  winner; requests for future videos solicited by DM.
