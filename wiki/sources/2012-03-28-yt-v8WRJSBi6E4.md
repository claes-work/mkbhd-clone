# Blue Spark Microphone Review! (@mkbhd, 2012-03-28)

## Summary
A review of the Blue Spark XLR condenser — explicitly framed as "a peek inside the MKBHD audio setup"
and reviewing **the mic he had already been recording his recent videos with**. Covers the XLR
requirement and the interface needed to get it into a computer, the shock mount and pop filter, and
the sound — demonstrated the only honest way available: the review is voiced through the microphone
being reviewed. Completes the audio-chain trilogy with the DAC PSA and the headphone reviews.

## Key claims
- [2012-03-28] **Own-gear disclosure, up front**: "this is the microphone that you've seen or heard me
  using in the past few videos" — reviewed as working equipment, not a loaned unit.
- [2012-03-28] **The demonstration IS the evidence**: "the most important part of having a microphone and
  why you spend all the money on it is how it sounds. So how does it sound? **You tell me. You're
  listening to it right now.**" He states the exact signal chain being heard: Spark → XLR → Blue Icicle
  → USB.
- [2012-03-28] **Anticipates the obvious blocker for his audience**: "some of you guys might be staring
  at your computer like, whoa, where's that port? I don't have that. Well, don't fret — your computer
  probably does not have an XLR port. So this here is the Blue Icicle. **Any audio interface will do.**"
  He names a specific product but explicitly generalizes the requirement.
- [2012-03-28] **Why it suits voice, with the mechanism**: "a large diaphragm condenser microphone, which
  means it gets the full dynamic range of your voice" — and with the focus mode off, "if you have a
  particularly deep voice you'll get all the bass tones… it'll sound really rich."
- [2012-03-28] **A claim demonstrated by its own recording quality**: "there is literally zero background
  noise — and that's thanks to it being really high quality, and also having a good USB and XLR cable."
  Immediately undercut, honestly and comically: "if you listen to this video in 1080p and you put
  headphones on, you might be able to hear **my roommate breathing in the background**."
- [2012-03-28] **Scoped recommendation**: "I'd recommend this Spark to just about anyone who's getting
  into **entry-level XLR microphones**" — bounded to a tier and an entry point rather than declared best.
- [2012-03-28] **The chain assembled explicitly across his own videos**: he points back to the DAC video
  ([[2012-03-17-yt-B2PCt-LQXzI]]) and a good pair of headphones — "nice pair of headphones, nice audio
  interface, you're pretty much set for audio."
- [2012-03-28] Accessories assessed with a reversal: the pop filter — "I didn't think it would work, but…
  it actually has worked surprisingly well"; the all-metal shock mount "isolates the mic from extra
  vibrations."
- [2012-03-28] **Dated living situation**: he has a **roommate** — consistent with the college-era
  timeline documented at synthesis pass 8.
