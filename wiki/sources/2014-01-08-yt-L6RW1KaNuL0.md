# 4K Cameras at CES 2014! (@mkbhd, 2014-01-08)

## Summary
A CES 2014 floor survey of 4K *shooting* options — restating his **shooting-vs-displaying-4K
gap / content-scarcity** thesis (everyone has 4K TVs, but shooting 4K is expensive and
inconvenient). Persona value is his production-gear literacy (Canon C500, 1DC, Sony FS700,
the first 4K Handicam) and the CES-floor-tech-is-expensive-but-will-come-down framing.

## Key claims
- [2014-01-08] **The shooting-vs-displaying-4K gap — content scarcity restated**: "everyone here
  has a 4K TV, but what about *shooting* it? It doesn't matter if you go above 4K — I've seen an
  8K TV, but nothing shoots 8K." The tech-outruns-the-content thesis (cf. the 2013 4K-Explained
  "price + content are the two barriers").
- [2014-01-08] **CES-floor-tech-is-expensive-but-will-come-down**: "a big part of CES is starting
  off ridiculous new tech that seems crazy at first but hopefully comes down in price over the
  coming months and years — we saw all these 4K TVs last year, now 4K monitors, and we're starting
  to see more 4K cameras." His adoption-curve framing.
- [2014-01-08] **Production-gear literacy — the 4K shooting landscape**: itemizes the options with
  prices and trade-offs — the Note 3 (4K but a small sensor + lower bit rate, falls apart on shaky
  motion, ~$600); the Canon C500 (needs an external recorder, "over $40,000" with the lens) and
  1DC (full-frame DSLR, 4K to SD cards, ~$12,000 body); the Sony FS700 (needs a recorder); and the
  first **Sony 4K Handicam** (the RX100's 1" sensor, records 4K internally, ~$2,000 — "way better
  than Note 3 quality, a great first step"). His deep camera-gear knowledge.
- [2014-01-08] **Values the compact-and-easy 4K balance**: "the Handicam line is famous for the
  best video in the palm of your hand and being easy to shoot with — that's the combo we're
  looking for. Putting 4K in a compact, easy format is a balance that's been really hard to
  strike." His judge-gear-by-usability (compact + easy) lens.
- [2014-01-08] **Request-driven CES coverage**: "I visited the Sony booth today, as you guys told
  me to on Twitter." The audience-programmed event coverage (per Update 10.0's promise).
