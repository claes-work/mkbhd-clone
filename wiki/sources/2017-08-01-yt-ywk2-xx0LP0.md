# The RED Raven Camera Kit: It's Back! (@mkbhd, 2017-08-01)

## Summary
The RED Raven (entry-level RED) returns as a camera kit sold **exclusively through Apple.com** — a notable
RED×Apple move. The clone-relevant gold is his **RAW-video explainer** — the clearest statement of *why he
switched to RED*: RAW video makes color temperature / ISO editable metadata (the Final Cut color-temp slider).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-08-01] **RAW video = the #1 reason he switched to RED (production).** "A RAW photo keeps all the
  sensor info unprocessed — you can change color temp/ISO/contrast after the fact without losing quality; a
  JPEG has it all baked in. Most video (phone, DSLR, camcorder) is like a JPEG — baked into a codec (ProRes ≈
  JPEG for video). RED is one of the few that shoots **RAW video**, so color temp/ISO become *metadata* —
  changeable after the fact. **That's the number one reason I switched to RED a couple years ago** (the
  Scarlet rental, for the glory of RAW video)." His definitive production rationale.
- [2017-08-01] **The workflow payoff: a color-temp slider in Final Cut.** "When I run-and-gun I focus on
  framing and camera movement (the creative things you *can't* change later) and get exposure/color close;
  then I bring the R3D files into Final Cut, install RED's plugin, and there's literally a color-temperature
  slider — like editing RAW photos. Some before/after frames would probably shock you. It's about as close to
  magic as it gets in my world." A concrete craft insight.
- [2017-08-01] **RED×Apple — "a pretty big move from a company making big moves lately."** "The Raven kit
  joins Apple.com alongside high-visibility products (Apple sells third-party accessories too), demoable in
  some Apple Stores." Reads the retail move as significant for RED's push into the mainstream.
- [2017-08-01] **What's in the kit + the modular framing.** The Raven brain, the 4.7" touchscreen monitor
  ("possibly my favorite piece of video gear of all time"), a Sigma 18–35 f/1.8, V-mount batteries, a mini-mag
  SSD + reader, a Nanuk case, Final Cut Pro, and the control app. "You add modules based on how you shoot —
  a one-stop shop to get this camera running." Notes the Raven shoots RED-code RAW **and** ProRes
  simultaneously ("if you don't need RAW on a shoot, you don't have to use it").
- [2017-08-01] **Who it's for (honest).** "Not for beginners — for someone who's been making video a while and
  wants to step up into the RED RAW world above 4K (a 4.5K sensor) and wants a modular system. Interesting
  that it'll be in Apple Stores where a lot of people see this for the first time." Grades it for its audience.
