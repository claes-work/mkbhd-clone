# The New Macbook Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2015-03-10)

## Summary
First impressions of the 12" **New MacBook** — and the **peak statement of his anti-thinness thesis**. The
laptop is "**too thin for normal batteries, too thin for any ports, too thin for a normal trackpad, even
too thin for a backlight for the Apple logo**." Apple turned the sacrifices into features (contoured
battery, butterfly keys, Force Touch trackpad, a single USB-C port), but the verdict is pure form-over-
function: **"people won't be buying this laptop for the power — they'll buy it for how pretty it looks,
and then deal with all the adapters and sacrifices."** A gorgeous Retina display and a clear first-gen-
caveat close it out.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-03-10] **The anti-thinness thesis, at its peak.** The thinnest/lightest laptop he's seen (2 lb,
  13 mm) — "you can't go this thin without making sacrifices," and he enumerates them: too thin for a
  normal battery (contoured stacked cells), for a backlit logo, for keyboard travel (new butterfly
  switches, "really thin keys, very low travel"), for ports, and for a mechanically-clicking trackpad.
- [2015-03-10] **The single port is the biggest inconvenience.** One USB-C port (power + data) on one
  side, headphone jack on the other — "if you ever need to plug in even a mouse or a flash reader, let
  alone a display, it turns this simple elegant machine into an octopus with adapters for arms."
- [2015-03-10] **Force Touch trackpad — a genuinely impressive "magic trick."** A glass surface over a
  vibration motor; pressure-sensitive so it *feels* like a mechanical click though nothing moves ("it
  fooled me"). Force Touch adds a third click (dictionary/Maps lookups, video scrubbing). "A risky move to
  change it, but really impressive."
- [2015-03-10] **Form-over-function verdict.** A Core M dual-core, fanless, non-upgradeable machine from
  $1,299 — "clearly not powerful… really more of a glorified tablet connected to a keyboard." Great for
  note-taking/students/everyday light use (8–10 hr). "People won't be buying this laptop for the power —
  they'll buy it for how pretty it looks, and then deal with all the adapters and sacrifices you made to
  get there."
- [2015-03-10] **The one thing it wasn't too thin for: pixels.** A sharp, bright, vivid 2304×1440 12"
  Retina display ("so good this guy thought it was a touchscreen"). Also gripes: a 480p webcam.
- [2015-03-10] **First-gen caveat.** "This is a first-gen product, so keep the second-gen in mind — Apple
  already has." Full review + comparisons to come.
