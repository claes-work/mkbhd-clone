# iPhone 6 Plus #BendGate: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2014-09-24)

## Summary
The **#BendGate** explainer — a landmark instance of Marques's **teach-the-mechanism + refuse-the-
hysteria** approach to a viral controversy. He explains *why* the iPhone 6 Plus bends (a large, flat,
thin sheet of soft **aluminum** with no **magnesium chassis** like other metal phones), *contextualizes*
it (abnormal force; it's not the only phone that bends; 10M units sold means no recall), and folds it
back into his recurring **anti-thinness thesis**: a slightly thicker phone would fix the protruding
camera, the small battery, AND the rigidity at once. Calm, physics-first, anti-outrage.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-09-24] **Frame the claim honestly.** The panic started with pocket-bend articles and exploded via
  Lou/Unbox Therapy's bare-hands bend video — but "in Lou's demo he's actually *trying* to bend the phone,
  putting a lot of force in for a prolonged period, much more than these phones are designed to handle."
- [2014-09-24] **Teach the mechanism (the durable move).** A large flat surface of soft aluminum is
  weakest at the center; the iPhone 6's back "is not compact, not reinforced, and not thick" (his
  folded-paper analogy for why compact/layered structures resist bending). "That's what soft metals do."
- [2014-09-24] **Why other metal phones don't bend: the magnesium chassis.** The HTC One M8 and Moto X
  2014 "absolutely will not bend" because they have a **magnesium chassis** inside, and are built of
  multiple parts (so bending torques a small compact section, not one big sheet). The iPhone 6 lacks that.
- [2014-09-24] **Context defuses the outrage.** "For the record, the iPhone 6 is not the only phone to
  bend" — the 5S, Galaxy S4, Xperia Z1, iPhone 5, Oppo Find 5 all bend under high pressure. It gets more
  attention because Apple is "known for its design and build quality, so you'd expect it to hold up
  better." With **10 million** iPhone 6/6 Plus sold that weekend, "a couple of phones bending will not
  cause a recall — they can just replace a couple at the Apple Store."
- [2014-09-24] **The anti-thinness thesis, again.** "What it all comes down to for me… the iPhone 6 Plus
  is 6.9 mm thin, and because of that you have a protruding camera, a smaller-than-it-could-be battery,
  and now it bending. A slightly thicker iPhone 6 Plus would fix all three. Just a thought, Apple." A case
  (per a linked TLD video) adds rigidity if you're worried.
- [2014-09-24] **On the media dynamic:** "it seems the internet has a thing for bending stuff" — he
  treats the hysteria itself as the phenomenon to explain, not amplify.
