# Open Back Headphones: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2015-06-24)

## Summary
The **audio explainer** he promised in the ATH-M70x review — an **"Explained!"** on **closed-back vs
open-back** headphones, and a showcase of his **audio literacy / know-your-domain**. Closed-back (most
headphones) seal for isolation; open-back (studio reference cans) have literally open backs, giving a
**wider, more natural soundstage** but **leaking sound** so "everyone can hear your song." A judge-by-
purpose framing: open-back for quiet solo/studio listening, closed-back for commutes/gyms/public.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-06-24] **Two types, chosen by environment.** **Closed-back** creates a seal to isolate your audio
  from the world — "what most of us want on a commute or at the gym." **Open-back** has no barrier between
  the drivers and the outside.
- [2015-06-24] **Open-back's pro: a wider soundstage.** "A much more natural sound where it sounds like the
  music is coming from the room around you instead of into your head — the guitar's back there, the drums
  over here… really immersive." Real-world sound mixes in (a pro or con): you can hear yourself snap along,
  or talk to someone next to you.
- [2015-06-24] **Open-back's real downside: sound leak.** "The diaphragm pushes audio inward AND outward…
  it'll sound like you're wearing mini speakers on your head — everyone can hear exactly what you're
  listening to." Fine alone/in a studio, "a little weird" on a bus or at the gym.
- [2015-06-24] **The gear ladder (know-your-domain).** The M50x (closed, "M = Monitor") isolate; the
  **ATH-R70x ("R = Reference," professional open-back)** and the **Sennheiser HD800** ("sound incredible…
  legitimately professional") are open-back — "a lot of the highest-end reference/studio headphones are
  open-back for these reasons." You don't need a huge budget: **Grado** makes great lower-priced open-backs
  (disclosed: "no one's paying me to say this").
