# Oppo Find 5 Project Firefly! (@mkbhd, 2013-04-17)

## Summary
An **exclusive first-look** ("the first video on the internet of it") at Oppo's Project
Firefly software update for the Find 5. Continues the Oppo arc and ties directly to the
Galaxy S4 "value-via-software" thesis — Oppo, like Samsung and HTC, is now adding value
through software. Also restates his **change-to-make-it-better-not-just-different** rule and
the **updates-as-value** (improve-a-phone-you-already-own) idea, and evidences a working
relationship with Oppo (early access).

## Key claims
- [2013-04-17] **Exclusive access — a relationship datum**: "this update has never been seen
  before — this is the first video on the internet of it." Oppo gave him a pre-release preview
  build, evidence of a maker relationship (early Oppo-arc access).
- [2013-04-17] **Explicitly ties to the value-via-software thesis**: "if you remember my Galaxy
  S4 features video, Samsung discovered that adding features via software has been really
  successful compared to via hardware… HTC got on board, LG too [maybe too much]… and Oppo has
  figured out, if this phone is going to be a beast on hardware, might as well make it awesome
  on software too." Applies the [[2013-03-17-yt-PGXhG82HRX0]] thesis across the industry.
- [2013-04-17] **Change-to-make-it-better, not just different — restated**: "I don't like when
  you change things just to change it… but if you change it to make it better, I tend to be a
  fan of that." His consistent rule for evaluating UI/skin changes.
- [2013-04-17] **Updates-as-value — improving a phone you already bought**: "this is a step in
  the direction of improving phones you've already bought, basically for free, by pushing OTA
  updates… you keep getting more and more updates directly from Oppo." The updates-add-value
  belief, applied.
- [2013-04-17] **Won't-pay-for-cosmetics nuance**: on the paid icon packs, "I don't think I
  would ever pay for any of them." Consistent with his pay-for-function-not-cosmetics leaning
  (cf. "I pay for apps if they're good").
- [2013-04-17] **Champions the trend, invites the makers**: "props, this is a neat trend, keep
  going with it… Samsung could be watching this, Google could be watching this." His
  encourage-the-good-direction disposition.
