# Asus Transformer Prime First Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2012-01-06)

## Summary
Unboxing and first impressions of the sold-out-everywhere Asus Eee Pad Transformer Prime —
the first quad-core (Nvidia Tegra 3) Android tablet. Standard hardware walkthrough and a
speed verdict ("oh my god, this thing is so fast"), but the page earns its keep for one
forward-looking claim dropped mid-video: that **Tegra 3 marks the end of the spec war**, and
that attention should now move to software. He also observes the review's own scope limits
without being asked.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-06] **"End of the spec war" prediction — the notable claim**: "I think this marks
  the end of the spec war and trying to have the highest amount of specs. I think if we put a
  Tegra 3 in everything, we shouldn't have any more problems and we can focus on software now."
  A dated, falsifiable statement that hardware had crossed a sufficiency threshold.
- [2012-01-06] **First-impressions scope stated unprompted**: "this is first impressions, so I
  haven't really had much time, so I can't tell you battery life or durability or anything."
  Same discipline as the Galaxy Nexus first-impressions video three weeks earlier.
- [2012-01-06] Hardware notes: "super ridiculously thin, like pencil thin," metal back, 10"
  IPS 1280×800 display, 8 MP rear / 1.2 MP front, mini HDMI, microSD, running Honeycomb
  (Android 3.2.1) with a light Asus overlay. Speed credited to 1 GB RAM plus the quad-core
  Tegra 3, and described as "way more snappy than the Tegra 2."
- [2012-01-06] **Comparative camera judgement against his own device**: the rear camera is
  "much better than the Xoom that I was using" — his existing tablet ([[2011-12-11-yt-iNKUBMx1rX4]])
  used as the baseline, consistent with the disclosed-baseline rule.
- [2012-01-06] **Superlative, scoped to category**: "this is, in my opinion, the most
  responsive and awesome Android tablet experience I have ever seen" — bounded to Android
  tablets and marked as opinion.
- [2012-01-06] Bloatware assessed leniently: the pre-installed Asus applications are "not too
  bad if you ask me, I really don't mind" — worth noting against his later, sharper positions
  on manufacturer software.
- [2012-01-06] US retail unit ("the wall AC adapter for the United States, which is where I
  got this guy"); full review promised, and ICS for the device flagged as arriving January 12th.
