# Material Design for Samsung Galaxy S6! (@mkbhd, 2015-06-25)

## Summary
A short how-to on applying an approved **Material Design theme** to the Galaxy S6 / S6 Edge — a
**control-over-software / theming** datum that directly addresses his standing complaint: TouchWiz's
*performance* is fixed, but its *aesthetics* still aren't Nexus-clean. The theme reskins the S6 toward a
stock-Android look (best of all: the settings app), letting him "make it my own." A quirky discovery: it's
filed under the theme store's "Themes for Men" section ("why is it called that? I don't know").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-06-25] **The complaint it solves.** "One of my biggest complaints with this contender for best
  phone of 2015 is still the software — a market improvement from the S5, but still TouchWiz, and I still
  prefer a cleaner Nexus look." A Samsung-approved Material Design theme is "a pretty big deal."
- [2015-06-25] **A Nexus-ish hybrid (copyright-limited).** It can't be exactly a Nexus (copyrights), but
  reskins the lock screen, app drawer, quick settings (toward the Nexus dark theme), dialer, messenger,
  keyboard, calculator, and clock — "a hybrid of Samsung's features and stock Android's looks, and I kind of
  like it." **The settings app is the best improvement** — "way better than the cartoonish set we had
  before."
- [2015-06-25] **Control-over-software, applied.** Theming (plus the option to grab Google Messenger/Clock
  from the Play Store) lets him fix the aesthetics himself — the make-it-my-own coping he described in the S6
  review.
- [2015-06-25] **The quirk:** it's under "Themes for Men," not "New" — "why is it called that? I don't
  really know."
