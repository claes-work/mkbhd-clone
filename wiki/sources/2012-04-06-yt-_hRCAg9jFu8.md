# Google Project Glass: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2012-04-06)

## Summary
**His first analysis of a wearable/AR product — the earliest new-form-factor evaluation in the
corpus.** Google had published the Project Glass concept video hours earlier; rather than react
immediately he says "I slept on it and I thought about it," then walks the concept video scene by
scene. Three lines of critique emerge, and all three are ones he still runs on new form factors:
**how do you control it**, **what does using it look like to other people**, and **how does the
company make money from it**.

## Key claims
- [2012-04-06] **Control is the unanswered question** — his first and most-repeated concern: the demo
  shows a notification moving from the periphery into focus with no visible input. "I'm really really
  hoping that there's a way to be able to choose when the notification goes from the corner to the main
  focus, because if you're doing something important like — well, I don't know, **driving a car** — you
  might want to be able to leave that notification in the corner." He asks whether it's eye gestures, a
  temple tap, a nod: "something to get the notification to be in your control. Very very important."
- [2012-04-06] **The social-legibility problem, stated by analogy** — the sharpest observation in the
  video: "if you thought people looked ridiculous wearing Bluetooth headsets and talking to nobody, at
  least you could tell there was something on their ear… With this Google Glass it's kind of just like
  someone walking up to you saying something to nobody, nodding in approval and walking away. It's the
  next level of that Bluetooth thing."
- [2012-04-06] **Second control worry, on the wearer's dignity**: "if you're blinking incessantly or
  nodding your head from side to side looking like a crazy person, I don't know."
- [2012-04-06] **The business-model read — prescient and unprompted**: "augmented reality is definitely
  the future. And **Google putting ads in it is also definitely the future** — hopefully not obtrusive
  ads. But like we've seen, Google makes a lot of money from ads, and it'll be interesting to see where
  they take it from here." He reasons from the company's revenue structure, not the demo.
- [2012-04-06] **What he likes is the integration, not the novelty**: the appeal is "the tons of Google
  services that I saw that are already on Android" — Calendar, Latitude notifications, Maps navigation,
  instant photo capture shared to Google+.
- [2012-04-06] **Product-stage caution**: "Google Glass will probably not be the final name — it's the
  name of the project that Google unveiled, but it's also still in sort of a super alpha testing." He
  declines to treat a concept video as a product.
- [2012-04-06] **Stated desire to test it first-hand**: "leave a thumbs up if you want me to try to get
  beta testing hands on these… maybe it's going to be like a Chromebook pilot program, but I really
  really really want to be one of the first people to use it." (He had applied to the Chrome OS pilot
  program in 2010 — [[2010-12-13-yt-Gj2wRJn5hHY]] if ingested, else see the Dec 2010 batch.)
- [2012-04-06] Incidental: he mentions being ill while recording, and teases "a very special video
  coming up this Sunday."
