# Moto G 2015 Review! (@mkbhd, 2015-08-07)

## Summary
A review of the 3rd-gen Moto G — framed as **"Motorola's real flagship"** (the best-selling phone they make,
by volume). A textbook **grade-on-the-price-tier** review: it strips premium parts (plastic edges, no NFC/
AC Wi-Fi) but keeps what matters at $180–$220 — the **Nexus 6 camera sensor**, a grippy textured back,
microSD, **IPX7 water resistance**, a front-facing speaker, and "**absurdly good**" battery life. "If this
were labeled a flagship killer with a huge price tag, those omissions would be head-scratching — but it's
not, so you can forgive it."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2015-08-07] **The Moto G is Motorola's real flagship.** "Sure, the Moto X Style and Play have higher-end
  specs, but the Moto G is the most important phone Motorola makes — it was their best-selling smartphone of
  all time." He recommends the $220 / 2 GB model.
- [2015-08-07] **Grade-on-the-price-tier (the frame).** Stripped-down premium: plastic edges, no 5 GHz/AC
  Wi-Fi, no NFC. "If this were being labeled a flagship killer or had a huge price tag, those would be head-
  scratching — but it's not, so you can forgive it."
- [2015-08-07] **What it keeps (premium parts that came down in price):** the **Nexus 6's 13 MP camera
  sensor** (no OIS/4K), the beloved dimple + a grippy textured back (won't slide off a table), a removable
  back + **microSD**, **IPX7 water resistance** (1 m / 30 min), and a big loud **front-facing speaker** ("not
  the Nexus 6 stereo, but far better than rear/downward-firing").
- [2015-08-07] **Battery is its best feature.** Snapdragon 410 + 720p 5" IPS + a 2470 mAh battery →
  **"absurdly good"** battery life ("maybe the best standby time of any smartphone").
