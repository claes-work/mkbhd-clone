# iPhone 7 Unboxing: Jet Black vs Matte Black! (@mkbhd, 2016-09-14)

## Summary
A rare live-unboxing video in which Marques opens two 256GB iPhone 7 units side by side — a matte black iPhone 7 Plus and a jet black (super-glossy) iPhone 7 — to help viewers decide between the two new black finishes. He walks the retail box experience for each (paperwork-on-top, phone underneath, then in-box accessories), highlights that jet black ships in a rare black Apple box, and inventories the contents: Lightning EarPods (not AirPods), the new Lightning-to-3.5mm headphone-jack adapter (a consequence of the removed headphone jack), a Lightning cable and wall adapter. He notes the jet black's fingerprint- and scratch-susceptibility, boots the matte black into iOS 10 to demo the new non-moving pressure-sensitive/taptic home button, and runs Geekbench 4 on the A10 Fusion chip. His through-line/POV: jet black is essentially a special-edition, higher-maintenance finish, so anyone struggling to choose should take "the safe route" with matte black.

## Key claims
- [2016-09-14] Both units being unboxed are 256GB iPhones — the matte black iPhone 7 Plus and the jet black iPhone 7.
- [2016-09-14] Buying a jet black iPhone gets you one of Apple's rare black retail boxes rather than the usual white box.
- [2016-09-14] In the box, the phone is not at the top — you first get the "Designed by Apple in California" paperwork, then the phone underneath.
- [2016-09-14] The plastic screen protector on a fresh unit is "about as fingerprint-free as this iPhone will ever be," foreshadowing that the glossy finish attracts fingerprints.
- [2016-09-14] The included earbuds are Lightning EarPods (Lightning-connector wired earbuds), explicitly not the wireless AirPods.
- [2016-09-14] Because the iPhone 7 removed the headphone jack, the box includes a Lightning-to-3.5mm headphone-jack adapter, which costs nine dollars from the Apple Store if you lose it.
- [2016-09-14] Box contents also include a Lightning cable (to connect to a computer/iTunes) and a wall adapter.
- [2016-09-14] The matte black also shows fingerprints, but not in the same way as the super-glossy jet black finish.
- [2016-09-14] The iPhone 7 Plus is still a lot bigger than a Galaxy S7 edge because it carries a much bigger bezel — a "big forehead," "big old chin."
- [2016-09-14] The iPhone 7's new home button is a non-moving piece of glass that's pressure-sensitive; when the phone is off it doesn't move at all, and iOS 10 lets you choose the taptic-feedback strength (setting 1 lightest, 2 default, 3 firmest).
- [2016-09-14] Geekbench 4 confirms the A10 Fusion chip with 3GB of RAM on the iPhone 7 Plus and 2GB on the iPhone 7, with a multi-core score above Snapdragon 820 devices.
- [2016-09-14] Buying advice: the jet black is almost like a special-edition phone that shows fingerprints and scratches, so anyone having trouble picking should take the safe route with matte black.
