# Samsung Galaxy Note 8 Review: A $1000 Android! (@mkbhd, 2017-09-21)

## Summary
The full Note 8 review — "the best phone Samsung makes," and a clean, explicit application of the **five
pillars** framework, plus a sharp meta-insight: the Note **nails four of five pillars, then invents its own
"sixth category"** (the S Pen + extras) that isn't strictly essential but *is* why people buy it. A model of
his rubric applied to a $1,000 flagship.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-09-21] **"The best phone Samsung makes" + the Note-7 callback.** "Also the most expensive. Last
  year's Note 7 went under the radar because of the recall, but it was low-key one of my favorites before all
  that blew up (oh wow, bad joke). This is Samsung swinging for the fences — let's treat it like that." Frames
  it as Samsung's best-possible attempt.
- [2017-09-21] **The five-pillars applied explicitly — four great, one short, plus a self-made sixth.** "If a
  phone does the five pillars well, it's worth the money — green light. The Note 8 does **four of the five
  really well (not just good, great)**, falls a bit short on the last, but then **makes up its own sixth
  category** with a bunch of extras. It doesn't *have* to nail that sixth one — it's not essential — but it's
  kind of why people buy this phone. **It's what makes the Note the Note.**" A refinement of the rubric: the
  essential five vs. the identity-defining extras.
- [2017-09-21] **"Samsung has had the best industrial design in smartphones for 2–3 years."** "A refinement
  of a refinement — the edges aren't sharp anymore, no camera bump so it doesn't rock, 83% screen-to-body
  (very 2017). Samsung helped popularize the glass-sandwich design we see everywhere now." A durable design
  verdict (fingerprints/breakability "fixable with a dbrand skin, zero effect on wireless charging").
- [2017-09-21] **The one real gripe: fingerprint placement.** "The worst part is the fingerprint reader —
  way up high next to the camera; on this big phone there's absolutely no natural way to hold it and reach the
  reader." His consistent Note-8/S8 ergonomics complaint (cf. the impressions).
