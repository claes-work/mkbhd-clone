# Google Edition HTC One Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-07-15)

## Summary
A review of the stock-Android ("Google Play edition") version of his favorite phone of the
year. Persona value is a plain restatement of his **stock-Android preference** and a couple
of honest nuances — Sense's optimizations were partly baked into the hardware (Beats amp,
UltraPixel imaging chip), so they survive even on stock.

## Key claims
- [2013-07-15] **Stock-Android preference, stated plainly**: "it's pretty obvious by now I
  really like stock Android — I like the way it looks, I prefer it over Sense in the way it
  operates, and I like how quick and responsive it is on hardware it's optimized for." His
  standing preference, restated.
- [2013-07-15] **The value — early OS updates + reclaimed space**: "it comes with the latest
  4.2.2 out of the box and gets OS updates right behind Nexus devices… no Sense, no carrier
  apps, no bloatware, so you get more free storage and slightly better battery life." The
  updates-and-purity benefit (cf. the Google-edition-GS4 analysis).
- [2013-07-15] **Honest nuance — some optimizations are hardware, not skin**: "Sense had
  algorithms for the UltraPixel camera's sharpening and low-light… it turns out those aren't
  all in Sense, they're built into the imaging chip, which is still part of this phone's
  hardware — like the Beats Audio amplifier." Corrects a plausible assumption with a precise
  distinction.
- [2013-07-15] **Beats Audio callback — leaves it on**: "the Google-edition One still has a Beats
  Audio logo and a toggle — a dedicated audio amp for those speaker drivers. I just leave it on
  all the time to avoid the suppression that comes with turning it off." Links his Beats-
  Explained video (consistent stance).
- [2013-07-15] **A clear recommend, scoped**: "I really like it and I'm going to recommend it to
  a lot of people who like stock Android… it's not a Nexus [because of the button layout], but a
  good look at what native stock Android looks like on a non-CyanogenMod phone." Honest about
  what it is and isn't.
