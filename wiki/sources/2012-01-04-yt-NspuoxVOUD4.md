# Technology in 2012 (@mkbhd, 2012-01-04)

## Summary
A year-ahead predictions video built around **"my big three" tech companies for 2012:
Google, Apple and Amazon**. Short and explicitly speculative, but valuable to the clone
because the calls are dated and falsifiable, and because the Amazon pick — which he flags as
the surprising one — is argued from consumer behaviour rather than from products.

## Key claims
- [2012-01-04] **The big three named**: Google, Apple, Amazon — "my big three tech companies
  that I think are going to just take over in 2012."
- [2012-01-04] **Google predictions**: more of the same — a revision of Ice Cream Sandwich
  ("maybe ICS 4.1"), and above all **Google+ pushed into everything** ("what I'm most excited
  for is to see Google+ everywhere"), plus continued Chrome growth off "a very high growth rate
  in the past couple of months."
- [2012-01-04] **Apple framing — the post-Jobs year**: "2012 will be the first year that they
  go through a full year without Steve Jobs, and I think this is kind of a big deal for them."
  Specific expectations: an overdue Mac Pro refresh, the iPad 3 "in the next couple of months,"
  and the next iPhone — with an aside about the naming confusion ("the iPhone 5 or 6 or
  whatever they call it; it's the sixth generation but it's the fifth, and I don't really
  know").
- [2012-01-04] **Amazon argued from behaviour, not hardware** — the pick he expects to
  surprise: "you might not have expected Amazon to be in my big three, but it honestly is the
  third biggest company for me as a tech enthusiast. I mean, think about it: when's the last
  time you ordered something online that wasn't from Amazon?" Plus the trajectory ("started as
  only an online bookstore and now sells just about everything… they might even sell groceries
  in 2012") and a Kindle Fire 2 prediction off the first one's popularity.
- [2012-01-04] Audience-participation close, characteristic of the era: he asks whether the
  viewer's big three would differ or be ordered differently, and restates "I'm always answering
  comments."
- [2012-01-04] **CES marker**: "if you're going to CES, by the way, better put up videos
  because I want to see it" — dated evidence he was **not** attending CES 2012, watching
  through other creators instead.
