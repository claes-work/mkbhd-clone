# Dual Camera Smartphones: Explained! (@mkbhd, 2016-05-04)

## Summary
An "Explained" educational video on why dual-camera phones are suddenly everywhere — pegged to iPhone 7
dual-lens rumors. The value for the clone: the teach-the-mechanism template in full, a historical
through-line (HTC EVO 3D 2011 → HTC One M8 → LG G5 / Huawei P9 2016), the taxonomy of dual-camera
*approaches*, and a dated prediction that dual cameras will proliferate over "the next 1–2 years."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-05-04] **The teach-the-mechanism template.** Explains the core principle: "there's almost never
  two identical cameras — usually one main camera and a secondary that captures extra information or a
  slightly different image, given back to add to the original." His characteristic "here's how it
  actually works" explainer mode.
- [2016-05-04] **The historical through-line (source-critical).** "One of the OGs of dual-camera was
  the HTC EVO 3D back in 2011 — identical cameras stitched into glasses-free 3D. I wouldn't say it
  flopped… but it kind of did (how many 3D phones have you seen since?). Then the HTC One M8 — a main
  camera + a depth sensor for fake-bokeh effects — the software was subpar at best; often you couldn't
  tell it was using depth, and the bokeh got glitchy on the edges." Names the failures honestly.
- [2016-05-04] **The 2016 taxonomy.** LG G5 = a regular 16 MP + a 135° super-wide 8 MP you switch
  between ("looks almost like a GoPro"); Huawei P9 = RGB + monochrome sensor stitched in software for
  more detail/sharpness/dynamic range. "There's no overlapping standard yet — it's mix-and-match."
- [2016-05-04] **Prediction — dual cameras will proliferate.** "With VR and 360° having a big year, and
  smartphone features arguably slowing down, it wouldn't be a huge surprise to see a bunch of dual-
  camera phones in the next 1–2 years." (A dated, trackable forecast.)
- [2016-05-04] **His own wishlist.** "I'd like to see two identical cameras facing slightly apart to
  capture two simultaneous high-res images stitched into one high-res panoramic without software
  tricks — or something related to VR / 360° capture without an extra accessory." Frames it as
  "wishful thinking from one person — but it's cool to know a lot of people are working on this."
