# New RED iPhone 7 Unboxing! (@mkbhd, 2017-03-21)

## Summary
An exclusive first-look unboxing of the (PRODUCT)RED iPhone 7 Plus — which lands right in his wheelhouse.
The clone-relevant core is the **red-and-black identity** ("this could be a full MKBHD Edition") and a
strong aesthetic verdict: **"the most photogenic phone I've seen in a long time."** Also his rare approval
of a mid-cycle color drop, and the charitable-cause context.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-03-21] **"The most photogenic phone I've seen in a long time."** "This matte red anodized aluminum
  is as ridiculously good as you'd think — as pretty in the flesh as in the bright ads, which is saying
  something. It's moved up to the top of my favorites — as much as I like matte black, this is honestly the
  most photogenic phone I've seen in a long time; the satin anodized look, and it won't show many
  fingerprints because it's not pure black." A strong aesthetic verdict.
- [2017-03-21] **Mid-cycle color drop — approved (a rare pass).** "New colors halfway through the year
  normally seem like a cheap grab for attention, but this one does not have me mad at all." Notes it's the
  third new iPhone color this past year (matte black, jet black, now red) and wishes Apple did more ("imagine
  a green or a blue").
- [2017-03-21] **The red-and-black "MKBHD Edition" dream — the white front holds it back.** "The only thing
  holding it back — for me and a lot of people on Twitter — is the white front. This could be a full-on MKBHD
  Edition red-and-black masterpiece with a black front; something about the white looks goofy, doesn't match.
  They could've made the Touch-ID ring red or black too — an obvious oversight." His red-and-black identity
  stated as a design ideal.
- [2017-03-21] **Three ways to go full red-and-black (customization, teach-the-mechanism).** "(1) The front
  glass is easy to swap — take a black front off another iPhone (but you'd need two phones); (2) a black skin
  on the front (but I don't like front skins, feels weird); (3) the easiest/most common — a red skin on the
  back of your current phone (won't be the exact anodized look, but gets you the red)." Practical
  customization options, consistent with his skins knowledge.
- [2017-03-21] **The charitable context, noted plainly.** "(PRODUCT)RED gives a portion to HIV/AIDS programs
  and research — a good cause, learn more at red.org." Reports the cause without overselling.
