# Wrapping Up 2022 and Blind Smartphone Test! (@Waveform, 2022-12-23)

## Summary
Year-end 2022 episode of the Waveform podcast, hosted by Marques Brownlee and Andrew Manganelli. The panel covers upcoming keyboard launches (a OnePlus × Keychron collaboration teased via a crowdfunding-style "red coins" pre-order, and a $350 Finalmouse keyboard with a video screen under transparent keys), the Cron calendar app and the hosts' productivity-app habits, a revisit of their 2022 tech predictions (Apple desktops, EVs, folding phones, drones), and a detailed breakdown of Waveform's 2022 blind smartphone camera test. Interspersed with the show's recurring trivia segment.

## Key claims
- (Marques) The 2022 blind smartphone camera test was run "science style" this year: 16 phones shot the same standard, portrait, and low-light photos; a purpose-built site served randomized head-to-head matchups scored with an ELO rating system. (2022-12-23)
- (Marques) The test accumulated well over 20 million votes from 600,000+ voters over ~3 days; vote distribution across phones was extremely even (every phone got ~2.7M votes; spread within ~5,000). (2022-12-23)
- (Marques) Overall winner by highest average ELO across all three categories was the Google Pixel 6A — beating flagships including the Pixel 7, iPhone 14 Pro, Galaxy S22 Ultra, Huawei Mate 50 Pro, Vivo X80 Pro+, and Oppo Find X5 Pro; last year's blind test winner was the Pixel 5A, so this was framed as a repeat, not a fluke. (2022-12-23)
- (Marques) Category standouts: Oppo Find X5 Pro strongest in standard photos (but near-last in portrait); Pixel 7 Pro dominated portrait mode; Vivo X80 Pro+ dominated low light; Realme 10 Pro+ was the "bang-for-buck" / votes-per-dollar standout as the cheapest phone in the test. (2022-12-23)
- (Marques) Test-design rationale: every test photo deliberately packs many variables (dynamic range, multiple skin tones, subject focus, depth-of-field falloff, hair and non-human cutouts) so no phone wins on a single trick; low-light shots test hand-movement isolation and exposure decisions. (2022-12-23)
- (Marques) A key finding: correctly-exposed photos beat over-bright ones — brightness/saturation help a bad camera avoid last place but a good exposure still wins; Samsung tended to over-brighten and did not win on that alone. (2022-12-23) ★
- (panel) OnePlus is teasing a keyboard co-created with Keychron via a "red coins" pre-order/investor scheme; the panel reads it as a Kickstarter-style demand-gauging move (compared to Teespring and to Nothing's pre-launch hype). Timeline hinted: testing Jan, launch Feb, mass production Mar–Apr 2023. (2022-12-23)
- (panel) Finalmouse announced a ~$350 mechanical keyboard with fully transparent keycaps over a video screen (e.g., a koi pond that scatters when keys are pressed); one host is openly biased against the company and skeptical of build value at that price. (2022-12-23)
- (uncertain/Marques) On Apple: a Mark Gurman/Bloomberg report indicates Apple is canceling the "M2 Extreme," will top out the Mac Pro at M2 Ultra, and appears to have canceled the iMac Pro; the 7K Pro Display XDR is delayed — described as Apple missing its own two-year Apple-silicon transition deadline. (2022-12-23)
- (panel) 2022 prediction scorecard: EV influx from legacy makers + startups largely hit (Volvo/Polestar, GM/Cadillac Lyriq/Hummer EV, Ford F-150 Lightning & Mach-E, Rivian scaling Amazon delivery vans, R1S reaching customers); a sub-$30k / 240+ mile EV arrived via the price-cut Chevy Bolt (259 mi, ~$27–28k); more folding phones (Oppo Find N2, Xiaomi, Samsung) but a "best foldable" award category was held back. Canoo was a miss. (2022-12-23)
- (panel) The Mac Studio essentially fulfilled a prediction for a pro-tier machine between the iMac and Mac Pro; MacBook Pros are strong enough that new hires pick them over desktops for creative/motion-graphics work. (2022-12-23)

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "I blind tested myself — which is really fun because now I didn't know which photo was attached to which picture — so my winners when I blind tested myself were, for the standard photo, Pixel 6A was my winner; for portrait mode and for low light, Pixel 7 was a winner."

> "I determine the winner by the one with the highest average ELO rating across all three categories… that winner was the Pixel 6A. It won the entire test against all the flagships, against the Pixel 7, its bigger brother."

> "Every picture we take for these tests intentionally has a bunch of different variables, because we don't want one of them to win just because it does one thing exactly right."
