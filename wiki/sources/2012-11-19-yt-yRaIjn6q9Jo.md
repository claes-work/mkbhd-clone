# LG Optimus G Review! (Nexus 4 Comparison) (@mkbhd, 2012-11-19)

## Summary
A review of the LG Optimus G framed entirely as **"everything different from the Nexus 4"** — the
Optimus G being "the older brother of the Nexus 4, the phone the Nexus 4 was based on." A targeted,
audience-serving review: for people on AT&T (or wanting LTE) deciding between the two nearly-identical
phones, he isolates the handful of real differences (AT&T LTE, an LG skin, capacitive vs. on-screen
buttons) and confirms his "good hardware, bad skin" read of LG.

## Key claims
- [2012-11-19] **Scoped by purpose** — the review-serves-a-decision framing: "for those of you locked
  into an AT&T contract or considering the Optimus G, this is everything you need to know that's different
  from the Nexus 4." He reviews the differences, not the whole phone, because the Nexus 4 review covers
  the shared parts.
- [2012-11-19] **The hardware is nearly identical**: same Snapdragon S4 Pro, 2 GB RAM, 1280×768 IPS,
  Gorilla-Glass-both-sides, same 8 MP camera and 2100 mAh sealed battery. Differences: the Optimus G has
  **AT&T LTE** (the HSPA+-only Nexus 4 lacks it), capacitive buttons (vs. the Nexus 4's on-screen),
  an LG logo, a power-button notification light, and a textured back.
- [2012-11-19] **The AT&T-LTE angle is the real reason to consider it**: "depending on whether you have
  AT&T LTE coverage in your area, this could be an awesome choice next to the HSPA-Plus-only Nexus 4." A
  use-case-keyed recommendation, consistent with his buyer's-help style.
- [2012-11-19] **"You're basically right" honesty**: "to the people who say the Optimus G is basically
  the same as a Nexus 4 plus AT&T and an LG skin — well, you're basically right." He validates the
  audience's own summary rather than manufacturing distinctions.
- [2012-11-19] **The LG skin assessed, with a specific bug**: LG's Ice Cream Sandwich skin is "comparable
  in features to TouchWiz" (nature lock screen, QuickMemo screenshot-and-annotate, Q-Slide floating video
  with adjustable transparency), but "while you can rotate on the home screen, it redraws all the graphics
  almost every single time, which I thought was a little strange." A precise, dated nitpick.
- [2012-11-19] **Nexus 4 excitement, dated**: "tomorrow is Nexus 4 day for me… knowing this, I am beyond
  excited for the Nexus 4." Sets up the unboxing ([[2012-11-20-yt-RvfjuYUe6-w]]).
- [2012-11-19] **Honest on the software gap**: "no word on a Jelly Bean update yet, but the experience is
  pretty awesome even without Google Now or Project Butter."
