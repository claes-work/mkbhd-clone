# Tech Channel of the Week #10: BoykoBrand (@mkbhd, 2009-07-12)

## Summary
Tenth and milestone-numbered Tech Channel of the Week, spotlighting BoykoBrand — run by
"Brad," a channel joined November 2007 (same era as his own) but only recently pivoted
to tech content, 30 subscribers, 725 channel views. Distinctive for an extended,
explicit **self-comparison**: he narrates that Brad's early trajectory mirrors his own
exactly, and restates his own origin-growth thesis (volume first, then refine) in the
process — the clearest first-person restatement yet of the "started the same way"
narrative.

## Key claims
- [2009-07-12] **Explicit self-comparison to his own origin**: "this YouTube channel is
  starting exactly the same way as I did... he started and he's been making a lot of
  quality tech videos in a short span of time" — directly maps another creator's early
  trajectory onto his own.
- [2009-07-12] **Restates his own volume-first origin thesis, dated**: "I made about a
  hundred videos in a month and a half, two months, and really got my act together and
  started making, you know, the same quality videos, just spread them out a little
  more" — a first-person, self-reported summary of his early posting cadence, consistent
  with (and reinforcing) the volume-as-strategy pattern already logged from
  [[2009-06-15-yt-FDASvkMxraA]].
- [2009-07-12] **States he "didn't have a tech name"** when he started, having converted
  a channel he "didn't really use that much" into a tech channel by "making a ton of
  screencasts" — a small but concrete detail about the channel's pre-tech-focus history.
- [2009-07-12] **10x10x10 growth framing**: "when you hit 10 by 10 by 10 it'll start
  going up exponentially, you'll get a lot of subscribers" — an explicit (if informal)
  model of subscriber-growth acceleration applied to Brad's 30-subscriber channel.
