# Apple iPhone X Unboxing! (@mkbhd, 2017-10-31)

## Summary
A solo first-impressions unboxing of the iPhone X — the phone Marques framed as the one worth skipping the iPhone 8 for, "the one that represents the future of Apple's smartphones." He notes the box never actually prints "X" (Apple's Roman-numeral "iPhone 10") but everyone will call it the X anyway. The unboxing contents are "pretty much exactly the same" as the iPhone 8 — headphones, adapter, stickers — with no surprises. From there he walks through what's genuinely new on his review unit after roughly a day of use: the 5.8-inch corner-to-corner OLED display in a footprint smaller than the iPhone 8 Plus; the notch and app compatibility; Face ID setup during first boot replacing Touch ID; the removal of the home button and the swipe/gesture and new button-combo language it forces (power + volume-up for screenshots, power + volume-up to reach power-off, long-press power for Siri, double-press for Apple Pay); the TrueDepth front camera enabling Face ID, selfie portrait mode, and Animoji. Everything is explicitly flagged as early impressions with a full review to follow.

## Key claims
- [2017-10-31] The iPhone X is the phone he told viewers to skip the iPhone 8 for — it "represents the future of Apple's smartphones"; despite "X" appearing nowhere on the front of the box, people will always call it the X and he's accepted that.
- [2017-10-31] The unboxing experience is "pretty much exactly the same" as the iPhone 8 and almost every other iPhone — headphones, adapter, stickers all in the box — and that lack of surprises is arguably a good thing.
- [2017-10-31] The silver unit is a "really shiny, chromy, reflective phone" with very shiny sides; the space gray one is "a bit more toned down."
- [2017-10-31] The only thing actually new during setup is that you now configure Face ID during first boot instead of the fingerprint/Touch ID.
- [2017-10-31] The biggest difference is the 5.8-inch OLED display that goes corner to corner yet fits in a footprint smaller than the iPhone 8 Plus (which has a smaller screen), so most attention will land on the panel and the notch and how apps handle it.
- [2017-10-31] First impression of the OLED panel is positive: bright, contrasty, well calibrated, and "definitely not over saturated like some OLEDs can be."
- [2017-10-31] With no home button, new interactions must be memorized: swipe up to go home, power + volume-up for a screenshot, long-press power + volume-up for the power-off menu, long-press power alone for Siri, double-press power for Apple Pay, plus multitasking/app-switching gestures — "if you're gonna make a phone that's all screen, obviously you have to be able to do it all without a lot of buttons."
- [2017-10-31] The TrueDepth front camera suite in the notch (IR blaster, depth sensor) enables Face ID, selfie portrait mode, and Animoji.
- [2017-10-31] Face ID first impression (about one day of use): setup was "quick and painless," it's been working well for unlocking, and it resists spoofing — it won't unlock from a photo, when you look away, or with eyes closed, so "your girl can't unlock your phone while you're asleep." Deeper Face ID vs. Touch ID thoughts (and a twins test) are deferred to an upcoming video.
- [2017-10-31] The front-facing selfie portrait mode "seems pretty average" — the Pixel 2 he's been testing seemed "way more confident" with front-camera portraits, showing a big difference on edges and hair, and the iPhone sometimes refused to blur the background or got picky about lighting; both should improve with software, but full testing is reserved for the review.
- [2017-10-31] Animoji physics — objects moving around as you map your face to a cartoon animal head for iMessage — are "actually pretty on point."
- [2017-10-31] This is a first look only; the full review will cover battery life, camera quality, and app performance, and he invites viewers to suggest what else to test.
