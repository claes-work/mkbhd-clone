# DSLR Video - Tips & Tricks (@mkbhd, 2010-12-28)

## Summary
A production-tips video (billed as separate from the upcoming formal
"YouTube Tips" series) sharing pros/cons of shooting video on the Canon T2i
DSLR: strong outdoor/natural-light performance regardless of lens, the
18-55mm kit lens as his current lens, the primacy of lens quality over
camera-body quality, manual-focus adjustment as a DSLR-specific skill,
indoor/tech-reviewer-setting lighting needs (deferred to a future tips
video), and shallow depth of field as a signature, easily-achieved DSLR
effect.

## Key claims
- [2010-12-28] **States a production philosophy explicitly**: "if you plan on
  improving the quality of your tech video reviews you really do want to make
  the gadget the star of the show, even if you do show your face or your
  hands in the video" — a dated, first-person articulation of shallow depth
  of field as a tool for foregrounding the product being reviewed, an early
  precursor of his mature visual-craft standards.
- [2010-12-28] **Lens-over-body claim**: "no matter how good your camera body
  is, it's not going to matter if you're using a terrible lens" — states lens
  quality as the binding constraint on footage quality, not the camera body.
- [2010-12-28] **Self-assessed as a beginner**: "keep in mind I am kind of a
  noob at this, so I'm still getting to know a lot of this myself" —
  self-reported, dated hedge; useful contrast point against his later
  established production authority.
- [2010-12-28] Recommends a specific starter kit for DSLR video beginners:
  Canon T2i + 50mm f/1.8 prime lens.
