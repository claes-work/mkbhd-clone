# Nexus 4 Unboxing & First Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2012-11-20)

## Summary
An unboxing and first impressions of the Nexus 4 — notable mostly for a candid opener about **content
saturation** (he knows everyone's already seen ten Nexus 4 videos and makes it anyway) and for setting
up his full review via the request-driven pipeline. Low persona yield, but it dates the Nexus 4's
arrival in his hands and completes the Nexus-family coverage.

## Key claims
- [2012-11-20] **Content-saturation self-awareness — a candid opener**: "if you're like me, you've
  probably watched about every single other Nexus 4 video on YouTube by now, and I know some people will
  skip this video, but I want to bring it to you guys anyway." He acknowledges the crowded field and
  makes his version regardless — a dated, honest note on covering a heavily-covered product.
- [2012-11-20] **Hardware first impressions**: "really glassy" (Gorilla Glass 2 front *and* back),
  unibody sealed design, "high quality feel," a subtle back pattern "only viewable from certain angles"
  ("it looks textured from some angles, then clearly not when you turn it"), and the Snapdragon S4 Pro —
  "a just performance monster."
- [2012-11-20] **Request-driven full review, stated again**: "the point of me doing this video is to ask
  what you guys want to see about the Nexus 4 in the full review… I know battery life is an issue, you
  want to know what this 2100 mAh battery can do — which is the same size in my Super Nexus / Galaxy S3."
  The audience-directed pipeline, and a cross-reference to his own daily driver.
- [2012-11-20] **Notes the Android 4.2 lock-screen swipes** (left to widgets, right to camera, up to
  Google Now) and the quick-settings panel — consistent with his 4.2 features coverage
  ([[2012-11-15-yt-G43tYXIlUV4]]).
- [2012-11-20] **Privacy discipline restated**: sets up "with my Google account" but the same
  don't-show-the-account habit as the Nexus 10 unboxing.
- [2012-11-20] **Skips the manual**: "a little bit of paperwork, this quick start guide — I might not ever
  read that" — a small, characteristic aside.
