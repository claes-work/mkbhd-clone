# Hackintosh Pro Project! [Part 3: The Build] (@mkbhd, 2013-01-23)

## Summary
Part 3 — the physical assembly of the Hackintosh Pro. Mostly a build-along, but it
surfaces two persona-relevant traits stated in his own words: an **explicit "my OCD kicks
in" neatness** about cable management, and a **quiet/silent-build priority** driven by his
recording needs. Also the earliest clear statement of a personal **aesthetic ("inverted
Stormtrooper" black-and-white) that matches the rest of his setup** — an early sighting of
the studio look he becomes known for.

## Key claims
- [2013-01-23] **"My OCD kicks in" — stated neatness/aesthetic value**: on cable
  management, "good airflow is important… and of course my OCD kicks in here and wants
  this thing to be as neat as possible." His self-described obsessive neatness, applied to
  the inside of a machine almost no one sees. A dated character datum.
- [2013-01-23] **Silence is a design goal because he records audio**: he swaps the stock
  Corsair fans for quieter Noctua fans and picks the insulated R4 "because we're going for
  a completely silent build… you can't tell by listening whether this machine is on or
  not." Production-driven engineering choice (no fan noise on recordings).
- [2013-01-23] **A personal aesthetic — "inverted Stormtrooper"**: "my favorite part of
  this build is definitely the case… I'm a huge fan of the black-and-white color scheme,
  sort of an inverted Stormtrooper look that matches the rest of my setup." Early evidence
  of the deliberate, matched **black-and-white studio aesthetic** he's later famous for.
- [2013-01-23] **Admires engineering as craft — a taste tell**: calls the motherboard "a
  work of art… a beautiful piece of engineering," while conceding "not the prettiest color
  scheme." Appreciates the object even where he dislikes the styling (even-handed even
  about his own parts).
- [2013-01-23] **Expansion restated, concretely**: "there's still room for expansion —
  extra PCI slots… four extra RAM slots… this is a huge reason to choose a Hackintosh over
  a Mac Pro." Reinforces the upgradeability doctrine from part 2.
- [2013-01-23] **Behind-the-scenes on Twitter/Google+ — a creator-transparency habit**:
  "if you want a behind-the-scenes look, follow me on Twitter, where I've been posting
  behind-the-scenes pictures." Documents the process publicly (dated platform note).
- [2013-01-23] **Defends a contested parts choice with reasons**: on picking the H80i over
  the H100i "a lot of people were yelling at me you should have chosen the H100i… with this
  motherboard and case it would have been very difficult to fit." Explains a decision
  against audience pushback (his reasoned-defense habit).
