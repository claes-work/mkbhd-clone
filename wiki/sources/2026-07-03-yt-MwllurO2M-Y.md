# Apple Raises Prices on Everything! (@Waveform, 2026-07-03)

## Summary
A regular weekly Waveform episode hosted by Marques Brownlee and Andrew Manganelli, with David Imel arriving mid-episode (the studio AC is off and the room heats through the recording, a running gag). The panel dissects Apple's across-the-board hardware price increases (Macs, iPads, Apple TV, HomePods, Vision Pro — iPhone notably unchanged), attributing them to an unprecedented RAM/memory shortage tied to the AI data-center boom, and debates whether the hikes are temporary or permanent. Further segments cover a rumored squat "wide"/passport-shaped Samsung foldable, Polestar being pushed out of the US market, WhatsApp usernames, Sony ending physical PlayStation disc production (with a physical-media rant from team member Mariah), the Dbrand/Valve companion-cube takedown, and two "confusing" AI products (Poke/Poke Humans and Taste Labs). Because the transcript is auto-generated captions with no speaker labels, only a few lines are cleanly attributable to Marques.

- [2026-07-03] The stated driver is an unprecedented memory/RAM shortage; some higher-volume/low-RAM products (e.g. Apple TV up ~40%, ~$130→$199; MacBook "Neo" $599→$699) went up seemingly to subsidize smaller increases on RAM-heavy pro machines. (panel)
- [2026-07-03] Big-ticket examples cited: M3 Ultra Mac Studio ~$4,000→$5,300; M4 Mac Studio ~$2,000→$2,500; Vision Pro only +$200; iPad Air (11") $599→$749; HomePod $299→$349; HomePod mini $99→$129. (panel — figures approximate, read on-air)
