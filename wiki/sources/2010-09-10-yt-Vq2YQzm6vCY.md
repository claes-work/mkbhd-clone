# Review: Altec Lansing Octane 7 (@mkbhd, 2010-09-10)

## Summary
Review of the Altec Lansing Octane 7 2.1-channel speaker system (twin satellites with
tweeter + downward-firing midrange cone, plus a compact 6.5" subwoofer). Walks through
the driver layout, controls, cabling, and frequency response, then **resolves the
royalty-free-music constraint** flagged in the two prior videos this batch by playing
an actual (licensed/free) track to demo bass, following a viewer's suggestion. Closes
with a numeric score.

## Key claims
- [2010-09-10] **Directly answers a viewer comment to fix the demo-audio gap**: "in
  the previous audio review I didn't actually play any copyrighted music but I did get
  a comment and it's a good suggestion of just letting the speaker thump at a high
  volume" — plays "Fifth Dimension" by boOom (a stated royalty-safe/free source) so
  viewers could judge the bass on their own speakers. This is a concrete instance of a
  suggestion from comments changing what a review shows, not just a stated intent —
  the audience-request-shapes-format pattern (previously seen with live unboxings)
  extended here to audio-demo methodology. ★
- [2010-09-10] **Explains the downward-firing midrange design as a deliberate
  tradeoff**: the mid-cones face down and "pump audio directly onto your desk," which
  he frames as "a good substitute for not having a mid-range speaker" rather than a
  flaw.
- [2010-09-10] **Compact-subwoofer-vs-large-subwoofer comparison**: claims the 6.5"
  subwoofer matches the frequency response (15-20Hz low end) of a "typical 10-inch
  subwoofer" while using less floor space — a space-efficiency argument for the
  product's main selling point.
- [2010-09-10] **Numeric score**: "these are an 8 and a half out of 10 in my book" —
  his most positive score of this batch of three consecutive speaker reviews (compare
  7.2/10 for the Cyber Acoustics system and no score for the Solar Sound 2).
