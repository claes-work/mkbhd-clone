# iPhone 15/15 Pro Impressions: Not Just USB-C! (@mkbhd, 2023-09-13)

## Summary
Solo hands-on from Apple's "Wonderlust" event covering the **iPhone 15 / 15 Plus / 15 Pro /
15 Pro Max**. He offers a recurring analytical **lens**: nearly every new feature is either
"**something we've already seen in some other phone for years**" or "**an ecosystem feature
that only works with other iPhones.**" Headline is **USB-C** ("Lightning is officially
dead"), but with a catch — only the Pros get USB 3 speeds (10 Gbps) via the A17 Pro's
controller; base 15s stay at USB 2.0 (~480 Mbps), and there's still **no fast charging**
(~20–25W). Base 15s inherit last year's A16 and the 48MP main camera plus next-gen
auto-Portrait. Pros add the **A17 Pro** (first 3nm phone chip), **titanium** build, a more
repairable back, the **Action Button** (his favorite feature), and cameras (Pro Max-only
5X folding telephoto). Prices up on the Pro Max ($1,199, 256GB base). Anker-sponsored.
Solo — persona/voice data.

## Key claims
- [2023-09-13] **Framing lens**: everything new "falls into two buckets — it's either
  something we've already seen in some other phone for years, or it's some ecosystem feature
  that only works with other iPhones… not necessarily a bad thing, but a lens to look
  through this stuff at."
- [2023-09-13] **USB-C is the headline**: "Lightning is officially dead… only ever been one
  port change in the iPhone ever" (2012 30-pin → Lightning). But it's "just USB" — the same
  port laptops/tablets have had for years. Enables one-cable charging and C-to-C reverse
  charging of accessories (e.g. the new USB-C AirPods case).
- [2023-09-13] **Key segmentation catch**: the A17 Pro's new USB 3 controller means only the
  **Pro** phones get up to 10 Gbps transfer; the base 15/15 Plus are "stuck at USB 2.0…
  ~480 megabits per second," basically Lightning speeds. He "kind of wishes they'd gone all
  the way" to Thunderbolt.
- [2023-09-13] **No fast charging** is "disappointing" — switching to USB-C was "a huge
  opportunity" but it's "the same 20 or so watt charging we've had for the iPhone for forever";
  still ~1.5–2h to charge. (Notes he discussed this on Waveform.)
- [2023-09-13] Base 15/15 Plus: last year's **A16 Bionic**, new 48MP main sensor,
  2,000-nit peak brightness, Dynamic Island, and the auto-capture **next-gen Portrait Mode**
  (adds/removes blur after the fact) — "the new baseline phone is basically last year's Pro
  phone repackaged." Prices held at $799 / $899.
- [2023-09-13] Pro adds: **A17 Pro** (first 3nm phone chip, ~10% faster CPU cores, up to 2×
  neural engine), lighter **grade-5 titanium** rails with slimmer bezels (but same ceramic
  shield glass — "it's the glass that breaks," not the rails), a **more repairable** back
  glass, and cameras (48MP main, improved ultrawide; **Pro Max-only 5X folding telephoto**;
  buried 24/28/35mm default focal-length presets).
- [2023-09-13] **Favorite feature: the Action Button** (Pro only), replacing the mute switch
  with a deeply customizable button (focus modes, camera, flashlight, voice memos,
  magnifier, or any Siri Shortcut → nearly any app). "Totally not what I expected."
- [2023-09-13] Pricing: 15 Pro holds at $999; **Pro Max bumped to $1,199** but is the only
  one with the 5X camera and starts at 256GB — "getting rid of the $1,100 phone."
