# OnePlus One: Revisited! (@mkbhd, 2014-07-26)

## Summary
A **"Revisited"** follow-up two months after the OnePlus One review — a dated instance of Marques
**updating a verdict with the passage of time**. His original review said the phone would "live or die on
CyanogenMod"; two months on, CyanogenMod has been "vigilant" with weekly updates that fixed the bugs and
brought performance up to **Nexus/Google-Play-edition smoothness** — so the software bet paid off. The
remaining problem has shifted to the **company/hardware side**: the still-in-place invite-only sales
system, "a logistical nightmare."

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-07-26] **The software verdict resolved positively.** The open question from the review — would
  CyanogenMod maintain it like a Nexus? — is answered: several updates, the first "pretty much cured all
  the major bugs," subsequent ones brought it to Nexus-level smoothness. "Impressively enough they've
  actually done that… it's really smooth now." (Notable: CM usually never quite matches stock Android;
  here it did.)
- [2014-07-26] **Added value over time:** a much bigger theme store (make it look like an LG G3, a Galaxy
  S5, Android L — "a crazy hybrid Frankenstein OS"), and OnePlus promised Android L within 90 days of its
  final release.
- [2014-07-26] **Honest downside of the customization:** the screen-off gestures got over-sensitive — the
  V-for-torch gesture kept activating in his pocket ("how did it get so warm? … the LED light was on,
  killing the battery"). "The beauty of CyanogenMod is those features can be deactivated."
- [2014-07-26] **The problem migrated to the company side** — the still-active **invite-only** purchase
  system is "kind of a logistical nightmare," with people "scouring the internet for invites."
