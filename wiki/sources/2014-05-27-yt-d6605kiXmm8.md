# LG G3 Impressions! (@mkbhd, 2014-05-27)

## Summary
An **exclusive first look** at the LG G3 (a pre-release Korean model), "one of the most anticipated
phones of 2014." The headline is the **2560×1440 QHD "2K" display** (>530 ppi) — and Marques's honest
framing that the **hardware is running ahead of the content**: right now almost nothing renders above
1080p, exactly like the moment 1080p phones were new and the content had to catch up. Also notable: the
**laser-autofocus** camera, thin bezels that keep a 5.5" phone compact, and a minor thermal
brightness-throttle con. A dated instance of his "judge the pixel benefit against what's actually
displayed" instinct and his "this could be an influential device" read.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-05-27] **Top-notch specs, handsome body:** Snapdragon 801, 3 GB RAM, the 5.5" 2560×1440 display,
  a plastic-but-nice back, improved rear buttons, a 3000 mAh **removable** battery, and microSD — "LG
  hearing everyone out."
- [2014-05-27] **Thin bezels are the design win** — the small chin/bezels make the 5.5" G3 feel as
  compact as a 5" Galaxy S5 or HTC One M8. "I'm a huge fan of keeping this design language, these thin
  bezels — I love it."
- [2014-05-27] **The camera adds laser autofocus, OIS, dual-tone flash, 4K video, 120 fps slow-mo** —
  the laser AF "actually fires lasers and uses the reflections" to focus fast; gesture selfie (clench a
  fist), one-press shutter via the rear buttons.
- [2014-05-27] **The QHD display's benefit is limited today because the content isn't there.** "This
  hardware is really ahead of the content" — only LG's own assets and high-res wallpapers exploit it;
  games, YouTube, and third-party apps still run at 1080p. He explicitly compares it to the Droid-DNA
  era when 1080p was new: **the content had to catch up, and it did.**
- [2014-05-27] **Cons are minor so far** — performance is snappy because most things render at 1080p
  anyway; the one real issue is a **thermal brightness throttle** (the display dims to ~85–90% when the
  phone heats up). Battery life TBD.
- [2014-05-27] **Read: potentially an influential Android device** like the Droid DNA — "if it goes
  well… you could expect the Galaxy S5 Prime or the HTC One M8 Prime to have QHD displays too." The full
  review process "begins now."
