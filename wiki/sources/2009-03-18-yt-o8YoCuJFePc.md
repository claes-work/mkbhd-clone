# iPhone 3Gs Software Overview (@mkbhd, 2009-03-18)

## Summary
A feature-by-feature breakdown (Marques, age 15) of **Apple's iPhone 3.0 software**, which
Apple previewed at a keynote **the day before (March 17, 2009)** as a free upgrade for iPhone
users. Reading from a Notepad list, he runs down the headline additions: **copy/cut/paste**
(long-awaited), **push notifications** (which he explicitly clarifies are *not* true background
multitasking — you get an alert but only one app runs at a time), **voice memos**, an expanded
**Spotlight** search (in-app search across mail/messages/music plus a system search screen),
a **landscape keyboard** across more apps, **MMS** and richer messaging, **stereo Bluetooth**,
and **in-game voice** chat (which he compares to Xbox). He notes there is **no camera video
recording** in this software update and speculates Apple may be holding features for new
hardware. He references his earlier **predictions video** and links the keynote. This is a
follow-up delivering the actual feature list after his earlier iPhone-3.0 wishlist.

> ⚠️ **Title vs. content:** the video is titled "iPhone 3Gs" but the content covers the
> **iPhone OS 3.0 *software*** previewed 2009-03-17; the iPhone **3GS hardware** was a separate,
> later announcement (June 2009). Treat this as a software-preview reaction, not a 3GS review.

## Key claims
- [2009-03-18] Apple's **iPhone 3.0 software** (previewed 2009-03-17) adds copy/cut/paste, push
  notifications, voice memos, expanded Spotlight search, a landscape keyboard, MMS, stereo
  Bluetooth and in-game voice — framed as "completing" the iPhone rather than adding
  breakthrough features.
- [2009-03-18] **Push notifications are not multitasking** — he corrects the common
  expectation: you get an alert, but background apps still don't run concurrently. (Early
  example of his careful spec/expectation-setting instinct.)
- [2009-03-18] **No video camera capability** in this update; he suspects Apple is reserving
  such features for unannounced hardware.
- [2009-03-18] Self-reported: he is **not an iPhone owner** — he demos voice memos on his own
  **LG Voyager** (Verizon), consistent with his origin-era wishlist video.

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
> "it's not really what we were expecting about like background tasks ... but it's not like more than one task running at once, but again um it's push notifications, so that's good"

> "A lot of the features it did not have um as a really great multimedia phone with the iPod and everything are um being put together into one great software package"
