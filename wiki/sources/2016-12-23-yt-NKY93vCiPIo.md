# Smartphone Awards 2016! (@mkbhd, 2016-12-23)

## Summary
The annual flagship Smartphone Awards for 2016 — Marques's solo one-stop buying guide handing out a first place, a runner-up, and honorable mentions in each category for the year's phones. He runs the categories in order: best big phone (Galaxy S7 Edge over LG V20, with the Note 7 as a doomed honorable mention), best compact (iPhone 7 over the smaller Google Pixel), best camera (Pixel/Pixel XL over iPhone 7 Plus, Galaxy S7 third), best budget (OnePlus 3T over ZTE Axon 7), best battery (Moto Z Play over Pixel XL, iPhone 7 Plus third), best build (Galaxy S7 Edge edging out the HTC 10, with the Xiaomi Mi Mix as craziest design), most improved (HTC 10), bust of the year (Note 7, plus dead Project Ara and the LG G5), and finally the overall MVP / phone of the year — the Google Pixel XL, with the iPhone 7 Plus as his second-pocket runner-up. The through-line is his recurring thesis that near-stock Android optimization is why he keeps gravitating back, and that 2016 rewarded every preference despite the Note 7 disaster.

## Key claims
- [2016-12-23] Best big phone: Samsung Galaxy S7 Edge (first), because a lot of Note features trickled into it — same camera as the Note, high-res big display, edge-display software, multi-window multitasking, big battery. Runner-up LG V20 (5.7-inch main screen plus a second screen up top, "a giant piece of metal"). Honorable mention Galaxy Note 7 — "one of the best phones of the year" but unrecommendable after the battery/fire recall.
- [2016-12-23] Best compact phone: iPhone 7 (first) — the best phone Apple makes, maybe the best raw performer of any phone on the table regardless of size, with fast storage, fast camera, optimized software. Runner-up Google Pixel (non-XL) — the smaller sibling packs the same Snapdragon 821, RAM, stock Android and Assistant as the XL. He says the flagship compact is now "a really rare breed."
- [2016-12-23] Best camera: Google Pixel / Pixel XL (first) for the best photos of any smartphone — Snapdragon 821 + HDR+ deliver great low-light, noise reduction, dynamic range, sharpness and saturated color, living up to the hype the Nexus line lacked. Runner-up iPhone 7 Plus (dual cameras, best video of any phone on the table, best app support for Snapchat/Periscope/Instagram stories). Third: Galaxy S7 (great selfies, fastest autofocus).
- [2016-12-23] Best budget phone: OnePlus 3T (first) at ~$429 — the top end of what he calls budget, but "100% a flagship" with Snapdragon 821, 6GB RAM, near-stock Android, and some of the fastest charging ever ("this is your Tesla Model S… fastest 0 to 60"). The older OnePlus 3 counts too. Runner-up ZTE Axon 7 for best-in-class front-facing stereo speakers plus near-stock Android. Honorable mentions: Alcatel Idol 4S and Huawei Mate 9.
- [2016-12-23] Recurring maxim: "good smartphones are getting cheap and cheap smartphones are getting good."
- [2016-12-23] Best battery life: Moto Z Play (first) — ~3,510 mAh, getting people around 6 hours of screen-on time; Motorola won this category last year and again this year. Runner-up Pixel XL (~3,450 mAh, 5+ hours screen-on via stock-Android optimization). Third iPhone 7 Plus (only ~2,900 mAh but fantastic standby thanks to iOS optimization — can end a day at 50%). Honorable mentions: OnePlus 3/3T (fastest recharge via Dash Charging) and Galaxy S7 Active (~4,000 mAh, durable).
- [2016-12-23] Best build (renamed from last year's "best design"): Galaxy S7 Edge (first, a near-tie) — waterproof, removable storage, metal bands, shiny; keeps a headphone jack, no USB-C yet, speakers not the best (likely due to waterproofing). Runner-up HTC 10, "built like an absolute tank," unibody metal with a big chamfer, industrial design that "does not mess around." Honorable mention / craziest design: Xiaomi Mi Mix (bezel-less full-display statement phone, also "maybe the slipperiest phone of all time").
- [2016-12-23] Most improved: HTC 10, back near its former heights after the HTC A9 was last year's bust — high-end specs return, great near-stock software that stays upgradeable.
- [2016-12-23] Bust of the year: Galaxy Note 7 ("literally busting open batteries all over the country") — was one of the best phones of the year before catching fire; replacements also exploded and it can't be bought anymore. Also flagged as busts: Project Ara (the hyped modular concept, now dead/killed) and the LG G5 (awkward pull-out-battery modular experiment that didn't land).
- [2016-12-23] MVP / phone of the year: Google Pixel XL — not the prettiest or best-feeling in hand, but software experience (stock Android optimization, battery, excellent camera, smooth fingerprint reader/Assistant/animations/gaming) more than makes up for it. Runner-up iPhone 7 Plus, his second-pocket phone for its performance and for photos/video/social sharing.
- [2016-12-23] He notes he hadn't yet done a full iPhone 7 review (only first impressions) and teases a possible "6 months later" review, and says the Note 7 likely would have been a close MVP runner-up if it hadn't started exploding.
