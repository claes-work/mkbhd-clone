# Top 5 Google I/O 2017 Recap! (@mkbhd, 2017-05-18)

## Summary
His Top-5 from Google I/O 2017 — a software/ML keynote (no new hardware). The clone-relevant throughline is
the **machine-learning-is-the-real-story** thesis in full: Google Lens as the culmination of years of
computer vision, and the Assistant escaping the Pixel to become platform-wide (even on iPhone).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2017-05-18] **#1 Google Lens = the culmination of ML/computer vision.** "So much of this conference is
  computer vision, machine learning, image recognition — and Lens is the culmination: point your camera at a
  flower and it names the species; at a restaurant (Maps knows where you are) and it overlays info; at a
  router's sticker and it logs you into the Wi-Fi instead of typing it. We've had this idea since Google
  Goggles in 2010 and Bixby — but the tech has gotten a lot better; it's almost a completely different
  product now." His ML-is-the-real-story read, with source-critical history.
- [2017-05-18] **#2 Google Assistant escapes the Pixel — now on iPhone.** "The Assistant is now on the
  iPhone — the exact same assistant, in the App Store — and the SDK is opening to third parties (order Panera
  from the Assistant). It went from Pixel-only to Android-only to much more widely available, and that's what
  I/O is all about." The Assistant's expansion is the platform story.
- [2017-05-18] **#3 Android O → beta** (points to his own Top-5 O video), highlighting **picture-in-picture**
  multitasking and **notification dots** (iOS-style badges you can long-press to preview). Notes O is "coming
  along nicely."
- [2017-05-18] **#4 Google Photos ML — "if it works that well."** "The improvements are all ML/AI —
  suggested sharing (recognizes faces, suggests sharing with them), shared libraries (auto-share every photo
  of your kids with your partner), and possibly the coolest: **removing an obstruction from the front of a
  photo** — the on-stage demo was incredible; I hope it works that well, but we have to see." His reserve-
  judgment-until-it-ships caution on a jaw-dropping demo.
- [2017-05-18] **#5 Google Home gains a display's worth of tricks.** Proactive assistance (lights up to
  tell you about traffic — "just ask what's up"), hands-free calling ("long overdue"), and — key — **it can
  now show visual answers on your TV/phone** ("before it made no sense to ask 'show me my calendar' with no
  display"). The ambient-computing thread advancing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "So much of the stuff at this conference is computer vision and machine learning and image recognition —

> "Google Assistant is now available on the iPhone… it went from being a Pixel-only thing to an Android-only
> thing to being much more widely available, and that's kind of what Google I/O is all about."
