# iPad 3 vs Transformer Prime! (@mkbhd, 2012-03-22)

## Summary
A cross-platform tablet head-to-head — "the kings of their kingdoms" — covering hardware, the
A5X-versus-Tegra-3 performance question, Wi-Fi, battery, and the buying decision. Its most
consequential content is methodological: he runs escalating real-world app tests instead of
benchmark scores and then states outright that **cross-platform benchmarks shouldn't be trusted**.
He also declares the iPad 3's retina display the deciding advantage — while remaining an
avowed Android user — and discloses that he'd previously bought and *returned* an iPad 2.

## Key claims
- [2012-03-22] **Distrust cross-platform benchmarks — stated as a rule**: "don't believe what you see in
  these silly cross-platform benchmarks, they're almost never completely accurate, so **pay attention to
  what you see in real life**."
- [2012-03-22] **The test design he uses instead**: "I started out with less intensive app equivalents on
  both devices and worked my way up in intensity" — matched-workload escalation across platforms rather
  than synthetic scores.
- [2012-03-22] **The honest null result**: "in real world use these two perform very very close to each
  other… it was never a substantial difference where I would sit back and say wow, this Tegra 3 just
  destroyed the iPad — or wow, this A5X just blows the Tegra 3 out of the water. I never ever found
  myself saying that." He reports a tie rather than manufacturing a winner.
- [2012-03-22] **Display as the deciding factor** — consistent with display primacy: "the most compelling
  reason to get the iPad 3 is easily, easily the retina display. I'd say it's even a reason to sell your
  iPad 1 or 2… this is absolutely the king of mobile displays and thumbs up to Apple, just very well
  done on this IPS display."
- [2012-03-22] **Dated personal-purchase disclosure**: "I had an iPad 2 for about two weeks a little
  while ago just testing it out, but it didn't really impress me and **I returned it** — but I would
  actually rather have an iPad 3 than an iPad 2, having used them both." Bought, tested, returned, and
  said so.
- [2012-03-22] **Why he personally leans Android, argued from his own stack**: heavy use of Google Talk,
  Google Voice, Google Calendar and YouTube — plus a specific feature case: "**widgets, dude.** You have
  no idea how awesome widgets on Android are until you don't have them anymore. I swear widgets are very
  very useful, pretty underrated right now."
- [2012-03-22] **The decision framed as taste, not superiority**: "it mostly depends on whether you like
  the dead easy simplicity of iOS 5.1 or the quirky but kind of fun and customizable sophistication of
  Android 4.0… in my opinion you can't go wrong with either of these. It's your cash — put your money
  where your heart is."
- [2012-03-22] **Shared flaw reported evenly**: both tablets have metal backs and "have had minor Wi-Fi
  issues… but overall I got decent results, so it's nothing really to worry about on either."
- [2012-03-22] Physics-not-defect framing on charging: the iPad's larger battery "means it's going to
  take longer to charge it fully — that's just simple physics."
- [2012-03-22] **A category dismissed with a joke**: "taking pictures on a tablet is forbidden among
  geeks… you look ridiculous, you might as well use your face" — while noting the front cameras matter
  for Hangouts and FaceTime.
