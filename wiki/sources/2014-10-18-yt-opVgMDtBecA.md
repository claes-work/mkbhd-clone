# Sharp Aquos Crystal - Bezelless Smartphone! (@mkbhd, 2014-10-18)

## Summary
A showcase of the **Sharp Aquos Crystal** — a $150 prepaid phone with a near-**bezelless** edge-to-edge
display (only a "Moto 360"-style chin at the bottom for the display driver). The whole video is an
expression of his **champion-the-risk-taker** value: "I like when manufacturers take risks like this…
even if this phone won't make a lot of money for Sharp, I'm glad they took that risk." He walks the
clever engineering (a screen-vibration earpiece replacing the missing top speaker) and the real trade-offs
(durability, un-caseable), and grades it honestly on its low-end tier. Also a Beats-EQ callback on the
"Harman Kardon audio" marketing.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2014-10-18] **The design is the entire reason to look.** A 5" 720p edge-to-edge display with glass
  that chamfers to the metal sides — "a pretty dope look"; the bottom chin (housing the display driver) is
  "the Moto 360 approach." "There really isn't anything else like this."
- [2014-10-18] **Champion-the-risk-taker (the core value).** "I like when manufacturers take risks like
  this — props to Sharp for thinking outside the box… I want people to see this, I want new eyeballs on
  this stuff, and hopefully other manufacturers try more outlandish/interesting things too. I'm glad it
  exists." Applied even to a phone he says isn't for him and won't make money.
- [2014-10-18] **A sacrifice turned into a feature.** No top earpiece (no room), so the **entire glass
  front vibrates** to conduct call audio anywhere you hold it to your head (like Google Glass's bone
  conduction) — "actually decent quality."
- [2014-10-18] **Honest trade-offs of a bezelless design:** you obstruct the screen holding it; **durability
  suffers** — "it's basically impossible to make a case that doesn't cover part of the screen," so you
  carry it naked and any non-bottom drop hits the screen directly.
- [2014-10-18] **Grade-on-the-tier.** It's a $150 low/mid prepaid phone (Cortex-A7, 1.5 GB RAM, 8 GB, a
  720p sub-300-ppi panel, occasional stutter, better-than-expected battery thanks to the 720p screen) —
  judged as such, not against flagships. The compact body is smaller than the 4.7" iPhone 6 despite the 5"
  screen.
- [2014-10-18] **Beats-EQ callback (consumer-education).** On the "Harman Kardon audio" marketing: "I'd
  put that in the same category as Beats Audio — I wouldn't really worry about that." Sharp's flashy
  charging/alarm animations are "Sharp saying hey, look how cool it is to not have a bezel."
