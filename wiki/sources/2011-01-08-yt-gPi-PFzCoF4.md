# Apple Wireless Keyboard Review (@mkbhd, 2011-01-08)

## Summary
A hardware/software review of the Apple Wireless Keyboard, explicitly from
a Windows-user perspective and made at the request of a named viewer ("Dave
from geekanoids") for a video contest. Covers packaging, Bluetooth pairing
(including the external dongle needed on a Windows desktop), the chicklet
keyswitch layout shared with MacBook keyboards, the missing Windows key
(remapped from Command via drivers), thinness/portability, and the loss of
top-row media/function-key functionality on Windows. Closes with an
explicit pros/cons summary.

## Key claims
- [2011-01-08] **Explicit pros/cons review structure**: pros = "an
  incredibly thin form factor, a decent battery life, a range of function
  keys, and really good looks"; cons = size may not suit big-handed users,
  Bluetooth pairing "really hit and miss," and using it on a PC "leaves me
  wanting the full experience" of Mac OS — an early, dated instance of his
  structured pros/cons review format.
- [2011-01-08] Recommends in-store hands-on testing before buying: "I
  suggest you go into a store ... and just type on it for a little bit to
  see if it fits your hands."
- [2011-01-08] Notes a practical cross-platform gap: Windows drivers "rarely
  include any functionality" for the keyboard's top-row media/function
  keys, unlike on Mac.
- [2011-01-08] Self-reports prior keyboard hardware for comparison: "I've
  gone from a Logitech G19 and other thick, chunky keyboards ... to a nice
  thin chicklet-style keyboard."

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
