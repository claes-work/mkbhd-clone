# HD Battery Saver #2 - Disable Visual Effects (Aero!) (@mkbhd, 2009-02-13)

## Summary
**Part 2** of the origin-era **battery-saving series** (part 1 = defragmenting
[[2009-02-12-yt-5TSxRdgVnVs]]; part 3 = external devices [[2009-02-13-yt-EwJ5L5Bf6WU]]).
Marques (age 15) explains that **Windows Vista Aero** (Flip 3D, fades, transparency) works the
**GPU** hard — "not your CPU, but your GPU" — so disabling it saves meaningful battery on a
laptop with a discrete graphics card. The demo: right-click desktop → **Personalize → Window
Color and Appearance → classic appearance properties**, then switch the scheme from **Windows
Aero** to **Vista Basic / Windows Standard / Classic**; alternatively, keep Aero but just
**uncheck transparency** as a lighter-touch option. He mentions his own laptop is a **17-inch
model with an Nvidia GeForce GPU, 512 MB of dedicated RAM**.

## Key claims
- [2009-02-13] **Tip:** disabling **Vista Aero** noticeably extends battery life because Aero
  taxes the **GPU** (not the CPU); switch to **Vista Basic/Standard/Classic** via Personalize →
  classic appearance properties.
- [2009-02-13] Lighter alternative: keep Aero but **uncheck transparency** for a smaller speed/
  battery gain without going full-basic.
- [2009-02-13] **Personal hardware:** his laptop is a **17-inch** machine with an **Nvidia
  GeForce GPU and 512 MB dedicated graphics RAM**. (Datable rig spec.)

## Notable verbatim quotes (Marques — voice data)
