# Top 5 Apple WWDC 2016 Recap! (@mkbhd, 2016-06-14)

## Summary
His Top-5 from WWDC 2016 — notable as "the first time I've gotten to go to one." A software-only keynote
(watchOS 3, tvOS, macOS Sierra, iOS 10, Siri). Two clone-relevant throughlines: the recurring **machine-
learning-is-the-real-story** observation (with Apple "playing catch-up to Google"), and the sharp closing
thesis that **Apple is "a hardware company first,"** which is *why* their hardware events matter more.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated)
- [2016-06-14] **First time attending WWDC.** "This is the first time I've gotten to go to one — a pretty
  quiet one this year, no new hardware." (Biography/access node — his industry-event footprint growing.)
- [2016-06-14] **watchOS 3 — "what I'm really most excited for is for it to be fast."** After the app-
  launch-speed demo: "if it's anything like this in real life, this'll be the biggest improvement to the
  Apple Watch in a while." Speed/responsiveness over features again — echoes his Apple-Watch-as-accessory
  ambivalence ("I don't know how many of you still use an Apple Watch").
- [2016-06-14] **Machine learning is the bigger trend — and Apple's playing catch-up.** iOS 10 Photos
  identifying subjects for search, Maps suggesting places — "that's something Google showed at I/O, and
  Google Photos has been doing on iPhones for a while. I like that they're both using the info they get
  from users to make it useful — but this whole event of Apple playing catch-up in software highlights a
  bigger trend." His recurring "the real story is ML" read.
- [2016-06-14] **THE THESIS — "Apple is a hardware company first."** "This whole event of Apple basically
  playing catch-up in software kind of highlights that they are a hardware company first — and that just
  makes their future events for the MacBook Pro or the new iPhone 7 that much more exciting to wait for."
  A durable framing of Apple's identity.
- [2016-06-14] **macOS Sierra continuity love + the 3D-Touch confession.** Favorite features: Universal
  Clipboard + Auto Unlock via Apple Watch. On iOS 10: candid admission "I kind of stopped using 3D Touch
  on my iPhone 6s… I guess with these new widgets we'll give it another shot." (Honest about a hyped
  feature he abandoned.) Praises Apple opening Siri/Maps to developers ("good job Apple").
- [2016-06-14] **Cross-creator citation.** Twice points viewers to "John from TLD" for the deeper iOS 10
  video — his habit of crediting/collaborating with other creators (attribution note: John Rettinger's
  content is not Marques's).
