# iPhone 12 Unboxing Experience + MagSafe Demo! (@mkbhd, 2020-10-20)

## Summary
First-look unboxing of the iPhone 12 (Product Red) and iPhone 12 Pro (Pacific Blue), centered on the two headline controversies of the launch: the new smaller box with **no charging brick and no headphones** inside, and **MagSafe**, the new magnetic charging/accessory standard. Marques walks the box contents (phone, USB-C-to-Lightning cable, paperwork, SIM tool, one sticker), then interrogates Apple's environmental defense of dropping the charger — noting the flaw that the bundled cable is USB-C while the wall brick "everybody already has" is USB-A, so most buyers still have to buy a USB-C brick "which Apple will conveniently sell you." He praises the flat-sided industrial design (iPhone 4/5 and iPad Pro inspiration), prefers the iPhone 12's matte aluminum sides over the Pro's fingerprint-prone glossy stainless steel, and complains this "Product Red" is the least-red Product Red he's seen (peach/coral). The MagSafe segment uses magnet-viewing paper to show the concentric-ring magnet array, demos the 15W puck charger, MagSafe wallet and case (each triggering its own on-screen animation via an NFC chip), and lands a skeptical verdict: the magnets are too weak for the accessories you'd most want to stay attached (a wallet pops off in your pocket; a car mount wouldn't survive a bump).

## Key claims
- [2020-10-20] The iPhone 12 box is roughly half its usual volume (half the height); the phone sits face-down filling the footprint; contents are just a phone and a USB-C-to-Lightning cable — no wall brick, no headphones. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] Apple's defense of removing the charger is environmental (fewer chargers shipped to landfills since people own bricks). Marques' counter: the brick "everybody already has" is USB-A, but the bundled cable is USB-C, so most iPhone 12 buyers must buy a USB-C brick anyway. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] He is a fan of the updated flat-sided industrial design — inspiration from the iPad Pro and iPhone 4/5 era, tight tolerances, antenna lines near the corners, flat screen, minimal (not zero) camera bump; the square sides make it more grippable though maybe less comfortable at first. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] The phone stands up on its own edge. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] iPhone 12 Pro (192g) has glossy stainless-steel sides; iPhone 12 (162g) has matte aluminum sides. Marques prefers the aluminum — stainless steel catches ugly fingerprints (same reason he doesn't want the stainless Apple Watch) and is heavier. The two phones are the exact same size (cases interchange). (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] Complaint: this Product Red is "the least red Product Red I've seen so far" — looks more peach / red-orange / coral from most angles. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] The extra cutout below the power button is for the 5G antennas; all four new iPhones have 5G. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] MagSafe = magnets embedded in the back around the wireless-charging coil for accessories. The MagSafe puck charges at 15W, is ~3ft USB-C, sits centered over the logo, and can spin freely. An NFC chip tells the phone which accessory attached and plays a matching animation. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] Verdict on MagSafe: "kind of cool but not quite as cool as I was hoping," mainly because the magnets are too loose — a wallet slides right off in a pocket, and a magnet-only car mount wouldn't survive a bump; third-party makers may use stronger magnets. (Marques)
- [2020-10-20] Fun fact / gentle troll: because it's still Qi, the MagSafe puck magnetizes to and wirelessly charges many Android phones — "every phone's had MagSafe, guys." (Marques)
