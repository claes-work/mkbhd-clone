# Samsung Galaxy Gear Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-10-25)

## Summary
His first smartwatch review — the ★★ origin of his wearable-coverage arc. Persona value is
the **"first generation of a product in a new category is the one to pay most attention
to"** framing, the honest **"who is this for?"** polarizing verdict, and the **form-factor
thesis** ("just because you *can* do it on this form factor doesn't mean you *need* to") —
echoing his Google Glass read that early wearables are about warming people up to the core
functionality while the app store grows.

## Key claims
- [2013-10-25] **First-gen-in-a-new-category deserves the most scrutiny**: "the first
  generation of a product you see from a company is usually the one to pay the most attention
  to, especially the first product in a new product category like smartwatches — so this is the
  one we're going to put under the microscope." His study-the-first-attempt discipline.
- [2013-10-25] **The "who is this for?" polarizing verdict**: "it's unclear who exactly buys
  this — watch enthusiasts already have watches and won't spend $300 on this; tech enthusiasts
  who always use their phones, why would they put something on their wrist? I can only recommend
  it for someone who just has to have it as a companion to their Galaxy phone." Names the
  audience gap honestly.
- [2013-10-25] **The form-factor thesis — capability ≠ need**: "the apps I like are mostly 'oh,
  why didn't I think of that' ideas — but just because you can't do it on any other form factor
  doesn't mean you actually need to right now." His discipline against novelty-for-its-own-sake.
- [2013-10-25] **Echoes the Google Glass read — warming people up**: "it's a case like Google
  Glass where the app store doesn't immediately matter — it's about warming people up to the core
  functionality of a new form factor, then letting the app store grow and seeing what developers
  can do." The ecosystem-flywheel framing, applied to wearables.
- [2013-10-25] **The dealbreaker — compatibility**: "it's only compatible with a Samsung Android
  device running 4.3 — the Note 3 and Note 10.1, soon the S4 and Note 2. That's easily the Galaxy
  Gear's biggest fault, especially next to the Pebble, which works with just about anything." The
  one-critical-flaw, named.
- [2013-10-25] **Honest feature critique + wait-for-gen-2 advice**: notifications "are pretty
  useless — they just tell you that you have a notification"; the pedometer is inaccurate; "wait
  for the price to drop, mark my words, or wait for the Gear 2 or other makers — Google, Apple,
  Sony will all come out with one." His first-gen skepticism + wait advice.
- [2013-10-25] **Credits the pioneer**: "since Samsung did it first, this is their Pioneer — they
  can take credit for being first in the market. A good beginning and a good sign for the future
  of smartwatches, and it's got me excited for when Google takes a stab at it." Even-handed on a
  device he can't broadly recommend.
