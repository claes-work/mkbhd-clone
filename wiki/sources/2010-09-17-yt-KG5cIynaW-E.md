# iKey Audio M808-V2 Studio Monitor Review (@mkbhd, 2010-09-17)

## Summary
Review of the iKey Audio (iAudio) M808-V2 professional studio monitors — announced
upfront as a first for the channel: **the first product ever to receive a 10/10** by
his review process. Covers the internal amplifier, 8" woofer + 1" tweeter driver
layout, flat frequency response, rear controls (volume, high-frequency trim, XLR/
balanced inputs), and setup guidance (45° angle toward the listener for the "sweet
spot"). Also directly addresses the red-and-black aesthetic thread.

## Key claims
- [2010-09-17] **Scoring milestone, explicitly named as a first**: "these speakers are
  the first ever to get a 10 out of 10 by my review process," restated at the close:
  "this is the first and the one and only product to actually receive a 10 out of 10
  by the MKBHD review standards" — the highest score recorded to date in the origin
  scoring ladder (previously topped by the 9.5/10 Wrapsol review). ★
- [2010-09-17] **Explicitly separates aesthetic preference from audio judgment**: "I
  know a lot of you are probably thinking I like these speakers because they're red
  and black, but let me tell you the audio quality that you get from the superior
  build quality of these monitors is absolutely amazing" — a self-aware acknowledgment
  of his own known red-and-black preference, used here to pre-empt the accusation that
  it's biasing the score. ★
- [2010-09-17] **States review methodology as objectively grounded**: claims to have
  found nothing wrong with the product ("I couldn't find anything wrong with the
  product itself"), and frames the one caveat (XLR cables not included at this premium
  price) as minor and cheaply solved (~$8/pair on Amazon) — the score isn't
  unconditional praise, it explicitly weighs a downside and still lands at a perfect
  10.
- [2010-09-17] **Practical setup guidance**: recommends angling both monitors ~45°
  toward the listener to hit "the sweet spot," rather than the parallel placement shown
  on camera.
