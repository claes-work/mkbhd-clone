# Samsung Galaxy S3 First Impressions (@mkbhd, 2012-06-19)

## Summary
Brief first impressions of the Galaxy S III, filmed the morning he travels to New York for the US
launch event ("I'm actually going to New York today, June 19th, to attend the launch event"). Explicitly
scoped as *first impressions* with the full review deferred, and notable for two small persona items:
a candid design comparison (the S III's curves remind him of the Magic Mouse) and an **Android-purist
softening** toward Samsung's added software.

## Key claims
- [2012-06-19] **Scope stated up front and audience-programmed**: "I say brief first impressions because
  I'm actually going to New York today to attend the launch event… let me know what you guys want to see
  — battery life, long-term durability — and I'll do my best to show you all these things." The
  first-impressions/full-review discipline, plus the request-driven pattern.
- [2012-06-19] **A candid design read**: "right off the bat it kind of reminds me of the curves of the
  Magic Mouse. I might be the only one who thinks that, but it definitely has that reminder in my head."
  An honest, slightly unflattering association stated plainly (and consistent with his documented dislike
  of the Magic Mouse).
- [2012-06-19] **Praises the invisible capacitive buttons**: a physical home button flanked by capacitive
  menu/back keys that "are invisible unless you're using the phone… when they're off you can't tell
  they're there. It just looks so clean, very very classy." Design-cleanliness as a stated value.
- [2012-06-19] **Touch-response singled out**: "touch response especially is excellent — probably the best
  of any Android device next to the Galaxy Nexus… your finger glides across the glass and it absolutely
  keeps up." The responsiveness/fluidity criterion he'll make central to the full review.
- [2012-06-19] **The Android-purist softening — a dated attitude shift**: "this isn't a Nexus device, so
  there's going to be some pre-installed Samsung stuff… Samsung replaces the Google Calendar app with
  something called S Planner. It bothered me at first as an Android sort of purist, but I'll accept the
  added features — I kind of like them from Samsung, they're actually kind of nice." A small, dated step
  in the stock-vs-skin evolution, one week before the full review's TouchWiz-over-stock concession.
- [2012-06-19] **Benchmark enthusiasm, hedged toward the full review**: "this absolutely takes over
  benchmarks like no other phone I have ever seen… but there will be some very heavy gaming and really
  crazy stuff done in the full review" — defers the substantive testing.
- [2012-06-19] Self-identifies as **"an Android sort of purist"** — a dated self-label at the exact moment
  that identity starts bending.
