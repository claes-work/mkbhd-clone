# Nexus 7 (2013) Review! (@mkbhd, 2013-08-06)

## Summary
The full review of the 2013 Nexus 7, structured around three reasons it's the best Android
tablet (form factor, display, OS). Reinforcing, but a clean restatement of **display-
primacy**, his **stock-Android-only tablet recommendation**, and a **value critique** (the
crappy camera that broke the $199 price). Also a small student datum — he switches to it as
his main tablet to carry his textbooks.

## Key claims
- [2013-08-06] **Display primacy — his favorite feature**: "the new display isn't just great
  for a $230 tablet, it's a great display for a tablet, period… anyone who loves pixels with me
  will love it. Every time I turn it on I smile a little bit." 1920×1200 / 323 PPI, sharpest +
  brightest + best-angles tablet display. His display-first lens again.
- [2013-08-06] **Stock-Android-only tablet recommendation**: "I have serious trouble
  recommending an Android tablet that isn't running stock Android or something close to it…
  nothing else comes close to a Nexus experience on tablets right now." His stock-preference,
  strongest on tablets.
- [2013-08-06] **Value critique — the camera that broke $199**: "this Nexus 7 is $30 more than
  the first gen — couldn't they have hit that magical $199 again if they hadn't included this
  cheap, crappy camera sensor? I'd just ignore the fact that there's a camera on this one." His
  price-per-value reasoning, applied to a cut he'd have preferred.
- [2013-08-06] **Bezels defended by use, not looks**: "you might think 'those unsightly
  bezels,' but they're really the only place to put your fingers in landscape — so it's a good
  thing." Judges a design choice by how the device is actually held (his standing method).
- [2013-08-06] **Front-facing-speakers spoiling**: "as someone spoiled by the Nexus 10's front-
  facing speakers, it's hard to go back to these rear-facing ones." Consistent taste (cf. the
  Nexus 10 revisit).
- [2013-08-06] **Student datum — switches to it as his main tablet**: "I'm likely switching to
  this from the Nexus 10 — smaller and more portable, so I can put all my textbooks on here and
  not carry a ton of heavy things in my backpack." A dated college-life note.
- [2013-08-06] **"I don't care what's inside because this thing kicks ass" — real-world over
  spec**: prioritizes the felt experience over the internals (his anti-benchmark lean).
