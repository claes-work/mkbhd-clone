# Verizon Galaxy Nexus First Impressions (@mkbhd, 2011-12-15)

## Summary
First-impressions coverage published the same day as the unboxing, opening with a direct
answer to a criticism circulating at the time — that the Galaxy Nexus wasn't the
best-specced phone available, so why buy it. His answer is the **reference-device argument**:
it's the only stock Android 4.0 device, "the pure Google experience." He then hands out his
first big superlative (best mobile display he's ever used), calls the camera sensor
"pitiful," declines to adopt Face Unlock, and — importantly — **draws an explicit line around
what a first-impressions video is allowed to conclude**, deferring battery life and the full
verdict to a review "a couple weeks" out.

## Key claims
- [2011-12-15] **Display superlative, dated**: "this is the best mobile display I've ever used.
  This is hands down the best mobile phone display." 4.65" 1280×720 OLED; blacks so deep you
  "can hardly tell them apart from the bezel."
- [2011-12-15] **Reference-device argument as the buying rationale**: "while it might be
  outclassed just ever so slightly by a few other handsets out there… this is a Nexus, this is
  the only stock Android 4.0 device, this is the pure Google experience." Software purity and
  reference status are weighted above raw specs.
- [2011-12-15] **Camera split verdict — software good, sensor bad**: "there is something
  awesomely awesome and something pitiful about this phone's camera." Instant shutter and the
  camera software are praised; "something that's kind of pitiful is the sensor — it's only a
  5 megapixel sensor… the camera photos don't look that great." Blunt criticism of a device he
  just bought and is visibly excited about.
- [2011-12-15] **Face Unlock rejected in practice**: "while it's nice that it works, I probably
  won't be using this, as the swipe gesture to unlock the screen is just much more fast." A
  reversal of tone from the anticipation video six weeks earlier, where the feature ranked #3 —
  worth noting as an early instance of hands-on use overriding anticipation.
- [2011-12-15] **Speed claim, with the bias named**: "the Galaxy Nexus is fast, and I'm not just
  saying that because I came from a 2-year-old OG Droid, or because I want you guys to believe
  it's fast." He surfaces the exact baseline problem his own methodology video warned about
  ([[2011-09-30-yt-dTPZ2tuyWFw]]) and addresses it head-on — "I have tested other phones
  before."
- [2011-12-15] **Review discipline, stated explicitly**: "I didn't really cover everything
  realistic, like battery life and — oh, I don't know — calling people on this phone… I'll cover
  a lot of these things in my full review in a couple weeks when I really get to know
  everything about this phone." The scope limit of a first-impressions piece is named on
  camera.
- [2011-12-15] Media-consumption emphasis: turning to landscape for a 720p YouTube video is
  described as the standout experience — "my jaw dropped."
