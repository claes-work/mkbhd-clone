# Panels (wallpaper app)

A mobile wallpaper app curating work from artists, launched by Marques Brownlee in 2024 and
shut down in 2025. Notable less for the product than for the **backlash** it triggered — a
rare public misstep for a creator whose brand is built on consumer trust.

## Launch [press-corroborated]

- **Launched September 2024** (iOS + Android). Curated artist-made wallpapers.
- **Pricing at launch:** **$11.99/month or $49.99/year.**
- **Free tier:** users had to watch **two unskippable ads** to download a single lower-
  resolution file.
- **Revenue split:** Brownlee said he took 50% and gave **50% to the artists.**
  [sources: benzinga.com/24/09/41006748; decrypt.co/251013; androidkenya.com/2024/09/panels-mkbhd-app]

## The controversy [press-corroborated]

- Immediate fan backlash over: the **$50/yr price** for wallpapers, the **ad-gated free
  tier**, and — most damaging — the app's **aggressive data collection** (location, contacts,
  IP address, device model, cookies) for what amounts to serving JPEGs.
- **13,000+ critical replies within ~20 hours** under his announcement.
- **Response:** Brownlee updated the app to address privacy concerns and **cut the price to
  ~$2/month**, but momentum never recovered.
  [sources: decrypt.co/251013; benzinga.com/24/09/41006748]

## Shutdown [press-corroborated]

- **Announced December 1, 2025**; app stops working **December 31, 2025.**
- **Reasons given:** the **development team's makeup changed** and no right-fit replacements
  were found; the business model was **unsustainable**; hard to build a paying market for
  something people won't normally pay for.
- **Annual-subscriber refunds** begin Dec 31, 2025; the **code to be open-sourced under the
  Apache 2.0 license in January 2026.**
  [sources: techcrunch.com/2025/12/01/mkbhds-wallpaper-app-panels-is-shutting-down;
  9to5google.com/2025/12/01/mkbhd-panels-app-shutting-down; macrumors.com/2025/12/01/mkbhd-wallpaper-app-shutdown]

## Significance for the persona

- A case where Brownlee — normally the consumer's advocate against overpricing and dark
  patterns — was on the receiving end of exactly that critique. Useful for understanding how
  he responds to criticism of his own products (he adjusted pricing publicly, then wound the
  product down transparently with refunds and open-sourcing).
