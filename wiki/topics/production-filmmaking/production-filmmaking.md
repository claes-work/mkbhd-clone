# Production & Filmmaking — hub

Cameras, color science, editing, and how MKBHD videos are actually made; the Kearny [[wiki/entities/the-studio|studio]] build and gear philosophy.

> Built by synthesis pass 1 (2026-07-19) from the 2013–2020 @mkbhd corpus.

## Gear & craft philosophy

- **"A camera is a small computer with a sensor attached."** How he describes the RED Weapon Dragon (unit #41) he shoots on. Frames cinema cameras as computing, not just optics. [2015-10-09] ([[2015-10-09-yt-cqybfPNbWBA]])
- **Shoot RED / RAW for craft, not delivered quality.** Openly admits RED is "definitely overkill for YouTube" — the files look far better on his computer than after YouTube's compression — but shoots it because he enjoys it and "learns from the RAW workflow every day." Craft/learning over spec-delivery. [2015-10-09] ([[2015-10-09-yt-cqybfPNbWBA]])
- **"Shoot high-resolution now / cameras are computers attached to sensors."** Justifies 8K cinema-gear investment (RED Epic-W) as future-proofing the archive. [2016-10-11] ([[2016-10-11-yt-J8esUVOF-G4]])
- **One-man-crew nimbleness.** Runs a deliberately minimal rig (battery, monitor, top handle) where most RED setups are heavily rigged for multi-person crews; modularity is RED's key draw for him. [2015-10-09] ([[2015-10-09-yt-cqybfPNbWBA]])
- **Signature look — "Team Crispy" / red-and-black studio aesthetic.** The RED Weapon/Dragon + Sigma 18-35 f/1.8 as his early signature glass; the crimson-and-black studio identity established early. [2015-10-09] ([[2015-10-09-yt-cqybfPNbWBA]]) · [2015-10-09 Dope Tech #1 / Awards era]
- **Wants motion-control in his own studio.** Covering cinema camera-robots, he says he wants that capability in-house — production-craft as a core value, not just review subject matter. [2018-04-03] ([[2018-04-03-yt-UIwdCN4dV6w]])

## Photography fundamentals

- **Get it right in-camera; "you can't change that later."** From shooting instant film (Polaroid SX-70): "you gotta frame it right… you gotta nail focus… can't change that later." Instant photography is paradoxically "a lot more work than what we're used to" — convenience hides real craft. [2019-12-02] ([[2019-12-02-yt-6oHrNuXPVck]])
- **Smartphone camera vs. DSLR.** "Shot on X" ads are technically true but rig-heavy; a phone never beats a DSLR on outright quality but always wins on being *with* you — enough to just start shooting. [2017-06-28] ([[2017-06-28-yt-OkPter7MC1I]])
- **Frame rate is king, but resolution buys immersion.** A consistent stance on the refresh-vs-resolution trade-off across his gaming/display coverage; 8K "makes you notice every intentional detail." [2020-09-23] ([[2020-09-23-yt-kFz9afj8lu0]])

## Platform-craft observations
- **Generational re-encode loss is real.** The "re-upload a YouTube video 1000 times" experiment demonstrates compounding compression artifacts — 720p lock-in, magenta drift, audio drift. A production-literacy demonstration. [2019-10-11] ([[2019-10-11-yt-JR4KHfqw-oE]])
- **Three levels of production.** Covering the XR/virtual-stage setup behind the Nothing Phone 2 Dope Tech, he lays out a tiered model of how much production a shot needs — matching the tool to the creative idea, not maxing gear for its own sake. [2023-07-03] ([[2023-07-03-yt-RM7SobH8ZO0]])
- **"The tool should enable the creative idea" (craft confession).** In Dope Tech Revisited he admits he shot manual-focus on the RED because its autofocus was weak — a candid "the gear serves the idea, not the reverse" statement, and the origin of his "Porsche 911 of ___" praise for iteratively-perfected tools. [2025-11-21] ([[2025-11-21-yt-RvP-uVNwnXo]])

## Origin toolchain (2009, age 15)
His earliest production stack, documented across the January-2009 tutorials: an **HP Pavilion dv7t** laptop (3 GB RAM, ExpressCard slot) screen-recorded with the **CamStudio** lossless codec, **RocketDock** launcher, on **Windows Vista**; later CamStudio + **DivX 6.8.7**, 30 fps, 720 HD @ ~3,000 kbps, 640×360 — with an early quality-obsession ("Windows Movie Maker is not good"). The review format and the "leave a comment and subscribe" CTA are already present in **video #1** (2009-01-01 HP dv7t remote overview, his self-described "first video"). [2009-01-01] ([[2009-01-01-yt-9gk_rl3y_SU]]) · [2009-01-30] ([[2009-01-30-yt-0hNd56lIAxY]])

### Origin deepening — the Feb-2009 stack, the HD obsession, and "started with nothing" (added synthesis pass 3, 2026-07-20)
- **The production stack, self-documented.** The "CamStudio review" is a primary-source record of the exact origin-era pipeline: **CamStudio + DivX 6.8.5** at a 720p profile / **quality 100**, recording **640×360** with auto-pan — reviewed transparently as "the tool I'm using right now." An early instance of showing his own workflow, not hiding it. [2009-02-11] ([[2009-02-11-yt-PkRXVGJ0ods]])
- **The HD obsession that named the channel.** "How to Upload HD Youtube Videos" is the earliest explicit statement of the HD/production-quality fixation behind "MKBHD" (the CamStudio → Movie Maker → 720p pipeline). It culminates in the **first "HD Test"** milestone — "I'll upload it and see if I get that watching-HD link that we all dream of" — a genuine origin-of-the-craft moment. [2009-01-31] ([[2009-01-31-yt-4vwd_pQHUBE]]) · [2009-02-07] ([[2009-02-07-yt-fa1Lr-W2aBU]])
- **"Started with nothing" gear-origin, on record.** States he has **no external mic** and records with the **built-in webcam mic** (+10 dB software boost); the 2009 rig is a **4 GB RAM, 7,200 RPM HDD** machine with an **Nvidia GeForce 9200M (512 MB)** GPU. The strongest primary source yet for the humble-origins narrative. [2009-02-03] ([[2009-02-03-yt-sTt-9R6FQ6E]])
- **Viewer-legibility as a craft instinct.** Deliberately **zooms / magnifies** his screencasts so viewers can follow his clicks — early evidence of making the footage legible for the audience rather than just recording it. [2009-02-03] ([[2009-02-03-yt-8OUGVgQLxNU]])

### Origin encoding standards & the "redo it better" ethos (Feb–Apr 2009) — added synthesis pass 4 (2026-07-20)
The Feb–Apr 2009 tutorials pin down not just the *toolchain* (pass 3) but Marques's earliest explicit **production standards** and the **quality-obsession loop** — the documented root of his later production rigor.
- **The earliest articulated encoding standard.** "What does YouTube Want?" is his first on-record **spec sheet for himself**: target **1280×720**, prefer the **h.264** codec, **24–25 fps**, **two-channel / two-mic audio**, and — crucially — **test the upload fully before publishing** so he never has to re-upload. The no-re-upload discipline is the seed of his later "get it right the first time" perfectionism. [2009-02-21] ([[2009-02-21-yt-ZPv25BKcQtU]])
- **The most detailed origin encoding pipeline.** The Camtasia Studio 6 "mega-widescreen" settings video documents the full export preset end-to-end: a **.mov / QuickTime** container, **H.264 @ 30 fps, Best-Quality multi-pass**, audio **MPEG-4 48 kHz / 16-bit / stereo recorded with two mics**, output **1280×720** (the max YouTube played back in 2009). The most complete primary source for the origin-era production stack. [2009-02-28] ([[2009-02-28-yt-8imsZ-ADhMM]])
- **"Redo it better" — the quality loop, dated.** Update 1.1 is the earliest on-record "upgrade my whole pipeline" moment: he moves off Windows Movie Maker + CamStudio onto **Camtasia Studio 6** to fix his audio, and states a plan to **re-record his older tutorials to be more professional** (while keeping the originals for their view counts). The origin of the reshoot/quality-obsession ethos. [2009-02-23] ([[2009-02-23-yt-SsgBuvCKJE4]])
- **Origin of the signature zoom/tilt push-in.** The "Tilt in Camtasia Studio 6" tutorial is the earliest dated construction of his **zoom / tilt push-in editing move** — the emphasis technique that becomes a lasting part of his edit grammar. [2009-03-03] ([[2009-03-03-yt-1E_1KfMIexQ]])
- **The dual-production method, stated outright.** Filming his HP Media-Center remote on a camera, he articulates the **two-track origin workflow** he still runs — "I do hardware things with this camera, I do software things with my screen recording device" — under the era's hard constraint of **no tripod**. The documented screencast→camera-filmed-hardware pivot. [2009-03-30] ([[2009-03-30-yt-WaqNXARd3Fo]])
- **Origin audio-post pipeline.** His "Remove Background Noise" tutorial documents the origin audio-cleanup chain — **Audacity Get-Noise-Profile → Camtasia 6 Audio Enhancements** — applied to his *own* uploads, with the intent to "focus exactly on my audio." Audio quality treated as a first-order concern from the start. [2009-04-02] ([[2009-04-02-yt-CwapC2jZ_JI]])
