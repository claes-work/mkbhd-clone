# Tech Reviews — hub

MKBHD's core format: review methodology, scoring, the "is it worth it" verdict, and what makes a review trustworthy. The through-line of the whole channel.

> Built by synthesis pass 1 (2026-07-19) from the 2013–2020 @mkbhd corpus + @Waveform. Frameworks below are dated and cited to their `wiki/sources/` page; each recurs across multiple reviews.

## Review frameworks & methodology

- **Two-axis camera evaluation (quality vs. control).** Scores a camera on two independent 1–10 scales — image *quality* and shooting *control/ease* — with the sweet spot being high on both rather than maxing one. He faults a camera that hits 10/10 quality but is hard for a stranger to use (Lumia 1020) versus a phone that balances ~8/8. [2013-10-12] ([[2013-10-12-yt-uBEaG6n0XDs]])
- **Price-to-value lens — grade on the right curve.** Refuses to grade a cheaper phone on a flagship curve: "B+ camera, A+ for the price" (OnePlus 6). Repeats it on the iPhone 11 — the best camera in a $700 phone, "the new iPhone that most people should buy." Value/pricing is a first-class review axis, not an afterthought. [2018-05-25] ([[2018-05-25-yt-0PrUr3bQdwM]]) · [2019-09-30] ([[2019-09-30-yt-OoY7zp8GkLI]])
- **"Bust" = expectations minus reality.** The Bust-of-the-Year / disappointment judgment is measured by the delta between what a product could have been and what shipped — not raw badness. "The sentiment about a phone… often takes into account how good that phone could have been… minus reality." (Pixel 4, 2019 — "explicitly not the worst phone on the desk.") [2019-12-24] ([[2019-12-24-yt-4OnwUz-I2GI]])
- **"Why now?" lens.** When a company finally ships an obvious/overdue feature, ask what it waited to get *right* rather than just dunking on the lateness. Applied to iOS 14 borrowing from Android. [2020-06-23] ([[2020-06-23-yt-ZLyDvABxGF0]])
- **The Apple-review verdict shape.** A recurring closing pattern: premium execution + a few glaring quirks + "if you're in Apple's ecosystem" → recommendation. "Sounds kind of like a lot of other Apple products, right?" (AirPods Max). [2020-12-16] ([[2020-12-16-yt-Gvvo6vUpJRc]])
- **Reference-vs-luxury taxonomy (audio).** Splits high-end headphones into *reference/production* (lightweight, flat, geared for creating) vs. *luxury listening* (great-sounding, wireless, ANC, geared for consuming); places a product in one bucket before judging it. Self-positions as "not an audiophile, but I know what I like." [2020-12-16] ([[2020-12-16-yt-Gvvo6vUpJRc]])
- **Dailies every phone he fully reviews.** Won't publish a full review of a device he hasn't lived with (the reason he had *not* done a full BlackBerry review despite the Most-Improved award). Reviews from longer-than-typical use windows. [2014-12-08] ([[2014-12-08-yt-f6pPIG3EvAs]]) · [2013-10-12] ([[2013-10-12-yt-uBEaG6n0XDs]])
- **Defers durability to specialists.** On scratch/drop testing he won't destroy his own unit — "I'm just going to live my life" — and defers the scratch/shatter verdict to JerryRigEverything's Zack. Knows his lane and cites others'. [2020-10-25] ([[2020-10-25-yt-X1b3C2081-Q]])
- **Defers benchmarks too.** In a gameplay/experience piece he explicitly says "there are no benchmarks in this video," deferring synthetic numbers to Linus Tech Tips / Hardware Canucks — experience-first, spec-sheet-second. [2020-09-23] ([[2020-09-23-yt-kFz9afj8lu0]])
- **The review as an act of compression.** In "What I REALLY Think of the iPhone" he frames reviewing as balancing / compressing a device that does "nine jobs in one" into one verdict — you can't judge a phone as any single tool because it's a point-and-shoot *and* a manual camera *and* a computer at once. Anti-hype coda: "glass is glass," don't over-index on any one hyped spec. [2021-03-05] ([[2021-03-05-yt-V7J9aMy_CFk]])
- **The golden rule — never buy for a promised future update.** The single most-restated review commandment: judge the product in front of you, not the roadmap. Stated verbatim on the iPhone 16 ("never buy a product based on the future promise of updates") and hardened into the Humane AI Pin verdict. [2024-09-10] ([[2024-09-10-yt-9lx11dy9J30]]) · [2024-04-14] ([[2024-04-14-yt-TitZV6k8zfA]])
- **"The Great Separation."** Hardware and the AI software that's supposed to justify it now ship on different timelines — so a phone must be reviewed on what it does today, not the AI features "coming later this year." A 2024 sharpening of the golden rule. [2024-09-10] ([[2024-09-10-yt-9lx11dy9J30]])
- **Judge new form factors against the phone ("smartphones are OP").** Any new personal-computing form factor (AI pin, headset) is graded against what a phone already does better, faster, and easier. The bar isn't "is it cool," it's "does it beat the thing already in your pocket." [2024-04-14] ([[2024-04-14-yt-TitZV6k8zfA]]) · [2024-01-31] ([[2024-01-31-yt-dtp6b76pMak]])
- **"Victim of its future ambition" + respect the attempt.** A first-gen product that deliberately hamstrings the present to protect a future vision (Humane refusing phone-pairing; Vision Pro) gets dinged for the present compromise — but he still respects the swing, panning the product without mocking the attempt. Note the exact, frequently-misquoted verdict: "the new worst product I think I've ever reviewed, **in its current state**" — qualified, not absolute. [2024-04-14] ([[2024-04-14-yt-TitZV6k8zfA]])
- **"Series zero" / "compared to what?" (first-gen pricing).** First-gen products are defined by early adopters + developers, not mainstream buyers; and a shocking price only means something "compared to what?" — a Vision Pro next to a loaded workstation setup reads differently than in a vacuum. [2023-06-06] ([[2023-06-06-yt-OFvXuyITwBI]])
- **The "two buckets" Apple lens.** Any new Apple feature falls into one of two buckets — *seen-elsewhere-first* (Android/others did it) or *ecosystem-locked* (only useful if you're already all-in). A fast triage for iPhone launches. [2023-09-13] ([[2023-09-13-yt-enR58PYHaWw]])
- **Iterative refinement compounds ("the Porsche 911 of X").** His highest structural praise: a product line that changes little each year but compounds into greatness over a decade. Applied to the iPhone 15 Pro and generalized as a reusable "Porsche 911 of ___" label for iteratively-perfected products. Paired with "more power only matters if you actually use it." [2023-09-28] ([[2023-09-28-yt-cBpGq-vDr2Y]]) · [2025-11-21] ([[2025-11-21-yt-RvP-uVNwnXo]])
- **The "was it worth it?" long-term review.** A distinct format: revisit a big-ticket, bleeding-edge purchase after living with it, and answer the value question with hindsight instead of launch-day hype (Tesla Solar Roof). [2023-07-29] ([[2023-07-29-yt-UJeSWbR6W04]])
- **Diminishing-returns value framework.** At the top of a category the extra money buys ever-smaller gains — a recurring lens for telling "best" apart from "worth it," central to the Smartphone Awards MVP logic. [2024-12-19] ([[2024-12-19-yt-E76CUtSHMrU]])
- **Utility-vs-aesthetics camera framework.** Judges a phone camera on two separate questions — is it a useful/accurate tool, and is it aesthetically pleasing — and names which one a given phone optimizes rather than collapsing them into one "best camera" score. [2024-01-26] ([[2024-01-26-yt-XaqOejIaFgM]])
- **"It's a VR headset; spatial computing is semantics."** Cuts through marketing category-names to the honest description, then judges by the honest one — and reframes the real question as "what's the killer app?", which for Apple is "the ecosystem." [2024-01-31] ([[2024-01-31-yt-dtp6b76pMak]])

## Origin of the review methodology (Feb 2009, age 15) — added synthesis pass 3 (2026-07-20)
The whole review apparatus is visibly present in embryo in the 2009 origin-era screencasts (all solo, persona-eligible):
- **Benchmark-led, side-by-side comparison — the clearest origin marker.** The **SRWare Iron first-look** already runs his mature method in miniature: a standardized **Acid3 benchmark** plus a **resource/RAM comparison in Task Manager** across browsers, a **hedged verdict**, and an explicit **audience-request → review loop** (the video exists because a commenter asked). [2009-02-09] ([[2009-02-09-yt-oNMFjL1M1rA]])
- **Standardized-test instinct.** The Safari 4 WebKit look-in leans on the **Acid3 score (75/100 stock vs 100/100 WebKit nightly)** to make a comparative claim — the earliest precursor to his later spec/benchmark-led evaluation. [2009-02-04] ([[2009-02-04-yt-NrOWLTqRLc8]])
- **Earliest explicit "review" framing.** "I'll start reviewing freeware" (Extended Task Manager); "Google Chrome, which I'll be doing a review on shortly" (Firefox Superbar) — on-record origin statements of the reviewing instinct and the review-format itself. [2009-02-01] ([[2009-02-01-yt-s9lU1eAXVn4]]) · [2009-02-08] ([[2009-02-08-yt-S1ThX4gDPrQ]])
- **The hardware-review + unboxing formats begin.** The **NZXT Cryo LX** is his **self-declared first hardware unboxing**; the **Microsoft Wireless Mouse 6000** (~304K views) shows the early **two-part hardware + software review** structure. [2009-01-31] ([[2009-01-31-yt-SWSqBFGT50A]]) · [2009-02-01] ([[2009-02-01-yt-RTxrS1lLwjY]])
- **Experience over the raw number (early).** In the boot-speed tutorial he keeps the **slower GUI boot** because he "actually like[s]" it — choosing the nicer experience over the faster benchmark, a seed of his later experience-first, spec-second stance. [2009-02-04] ([[2009-02-04-yt-OBNkCLRGx2k]])

**Origin deepening (Feb 2009) — added synthesis pass 4 (2026-07-20):**
- **The "is it worth it?" verdict + source-citing discipline, on day one.** "Could Windows 7 Be a $50 Upgrade?" is the earliest **value judgment** in the corpus — he weighs price against worth ("wasn't worth as much money as you may have spent") and, tellingly, **attributes the $50 rumor to CNET rather than asserting it himself**. The origin of both the "worth it" instinct and his cite-your-source habit. [2009-02-13] ([[2009-02-13-yt-or8t_Zi7S38]])
- **The earliest structured buying-guide.** The "HD Laptop Buying Guide" runs a **component-by-component trade-off walkthrough** (CPU / RAM / HDD / GPU / screen / battery) with a "what's worth paying for" framing — the earliest review-methodology artifact in the buyer's-guide format that later becomes the Smartphone Awards. [2009-02-19] ([[2009-02-19-yt-yDie-JzVlKM]])
