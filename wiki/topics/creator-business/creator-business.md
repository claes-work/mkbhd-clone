# Creator Business — hub

YouTube as a business: the team, sponsorship ethics, merch, and ventures like the [[wiki/entities/panels|Panels]] app. How a creator operation runs.

> Built by synthesis pass 1 (2026-07-19) from the 2013–2020 @mkbhd corpus + @Waveform.

## Frameworks

- **Margins + perceived value.** Price = manufacturing cost plus a gap that funds R&D, packaging, and advertising. "Perceived value is really the name of the game… the more people think your product is worth, the more they're willing to pay." Paying for brand/image is a *legitimate* consumer choice — "not necessarily a bad thing" — the buyer's job is to decide whether they're paying for performance or for the name. (Beats by Dre: 64% of the >$100 headphone market on marketing, not sound.) [2014-08-31] ([[2014-08-31-yt-ZsxQxS0AdBY]])
- **Dope Tech series thesis.** A recurring showcase format where "the only qualification is that I think it's dope — anything with an on button is game." Credited to TLD's "Coolest Tech on the Planet" as inspiration. His vehicle for aspirational/novelty hardware; later widened to "tech as moving art" (kinetic tables, rehoused lenses). [2015-10-09] ([[2015-10-09-yt-cqybfPNbWBA]]) · [2019-12-21] ([[2019-12-21-yt-07_IZIoN60Q]])
- **Novelty can be worth it on its own axis.** "Don't buy these for the build quality… buy these for the immense flex" (3X Pods) — explicit that a gadget can be worth it purely for novelty/flex, stated as such. [2020-07-18] ([[2020-07-18-yt-nF4hg6g7SwE]])

## Sponsorship ethics
- **Discloses paid placements up-front.** The Hachi projector segment is stated as a paid placement before the pitch — a concrete data point for the sponsorship-ethics thread. Vendor ties (dbrand, ColorWare) surface early and openly. [2020-08-17] ([[2020-08-17-yt-5NNO5Kb-PZo]]) · [2015-11-03] ([[2015-11-03-yt-YuHD9h5gTS4]])
- **Self-sponsorship is a creator-business lever.** The "$40,000 to unbox a sealed original iPhone" video is funded from his own MKBHD/shop budget rather than a third-party ad — a spend-to-make-content decision, treated as a business move, not an ad read. The loss-leader logic behind it he credits to Barbara Corcoran (concept hers; the application his). [2023-04-20] ([[2023-04-20-yt--BwUyTrU9fo]]) · [2023-05-15] ([[2023-05-15-yt-tFdnCzfJPJ0]])

## Platform / creator-economy positions (from @Waveform)
- **Platforms must auto-surface AI/provenance labels** and **de-monetize stolen short-form** — stances on how platforms should police AI content and reposted clips. Attribution-to-confirm against his solo channel before hardening. [2026-06-24] ([[2026-06-24-yt-1YO2_PLGP-M]]) · [2026-07-17] ([[2026-07-17-yt-63m_fsOE-eQ]])
