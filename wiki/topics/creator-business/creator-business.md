# Creator Business — hub

YouTube as a business: the team, sponsorship ethics, merch, and ventures like the [[wiki/entities/panels|Panels]] app. How a creator operation runs.

> Built by synthesis pass 1 (2026-07-19) from the 2013–2020 @mkbhd corpus + @Waveform.

## Frameworks

- **Margins + perceived value.** Price = manufacturing cost plus a gap that funds R&D, packaging, and advertising. "Perceived value is really the name of the game… the more people think your product is worth, the more they're willing to pay." Paying for brand/image is a *legitimate* consumer choice — "not necessarily a bad thing" — the buyer's job is to decide whether they're paying for performance or for the name. (Beats by Dre: 64% of the >$100 headphone market on marketing, not sound.) [2014-08-31] ([[2014-08-31-yt-ZsxQxS0AdBY]])
- **Dope Tech series thesis.** A recurring showcase format where "the only qualification is that I think it's dope — anything with an on button is game." Credited to TLD's "Coolest Tech on the Planet" as inspiration. His vehicle for aspirational/novelty hardware; later widened to "tech as moving art" (kinetic tables, rehoused lenses). [2015-10-09] ([[2015-10-09-yt-cqybfPNbWBA]]) · [2019-12-21] ([[2019-12-21-yt-07_IZIoN60Q]])
- **Novelty can be worth it on its own axis.** "Don't buy these for the build quality… buy these for the immense flex" (3X Pods) — explicit that a gadget can be worth it purely for novelty/flex, stated as such. [2020-07-18] ([[2020-07-18-yt-nF4hg6g7SwE]])

## Sponsorship ethics
- **Discloses paid placements up-front.** The Hachi projector segment is stated as a paid placement before the pitch — a concrete data point for the sponsorship-ethics thread. Vendor ties (dbrand, ColorWare) surface early and openly. [2020-08-17] ([[2020-08-17-yt-5NNO5Kb-PZo]]) · [2015-11-03] ([[2015-11-03-yt-YuHD9h5gTS4]])
- **Self-sponsorship is a creator-business lever.** The "$40,000 to unbox a sealed original iPhone" video is funded from his own MKBHD/shop budget rather than a third-party ad — a spend-to-make-content decision, treated as a business move, not an ad read. The loss-leader logic behind it he credits to Barbara Corcoran (concept hers; the application his). [2023-04-20] ([[2023-04-20-yt--BwUyTrU9fo]]) · [2023-05-15] ([[2023-05-15-yt-tFdnCzfJPJ0]])

Attribution-to-confirm against his solo channel before hardening. [2026-06-24] ([[2026-06-24-yt-1YO2_PLGP-M]]) · [2026-07-17] ([[2026-07-17-yt-63m_fsOE-eQ]])

## Origin nodes (2009, age 15) — added synthesis pass 3 (2026-07-20)
- **Earliest channel-branding move.** A "free YouTube layouts" tutorial is the first evidence of him **customizing / branding his own channel** — the creator-business instinct present before any of it was a business. [2009-02-11] ([[2009-02-11-yt-jxSkySN3kks]])
- **The "MKB" identity, literally on-screen.** Puts his **"MKB" initials** (Marques Keith Brownlee) on his first PowerPoint YouTube avatar, and mentions his prior avatar was "a picture of me playing golf" — the initials that become the channel name, dated. [2009-02-01] ([[2009-02-01-yt-zArasubfsPI]])
- **The audience-request content loop, from day one.** Repeatedly invites email/PM tutorial requests and makes videos in direct response ("PM me for a tutorial"; the SRWare Iron and OS X-cursors videos exist because commenters asked) — the two-way creator↔audience relationship that defines the channel, already running at age 15. [2009-02-09] ([[2009-02-09-yt-oNMFjL1M1rA]]) · [2009-02-01] ([[2009-02-01-yt-s9lU1eAXVn4]])

### Origin growth timeline, formats & branding (Feb–Apr 2009) — added synthesis pass 4 (2026-07-20)
The recurring **"Update" milestone videos** give a rare dated subscriber timeline of the channel's first months, plus the origins of formats and branding that persist.
- **Dated subscriber timeline (2009, age 15).** ~**74 subscribers at video #100** (~2 months in, on a daily-upload cadence) → **95 subs** a few days later → **260+ subs** (Update 2.0) → **450 subs / 100k video views** (Update 2.1) — a slow, self-documented grind. He tracks growth publicly from the start and states audience-building intent openly. [2009-02-19] ([[2009-02-19-yt-FcugVUydSBY]]) · [2009-02-22] ([[2009-02-22-yt-0M_K6f-7vWc]]) · [2009-03-08] ([[2009-03-08-yt-Vi6_MQ-2FMc]]) · [2009-03-21] ([[2009-03-21-yt-49DVDqDm_tI]])
- **Origin of the commentary format + an explicit growth strategy.** "New Ideas" announces a planned **tech-talk / tech-news commentary** format (beyond how-to tutorials) alongside an explicit channel-growth goal — at 15, the earliest seed of the industry-commentary side of the channel. [2009-02-17] ([[2009-02-17-yt-L8JTCiWXu2A]]) → also [[../tech-industry-commentary/tech-industry-commentary|Tech Industry Commentary hub]]
- **Recurring "Update / Inbox" formats founded.** Update 2.0 launches the recurring **Inbox** Q&A segment; the "Update" series itself is his first recurring non-tutorial format — direct-to-audience channel bookkeeping that prefigures later community engagement. [2009-03-08] ([[2009-03-08-yt-Vi6_MQ-2FMc]])
- **Re-record-for-quality as a stated business instinct.** Update 2.1 states he'll **re-record his early CamStudio/Movie-Maker tutorials** to a higher standard while keeping the originals for their view counts — an early cost/benefit read on his own back-catalog. [2009-03-21] ([[2009-03-21-yt-49DVDqDm_tI]])
- **The MKBHD wordmark, hand-built.** The "Create A Channel Icon Using GIMP" tutorial is the **literal construction of the MKBHD logo**: the **MKB initials + HD**, three text layers (including a giant low-opacity "X"), a **black-to-red gradient** — the earliest **red-on-black MKBHD identity** as a deliberate branding artifact ("my entire channel isn't HD so I might as well make my logo HD"). This upgrades the pass-3 "candidate red-on-black aesthetic seed" to a concrete dated branding decision — though a *causal* line to the later crimson studio brand is still left open. [2009-03-29] ([[2009-03-29-yt-oix079jUkVU]])
- **Early influence + monetization ambition.** "Are You A Geek?" plugs **Chris Pirillo** (an early influence) and frames the channel's geek identity; the Dafont tutorial states an explicit **YouTube Partner** goal — the first on-record intent to monetize. [2009-03-12] ([[2009-03-12-yt-qtk1maA23k8]]) · [2009-03-13] ([[2009-03-13-yt-EEldRjkhZ8o]])
- **Deliberate series/curation from the start.** The "YouTube Playlists" tutorial reveals early, intentional **series structure** (Laptop Battery Savings / 2009 Apple Releases / Free Software / Updates-Inbox) — channel curation treated as a craft at 15. [2009-03-28] ([[2009-03-28-yt-j2cYJDwSH0g]])
