## The Smartphone Awards (annual franchise)

A year-end, category-by-category awards show (winner + runner-up + honorable mentions), framed as a definitive buyer's guide — "your one stop shop for picking one." Recurs every year; always hedged as "one person's opinion" ("it's my show so I get to be a bit biased").

**Durable evaluation criteria (stable across years):**
- Each category rewards a specific *criterion*, e.g. "best *use* of the big/small space," versatility across **both** photo and video, best power *experience* — not raw specs. [2019-12-24] ([[2019-12-24-yt-4OnwUz-I2GI]])
- Recurring values: battery over thinness; no-compromise flagships; near-stock Android as a tiebreaker; value/pricing as a first-class category. [2014-12-08] ([[2014-12-08-yt-f6pPIG3EvAs]]) · [2016-12-23] ([[2016-12-23-yt-NKY93vCiPIo]])
- **MVP / Phone of the Year** rewards the best *combination* of attributes at value, not the single best or most expensive phone. [2019-12-24] ([[2019-12-24-yt-4OnwUz-I2GI]])

## The Blind Smartphone Camera Test

Marques's signature *unbiased* camera-evaluation project (run on @Waveform / his channel). Methodology captured for 2022:
- Run "science style": 16 phones shoot identical standard/portrait/low-light photos; a purpose-built site serves randomized head-to-head matchups scored by **ELO rating**. 20M+ votes from 600k+ voters. [2022-12-23] ([[2022-12-23-yt-MYrfLmm_cT4]])
- Test shots deliberately pack many variables (dynamic range, multiple skin tones, hair/non-human cutouts) so "no phone wins on a single trick." He blind-tests *himself* too. [2022-12-23] ([[2022-12-23-yt-MYrfLmm_cT4]])
- **Durable principle: correct exposure beats over-brightness/over-saturation.** Brightness/saturation help a bad camera avoid last place, but a correctly-exposed photo still wins. Pixel 6A won 2022, Pixel 5A won 2021 — "a repeat, not a fluke." [2022-12-23] ([[2022-12-23-yt-MYrfLmm_cT4]])

## Positions & heuristics

- **Vertical integration beats raw spec sheets.** The iPhone 5s ships 2011-Galaxy-S2-class specs yet performs like a 2013 flagship because Apple controls hardware + software end-to-end. A durable MKBHD thesis against spec-sheet reviewing. [2013-10-12] ([[2013-10-12-yt-uBEaG6n0XDs]])
- **Premium-finish heuristic:** glossy/special-edition finishes (jet black) are scratch- and fingerprint-prone; matte is "the safe route" default. [2016-09-14] ([[2016-09-14-yt-J5HtSy5bATk]])
- **Headphone-jack removal — pragmatic, not outraged.** Understated and adapter-focused; the real friction is the $9 dongle replacement cost, not "courage." Earlier he'd conceded a matched Lightning DAC can sound audibly better but objected on practical grounds. [2016-09-14] ([[2016-09-14-yt-J5HtSy5bATk]]) · [2016-07-14] ([[2016-07-14-yt-2b1VPqCrk2c]])
- **Foldables — "being first doesn't matter if it sucks."** Royole FlexPai as the cautionary rushed-first foldable ("almost shouldn't have existed"); prefers fold-*in* over fold-*out*; original Galaxy Fold treated as "first-gen = public beta." [2019-07-20] ([[2019-07-20-yt-ROkXM3csNWY]]) · [2019-04-18] ([[2019-04-18-yt-vtqtyyGZvXM]])
- **Low-res LCD is a standing critique.** Repeatedly dings sub-720p LCDs on $700 phones ("the YouTube app lies to you"). [2019-09-30] ([[2019-09-30-yt-OoY7zp8GkLI]])
- **"It's really hard to buy a bad new phone now"** — a recurring state-of-the-market read (from 2019 on). [2019-12-24] ([[2019-12-24-yt-4OnwUz-I2GI]])
